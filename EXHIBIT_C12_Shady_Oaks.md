# EXHIBIT C-12: Shady Oaks / Housing Records

**Bates Range:** PIGORS-C-12
**Filing:** Federal Civil Rights Complaint, 42 USC 1983
**Court:** United States District Court, Western District of Michigan
**Case:** Pigors v. Watson et al.

---

## Description

This exhibit comprises property records, housing records, and government agency records documenting the conditions and actions at Shady Oaks Park, a manufactured home community where Plaintiff Andrew James Pigors resided. The records document a pattern of property-related violations including unlawful lockout from the property, destruction of personal property, water shutoff, and sewage system failures that resulted in environmental violations documented by the Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy (EGLE) under Violation Notice VN-017235.

Shady Oaks Park is operated through a corporate chain: Alden Global Capital, a New York-based hedge fund, controls Homes of America LLC, which in turn controls Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC, the direct operator of the community. This multi-layered corporate structure is relevant to both the identification of responsible parties and the pattern of institutional indifference to residents' rights that pervaded the community's management.

The records document that Plaintiff was locked out of his property, that his personal belongings were destroyed or disposed of without notice or legal process, that water service was terminated, and that sewage conditions created health and environmental hazards. These actions were taken without any court order authorizing the deprivation, without any legal process, and without any opportunity for Plaintiff to be heard. The housing violations occurred contemporaneously with the custody and contempt proceedings, compounding the economic and personal devastation Plaintiff experienced.

## Legal Significance

The Shady Oaks records establish additional dimensions of constitutional harm and support the Section 1983 claim by demonstrating the breadth of Plaintiff's deprivations across multiple areas of fundamental rights. While the custody and contempt proceedings (Exhibits C-1 through C-5) address the deprivation of parental rights and liberty, the housing records address the deprivation of property rights and the right to habitable housing.

The records are particularly significant to the federal complaint in the context of the coordinated judicial action documented in Exhibits C-6 and C-7. When Plaintiff sought judicial redress for the housing violations by filing Case No. 2025-002760-CZ, the case was assigned to Chief Judge Kenneth Hoopes, a former law partner of Defendant Hon. Jenny L. McNeill, who dismissed the claims with prejudice without an evidentiary hearing (Exhibit C-7). The Shady Oaks records thus demonstrate both the underlying constitutional harm and the denial of any state remedy for that harm, which satisfies the requirement under Section 1983 that state remedies be inadequate.

The EGLE violation (VN-017235) establishes that government regulatory authorities independently confirmed the existence of the environmental and habitability violations that Plaintiff alleged. This independent government finding corroborates Plaintiff's claims and undermines any suggestion that the housing complaints were frivolous or unfounded. The state court's dismissal of claims that a state regulatory agency had independently validated demonstrates the depth of the due process failure in the state court proceedings.

## Key Evidence

- **Property lockout:** Plaintiff was locked out of his residence at Shady Oaks Park without any court order or legal process
- **Personal property destruction:** Plaintiff's personal belongings were destroyed or disposed of without notice, opportunity to retrieve, or legal authorization
- **Water shutoff:** Water service to Plaintiff's unit was terminated, creating immediate habitability concerns
- **Sewage violations:** Sewage system failures at the community created environmental and health hazards
- **EGLE Violation Notice VN-017235:** Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy issued a formal violation notice confirming environmental violations at Shady Oaks Park
- **Corporate chain:** Alden Global Capital (New York hedge fund) controls Homes of America LLC, which controls Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC
- **No legal process:** Property lockout and personal property destruction occurred without any court order, notice, or hearing
- **State court dismissal:** When Plaintiff sought judicial relief in Case No. 2025-002760-CZ, the claims were dismissed with prejudice by Chief Judge Hoopes (Exhibit C-7)
- **Contemporaneous with custody proceedings:** Housing violations occurred during the same period as the custody and contempt proceedings, compounding Plaintiff's losses

## Constitutional Implications

The Shady Oaks records implicate both the Due Process Clause and the Fourth Amendment's protection against unreasonable seizures. The lockout from property and destruction of personal belongings without any legal process constitutes a deprivation of property without due process of law. The Fourteenth Amendment prohibits such deprivations regardless of whether the actor is a state official or a private party acting in concert with state authority. Where, as here, the state court system subsequently ratified the deprivation by dismissing the victim's claims without a hearing, the state has effectively endorsed the unconstitutional conduct.

The housing records also demonstrate the totality of the constitutional harm suffered by Plaintiff, which is relevant to the damages analysis under Section 1983. Plaintiff did not merely lose custody of his child; he simultaneously lost his home, his personal property, his employment, and his liberty. The convergence of these deprivations, occurring across multiple courts controlled by former law partners, presents a picture of comprehensive, coordinated constitutional destruction that warrants substantial compensatory and punitive damages. The EGLE violation independently validates the seriousness of the housing conditions and forecloses any argument that Plaintiff's complaints were exaggerated or unfounded.
