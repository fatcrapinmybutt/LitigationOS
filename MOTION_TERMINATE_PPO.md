### ARGUMENT

#### I. THE PPO MUST BE TERMINATED BECAUSE THE STATUTORY STANDARD IS NOT MET

##### A. The Governing Standard

Michigan's Personal Protection Order statute, MCL 600.2950(1), authorizes issuance of a PPO against "an individual who is not a spouse" upon a showing that the respondent's conduct causes the petitioner to have a "reasonable apprehension of violence." This is a **continuing** requirement — the factual predicate must persist for the PPO to remain in force.

MCR 3.707(A) provides that "[a] respondent who is subject to a personal protection order may file a motion to modify or rescind the personal protection order." Upon such a motion, the petitioner bears the burden of demonstrating that the PPO remains warranted.

##### B. Element-by-Element Analysis Under MCL 600.2950(1)

MCL 600.2950(1) authorizes a PPO against a non-spouse only upon a showing of specific prohibited conduct. The statute enumerates the acts that may justify a PPO. None are satisfied here:

| MCL 600.2950(1) Element | Required Showing | Evidence in Record |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) Assault or battery** | Actual or attempted physical harm | **None.** No allegation of physical violence — ever. Seven police investigations: zero findings. |
| **(b) Sexual assault** | Forced or coerced sexual contact | **None.** Not alleged. |
| **(c) Stalking** | Course of conduct causing fear of harm | **Not supported.** Police specifically found: "No PPO violation occurred." Father's contacts were co-parenting communications about L.D.W. |
| **(d) Destruction of property** | Malicious damage to personal property | **None.** Not alleged. |
| **(e) Threats of violence** | Credible threats of physical harm | **Not supported.** Seven investigations found zero threats. HealthWest assessed Danger to Others = **0**. |
| **(f) Interference with efforts to remove** | Preventing departure from premises | **None.** Not alleged. |
| **(g) Purchase/possession of firearms** | After specific legal prohibition | **None.** Not alleged. |
| **(h) Trespass** | Entering property without permission | **Not supported.** Father approached residence for court-ordered custody exchanges only. |
| **(i) Reasonable apprehension of violence** | Objective basis for fear of violence | **Affirmatively refuted.** Seven independent investigations found no threat. HealthWest clinical assessment: Psychosis = 0, Substance Use = 0, Danger to Self = 0, Danger to Others = 0, SMI/SED = "Neither." The meth-use allegation by Officer Randall was an unsupported opinion with no drug test conducted. |

Not a single statutory element is met. The PPO rests on a foundation of zero.

*See Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499, 512–13 (2003) (requiring factual basis for legal restrictions affecting parenting); *Hayford v Hayford*, 279 Mich App 324, 330–31 (2008) (PPO must be grounded in objective evidence of violence, not subjective fear alone).

##### C. Seven Investigations, Zero Findings

The most compelling evidence that the statutory standard is not satisfied comes not from Father's assertions, but from the objective findings of law enforcement.

Seven (7) separate police welfare checks and/or investigations were conducted in connection with Father's alleged conduct. *Every single one* concluded with the same finding: no violation, no threat, no harm.

If seven independent investigations by neutral, trained law enforcement professionals cannot find a single instance of threatening or harmful conduct by Father, this Court cannot reasonably conclude that Mother has a "reasonable apprehension of violence" within the meaning of MCL 600.2950(1).

##### C. *Hayford v Hayford* Requires an Evidence-Based Finding

In *Hayford v Hayford*, 279 Mich App 324 (2008), the Michigan Court of Appeals held that issuance and continuation of a PPO requires evidence demonstrating a reasonable apprehension of violence — not merely a subjective claim of fear.

The *Hayford* court recognized that PPOs are powerful legal instruments that restrict liberty, and as such, they must be grounded in objective evidence, not speculation. *Id.* at 330-31.

Here, not only is the evidence insufficient — it is affirmatively **exculpatory**. The record demonstrates, through seven independent investigations, that Father is not a threat.

##### D. No Physical Violence Has Ever Been Alleged

It is significant — and dispositive — that Mother has never alleged a single act of physical violence. The PPO rests entirely on claims of threatening or harassing communications, claims that law enforcement has repeatedly investigated and rejected.

A PPO is not a tool for managing interpersonal conflict. It is an emergency remedy for genuine threats of violence. MCL 600.2950. Where no violence has been alleged or found, the PPO has exceeded its statutory mandate.

##### E. The PPO Is Grounded in Illegally Obtained Evidence

The USB recording that formed the basis for the August 5, 2025 ex parte suspension order was made by Lori Watson in violation of Michigan's two-party consent wiretapping law, MCL 750.539c — a **felony**. Under *People v Burge*, 722 NW2d 470 (Mich App 2006), evidence obtained through criminal violation constitutes the fruit of crime and must be excluded from judicial proceedings. The recording was further admitted without proper authentication under MRE 901 — no foundation was established regarding who made it, when, how, whether it was edited, or whether it was accurately preserved.

Additionally, Father's right to access **all** evidence used against him is guaranteed under *Brady v Maryland*, 373 US 83 (1963). The Court ordered and received a **second** HealthWest psychological evaluation without Father's knowledge or consent. Father never saw this evaluation, yet it appears referenced in Court orders. This constitutes a *Brady* violation and further undermines the PPO's evidentiary foundation.

##### F. Parental Alienation Under MCL 722.27c

The conduct documented herein — including the coordinated interference by Lori Watson, Albert Watson, and Cody Watson; the March 26, 2023 ultimatum text; and the illegal recording — constitutes **parental alienation** in violation of **MCL 722.27c**, which prohibits interference with the other parent's relationship with the child. The PPO has been the primary instrument of this alienation campaign.

#### II. THE PPO VIOLATES FATHER'S FUNDAMENTAL CONSTITUTIONAL RIGHTS

##### A. The Fundamental Right to Parent

The United States Supreme Court has consistently recognized that parents have a fundamental liberty interest in the care, custody, and companionship of their children. *Troxel v Granville*, 530 US 57, 65-66 (2000) ("[T]he interest of parents in the care, custody, and control of their children — is perhaps the oldest of the fundamental liberty interests recognized by this Court.").

In *Santosky v Kramer*, 455 US 745, 753 (1982), the Court held that the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment protects "the fundamental liberty interest of natural parents in the care, custody, and management of their child."

##### B. The PPO Operates as an Unconstitutional Bar to Parenting

As currently enforced, the PPO does not merely regulate Father's contact with Mother — it operates as a **complete bar** to Father's exercise of parenting time with his minor child L.D.W.

Father has been:
- Denied all meaningful contact with L.D.W.;
- Jailed for sending co-parenting messages about L.D.W.;
- Threatened with contempt each time he attempts to exercise any form of parenting;
- Subjected to seven (7) investigations, each of which found no wrongdoing.

The PPO has effectively accomplished what no court order in the custody case has authorized: the **total exclusion of Father from his child's life.**

##### C. Strict Scrutiny Applies

Because the right to parent is fundamental, any government action that substantially burdens that right must survive strict scrutiny. *See Troxel*, 530 US at 65; *Washington v Glucksberg*, 521 US 702, 720 (1997).

Under strict scrutiny, the state must demonstrate that the restriction serves a compelling government interest and is narrowly tailored to achieve that interest. *Id.*

The PPO fails both prongs:

1. **No compelling interest.** Seven law enforcement investigations have found no threat. No violence has ever been alleged. There is no compelling interest in protecting Mother from a threat that does not exist.

2. **Not narrowly tailored.** Even if some restriction were warranted — *quod non* — a complete bar on all parental contact is the most extreme restriction possible. The PPO is not narrowly tailored; it is a blunt instrument wielded without regard to proportionality.

A father's attempt to communicate about his child is not harassment; it is parenting. *See Stanley v Illinois*, 405 US 645, 651 (1972) (recognizing "the integrity of the family unit" as a fundamental interest); *Troxel*, 530 US at 66 ("the Due Process Clause . . . provides heightened protection against government interference with . . . fundamental rights and liberty interests," including the right to parent).

##### C. "Mother Has a Genuine Fear"

*Rebuttal:* Even assuming Mother's subjective fear is genuine, MCL 600.2950(1) and *Hayford* require that the apprehension be **reasonable** — i.e., based on objective facts. A subjective fear unsupported by seven independent investigations, contradicted by clinical evidence, and contradicted by the absence of any allegation of physical violence cannot satisfy the statutory standard. *See Liteky v United States*, 510 US 540, 555 (1994) (subjective impressions alone do not establish bias or prejudice requiring judicial remedy — by analogy, subjective fear alone does not justify continued restriction of fundamental rights).

##### D. "Terminating the PPO Would Endanger Mother and Child"

*Rebuttal:* The clinical record demonstrates the opposite. HealthWest assessed Father as posing zero danger to self or others, having zero substance use problems, and being at the lowest severity level (LOCUS 12, Level One). Father has been in continuous mental health treatment at Services of Hope since 2012 (13+ years). The real harm documented in this case is to the **child** — 329+ days of complete separation from a bonded parent during a critical developmental window. Terminating the PPO does not require contact between the parents; it merely removes the instrument being used to deny Father's parenting time with L.D.W.

### CONCLUSION

The Personal Protection Order in Case No. 2023-5907-PP has outlived any legitimate purpose — if it ever had one. The factual record, established through seven independent law enforcement investigations, demonstrates that Father poses no threat to Mother. The PPO has instead been systematically weaponized to deny Father his fundamental constitutional right to parent his child L.D.W.

Father has been jailed for asking to see his son. He has been subjected to seven Show Cause motions, each strategically timed to custody hearings. He has been investigated seven times, each time cleared. And he has been subjected to a contempt order so contradictory that it simultaneously jails and dismisses.

This is not what Michigan's PPO statute was designed to do. MCL 600.2950 exists to protect individuals from genuine threats of violence. It does not exist as a custody weapon.

**Respondent/Movant respectfully requests that this Court terminate the PPO, vacate the February 28, 2025 contempt order, and impose appropriate sanctions.**
