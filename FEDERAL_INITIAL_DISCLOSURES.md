Plaintiff will seek punitive damages against defendants Watson, Berry, and McNeill (individual capacity) pursuant to 42 U.S.C. § 1983 and *Smith v. Wade*, 461 U.S. 30 (1983). Punitive damages are appropriate where defendants acted with reckless or callous indifference to Plaintiff's federally protected rights. Amount to be determined at trial based on evidence of willfulness, malice, and/or reckless indifference.

**Estimated Punitive Damages Range:** $100,000 — $1,000,000 (per defendant, subject to proof)

## V. TIMING AND SERVICE REQUIREMENTS

### FRCP 26(a)(1)(C) — Deadline
Initial disclosures must be made **within 14 days after the parties' Rule 26(f) conference**, unless a different time is set by stipulation or court order.

### FRCP 26(f) Conference — Planning Meeting
Before the scheduling conference with the court, the parties must:
1. Confer to consider the nature and basis of claims and defenses
2. Discuss possibilities for promptly settling or resolving the case
3. Make or arrange for required disclosures
4. Discuss preservation of discoverable information
5. Develop a proposed discovery plan

**Note:** The Rule 26(f) conference must occur at least **21 days before the scheduling conference** is held or a scheduling order is due under Rule 16(b).
