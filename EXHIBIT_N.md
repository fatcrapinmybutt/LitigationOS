### On-Record Statements by Hon. Jenny L. McNeill

#### 1. Denial of Access to Courts

> "Do not file anymore, I will not look at it"

A blanket denial of access to courts — a fundamental First Amendment right. A judge cannot bar a party from filing motions.

#### 2. Silencing the Plaintiff

> Told Plaintiff to "shut my mouth" during hearing

During the fourth continued hearing, Plaintiff objected to the judge and Emily A. Watson discussing prescription medication as a condition for seeing L.D.W. Plaintiff stated neither was qualified or legally authorized to mandate medication. The judge responded by telling him to shut his mouth and subsequently sentenced him to contempt.

#### 3. Medication Coercion

On the record, Hon. Jenny L. McNeill and Emily A. Watson discussed that prescription medication would be the "only way" Father could see his child:

- Unlawful practice of medicine: Neither is a licensed medical professional
- Coercive conditioning of parental rights on medication compliance
- Due process: Fundamental rights cannot be conditioned on medical treatment

#### 4. Contempt for Objecting

Plaintiff's "contempt" was objecting while the judge cross-examined witnesses herself. Andrew successfully cross-examined witnesses despite judicial interference — then was sentenced to 14 days jail for objecting to the medication discussion.
