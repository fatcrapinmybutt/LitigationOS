## I. INTRODUCTION

This motion seeks immediate restoration of Plaintiff's parenting time pending a full evidentiary hearing on the merits. The August 8, 2025 ex parte suspension of all parenting time, and its subsequent continuations, violate Michigan law, constitutional due process, and the fundamental presumption favoring parenting time under MCL 722.27a. The suspension was issued without an affidavit, without an evidentiary hearing, without clear and convincing proof of endangerment, and without adequate findings. The continued deprivation—now exceeding 192 days—causes irreparable harm to the child and violates Plaintiff's fundamental liberty interest in the parent-child relationship.

## II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

### A. Pre-Suspension Status

Until August 8, 2025, Plaintiff exercised parenting time pursuant to the July 17, 2024 custody order, which awarded him 79 overnights per year following the custody trial. This represented a reduction from the prior 182.5 overnights (50/50 arrangement), but was the operative parenting schedule.

### B. The Ex Parte Suspension

On August 8, 2025, the Court issued an ex parte order suspending all of Plaintiff's parenting time based on an unsworn motion filed by Defendant. The motion contained no affidavit, no verified pleading, no specific facts, and no showing of immediate and irreparable harm. The order was issued without an evidentiary hearing and without the opportunity for Plaintiff to be heard.

### C. Lack of Procedural Safeguards

The ex parte order violates MCR 3.207(B), which permits ex parte relief only when "specific facts in an affidavit or verified pleading" demonstrate "immediate and irreparable" harm from notice delay. The August 8 motion satisfied none of these requirements:

- **No Affidavit:** The motion was unsworn
- **No Verified Pleading:** The motion contained no verification
- **No Specific Facts:** The motion lacked child-specific factual allegations
- **No Immediate/Irreparable Harm:** No emergency circumstances were alleged
- **No Hearing:** Plaintiff was not afforded an opportunity to respond

### D. Lack of Clear and Convincing Evidence

MCL 722.27a(3) provides that parenting time shall not be suspended or substantially limited unless there is **clear and convincing evidence** that parenting time would **endanger the child's life, physical health, or emotional well-being**. The record contains no such evidence:

- **No Substantiated Abuse:** No CPS findings; no police reports of abuse
- **No Mental Health Diagnosis:** HealthWest evaluation (completed at Court's direction) contains no findings supporting suspension
- **No Endangerment Finding:** Court's orders contain no findings that parenting time would endanger the child
- **No Evidentiary Hearing:** No testimony under oath; no cross-examination; no evidence presented

### E. Continued Deprivation

The suspension has been continued at multiple hearings without resolution:
- September 4, 2025
- October 29, 2025
- November 26, 2025
- (Additional continuations through present)

At each continuation, the Court has failed to:
- Hold an evidentiary hearing
- Require clear and convincing proof of endangerment
- Issue findings of fact and conclusions of law
- Address Plaintiff's evidence (HealthWest evaluation, police reports, medical records)

### F. Harm to Child and Plaintiff

The 192+ day deprivation has caused:
- **Disruption of Established Relationship:** Child has been deprived of meaningful contact with father
- **Emotional Harm:** Child has experienced loss of paternal relationship
- **Developmental Harm:** Deprivation of father's involvement in daily life, activities, and decision-making
- **Irreparable Harm to Plaintiff:** Loss of parenting time cannot be fully remedied by "makeup" time; the lost moments are gone forever

## III. LEGAL STANDARD FOR EMERGENCY RELIEF

### A. Parenting Time Presumption

MCL 722.27a(1) establishes a presumption that parenting time is in the best interest of the child. This presumption may only be overcome by **clear and convincing evidence** that parenting time would endanger the child's life, physical health, or emotional well-being. MCL 722.27a(3).

### B. Ex Parte Procedures

MCR 3.207(B) permits ex parte relief in domestic relations matters only when:
1. An affidavit or verified pleading is filed
2. The affidavit/pleading contains specific facts
3. The specific facts demonstrate immediate and irreparable harm
4. The harm would result from notice and delay

### C. Constitutional Liberty Interest

The parent-child relationship is a fundamental liberty interest protected by the Fourteenth Amendment. Troxel v. Granville, 530 US 57 (2000). Deprivation of this interest requires due process protections, including:
- Notice and opportunity to be heard
- Clear and convincing evidence
- Adequate findings of fact

Santosky v. Kramer, 455 US 745 (1982).

### D. Irreparable Harm Standard

To obtain emergency relief, a party must demonstrate:
1. A strong likelihood of success on the merits
2. Irreparable harm absent relief
3. The balance of equities favors the moving party
4. Granting relief is in the public interest

## IV. ARGUMENT

### A. Plaintiff Is Likely to Succeed on the Merits

#### 1. The Ex Parte Order Violated MCR 3.207(B)

The August 8, 2025 order was issued without compliance with MCR 3.207(B). The motion was unsworn, contained no affidavit or verified pleading, alleged no specific facts, and demonstrated no immediate and irreparable harm. Under the plain language of the rule, the ex parte order was procedurally defective and should be vacated.

#### 2. The Suspension Violates MCL 722.27a(3)

MCL 722.27a(3) requires clear and convincing evidence that parenting time would endanger the child before parenting time may be suspended or substantially limited. The record contains no such evidence:

**No Substantiated Abuse:** Plaintiff has no history of abuse, neglect, or harm to the child. There are no CPS findings, no police reports of abuse, and no medical evidence of injury.

**Favorable Mental Health Evaluation:** The HealthWest evaluation, which Plaintiff completed at the Court's direction, contains no findings supporting suspension. The evaluation does not conclude that Plaintiff is a danger to the child or that parenting time would be harmful.

**No Endangerment Findings:** The Court's orders contain no findings that parenting time would endanger the child's life, physical health, or emotional well-being. The orders lack the required clear and convincing findings.

**Evidentiary Hearing Required:** MCL 722.27a(3) contemplates an evidentiary hearing where evidence is presented, testimony is given under oath, and cross-examination occurs. No such hearing has been held.

#### 3. The Suspension Violates Due Process

The Fourteenth Amendment guarantees due process before deprivation of a fundamental liberty interest. The parent-child relationship is such an interest. Troxel, 530 US at 65-66.

Due process requires:
- **Notice:** Plaintiff received notice of the motion but had no opportunity to respond
- **Hearing:** No evidentiary hearing was held
- **Evidence:** No evidence was presented under oath or subject to cross-examination
- **Findings:** No findings of fact were issued

The ex parte suspension, continued without an evidentiary hearing, violates due process.

#### 4. The Suspension Violates MCR 3.210(D)

MCR 3.210(D) requires that orders affecting parenting time include findings of fact and conclusions of law. The August 8 order and subsequent continuations lack:
- Findings regarding the best-interest factors under MCL 722.23
- Findings regarding clear and convincing endangerment
- Findings addressing Plaintiff's evidence
- Conclusions of law explaining the legal basis for the suspension

The orders are legally deficient.

### B. Irreparable Harm Absent Relief

The deprivation of parenting time causes irreparable harm that cannot be fully remedied by makeup time:

**Lost Time:** Each day of deprivation represents lost moments with the child that cannot be recovered. The child experiences developmental milestones, school events, and daily life without the father's involvement.

**Relationship Damage:** Prolonged deprivation damages the parent-child relationship. The child may internalize the deprivation as rejection or abandonment.

**Emotional Harm:** The child experiences emotional harm from loss of paternal relationship, particularly when the deprivation is unexplained or appears punitive.

**Constitutional Harm:** Plaintiff's fundamental liberty interest is violated each day of deprivation.

### C. Balance of Equities Favors Restoration

**Plaintiff's Interest:** Plaintiff has a fundamental constitutional interest in maintaining the parent-child relationship and exercising parenting time.

**Child's Interest:** The child has an interest in maintaining the established relationship with both parents. The presumption under MCL 722.27a(1) reflects the law's recognition that parenting time is in the child's best interest.

**Defendant's Interest:** Defendant's interest in restricting parenting time is not supported by clear and convincing evidence of endangerment.

**Public Interest:** The public interest is served by enforcing statutory presumptions, requiring procedural compliance, and protecting constitutional rights.

### D. Public Interest Favors Relief

Granting relief serves the public interest by:
- Enforcing the statutory presumption favoring parenting time
- Requiring procedural compliance with MCR 3.207(B) and MCR 3.210(D)
- Protecting constitutional due process rights
- Ensuring that parenting time is not suspended without clear and convincing evidence

4. **Award** Plaintiff makeup parenting time equal to all days wrongfully denied from August 8, 2025 to the date of restoration;

5. **Impose** sanctions on Defendant for filing an unsworn motion and for the frivolous ex parte suspension;

6. **Award** Plaintiff attorney fees and costs incurred in bringing this motion;

7. **Grant** such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
