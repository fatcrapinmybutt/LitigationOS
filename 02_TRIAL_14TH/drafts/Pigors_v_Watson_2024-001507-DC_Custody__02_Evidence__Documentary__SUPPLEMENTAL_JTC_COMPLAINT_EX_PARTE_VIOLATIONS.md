## III. VIOLATION 1: EX PARTE REVIEW BEFORE SERVICE (MCR 3.207, Due Process)

### A. The Violation

Judge McNeill and her staff reviewed the video recording before Father was served with the evidence and before Father was given notice of the ex parte hearing. This violates MCR 3.207 and due process.

### B. Legal Standard

**MCR 3.207(B)** provides: "A court may issue an ex parte order only when it appears on the basis of specific facts shown by affidavit or by the verified complaint that immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage will result to a party before that party can be heard in response."

The critical language is "before that party can be heard in response." This means:
- The party must have notice of the ex parte proceeding
- The party must have opportunity to respond
- The judge must not prejudge the case before hearing from the party

**14th Amendment Due Process** requires:
- Notice of charges/allegations
- Opportunity to be heard
- Impartial judge
- No prejudgment before hearing

### C. Judge McNeill's Violation

Judge McNeill violated both MCR 3.207 and due process by:

1. **Reviewing evidence before Father was served**
   - USB recording submitted directly to clerk's office
   - Judge's staff listened to recording same day
   - Father not served until 4 days later
   - Judge prejudged case before Father could respond

2. **Prejudging case based on one-sided evidence**
   - Judge listened to recording without Father's explanation
   - Judge listened to recording without Father's context
   - Judge listened to recording without Father's opportunity to respond
   - Judge formed opinions before Father could present his side

3. **Issuing order without hearing from Father**
   - Order issued same day as evidence submission
   - Father not given notice before order issued
   - Father not given opportunity to respond before order issued
   - Father deprived of parental rights without due process

### D. Evidence of Violation

**Father's Question at First Objection Hearing:**
Father asked Judge McNeill on record: "Your Honor, have you already listened to this recording?"

**Judge McNeill's Admission:**
Judge McNeill responded on record: "Yes, I had my staff listen to it."

**Timeline Evidence:**
- August 5, 2025: USB submitted to clerk's office
- August 5, 2025: Judge's staff listens to recording
- August 5, 2025: Judge issues suspension order
- August 8, 2025: Father receives notice of order
- August 8, 2025: Father receives copy of recording via email

**Prejudicial Effect:**
Judge McNeill's pre-review of the evidence demonstrates that:
- She prejudged the case before Father could respond
- She formed opinions based on one-sided evidence
- She was not impartial when she issued the order
- She violated due process by depriving Father of opportunity to be heard

## IV. VIOLATION 2: INADEQUATE SERVICE OF EVIDENCE (MCR 2.305, Due Process)

### A. The Violation

Emily Watson served Father with the video recording only **4 days before the hearing**, on a **Friday**, with the hearing scheduled for **Monday**. Judge McNeill allowed this inadequate service and did not grant a continuance.

### B. Legal Standard

**MCR 2.305** governs discovery and service of evidence. The rule requires:
- Service of evidence with adequate time for opposing party to respond
- Service by proper means
- Compliance with court orders regarding service

**MCR 2.119** governs motions and requires:
- Service at least 5 business days before hearing (or as ordered by court)
- Adequate time for opposing party to respond

**Due Process** requires:
- Adequate notice of charges/allegations
- Adequate time to prepare response
- Adequate time to consult with attorney
- Adequate time to obtain evidence and witnesses

### C. Judge McNeill's Violation

Judge McNeill violated MCR 2.305 and due process by:

1. **Allowing inadequate service**
   - Evidence served Friday, August 8, 2025
   - Hearing Monday, August 11, 2025
   - Only 3 business days for Father to review and respond
   - MCR 2.305 requires 5 business days minimum

2. **Not enforcing service requirements**
   - Judge did not require proper service
   - Judge did not sanction Emily Watson for inadequate service
   - Judge did not enforce MCR 2.305
   - Judge allowed pattern to continue

3. **Not granting continuance**
   - Father had insufficient time to prepare
   - Father had insufficient time to consult attorney
   - Father had insufficient time to obtain counter-evidence
   - Judge did not grant continuance despite inadequate service

4. **Proceeding with hearing despite inadequate service**
   - Judge proceeded with hearing on Monday as scheduled
   - Judge did not delay hearing to allow adequate preparation
   - Judge did not allow Father adequate time to respond

### D. Evidence of Violation

**Timeline:**
- August 8, 2025 (Friday): Emily Watson serves Father via email
- August 11, 2025 (Monday): Hearing scheduled
- Only 3 business days between service and hearing

**Father's Objection:**
Father objected at the hearing to inadequate service of evidence.

**Judge McNeill's Response:**
Judge McNeill ignored Father's objection and proceeded with hearing.

**Pattern of Inadequate Service:**
This was the **4th documented instance** of Emily Watson serving evidence inadequately. Father stated on record each time that service was inadequate.

**MRE 901** requires authentication of evidence. Evidence must be authenticated by testimony from someone with personal knowledge.

**MCR 3.101** governs filing of documents. All documents must be filed with the clerk's office and served on opposing parties.

## VIII. VIOLATION 6: WIRETAPPING LAW VIOLATION (MCL 750.539)

### A. The Violation

The video recording was made by Emily Watson's mother without Father's knowledge or consent. Michigan is a **two-party consent state**. Recording a phone call without consent of both parties is a **felony**.

### B. Legal Standard

**MCL 750.539** provides: "A person shall not use a device to record, or transmit a wire, oral, or electronic communication without the consent of all parties to the communication."

Michigan is a two-party consent state. Both parties to a phone call must consent to recording.

**Consequence:** Violation is a felony punishable by imprisonment.

**Judicial Response:** Judges should exclude evidence obtained in violation of wiretapping law as fruit of crime.

### C. Judge McNeill's Violation

Judge McNeill violated MCL 750.539 by:

1. **Accepting recording obtained in violation of law**
   - Recording made without Father's consent
   - Recording violates MCL 750.539
   - Judge should have excluded recording

2. **Not excluding recording as fruit of crime**
   - Recording is product of felony
   - Judge should have excluded as fruit of crime
   - Judge should have sanctioned Emily Watson

3. **Using recording as basis for suspension order**
   - Recording admitted without legal basis
   - Recording used to justify deprivation of parental rights
   - Recording obtained through criminal conduct

### D. Evidence of Violation

**Recording Made Without Consent:**
Emily Watson's mother recorded phone call without Father's knowledge or consent.

**Violation of MCL 750.539:**
Recording violates Michigan wiretapping law (two-party consent required).

**Judge's Use of Recording:**
Judge used recording as basis for suspension order.
