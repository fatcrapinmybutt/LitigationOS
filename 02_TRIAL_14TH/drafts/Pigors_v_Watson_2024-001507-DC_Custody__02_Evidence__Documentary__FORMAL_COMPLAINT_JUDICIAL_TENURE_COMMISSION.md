## III. GROUND 1: VIOLATION OF PROCEDURAL RULES (MCR 3.207)

### A. The Violation

On August 8, 2025, Judge McNeill issued an order suspending Father's parenting time based entirely on an unsworn telephone call from Emily Watson. The order violates MCR 3.207(B), which requires:

1. Written affidavit under oath
2. Specific facts supporting emergency relief
3. Certification that efforts to provide notice were made
4. Opportunity for Father to respond

**The August 8, 2025 Order:**
- Based on phone call, not affidavit
- No written affidavit in the record
- No opportunity for Father to respond
- No emergency circumstances documented
- No findings of imminent danger to child

### B. Legal Standard

MCR 3.207(B) provides: "A court may issue an ex parte order only when it appears on the basis of specific facts shown by affidavit or by the verified complaint that immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage will result to a party before that party can be heard in response."

The rule requires:
1. **Affidavit or verified complaint** - Not satisfied (phone call is neither)
2. **Specific facts** - Not satisfied (vague allegations)
3. **Immediate and irreparable injury** - Not satisfied (no emergency circumstances)
4. **Before party can be heard** - Not satisfied (no hearing provided)

### C. Judge McNeill's Violation

Judge McNeill issued the suspension order without:
- Written affidavit
- Specific facts
- Emergency circumstances
- Opportunity for Father to be heard
- Findings supporting emergency relief

This is a clear violation of MCR 3.207(B) and demonstrates abuse of judicial authority.

### D. Evidence of Violation

1. Court order dated August 8, 2025 references "phone call"
2. No affidavit in the record
3. No hearing transcript for August 8, 2025
4. No emergency findings issued
5. No notice provided to Father
6. No opportunity for Father to respond

### E. Prejudicial Effect

The violation resulted in:

### B. Legal Standard

Massey Coal Co., 556 U.S. 868 (2009), established that a judge must recuse when there is "a probability of actual bias" or when "the probability of actual bias on the part of the judge is too high to be constitutionally tolerable."

The Caperton standard is lower than requiring proof of actual bias; it requires only that a reasonable observer would question the judge's impartiality.

**Pattern 5: Failure to Apply Statutory Standards**

### A. The Violation

Judge McNeill systematically admitted unauthenticated evidence from Emily Watson while excluding authenticated evidence from Father, violating the Michigan Rules of Evidence and due process.

### B. Legal Standard

**MRE 901** requires authentication of evidence. Evidence must be authenticated before admission unless an exception applies.

**MRE 702-703** govern expert testimony. Expert testimony must meet the Daubert standard and be based on reliable methodology.

**MRE 401-403** govern relevance. Relevant evidence may be excluded if its probative value is substantially outweighed by unfair prejudice.

### C. Specific Violations

**Violation 1: Unauthenticated HealthWest Evaluation**

Judge McNeill admitted a "second" HealthWest evaluation without:
- Proper authentication (MRE 901)
- Proper foundation for expert testimony (MRE 702-703)
- Explanation for why "second" evaluation was used instead of first
- Comparison to first evaluation

The first HealthWest evaluation was favorable to Father. The "second" evaluation was admitted without proper foundation, violating evidence rules.

**Violation 2: Exclusion of Father's Evidence**

Judge McNeill excluded Father's evidence without legal basis:
- Police reports (relevant, authenticated, excluded)
- Father's declarations (relevant, authenticated, excluded)
- HealthWest first evaluation (relevant, authenticated, excluded)
- Father's testimony (relevant, excluded or mischaracterized)

**Violation 3: Admission of Emily Watson's Evidence**

Judge McNeill admitted Emily Watson's evidence without proper authentication:
- Allegations of arsenic poisoning (no medical evidence, not authenticated)
- Allegations of physical assault (no medical evidence, not authenticated)
- Allegations of surveillance (normal devices mischaracterized)
- Allegations of drug use (no medical evidence, not authenticated)
- Allegations of threat to child (contradicted by court's own custody order)

**Violation 4: Misapplication of Evidence Rules**

Judge McNeill applied evidence rules selectively:
- Father's evidence excluded under MRE 401-403 (probative value outweighed by unfair prejudice)
- Emily Watson's evidence admitted despite lack of authentication
- Father's expert testimony excluded
- Emily Watson's allegations admitted despite lack of foundation

### D. Evidence of Violation
