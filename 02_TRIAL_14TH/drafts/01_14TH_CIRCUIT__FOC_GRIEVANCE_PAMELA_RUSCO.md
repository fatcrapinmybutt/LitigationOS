## I. NATURE OF GRIEVANCE

This grievance is filed pursuant to **MCL 552.507(4)** and **MCR 3.218**, which provide that any party may file a written grievance with the Friend of the Court regarding the conduct of FOC staff. Complainant alleges that FOC Case Manager Pamela Rusco engaged in **prohibited ex parte communications** and **unauthorized witness coordination** that violated Michigan Judicial Ethics Standards, denied Complainant due process, and tainted the evidentiary record in the above-captioned cases.

## III. RULES AND STANDARDS VIOLATED

### A. MCJC Canon 3(A)(4) — Prohibition on Ex Parte Communications

Canon 3(A)(4) of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct provides that court personnel — including FOC staff acting under judicial direction — shall not engage in ex parte communications concerning a pending case. FOC case managers are officers of the court subject to the same prohibitions on ex parte contact that govern judges.

### B. MCR 3.218 — FOC Conduct Standards

MCR 3.218 establishes standards for FOC conduct, including the requirement that FOC actions be impartial, transparent, and subject to judicial oversight. Arranging witness testimony via ex parte telephone call — on the eve of a hearing — violates these standards.

### C. Due Process — US Constitution Amend. XIV; Mich. Const. Art. 1, § 17

Both the federal and Michigan constitutions guarantee that no person shall be deprived of a fundamental liberty interest without due process of law. The right of a parent to the care, custody, and control of his child is among the most fundamental rights recognized by law. *Troxel v Granville*, 530 U.S. 57, 65 (2000). Ex parte witness coordination by a court officer in a proceeding that determines parental rights denies the affected party the opportunity to be heard — the bedrock of due process.

## IV. HARM TO COMPLAINANT

1. **Tainted Evidence:** The HealthWest testimony arranged by Rusco's ex parte call was based on a second evaluation that was itself the product of court-influenced bias. This created a false evidentiary record that was used to justify continued suspension of Complainant's parenting time.

2. **Denied Opportunity to Prepare:** Complainant was not informed that HealthWest testimony was being arranged for the hearing, denying him the ability to prepare cross-examination, subpoena the first (favorable) evaluator, or challenge the circumstances of the second evaluation.

3. **Ongoing Deprivation:** Based in part on this tainted process, Complainant has been denied all contact with his three-year-old son L.D.W. for **200+ consecutive days** — the most critical developmental window for parent-child attachment.
