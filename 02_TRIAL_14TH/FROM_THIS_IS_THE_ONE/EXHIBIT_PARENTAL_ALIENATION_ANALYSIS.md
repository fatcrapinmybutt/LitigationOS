## VI. LEGAL FRAMEWORK: MCL 722.23 FACTOR (j) ANALYSIS

**Factor (j): The willingness and ability of each of the parties to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent or the child and the parents.**

Factor (j) is the **alienation factor**. Michigan courts have recognized it as one of the most important factors in custody determinations. See *Foskett v Foskett*, 247 Mich App 1, 10 (2001) ("Factor (j) is perhaps the most important factor in a custody dispute"); *Brown v Loveman*, 260 Mich App 576, 600 (2004) (emphasizing Factor (j) in alienation context).
