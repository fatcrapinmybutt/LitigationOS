# PROPOSED ORDER FOR DISQUALIFICATION AND REASSIGNMENT

## (MCR 2.003)

---

THE COURT, having considered Plaintiff's Verified Motion for Disqualification of Judge pursuant to MCR 2.003, the accompanying Affidavit of Bias of Andrew James Pigors, the exhibits submitted in support thereof, the arguments of the parties, and the court record in its entirety;

AND THE COURT, finding that the Plaintiff has demonstrated grounds for disqualification under MCR 2.003(C)(1)(a) (personal bias or prejudice), MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b)(i) (serious risk of actual bias impacting due process rights), and MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b)(ii) (failure to adhere to the appearance of impropriety standard set forth in Canon 2 of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct);

AND THE COURT, finding that a reasonable, objective person, fully informed of the circumstances documented in the Motion and Affidavit — including but not limited to ex parte orders entered without notice or hearing, the muting of the Plaintiff during a hearing followed by incarceration, a categorical refusal to consider the Plaintiff's future filings, ex parte communications between the judge's chambers and the prosecution and clinical witnesses, and the manipulation of a professional evaluation through ex parte contact — would harbor significant doubt about the presiding judge's ability to maintain impartiality;

AND THE COURT, finding that the probability of actual bias is too high to be constitutionally tolerable under *Caperton v A.T. Massey Coal Co.*, 556 U.S. 868 (2009), as adopted by MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b)(i);

NOW, THEREFORE:

---

## IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:

**1.** The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill is **DISQUALIFIED** from presiding over any further proceedings in Case No. 2024-001507-DC (Pigors v. Watson — Custody, Parenting Time, and Child Support) and the consolidated Case No. 2023-5907-PP (Personal Protection Order proceedings).

**2.** All pending matters in Case No. 2024-001507-DC and Case No. 2023-5907-PP are hereby **TRANSFERRED** and **REASSIGNED** to:

```
    _______________________________________________
    [Name of Reassigned Judge]
    [14th Circuit Court / Visiting Judge]
```

for all further proceedings, pursuant to MCR 2.003 and MCL 600.225.

**3.** In the event that structural conflicts within the 14th Judicial Circuit Court preclude assignment to a sitting judge of this bench, the Chief Judge shall request that the State Court Administrative Office assign a visiting judge from outside the 14th Judicial Circuit, pursuant to MCR 2.003(D)(3)(c) and MCL 600.225.

**4.** The successor judge shall conduct **de novo review** of all pending motions, including but not limited to any motions for modification of parenting time, enforcement of parenting time, and reconsideration of contempt findings.

**5.** The ex parte orders of July 17, 2025, and August 8, 2025, suspending Plaintiff's parenting time, and the contempt orders of November 15, 2025, and November 26, 2025, are **REFERRED** to the successor judge for de novo review, with such orders to remain in effect only until the successor judge has had the opportunity to review them on notice to all parties and after hearing.

**6.** The $250 filing deposit requirement previously imposed upon Plaintiff is **VACATED** effective immediately.

**7.** The Clerk of the Court shall transmit the complete case files for Case No. 2024-001507-DC and Case No. 2023-5907-PP to the successor judge within seven (7) days of the entry of this Order.
