## IV. LEGAL STANDARD

### A. Proper Cause and Change of Circumstances — *Vodvarka v. Grasmeyer*

24\. Before the Court may revisit a custody or parenting time order, the movant must demonstrate either "proper cause" or a "change of circumstances." *Vodvarka v. Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499, 509; 675 NW2d 847 (2003).

25\. "Proper cause" means "one or more appropriate grounds that have or could have a significant effect on the child's life sufficient to warrant a reevaluation of the custody order." *Id.* at 512. "Change of circumstances" requires "conditions that have or could have a significant effect on the child's well-being" that have arisen since the last custody order. *Id.* at 513.

26\. The threshold for establishing proper cause or change of circumstances is **preponderance of the evidence**. *Id.* at 508. If the established custodial environment would not be changed, the movant need prove only that modification is in the child's best interest by a preponderance. If the established custodial environment would be changed, the movant must prove best interest by clear and convincing evidence. *Id.*

### B. Parenting Time Rights — MCL 722.27a

27\. Michigan law creates a statutory presumption that "it is in the best interest of a child to have a strong relationship with both of his or her parents." **MCL 722.27a(1)**. Parenting time shall be granted in "sufficient frequency, duration, and type" to permit a child to maintain a strong relationship with both parents. *Id.*

28\. Parenting time may be restricted **only** upon a showing by "clear and convincing evidence that parenting time . . . would endanger the child's physical, mental, or emotional health." **MCL 722.27a(3)**. The August 8, 2025 order suspending all parenting time was entered without this mandatory finding.

29\. Further, **MCL 722.27a(7)** provides that "[t]he court may order makeup parenting time . . . for parenting time wrongfully denied." Plaintiff has been wrongfully denied 223 days of parenting time and is entitled to compensatory makeup time.

### C. Fundamental Constitutional Rights

30\. The right of a parent to the care, custody, and control of his child is a fundamental liberty interest protected by the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment. *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 U.S. 57, 65 (2000); *Meyer v. Nebraska*, 262 U.S. 390, 399 (1923).

31\. "The fundamental liberty interest of natural parents in the care, custody, and management of their child does not evaporate simply because they have not been model parents." *Santosky v. Kramer*, 455 U.S. 745, 753 (1982).

32\. Due process requires, at minimum, that a parent receive notice and an opportunity to be heard before parental rights are restricted. *Stanley v. Illinois*, 405 U.S. 645, 649 (1972). Plaintiff was denied both.

33\. A parent's fundamental right to the companionship of his child "undeniably warrants deference and, absent a powerful countervailing interest, protection." *Hunter v. Hunter*, 484 Mich 247, 257; 771 NW2d 694 (2009).
