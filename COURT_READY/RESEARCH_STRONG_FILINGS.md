### Recommendations — F1

1. **Strongest citation gap:** The absence of *Harvey v Harvey* (Michigan Supreme Court) is the most significant omission. This is the controlling MSC authority on mandatory best-interest judicial review and would powerfully support the argument that the Aug 8 ex parte order was entered without the required independent judicial assessment.
2. **Add *Eldred v Ziny*:** Establishes that court-ordered parenting time creates enforceable legal rights — the custodial parent cannot unilaterally revoke it. This frames Watson's conduct as violating an enforceable legal obligation, not merely a "preference."
3. **Hunter v Hunter** citation should be manually verified at a law library. The proposition is sound and consistent with Michigan Supreme Court doctrine, but the specific pinpoint (484 Mich at 257) could not be independently confirmed via web search.
4. **Academic citations (Bowlby, Wallerstein):** While persuasive, consider adding a Michigan-specific citation to a published opinion recognizing developmental harm from prolonged separation. Courts are more persuaded by judicial citations than academic ones.
