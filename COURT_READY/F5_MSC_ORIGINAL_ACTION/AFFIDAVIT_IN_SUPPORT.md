## II. THE SUCCESSFUL CUSTODY ARRANGEMENT (438 DAYS)

4. I initiated custody proceedings on or about April 1, 2024, by filing in the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, Family Division, Muskegon County (Case No. 2024-001507-DC). The case was assigned to Hon. Jenny L. McNeill.

5. A related Personal Protection Order case, No. 2023-5907-PP, was initiated by Emily A. Watson ("Watson") on or about December 3, 2023, and was assigned to the same judge.

6. From approximately March 26 through May 5, 2024 — a period of 40 days — Watson withheld L.D.W. from me entirely. This was the first period of complete parent-child separation.

7. On May 5, 2024, I obtained an ex parte temporary custody order establishing 50/50 equal parenting time with L.D.W.

8. From May 5, 2024 through July 17, 2025 — a period of approximately 438 consecutive days — I exercised equal 50/50 parenting time with L.D.W. pursuant to this court order. During this entire period, I complied with all court orders. The arrangement operated successfully and without incident for over fourteen months, demonstrating that shared custody was both workable and in L.D.W.'s best interest.

9. No court has ever found me to be an unfit parent. No evaluation has ever concluded that contact between me and L.D.W. would be harmful.

---

## III. THE EX PARTE DESTRUCTION OF CUSTODY (AUGUST 2025)

10. On July 17, 2025, Watson's objection to the temporary custody order was granted. The ex parte order was overturned. Watson was awarded 100% custody — without an evidentiary hearing and without any findings on the 12 best interest factors required by MCL 722.23(a)–(l).

11. On August 8, 2025, Watson filed an ex parte motion to suspend all of my parenting time. Judge McNeill granted the motion the same day it was filed. The order was entered without notice to me, without any opportunity for me to be heard, without a hearing, and without the specific findings of harm required by MCL 722.27a(3) before modifying parenting time.

12. Since August 8, 2025, I have had absolutely zero contact with my son L.D.W. — no visits, no telephone calls, no video calls, no communication of any kind. As of the date of this affidavit, this complete separation has lasted more than **223 consecutive days** and is ongoing.

13. No evidentiary hearing has been held in the 223+ days since the August 8, 2025 ex parte order — in violation of MCR 3.207(C), which requires a hearing within 21 days of an ex parte order.

14. Combined with the initial 40-day withholding, I have been denied all contact with my child for a total of approximately **269 days** across two separate periods — despite 438 days of demonstrated successful shared custody between those periods.
