### MSC Original Jurisdiction Research

#### When Does MSC Grant Original Jurisdiction?

The MSC exercises original jurisdiction under Const 1963, art 6, §4 and MCR 7.306 **only in extraordinary circumstances**:

1. **Clear legal duty exists** — The lower court must have a non-discretionary obligation ("shall" language, not "may")
2. **Duty was not performed** — Must be a refusal or failure, not merely an error of judgment
3. **No adequate alternative remedy** — Normal appeal must be insufficient
4. **Direct benefit** from the writ — The relief must actually correct the problem

The standard is strict: *In re Recorder's Court Bar Ass'n*, 443 Mich 110, 134 (1993) holds that superintending control will not lie for errors within a court's lawful discretion — only for failures on clear, non-discretionary duties.

### Key Recommendation for F10

The biggest vulnerability is the **custodial interference count** (MCL 750.350a). Since the custody orders were issued by a court (however improperly), Watson will argue she was complying with court orders, not "concealing" the child.
