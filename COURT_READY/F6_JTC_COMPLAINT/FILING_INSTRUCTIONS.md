## 3. FORMAT REQUIREMENTS

### What to Submit

The filing package must include:

| Document | Format | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| **Request for Investigation (RFI) Form** | Official JTC form (PDF), printed and completed | Download from JTC website; must be notarized |
| **Verified Complaint (Narrative Statement)** | Typed, attached as additional pages to Section III of the RFI | Professional formatting; numbered paragraphs |
| **Affidavit of Misconduct** | Typed, signed, notarized | Separate from the RFI — supporting sworn statement |
| **Exhibit Index** | Typed, attached to package | Identifies each supporting document |
| **Exhibits (Copies Only)** | Paper copies, tabbed and organized | NEVER send originals — the JTC will not return materials |

### Submission Method

| Method | Accepted? | Notes |
|--------|:---------:|-------|
| **U.S. Mail** | ✅ | **Recommended** — send via Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested |
| **Personal Delivery** | ✅ | Hand-deliver to JTC office in Detroit |
| **Email** | ❌ | **NOT ACCEPTED** |
| **Fax** | ❌ | **NOT ACCEPTED** |
| **Electronic Filing** | ❌ | **NOT ACCEPTED** — original signatures required |

### Signature and Notarization

- The RFI form **must** be signed in the presence of a notary public
- The Affidavit **must** be signed in the presence of a notary public with a separate jurat
- **Do NOT sign** any document until you are in the notary's presence
- Both the RFI and Affidavit require separate notarizations
- Most banks, UPS Stores, and law offices provide notary services (fees typically $5–$15 per notarization)

## 5. CONFIDENTIALITY RULES (MCR 9.220)

- All complaints and investigations are **confidential** until:
  - The JTC issues a formal complaint (MCR 9.205), **OR**
  - The Commission makes a public recommendation to the Michigan Supreme Court

- **What this means for you:**
  - You may tell people you filed a complaint
  - You should not share the JTC's investigation details, correspondence, or internal communications
  - The JTC will not confirm or deny the existence of a complaint to third parties
  - You will be notified in writing of the disposition

- **Immunity:** MCR 9.221 grants **absolute immunity** from civil suit to complainants and witnesses. You cannot be sued for filing a JTC complaint in good faith.

## 6. EXPECTED TIMELINE

| Phase | Estimated Timeline | What Happens |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Receipt** | Within 1–2 weeks | JTC acknowledges receipt in writing |
| **Staff Review** | 2–6 weeks | JTC staff screens for completeness, jurisdiction, and facial merit |
| **Preliminary Investigation** | Weeks to months | Staff investigates: collects documents, reviews court records, contacts witnesses (MCR 9.204) |
| **Commission Consideration** | Quarterly meetings | Staff presents findings to the full 9-member Commission; Commission votes on further action |
| **Possible Outcomes at This Stage:** | | |
| — Dismissal | | JTC finds insufficient cause; notifies complainant in writing |
| — Private Admonishment | | JTC issues private warning to judge; complainant notified of disposition (not details) |
| — Formal Complaint | | JTC finds reasonable cause; issues formal complaint under MCR 9.205 |
| **Formal Proceedings** | Months | Respondent judge must answer; discovery; evidentiary hearing before appointed master (MCR 9.206) |
| **Master's Report** | After hearing | Master issues findings of fact and conclusions of law (MCR 9.207) |
| **Commission Recommendation** | After report | Commission recommends discipline to Michigan Supreme Court (MCR 9.208) |
| **Supreme Court Decision** | Final | Michigan Supreme Court issues final order — this is the ultimate authority (MCR 9.210) |

**Total estimated timeline:** 6 months to 2+ years for complex cases. There is no statutory deadline. The JTC states it acts "as expeditiously as possible."

## 7. FOLLOW-UP PROCEDURES

### If the JTC Requests Additional Information
- Respond promptly and completely
- Provide any additional documents in copy form only
- Maintain professional tone in all correspondence

### If the JTC Dismisses the Complaint
- You will receive written notification
- You may file a new complaint if new misconduct occurs
- The original complaint remains on file and may inform future investigations
- Dismissal at this stage does NOT mean the JTC found the judge innocent — it means they did not find sufficient cause to proceed to formal proceedings at this time

### If the JTC Issues a Formal Complaint
- You may be contacted as a witness
- Preserve all evidence — it may be needed for the hearing
- You have absolute immunity as a complainant (MCR 9.221)

### Supplemental Filings
- If additional misconduct occurs after you file, you may submit supplemental complaints
- Reference the original filing and provide new specific incidents
- Each supplemental filing should be notarized

---

## 8. WHAT THE JTC CANNOT DO

The JTC does not:
- **Reverse or modify court orders** — address conduct only, not legal error
- **Provide legal advice** to complainants
- **Serve as an appellate remedy** — pursue appeals separately through the Court of Appeals
- **Provide updates during investigation** — the process is confidential until formal action
- **Expedite based on complainant request** — the Commission sets its own schedule

## 9. PARALLEL REMEDIES (PURSUE INDEPENDENTLY)

Filing a JTC complaint does not replace other legal remedies. These should be pursued independently and simultaneously:

| Remedy | Venue | Purpose |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Motion for Disqualification** | 14th Circuit Court | Remove McNeill from the case (MCR 2.003) |
| **Appeal** | Michigan Court of Appeals | Challenge legal errors in orders |
| **Application for Leave to Appeal** | Michigan Court of Appeals (COA 366810 if applicable) | Interlocutory review of ex parte orders |
| **Emergency Motion** | Court of Appeals | Seek immediate relief pending appeal |
| **Complaint to State Court Administrative Office (SCAO)** | SCAO | Address court administration issues |
| **Superintending Control** | Court of Appeals or Supreme Court | Extraordinary remedy for abuse of judicial authority |

## 10. IMPORTANT REMINDERS

1. **Keep copies of everything.** The JTC does not return materials and will not make copies for you.
2. **Never send originals.** Copies only for all exhibits and supporting documents.
3. **Do not contact the judge** about the complaint. This could be characterized as improper contact.
4. **Be truthful.** Filing a knowingly false complaint is a criminal offense. Every allegation must be supportable by evidence.
5. **Be patient.** The process is slow by design. Continue pursuing other legal remedies in parallel.
6. **Document ongoing conduct.** If McNeill engages in further misconduct, document it for supplemental filings.
7. **Absolute immunity protects you.** MCR 9.221 shields complainants from civil liability for filing JTC complaints in good faith.
