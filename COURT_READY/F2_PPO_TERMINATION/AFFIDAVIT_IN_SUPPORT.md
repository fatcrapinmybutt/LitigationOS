## CIRCUMSTANCES OF ORIGINAL PPO

4. On or about December 3, 2023, Petitioner Emily A. Watson filed a petition for a Personal Protection Order against me. The PPO was issued the same day, ex parte, without notice to me and without any evidentiary hearing.

5. The PPO petition contained the following allegations, each of which I state under oath to be false:

   a. **ARSENIC POISONING:** Petitioner alleged I poisoned her with arsenic. In fact, Petitioner injected herself with a substance from the household refrigerator that she stated was intended to help her lose weight. She developed a skin reaction and blamed me. No medical evidence has ever confirmed arsenic poisoning. Hospital records affirmatively disprove the allegation.

   b. **PHYSICAL VIOLENCE:** Petitioner alleged physical violence. The police report from the alleged altercation confirms no injuries were documented and no charges were filed against me.

   c. **ELECTRONIC SURVEILLANCE:** Petitioner alleged I surveilled her through an electronic device. The alleged "surveillance device" was a baby camera — a standard child-monitoring device used for the care of our minor child, L.D.W. I did not surveil Petitioner.

   d. **DRUG USE ATTRIBUTION:** Petitioner has represented that law enforcement attributed methamphetamine use to me. Police Report NS2505044 (Officer Ella Randall, Badge #47437) documents that the statement regarding "meth use" originated from Petitioner herself, not from any law enforcement finding attributed to me.

6. The genesis of the PPO traces to the so-called "straw incident" in October 2023, during which Petitioner alleged that I threw a drinking straw at her. This trivial allegation was inflated through subsequent filings into a predicate for the PPO petition. There were no injuries, no medical treatment, and no criminal charges resulting from this incident.

I sent those messages out of love for my child, not to threaten, harass, or intimidate Petitioner.
