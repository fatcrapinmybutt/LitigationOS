## II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

### A. Sewage Crisis Requiring Immediate Repair

4. Beginning in approximately late 2024, raw sewage began overflowing from an interceptor tank onto the ground surface near Lot 17 and adjacent lots within Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community.

5. Plaintiff sent formal written complaints to Defendants on **March 7, 2025** and **March 17, 2025**, demanding remediation. Defendants did not respond to either communication.

6. EGLE inspected the property on **March 20, 2025** and confirmed raw sewage discharging onto the ground surface, pooled sewage, staining, odor, and an unsecured septic/holding tank lid. EGLE rated the facility **"NOT IN COMPLIANCE"** for both Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance.

7. EGLE issued **Violation Notice VN-017235** on **April 15, 2025**, citing violations of Part 31 NREPA and the Mobile Home Commission Act, including illegal sewage discharge and failure to report.

8. Defendants performed only minimal remediation (jetting and TV inspection — no excavation or pipe replacement). Sewage resumed leaking in late June 2025 during heavy rains, killing vegetation near occupied homes.

9. As of the date of this Motion, **Defendants have not performed adequate remediation** to resolve the sewage discharge. The underlying infrastructure failure remains unrepaired.

### B. Water Shutoff and Utility Fraud

10. On **May 20, 2025**, Defendants shut off water to Plaintiff's home while Plaintiff's minor child L.D.W. was present — a dangerous act that constitutes constructive eviction and violates MCL 554.139.

11. Defendants imposed unauthorized submeter charges with fabricated readings (water consumed = sewer discharged every month — a physical impossibility).

### C. Property Destruction Already Occurred

12. On **July 17, 2025**, Defendants forcibly entered Plaintiff's titled mobile home, drilled locks, removed all personal property, and posted a "FREE" sign inviting public scavenging — all without any court order or lawful writ.

13. Plaintiff is the titled owner of the 1970 Marlette mobile home (VIN 1646732). Defendants have no ownership interest in the mobile home. The lot lease grants occupancy of land only.

### D. Custody Impact

14. The habitability conditions created by Defendants have been used against Plaintiff in custody proceedings (Case No. 2024-001507-DC, Pigors v. Watson). The opposing party argued that Plaintiff's home was unfit for his minor child. The destruction of Plaintiff's home on July 17, 2025 rendered Plaintiff homeless and contributed to separation from his child since August 8, 2025.

15. **Immediate court-ordered repairs are essential** not only for public health, but to preserve Plaintiff's ability to maintain a suitable home for custodial visits with his minor child.

## III. LEGAL STANDARD

16. Under **MCR 3.310(B)**, a court may issue a temporary restraining order if:

> It clearly appears from specific facts shown by affidavit or verified complaint that **immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage** will result from the delay required to effect notice, or that notice itself will precipitate adverse action before a restraining order can be issued.

17. The four factors governing issuance of a TRO and preliminary injunction under Michigan law, **Thermatool Corp v Borzym**, 227 Mich App 366 (1998):

> (1) Irreparable harm to the movant absent injunctive relief;
> (2) Likelihood of success on the merits;
> (3) The balance of hardships favors the movant; and
> (4) The public interest would not be adversely affected.

---

## IV. APPLICATION OF THE FOUR FACTORS

### A. Factor One: Irreparable Harm

18. **Plaintiff and other residents face ongoing irreparable harm from sewage contamination.** Raw sewage near occupied homes — including homes with children — creates biological hazards (E. coli, Salmonella, hepatitis), physical dangers (unsecured tank lid), and environmental contamination that cannot be remedied by monetary damages alone.

19. **Plaintiff has already lost his home.** On July 17, 2025, Defendants destroyed Plaintiff's titled mobile home and all personal property. Without injunctive relief, Defendants will continue to deny accountability and block any recovery.

20. **Plaintiff's custody relationship is being destroyed.** The habitability conditions and homelessness caused by Defendants are being used to deny Plaintiff parenting time with his child. Each day without repairs deepens the irreparable harm to the parent-child relationship.

21. Money damages, while available, **cannot undo** the homelessness, displacement of a minor child, health exposure, or destruction of irreplaceable personal property.

### B. Factor Two: Likelihood of Success on the Merits

22. **Plaintiff is substantially likely to succeed on the merits** for multiple independent reasons:

   a. **MCL 554.139 is non-waivable.** The implied warranty of habitability cannot be waived. *Trentadue v Gorton*, 479 Mich 378 (2007). EGLE's inspection confirmed sewage discharge and "NOT IN COMPLIANCE" ratings;

   b. **EGLE's findings are dispositive.** Violation Notice VN-017235 is official government documentation of Defendants' violations. This is not a disputed "he-said, she-said" — a state agency inspected and found violations;

   c. **Self-help eviction violates MCL 600.2918**, which imposes treble damages for forcible entry and detainer;

   d. **The operating entity is dissolved.** Homes of America, LLC lacks legal capacity under MCL 450.4802. All three eviction judgments are void ab initio;

   e. **The ledger fraud is documented.** Three irreconcilable ledger versions from Defendants' own software constitute powerful evidence of manipulation;

   f. **Retaliatory motive is presumed.** MCL 600.5720(2) creates a rebuttable presumption of retaliation for actions within 90 days of a tenant's complaint to governmental authorities.

### C. Factor Three: Balance of Equities

23. **The balance overwhelmingly favors Plaintiff:**

   a. Plaintiff is a pro se individual with a minor child who has lost his home and all possessions. Plaintiff stands to suffer continued homelessness and loss of his parental relationship;

   b. Defendants are a New Jersey LLC that purchased the park for $1,400,000. They are a substantial commercial entity. An order requiring them to repair sewage infrastructure they are legally obligated to maintain imposes no undue burden;

   c. An order preserving Plaintiff's titled property costs Defendants nothing — they have no ownership interest in the mobile home;

   d. If injunctive relief is denied and Defendants continue to ignore the sewage conditions, the harm is ongoing and irreversible.

### D. Factor Four: Public Interest

24. **The public interest strongly favors injunctive relief:**

   a. Michigan's manufactured housing regulatory scheme exists specifically to protect residents from the conduct Defendants have engaged in;

   b. EGLE has already cited Defendants for violations — court enforcement complements the regulatory process;

   c. Other residents of Shady Oaks are also affected by the sewage conditions and would benefit from court-ordered repairs;

   d. The public has a strong interest in ensuring that landlords cannot avoid habitability obligations through entity manipulation and self-help eviction;

   e. The welfare of Plaintiff's minor child L.D.W. requires safe, habitable housing.

## V. SPECIFIC RELIEF REQUESTED

25. Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court enter a Temporary Restraining Order and Preliminary Injunction ordering as follows:

### A. Immediate Repairs

**(a)** Defendants shall, within **fourteen (14) days** of entry of this Order, retain a licensed contractor to perform a complete assessment and remediation of the sewage discharge at Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community, including but not limited to: excavation and inspection of all sewer lines serving Lot 17 and adjacent lots; replacement of any damaged, deteriorated, or failing sewer infrastructure; remediation of all ground contaminated by sewage discharge; and securing of all septic/holding tank lids;

**(b)** Defendants shall provide Plaintiff and this Court with a written remediation plan within **seven (7) days** of entry of this Order, identifying the licensed contractor, scope of work, and projected completion date;

**(c)** Defendants shall permit EGLE or Muskegon County Health Department to conduct a follow-up inspection upon completion of repairs to verify compliance;

### B. Utility Protection

**(d)** Defendants are **prohibited** from shutting off water, sewer, electric, or any other utility service to any occupied dwelling at Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community as a means of constructive eviction or retaliation;

### C. Property Preservation

**(e)** Defendants, and all persons acting in concert with them, are **prohibited** from seizing, moving, towing, damaging, demolishing, disposing of, encumbering, entering, or interfering with Plaintiff's mobile home (1970 Marlette, VIN 1646732) or any personal property of Plaintiff;

**(f)** Defendants are **prohibited** from posting signs, advertising, or otherwise inviting third parties to take Plaintiff's property;

**(g)** Defendants are **prohibited** from entering Plaintiff's mobile home or the curtilage thereof without Plaintiff's express written consent or a valid court order;

### D. Financial Accounting

**(h)** Defendants shall provide Plaintiff with a **complete and accurate accounting** of all payments received from or on behalf of Plaintiff, all charges assessed to Plaintiff's account, and the current balance — supported by original source documents — within **fourteen (14) days** of entry of this Order;

**(i)** Defendants are **prohibited** from assessing any new charges to Plaintiff's account until such accounting is completed and provided;

### E. Bond

**(j)** Plaintiff requests that the Court waive or set a nominal bond under MCR 3.310(D), given that Plaintiff is a pro se litigant of limited means, the relief requested preserves the status quo, and Defendants suffer no cognizable harm from being required to comply with existing legal obligations.
