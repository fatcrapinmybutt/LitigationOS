## COUNT I — BREACH OF IMPLIED WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY
### MCL 554.139

44. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates by reference paragraphs 1 through 43 as if fully set forth herein.

45. Under MCL 554.139(1), in every lease or license of residential premises, the lessor covenants that:

   (a) The premises and all common areas are fit for the use intended by the parties; and

   (b) The premises are kept in reasonable repair during the term of the lease, and the premises comply with applicable health and safety laws of the state and local governmental units.

46. MCL 554.139(3) provides that this covenant **cannot be waived** by any provision of the lease or otherwise. *Trentadue v Gorton*, 479 Mich 378 (2007).

47. Defendants breached the implied warranty of habitability in the following respects:

   a. **Raw sewage discharge**: Beginning in approximately late 2024, raw sewage overflowed from an interceptor tank onto the ground surface near Plaintiff's home and adjacent lots, confirmed by EGLE inspection on March 20, 2025, and documented in EGLE Violation Notice VN-017235 (April 15, 2025);

   b. **Environmental non-compliance**: EGLE rated the facility "NOT IN COMPLIANCE" for both Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance, citing violations of Part 31 NREPA (MCL 324.3101 et seq.) and the Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.);

   c. **Failure to remediate**: Despite written notice from Plaintiff (March 7 and 17, 2025), state inspection (March 20, 2025), and formal violation notice (April 15, 2025), Defendants performed only minimal remediation (jetting and TV inspection, no excavation or pipe replacement). Sewage resumed leaking in late June 2025;

   d. **Unauthorized and defective utility service**: Installation of water submeters without notice or consent in violation of MCL 125.2328(1)(b), with demonstrably fabricated meter readings;

   e. **Water shutoff with child present**: On May 20, 2025, Defendants shut off water to Plaintiff's home while Plaintiff's minor child L.D.W. was present, endangering the child's health and welfare;

   f. **Unsafe conditions**: Unsecured septic/holding tank lid, pooling sewage near occupied homes including homes with children, and conditions creating a public health hazard.

48. Defendants had actual notice of the habitability defects through Plaintiff's written complaints (March 7 and 17, 2025) and through EGLE's communications, and had more than a reasonable time to repair — exceeding four months from first notice to the forced removal on July 17, 2025.

49. As a direct and proximate result of Defendants' breach, Plaintiff has suffered damages including but not limited to: diminished use and enjoyment of the premises; health risks from raw sewage exposure near his home where his minor child L.D.W. resided; costs of temporary remediation measures; constructive eviction from conditions rendering the premises uninhabitable; and harm to Plaintiff's custody case resulting from conditions Defendants created.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendants, jointly and severally, for full rent abatement for the period of uninhabitable conditions, repair and remediation costs, consequential damages including custody-related harm, and such other relief as this Court deems just.

## COUNT II — VIOLATIONS OF MOBILE HOME COMMISSION ACT
### MCL 125.2301 et seq.

50. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates by reference paragraphs 1 through 43 as if fully set forth herein.

51. The Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.) provides heightened protections for residents of mobile home communities that are **more protective** than general landlord-tenant law.

52. Defendants violated the Mobile Home Commission Act in the following respects:

   a. **Failure to maintain premises (MCL 125.2307)**: Defendants failed to maintain common areas and utility infrastructure in a safe and sanitary condition, as evidenced by the raw sewage discharge, unsecured septic tank lid, and failing sewer system documented by EGLE;

   b. **Improper rent increases (MCL 125.2328(1)(c))**: Defendants increased Plaintiff's lot rent by 121% ($325 → $720) without providing the required 30-day written notice for each increase. No statutory notice was produced for any of the increases;

   c. **Unauthorized submeter installation (MCL 125.2328(1)(b))**: Defendants installed water submeters and imposed charges without notice or consent as required by the Act;

   d. **Eviction without good cause (MCL 125.2313)**: The Mobile Home Commission Act requires "good cause" for eviction, with specific enumerated grounds. Defendants' eviction was based on fabricated arrears from a manipulated ledger, which does not constitute good cause;

   e. **Failure to comply with EGLE regulations**: EGLE Violation Notice VN-017235 (April 15, 2025) cited violations of both Part 31 NREPA and the Mobile Home Commission Act, including illegal sewage discharge and failure to report;

   f. **Interference with home sale (MCL 125.2330b)**: Under the Act, a mobile home owner has the right to sell their home in place. Defendants blocked at least three sales through false statements, denying Plaintiff's statutory right to dispose of his personal property;

   g. **Operating through dissolved entity**: Defendants operated the park through Homes of America, LLC — a dissolved entity — in violation of MCL 450.4802, while using the regulatory license (License #1201891) obtained by a different entity.

53. MCL 125.2345 provides that violations of the Mobile Home Commission Act give rise to civil liability, and the Act does not preempt common law remedies.

54. As a direct and proximate result of Defendants' violations, Plaintiff has suffered damages including loss of his home, loss of sale proceeds, excess rent payments, exposure to hazardous conditions, and loss of parenting time with his child.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendants, jointly and severally, for all damages arising from violations of the Mobile Home Commission Act, including statutory remedies, and such other relief as this Court deems just.

## COUNT III — VIOLATIONS OF TRUTH IN RENTING ACT
### MCL 554.631 et seq.

55. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates by reference paragraphs 1 through 43 as if fully set forth herein.

56. The Michigan Truth in Renting Act (MCL 554.631 through MCL 554.641) protects residential tenants from unfair lease provisions and deceptive rental practices.

57. MCL 554.633 provides that a rental agreement shall not include a clause which waives any of the tenant's rights under the Act, requires the tenant to waive legal rights or remedies, or contains provisions that are contrary to state law or against public policy. Any such provision is **void and unenforceable** under MCL 554.634.

58. Defendants violated the Truth in Renting Act in the following respects:

   a. **$750 buyout with complete rights waiver**: Defendants offered Plaintiff $750 conditioned upon execution of a complete waiver of all legal rights and claims — a provision that is unenforceable under MCL 554.633(1) because it requires waiver of rights guaranteed by Michigan law;

   c. **Coerced entity substitution**: The March 26, 2024 lease transferred Plaintiff's tenancy to a different entity (Cricklewood MHP LLC) under threat of eviction, without proper disclosure of the entity change or its implications;

   d. **Rent increases without proper notice**: Rent increases exceeding 10% without the statutorily required written notice under MCL 554.632 constitute violations of Plaintiff's rights under the Act;

   e. **Unauthorized fee provisions**: Lease provisions purporting to authorize charges for Defendants' attorney fees to be billed directly to the tenant's account before any court judgment;

   f. **Fraudulent ledger practices**: Maintaining irreconcilable ledger versions and omitting documented payments constitute deceptive practices under the Act.

59. Under MCL 554.636, a tenant may recover actual damages sustained, plus a statutory penalty of **$250 for each violation**, plus reasonable attorney fees and costs.

60. Plaintiff has identified at least six separate violations of the Truth in Renting Act, entitling Plaintiff to actual damages plus statutory penalties of at least $1,500.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendants, jointly and severally, for actual damages, statutory penalties of $250 per violation, costs and fees under MCL 554.636, and such other relief as this Court deems just.

## COUNT V — NEGLIGENCE AND PREMISES LIABILITY

68. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates by reference paragraphs 1 through 43 as if fully set forth herein.

69. As the owner and operator of Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community, Defendants owed a duty of reasonable care to maintain the premises — including all common areas, utility infrastructure, and the sewage system — in a safe condition for tenants and their families, including minor children. *See Trentadue v Gorton*, 479 Mich 378 (2007); *Allison v AEW Capital Management*, 481 Mich 419 (2008).

70. Defendants breached this duty of care in the following respects:

   a. **Failure to maintain sewage infrastructure**: Defendants failed to repair, maintain, or replace the sewage interceptor tank and associated piping, resulting in raw sewage discharging onto the ground surface near occupied homes — a condition confirmed by EGLE inspection and Violation Notice VN-017235;

   b. **Failure to secure hazardous conditions**: Defendants left the septic/holding tank lid unsecured, creating a physical danger to residents and children who could fall into the tank;

   c. **Failure to respond to notice**: Despite receiving written notice from Plaintiff (March 7 and 17, 2025), an EGLE inspection finding non-compliance (March 20, 2025), and a formal EGLE Violation Notice (April 15, 2025), Defendants failed to perform adequate remediation for months;

   d. **Inadequate remediation**: Defendants' response — limited to jetting and TV inspection without excavation or pipe replacement — was grossly inadequate to address the known sewage discharge, as demonstrated by the recurrence of sewage leaking in late June 2025;

   e. **Water shutoff endangering child**: Shutting off water to Plaintiff's home on May 20, 2025, while Plaintiff's minor child L.D.W. was present, creating a dangerous living condition;

   f. **Failure to warn**: Defendants failed to notify residents of the known sewage contamination, the EGLE inspection findings, or the health hazards posed by the conditions.

71. Defendants knew or should have known of the dangerous conditions on the premises. EGLE inspectors noted that the sewage problems were longstanding, and neighboring residents confirmed persistent odors and plumbing backups preceding Plaintiff's formal complaints.

As a direct and proximate result of Defendants' extreme and outrageous conduct, Plaintiff has suffered severe emotional distress, including: the trauma of losing his home and all possessions; the anguish of losing parenting time with his child; anxiety and stress from being rendered homeless; the humiliation of having his property posted with a "FREE" sign; and the ongoing emotional harm of Defendants' continued harassment.

   a. **Coerced rent overpayments**: The differential between the original $325/month rent and the coerced rates up to $720/month;

   d. **Property conversion**: Taking possession of Plaintiff's titled mobile home and personal property without compensation;

   e. **Blocked sale proceeds**: Retaining the economic value of Plaintiff's property by blocking sales through false statements.

103. It would be inequitable and unjust for Defendants to retain these benefits.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendants, jointly and severally, for restitution of all overpayments, return of diverted payments, fair market value of converted property, disgorgement of all unjust enrichment, and such other relief as this Court deems just.
