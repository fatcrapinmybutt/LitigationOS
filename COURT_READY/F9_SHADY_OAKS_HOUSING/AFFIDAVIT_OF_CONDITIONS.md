## III. SEWAGE CONTAMINATION AND ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS

### A. Onset of Sewage Discharge

7. Beginning in approximately late 2024, I observed raw sewage overflowing from an interceptor tank located near Lot 17 and adjacent lots within Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community. The sewage discharged onto the ground surface, pooling near occupied homes where children reside, including my home where my minor child L.D.W. was present during custodial periods.

8. The sewage discharge created a severe and ongoing public health hazard. I observed and can attest to the following conditions: visible pooling of raw sewage on the ground surface; persistent foul sewage odor permeating the area around my home; staining on the ground from sewage accumulation; dead and discolored vegetation from sewage exposure; and an unsecured septic/holding tank lid that posed a physical danger to residents and children.

### B. Written Complaints to Defendants

9. On **March 7, 2025**, I sent a formal written complaint to Defendants at the email address shadyoaks@ourhomesofamerica.com, demanding remediation of the sewage conditions within 48 hours. I described the health hazard in detail and requested immediate action.

10. On **March 17, 2025**, having received no response to my March 7 complaint, I sent an urgent follow-up email citing MCL 554.139 (implied warranty of habitability) and stating my intention to file complaints with Muskegon County Health Department, the City of Muskegon Water & Sewer Department, the Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy ("EGLE"), and Housing Law Enforcement if the conditions were not immediately remediated. Defendants did not respond to this communication either.

### C. EGLE Inspection and Violation Notice

11. On **March 18, 2025**, EGLE received a complaint from Muskegon County Public Health regarding the sewage conditions at Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community.

12. On **March 20, 2025**, EGLE Environmental Quality Analysts Connor O'Brien and Amanda St. Amour conducted an on-site inspection of the property. The EGLE inspection confirmed the following:

   a. Raw sewage was discharging from an interceptor tank onto the ground surface near occupied homes;

   b. Residual pooled sewage, staining, and sewage odor were present;

   c. The septic/holding tank lid was unsecured;

   d. Neighboring residents confirmed persistent odors and plumbing backups;

   e. EGLE rated the facility **"NOT IN COMPLIANCE"** for both Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance.

13. On **March 26, 2025**, EGLE left a voicemail on the posted office telephone number for Shady Oaks. As of **April 4, 2025** — seventeen days after the inspection — no one from Shady Oaks had contacted EGLE in response.

14. On **April 15, 2025**, EGLE issued **Violation Notice VN-017235**, citing the following violations:

   a. Violation of Part 31 of the Natural Resources and Environmental Protection Act (MCL 324.3101 et seq.);

   b. Violation of the Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.);

   c. Illegal discharge of sewage onto the ground surface;

   d. Failure to notify EGLE or health authorities within 24 hours of a sewage release;

   e. Failure to submit required written reports within five days.

### D. Inadequate Remediation

15. Following the EGLE Violation Notice, Defendants performed only minimal remediation consisting of jetting (pressure washing) the sewer lines and conducting a TV camera inspection. No excavation or pipe replacement was performed.

16. In **late June 2025**, I again observed sewage leaking during heavy rains, which was killing vegetation near occupied homes. This demonstrated that Defendants' minimal remediation efforts were inadequate and that the underlying infrastructure failure had not been repaired.

## IV. WATER SHUTOFF AND UTILITY FRAUD

17. Beginning approximately **May 2025**, Defendants imposed unauthorized water and sewer submeter charges of approximately $87 per month on my account. The water meter was installed without my notice or consent, in violation of MCL 125.2328(1)(b).

18. Upon reviewing the Zego billing portal records maintained by Defendants, I discovered that water gallons consumed and sewer gallons discharged were reported as **identical every month**:

   - April 2025: 4,245 gallons consumed = 4,245 gallons discharged;
   - May 2025: 3,233 gallons consumed = 3,233 gallons discharged;
   - June 2025: 3,100 gallons consumed = 3,100 gallons discharged.

   This is a physical impossibility. I had installed an outdoor spigot connected to the main water line for outdoor use (a lawn sprinkler), which drained into the ground and never entered the sewer system. Water consumed must always exceed sewer discharged. The identical readings demonstrate that the metering was fabricated or fraudulently calculated.

19. On **May 20, 2025**, Defendants shut off water service to my home while my minor child **L.D.W.** was present at the residence. Cutting off water to an occupied dwelling with a child present created an immediate health and safety emergency and constituted a dangerous act.
