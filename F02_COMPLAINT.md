### NATURE OF THE ACTION

1. This is a civil action arising from a coordinated scheme by a private-equity-controlled mobile home park and its agents to unlawfully evict Plaintiff from his manufactured home, destroy his personal property, defraud him through manipulated billing ledgers, sabotage the sale of his home, and subject him to constructive eviction — all while raw sewage contaminated the park grounds in violation of state environmental law. Plaintiff brings this action under the Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. §§ 3601–3619; the Civil Rights Act, 42 U.S.C. § 1983; and supplemental state-law claims for wrongful eviction, statutory conversion, fraud, and violations of the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act.

2. Defendants operate as a single enterprise through an interconnected web of shell entities controlled by Defendant Alden Global Capital LLC, a New York–based private equity firm managing a $275 million manufactured housing portfolio. The corporate structure was designed to maximize revenue extraction from vulnerable tenants, evade regulatory accountability, and frustrate legal redress.

3. On July 17, 2024 — the very day Plaintiff was compelled to appear at a custody trial in state court — Defendants executed a lockout of Plaintiff's manufactured home, drilled the locks, removed and destroyed personal property, and posted a "FREE" sign on Plaintiff's belongings. Plaintiff, a father of a minor child (L.D.W.), was rendered homeless while simultaneously fighting for custody of his son.

### FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

#### A. Plaintiff's Ownership and Residency at Shady Oaks Park

16. On or about February 2, 2022, Plaintiff purchased a manufactured home located at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Park, 1977 Whitehall Road, Muskegon County, Michigan. Title was issued by Shady Oaks MHP LLC through Joe Matyk.

17. At the time of purchase, Plaintiff's monthly lot rent was approximately $395.00. The lot rent covered the right to place his titled manufactured home on the lot and receive basic park services including water and sewer.

18. Plaintiff resided at the home with his minor child, L.D.W. Plaintiff's home at Shady Oaks Park was the primary residence where he exercised parenting time with his son.

#### B. The Coerced Lease and Unconscionable Rent Increases

19. Beginning in or about November 2023, Defendants initiated a campaign of rent increases that, over the course of approximately 18 months, raised Plaintiff's lot rent from $395.00 to approximately $720.00 per month — an increase of approximately 82% with no justification, no corresponding improvement in park services, and no proper notice as required by the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act, MCL 554.601 et seq.

20. On or about March 26, 2024, Defendants coerced Plaintiff under threat of eviction into signing an off-site lease ("the Cricklewood lease") at different terms and substantially higher rent. The coerced lease signing occurred while Plaintiff was under extreme duress from simultaneous custody litigation. Plaintiff signed under protest.

21. The lease modifications were executed in the name of the dissolved entity Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC, which lacked legal authority to contract after its dissolution in approximately 2022. Any agreements executed by a dissolved entity beyond winding-up activities are void or voidable under MCL 450.4802.

#### C. Ledger Manipulation and Financial Fraud

22. Defendants maintained and produced at least three materially irreconcilable versions of Plaintiff's billing ledger. Each version showed different balances, charges, credits, and payment histories.

23. At a civil disposition hearing in the 60th District Court, the presiding judge stated on the record that Plaintiff's account was paid in full through December 2024. Despite this judicial finding, Defendants failed to provide a corrected ledger and continued to assert false arrears.

24. Defendants manipulated billing ledgers to create fictitious arrears, misapply payments, and impose unauthorized fees, thereby obtaining money from Plaintiff through false pretenses in violation of MCL 750.218.

25. A $1,300.26 check issued by Plaintiff payable to "Shady Oaks MHP" was endorsed and cashed by "Partridge Securities for Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC." Partridge Securities was never identified to Plaintiff as the payee or as an entity authorized to receive payments on behalf of the landlord. This constitutes fraud in the disbursement of tenant funds.

#### D. Retaliatory Eviction and the July 17, 2024 Lockout

26. On or about July 17, 2024 — the same day Plaintiff was required to appear at a custody trial in the 14th Judicial Circuit Court (Case No. 2024-001507-DC) — Defendants executed an unlawful lockout of Plaintiff's manufactured home.

27. The lockout was carried out by agents of Homes of America LLC, including Individual Cox and Individual Brown, who drilled the locks on Plaintiff's home, removed personal property and belongings, and placed a "FREE" sign on Plaintiff's possessions — inviting members of the public to take Plaintiff's property.

28. The lockout and property destruction were carried out without a valid court order authorizing removal of Plaintiff's titled manufactured home, without proper service of any eviction writ on Plaintiff personally, and without compliance with the mandatory notice and relocation requirements of the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act.

29. Plaintiff's personal property destroyed, removed, or stolen during and after the lockout included household furnishings, appliances, clothing, tools, personal documents, children's toys and belongings, and other items of personal and sentimental value. The value of destroyed personal property exceeds $25,000.

30. The timing of the lockout — on the precise date of Plaintiff's custody trial — demonstrates coordination and/or retaliatory intent. Defendants knew or should have known that Plaintiff would be unable to protect his property while compelled to attend court.

#### E. Sabotaged Home Sale and False "Abandonment" Claims

31. Following the lockout, Plaintiff attempted on at least three occasions to sell his titled manufactured home. On each occasion, Defendant Partridge and/or other agents of the Defendant entities interfered with the prospective sale by making false representations to prospective buyers, including: (a) telling buyers that Plaintiff "does not own the home"; (b) claiming the home was "abandoned"; and (c) representing that Defendants would sell the home through "their legal team."

32. These misrepresentations were knowingly false. Plaintiff held valid title to the manufactured home. Plaintiff had not abandoned the property — he was locked out by Defendants and subsequently incarcerated on contempt orders in the custody case, rendering him physically unable to access the property.

33. Defendants' interference with the sale deprived Plaintiff of estimated equity proceeds of $60,000 to $105,000.

#### F. EGLE Sewage Violations and Environmental Hazards

34. Beginning in late 2024, raw sewage began overflowing from an interceptor tank onto the ground surface near Lot 17 and adjacent lots at Shady Oaks Park, creating a severe public health hazard. Black, foul-smelling sludge pooled on vacant Lot 21 near elderly tenants' homes and drained toward Plaintiff's lot.

35. On or about March 7, 2025, Plaintiff reported the sewage contamination to the Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy ("EGLE").

36. EGLE inspected the facility on or about March 20, 2025, rated the park "NOT IN COMPLIANCE," and issued Violation Notice VN-017235 on April 15, 2025, citing violations of Part 31 of the Natural Resources and Environmental Protection Act (MCL 324.3101 et seq.) and the Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.), including: (a) illegal discharge of sewage onto the ground surface; (b) failure to notify EGLE or health authorities within 24 hours of a sewage release; and (c) failure to maintain the on-site wastewater system.

37. Despite the documented sewage contamination and EGLE violation, Defendants continued to bill Plaintiff and other tenants for water and sewer service without providing any remediation, abatement, or credit.

38. Defendants retaliated against Plaintiff for reporting the sewage violation to EGLE by escalating eviction threats and harassment.

#### G. Dissolved Entity Fraud and Corporate Shell Game

39. Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC was dissolved under New Jersey law in approximately 2022. Notwithstanding its dissolution, the entity continued to: (a) file eviction proceedings against Plaintiff in Michigan courts; (b) execute leases; (c) collect rent; and (d) endorse and cash checks — all in violation of MCL 450.4802, which limits a dissolved LLC's authority to winding up its affairs.

40. The following entities are alter egos of one another, sharing common ownership, management, employees, office space, and commingled assets: (a) Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC (dissolved ~2022); (b) Homes of America LLC; (c) Cricklewood MHP LLC; (d) VRM Capital Corp; and (e) Partridge Equity Group / Partridge Securities LLC.

41. Alden Global Capital LLC exercises pervasive control over all subsidiary entities, setting uniform policies for rent increases, maintenance standards, tenant relations, and eviction practices across its manufactured housing portfolio.

42. Another entity in Alden Global Capital's manufactured housing portfolio — North Morris Estates LLC in New Jersey — was criminally convicted for unlicensed mobile home park operation, establishing a pattern of willful regulatory noncompliance across the enterprise.

#### H. Coordination with State Court Proceedings

43. The unlawful lockout and property destruction occurred on July 17, 2024, precisely when Plaintiff was compelled to appear at a custody trial before Hon. Jenny L. McNeill in the 14th Judicial Circuit Court.

44. Subsequently, Judge McNeill sentenced Plaintiff to a total of 59 days of incarceration on contempt-of-court orders. While Plaintiff was incarcerated, Defendants continued to accrue charges, assert false abandonment, and interfere with property sales.

45. Upon information and belief, the state court housing case (Case No. 2025-002760-CZ) was assigned to Chief Judge Kenneth Hoopes of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court. Judge Hoopes was formerly a law partner of Judge McNeill at the firm of Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill, located at 435 Whitehall Road, Muskegon, Michigan.

46. Judge Hoopes dismissed Plaintiff's housing case without an evidentiary hearing and left Plaintiff's objections unaddressed on the Record of Action, depriving Plaintiff of due process.

47. The convergence of the lockout timing (same day as custody trial), Plaintiff's subsequent incarceration (preventing him from defending his housing rights), and the dismissal by a judge who was the former law partner of the incarcerating judge, establishes a pattern of coordinated deprivation of Plaintiff's housing and property rights.

#### I. Familial Status and Disparate Impact

48. Plaintiff is a custodial father of a minor child, L.D.W. (DOB November 9, 2022). Plaintiff's manufactured home at Shady Oaks Park was the primary residence where he exercised parenting time with his son.

49. The destruction of Plaintiff's home directly and foreseeably resulted in the loss of Plaintiff's ability to maintain a stable residence for his child, which contributed to the state court's decision to suspend and ultimately terminate Plaintiff's parenting time.

50. Upon information and belief, Defendants' revenue-maximization and displacement practices disproportionately impact families with children, who are less able to relocate on short notice and who suffer greater harm from housing instability. Familial status is a protected class under the Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. § 3602(k).

### COUNT I — FAIR HOUSING ACT RETALIATION
### 42 U.S.C. § 3617

51. Plaintiff incorporates by reference all preceding paragraphs.

52. Plaintiff engaged in activity protected under the Fair Housing Act by: (a) exercising his right to occupy his titled manufactured home; (b) reporting sewage violations and habitability defects to EGLE; and (c) asserting his rights as a tenant under the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act.

53. Defendants retaliated against Plaintiff for exercising these protected rights by: (a) imposing unconscionable rent increases; (b) coercing Plaintiff into signing a new lease under threat of eviction; (c) executing an unlawful lockout and destroying Plaintiff's personal property; (d) escalating harassment and eviction threats after Plaintiff reported the sewage violation to EGLE; and (e) sabotaging the sale of Plaintiff's home through false statements to prospective buyers.

54. Section 3617 of the Fair Housing Act provides: "It shall be unlawful to coerce, intimidate, threaten, or interfere with any person in the exercise or enjoyment of, or on account of his having exercised or enjoyed, or on account of his having aided or encouraged any other person in the exercise or enjoyment of, any right granted or protected by section 3603, 3604, 3605, or 3606 of this title."

55. Defendants' retaliatory conduct violates 42 U.S.C. § 3617. Plaintiff is entitled to actual damages, punitive damages, injunctive relief, and reasonable attorney's fees and costs under 42 U.S.C. § 3613(c).
