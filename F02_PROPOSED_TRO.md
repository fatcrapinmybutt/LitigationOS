Upon consideration of Plaintiff's Complaint, Affidavit of Andrew James Pigors, and supporting documents, and the Court being sufficiently advised in the premises, the Court finds:

### FINDINGS

1. Plaintiff Andrew James Pigors is the titled owner of a manufactured home located at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Park, 1977 Whitehall Road, Muskegon County, Michigan.

2. Plaintiff has presented credible evidence that Defendants have: (a) unlawfully locked Plaintiff out of his home; (b) destroyed, removed, and/or allowed third parties to take Plaintiff's personal property; (c) interfered with Plaintiff's attempts to sell his titled property by making false statements to prospective buyers; (d) shut off water service to Plaintiff's unit; and (e) continued to accrue charges on Plaintiff's account under fraudulent ledgers.

3. Plaintiff has demonstrated a likelihood of success on the merits of his Fair Housing Act claims (42 U.S.C. §§ 3604, 3617), wrongful eviction claim (MCL 600.2918), and conversion claim (MCL 600.2919a).

4. Plaintiff faces irreparable harm absent injunctive relief: his titled property is at ongoing risk of further destruction, deterioration, unauthorized disposal, or sale by Defendants; and each day of continued interference compounds the harm to Plaintiff's property rights and equity.

5. The balance of equities tips in favor of Plaintiff: Defendants have no legitimate interest in destroying, selling, or interfering with property to which Plaintiff holds valid title, whereas Plaintiff's property will be irretrievably lost absent this Court's intervention.

6. The public interest favors entry of this Order: the Fair Housing Act and Michigan statutory law protect the rights of manufactured homeowners against self-help eviction and property destruction.

---

### IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:

**A. Property Preservation:**

7. Defendants Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC, Homes of America LLC, Alden Global Capital LLC, Travis Partridge, Individual Cox, Individual Brown, and all persons acting in concert with them, are **IMMEDIATELY RESTRAINED AND ENJOINED** from:

 a. Entering, altering, damaging, destroying, removing, or disposing of Plaintiff's manufactured home located at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Park, 1977 Whitehall Road, Muskegon County, Michigan, or any personal property contained therein or on the lot;

 b. Changing, drilling, or altering locks on Plaintiff's manufactured home;

 c. Shutting off, disconnecting, or interfering with water, sewer, electrical, or any other utility service to Plaintiff's manufactured home;

 d. Posting "FREE," "ABANDONED," "FOR SALE," or any similar signage on or near Plaintiff's property without Plaintiff's express written consent;

 e. Making any statements or representations to third parties, including but not limited to prospective buyers, real estate agents, park residents, or government officials, that Plaintiff has "abandoned" his home, that Plaintiff "does not own" the home, or that Defendants have authority to sell or dispose of Plaintiff's home.

**B. Access:**

8. Defendants shall provide Plaintiff reasonable access to his manufactured home at Lot 17 for purposes of inspecting the condition of the property, inventorying remaining personal property, and facilitating a sale of the home to a willing buyer.

**C. Accounting:**

9. Within fourteen (14) days of service of this Order, Defendants shall provide Plaintiff with a complete and accurate accounting of all charges, payments, credits, and adjustments to Plaintiff's account from the date of initial occupancy through the present, including identification of which entity received each payment.

**D. Document Preservation:**

10. Defendants shall preserve all documents, records, communications, ledgers, billing records, corporate records, lease agreements, eviction filings, and correspondence relating to Plaintiff's tenancy at Lot 17, Plaintiff's manufactured home, or the operation of Shady Oaks Park. No documents shall be destroyed, deleted, altered, or concealed.
