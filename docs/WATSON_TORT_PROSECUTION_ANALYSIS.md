## 6. WEAKNESSES AND COUNTERARGUMENTS — HONEST ASSESSMENT

### What Emily's Attorney (Jennifer Barnes) Will Argue:

**1. "Litigation Privilege / Judicial Proceedings Privilege"**
Barnes will argue that everything Emily said in court, in motions, and to CPS is absolutely privileged under Michigan's judicial proceedings privilege.

*Your Response:* Privilege doesn't cover knowingly false statements (*Couch v. Schultz*, 193 Mich App 292). Privilege doesn't cover abuse of process — the *misuse of proceedings themselves* is the tort. And statements to neighbors, employers, and community are NOT privileged.

**Honest Risk Level: MEDIUM.** A trial court could dismiss the defamation/slander counts on privilege grounds for in-court statements. But abuse of process and fraud on the court survive privilege.
