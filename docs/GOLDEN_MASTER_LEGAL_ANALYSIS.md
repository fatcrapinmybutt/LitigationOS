### Analysis: PPO Was Weaponized
The statutory firewall between PPO and custody proceedings was deliberately breached:

1. **MCL 600.2950(4)** expressly states a PPO "shall not in any manner affect the provisions of a custody or parenting time order." The trial court treated the PPO as dispositive on custody.
2. The PPO was issued on **unverified claims** without the evidentiary standard of MCL 600.2950(1) (assault, stalking, specific threatening behavior).
3. The **timing** is probative: PPO filed before custody complaint, creating a presumption against Father before he could even present his case.
4. The same judge (McNeill) presided over both PPO and custody — creating a bias feedback loop.

### Strength: ★★★★★
This is the **linchpin** of the entire case. If the PPO was improperly entered, then every downstream custody restriction based on it is void. *Shade v Wright*, 291 Mich App 17 (2010); *Brown v Loveman*, 260 Mich App 576 (2004).
