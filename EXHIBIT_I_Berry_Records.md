# EXHIBIT I: Cavan Berry — McNeill Marriage and FOC Overlap Records

**Bates Range:** PIGORS-A-I
**Filing:** Petition for Superintending Control, MCR 7.306
**Court:** Michigan Supreme Court
**Case:** Pigors v. Watson, No. 2024-001507-DC (14th Circuit Court)

---

## Description

This exhibit presents public records documenting the marriage between Hon. Jenny L. McNeill and Cavan Berry, and the physical overlap between Berry's office and the Friend of the Court (FOC). Cavan Berry serves as an attorney magistrate at the 60th District Court. His office is located at 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, Michigan — the same address as the FOC office where Pamela Rusco, the FOC case manager assigned to the custody case, works. Meanwhile, Defendant Emily A. Watson's boyfriend, Ronald Berry, shares the Berry surname with Judge McNeill's husband — a connection that, combined with the other structural conflicts documented herein, deepens the appearance of impropriety.

## Legal Significance

This exhibit compounds the structural conflict documented in Exhibit H (law partnership) by adding an additional layer: the presiding judge's spouse works in the same building as the FOC officer making recommendations in the judge's custody cases.

1. **McNeill–Berry Marriage.** Hon. Jenny L. McNeill is married to Cavan Berry. Public records (PeopleFinders) confirm they share a residence at 4084 Oak Hollow Court, North Muskegon, Michigan.

2. **990 Terrace Street Overlap.** Cavan Berry's office (60th District Court, attorney magistrate) and the FOC office of Pamela Rusco are both located at 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, Michigan. The judge's spouse and the FOC officer assigned to the custody case work in the same building.

3. **Ronald Berry Connection.** Defendant Emily A. Watson's boyfriend, Ronald Berry, resides with her at 2160 Garland Drive, Norton Shores, Michigan. Ronald Berry shares the Berry surname with Judge McNeill's husband, Cavan Berry. The exact familial relationship between Ronald Berry and Cavan Berry has not been confirmed through public records, but the shared surname combined with the other documented connections warrants disclosure and inquiry.

4. **Ex Parte Communication Indicators.** Records show a pattern consistent with ex parte communication: (a) Ronald Berry's voicemail was submitted to the court and pre-listened in chambers; (b) Emily A. Watson's filings were ruled upon the same day they were filed; (c) Berry appears to have acted within 48 hours of Watson's filings on multiple occasions.

5. **FOC–HealthWest Contact.** Pamela Rusco (FOC, 990 Terrace Street) contacted the HealthWest clinician who evaluated Father on October 29, 2025 — one day before a hearing. This contact was not disclosed to Father.

## Key Evidence

- **Marriage:** Hon. Jenny L. McNeill and Cavan Berry are married. Shared residence: 4084 Oak Hollow Court, North Muskegon, Michigan. (Source: Public records, PeopleFinders.)
- **Cavan Berry's Role:** Attorney magistrate, 60th District Court, 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, Michigan.
- **Pamela Rusco's Office:** Friend of the Court, 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, Michigan — the same address as Cavan Berry's office.
- **Ronald Berry:** Resides with Emily A. Watson at 2160 Garland Drive, Norton Shores, Michigan. Shares surname with Judge McNeill's husband.
- **Educational Connection:** McNeill attended NIU College of Law (1996); Cavan Berry attended NIU College of Law (1998) — same institution, two years apart.
- **McNeill's Non-Disclosure:** Judge McNeill never disclosed to the parties that her husband works in the same building as the FOC officer assigned to the custody case.
- **Berry Voicemail Pre-Listened in Chambers:** Ronald Berry's voicemail was submitted to the court and pre-listened by the judge in chambers — an ex parte communication.
- **FOC Ex Parte Contact:** Pamela Rusco contacted the HealthWest evaluator on October 29, 2025, the day before a hearing, without Father's knowledge or consent.

## Constitutional Implications

The overlapping personal and professional relationships documented in this exhibit — marriage, shared office building, shared surname, shared educational institution — create a web of structural bias that no single disclosure can cure. Under *Caperton v. A.T. Massey Coal Co.*, 556 U.S. 868 (2009), due process is violated when the "probability of actual bias" is "too high to be constitutionally tolerable."

When the presiding judge's spouse works in the same building as the FOC officer recommending against Father, when the opposing party's boyfriend shares the judge's married surname, and when the FOC officer contacts Father's evaluator ex parte the day before a hearing — the cumulative effect is a judicial process in which the outcome appears predetermined. This Court's intervention is necessary to restore the appearance and reality of impartial justice.
