## GOVERNING AUTHORITY & GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

### Michigan Court Rules
- **MCR 2.305** — Subpoena; Order to Produce
- **MCR 2.305(A)(1)** — A subpoena may command a person to attend and give testimony at a trial, hearing, or deposition, and/or to produce documents, electronically stored information, or tangible things.
- **MCR 2.305(A)(3)** — A subpoena for production of documents without testimony (subpoena duces tecum for records only) must allow at least **14 days** for compliance.

### Service Requirements (ALL Subpoenas)
| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Who serves** | Any person who is not a party and is at least 18 years old (MCR 2.103(A)) |
| **Method** | **Personal service** on the witness (MCR 2.105(A)(1)) — hand delivery to the individual |
| **Witness fee** | **$12.00 per day** of attendance (MCL 600.2552(1)) — must be tendered at time of service |
| **Mileage** | **$0.10 per mile** (one way) from witness's residence to place of attendance (MCL 600.2552(1)) |
| **When to serve** | Reasonable time before compliance date — minimum **14 days** for document-only subpoenas |
| **Proof of service** | Server completes proof of service (affidavit) and returns to issuing party |

### Form Reference
- **MC 11** — Subpoena (Michigan SCAO standard form)
- Available at: https://courts.michigan.gov/forms (search "MC 11")
- Pro se litigants may issue subpoenas through the **court clerk** — present the completed MC 11 to the clerk for issuance

### Special Considerations for Records Subpoenas
- **HIPAA Authorization** — Required for medical/health records (see Appendix A)
- **FERPA Authorization** — Required for education records (see Appendix B)
- **Qualified Protective Order** — May be needed for CPS/MDHHS records
- Records custodians may assert privileges — be prepared to file a **Motion to Compel** if needed

### Cost Estimates Per Subpoena

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|------|---------------|
| Court issuance fee | $0 (varies by county — may be waived for indigent) |
| Process server fee | $30–$75 per service |
| Witness fee | $12.00 per day |
| Mileage | $0.10/mile (varies) |
| Copying costs (charged by custodian) | $0.10–$1.00/page |
| Certification fee (per record set) | $10–$25 |
| **Average total per subpoena** | **$50–$150** (plus copying costs) |

### Special Notes
- **FERPA:** The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (20 U.S.C. § 1232g) restricts release of student records. **However:**
  - A parent has the right to inspect their child's education records (34 C.F.R. § 99.10)
  - A subpoena is a valid exception (34 C.F.R. § 99.31(a)(9))
  - The school must notify the parent (Watson) before releasing records in response to a subpoena, giving her an opportunity to object — unless Andrew has obtained a court order
- **Best approach:** File a **FERPA consent form** with the subpoena (Appendix B) AND separately request records directly as a parent under 34 C.F.R. § 99.10.
- **If Watson removed Andrew from school records:** This itself is evidence of parental alienation. Subpoena the records of who changed the emergency contacts and when.
- **Cost estimate:** Schools typically provide records at no cost to parents. If the subpoena is served on the school, copying fees may apply: ~$0.10/page. Estimated: $10–$50.
