IV. WHY THIS PATTERN VIOLATES JUDICIAL ST ANDARDS
Based on my understanding of the Michigan Constitution, the Micliigan Court Rules, and
the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, the conduct described above appears to violate
several core obligations.
1. Canon l- Integrity and independence of the judiciary. (Michigan Courts)
o Canon 1 emphasizes that "an independent and honorable judiciary is
indispensable to justice," and tliat a judge must personally observe liigli
9


standards of conduct so that "the integrity and independence of the
judiciary may be preserved."
o Off-record receipt of evidence, one-sided ex parte coiui'nunications, and a
sustained pattern of favoring one party over anotlier in family cases
unden'nine public confidence in the neutrality of the court.
2. Canon 2(A) and 2(B) - Avoiding impropriety and the appearance of
impropriety; equal treatment. (Revize)
o Canon 2(A) requires judges to avoid both impropiiety and the
appearance of impropriety. Canon 2(B) requires judges to conduct
tl'iemselves in a manner that promotes public confidence in the integrity
and impartiality of the judiciary and to treat every person "fairly, with
courtesy and respect."
o Here, the combination of (a) ex parte submissions, (b) refusal to review
my exculpatory photos, (c) willingness to rely on hearsay from the other
side, and (d) hostile statements toward me as a pro se parent creates a
powerful appearance that I have not been treated with equal respect or
neutrality.
3. Canon 3 - Impartial, diligent performance and prohibition on ex parte
communications. (Michigan Courts)
o Canon 3 requires that a judge perform judicial duties impartially and
diligently, and bars ex parte communications on the merits of pending
matters except under narrow circumstances.
o Directing that a critical mental-health evaluation be emailed to chambers
instead of filed, and reviewing a USB recording through staff outside my
presence, are fori'ns of ex parte evidence liandling inconsistent with Canon
3 and with the requirement that decisions be based on the record.
4. MCR 3.207 and related authorities governing ex parte orders and hearings.
(Michigan Courts)
o MCR 3.207(B)(1) and (B)(5)-(6) govern ex parte custody and parenting-
time orders and require:
Specific facts in a verified pleading or affidavit showing that
iini'nediate and irreparable injury will result without ex parte relief,
10


Notice provisions infori'ning the other paity how to object; and
A prompt hearing and resolution of the dispute within a defined
timeframe if an objection is filed.
o Michigan appellate decisions emphasize tliat even when ex parte orders
are initially justified, the court must hold a hearing and address the child's
best interests within a reasonable time, and that failure to comply with
MCR 3.207's notice and hearing requirements is error. (FindLaw Case
o In my case, an ex parte order completely suspending parenting time
remained in effect for approximately three months before a full
evidentiary hearing occurred, despite my objection and repeated requests,
and without detailed best-interest findings during tlie extended suspension.
5. Canon 3(A)(3) - Patience, dignity, and courtesy; fair opportunity to be
heard.
o Muting my microphone, cutting off objections, and telling me not to file
additional materials because they would not be considered, particularly
wliere the other side had counsel, contradicts the duty to treat litigants
with patience, dignity, and courtesy, and to ensure a fair opportunity to be
heard.
6. Canon 3(C) and MCR 2.003 - Duty to recuse when impartiality may
reasonably be questioned. (vLex)
o Canon 3(C) and MCR 2.003 require disqualification where a judge's
impartiality might reasonably be questioned, even if the judge believes
they can be fair.
o A reasonable observer, reviewing this record of one-sided evidentiary
rulings, ex parte practices, punitive bond requirements, and dismissive
treatment of a pro se parent, would in my view question whether Judge
McNeill can remain impartial in my cases.
In short, this is not a complaint about a single ruling or isolated comi'nent. It is about a
systemic pattern over multiple years, across both PPO and custody cases, that appears to
violate Canon 1, Canon 2, and Canon 3, and that has severely harmed my parental
relationship and public confidence in the Muskegon family court.
