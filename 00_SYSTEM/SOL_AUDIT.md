## 📚 TOLLING DOCTRINES REFERENCE

### Continuing Violations
Where conduct is part of a continuing pattern, SOL runs from last act. Especially applicable to IIED, conspiracy, housing violations.

### Discovery Rule
SOL begins when plaintiff knew or should have known of the injury. MCL 600.5827. Applicable to fraud, concealed torts.

### Fraudulent Concealment
MCL 600.5855 — If defendant concealed cause of action, SOL tolled until discovery. 2-year extension.

### Equitable Tolling
Courts may toll SOL where plaintiff was prevented from filing by defendant's conduct (e.g., abuse of process, intimidation).

### Minority/Disability
MCL 600.5851 — SOL tolled during plaintiff's legal disability.

### Continuing Harm (Federal)
For §1983 claims, each discrete constitutional violation restarts the SOL. National Railroad Passenger Corp. v. Morgan, 536 U.S. 101.

### Relation Back (Federal)
Fed. R. Civ. P. 15(c) — amendments relate back to original filing date if same transaction/occurrence.

## 🎯 STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### Priority Filing Order (SOL-Driven)

1. **Defamation (Watson Tort)** — 1-year SOL is shortest; must file or preserve FIRST
2. **Fair Housing Act claims** — 2-year federal SOL; file before tort claims
3. **All 3-year tort claims** — IIED, conspiracy, interference, abuse of process
4. **Federal §1983 claims** — 3-year borrowed SOL; continuing violations extend
5. **MI RICO** — 5-year SOL provides more breathing room
6. **Fraud / Breach of Contract / Truth in Renting** — 6-year SOL; lowest urgency
7. **No-SOL filings** — JTC, Bar, Appeals, Emergency motions; file strategically

### Defamation — SPECIAL ALERT

The **1-year SOL on defamation** (MCL 600.5805(2)) is the most dangerous deadline.
- Each defamatory statement has its own 1-year clock
- Michigan follows the **single publication rule** — SOL runs from first publication
- **Continuing defamation** doctrine may extend if new publications occur
- RECOMMENDATION: File defamation count ASAP or ensure latest statements are within 1 year

### Continuing Violations — Key Strategy

For most Watson Tort and Shady Oaks counts, the **continuing violations doctrine** is critical:
- If the tortious conduct is ongoing (e.g., continued alienation, ongoing housing code violations),
  the SOL runs from the *last* act in the series, not the first
- Document the MOST RECENT instances of each violation pattern
- This effectively extends the SOL window for all related earlier acts
