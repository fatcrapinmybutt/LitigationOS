📌 SIX CORE ALLEGATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. EX PARTE HANDLING OF CRITICAL EVIDENCE AND COMMUNICATIONS
   - Directions to submit materials directly to chambers instead of filing
   - Review of USB audio recording outside complainant's presence
   - HealthWest evaluation routed to chambers email instead of official court file

2. REPEATED "EMERGENCY" EX PARTE PARENTING-TIME SUSPENSION
   - Early August 2025: Judge signed ex parte emergency order suspending ALL parenting time
   - Suspension involved three-year-old child (Lincoln)
   - No timely evidentiary hearing (lasted ~3 months until October 29, 2025)
   - No required findings under Michigan law MCR 3.207(B)(1)
   - No specific facts showing "immediate, irreparable harm"

3. UNEQUAL AND HOSTILE TREATMENT OF PRO SE PARENT
   - Muting during hearings
   - Silencing during hearings
   - Discouraging filing of pleadings
   - "Closing the courtroom door" to self-represented parent

4. DISREGARD OF EXCULPATORY EVIDENCE; SELECTIVE RELIANCE ON HEARSAY
   - Refused to examine photos from last normal parenting exchange (showing child safe/happy)
   - Accepted hearsay about alleged child statements to neighbor
   - Neighbor never testified; statement untested
   - Selective reliance on "disputed mental-health materials"

5. FINANCIAL BARRIERS TO COURT ACCESS
   - May 16, 2025 order imposed .00 FILING BOND in custody case
   - Functions as financial gate against bringing necessary motions
   - Disproportionate impact: complainant experienced job loss, housing instability

6. REFUSAL TO RECUSE DESPITE DOCUMENTED BIAS
   - October 29, 2025: Oral motion to disqualify filed during 3rd custody hearing
   - Motion detailed pattern of one-sided rulings, remarks, and irregular evidence handling
   - Judge refused to recuse
   - Judge continues to preside over both PPO and custody matters
   - Opposing party is known employee of Kent County Prosecutors Office (Family Court Division)
