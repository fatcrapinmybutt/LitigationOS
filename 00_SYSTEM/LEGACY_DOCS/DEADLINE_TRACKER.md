## Service Timing — MCR 2.107

| Method of Service | Extra Days Added |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Personal service (hand delivery) | 0 days |
| First-class mail | **+3 days** (MCR 2.107(C)(3)) |
| Electronic service (email) | **+1 day** (MCR 2.107(C)(4)) |

**Example:** If a hearing is in 9 days and you serve by mail, you must mail **12 days** before the hearing (9 + 3).

## Federal Court — §1983 Claims

| Claim | Statute | Limitations Period | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------------------|-------|
| 42 USC § 1983 — Due Process | Borrowed from state: MCL 600.5805(10) | **3 years** from violation | Federal courts borrow the state personal injury statute |
| 42 USC § 1983 — Equal Protection | Same | **3 years** from violation | Each discrete act starts its own clock |
| 42 USC § 1983 — 1st Amendment (access to courts) | Same | **3 years** from violation | Applies to retaliation for filing motions |
| 42 USC § 1983 — 14th Amendment (parental rights) | Same | **3 years** from deprivation | Ongoing deprivation = continuing violation doctrine may apply |

**Continuing Violation Doctrine:** Where the constitutional violation is ongoing (e.g., continued denial of parenting time), the statute of limitations may not begin to run until the violation ends. Document every instance of continued deprivation.

**Discovery Rule:** The statute begins to run when the plaintiff knew or should have known of the constitutional violation, not necessarily when the violation occurred.

# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════
# SECTION 8: RULES OF THUMB — NEVER FORGET
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════

1. **21 days** — The most common deadline in Michigan practice. Appeals, reconsideration, answers — all 21 days.
2. **File EARLY, not late** — If a deadline falls on a weekend/holiday, the deadline extends to the next business day (MCR 1.108(1)), but don't rely on this. File at least 2 days before any deadline.
3. **Mailing adds 3 days** — If you serve by mail, add 3 days to every deadline calculation (MCR 2.107(C)(3)).
4. **Keep EVERYTHING** — Every filing receipt, every certified mail receipt, every confirmation email. These are your proof.
5. **Calendar immediately** — The moment you file, calculate and calendar every resulting deadline.
6. **Follow up proactively** — If a court has not acted on an emergency motion within 7 days, call the clerk. If 14 days, file a follow-up.
7. **Proof of service is mandatory** — No proof of service = motion may be stricken. File it with everything.
8. **One year for defamation** — Unlike the 3-year period for most torts, defamation is only 1 year (MCL 600.5805(9)).
9. **56 days to MSC** — After a COA decision, you have 56 days (not 21) to seek MSC review.
10. **90/91 days for service** — Federal: 90 days (FRCP 4(m)); State: 91 days (MCR 2.102(D)). Close but NOT the same.
