## MCL 125.530-125.538: Truth in Renting Act {#truth-in-renting}

### Overview and Statutory Scheme

The **Truth in Renting Act** (MCL 125.530-125.538) is one of the most powerful tools available to tenants in mobile home parks. It provides for treble (triple) damages plus attorney fees for violations.

### Statutory Requirements (MCL 125.530)

**MCL 125.530** requires that every residential lease agreement must include:

1. **Date of lease:** When agreement was made

2. **Identification of parties:** Names and addresses of all parties

3. **Identification of premises:** Specific lot number and address

4. **Term of tenancy:** Start and end dates, or "at will" designation

5. **Rent amount and due date:** Clear statement of:
   - Rental amount per period
   - When rent is due
   - Late fees (if any)
   - Utility charges separate from rent
   - Any other charges or fees

6. **Maintenance responsibilities:** Clear allocation of who maintains:
   - Roof
   - Exterior walls
   - Lot (landscaping, grading)
   - Utilities
   - Common areas

7. **Default and remedy:** What constitutes default and what remedies are available

8. **Termination procedures:** How tenancy can be terminated, notice periods required

9. **Landlord/Agent name and address:** For receiving notices and complaints

10. **All terms and conditions:** Every material term of the rental agreement

### Prohibited Practices (MCL 125.531)

MCL 125.531 prohibits landlords from:

1. **Fraudulent agreements:** Using misleading or incomplete lease agreements

2. **Failing to provide lease copy:** Landlord must provide tenant with signed copy of lease

3. **Concealing material terms:** Material terms cannot be hidden in fine print or verbal additions

4. **Altering without consent:** Lease terms cannot be changed without tenant's knowledge and agreement

5. **Charging undisclosed fees:** Cannot charge fees not specified in lease

6. **Requiring waivers of rights:** Cannot require tenant to waive statutory protections

7. **Retention of security deposit without accounting:** Must provide itemized accounting

### Application to Shady Oaks Case

**Fraudulent Ledgers:** If park management maintained false ledgers regarding lot rent and utilities, this violates MCL 125.531:
- Misrepresenting amount owed
- Concealing actual terms
- Charging undisclosed fees
- Failing to provide accurate accounting

**Evidence Required:**
- Actual lease agreement provided to Andrew
- Park's internal records showing discrepancies
- Bank statements showing payments made vs. amounts claimed
- Testimony from other residents about similar practices
- Park's ledger records showing false entries

### Treble Damages and Attorney Fees (MCL 125.535)

**MCL 125.535** is the key remedial provision:

"Any landlord who violates the provisions of this act is liable to the tenant for actual damages and attorney fees, plus an additional amount equal to **two times the actual damages**, but not less than $250."

**Calculation Example:**
- If Andrew can prove actual damages of $5,000 (overpaid rent, fraudulent charges)
- Treble damages = $5,000 + (2 × $5,000) = $15,000 total recovery
- Plus attorney fees incurred in litigation

**Advantages:**
- No need to prove intent or fraud (liability is strict)
- Minimum recovery of $250 per violation
- Attorney fees recovery incentivizes litigation
- Multiple violations can accumulate

**For Andrew's Case:**
If fraudulent ledgers show systematic overcharging or misrepresentation of utilities/charges, Andrew may recover treble damages on each instance. With 49 claims identified, this could represent substantial TIRA liability.

### Retaliation Protection (MCL 125.537)

MCL 125.537 provides:

"A landlord shall not retaliate against a tenant because the tenant has:
1. Made a good faith complaint to the landlord about non-compliance with this act
2. Made a report to a government agency
3. Exercised rights under this act or tenant protection laws"

**Anti-Retaliation Rule:** If park management increased lot rent, threatened eviction, or took adverse action within 90 days after Andrew complained about the lease or fraudulent ledgers, this constitutes retaliation.

**Rebuttable Presumption:** MCL 125.537 creates a rebuttable presumption that retaliation occurred if adverse action is taken within 90 days of complaint.

**For Andrew's Case:** If the July 2025 set-out occurred shortly after Andrew complained about fraudulent charges, retaliation may be established. This further supports treble damages recovery.

## MCL 600.5714-5720: Summary Proceedings and Eviction Law {#summary-proceedings}

### Eviction Procedures (MCL 600.5714-5720)

Michigan law strictly regulates eviction procedures. Any deviation from statutory requirements may invalidate an eviction.

### Notice Requirements (MCL 600.5714)

**MCL 600.5714** requires that before commencing summary proceedings for eviction, the landlord must:

1. **Provide written notice:** Must be in writing (not verbal)

2. **State violation:** Notice must specifically state what breach occurred

3. **Provide cure period:**
   - **For non-payment of rent:** 7 days to pay
   - **For other breaches:** 30 days to cure the breach
   - **For illegal activity:** 24 hours to vacate (no cure period)

4. **Notice delivery:** Must be delivered:
   - Personally to tenant, OR
   - Left at dwelling in conspicuous place, OR
   - Sent by first-class mail

### Summary Proceedings Commencement (MCL 600.5715)

**MCL 600.5715** requires:

1. **Complaint filing:** Formal complaint must be filed with court (not self-help)

2. **Court process:** Proper service of summons and complaint on tenant

3. **Hearing:** Tenant has right to hearing before eviction

4. **Judgment:** Court must enter judgment after hearing and proper procedures

### Judgment for Possession (MCL 600.5716)

**MCL 600.5716** specifies:

- Judgment for possession must be specific
- Judgment for unpaid rent (if applicable) must be separate
- Court must explain remedies available to tenant
- Judgment must clearly state possession date

### Writ of Restitution (MCL 600.5717)

**MCL 600.5717** governs issuance of writ of restitution (actual removal):

- Writ may be issued only AFTER judgment for possession is entered
- Writ cannot be issued until 10 days after judgment (unless emergency)
- Sheriff must execute writ (not private persons)
- Tenant's property must be handled per law (MCL 600.5718)

### Tenant's Property on Eviction (MCL 600.5718)

**Critical Protection:** MCL 600.5718 regulates what happens to tenant's property during eviction:

1. **Property must be stored:** Tenant's personal property cannot be destroyed

2. **Reasonable care:** Landlord/sheriff must take reasonable care of property

3. **Tenant right of removal:** Tenant has reasonable time to retrieve property before disposal

4. **Notice of storage:** If property is stored off-premises, landlord must notify tenant of location

5. **Storage costs:** Landlord can charge reasonable storage costs (must be reasonable)

### Self-Help Eviction is ILLEGAL (MCL 600.2918)

**MCL 600.2918** explicitly prohibits "self-help" eviction:

"A landlord shall not:
1. Remove or exclude a tenant from a rental dwelling without judicial process
2. Remove or exclude occupants of a dwelling without following statutory procedures
3. Change locks
4. Remove a tenant's property or belongings
5. Shut off utilities to force departure
6. Remove doors or windows
7. Otherwise make the dwelling uninhabitable or inaccessible"

**Penalties:**
- Tenant may recover **actual damages**
- Tenant may recover **punitive damages** (triple actual damages)
- Tenant may recover **attorney fees**
- Tenant may obtain injunctive relief to restore possession
- Criminal liability may apply (MCL 750.552)

### Application to Shady Oaks Park "Set-Out"

If Shady Oaks Park LLC conducted the July 2025 "set-out" without:
- Court judgment for possession
- Proper summary proceedings
- Judicial process
- Sheriff involvement

Then the set-out violates MCL 600.5714-5720 and specifically violates MCL 600.2918.

**Andrew's Remedies:**
1. Recovery of actual damages (cost to retrieve property, moving, etc.)
2. Treble punitive damages (3× actual damages under MCL 600.2918)
3. Attorney fees
4. Restoration of possession if home still occupies lot
5. Damages for destroyed property

**Criminal Referral:** Self-help eviction may also violate MCL 750.552 (criminal statute for unauthorized removal), potentially supporting criminal complaint to Prosecutor.

## MCL 554.601-554.641: Landlord-Tenant Relationships {#landlord-tenant}

### Security Deposit Regulations (MCL 554.601-554.610)

**MCL 554.606** requires landlord to:

1. **Deposit in escrow or interest-bearing account:** Security deposit must not be commingled with landlord's funds

2. **Return within 30 days:** Upon tenancy termination, deposit must be returned within 30 days

3. **Provide itemized accounting:** If deductions made, landlord must provide:
   - Itemized list of deductions
   - Amount of each deduction
   - Description of damage/loss
   - Remaining deposit amount

4. **Pay interest:** On amounts held over 6 months (unless account is exempt)

### Violations Result in Double Damages (MCL 554.619)

"If a landlord fails to return a security deposit and furnish an itemized accounting as required by this act, the landlord shall pay to the tenant **double the amount improperly withheld**, plus interest and costs."

**For Andrew's Case:** If Shady Oaks Park retained security deposit without proper accounting or returned it after improper deductions, Andrew may recover double damages.

### Right to Habitability (MCL 554.601)

MCL 554.601 establishes that rental dwelling must be suitable for human occupancy:

- Must have proper structure and roof
- Must have functioning heating system
- Must have proper sanitation (plumbing, sewage)
- Must have adequate light and ventilation
- Must comply with building/housing codes
- Must be kept in safe condition

### Implied Warranty Against Violations

The implied warranty of habitability is a **non-waivable right**. A landlord cannot require tenant to waive this warranty.

## MCL 125.2301+: Mobile Home Commission Act {#mobile-home-act}

### Mobile Home Park-Specific Protections

The **Mobile Home Commission Act** (MCL 125.2301 et seq.) provides special protections for mobile home residents, which are MORE protective than general landlord-tenant law.

### MCL 125.2313: Termination of Tenancy

MCL 125.2313 specifies that mobile home tenancy can be terminated only for "good cause":

**Good Cause Definition:** "Good cause" means:
1. Non-payment of rent (after proper notice)
2. Substantial violation of park rules (after 30-day notice to cure)
3. Criminal activity on premises
4. Condemnation of lot or park closure
5. Other material breach of lease (after notice to cure)

**Notice Requirements:**
- For non-payment: 7 days notice
- For other violations: 30 days notice to cure
- Notice must be in writing
- Notice must specify violation

**Key Distinction:** Mobile home residency termination requires "good cause" — the landlord cannot simply terminate at will as may be permissible in some residential rental contexts.

### MCL 125.2315: Eviction Procedures for Mobile Home Parks

MCL 125.2315 specifies:

1. **Court process required:** Landlord must obtain judgment from court

2. **Tenant hearing rights:** Tenant is entitled to hearing before eviction

3. **30-day notice:** After judgment, must provide 30 days notice before sheriff can execute writ

4. **Tenant property protection:** Tenant's property must be handled per MCL 600.5718 (stored safely, not destroyed)

### MCL 125.2317: Lot Rent Limitations

MCL 125.2317 limits what lot rent increases are permissible:

1. **Rent increase notice:** Landlord must provide 30 days notice before lot rent increase

2. **Reasonableness requirement:** Lot rent increases must be reasonable and nondiscriminatory

3. **Timing:** Lot rent increase effective date cannot be less than 30 days from notice

**For Andrew's Case:** If park charged inflated lot rent or increased rent without proper notice, this violates MCL 125.2317.

### MCL 125.2319: Mobile Home Commission Authority

The Mobile Home Commission (now part of Michigan Department of Energy, Labor & Economic Growth) has authority to:

1. Investigate complaints against park management
2. Enforce mobile home act protections
3. Suspend or revoke park license for violations
4. Assess fines and penalties

**Andrew's Option:** File complaint with Mobile Home Commission (now EGLE) regarding fraudulent ledgers, improper eviction, and property destruction.

## MCL 445.903: Michigan Consumer Protection Act {#mcpa}

### Unfair/Deceptive Practices (MCL 445.903)

The **Michigan Consumer Protection Act** (MCL 445.903) prohibits unfair, unconscionable, and deceptive methods, acts, or practices in trade or commerce.

**Key Provisions:**

1. **Unfair practices:** Practices that cause substantial injury to consumers not reasonably avoidable and not outweighed by countervailing benefits

2. **Deceptive practices:** Misrepresentation, concealment, or omission of material facts likely to mislead consumers

3. **Unconscionable practices:** Exploitation of knowledge of consumer's inability to discover truth through reasonable diligence

### Examples of MCPA Violations

- Fraudulent ledgers (deception)
- Misrepresenting charges or rent amount (deception)
- Concealing actual costs in fine print (deception)
- Taking property without authorization (unfair/deceptive)
- Destroying property without notice (unfair)

### Private Right of Action (MCL 445.911)

MCL 445.911 provides that any consumer injured by violation may sue and recover:

1. **Actual damages:** Amount of injury/loss

2. **Treble damages:** Up to 3 times actual damages (if violation is intentional)

3. **Attorney fees and court costs:** Full fee recovery

4. **Injunctive relief:** Court order to stop violative practices

### For Andrew's Case

MCPA violations can be pleaded in addition to TIRA violations, providing additional basis for treble damages recovery.

## Implied Warranty of Habitability {#habitability}

### Common Law Doctrine

Michigan recognizes an **implied warranty of habitability** — the principle that rental property must be fit for human habitation. This warranty cannot be waived.

### Case Authority: *Trentadue v Gorton* (Leading Case)

**Trentadue v Gorton**, 261 Mich App 250 (2004) establishes:

1. **Non-waivable right:** Implied warranty of habitability exists and cannot be contractually waived

2. **Scope:** Dwelling must be:
   - Structurally sound
   - Safe from hazards
   - Adequate for human habitation
   - Compliant with housing codes

3. **Breach remedies:** Tenant may:
   - Repair and deduct from rent
   - Withhold rent (in escrow)
   - Terminate tenancy
   - Sue for damages

### Breach Elements

To establish breach of implied warranty:

1. **Defect existed:** Condition made dwelling unfit for occupation

2. **Defect not caused by tenant:** Landlord or natural wear caused condition

3. **Notice to landlord:** Tenant gave landlord notice of defect

4. **Unreasonable delay to repair:** Landlord failed to repair within reasonable time

5. **Damage resulted:** Tenant suffered damages (health, personal injury, loss of use)

### For Andrew's Case

If Shady Oaks Park failed to maintain:
- Proper lot grading/drainage
- Road access
- Utilities
- Common area maintenance
- Environmental compliance

Andrew may pursue breach of habitability claims with attendant damages.

## Illegal Self-Help Eviction (MCL 600.2918) {#self-help-eviction}

### The Statute

**MCL 600.2918** provides:

"A landlord shall not remove or exclude a tenant from a rental dwelling without following the appropriate legal procedure. A landlord who violates this section is liable to the tenant for damages and attorney fees. The tenant may recover an amount not less than $200 and not more than $500 in addition to actual damages."

### Prohibited Conduct

Self-help eviction includes:

1. **Removing property:** Taking tenant's belongings without court order
2. **Changing locks:** Preventing entry by changing locks or removing keys
3. **Removing doors/windows:** Making unit uninhabitable
4. **Shutting utilities:** Cutting off water, electric, gas, heat
5. **Other harassment:** Any action that prevents tenant occupancy

### Damages

**MCL 600.2918 damages include:**

1. **Statutory minimum:** $200-$500 per violation (minimum recovery)

2. **Actual damages:** Any provable damage (storage costs, relocation, etc.)

3. **Exemplary/punitive damages:** Trial court may award punitive damages in egregious cases

4. **Attorney fees:** Full attorney fees and costs

5. **Injunctive relief:** Court order to restore possession, remove lock changes, etc.

### For Andrew's Case: The July 2025 Set-Out

If Shady Oaks Park physically removed Andrew's mobile home without:
- Court judgment for eviction
- Sheriff involvement
- Proper legal procedures

This is a clear MCL 600.2918 violation.

**Andrew's Recovery:**
- Minimum: $200-$500
- Actual damages: Cost of retrieval, moving, storage (likely $1,000s)
- Attorney fees
- Punitive damages: Potentially $5,000-$10,000+ if conduct was particularly egregious
- Restoration of possession (if home can be restored)

## Property Conversion and Replevin {#conversion}

### Conversion as Property Tort

**Conversion** is the unauthorized exercise of dominion over property that deprives the owner of its possession.

**Elements:**
1. Plaintiff owned property
2. Defendant took possession/exercised control
3. Defendant's action was unauthorized
4. Plaintiff was deprived of possession

### For Andrew's Case

The destruction of property valued at $15,000+ may constitute conversion if:
- Shady Oaks Park took physical possession
- Park then destroyed or disposed of property
- Andrew received no compensation or authorization

### Conversion Damages

Damages for conversion include:

1. **Full fair market value** of property at time of conversion
2. **Lost use damages:** Loss of use of property during conversion period
3. **Emotional distress** (if particularly egregious)
4. **Attorney fees** (if statutory basis applies, or in egregious cases courts may award)

### Replevin Action (MCL 600.2901-2916)

**Replevin** is the legal remedy to recover possession of specific personal property wrongfully taken or detained.

**Replevin Procedure:**
1. File complaint in court alleging wrongful possession
2. Post replevin bond (typically 1.5× property value)
3. Court issues writ of replevin authorizing sheriff to take possession
4. Sheriff retrieves property and delivers to plaintiff
5. Defendant must post bond to regain possession pending judgment

### For Andrew's Case

If the mobile home itself or significant contents are still in park's possession or control:

**Replevin remedy:**
- File replevin action to recover home/contents
- Post replevin bond
- Obtain writ ordering sheriff to retrieve property
- Recover possession through judicial process

**Advantages:**
- Quick recovery of property through court order
- Defendant must post bond to contest
- Puts immediate pressure on defendant

## Security Deposit Violations {#security-deposits}

### Statutory Requirements (MCL 554.601 et seq.)

Michigan law strictly regulates security deposits:

### Commingling Prohibition (MCL 554.606(1))

"A lessor shall place a security deposit in an escrow account at a State or Federal bank, or at a savings and loan association, or at a credit union chartered under State law."

**Key requirement:** Deposit cannot be commingled with landlord's business funds.

### Return Requirements (MCL 554.606(2))

"A lessor shall return a security deposit or furnish an accounting as provided in this section within 30 days after the termination of tenancy."

### Itemized Accounting (MCL 554.606(3))

If deductions are made:

"The lessor shall give the lessee a written itemized accounting of the deposit, including:
1. The cost of repairs
2. The cost of cleaning necessary to place the rental dwelling in a clean condition
3. Any other costs authorized by this act
4. The balance owed the lessee, if any
5. Payment of the balance"

### Double Damages for Violation (MCL 554.619)

**Critical provision:** "If a lessor fails to return a security deposit and furnish an itemized accounting as required by this section, the lessor shall pay to the lessee **double the amount improperly withheld**, plus interest."

### For Andrew's Case

**Potential violation:**
- If park retained security deposit
- Without proper escrow account
- Without itemized accounting
- Without 30-day return

**Recovery:**
- Double damages (2× retained amount)
- Plus interest

**Example:**

## Retaliatory Eviction Defense {#retaliatory-eviction}

### MCL 600.5720: Retaliation Prohibition

MCL 600.5720 provides:

"A landlord shall not retaliate by:
1. Increasing rent
2. Decreasing services
3. Bringing an eviction proceeding
4. Threatening any of these actions

...against a tenant because the tenant:
1. Complained to the landlord about housing code violations
2. Filed complaint with government agency
3. Organized or participated in tenant organization
4. Exercised legal rights"

### Rebuttable Presumption (MCL 600.5720(3))

**"If a landlord takes retaliatory action within 30 days after a tenant has:
1. Complained to the landlord
2. Made complaint to government agency
3. Organized tenants

Then it is presumed the action was retaliatory, unless the landlord shows by clear and convincing evidence that the action was taken for a non-retaliatory reason."**

### For Andrew's Case

**Timeline Analysis:**
- Date Andrew complained about fraudulent ledgers: [date]
- Date of July 2025 set-out: July 2025
- Time between complaint and set-out: [calculate]

**If within 30 days:** Retaliation is presumed. Park must prove non-retaliatory reason by "clear and convincing evidence."

**If within 90 days (TIRA provision):** Retaliation presumed under TIRA.

**Even if after 30 days:** Andrew can still prove retaliation by circumstantial evidence showing:
- Clear timing connection
- Motive to retaliate
- Pattern of retaliation against other residents

### Retaliation Defense in Eviction

If park initiates eviction proceeding, Andrew can raise retaliation as affirmative defense:

**Defense Strategy:**
1. Prove complaint was made (documentary evidence)

3. Argue presumption of retaliation (if within 30 days)

5. Request dismissal of eviction and damages

## Corporate Veil Piercing {#corporate-veil}

### Separate Legal Entities

"Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC" is separate legal entity from individual owners/managers.

### Piercing the Veil Doctrine

Michigan courts will "pierce the corporate veil" and hold individual owners liable if:

1. **Improper purpose:** Entity used for improper or fraudulent purpose

2. **Inadequate capitalization:** Entity not properly capitalized to meet liabilities

3. **Commingling of funds:** Entity funds mixed with personal funds

4. **No respect for formalities:** Owners/managers treat entity as alter ego

5. **Fraud or injustice:** Allowing entity shield to stand would perpetuate fraud or injustice

### For Andrew's Case

**Factors supporting veil piercing:**
- Fraudulent ledgers (improper purpose)
- Illegal set-out procedures (improper purpose)
- Destruction of property (improper purpose)
- Evidence that park management acted in personal capacity

**Discovery targets:**
- Entity formation documents
- Capitalization information
- Bank account records (commingling evidence)
- Meeting minutes (or lack thereof)
- Asset transfers

**Benefit:** If veil pierced, individual owners/managers become personally liable for park's conduct.
