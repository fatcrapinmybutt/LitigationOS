## 5. LEGAL THEORY APPLICATION

### 42 USC §1985(3) — Federal Civil Rights Conspiracy
The Watson family's coordinated actions constitute a conspiracy to deprive Andrew Pigors
of his constitutional right to parent L.D.W. The conspiracy involves:

- **Agreement:** Multiple family members acting in concert to alienate father from child
- **Overt acts:** False PPO filings, perjured testimony, direct threats, custodial interference
- **Class-based animus:** Targeting Andrew's parental status as a non-custodial father
- **Damages:** Loss of parenting time, emotional distress, legal costs

### MCL 750.157a — Michigan Criminal Conspiracy
Under Michigan law, when two or more persons conspire to commit an offense against the state,
each is guilty of the conspiracy. Relevant offenses include:

- MCL 750.422 — Perjury (Emily's false PPO testimony)
- MCL 750.81 — Assault (threats by Albert, Cody)
- MCL 600.916 — Unauthorized Practice of Law (Berry)
- MCL 750.136b — Child abuse (parental alienation as emotional abuse)

### Common Law Civil Conspiracy (Michigan)
Under *Advocacy Org. for Patients & Providers v. Auto Club Ins. Ass'n*, 257 Mich App 365 (2003),
a civil conspiracy requires: (1) a concerted action, (2) by combination of two or more persons,
(3) to accomplish an unlawful purpose, (4) or to accomplish a lawful purpose by unlawful means.
