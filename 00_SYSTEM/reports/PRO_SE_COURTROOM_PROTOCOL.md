### Detailed Objection Scripts

#### Hearsay (MRE 801-806)
**When:** Opposing party offers out-of-court statement for truth
**Say:** *"Objection, Your Honor — hearsay. The statement is offered for the truth of the matter asserted."*

#### Relevance (MRE 401-402)
**When:** Evidence does not relate to any issue in the case
**Say:** *"Objection — relevance. This evidence does not tend to prove or disprove any fact of consequence."*

#### Leading question (MRE 611(c))
**When:** Attorney asks leading questions on direct examination
**Say:** *"Objection — leading. Counsel is suggesting the answer to their own witness."*

#### Foundation (MRE 901)
**When:** Document or evidence not properly authenticated
**Say:** *"Objection — lack of foundation. The exhibit has not been properly authenticated."*

#### Speculation (MRE 602)
**When:** Witness testifies about something they have no personal knowledge of
**Say:** *"Objection — speculation. The witness has no personal knowledge of this matter."*

#### Best evidence rule (MRE 1002)
**When:** Party offers copy when original is available
**Say:** *"Objection — best evidence rule. The original document should be produced."*

#### Cumulative (MRE 403)
**When:** Same point made repeatedly with multiple witnesses
**Say:** *"Objection — cumulative. This evidence is duplicative of what has already been presented."*

#### Assumes facts not in evidence (MRE 611)
**When:** Question presupposes something not yet proven
**Say:** *"Objection — the question assumes facts not in evidence."*

## EVIDENCE PRESENTATION PROTOCOL
Follow these steps EXACTLY for every exhibit:

- Step 1: Mark the exhibit (e.g., "I would like to mark this as Plaintiff's Exhibit 1")
- Step 2: Show to opposing party ("May I approach? I am showing opposing party Exhibit 1")
- Step 3: Lay foundation ("Your Honor, I would like to have this exhibit admitted. It is a [describe]...")
- Step 4: Authenticate ("I can authenticate this document as [basis]...")
- Step 5: Move for admission ("I move to admit Plaintiff's Exhibit 1 into evidence")
- Step 6: Wait for ruling before using the exhibit in argument
- Step 7: If objection, respond to the specific objection (not a general argument)

## TOP 10 PRO SE MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Speaking when it is not your turn — wait for the judge to address you**
2. **Arguing with the judge — state your position once, then say "For the record, I respectfully disagree"**
3. **Getting emotional — courts reward calm, factual presentation**
4. **Reading your entire brief aloud — summarize key points, judges can read**
5. **Failing to make a record — if the judge rules against you, state your objection clearly for appeal**
6. **Not having organized exhibits — tab and number everything, have copies ready**
7. **Addressing opposing party directly — always speak to the judge, never to the other side**
8. **Making personal attacks — focus on actions and evidence, not character**
9. **Failing to preserve issues for appeal — object, cite the rule, get a ruling on the record**
10. **Waiving time limits — if you need more time, ask BEFORE the deadline passes**
