# 🛡️ APPEAL PRESERVATION CHECKLIST

---

> **THE #1 MISTAKE pro se litigants make on appeal:**
> **Failing to object at the trial level.**
> **An unpreserved error is reviewed ONLY for plain error — nearly impossible to win.**

## Before Every Hearing

| Action | Why | Rule |
|--------|-----|------|
| File written motion/brief | Creates paper trail for appellate record | MCR 2.119 |
| Request court reporter/transcript | Without transcript, no appellate review | MCR 7.210(B) |
| Prepare written proposed order | Shows exactly what you asked for | MCR 2.602 |

## During Hearing — Objections

| Action | Why | Rule |
|--------|-----|------|
| Object to EVERY improper ruling ON THE RECORD | Unpreserved errors are reviewed for plain error only | MRE 103(a) |
| State specific legal basis for objection | "Objection" alone is insufficient | MRE 103(a)(1) |
| Request a continuing objection if issue recurs | Avoids repeated objections on same issue | MRE 103(b) |
| Make offer of proof for excluded evidence | COA can't review without knowing what was excluded | MRE 103(a)(2) |

## During Hearing — Constitutional Issues

| Action | Why | Rule |
|--------|-----|------|
| Raise due process objection specifically | Constitutional errors require specific objection | 14th Amend / Const 1963 art 1 §17 |
| Cite Troxel v Granville for parental rights | Fundamental right — strict scrutiny applies | 530 US 57 (2000) |
| Object to lack of evidentiary hearing | Mathews v Eldridge due process requires hearing | 424 US 319 (1976) |
| Note for record: no counsel provided despite complexity | Preserves potential ineffective assistance claim if relevant | Turner v Rogers 564 US 431 |

## After Adverse Ruling

| Action | Why | Rule |
|--------|-----|------|
| State for the record: "I object to this ruling" | Preserves the issue | MCR 2.517 |
| Request specific findings of fact | COA reviews findings for clear error | MCR 2.517(A) |
| File motion for reconsideration within 21 days | Some issues require reconsideration before appeal | MCR 2.119(F) |
| Order transcript within 14 days | Transcript deadline is jurisdictional for appeal | MCR 7.210(B)(3) |
| File claim of appeal within 21 days (if final order) | 21-day deadline is JURISDICTIONAL | MCR 7.204(A) |

## Magic Words for the Record

| Action | Why | Rule |
|--------|-----|------|
| "For the record, I preserve this issue for appeal" | Explicit preservation statement | Best practice |
| "I object on due process grounds under the 14th Amendment" | Constitutional preservation | US Const Amend XIV |
| "I request findings of fact and conclusions of law" | Required for meaningful appellate review | MCR 2.517(A) |
| "I note my objection to proceeding without [X]" | Preserves procedural objection | Various |
