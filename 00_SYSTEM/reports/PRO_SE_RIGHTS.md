## 1. Right to Be Heard
**Authority:** US Const Amend XIV; Mich Const Art 1 §17; Mathews v Eldridge, 424 US 319 (1976)

Every party has the right to be heard before a court takes action affecting their rights.

### 🎤 Script (Say This in Court):
> "Your Honor, I respectfully assert my right to be heard on this matter before any order is entered. Due process requires notice and an opportunity to be heard."

### ⚠️ Violations in This Case:
Ex parte orders entered without notice; "don't speak" directive; orders entered based solely on Emily's filings.

## 6. Liberal Construction of Pro Se Filings
**Authority:** Haines v Kerner, 404 US 519 (1972); Estelle v Gamble, 429 US 97 (1976)

Pro se filings are held to less stringent standards than attorney filings and should be liberally construed.

### 🎤 Script (Say This in Court):
> "Your Honor, as a self-represented litigant, my filings are entitled to liberal construction under Haines v Kerner. I ask the Court to consider the substance of my arguments, not merely their form."

### ⚠️ Violations in This Case:
Filings potentially held to attorney standards rather than pro se standards.
