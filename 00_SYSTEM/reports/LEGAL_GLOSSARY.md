## Courtroom

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Sustained** | The judge AGREES with the objection. The question/evidence is blocked. |
| **Overruled** | The judge DISAGREES with the objection. The question/evidence is allowed. |
| **Stipulate** | Both parties agree to a fact without needing to prove it. "Do you stipulate to...?" |
| **Foundation** | The preliminary facts needed before evidence can be admitted. "Lay a foundation." |
| **Proffer** | A preview of what evidence would show, offered outside jury hearing. "I proffer that..." |
| **In Camera** | In the judge's chambers, privately. "May we approach in camera?" |
| **On the record** | Being recorded by the court reporter. Everything said is preserved. |
| **Off the record** | NOT being recorded. Informal discussion. Be careful — still in public. |
| **Sua sponte** | The judge acting on their own, without a party requesting it. |
| **Res judicata** | Already decided — can't relitigate the same claim. |
| **Collateral estoppel** | An issue already decided in another case binds the parties. |

## Motion Practice

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Ex parte** | Without notice to the other party. Requires emergency justification. |
| **Motion in limine** | Pre-trial motion to exclude evidence before the hearing begins. |
| **Summary disposition** | Asking the judge to decide without a full hearing (MCR 2.116). |
| **Show cause** | Order requiring a party to explain why they should not be held in contempt. |
| **Stay** | Pause enforcement of an order, usually pending appeal. |
| **Interlocutory** | A ruling during the case (not final). Can sometimes be appealed. |
| **Nunc pro tunc** | Retroactive effect — "now for then." An order applying back to an earlier date. |
| **Supersedeas bond** | Bond posted to stay enforcement of a judgment pending appeal. |
| **Brief** | Written legal argument. NOT the same as a motion — a brief SUPPORTS a motion. |
| **Memorandum of law** | Same as a brief — written legal argument with citations. |

## Evidence (MRE)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Hearsay** | Out-of-court statement offered for truth. Generally inadmissible (MRE 802). |
| **Admission** | Opposing party's own statement — NOT hearsay (MRE 801(d)(2)). Very powerful. |
| **Business record** | Records kept in regular course of business. Exception to hearsay (MRE 803(6)). |
| **Excited utterance** | Statement made under stress of event. Exception to hearsay (MRE 803(2)). |
| **Impeachment** | Attacking a witness's credibility. Prior inconsistent statements (MRE 613). |
| **Authentication** | Proving evidence is what it claims to be (MRE 901). |
| **Best evidence rule** | Must produce original document, not a copy, if available (MRE 1002). |
| **Privilege** | Protected communication (attorney-client, spousal). Cannot be compelled. |
| **Probative value** | How much the evidence proves a fact. Balanced against prejudice (MRE 403). |
| **Relevance** | Evidence that makes a fact more or less probable (MRE 401). Low threshold. |

## Family Law

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Best interest factors** | 12 factors under MCL 722.23 that determine custody. |
| **Parenting time** | Court-ordered time with child. Different from "custody" (decision-making). |
| **Legal custody** | Decision-making authority (education, healthcare, religion). |
| **Physical custody** | Where the child lives primarily. |
| **Friend of the Court (FOC)** | County office that investigates and makes custody/support recommendations. |
| **Guardian ad litem (GAL)** | Attorney appointed to represent the CHILD'S interests. |
| **PPO** | Personal Protection Order — Michigan's version of a restraining order. |
| **Proper cause** | Threshold to modify custody — change since last order. |
| **Established custodial environment** | Where child has been living — harder to change (clear & convincing). |
| **Parental fitness** | Whether a parent is fit to care for child. Presumption of fitness. |

## Appellate

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Abuse of discretion** | Standard of review — judge's decision was so wrong no reasonable judge would agree. |
| **De novo review** | Appellate court decides the issue fresh, giving no deference to trial court. |
| **Clearly erroneous** | Standard for factual findings — definitely and firmly wrong. |
| **Preserved error** | You objected at trial, so you can raise it on appeal. |
| **Harmless error** | Error occurred but didn't affect the outcome — no reversal. |
| **Mandamus** | Extraordinary order compelling a lower court to act. |
| **Certiorari** | Discretionary review by a higher court (like MSC reviewing COA). |
| **Remand** | Higher court sends case back to lower court for further proceedings. |
| **Leave to appeal** | Permission from appellate court to hear the appeal. |
| **Interlocutory appeal** | Appeal of a non-final order (requires leave in most cases). |
