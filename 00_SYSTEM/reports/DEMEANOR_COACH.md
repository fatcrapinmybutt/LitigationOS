## Before Court (Preparation)

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Arrive 30 minutes early | Allows time to find the courtroom, calm nerves, and review notes |
| Dress professionally (suit or business casual) | Appearance signals you take the court seriously |
| Bring 3 copies of everything (judge, opposing party, yourself) | Courts expect copies — being unprepared looks bad |
| Organize documents in a labeled binder with tabs | Fumbling for papers wastes the court's time |
| Eat and hydrate beforehand | You need to be sharp — hunger and dehydration cloud thinking |
| Use the bathroom before entering | Asking to leave mid-hearing looks unprofessional |
| Silence your phone completely | A ringing phone can result in contempt |
| Review your key arguments one final time | Fresh memory of main points prevents freezing |

## Entering the Courtroom

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Stand when the judge enters | Universal sign of respect — EVERYONE does this |
| Do not chew gum or eat | Immediate disrespect — judges notice everything |
| Sit at the plaintiff/petitioner table (usually left) | Court convention — ask clerk if unsure |
| Place documents on the table neatly organized | Visual organization signals mental organization |

## Speaking to the Judge

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Always address the judge as "Your Honor" | Required — anything else is disrespectful |
| Stand when speaking unless told otherwise | Court convention for all speakers |
| Speak clearly and at a moderate pace | The court reporter needs to capture every word |
| Never interrupt the judge | Fastest way to lose credibility and possibly be held in contempt |
| Answer questions directly — then explain | Judges hate evasion; answer YES/NO first, then elaborate |
| Say "I don't know" if you don't know | Better than guessing — honesty builds credibility |
| Ask permission before approaching the bench or witness | "May I approach, Your Honor?" — always ask first |

## Body Language

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Maintain respectful eye contact with the judge | Shows confidence and honesty |
| Keep hands visible on the table or at your sides | Hidden hands signal deception (subconsciously) |
| Sit up straight | Slouching signals disinterest or disrespect |
| Nod slightly when the judge speaks to show you're listening | Active listening builds rapport |
| Do NOT point at opposing party | Aggressive gesture — refer to them by name instead |

## After the Hearing

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Thank the judge regardless of outcome | "Thank you, Your Honor" — always professional |
| Do not celebrate or show disappointment visibly | The judge may still be watching |
| Immediately write down everything that happened | Memory fades fast — detailed notes preserve the record |
| Ask the clerk for copies of any orders entered | You need the exact language for compliance or appeal |
| Do not confront opposing party in the hallway | Anything you say can be used against you |
