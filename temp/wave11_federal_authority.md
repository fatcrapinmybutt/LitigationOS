## 8. EMERGING §1983 THEORIES

### Theory 1: Immunity Erosion via "Clear Absence of Jurisdiction"
- McNeill's 24 ex parte orders issued without notice/hearing create argument for "clear absence of all jurisdiction"
- *Mireles* two-exception framework deployed: (a) non-judicial acts, (b) clear absence of jurisdiction
- The ex parte evaluator contact (biasing letter to HealthWest) is a non-judicial act outside any case function
- **Strength: HIGH** — well-supported by record facts

### Theory 2: Domestic Relations Exception Narrowing
- *Ankenbrandt* + *Catz* chain establishes that §1983 survives in family context
- Complaint explicitly frames claims as constitutional violations, NOT custody dispute
- Each count targets the PROCESS (ex parte, no notice, no hearing), not the OUTCOME
- **Strength: HIGH** — consistent with 6th Circuit trend

### Theory 3: Monell Expansion (Pattern/Practice in Family Court)
- Evidence chain shows 210+ evidence items supporting Monell claim
- Pattern: systematic ex parte orders, no notice/hearing protocol, fee barriers for indigent
- Muskegon County's failure to supervise/train judges on basic DP requirements
- Damages calculation identifies County as deep-pocket defendant
- **Strength: MODERATE** — needs more pattern evidence beyond single judge

### Theory 4: Private Party Conspiracy (Watson-Berry-McNeill Triangle)
- *Dennis v. Sparks*, 449 U.S. 24: private conspiring with judge = under color of law
- Circumstantial evidence: temporal coordination (file + same-day ruling), Berry voicemail
- Berry's non-attorney drafting of legal filings = unauthorized practice + conspiracy evidence
- Watson/Berry CANNOT claim qualified immunity (*Wyatt v. Cole*)
- **Strength: HIGH** — unique angle, well-documented

### Theory 5: Heck Bar Avoidance
- *Schreiber v. Moe*, 596 F.3d 323: process claims ≠ outcome challenges
- *Powers v. Hamilton County*, 501 F.3d 592: counsel deprivation claims survive Heck
- Complaint challenges the PROCESS of the proceedings, not their result
- **Strength: HIGH** — 6th Circuit law is favorable

### Theory 6: Younger Abstention Bad Faith Exception
- *Kugler v. Helfant* + *Fieger v. Cox* establish that Younger is not automatic
- Documented pattern of bad faith: ex parte orders, evaluator corruption, fee barriers, "do not file" threat
- State remedies shown to be futile (exhaustion narrative in complaint ¶¶42-48)
- **Strength: HIGH** — extensive factual record of futility
