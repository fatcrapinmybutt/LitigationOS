## VI. RECOMMENDATIONS FOR F7 CUSTODY MODIFICATION ENHANCEMENT

### A. Strengthen Gardner Framework Section
The F7 filing references alienation patterns but does NOT explicitly map evidence to the 8 Gardner criteria. **Recommend adding a structured section** citing this Wave 14 analysis that walks through each criterion with pinpoint evidence, as courts increasingly recognize the Gardner framework. Cite *Mogle v. Scriver*, 241 Mich App 192, 201 (2000) and MCL 722.27a(8).

### B. Add MCL 722.27a(8) Stand-Alone Argument
The filing mentions MCL 722.27a(8) but should elevate it to a **stand-alone legal argument**:
- "The court shall consider evidence of parental alienation"
- "Take appropriate action if it determines that a parent is encouraging or enabling alienation"
- This is MANDATORY language — the court MUST consider and act

### C. Strengthen Child Statements Section
The current filing mentions "momma hurt me everyday" and "brother hurts me" in ¶51 but does not:
- Contrast coached vs. spontaneous statements (the "daddy hit momma" vs. "momma hurt me everyday" contrast)
- Note the developmental impossibility of a 2-3 year old independently constructing "daddy hit momma"
- Request forensic child interview by GAL rather than just mentioning GAL "below"

### D. Add Watson Family Conspiracy Exhibit
Create a **stand-alone exhibit** documenting the 212 database-referenced Watson family actions organized by family member, with the civil conspiracy framework under MCL 600.2954. This is currently scattered across ¶58A — consolidate into an exhibit.

### E. Prior Father Pattern — Obtain Declaration
The Facebook conversation from the father of Emily's older child is referenced but not attached. **Recommend obtaining a sworn declaration** from this individual. Prior-pattern evidence eliminates the "coincidence" defense and is devastating under Factor (j) and Factor (e).

### F. Quantify Harm to L.D.W.
