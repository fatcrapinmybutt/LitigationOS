## § 13. PARENTAL ALIENATION & ESTRANGEMENT — MICHIGAN DOCTRINE

### 13.1 Governing Law — MCL 722.23(j)

Michigan's Best Interest of the Child Act, MCL 722.23, defines 12 factors courts must evaluate. **Factor (j)** is the alienation factor:

> *"The willingness and ability of each of the parties to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent or the child and the parents."*

Michigan law does not use the term "parental alienation" as a statutory label, but courts evaluate alienation conduct directly under Factor (j). A parent who systematically undermines, sabotages, or obstructs the child's relationship with the other parent is found to have violated this factor.

## § 25. PROSECUTION & FORCED PROSECUTION — LEGAL PATHWAYS

### 25.1 Overview

When a county prosecutor refuses to act against perjury, fraud upon the court, and coordinated abuse of legal process — particularly when the perpetrators have connections to the court system itself — Michigan law provides multiple alternative pathways for compelling accountability.

### 25.2 Pathway 1: One-Person Grand Jury (MCL 767.3) — PRIMARY MECHANISM

**The statute**: When a complaint is filed before "any judge of a court of law and of record" alleging probable cause to suspect a crime, the judge "in his discretion may make an order directing that an inquiry be made."

**Key features**:
- **Any person may file** — the complaint "may be upon information and belief"
- Filed with a **Circuit Court judge** — bypasses the prosecutor entirely
- If probable cause found, judge conducts **secret inquiry** (MCL 767.4 — all proceedings confidential)
- Judge may **subpoena witnesses and take testimony**
- If judge finds probable cause of guilt, judge "may cause the apprehension of such person by proper process"
- Case then proceeds "**in like manner as upon formal complaint**" — preliminary examination required
- Inquiry judge is **disqualified** from acting as examining magistrate or trial judge
- Duration: 6 months, extendable to 12

**Critical caselaw**:
- *People v. Peeler*, 509 Mich. 381 (2022) — MSC landmark: one-person grand jury is investigatory, not charging; preliminary exam required after
- *In re Colacasides*, 379 Mich. 69 (1967) — Legislature enacted one-person grand jury because "regularly constituted law enforcement agencies sometimes are unable effectively and lawfully to enforce the laws, particularly with respect to corrupt conduct by officers of government and conspiratorial criminal activity on an organized and continuing basis"

**Strategic note**: Must file in a **different county** (e.g., Eaton County Circuit Court) because all Muskegon County judges are compromised through the Ladas-Hoopes-McNeill web.

**Target crimes**:
- MCL 750.422/423: Perjury (15 years) — Emily's false sworn statements
- MCL 750.424: Subornation of perjury (15 years) — Lori's role in creating false PPO
- MCL 750.425: Inciting perjury (5 years) — coaching false allegations

### 25.3 Pathway 2: Private Criminal Complaint (MCL 764.1, 764.1a)

**Process**:

2. Magistrate evaluates **reasonable cause** based on complaint, testimony, affidavits
3. Factual allegations "may be based upon personal knowledge, information and belief, or both" (MCL 764.1a(5))
