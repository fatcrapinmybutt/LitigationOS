## Pigors v. Watson et al. — Cross-Examination Scripts
### Generated: 2026-03-09 | LitigationOS APEX MANBEARPIG

## PURPOSE

This playbook provides step-by-step cross-examination scripts for impeaching each adverse witness. Based on the Impeachment Arsenal (Parts A & B) plus the witness lists. Each script follows the MRE 613 impeachment framework.

**MRE 613 Protocol:**
1. Establish the witness made a prior statement (specific time, place, to whom)
2. Ask the witness about the current testimony
3. Confront with the prior inconsistent statement
4. If witness denies → introduce extrinsic evidence

## WITNESS 1: EMILY A. WATSON

### Impeachment Theme: "Pattern of Deception"

**Setup Questions (rapport/lock-in):**
```
Q: Ms. Watson, you want what's best for your child, correct?
A: [Expected: Yes]
Q: And you believe children benefit from having both parents in their lives?
A: [Expected: Yes or hedging]
Q: You would agree that denying a child contact with a loving parent harms the child?
A: [Expected: hedging/qualifying — either answer works]
```

**Impeachment Line 1: Interference with Parenting Time**
```
Q: Ms. Watson, how many times in the past 19 months has the father had 
   parenting time with L.D.W.?
A: [Expected: deflection]
Q: The answer is zero, correct? Not a single visit in 569 days?
A: [Lock in answer]
Q: And during those 569 days, the father was paying you $1,056.60 every 
   month in child support, correct?
A: [Expected: Yes]
Q: So you received approximately $20,075 in support while providing zero 
   access to the child?
```

**Impeachment Line 2: False Allegations**
```
Q: Ms. Watson, you made allegations against the father that led to 
   investigations, correct?
A: [Expected: Yes]
Q: And how many of those investigations found the father had done 
   anything wrong?
A: [Expected: deflection]
Q: The answer is zero, isn't it? Every investigation cleared him?
   [ANDREW_REQUIRED: Confirm exact investigation outcomes]
Q: So you made allegations, multiple agencies investigated, and not one 
   found any basis for your claims?
```

**Impeachment Line 3: Ex Parte Orders**
```
Q: Ms. Watson, you're aware that 24 orders in this case were issued 
   ex parte — meaning without the father being present or notified?
A: [Lock in knowledge]
Q: Did you or your attorney request those ex parte orders?
A: [Expected: attorney handled it / I don't know]
Q: You benefited from every one of those orders, didn't you?
Q: And the father was denied his right to be heard before each one?
```

**Impeachment Line 4: Prior Inconsistent Statements**
```
[ANDREW_REQUIRED: Specific prior statements by Watson that contradict 
current positions — from texts, emails, court filings, affidavits]

Q: Ms. Watson, I'm showing you Exhibit [X]. Do you recognize this 
   as a text message you sent on [date]?
A: [Confirm]
Q: Please read the highlighted portion to the court.
A: [Reads contradictory statement]
Q: And today you're telling the court [opposite thing]?
Q: So which time were you telling the truth — then or now?
```

## WITNESS 4: JUDGE BETH McNEILL (If Deposed in Federal Case)

### Impeachment Theme: "Procedural Irregularity Pattern"

**Note:** Judicial witnesses are deposed differently. Be respectful but firm. Focus on procedures, not motivations.

```
Q: Judge McNeill, in this case you issued approximately 131 orders, 
   correct?
Q: Of those, approximately 24 were ex parte orders?
Q: That's an 18.26% ex parte rate. Are you aware the judicial norm 
   for ex parte orders is approximately 5%?
Q: Before issuing each ex parte order, did you verify that MCR 3.207(B) 
   requirements were met?
Q: MCR 3.207(B) requires specific findings before ex parte custody 
   orders. Can you point to where in the record those findings appear 
   for each of the 24 orders?
Q: Were you aware that Mr. Pigors was denied all parenting time for 
   569+ consecutive days under your orders?
Q: Did you at any point order a review hearing to assess whether 
   continued denial of parenting time was in the child's best interest?
```

## GENERAL IMPEACHMENT TOOLS

### Tool 1: Prior Inconsistent Statement (MRE 613)
1. Ask witness about current testimony position
2. "Do you recall making a statement on [date] to [person/in document]?"
3. "Let me show you Exhibit [X]" — have witness read contradictory statement
4. "Which version is accurate?"

### Tool 2: Bias/Interest (MRE 616)
- Financial interest in outcome
- Family relationship to party
- Coordinated testimony preparation
- Animosity toward opposing party

### Tool 3: Character for Truthfulness (MRE 608)
- Prior instances of dishonesty (not convictions — those are MRE 609)
- Reputation for untruthfulness in the community
- Specific instances of conduct showing untruthful character

### Tool 4: Learned Treatise (MRE 803(18))
- For expert witnesses: confront with authoritative texts that contradict their opinion
- Applicable to custody evaluator, psychologist, housing inspector

### Tool 5: Absence of Evidence
"If [X] actually happened, wouldn't you expect there to be [documentation/witnesses/records]?"
"And no such [documentation] exists, does it?"

## PRO SE CROSS-EXAMINATION TIPS

1. **Ask leading questions ONLY** — Cross-examination = yes/no questions that tell the story
2. **Never ask "why"** — It gives the witness a platform to explain
3. **One fact per question** — Short, controlled questions
4. **Know the answer before you ask** — Never ask a question you don't know the answer to
5. **Control the witness** — If they ramble, say "I asked a yes or no question"
6. **Use documents** — "Let me direct your attention to Exhibit A, page 3, line 7"
7. **Stay calm** — Anger helps the witness, not you
8. **End strong** — Last question should be devastating and unanswerable
9. **Sit down when you're ahead** — Don't over-cross and give them a chance to recover
