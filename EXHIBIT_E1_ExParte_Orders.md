# EXHIBIT E-1: August 8–9, 2025 Ex Parte Orders — Five Orders Without Notice

**Bates Range:** PIGORS-E-1
**Filing:** Formal Complaint to Judicial Tenure Commission
**Respondent:** Hon. Jenny L. McNeill (P58235)
**Case:** Pigors v. Watson, No. 2024-001507-DC, 14th Circuit Court

---

## Description

This exhibit documents the issuance of five ex parte orders on August 8, 2025, by Hon. Jenny L. McNeill in a single day, without notice to Father and without an opportunity to be heard. These orders resulted in the immediate and complete suspension of Father's parenting time with his minor child, L.D.W. The orders were issued the day after NSPD Case NS2505044 was filed — a police report that itself reveals the ex parte filings were premeditated (see Exhibit E-2).

## Relevant Canon Violations

**Canon 3(B)(7) — Ex Parte Communications:** A judge shall not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communications made to the judge outside the presence of the parties concerning a pending or impending proceeding. Hon. McNeill received and acted upon ex parte submissions without providing Father any notice, any opportunity to respond, or any hearing — in a matter directly affecting his fundamental parental rights.

**Canon 3(B)(2) — Right to Be Heard:** A judge shall afford every person who has a legal interest in a proceeding the right to be heard according to law. Father was afforded no hearing, no notice, and no opportunity to contest the allegations before his parenting time was terminated.

**Canon 1 — Upholding the Integrity of the Judiciary:** Issuing five orders in a single day stripping a parent's rights without any adversarial process undermines public confidence in the judiciary.

## Key Evidence

### The Five Orders — August 8, 2025

On August 8, 2025, Hon. McNeill signed five separate orders in case 2024-001507-DC. These orders collectively:

1. Suspended all of Father's parenting time effective immediately
2. Modified custody to grant Mother sole legal and physical custody
3. Imposed conditions on Father without a hearing
4. Were entered without any affidavit from Mother meeting the statutory requirements for ex parte relief
5. Were entered without any notice to Father or his opportunity to be heard

Father's last contact with L.D.W. was July 29, 2025. The orders formalized what had already become a de facto exclusion of Father from L.D.W.'s life.

### Premeditation — NSPD Case NS2505044 (August 7, 2025)

The day before these orders were issued, Emily A. Watson's father, Albert Watson, called the Norton Shores Police Department and filed a harassment report. The responding officer, Officer Ella Randall (#47437), documented the following statement from Albert Watson in NSPD Case NS2505044:

> "Albert said they want this incident documented so Emily can go tomorrow to get an Ex Parte order for full custody of her son."

This statement establishes that the ex parte filings were planned in advance and that the police report itself was orchestrated to create a paper trail for the next day's court filings. The temporal sequence is dispositive:

- **August 5, 2025:** USB recording made at Watson home
- **August 7, 2025 (10:49 AM):** Albert Watson calls police to create report (NS2505044)
- **August 8, 2025:** Emily A. Watson files ex parte motions; Hon. McNeill signs five orders the same day

### No Statutory Basis for Ex Parte Relief

Michigan law authorizes ex parte orders in custody matters only upon a showing of "immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage" (MCR 3.207(B)). No affidavit was filed establishing any immediate danger to L.D.W. No evidence of abuse, neglect, or emergency was presented. The nine prior NSPD police contacts involving Father had resulted in zero arrests and zero charges.

## Significance

This exhibit demonstrates a pattern of judicial conduct that violates the most fundamental principles of due process and judicial ethics. The issuance of five ex parte orders in a single day — acting on submissions that were demonstrably premeditated and orchestrated — represents a complete abandonment of the adversarial process. The resulting harm to Father and L.D.W. has been catastrophic: Father has been separated from his child since July 29, 2025, and has lost two homes and two jobs as a consequence of the cascade of orders that followed.
