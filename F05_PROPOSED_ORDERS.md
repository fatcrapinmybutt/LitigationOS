## ORDER I: EMERGENCY STAY AND INTERIM PARENTING TIME

The Court, having reviewed Petitioner's Complaint for Original Action and finding that immediate and irreparable harm to the minor child L.D.W. and the parent-child relationship necessitates emergency relief:

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED** that:

1. All orders entered by the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill in Case No. 2024-001507-DC on and after August 8, 2025, are **STAYED** pending resolution of this action.

2. The parenting time schedule established by the April 29, 2024 consent order is **IMMEDIATELY REINSTATED** on an interim basis.

3. Emily A. Watson shall make L.D.W. available for parenting time with Andrew James Pigors in accordance with the April 29, 2024 schedule beginning within forty-eight (48) hours of service of this Order.

---

## ORDER II: SUPERINTENDING CONTROL

The Court, having reviewed the full record and finding that the 14th Circuit Court's actions constitute an abuse of discretion so severe as to amount to a denial of due process:

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED** that:

1. Superintending control over Case No. 2024-001507-DC and Case No. 2023-5907-PP is **ASSUMED** by this Court pursuant to MCR 7.306 and Const. 1963, art. 6, Section 4.

2. The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill is **DISQUALIFIED** from further proceedings in these matters.

3. The Honorable Kenneth Hoopes, as Chief Judge, is also **DISQUALIFIED** from reassignment authority due to documented conflicts of interest.

4. The State Court Administrative Office shall assign a judge from outside the 14th Judicial Circuit.

---

## ORDER III: WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS

The Court, having found that L.D.W. is being unlawfully withheld from the custody of Petitioner Andrew James Pigors without due process of law:

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED** that:

1. A Writ of Habeas Corpus is **ISSUED** pursuant to Const. 1963, art. 1, Section 12 and MCL 600.4301 et seq.

2. Emily A. Watson shall produce L.D.W. at the time and place designated by the replacement judge for the purpose of restoring the parenting time schedule.

3. The Muskegon County Sheriff shall enforce this Writ if voluntary compliance is not achieved within forty-eight (48) hours.
