# EXHIBIT A: Consent Order — Joint Legal and Physical Custody

**Bates Range:** PIGORS-A-A
**Filing:** Petition for Superintending Control, MCR 7.306
**Court:** Michigan Supreme Court
**Case:** Pigors v. Watson, No. 2024-001507-DC (14th Circuit Court)

---

## Description

This is the Consent Order entered on April 29, 2024, by the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill in the 14th Circuit Court, Muskegon County, Family Division. The order established joint legal and physical custody of the minor child, L.D.W., with an equal 50/50 parenting time schedule between Plaintiff Andrew Pigors and Defendant Emily A. Watson. This order represented the judicial determination that both parents were fit, capable, and suitable custodians.

## Legal Significance

This Consent Order is the critical baseline against which all subsequent orders must be measured. Under MCL 722.27(1)(c), an established custodial environment (ECE) existed with **both** parents by operation of this order. Once an ECE is established, any modification requires clear and convincing evidence — the highest evidentiary standard in civil law — that the change serves the child's best interests.

The subsequent ex parte orders of August 8–9, 2025, and the September 28, 2025 custody order, destroyed this ECE without any hearing at which Father could present evidence or cross-examine witnesses. This Court's superintending control is necessary because the 14th Circuit Court obliterated a judicially established custody arrangement through ex parte proceedings that violated the foundational protections of MCR 3.207(A) and MCL 722.27(1)(c).

## Key Evidence

- **Order Date:** April 29, 2024, entered by agreement of both parties.
- **Custody Provision:** Joint legal custody and joint physical custody of L.D.W.
- **Parenting Time:** Equal 50/50 schedule, demonstrating Father's fitness and active parental role.
- **Duration of ECE:** Father exercised custody under this order for over fifteen (15) months — from April 29, 2024, through July 29, 2025 — without incident, complaint, or finding of unfitness.
- **No Modification Hearing:** This order was never modified through a proper noticed hearing with an opportunity to be heard. It was unilaterally destroyed by ex parte orders issued on August 8–9, 2025.

## Constitutional Implications

The Fourteenth Amendment protects the fundamental liberty interest of parents in the care, custody, and control of their children. *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 U.S. 57, 65 (2000). A parent's right to the companionship of his child is a fundamental right protected by both the United States and Michigan Constitutions. *In re Sanders*, 495 Mich. 394, 409 (2014).

The destruction of a fifteen-month established custodial environment without notice, hearing, or any opportunity to present evidence constitutes a deprivation of Father's fundamental parental rights without due process of law. The Consent Order proves Father was adjudicated fit — and that fitness determination was never revisited through any constitutionally adequate proceeding.
