# MOTIONS IN LIMINE — ALL CASES
## Pigors v. Watson et al.
### Generated: 2026-03-09 | LitigationOS APEX MANBEARPIG

---

## WHAT ARE MOTIONS IN LIMINE?

Motions in limine are pre-trial motions asking the court to rule on evidence admissibility BEFORE trial. Filing these early prevents prejudicial evidence from being mentioned in front of the jury/trier of fact. Under MRE 103 and MRE 104, the court has authority to rule on admissibility issues in advance.

**When to file:** 21 days before trial (check local rules for 14th Circuit — some require 14 days).

## LANE A — CUSTODY TRIAL (2024-001507-DC)

### Motion in Limine No. 1: EXCLUDE References to Andrew's Incarceration as Evidence of Unfitness

**Argument:** Andrew's 59-day jailing was for civil contempt (failure to pay support while unemployed due to litigation), NOT criminal conduct. Under MRE 404(b), evidence of other acts is inadmissible to prove character. The contempt was itself a consequence of the custody dispute, and allowing Watson to use it as evidence of unfitness creates circular reasoning. Under *People v Starr*, 457 Mich 490 (1998), the probative value is substantially outweighed by unfair prejudice (MRE 403).

**Requested ruling:** Exclude all references to Andrew's incarceration as evidence of parental unfitness. If admitted for any purpose, require limiting instruction.

---

### Motion in Limine No. 2: EXCLUDE Unsubstantiated Abuse Allegations

**Argument:** Watson has made allegations of abuse that resulted in ZERO findings after investigation. Under MRE 403, unsubstantiated allegations are unfairly prejudicial and misleading. Under *Merrow v Bofferding*, 458 Mich 617 (1998), the court should not consider unproven allegations in best-interest analysis. Each allegation investigated and unfounded should be excluded, OR an instruction given that unfounded allegations carry no weight.

**Requested ruling:** Exclude testimony about unsubstantiated abuse allegations unless Watson can first establish evidentiary foundation outside jury presence.

---

### Motion in Limine No. 3: ADMIT All Evidence of Parental Alienation Under MCL 722.23(j)

**Argument:** Factor (j) of the Child Custody Act expressly requires the court to consider "the willingness and ability of each of the parties to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent." Andrew has documented 305 interference incidents across 569+ days. This evidence is directly relevant under MCL 722.23(j) and must be admitted in full.

**Requested ruling:** Admit all evidence of interference with parenting time, alienation behaviors, and obstruction of the parent-child relationship.

---

### Motion in Limine No. 4: EXCLUDE Improper Character Evidence from Watson Family Members

**Argument:** Testimony from Albert Watson, Lori Watson, Lane Watson, and Cody Watson is likely to consist of character opinions without proper foundation. Under MRE 405, character evidence is only admissible through reputation or opinion testimony by qualified witnesses. Family members with obvious bias do not constitute reliable character witnesses. Under MRE 403, their testimony's minimal probative value is outweighed by cumulative prejudice.

**Requested ruling:** Require Watson to proffer specific, non-character testimony from family witnesses before they testify. Exclude cumulative character testimony.

---

### Motion in Limine No. 5: ADMIT Andrew's Police Reports and CPS Contact

**Argument:** Andrew contacted CPS once and filed police reports regarding bruising/marks on L.D.W. These reports are admissible under MRE 803(8) (public records) and show Andrew's concern for child welfare — directly relevant to MCL 722.23(a) (love, affection, emotional ties) and (c) (capacity to provide care). Watson's attempt to characterize these protective actions as "harassment" should be excluded as improper characterization.

**Requested ruling:** Admit police reports and CPS contact records. Exclude Watson's characterization of protective actions as harassment.

---

### Motion in Limine No. 6: EXCLUDE Ex Parte Orders Issued Without Notice

**Argument:** The 24 ex parte orders issued without proper notice or hearing under MCR 3.207(B) violated Andrew's due process rights. These orders should not be treated as evidence of Andrew's unfitness — they were obtained through procedural irregularity. Any reference to these orders should be accompanied by disclosure of their ex parte nature.

**Requested ruling:** If ex parte orders are referenced, require disclosure that they were issued without notice or hearing. Exclude as evidence of unfitness.

## LANE B — HOUSING TRIAL (2025-002760-CZ)

### Motion in Limine No. 1: ADMIT EGLE Violation Records

**Argument:** EGLE environmental violation records are public records admissible under MRE 803(8). They directly prove habitability violations under MCL 554.139. No foundation witness required for certified government records.

---

### Motion in Limine No. 2: ADMIT North Morris Operating Company Criminal Conviction

**Argument:** Prior criminal conviction of the property management company is admissible under MRE 404(b) to show pattern, plan, and knowledge of habitability issues. Also admissible for impeachment under MRE 609 if corporate representative testifies.

---

### Motion in Limine No. 3: EXCLUDE "Tenant Fault" Defense Without Expert Testimony

**Argument:** If defendants claim habitability issues were caused by tenants, they must present expert testimony (building inspector, engineer) to establish causation. Lay opinions about causation for structural/environmental issues are inadmissible under MRE 701-702.

## FEDERAL §1983 TRIAL (W.D. Michigan)

### Motion in Limine No. 1: EXCLUDE Judicial Immunity Defense at Trial

**Argument:** Judicial immunity is a legal defense to be resolved on summary judgment, not a factual issue for the jury. If the court has already denied immunity at the MSJ stage, defendants should not be permitted to argue immunity before the jury. *Mireles v Waco*, 502 US 9 (1991).

---

### Motion in Limine No. 2: ADMIT Statistical Evidence of Judicial Bias

**Argument:** Statistical analysis showing 18.26% ex parte rate (3.65x judicial norm) is admissible under FRE 401-403 as relevant to proving pattern of constitutional violations. Expert foundation may be required — Andrew should consider retaining a judicial conduct expert.

---

### Motion in Limine No. 3: ADMIT All State Court Records

**Argument:** State court records (orders, transcripts, filings) are self-authenticating under FRE 902(4) (certified copies of public records). They are admissible as evidence of the constitutional violations alleged.
