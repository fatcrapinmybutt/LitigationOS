## INTRODUCTION & SUMMARY OF ARGUMENT
This appeal challenges a structural failure in the handling of two intertwined family‑division dockets. Father’s 50/50 relationship was reduced to 79 overnights at the July 17, 2024 custody trial, and then effectively to **zero** by an **August 8, 2025 ex parte suspension** that was granted on an **unsworn** motion and repeatedly extended without sworn testimony, best‑interest findings, or clear‑and‑convincing proof of endangerment. When Father complied with a court‑directed **HealthWest** evaluation and **served** it with his objections, the court **refused to admit or weigh** it yet relied on a **“second” evaluation** Father never took and that was never authenticated. Simultaneously, the court discouraged Father’s motion practice (“**Do not file any more. I will not look at it**”), imposed a **bond requirement** without a vexatious‑litigant finding, and used **PPO** show‑causes and contempt to criminalize exchange‑related communications even when police found **no violation**—while failing to sanction documented **gatekeeping** by Mother and her family.

Under *Troxel*, *Santosky*, *Sanders*, *Vodvarka*, *Pierron*, and *Shade*, the Constitution and the Child Custody Act do not allow the **total deprivation** of parenting time on unsworn accusations and **unauthenticated** mental‑health paper. The ex parte order and continuations must be vacated; reliance on the “second” evaluation must be barred absent proper authentication and foundation; the HealthWest report must be admitted and weighed; PPO‑based contempt must be set aside if not proven; findings under the **best‑interest factors** and **MCL 722.27a(3)** must be made; and the matter must be reassigned under **MCR 2.003(C)**.

## STANDARD OF REVIEW
Questions of law and court‑rule interpretation are reviewed **de novo**. Factual findings are reviewed for **clear error**. Parenting‑time decisions are reviewed for **abuse of discretion**. *Fletcher*, 447 Mich at 876–877. Changes that affect the established environment require correct CCA application and adequate findings. *Pierron*, 486 Mich at 85–93; *Shade*, 291 Mich App at 28–31. Due‑process and ex parte procedure questions are reviewed de novo. *Sanders*, 495 Mich at 404–412.

**Preservation.** Father filed timely written objections, moved to terminate the ex parte suspension and for make‑up time, moved for findings (MCR 2.517; 3.210(D)), and attempted to admit HealthWest and other defense exhibits at multiple hearings. Even if any point were deemed unpreserved, review is warranted because the errors are structural and affected fundamental rights.

## ARGUMENT

### Issue I — Ex Parte Suspension Violated MCR 3.207(B) and MCL 722.27a(3)

**Law.** **MCR 3.207(B)(1)** permits ex parte relief only when **specific facts in an affidavit or verified pleading** show **immediate and irreparable** harm from notice delay. **MCL 722.27a(1), (3)** presumes parenting time and demands **clear and convincing** proof that parenting time would **endanger** the child to justify restriction/suspension. Best‑interest findings are required when the environment is altered. *Pierron*; *Shade*; *Lieberman*.

**Application.** The August 8, 2025 motion was **unsworn** and lacked child‑specific immediate‑harm facts. The court granted and then **continued** a total deprivation without sworn testimony or best‑interest findings. That inverts the statute and rule, flipping the presumption against Father.

**Relief.** Vacate the ex parte suspension and continuations; reinstate ordinary parenting time pending an expedited evidentiary hearing with written findings under **MCL 722.23** and **MCL 722.27a(3)**.

---

### Issue II — Unauthenticated “Second” Evaluation vs. HealthWest (MRE 401–403, 702–703, 901; Due Process)

**Law.** **MRE 901** requires authentication; **MRE 702–703** require reliable principles and sufficient facts; **MRE 401–403** require relevance and guard against unfair prejudice. Severe parental‑right restrictions require heightened reliability. *Santosky*; *Yost*.

**Application.** Father complied with HealthWest; the court refused to admit/credit it while **relying** on an **unauthenticated, never‑taken “second” evaluation**. No custodian, author, or expert sponsor testified. That is textbook MRE 901 and 702/703 error, and a due‑process problem given the remedy imposed (total suspension).

**Relief.** Bar reliance on the “second” evaluation absent live authentication and expert foundation; admit and weigh the HealthWest report; if mental‑health proof remains outcome‑determinative, appoint a neutral **MRE 706** expert with full discovery and cross‑examination.

---

### Issue III — Suppression of Defense Evidence & Barriers to Motion Practice (Due Process; MCR 2.517; 3.210(D))

**Law.** Parents have a fundamental liberty interest; due process guarantees a **meaningful opportunity to be heard**. *Troxel*; *Sanders*. Courts must issue **sufficient findings**. **MCR 2.517; 3.210(D)**.

**Application.** The court excluded or ignored HealthWest, AppClose logs, and police clarifications; told Father **“Do not file any more”**; imposed a **bond** without a vexatious‑litigant record; and issued orders **without** child‑specific findings. This denied a fair adversarial process on a fundamental interest.

**Relief.** Vacate orders entered while defense evidence was excluded; remove the bond barrier absent a proper record; require written findings addressing the statutory standards.

---

### Issue IV — PPO Misuse & Contempt (MCR 3.708; Abuse of Discretion)

**Law.** **MCR 3.708** governs PPO contempt; punitive contempt requires proof **beyond a reasonable doubt** and due‑process protections. *Henry*. PPOs do not alter the Child Custody Act framework. *Hunter*.

**Application.** The court maintained a **parallel PPO** track, using show‑causes to criminalize exchange messages, culminating in **jail** and further custody constraints **even when police found no violation**, while documented **withholding/gatekeeping** drew no enforcement. This is asymmetric and distorts the CCA.

**Relief.** Vacate contempt orders lacking proper proof; prohibit the use of PPO practice to bypass **MCL 722.27a**; require balanced enforcement addressing alienation under the CCA.

---

### Issue V — Bias / Appearance of Impropriety (MCR 2.003(C); Cain; Caperton)

**Law.** Disqualification is required where impartiality might **reasonably** be questioned, including where a judge has personal knowledge of disputed evidentiary facts or displays deep‑seated favoritism. **MCR 2.003(C)(1)(a), (c), (f)**; *Cain*; *Caperton*.

**Application.** Ex parte/chambers routing of HealthWest; reliance on an unauthenticated “second” eval; a directive **not to file**; selective PPO/custody enforcement; stigmatizing mental‑health comments before any neutral expert—all create at least the **appearance of bias** and a constitutionally intolerable **probability of actual bias**.

**Relief.** Disqualify and reassign on remand; require a fresh evidentiary hearing before a different judge.

---

### Issue VI — Best‑Interests and Vodvarka Threshold Were Ignored (MCL 722.23; Vodvarka)

**Law.** **Vodvarka** requires **proper cause or change of circumstances** before modifying custody/parenting time in a way that alters the established environment. Courts must analyze and weigh each relevant **MCL 722.23** factor and make findings.

**Application.** The path from **50/50 → 79 → near‑zero** occurred without a threshold **Vodvarka** analysis and without factor‑by‑factor findings. Factor **(j)** (willingness to facilitate) weighs heavily for Father given written **gatekeeping** and trespass threats from maternal family and Father’s compliant AppClose communications.

**Relief.** At minimum, remand for a full **Vodvarka** and **MCL 722.23** analysis with written findings; appoint a neutral **GAL** if needed to protect the child’s independent interests.

---

## HARMLESS‑ERROR ANALYSIS
These errors are not harmless. The ex parte suspension and continuations **removed** a parent for months, eroding the bond and altering the environment. The unauthenticated “second” evaluation, exclusion of HealthWest and other defense exhibits, and barriers to motion practice **shaped the outcome**. Substantial rights were affected; reversal or, at minimum, a new hearing before a neutral judge is required.

## CONSOLIDATION / COORDINATION ON REMAND
