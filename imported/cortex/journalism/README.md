## Why CORTEX for Investigative Journalism?

Every major investigation — from the Panama Papers to the Pandora Papers — was built on the same foundation: **connecting entities across documents, records, and datasets to reveal hidden relationships.** That work has traditionally required either a team of 30 data journalists with custom scripts, or enterprise tools costing $10,000+ per seat.

## 💡 Use Cases

### Source Verification & Corroboration
Map your source network with compartmented anonymization. Track which sources corroborate which claims. Build a verification chain that satisfies editorial standards without exposing identities. CORTEX keeps it all local — no cloud, no metadata leaks.

### Corporate Ownership Tracing
Follow the money through nested LLCs, offshore holding companies, and nominee director structures. Import secretary of state filings, SEC EDGAR data, and FOIA responses to build the complete beneficial ownership chain that connects a shell company to the real humans behind it.

### Public Records Analysis
Bulk-import FOIA productions, court records, property deeds, and government datasets. CORTEX auto-extracts entities and maps relationships, turning thousands of pages of documents into an interactive investigation graph. Track FOIA request status, appeal deadlines, and exemption patterns across agencies.

### Campaign Finance Network Mapping
Import FEC filing data to visualize donor networks, bundler relationships, and dark money flows. Trace how PAC money moves through multiple committees before reaching candidates. Identify straw donor patterns and coordination between supposedly independent expenditure groups.

### Leak Analysis & Document Authentication
When a source provides documents, build the metadata chain: who created them, when, what systems touched them, what's been redacted, what's consistent with authentic documents from the same source. All analysis stays air-gapped on your machine.

### Cross-Border Investigation Coordination
Working with ICIJ partners or foreign correspondents? Export sanitized subgraphs that share investigation structure without exposing sources. Each journalist maintains their own local CORTEX instance — no shared cloud infrastructure for adversaries to target.

## 🔒 Source Protection by Design

CORTEX was designed with a principle that matters to journalists above all else: **your investigation data never leaves your machine.**

- **Zero telemetry** — no usage analytics, no crash reports, no phone-home
- **Zero cloud** — no sync, no backup, no API calls to external services
- **Zero network** — runs fully air-gapped; pull the ethernet cable and it works identically
- **Local-only storage** — your investigation graph is a file on your disk, not rows in someone else's database
- **No account required** — no login, no email, no registration that creates a record connecting you to the tool

This isn't just a privacy feature. It's a **legal shield**. When your investigation graph exists only on your encrypted laptop, it's protected by reporter's privilege and the First Amendment. When it's on a SaaS platform's server, it's one subpoena away from disclosure.
