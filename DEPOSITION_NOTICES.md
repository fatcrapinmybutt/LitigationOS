## GOVERNING AUTHORITY

- **MCR 2.306** — Depositions Upon Oral Examination
- **MCR 2.306(B)(1)** — A party desiring to take the deposition of any person upon oral examination shall give **reasonable notice in writing** to every other party to the action. The notice must state the **time and place** for taking the deposition and the **name and address** of each person to be examined, if known, or a general description sufficient to identify the person or class of persons.
- **MCR 2.306(B)(5)** — The notice to a party deponent may be accompanied by a request made in compliance with **MCR 2.310** (Request for Production of Documents) for the production of documents and tangible things at the taking of the deposition (**duces tecum**).
- **Minimum Notice Period:** At least **7 days** before the deposition date (reasonable notice standard).

### Pro Se Tips for Conducting Depositions
1. **Prepare an outline, not a script.** Organize by topic area. Listen to the answer before asking the next question.
2. **Start with background questions** to establish rapport and lock in basic facts.
3. **Use short, simple questions.** One fact per question. Avoid compound questions.
4. **Do not argue with the witness.** Your job is to collect testimony, not to persuade.
5. **Pin down specifics:** dates, times, who was present, exact words used.
6. **Use "Tell me everything" questions** to exhaust the witness's knowledge on a topic before moving on.
7. **Bring copies of all exhibits** you plan to reference. Mark them as deposition exhibits.
8. **Record exact quotes from documents** — read them into the record and ask the witness to confirm.
9. **Always close with:** "Is there anything else you would like to add?" and "Have your answers been truthful and complete?"
10. **Objections:** If opposing counsel objects, the witness still answers unless instructed not to (MCR 2.306(D)(1)). Note: as a pro se party you may face an attorney — remain calm and professional.
