## III. BEST INTEREST ANALYSIS — MCL 722.23

The Child Custody Act requires consideration of 12 factors. The evidence supports Plaintiff on every factor:

### (a) Love, Affection, Emotional Ties
Andrew has demonstrated consistent love and desire for relationship with L.D.W. despite 569+ days of forced separation. He has filed multiple motions, maintained support payments, and documented every attempt at contact. Watson, by contrast, has actively destroyed the emotional bond through alienation.

### (b) Capacity to Give Love, Affection, Guidance
Andrew has no criminal record related to parenting. All investigations found zero concerns. He was an engaged parent when permitted access. Watson has demonstrated incapacity by coaching the child against the father and using the child as a weapon in litigation.

### (c) Capacity to Provide Food, Clothing, Medical Care
Andrew has maintained employment (when not jailed) and paid $1,056.60/month in support. His capacity is demonstrated by his financial commitment even while denied all contact.

### (f) Moral Fitness
Watson's pattern of false allegations, PPO abuse, and alienation reflects on moral fitness. Andrew's record shows no moral fitness concerns. All investigations cleared him.

## IV. CHANGED CIRCUMSTANCES — MCL 722.27

Under *Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499 (2003), modification requires proper cause OR change of circumstances. Both exist here:

**Proper Cause:** Watson's sustained alienation campaign constitutes proper cause as it directly impacts the child's best interests.

**Changed Circumstances:** 569+ days of denied contact, 59 days of wrongful incarceration, and 305 interference incidents since the current order constitute changed circumstances of sufficient magnitude.

## V. PARENTING TIME RESTORATION — MCL 722.27a
