## OVERVIEW

Jury instructions are read to the jury before deliberation. Michigan uses the **Michigan Model Civil Jury Instructions (M Civ JI)**. Federal cases use **Sixth Circuit Pattern Jury Instructions**. Proposed instructions should be submitted 7-14 days before trial (check local rules).

**Which cases get jury trials?**
- Housing (Lane B): YES — right to jury trial on damages claims
- Federal §1983: YES — right to jury trial under 7th Amendment
- Custody (Lane A): NO — bench trial (family law, no jury right in Michigan)

## PART 1: HOUSING CASE — State Court (2025-002760-CZ)

### Instruction 1: Burden of Proof (M Civ JI 8.01)
"Plaintiff has the burden of proving his claims by a preponderance of the evidence. Preponderance of the evidence means that you must be persuaded that it is more likely than not that the claims are true."

### Instruction 2: Credibility of Witnesses (M Civ JI 2.06)
"You are the sole judges of the credibility of the witnesses and the weight to be given their testimony. In judging credibility, you may consider the witness's interest in the outcome, bias, demeanor, consistency of testimony, and whether the testimony is corroborated by other evidence."

### Instruction 3: Implied Warranty of Habitability (M Civ JI 65.31 — modified)
"Under Michigan law, MCL 554.139, every landlord covenants that the premises and all common areas are fit for their intended use and will be kept in reasonable repair during the term of the lease. This warranty cannot be waived. If you find that defendants failed to maintain the premises in a condition fit for use as a residence, you should find for plaintiff on this claim."

### Instruction 4: Michigan Consumer Protection Act (M Civ JI 128.01 — modified)
"The Michigan Consumer Protection Act, MCL 445.903, prohibits unfair, unconscionable, or deceptive methods, acts, or practices in trade or commerce. If you find that defendants engaged in such practices, you must award plaintiff actual damages. The statute also permits the court to award treble damages, meaning three times the actual damages, plus attorney fees."

### Instruction 5: Fraud/Misrepresentation (M Civ JI 128.04)
"To prove fraud, plaintiff must show: (1) defendant made a material representation, (2) the representation was false, (3) defendant knew it was false or made it recklessly without knowing whether it was true, (4) defendant intended plaintiff to rely on it, (5) plaintiff actually relied on it, and (6) plaintiff was damaged as a result."

### Instruction 6: Negligence — Premises Liability (M Civ JI 19.01)
"A landlord has a duty to exercise reasonable care in maintaining the premises. If you find that defendants failed to exercise reasonable care and that this failure caused plaintiff's injuries, you should find for plaintiff on this claim."

### Instruction 7: Damages — Compensatory (M Civ JI 50.01)
"If you find for plaintiff, you must determine the amount of damages. Compensatory damages are intended to make plaintiff whole — to put him in the position he would have been in but for defendants' wrongful conduct. You may award damages for: rent overcharges, property damage, relocation costs, emotional distress, and any other proven losses."

### Instruction 8: Multiple Defendants — Joint and Several (M Civ JI 11.01)
"If you find that more than one defendant is liable, each defendant is responsible for the full amount of damages. This is known as joint and several liability. Plaintiff may collect the entire judgment from any one or more defendants."

### Instruction 9: Corporate Liability (M Civ JI 120.02 — modified)
"A corporation acts through its officers, directors, and employees. If you find that individual defendants acted within the scope of their authority for the corporate defendants, the corporations are liable for their actions."

## PART 2: FEDERAL §1983 CASE (W.D. Michigan)

### Instruction 1: Section 1983 (6th Cir. Pattern 13.01)
"Section 1983 of Title 42 of the United States Code provides that any person who, under color of state law, deprives another of any rights secured by the Constitution shall be liable to the injured person. Plaintiff must prove: (1) the defendant acted under color of state law, and (2) the defendant deprived plaintiff of a right secured by the Constitution."

### Instruction 2: Due Process — Parental Rights (6th Cir. Pattern 13.04 — modified)
"The Fourteenth Amendment protects the fundamental right of parents to the care, custody, and control of their children. The government may not deprive a person of this right without due process of law. Due process requires, at minimum, notice and an opportunity to be heard before a significant deprivation of parental rights."

### Instruction 3: Equal Protection (6th Cir. Pattern 13.05)
"The Fourteenth Amendment guarantees equal protection of the laws. If you find that defendant treated plaintiff differently from similarly situated persons without rational basis, and that this different treatment was intentional, you should find a violation of equal protection."

### Instruction 4: Punitive Damages (6th Cir. Pattern 14.03)
"If you find that defendant's conduct was motivated by evil intent or involved reckless or callous indifference to plaintiff's constitutional rights, you may award punitive damages. Punitive damages are intended to punish the wrongdoer and to deter others from similar conduct."

### Instruction 5: Compensatory Damages — Constitutional Injury (6th Cir. Pattern 14.01)
"If you find for plaintiff, you must determine compensatory damages. For constitutional violations, you may award damages for: emotional distress, humiliation, loss of liberty, loss of parental relationship, and any other proven harm flowing from the constitutional deprivation."

### Instruction 6: Conspiracy Under § 1983 (6th Cir. Pattern 13.08)
"To prove a conspiracy to deprive plaintiff of constitutional rights, plaintiff must show: (1) an agreement between two or more persons, (2) to deprive plaintiff of constitutional rights, and (3) an overt act in furtherance of the conspiracy that caused injury to plaintiff."

## PART 3: SPECIAL INSTRUCTIONS TO REQUEST

### Adverse Inference Instruction
"If a party fails to produce evidence within its control, you may infer that the evidence would have been unfavorable to that party."
*Use when: Watson fails to produce communications, Housing defendants fail to produce financial records*

### Spoliation Instruction
"If you find that a party destroyed or failed to preserve evidence that it knew or should have known was relevant to this case, you may infer that the evidence would have been unfavorable to that party."
*Use when: evidence of destruction of communications, maintenance records, etc.*

### Pro Se Party Instruction
"Plaintiff is representing himself without an attorney. You should not hold this against him or give his case less weight because he does not have an attorney."
