## Relevant Canon Violations

**Canon 3(B)(1) — Faithful to the Law:** A judge shall be faithful to the law and maintain professional competence in it. Hon. McNeill had a duty to scrutinize ex parte submissions, particularly in a case with extensive prior litigation history and nine prior police contacts yielding zero arrests. Granting sweeping ex parte relief the day after a police report was filed — without examining the circumstances or providing notice to Father — violated this duty.

**Canon 3(B)(7) — Ex Parte Communications:** A judge shall not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communications. The submissions acted upon were themselves the product of a premeditated scheme, as documented in the police report. The court was used as an instrument of the scheme.

**Canon 2(A) — Appearance of Impropriety:** Acting on orchestrated ex parte filings without any independent verification creates the appearance that the court was a willing participant in the deprivation of Father's rights.
