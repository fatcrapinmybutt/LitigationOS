# EXHIBIT E-3: HealthWest Evaluation — Court-Ordered Assessment Excluded from Record

**Bates Range:** PIGORS-E-3
**Filing:** Formal Complaint to Judicial Tenure Commission
**Respondent:** Hon. Jenny L. McNeill (P58235)
**Case:** Pigors v. Watson, No. 2024-001507-DC, 14th Circuit Court

---

## Description

This exhibit documents Hon. Jenny L. McNeill's exclusion of a court-ordered HealthWest mental health evaluation from the record in Pigors v. Watson. The evaluation was ordered by the court itself, completed by a licensed clinician, and returned results showing Father was fit: LOCUS Score 12 (Level One — community services only), Psychosis 0, Substance Abuse 0, Danger to Self/Others 0. Despite these results directly contradicting the allegations used to justify the suspension of Father's parenting time, Hon. McNeill excluded the evaluation from the record and continued to rely on the unsubstantiated allegations. This exhibit also documents FOC Supervisor Pamela Rusco's contact with the HealthWest clinician on October 29, 2025 — the day before a hearing — raising concerns about improper influence.

## Relevant Canon Violations

**Canon 3(B)(1) — Faithful to the Law:** A judge shall be faithful to the law and maintain professional competence in it. Excluding a court-ordered evaluation that directly contradicts the factual basis for the court's own orders violates the judge's duty to consider all relevant evidence. The Michigan Rules of Evidence (MRE 702, 703) require consideration of expert testimony that is relevant and reliable.

**Canon 3(B)(5) — Performance of Judicial Duties Without Bias:** A judge shall perform judicial duties without bias or prejudice. Excluding evidence favorable to Father while crediting unsubstantiated allegations from Mother demonstrates outcome-determinative bias.

**Canon 2(A) — Appearance of Impropriety:** The exclusion of exculpatory evidence — particularly a court-ordered evaluation — creates the appearance that the court predetermined the outcome regardless of the evidence.

## Key Evidence

### HealthWest CANS Assessment Results

The HealthWest evaluation, conducted pursuant to the court's own order, included a Comprehensive Assessment of Needs and Strengths (CANS). The results were as follows:

| Domain | Score | Interpretation |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| LOCUS Score | 12 | Level One — Community Services (least restrictive) |
| Psychosis (Thought Disorder) | 0 | No evidence of psychosis |
| Substance Abuse | 0 | No evidence of substance abuse |
| Danger to Self | 0 | No danger to self |
| Danger to Others | 0 | No danger to others |
| Depression | Assessed | Within normal parameters |
| Anxiety | Assessed | Within normal parameters |
| Anger Control | Assessed | Within normal parameters |
| Interpersonal Problems | Assessed | Within normal parameters |

These results directly contradict every allegation used by Emily A. Watson to justify the suspension of Father's parenting time:

- **Allegation: Father is suicidal** → LOCUS Score 12, Danger to Self 0
- **Allegation: Father has substance abuse issues** → Substance Abuse 0
- **Allegation: Father is dangerous** → Danger to Others 0
- **Allegation: Father has psychosis** → Psychosis 0

### The Court's Own Order Required the Evaluation

Hon. McNeill ordered Father to obtain a mental health assessment. The court's order stated:

> "IT IS HEREBY ORDERED that the Plaintiff, Andrew Pigors, shall obtain a mental health assessment and provide it to the Court."

Father complied. The assessment was completed. The results were favorable. Hon. McNeill then excluded the results from the record — effectively punishing Father for complying with the court's own order and obtaining a favorable result.

### FOC Contact with HealthWest Clinician — October 29, 2025

FOC Supervisor Pamela Rusco contacted the HealthWest clinician on October 29, 2025 — the day before a scheduled hearing. This contact raises serious concerns:

1. Rusco's office is located at 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon — the same address as the office of Cavan Berry, Hon. McNeill's spouse
2. The contact occurred the day before a hearing, suggesting an attempt to influence the clinician's testimony or findings
3. No notice of this contact was provided to Father

### Evidentiary Impact

The exclusion of the HealthWest evaluation left the record devoid of any professional assessment of Father's mental health. The only "evidence" remaining was the unsubstantiated allegations of Emily A. Watson — allegations contradicted by nine police contacts yielding zero arrests, zero charges, and an ER toxicology screen negative for the alleged arsenic poisoning.

## Significance

This exhibit establishes that Hon. McNeill excluded the single most relevant piece of evidence in the case — a court-ordered professional evaluation that cleared Father on every dimension alleged to justify the suspension of his parenting time. The exclusion cannot be explained by any legitimate evidentiary ruling: the evaluation was ordered by the court, completed by a licensed clinician, and directly relevant to the dispositive issues. Combined with Pamela Rusco's day-before-hearing contact with the HealthWest clinician, this exhibit supports the conclusion that the court was not seeking truth but was instead engineering a predetermined outcome.
