## ORDER GRANTING PETITION FOR SUPERINTENDING CONTROL

At a session of the Michigan Supreme Court, held at Lansing, Michigan, on _________________, 2026.

PRESENT: _______________________________________________

The Court, having reviewed Petitioner Andrew James Pigors's Petition for Superintending Control pursuant to MCR 7.306, the supporting brief, affidavit, and exhibits, and being otherwise fully advised in the premises:

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED** that:

1. The Petition for Superintending Control is **GRANTED**.

2. All orders entered by the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill in Case No. 2024-001507-DC (14th Circuit Court, Muskegon County) on and after August 8, 2025, are **VACATED** as having been entered in violation of Petitioner's due process rights under the Michigan and United States Constitutions.

3. The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill is **DISQUALIFIED** from further proceedings in Case No. 2024-001507-DC and Case No. 2023-5907-PP pursuant to MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b).

4. Case No. 2024-001507-DC is **REASSIGNED** to a judge from outside the 14th Judicial Circuit, to be selected by the State Court Administrative Office.

5. Pending reassignment, the ex parte order of August 8-9, 2025 suspending Petitioner's parenting time is **IMMEDIATELY STAYED**, and the parenting time schedule established by the April 29, 2024 consent order is **REINSTATED**.

6. The 14th Circuit Court is **DIRECTED** to transmit the complete case file, including all sealed and restricted records, to this Court within fourteen (14) days.
