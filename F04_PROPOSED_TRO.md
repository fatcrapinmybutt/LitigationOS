## TEMPORARY RESTRAINING ORDER

THIS MATTER having come before the Court on Plaintiff's Motion for Temporary Restraining Order and Preliminary Injunction pursuant to Fed. R. Civ. P. 65, and the Court having reviewed the Complaint, supporting affidavit, and exhibits, and finding that:

(a) Plaintiff is likely to succeed on the merits of his claims under 42 U.S.C. Section 1983;
(b) Plaintiff will suffer irreparable harm absent injunctive relief, including continued deprivation of his fundamental right to the care, custody, and companionship of his minor child L.D.W.;
(c) The balance of equities tips in Plaintiff's favor; and
(d) An injunction is in the public interest;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED** that:

1. Defendant Emily A. Watson is **TEMPORARILY RESTRAINED** from interfering with Plaintiff's constitutionally protected parenting time with L.D.W. pending further order of this Court.

2. The parenting time schedule established by the April 29, 2024 consent order of the 14th Circuit Court is **REINSTATED** on a temporary basis pending hearing on Plaintiff's Motion for Preliminary Injunction.

3. This Order shall remain in effect for fourteen (14) days from the date of entry, unless extended by the Court for good cause pursuant to Fed. R. Civ. P. 65(b)(2).

4. A hearing on Plaintiff's Motion for Preliminary Injunction is scheduled for _________________, 2026 at _________ a.m./p.m.

5. Plaintiff shall post security in the amount of $__________ pursuant to Fed. R. Civ. P. 65(c), or the Court waives the security requirement upon finding that Plaintiff proceeds in forma pauperis.
