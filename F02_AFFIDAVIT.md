### I. IDENTITY AND CAPACITY

1. I am the Plaintiff in this action. I am a citizen of the State of Michigan, currently residing at 1977 Whitehall Road, Lot 17, North Muskegon, Michigan 49445. I am over eighteen years of age and competent to testify to the matters set forth herein.

2. I am the father of a minor child, L.D.W. (DOB: November 9, 2022). I make this affidavit in support of my Complaint for violations of the Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. § 1983, and related state-law claims.

3. The facts stated herein are within my personal knowledge, or are based upon review of documents in my possession, or are stated upon information and belief as specifically noted.

---

### II. HOME PURCHASE AND RESIDENCY

4. On or about February 2, 2022, I purchased a manufactured home at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Park, 1977 Whitehall Road, Muskegon County, Michigan. Title was transferred to me by Shady Oaks MHP LLC through Joe Matyk. I have the original title in my possession.

5. At the time of purchase, my monthly lot rent was approximately $395.00 per month. I paid rent timely and maintained the home in good condition.

6. I resided at the home with my minor son, L.D.W., during my parenting time. The home was the primary location where I exercised my custodial rights.

7. I invested substantial time, labor, and money into remodeling and improving the home, including interior repairs and upgrades. This increased the home's fair market value.

---

### III. COERCED LEASE AND RENT INCREASES

8. Beginning in or about November 2023, Defendants began a campaign of rent increases. My lot rent was raised from $395.00 to approximately $695.00 — an increase of approximately 76% — with no corresponding improvement in park services, no proper notice as required by Michigan law, and no justification.

9. On or about March 26, 2024, I was presented with a new lease and coerced under threat of eviction into signing it. The signing occurred off-site at a location associated with "Cricklewood," another entity in the corporate family. I signed under duress because I was told that if I did not sign, I would be evicted.

10. By mid-2025, my rent had been increased to approximately $720.00 per month — an increase of approximately 82% over the original $395.00.

11. I later learned that Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC had been dissolved under New Jersey law in approximately 2022, meaning the entity that executed the lease lacked legal authority to contract.

---

### IV. LEDGER MANIPULATION AND FINANCIAL FRAUD

12. Over the course of my tenancy, I received or was shown at least three different versions of my billing ledger, each showing materially different balances, charges, and payment histories. The ledgers were irreconcilable — they could not all be accurate.

13. At a civil disposition hearing in the 60th District Court, the presiding judge stated on the record that my account was paid in full through December 2024. Despite this judicial finding, Defendants never provided a corrected ledger and continued to assert that I owed arrears.

14. I issued a check for $1,300.26 payable to "Shady Oaks MHP." I later discovered that this check was endorsed and cashed by "Partridge Securities for Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC." I was never informed that Partridge Securities would receive my payment. I did not authorize this entity to cash my check.

15. Upon information and belief, after forensic accounting of the ledger discrepancies, the corrected balance shows a credit in my favor of approximately $2,280.55.

---

### V. THE JULY 17, 2024 LOCKOUT

16. On July 17, 2024, I was required to appear at a custody trial in the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, Case No. 2024-001507-DC, before Hon. Jenny L. McNeill. I had no choice but to attend — failure to appear would have resulted in a default judgment against me in the custody case.

17. On that same day — while I was in court — agents of Defendants entered my manufactured home at Lot 17, drilled the locks, and removed my personal property and belongings. A "FREE" sign was placed on my possessions, inviting members of the public to take my property.

18. I was not given any notice prior to the lockout. I was not served with any eviction writ personally. No valid court order authorized the removal of my titled manufactured home or my personal property from the lot.

19. When I discovered what had occurred, my home had been ransacked. Personal belongings including household furniture, kitchen appliances, clothing, tools, personal documents, electronics, and my son's toys and clothing had been removed, destroyed, or stolen by third parties who responded to the "FREE" sign.

20. The timing of the lockout — on the exact day of my custody trial — was either a deliberate act of coordination or a calculated exploitation of my known absence. Either way, it deprived me of any opportunity to protect my property.

---

### VI. WATER SHUTOFF AND HABITABILITY

21. At various times during my tenancy, Defendants shut off water service to my unit without prior notice and without a valid court order. The water shutoff made the home uninhabitable.

22. Under Michigan law, a landlord may not shut off utilities as a means of self-help eviction. The water shutoff constituted constructive eviction.

---

### VII. SEWAGE CONTAMINATION AND EGLE VIOLATIONS

23. Beginning in late 2024, I observed raw sewage overflowing from an interceptor tank near my lot. Black, foul-smelling sludge pooled on the ground surface near Lot 17 and on adjacent vacant Lot 21.

24. On or about March 7, 2025, I reported the sewage contamination to the Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy (EGLE).

25. EGLE inspected the park on or about March 20, 2025, and rated the park "NOT IN COMPLIANCE." On April 15, 2025, EGLE issued Violation Notice VN-017235, citing violations including illegal discharge of sewage onto the ground surface and failure to maintain the wastewater system.

26. Despite the active sewage contamination and EGLE violation, Defendants continued to bill me for water and sewer service. I received no credit, abatement, or remediation.

27. After I reported the sewage violation, Defendants escalated their harassment and eviction threats against me. I believe this was retaliation for my report to EGLE.

---

### VIII. FALSE "ABANDONMENT" CLAIMS AND SALE SABOTAGE

28. After the July 2024 lockout, I attempted to sell my manufactured home on at least three separate occasions. I identified willing buyers and negotiated sale terms.

29. On each occasion, Defendant Partridge or other agents of the park interfered with the sale by telling prospective buyers that I had "abandoned" the property, that I "did not own" the home, and that the park would sell the home through "their legal team."

30. These statements were false. I held valid title to the home. I had not abandoned the property — I was locked out by Defendants and subsequently incarcerated on contempt orders entered in the custody case, which made it physically impossible for me to access the property.

31. As a result of Defendants' interference, all three prospective sales fell through. I lost the ability to recover my equity in the home, which I estimate to be between $60,000 and $105,000.

---

### IX. INCARCERATION AND INABILITY TO DEFEND HOUSING RIGHTS

32. Between November 2024 and early 2026, I was incarcerated for a total of 59 days on contempt-of-court orders entered by Hon. Jenny L. McNeill in the custody case. The basis for the contempt findings included sending birthday messages to my son via AppClose — a court-approved communication platform.

33. My incarceration caused me to lose two jobs and two homes. While I was in jail, I was unable to attend court hearings in my housing case, unable to access my manufactured home, and unable to prevent Defendants from destroying my property or interfering with sales.

34. Upon information and belief, the state court housing case (Case No. 2025-002760-CZ) was assigned to Chief Judge Kenneth Hoopes of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court. Judge Hoopes is the former law partner of Judge McNeill from the firm of Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill. Judge Hoopes dismissed my housing case without an evidentiary hearing and without addressing my objections.

---

### X. DISSOLVED ENTITY AND CORPORATE SHELL STRUCTURE

35. Upon information and belief, Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC was dissolved under New Jersey law in approximately 2022. Despite its dissolution, the entity continued to operate in Michigan — filing court actions, executing leases, and collecting rent.

36. Upon information and belief, Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC, Homes of America LLC, Cricklewood MHP LLC, VRM Capital Corp, and Partridge Equity Group / Partridge Securities are all alter egos of one another, controlled by Defendant Alden Global Capital LLC.

37. Another entity in Alden Global Capital's manufactured housing portfolio — North Morris Estates LLC in New Jersey — was criminally convicted for operating an unlicensed mobile home park. This demonstrates a pattern of willful regulatory noncompliance across the enterprise.
