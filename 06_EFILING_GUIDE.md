**Lane:** F4 (42 USC §1983 Civil Rights Action)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **System** | CM/ECF (NextGen) |
| **URL** | <https://www.miwd.uscourts.gov/electronic-filing> |
| **Filing Fee** | $405.00 (or file IFP — see Section 4) |
| **Format** | PDF only — no Word, no scanned images of text |
| **Pro Se Handbook** | <https://www.miwd.uscourts.gov/pro-se-forms> ("Filing Your Lawsuit in Federal Court") |

#### Step-by-Step Registration & Filing

1. **Create a PACER Account**
   - Go to: <https://pacer.uscourts.gov/register-account/non-attorney-filers-cmecf>
   - Select "Register for a Non-Attorney Filer Account"
   - Complete the registration form with your full legal name, address, phone, and email
   - You will receive a PACER login and password by email

2. **Complete the Pro Se E-Filing Application**
   - Download the "Pro Se Application for Electronic Filing and Service" from:
     <https://www.miwd.uscourts.gov/pro-se-forms>
   - Fill out completely — include your PACER login name
   - Submit the application to the Clerk's Office (can be emailed or mailed)

3. **Wait for Judge Approval**
   - The assigned judge must approve your e-filing privileges
   - This can take 1–2 weeks
   - You will receive email confirmation when approved
   - Until approved, you may file paper copies at the Clerk's Office:
     **Gerald R. Ford Federal Building, 110 Michigan Street NW, Grand Rapids, MI 49503**

4. **File Through CM/ECF**
   - Log into CM/ECF at <https://ecf.miwd.uscourts.gov/>
   - Select "Civil" → "File a Complaint" (for initial filing)
   - Upload your complaint as a single PDF
   - Upload exhibits as separate PDF attachments
   - Pay the filing fee online ($405) OR upload IFP application (Form AO 240)
   - System generates a Notice of Electronic Filing (NEF) — save this as your proof

5. **Subsequent Filings**
   - Log in → select appropriate event type (Motion, Brief, Notice, etc.)
   - Attach PDF documents
   - CM/ECF automatically serves all registered parties via email

#### Federal Filing Requirements

- **Page limits:** Check Local Rules (W.D. Mich. LCivR) — briefs generally 25 pages
- **Font:** 14-point for proportional fonts, 12-point for monospaced
- **Margins:** 1 inch on all sides
- **Page numbers:** Bottom center of every page
- **Caption:** Must include case number once assigned
- **Certificate of Service:** Required on every filing
- **Signature:** Type "/s/ Andrew James Pigors" on the signature line

#### Key Federal Forms (available at <https://www.miwd.uscourts.gov/pro-se-forms>)

| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Complaint (Pro Se) | Initial civil rights complaint |
| AO 240 | Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis |
| AO 440 | Summons in a Civil Action |
| Civil Cover Sheet (JS 44) | Required with every new complaint |
| Consent/Decline Form | Consent to magistrate judge |

#### Step-by-Step Registration & Filing

1. **Register for MiFILE**
   - Go to: <https://mifile.courts.michigan.gov/register>
   - Select **"I will file to a case where I am representing myself (pro se)"**
   - Enter your full legal name, mailing address, phone number, and email
   - Create a password — this is your permanent MiFILE account
   - **One account covers both MSC and COA** — you do not need separate registrations

2. **Initiate a New Case**
   - Log into MiFILE
   - Select "File to a New Case"
   - Choose "Michigan Supreme Court" as the court
   - Select the appropriate filing type (e.g., "Complaint for Superintending Control")
   - Complete the case information form (parties, lower court case numbers)

3. **Upload Documents**
   - Upload your primary filing as a PDF
   - Attach exhibits as separate PDFs (label each clearly)
   - Add a Certificate of Service as a separate document
   - Attach IFP application if requesting fee waiver

4. **Submit and Pay**
   - Review all documents before submitting
   - Pay filing fee online (credit/debit card) or submit IFP
   - MiFILE generates a confirmation receipt — **save this immediately**
   - The filing is timestamped when you click "Submit"

5. **Monitor Your Case**
   - Log into MiFILE to check case status
   - You will receive email notifications for filings and orders
   - Download all court orders promptly

#### MSC-Specific Requirements

- **MCR 7.300 et seq.** governs MSC proceedings
- **Proof of Service:** Required on every document filed
- **Copies:** MiFILE handles distribution — no physical copies needed for e-filed documents
- **Original jurisdiction complaints** require specific factual allegations showing why the MSC should act

### Instructions for Using This Template

1. **Fill in all bracketed sections** — Replace every `[bracket]` with your actual information. Delete instructional text.

2. **Be thorough but objective** — The settled statement must be a fair summary. The opposing party will have a chance to object, and the trial court will resolve disputes. Do not editorialize — state what happened factually.

3. **Include EVERY relevant hearing** — Any hearing that relates to the issues you are raising on appeal must be included. Omitting a hearing weakens your record.

4. **Cross-reference your evidence** — When describing exhibits or evidence discussed at hearings, reference the specific exhibit numbers you will use in your appellate appendix.

5. **Filing sequence:**
   - File the Proposed Settled Statement with the **trial court** (14th Circuit Court) AND the **COA** (via MiFILE under Case 366810)
   - Serve on Emily A. Watson (or her counsel) by certified mail
   - Emily has the right to file objections or proposed amendments
   - The trial court then settles any disputes and certifies the statement
   - The certified statement becomes the official record for your appeal

6. **Deadline coordination:**
   - The settled statement process takes time — file EARLY
   - Coordinate with your appellate brief deadline
   - If the brief deadline arrives before the statement is certified, file a motion to extend the briefing schedule in the COA

7. **Trial court's role:**
   - Under MCR 7.210(B)(2), the trial court settles the statement
   - The trial court may hold a hearing to resolve disputes
   - Once the trial court certifies the statement, it replaces the transcript

## SECTION 4: IFP (IN FORMA PAUPERIS) GUIDANCE

In Forma Pauperis (IFP) status allows you to file without paying court fees if you demonstrate financial hardship.

---

### A. Federal IFP — Form AO 240 (28 USC §1915)

**Statute:** 28 USC §1915(a)(1) — "any court of the United States may authorize the commencement, prosecution or defense of any suit, action or proceeding... without prepayment of fees."

**Form:** AO 240 — "Application to Proceed in District Court Without Prepaying Fees or Costs"

**Where to get it:** <https://www.uscourts.gov/forms/fee-waiver-forms/application-proceed-district-court-without-prepaying-fees-or-costs>

#### Financial Information Required on Form AO 240

The form requires you to disclose:

| Category | What to Provide |
|----------|----------------|
| **Employment** | Current employer, monthly income (gross and net), pay frequency |
| **Other Income** | Government benefits, child support received, disability, pensions, etc. |
| **Spouse Income** | Spouse/partner income (if applicable) |
| **Cash & Bank Accounts** | Checking, savings, and any other accounts — exact balances |
| **Real Property** | Any real estate owned and its estimated value |
| **Motor Vehicles** | Any vehicles owned and their estimated value |
| **Other Assets** | Stocks, bonds, valuable personal property |
| **Monthly Expenses** | Rent/mortgage, utilities, food, transportation, medical, child care, etc. |
| **Dependents** | Names (initials for minors), ages, relationship |
| **Debts** | Outstanding debts and monthly payment obligations |
| **Prior IFP** | Whether you've previously been granted IFP status |

#### Federal IFP Process

1. Complete Form AO 240 honestly and thoroughly
2. Sign under penalty of perjury
3. File it WITH your complaint (do not file separately)
4. The judge reviews your application
5. If granted, the court waives the $405 filing fee
6. **Bonus under 28 USC §1915(d):** If IFP is granted, the court will order the U.S. Marshal to serve the defendant at no cost to you (see Section 6)

> **⚠️ WARNING:** Providing false information on Form AO 240 is a federal offense.
> Be completely truthful. If your financial situation is borderline, explain the
> hardship in an attached declaration.

---

### B. State IFP — MCR 2.002 (Michigan Courts — MSC and COA)

**Court Rule:** MCR 2.002 — "Waiver and Suspension of Fees and Costs"

**Applies to:** Michigan Supreme Court, Michigan Court of Appeals, and all Michigan trial courts

#### MCR 2.002 Requirements

Under MCR 2.002(B), the court shall waive fees if the party:

1. Is currently receiving public assistance (e.g., FIP, SSI, Medicaid, food assistance), **OR**
2. Has an income at or below 125% of the federal poverty guidelines, **OR**
3. Is unable to pay fees because of other factors (extraordinary expenses, medical costs, etc.)

#### What to File

**Option 1 — Receiving Public Assistance:**
- File a verified statement identifying the type of public assistance you receive
- Attach documentation (benefit letter, EBT card copy, Medicaid card, etc.)
- Fee waiver is essentially automatic if you prove current public assistance

**Option 2 — Income-Based:**
- File a verified statement including:
  - Your monthly income from all sources
  - Your monthly expenses (rent, utilities, food, medical, transportation, etc.)
  - Number of dependents
  - Bank account balances
  - Outstanding debts
- The court applies the 125% poverty guideline test

**Option 3 — Other Financial Hardship:**
- File a verified statement explaining the specific hardship
- Provide supporting documentation
- The court has discretion to waive fees based on the totality of circumstances

### Evidentiary Basis

### Legal Framework

1. **Party admissions and statements** — A party's own written statements are admissible as declarations of fact. Under MRE 801(d)(2), a party's own statements are not hearsay when offered against the party. When offered BY the party as their own declarations, they are admissible under the party-statement doctrine or as foundation for an affidavit.

2. **Incorporation into affidavits** — The statements can be authenticated and sworn to by incorporating them into a formal affidavit using language such as:

> "I, Andrew James Pigors, declare under penalty of perjury pursuant to 28 USC §1746 that the following statements, which I previously authored in the course of preparing this litigation, are true and correct to the best of my knowledge, information, and belief."

3. **Authentication** — The ChatGPT/Copilot conversation exports can be authenticated under MRE 901(b)(1) (testimony of a witness with knowledge) by Andrew's testimony that he personally typed the statements and they accurately reflect his input.

### A. Federal (USDC W.D. Michigan) — Service of Process

#### Initial Complaint Service

| Method | Rule | Detail |
|--------|------|--------|
| **U.S. Marshal** | 28 USC §1915(d), Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(c)(3) | If IFP is granted, the court orders the U.S. Marshal to serve defendant at no cost |
| **Personal Service** | Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(e)(2)(A) | Deliver summons + complaint personally to defendant |
| **Substitute Service** | Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(e)(2)(B) | Leave at defendant's dwelling with person of suitable age |
| **Certified Mail** | Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(e)(1) + MCR 2.105(A)(2) | Michigan allows service by registered/certified mail (incorporated by Rule 4(e)(1)) |
| **Waiver of Service** | Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(d) | Mail waiver request to defendant — saves costs, extends response time to 60 days |

> **Pro Se IFP Tip:** If you are granted IFP status, you do NOT need to arrange service
> yourself. Request in your complaint that the court direct the U.S. Marshal to effect
> service under 28 USC §1915(d). The Marshal will serve the summons and complaint
> on the defendant at no charge.
