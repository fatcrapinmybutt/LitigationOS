# Appellate Rules — Appellate Strategist Reference

## Michigan Court of Appeals (COA) Rules — MCR 7.201-7.215

### MCR 7.201 — Organization; Quorum; Chief Judge

```
- COA is organized into districts and divisions
- Cases assigned to three-judge panels
- Majority of panel decides the case
- Chief Judge has administrative authority
- Cases from 14th Circuit (Muskegon County) go to COA
```

### MCR 7.202 — Definitions

```
Key definitions for appellate practice:
- "Final judgment": Judgment that disposes of all claims
  (MCR 7.202(6)(a)(i))
- "Final order": Includes custody orders (MCR 7.202(6)(a)(iv))
- "Interlocutory order": Order that does not dispose of all claims
- "Entry": Date order is signed or stamped by court clerk
- Lane A note: Custody orders are "final orders" for appeal purposes
  even though custody can be modified — MCR 7.202(6)(a)(iv)
- Lane B note: Summary disposition orders are "final judgments"
  when they dispose of all claims — MCR 7.202(6)(a)(i)
```

### MCR 7.203 — Jurisdiction of the Court of Appeals

```
(A) Appeal of Right. COA has jurisdiction over:
    (1) Final judgments or final orders of circuit court
    (2) As provided by law
    Key point: Both Lane A (custody) and Lane B (CZ case) produce
    appealable orders under this section.

(B) Appeal by Leave. COA may grant leave to appeal:
    (1) Interlocutory orders of circuit court
    (2) Other orders as provided by law
    Key point: PPO orders (Lane A) may require leave to appeal
    depending on the specific order type.
```

### MCR 7.204 — Filing the Claim of Appeal

```
(A) Time Requirements:
    - 21 days after entry of judgment or order (civil cases)
    - 21 days after entry of order (custody cases)
    - CRITICAL: This is jurisdictional in practice
    - Missing this deadline = loss of appeal of right

(B) How to File:
    - File claim of appeal with COA Clerk
    - Serve on all parties
    - Pay filing fee ($375 as of 2025)
    - Include:
      * Claim of appeal form
      * Copy of judgment/order appealed from
      * Proof of service
      * Filing fee or fee waiver application

Lane A specifics:
    - Custody order appeal: 21 days from order, not final hearing
    - PPO appeal: depends on order type (see MCR 3.745)
    - Judge McNeill's orders: check entry date on register of actions

Lane B specifics:
    - Summary disposition order: 21 days from entry
    - Judge Hoopes: verify order is final (all claims resolved)
    - If partial summary disposition: may need leave (interlocutory)
```

### MCR 7.205 — Applications for Leave to Appeal

```
(A) Time for Filing:
    - Within 21 days of entry of order sought to be appealed
    - Must state grounds for granting leave

(F) Late Application:
    - May be filed within 6 months of final judgment
    - Must show good cause for late filing
    - NOT automatic — COA has discretion to deny
    - Do NOT rely on this — treat 21-day deadline as hard

(G) Factors COA considers for leave:
    - Likelihood of success on the merits
    - Whether issue is of significant public interest
    - Irreparable harm if leave denied
```

### MCR 7.210 — Record on Appeal

```
(A) Record Defined:
    (1) Original papers filed in trial court
    (2) Register of actions
    (3) Transcripts of proceedings
    (4) Exhibits

(B) Transcript Requirements:
    (1) Appellant must order transcript within:
        - 14 days of filing claim of appeal (if not already ordered)
    (2) Court reporter must file transcript within:
        - 56 days of order (or as directed by COA)
    (3) If no transcript available:
        - Appellant may file settled statement (MCR 7.210(B)(3))

Lane A specifics:
    - ORDER ALL HEARING TRANSCRIPTS — custody hearings, FOC conferences
    - Include any in camera interviews with children
    - Include PPO hearings if relevant

Lane B specifics:
    - Order all motion hearing transcripts
    - Include any case evaluation proceedings
    - Include discovery dispute hearings if relevant
```

### MCR 7.212 — Briefs

```
(B) Form Requirements:
    - Paper size: 8.5 x 11 inches
    - Font: 12-point proportionally spaced OR 10-point monospaced
    - Margins: 1 inch minimum on all sides
    - Page limit: 50 pages (appellant and appellee briefs)
    - Reply brief: 25 pages
    - Leave for excess pages: must petition COA
    - Double-spaced (footnotes may be single-spaced)

(C) Content Requirements — Appellant's Brief:
    (1) Table of contents
    (2) Index of authorities (with page references)
    (3) Statement of jurisdiction
    (4) Statement of questions presented
    (5) Statement of facts (with record references)
    (6) Argument (each issue with standard of review)
    (7) Relief requested
    (8) Signature

(D) Content Requirements — Appellee's Brief:
    - Same format as appellant's brief
    - May include counter-statement of questions
    - May include counter-statement of facts

(E) Reply Brief:
    - Limited to rebuttal of appellee's arguments
    - No new issues may be raised
    - 25-page limit
```

### MCR 7.215 — Opinions, Orders, Judgments

```
(A) Types of Dispositions:
    - Published opinions (precedential)
    - Unpublished per curiam opinions (not precedential)
    - Orders (various types)

(C)(1) Unpublished Opinions:
    - NOT precedentially binding
    - May be cited with notation "unpublished"
    - Court gives them "such persuasive effect as [it] deems appropriate"
    - CRITICAL: Always identify as unpublished when citing

(J) Reconsideration:
    - Motion within 21 days of opinion/order
    - Must state with specificity error in opinion
    - Granted only in exceptional cases
```

## Michigan Supreme Court Rules — MCR 7.301-7.316

### MCR 7.305 — Application for Leave to Appeal

```
(B) Grounds — MSC considers whether to:
    (1) Resolve conflict between COA panels
    (2) Address issue of significant public interest
    (3) Correct clear legal error by lower court
    (4) Address constitutional question
    (5) Exercise superintending control

(C) Time for Filing:
    - Within 42 days of COA opinion/order
    - Cross-application: within 42 days OR 21 days after
      opposing application, whichever is later

(D) Format:
    - Must include concise statement of reasons for granting
    - Copy of COA opinion and trial court opinion/order
    - Relevant portions of record
```

## Local Rules — 14th Judicial Circuit (Muskegon County)

```
Key Local Rules Affecting Appeals:
- Record preparation: Contact Circuit Court Clerk for record assembly
- Transcript ordering: Contact court reporter directly
- Exhibits: Physical exhibits held by court clerk
- Case-specific: Check for any scheduling orders that affect timelines

Judge McNeill (Lane A) Specifics:
- Issues specific oral findings — transcription critical
- May issue written opinions — obtain copies for record

Judge Hoopes (Lane B) Specifics:
- Typically rules from the bench — transcript essential
- Written opinions on summary disposition — include in record
```
