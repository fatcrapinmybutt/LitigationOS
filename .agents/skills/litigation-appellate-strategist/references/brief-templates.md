## Template 2: Lane A Specific — Custody Appeal Brief Outline

```
QUESTIONS PRESENTED:

I. Whether the trial court clearly erred in finding that [specific
   best-interest factor(s)] favored [Watson/other party], where
   the evidence showed [contrary facts].

II. Whether the trial court abused its discretion in [modifying /
    refusing to modify] custody, where [proper cause / change of
    circumstances existed / did not exist].

III. Whether the trial court erred in [PPO-related ruling], where
     [specific error].

STANDARD OF REVIEW MAPPING:
  - Best-interest factor findings: Great weight (MCL 722.28)
  - Established custodial environment: Clear error
  - Custody modification decision: Abuse of discretion
  - PPO issuance/modification: Abuse of discretion
  - Legal interpretation of CCA: De novo

KEY AUTHORITIES:
  - MCL § 722.23(a)-(l) — best interest factors
  - MCL § 722.27 — modification standards
  - MCL § 722.28 — appellate review standard
  - Vodvarka v Grasmeyer, 259 Mich App 499 (2003)
  - Pierron v Pierron, 486 Mich 81 (2010)
  - Shade v Wright, 291 Mich App 17 (2010)

JUDGE McNEILL SPECIFIC NOTES:
  - Review how Judge McNeill weighed each factor
  - Identify factors where findings lack record support
  - Note any factors not addressed (reversible error if material)
```

## Template 3: Lane B Specific — Housing/CZ Appeal Brief Outline

```
QUESTIONS PRESENTED:

I. Whether the trial court erred in granting/denying summary
   disposition under MCR 2.116(C)(10), where [genuine issue of
   material fact existed / did not exist] regarding [housing code
   violations / warranty of habitability / contract breach].

II. Whether the trial court erred in its interpretation of MCL
    § 554.139, where [specific interpretive error].

III. Whether the trial court abused its discretion in [discovery
     ruling / evidentiary ruling], where [specific error].

STANDARD OF REVIEW MAPPING:
  - Summary disposition: De novo (MCR 2.116)
  - Statutory interpretation: De novo
  - Contract interpretation: De novo
  - Discovery rulings: Abuse of discretion
  - Damage calculations: Clear error

KEY AUTHORITIES:
  - MCL § 554.139 — warranty of habitability
  - MCL § 600.2918 — property damages
  - MCL § 445.903 — consumer protection
  - Allison v AEW Capital Management, 481 Mich 419 (2008)
  - Maiden v Rozwood, 461 Mich 109 (1999) — summary disposition
  - Barnard Mfg v Gates Performance, 285 Mich App 362 (2009)

JUDGE HOOPES SPECIFIC NOTES:
  - Review Judge Hoopes' summary disposition analysis
  - Check if all elements of each claim were addressed
  - Note any failure to view evidence in light most favorable
    to non-moving party
```

## Template 4: Reply Brief Structure

```
================================================================
APPELLANT'S REPLY BRIEF

================================================================
TABLE OF CONTENTS
[Same format as main brief]

================================================================
ARGUMENT IN REPLY

I. [RESPOND TO APPELLEE'S ARGUMENT ON ISSUE I]

   Appellee argues that [summarize appellee's argument]. This
   argument fails because [reasons].

   [Appellee's argument is contrary to / ignores / misapplies]
   [cite authority].

   [Rebut specific factual claims with record cites.]

II. [RESPOND TO APPELLEE'S ARGUMENT ON ISSUE II]

    [Same structure as above.]

================================================================
NOTES:
  - Reply brief limited to 25 pages (MCR 7.212(E))
  - NO new issues may be raised
  - Focus on rebutting appellee's strongest arguments
  - Do NOT simply repeat appellant's brief arguments
  - Address any new cases or authorities cited by appellee
================================================================
```

## Brief Quality Checklist

```
Before filing any appellate brief, verify:

FORMAT:
[ ] 12-point proportionally spaced font
[ ] 1-inch margins all sides
[ ] Double-spaced (footnotes may be single-spaced)
[ ] Within page limit (50 pages main, 25 reply)
[ ] Table of contents with accurate page numbers
[ ] Index of authorities complete and accurate

CONTENT:
[ ] Jurisdictional statement with claim of appeal date
[ ] Questions presented framed favorably but accurately
[ ] Every fact has a record citation (Tr at X; Ex Y)
[ ] Every legal proposition has authority citation
[ ] Standard of review stated for each issue
[ ] Relief requested is specific
[ ] Signature block complete with bar number

SUBSTANCE:
[ ] Strongest issue presented first
[ ] Maximum 4 issues
[ ] No frivolous arguments
[ ] All authorities validated (currency, existence)
[ ] Record accurately cited (verified against transcript)
[ ] Appellee's likely counterarguments anticipated
```
