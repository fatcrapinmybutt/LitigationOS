# Michigan Child Support Formula — Reference

## Statutory Framework

### MCL 552.517 — Child Support Formula
The Michigan Child Support Formula (MCSF) is promulgated by the State Court Administrative Office (SCAO) and adopted by the Michigan Supreme Court. Courts MUST use the formula unless deviation is justified under MCL 552.605(2).

### MCSF Calculation Components

#### Step 1: Determine Each Parent's Income

**Gross Income Sources (MCL 552.602(f)):**
| Source | Include? | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Wages/salary | YES | Base pay |
| Overtime | YES (if regular) | Average over 2+ years |
| Bonuses | YES | Average over 2+ years |
| Commissions | YES | Average or recent pattern |
| Self-employment net | YES | After legitimate business expenses |
| Rental income (net) | YES | After mortgage/maintenance |
| Interest/dividends | YES | From all accounts |
| Trust distributions | YES | Actual distributions received |
| Pension/retirement | YES | If currently receiving |
| Social Security | PARTIAL | Exclude SSI; include SSDI |
| Workers' compensation | YES | After tax adjustment |
| Unemployment benefits | YES | Temporary — may trigger imputation |
| Spousal support received | YES | Add to recipient's income |
| Spousal support paid | DEDUCT | From payor's income |

**Income Exclusions:**
- Means-tested public assistance (TANF, food assistance)
- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- Foster care payments
- Child support received for other children (handled separately)

#### Step 2: Calculate Net Income
```
Gross Income
- Federal income tax (using proper filing status)
- State income tax (Michigan 4.25% flat rate)
- FICA (Social Security 6.2% + Medicare 1.45%)
- Mandatory retirement contributions
- Union dues (if mandatory)
- Health insurance premiums (for children only)
= Net Income
```

#### Step 3: Determine Base Support Obligation
The MCSF manual provides income-based tables:
- Combined net income determines total child obligation
- Each parent's proportional share based on income percentage
- Adjustment for number of children

#### Step 4: Overnight Adjustment
```
Parenting Nights:
- Parent A: ___ nights per year
- Parent B: ___ nights per year
- Total: 365 nights

If non-custodial parent has ≥ 128 overnights:
  → Shared custody formula applies
  → Reduces base obligation proportionally
```

#### Step 5: Add-On Expenses
| Expense | Allocation | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Child care (work-related) | Proportional to income | Must be necessary for employment |
| Health insurance premium | Proportional to income | Child's share only |
| Uninsured medical expenses | Proportional to income | After first $357/year per child |
| Extraordinary education | As agreed or ordered | Private school, tutoring |
| Transportation for parenting time | As ordered | Long-distance situations |

### MCL 552.605(2) — Deviation Factors

The court may deviate from the formula when it would be "unjust or inappropriate" considering:

1. **Child has special needs** — medical, educational, therapeutic
2. **Child has extraordinary educational expenses**
3. **Either parent has extraordinary medical expenses**
4. **Either parent earns income exceeding the formula's income cap**
5. **A parent has significant non-income assets**
6. **Prior support orders for other children**
7. **Actual cost of child care significantly differs from formula assumptions**
8. **Established lifestyle of the child** during marriage
9. **Other relevant factors** the court deems appropriate

**Deviation Documentation Requirement:**
Per MCL 552.605(2), the court MUST:
- Make written findings of fact
- State the support amount under the formula
- State the support amount ordered
- Explain why deviation is appropriate

### MCL 552.517(5) — Modification Requirements
Support may be modified upon showing:
- Change in circumstances sufficient to warrant modification
- Change affects the child support obligation by 10% or more
- Changed circumstances must be non-temporary

## Arrearage Calculation

### MCL 552.603(2) — Payment Application
Payment application order:
1. Current support obligation FIRST
2. Then to arrearages
3. Then to interest on arrearages

### MCL 552.607 — Arrearage Interest
- Arrearages accrue interest at statutory rate
- Interest compounds annually
- Some courts use simple interest — check local practice

### MCL 552.625f — Income Withholding
- Support orders include automatic income withholding
- Employer must withhold and remit within 3 business days
- MCL 552.628 — penalties for employer non-compliance
- 42 USC § 652(k) — passport denial for arrearages over $2,500
