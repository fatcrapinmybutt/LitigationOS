# Sanctions Matrix — All Michigan Sanction Types

> Complete reference mapping every Michigan sanction authority to elements,
> procedure, available remedies, and application in Pigors v Watson.

---

## I. MCR 2.114 — Signing Sanctions (Frivolous Filings)

### Statutory Framework
Every pleading, motion, or other document signed by an attorney or party certifies:
1. **Well grounded in fact** after reasonable inquiry
2. **Warranted by existing law** or good faith argument for modification
3. **Not interposed for improper purpose** (harassment, delay, needless cost increase)

### Elements to Prove
| Element | Standard | Evidence Required |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| Document was filed | Fact | Certified copy of filed document |
| Signer certified compliance | Presumed by filing | Signature on document |
| Certification was violated | Objective standard | Show factual or legal baselessness |
| Damages resulted | Causation | Show expenses incurred because of the filing |

### Procedure
1. File motion identifying specific document and specific violation
2. OR court may act sua sponte (on its own initiative)
3. Hearing required before sanctions imposed
4. Safe harbor: Consider giving opposing party opportunity to withdraw the offending filing

### Remedies Available
- Reasonable expenses including attorney fees caused by the filing
- "Appropriate sanction" including penalty payable to the court
- Individual: Up to $5,000 penalty
- Entity: Up to $10,000 penalty

### Lane A Application
- Amy Watson files motion containing demonstrably false allegations about Plaintiff's conduct → MCR 2.114(E) motion
- GAL Rusco files recommendation based on biased investigation → MCR 2.114 if recommendation constitutes a "document" filed with court

### Lane B Application
- Shady Oaks files answer denying habitability problems contradicted by their own inspection records → MCR 2.114(E)
- Corporate defendant files frivolous motion to dismiss → MCR 2.114(E)

## II. MCR 2.313 — Discovery Sanctions (Graduated Framework)

### Tier 1: Expenses for Motion to Compel — MCR 2.313(A)(5)
**Trigger:** Court grants motion to compel
**Remedy:** Reasonable expenses including attorney fees
**Defense:** Substantially justified OR unjust to award

### Tier 2: Failure to Comply with Order — MCR 2.313(B)(2)
**Trigger:** Party violates court order compelling discovery
**Available sanctions (court may choose one or more):**

| Sanction | MCR Cite | Effect |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Designated facts established | 2.313(B)(2)(a) | Court treats the matters as established |
| Refusing party cannot support/oppose claims | 2.313(B)(2)(a) | Designated claims/defenses barred |
| Evidence prohibited | 2.313(B)(2)(b) | Cannot introduce designated evidence at trial |
| Pleading stricken | 2.313(B)(2)(b) | Portions of pleading removed |
| Proceedings stayed | 2.313(B)(2)(c) | Case frozen until compliance |
| Dismissal | 2.313(B)(2)(c) | Plaintiff's case dismissed (if plaintiff violates) |
| Default judgment | 2.313(B)(2)(c) | Judgment against defendant (if defendant violates) |
| Contempt | 2.313(B)(2)(d) | Full contempt proceedings |

### Tier 3: Failure to Attend Deposition / Respond to Discovery — MCR 2.313(D)
**Trigger:** Complete failure to appear for deposition, serve answers to interrogatories, or respond to RFPs
**Remedy:** Same as Tier 2 sanctions — no need for prior motion to compel order

### Escalation Requirements
1. First violation → Expenses (Tier 1)
2. Second violation (after order) → Evidentiary sanctions (Tier 2)
3. Continued willful violation → Case-dispositive sanctions (Tier 2, final level)
4. Direct defiance → Contempt (Tier 2, MCR 2.313(B)(2)(d))

## III. MCR 2.603 — Default and Default Judgment

### Entry of Default — MCR 2.603(A)
| Step | Action | Form |
|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Defendant fails to plead/defend within time allowed | 21 days from service (or 28 if stipulated) |
| 2 | Plaintiff files request for entry of default | SCAO Form MC 13 (Affidavit and Request) |
| 3 | Clerk enters default | Ministerial act; clerk may not refuse if requirements met |
| 4 | Defendant loses ability to file responsive pleading | Must first move to set aside default |

### Default Judgment — MCR 2.603(B)
**Liquidated claims:** Clerk enters judgment on affidavit
**Unliquidated claims:** Application to court required; prove-up hearing

### Setting Aside Default — MCR 2.603(D)
Three factors:
1. **Good cause** for failure to plead timely
2. **Meritorious defense** exists
3. **No substantial prejudice** to plaintiff from delay

*Courts strongly favor resolution on the merits.*

### Corporate Default Special Rules
- Corporations cannot appear pro se in Michigan
- Failure to retain counsel = failure to plead = default eligible
- BUT courts routinely grant time extensions for counsel retention
- Strategy: Request default promptly; oppose any motion to set aside by showing prejudice

## IV. MCR 3.606 — Contempt of Court

### Types of Contempt in Michigan

#### In-Court (Summary) Contempt
- Conduct in the judge's presence that disrupts proceedings
- Immediate sanction authority
- Limited due process required (the judge witnessed the conduct)

#### Out-of-Court Contempt
- Violation of court order outside judge's presence
- Requires motion, notice, and hearing
- Full due process protections apply

### Civil Contempt Procedure
1. File motion identifying: (a) the court order; (b) knowledge of order; (c) specific violation; (d) willfulness
2. Serve motion on alleged contemnor
3. Hearing scheduled with opportunity to be heard
4. Prove elements by preponderance of evidence
5. If found in contempt: court specifies purge condition
6. Purge condition must be achievable (ability-to-comply finding)

### Criminal Contempt Procedure
1. Formal petition or charges filed
2. Notice of specific charges to alleged contemnor
3. Right to counsel (if incarceration possible)
4. Right to jury trial if sentence exceeds 6 months
5. Prove beyond reasonable doubt
6. Definite sentence (not "until compliance")

### Contempt Sanctions
| Sanction | Civil | Criminal |
|----------|-------|----------|
| Compensatory fine | Yes | No |
| Coercive fine | Yes | Yes |
| Punitive fine | No | Yes |
| Incarceration until compliance | Yes (with purge) | No |
| Definite incarceration | No | Yes |
| Compensatory damages | Yes | No |

## V. MCL 600.2591 — Frivolous Civil Actions/Defenses

### Triggering Conduct — MCL 600.2591(3)(a)
An action or defense is frivolous if at least one of the following applies:
1. The party's legal position was **devoid of arguable legal merit** (subsection (ii))
2. The party had **no reasonable basis in law** for the position (subsection (i))
3. The action was **primarily** filed to harass, embarrass, or injure (subsection (iii))

### Mandatory Fee Award
Once frivolousness is found, the award of costs and fees to the prevailing party is **mandatory** — the court has NO discretion to deny.

### Procedure
1. Action/defense must be resolved (final judgment or order)
2. Prevailing party files motion within **56 days** of final order
3. Court holds hearing
4. If frivolous → mandatory cost/fee award
5. Court determines reasonable amount

### Recoverable Costs
- All reasonable costs of the action
- Reasonable attorney fees (or pro se equivalent)
- Costs directly resulting from the frivolous action/defense

### Vexatious Litigant Designation — MCL 600.2591(4)
If a party has a pattern of frivolous litigation, court may require:
- Security bond before filing future actions
- Pre-filing review by the court
- Other conditions to prevent abuse

## VI. Cross-Reference: Sanction Authority Selection Guide

```
Sanctionable conduct identified
│
├─ Is it a FRIVOLOUS FILING?
│   ├─ YES → MCR 2.114 (signing sanctions) + MCL 600.2591 (after final order)
│   └─ NO → Continue
│
├─ Is it DISCOVERY ABUSE?
│   ├─ YES → MCR 2.313 (graduated sanctions)
│   └─ NO → Continue
│
├─ Is it FAILURE TO PLEAD/DEFEND?
│   ├─ YES → MCR 2.603 (default)
│   └─ NO → Continue
│
├─ Is it VIOLATION OF COURT ORDER?
│   ├─ YES → MCR 3.606 (contempt)
│   └─ NO → Continue
│
└─ Is it GENERAL BAD FAITH LITIGATION CONDUCT?
    └─ YES → Combination of above + MCR 2.114 + inherent court authority
```
