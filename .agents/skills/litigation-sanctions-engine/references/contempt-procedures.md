# Contempt Procedures — Civil vs. Criminal in Michigan

> Complete procedural guide for contempt proceedings under MCR 3.606
> with due process requirements, purge conditions, and defenses.

---

## I. Contempt Authority in Michigan

### Constitutional Basis
Contempt power is inherent in Michigan courts as necessary to enforce orders, maintain order, and ensure the administration of justice. Codified in MCR 3.606 and various statutes.

### Types of Contempt

| Type | Purpose | Proof Standard | Result |
|------|---------|---------------|--------|
| **Civil contempt** | Coerce future compliance | Preponderance of evidence | Confinement until compliance; compensatory fine |
| **Criminal contempt** | Punish past conduct | Beyond reasonable doubt | Definite sentence; punitive fine |
| **Summary contempt** | Immediate court control | Judge witnessed the conduct | Immediate sanctions for in-court disruption |

### The Critical Distinction
**Civil:** "Keys to the cell" — the contemnor can end confinement by complying
**Criminal:** "Doing time" — the sentence is fixed regardless of compliance

This distinction controls the entire procedural framework. Miscategorizing contempt is reversible error.

## II. Civil Contempt — Detailed Procedure

### Step 1: Filing the Motion

**Required contents:**
1. Caption and case number
2. Identification of the court order allegedly violated
3. Date the order was entered
4. Specific terms of the order violated
5. Facts showing the contemnor had knowledge of the order
6. Facts showing the specific acts or omissions constituting the violation
7. Facts showing the violation was willful (not excusable)
8. Facts showing the contemnor has the present ability to comply
9. Prayer for relief specifying the purge condition sought

### Step 2: Service on Contemnor

- Motion must be personally served on the alleged contemnor
- Adequate notice of the hearing date and time
- Sufficient time to prepare a defense (minimum 7 days recommended)
- Service by mail may be insufficient for contempt proceedings

### Step 3: Hearing

**Due process requirements (civil contempt):**
- Notice of the alleged violation
- Opportunity to be heard (present evidence, cross-examine, argue)
- Right to counsel (not constitutionally required for civil contempt, but court may appoint)
- Impartial decision-maker

**Turner v. Rogers safeguards (for unrepresented contemnors):**
When the opposing party is unrepresented AND the contemnor faces incarceration, courts must provide:
1. Notice that ability to pay is a critical issue
2. Form to report financial circumstances
3. Opportunity to respond to questions about ability to comply
4. Express judicial finding of ability to comply

### Step 4: Findings

Court must make specific findings:
1. A clear and definite court order existed
2. The contemnor had knowledge of the order
3. The contemnor violated the order
4. The violation was willful
5. The contemnor has the **present ability** to comply

### Step 5: Purge Condition

**MANDATORY for civil contempt.** The contemnor must be given a clear way to purge the contempt:

**Valid purge conditions:**
- "Comply with the parenting time schedule within 48 hours"
- "Produce the discovery documents within 14 days"
- "Pay the ordered support within 30 days"
- "Provide makeup parenting time on [specific dates]"

**Invalid purge conditions:**
- Impossible to perform (past time cannot be returned)
- Vague or ambiguous ("follow the law")
- Requires third-party action beyond contemnor's control
- Punitive rather than coercive

### Application to Lane A — Parenting Time Contempt

**Order violated:** Custody/parenting time order in Case No. 2024-001507-DC
**Contemnor:** Amy Watson
**Violation:** Denial of Plaintiff's court-ordered parenting time on [specific dates]
**Knowledge:** Order served on Amy Watson on [date]; signed acknowledgment of receipt
**Willfulness:** No emergency or legitimate excuse; pattern of repeated denials
**Ability to comply:** Children in Amy Watson's physical custody; nothing prevents compliance
**Purge condition:** (1) Immediate compliance with parenting time schedule; (2) Makeup parenting time for missed dates; (3) Written parenting time log submitted to court monthly

## III. Criminal Contempt — Detailed Procedure

### Enhanced Due Process Requirements

Criminal contempt is a crime. Full criminal procedural protections apply:

| Right | Source | Application |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Notice of charges | Due Process (14th Amend.) | Specific written charges describing the contemptuous conduct |
| Right to counsel | 6th Amendment | Appointed counsel if indigent and facing incarceration |
| Right to jury trial | 6th Amendment | If potential sentence exceeds 6 months |
| Privilege against self-incrimination | 5th Amendment | Cannot be compelled to testify |
| Proof beyond reasonable doubt | In re Winship | Highest burden of proof |
| Right to confront witnesses | 6th Amendment | Cross-examination of all witnesses |
| Right to present defense | Due Process | Opportunity for full defense |

### When Criminal Contempt Applies
- The purpose is to **punish** the contemnor for past disobedience
- The court wants to deter future violations
- Civil contempt is inadequate (e.g., compliance is no longer possible)
- The contemnor's conduct was particularly egregious or defiant

### Prosecutor Requirement
- Criminal contempt typically requires a prosecutor or independent attorney
- The judge should not serve as both prosecutor and judge
- **Young v. United States ex rel. Vuitton et Fils, 481 U.S. 787 (1987):** Appointment of private attorney as prosecutor for criminal contempt must be disinterested

### Sentencing
- Definite term of incarceration (not "until you comply")
- Fine (punitive, not compensatory)
- Probation with conditions
- Michigan: Maximum sentence varies by the underlying order violated

## IV. Contempt Defenses

### Complete Defenses

| Defense | Description | Application |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Inability to comply** | Contemnor cannot presently comply with the order | Must show inability is genuine, not self-created |
| **Ambiguous order** | The court order was unclear about what was required | If reasonable minds could differ on meaning, contempt fails |
| **No knowledge of order** | Contemnor did not know about the order | Must show lack of actual or constructive notice |
| **Compliance** | Contemnor actually complied with the order | Factual defense; evidence of compliance |
| **Impossibility** | Compliance was literally impossible | E.g., court ordered production of documents that don't exist |
| **Order was void** | Court lacked jurisdiction to enter the order | Collateral attack on underlying order (limited circumstances) |

### Partial Defenses / Mitigation

| Defense | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Substantial compliance | May reduce sanctions; shows good faith |
| Good faith effort to comply | May defeat "willful" element |
| Changed circumstances | Order may no longer be enforceable as written |
| De minimis violation | Court may decline to exercise contempt power |
| First-time violation | Mitigates sanctions severity |

## V. Contempt in Specific Michigan Contexts

### Parenting Time Contempt
**MCL 722.27a(7):** Court that finds parenting time interference:
- Make-up parenting time
- Modification of custody or parenting time
- Civil fine up to $100
- Contempt under MCR 3.606
- Counseling or mediation costs
- Attorney fees / pro se equivalent

**Parenting Time Act (MCL 722.27a)** provides specific statutory remedy IN ADDITION to general contempt power.

### PPO Violation
**MCL 600.2950(23):** Violation of PPO is criminal contempt (misdemeanor, up to 93 days)
- Police can arrest without warrant for PPO violation
- Both criminal prosecution AND contempt are possible
- Double jeopardy considerations if both pursued

### Discovery Order Contempt
**MCR 2.313(B)(2)(d):** Contempt as ultimate discovery sanction
- Typically reached only after Level 1-3 sanctions failed
- Must show willful, continued defiance of discovery order
- Due process requires notice and hearing

### Support Order Contempt
**MCL 552.631-637:** Specific contempt procedures for child support
- Separate statutory framework from MCR 3.606
- Income withholding as alternative to contempt
- Ability-to-pay analysis required (Turner v. Rogers)

## VI. Contempt Motion Template

```
STATE OF MICHIGAN
14TH JUDICIAL CIRCUIT COURT — MUSKEGON COUNTY
[FAMILY DIVISION]

[PLAINTIFF],                          Case No. [NUMBER]
        Plaintiff,                    Hon. [JUDGE]
v.
[DEFENDANT],
        Defendant.
________________________________________/

MOTION FOR CIVIL CONTEMPT

NOW COMES Plaintiff [NAME], appearing pro se, and moves this Court for an
order finding Defendant [NAME] in civil contempt of court, and states:

I. THE COURT ORDER

1. On [DATE], this Court entered an order [describe the order specifically].
   A true copy is attached as Exhibit A.

2. The order specifically requires Defendant to [quote relevant provisions].

II. KNOWLEDGE OF ORDER

3. Defendant received a copy of the order on [DATE] by [method].
   [Attach proof of service as Exhibit B.]

III. VIOLATION

4. Defendant has violated the order as follows:
   a. On [DATE], Defendant [specific act or omission].
   b. On [DATE], Defendant [specific act or omission].
   c. [Continue for each violation.]

5. Evidence of each violation is documented by [describe: text messages,
   emails, witness statements, photographs, etc.]. [Attach as Exhibits C-X.]

IV. WILLFULNESS

6. Defendant's violations are willful because:
   a. Defendant has no legitimate excuse for non-compliance.
   b. Defendant has been reminded of the order on [dates].
   c. The pattern of violations shows intentional disregard.

V. ABILITY TO COMPLY

7. Defendant has the present ability to comply because [state facts
   showing compliance is within Defendant's power].

VI. PURGE CONDITION

8. Plaintiff requests the Court establish the following purge condition:
   [Specific, achievable action Defendant must take to purge contempt.]

VII. PRAYER FOR RELIEF

Plaintiff respectfully requests this Court:
a. Find Defendant in civil contempt of this Court's order;
b. Establish a purge condition as described above;
c. Award Plaintiff reasonable expenses and pro se fee equivalent;
d. Order such other relief as this Court deems just and equitable.

Respectfully submitted,

[PLAINTIFF NAME], Pro Se
[Address] | [Phone] | [Date]
```
