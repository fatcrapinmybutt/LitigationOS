# Fee Petition — Documentation Requirements and Templates

> Complete guide to quantifying and recovering litigation expenses
> for a pro se plaintiff in Michigan courts.

---

## I. Lodestar Method — The Standard Framework

Michigan courts use the lodestar method to calculate reasonable fees:

**Lodestar = Reasonable Hours × Reasonable Hourly Rate**

The court may then adjust the lodestar up or down based on the factors in
**Wood v. DAIIE, 413 Mich 573 (1982)** and **Smith v. Khouri, 481 Mich 519 (2008)**.

### Smith v. Khouri Factors
1. The professional standing and experience of the attorney (or pro se equivalent)
2. The skill, time, and labor involved
3. The amount in question and the results obtained
4. The difficulty of the case
5. The expenses incurred
6. The nature and length of the professional relationship
7. The likelihood (at the outset) of success
8. Whether the fee is fixed or contingent

## II. Pro Se Fee Recovery — Legal Basis

### Michigan Authority for Pro Se Fee Equivalents

Michigan courts have recognized pro se fee recovery in several contexts:

1. **MCR 2.313 discovery sanctions:** "Reasonable expenses including attorney fees" — courts have interpreted this to allow pro se time compensation
2. **MCL 600.2591 frivolous actions:** "Costs and fees" — available to prevailing party regardless of representation status
3. **MCR 2.312(D) RFA costs:** Costs of proving matters unreasonably denied in RFAs
4. **FOC proceedings:** Pro se parties can recover costs for contempt proceedings

### Limitations on Pro Se Recovery
- **42 USC § 1988 (federal):** Pro se litigants who are NOT attorneys cannot recover fees under § 1988 — Kay v. Ehrler, 499 U.S. 432 (1991)
- **Michigan distinction:** State fee-shifting statutes may not have the same limitation
- **Hourly rate cap:** Courts typically apply lower rates for pro se than for attorneys

## III. Contemporaneous Time Records

### Required Format

Every fee petition must be supported by contemporaneous time records showing:

| Date | Task Description | Time Spent | Attorney/Pro Se |
|------|-----------------|------------|-----------------|
| MM/DD/YYYY | Specific task description | X.X hrs | [Name] |

### Task Description Best Practices

**GOOD descriptions (specific and verifiable):**
- "Drafted First Set of Interrogatories to Defendant Amy Watson (15 interrogatories)"
- "Researched MCR 2.313 sanctions standards; located 3 controlling Michigan cases"
- "Prepared meet-and-confer letter to Defendant's counsel regarding deficient discovery responses"
- "Attended hearing on Motion to Compel before Hon. McNeill (1.5 hours in court)"

**BAD descriptions (too vague, will be reduced):**
- "Legal research" (what topic? what source? what found?)
- "Work on case" (what specific task?)
- "Phone calls" (to whom? about what? how many?)
- "Document review" (what documents? how many? for what purpose?)

### Block Billing Prohibition
Do not combine multiple tasks into single time entries:

**WRONG:** "Research, draft motion, prepare exhibits — 8.0 hours"
**RIGHT:**
| Task | Time |
|------|------|
| Research MCR 2.313 sanctions framework | 2.0 hrs |
| Draft Motion to Compel body | 3.5 hrs |
| Prepare Exhibits A-D (discovery requests, responses, correspondence) | 2.5 hrs |

## IV. Hourly Rate Justification

### Muskegon County Market Rate Survey

To justify your hourly rate, reference local attorney rates:

| Attorney Type | Estimated Hourly Rate (Muskegon County) |
|---------------|----------------------------------------|
| Junior associate (0-3 years) | $150 - $200 |
| Mid-level associate (3-7 years) | $200 - $275 |
| Senior associate (7-12 years) | $250 - $325 |
| Partner (12+ years) | $300 - $400+ |
| Family law attorney | $200 - $350 |
| Civil litigation attorney | $225 - $375 |
| **Pro se equivalent (recommended)** | **$150 - $225** |

### Justifying a Higher Rate
- Documented expertise in the subject matter
- Complexity of the issues involved
- Results obtained (successful motions, favorable orders)
- Comparison to what opposing counsel charges

### Justifying a Lower Rate Still Being Reasonable
- Courts recognize pro se litigants need MORE time than attorneys
- But courts also reduce total hours for "learning curve" time
- Strategy: Bill fewer hours at a reasonable rate rather than many hours at a low rate

## V. Cost Itemization

### Recoverable Costs Under Michigan Law

| Cost Category | Documentation | Rate |
|---------------|---------------|------|
| Filing fees | Court receipt | Actual amount |
| Service of process | Process server receipt | Actual amount |
| Copies | Receipt or calculation | $0.10 - $0.25/page |
| Postage | USPS receipt or postage meter | Actual amount |
| Certified mail | Green card receipt | Actual amount |
| Mileage | Travel log (origin, destination, miles) | IRS rate ($0.67/mile for 2024) |
| Parking | Receipt | Actual amount |
| Deposition transcripts | Transcript invoice | Actual amount |
| Witness fees | Payment receipt | $12/day + mileage |
| Expert fees | Engagement letter + invoice | Reasonable amount |
| Electronic research | Subscription/usage records | Reasonable amount |

### Non-Recoverable Items (Generally)
- Meals and personal expenses
- Childcare during hearings (unless extraordinary circumstances)
- Lost wages from employment (separate claim, not costs)
- Emotional distress (separate tort claim)
- Technology purchases (computer, printer, etc.)

## VII. Defending Against Fee Reduction

Courts commonly reduce fee petitions for these reasons. Preemptively address each:

| Reduction Reason | Preemptive Response |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Excessive hours | Show each task was necessary; compare to attorney billing norms |
| Learning curve time | Exclude pure legal education; bill only case-specific research |
| Block billing | Use itemized entries with specific task descriptions |
| Inflated rate | Provide market rate evidence; use conservative rate |
| Unsuccessful claims | If partially successful, show fee should be for entire action (if interrelated claims) |
| Vague descriptions | Use specific, verifiable task descriptions |
