# Damages Taxonomy — Michigan Law

Complete taxonomy of damages types available in Pigors v Watson litigation, organized by claim category with Michigan authority and availability.

## I. Compensatory Damages — Economic

Economic damages compensate for quantifiable, out-of-pocket financial losses. They require documentation and do not require subjective valuation.

### A. Lost Wages and Earning Capacity

| Element | Description | Evidence Required | Michigan Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Past Lost Wages | Income lost due to court appearances, moving, recovery | Pay stubs, W-2s, employer letter | Standard tort measure |
| Future Lost Earning Capacity | Projected income reduction from ongoing harm | Expert testimony, earnings history | Precopio v Detroit, 415 Mich 457 (1982) |
| Lost Employment Benefits | Health insurance, retirement contributions lost | Benefit statements, HR records | Part of lost wages calculation |
| Self-Employment Losses | Business income reduction from litigation burden | Tax returns, P&L statements, client records | Wolverine Ins Co v Klomparens, 273 Mich 493 (1935) |

### B. Housing-Related Economic Damages

| Element | Description | Evidence Required | Michigan Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rent Differential | Difference between rent paid and fair value of defective unit | Rent receipts, comparable rental data, habitability report | Teller v McCoy, 162 W Va 367 (1978) (persuasive) |
| Security Deposit | Withheld deposit + statutory penalty | Lease, deposit receipt, demand letter | MCL 554.613 (double deposit penalty) |
| Repair Costs | Tenant-paid repairs for landlord's obligations | Receipts, invoices, photos (before/after) | Lease terms + implied warranty |
| Moving/Relocation | Costs of forced relocation from uninhabitable conditions | Moving company invoices, truck rental, deposits | Consequential damages from breach |
| Temporary Housing | Hotel/motel costs during displacement | Receipts, booking confirmations | Consequential damages from breach |
| Utility Overpayment | Excess utility costs from defective conditions | Utility bills (comparison periods) | Part of habitability damages |
| Storage Costs | Property storage during displacement | Storage unit receipts | Consequential damages |
| Lost Property | Personal property damaged by housing defects | Inventory list, replacement cost, photos | Standard property damage measure |

### C. Medical and Therapy Costs

| Element | Description | Evidence Required | Michigan Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Past Medical Bills | Treatment costs related to injury/distress | Medical records, billing statements | Standard compensatory measure |
| Future Medical Costs | Projected ongoing treatment needs | Expert testimony, treatment plan | Must be proven with reasonable certainty |
| Therapy/Counseling | Mental health treatment for emotional harm | Therapist records, billing, treatment plan | Compensable as economic damage |
| Medication Costs | Prescription costs for conditions caused by defendant | Pharmacy records, prescriptions | Part of medical damages |
| Children's Therapy | Treatment for children harmed by defendant's conduct | Therapist records (next friend standing) | Separate claim, MCR 2.201(E) |

### D. Litigation-Related Costs

| Element | Description | Evidence Required | Michigan Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Filing Fees | Court filing costs incurred | Receipts, fee waiver denials | Recoverable as costs under MCR 2.625 |
| Transcript Costs | Court reporter fees for appeal | Invoices, payment records | MCR 2.625; MCR 7.219 |
| Copy/Print Costs | Document reproduction for filings | Receipts | Taxable costs |
| Travel Costs | Mileage to/from court | Mileage log, IRS rate | MCR 2.625 |

## II. Compensatory Damages — Non-Economic

Non-economic damages compensate for subjective, non-quantifiable harms. They are the most contested category and require strong evidence strategies.

### A. Pain and Suffering

Includes physical pain, discomfort, and bodily suffering resulting from defendant's conduct.
- **Availability**: All tort claims involving physical injury or physical manifestation
- **Michigan Standard**: Jury determines reasonable amount; no formula required; M Civ JI 50.01
- **Cap**: No cap in Michigan except medical malpractice (MCL 600.1483)

### B. Emotional Distress

| Sub-Type | Description | Availability | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|---|
| Parasitic | Attached to another recognized tort | All tort claims | No independent showing needed beyond underlying tort |
| IIED (Independent) | Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress | Standalone claim | Extreme/outrageous conduct; Roberts v Auto-Owners, 422 Mich 594 (1985) |
| NIED (Independent) | Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress | Limited in Michigan | Physical impact or zone of danger; Daley v LaCroix, 384 Mich 4 (1970) |

### C. Loss of Consortium / Companionship

| Sub-Type | Applies To | Michigan Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Parent-Child Relationship | Custody interference | Berger v Weber, 411 Mich 1 (1981) — loss of society and companionship |
| Loss of Parenting Time | Denied/interfered court-ordered time | Calculated by time lost × relationship value |
| Child's Loss of Parent | Child's claim for deprivation of parent | Derivative claim filed as next friend |

### D. Loss of Enjoyment of Life (Hedonic Damages)

Compensates for reduced ability to enjoy daily activities, hobbies, and life experiences.
- **Michigan Status**: Recognized as component of non-economic damages
- **Evidence**: Pre-injury lifestyle vs. post-injury limitations; testimony of family/friends
- **Note**: Often subsumed within pain and suffering rather than as separate category

## III. Punitive / Exemplary Damages

Punitive damages punish egregious misconduct and deter future similar conduct.

### Michigan State Law

- **Standard**: "Malicious, willful, or wanton" conduct — Veselenak v Smith, 414 Mich 567 (1982)
- **Statute**: MCL 600.6304 — allocation: 50% to plaintiff, 50% to state
- **Constitutional Limit**: Generally single-digit ratio to compensatory; State Farm v Campbell, 538 US 408 (2003)
- **Availability**: Not available against government entities; available against individuals

### Federal § 1983 Claims

- **Standard**: Reckless or callous indifference to constitutional rights; Smith v Wade, 461 US 30 (1983)
- **Against Individuals**: Available (qualified immunity defense must be overcome)
- **Against Municipalities**: NOT available; City of Newport v Fact Concerts, 453 US 247 (1981)

## IV. Statutory Damages

| Statute | Damages Provision | Key Requirements | Pigors Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| MCPA (MCL 445.911(2)) | Treble actual damages OR $250 minimum | Unfair/deceptive trade practice in consumer transaction | Housing fraud, deceptive practices |
| Security Deposit Act (MCL 554.613) | Double the deposit amount | Failure to return deposit + itemized list within 30 days | Withheld security deposit |
| Truth in Renting (MCL 554.631 et seq.) | Actual damages + $250 | Lease provisions violating act | Illegal lease terms |
| Lead Hazard (MCL 333.5473 et seq.) | Treble damages | Failure to disclose/remediate lead paint | If applicable to housing conditions |

## V. Nominal Damages

Available when a legal right has been violated but actual damages cannot be proven or are de minimis.
- **Amount**: Typically $1.00
- **Primary Use**: Constitutional tort claims under § 1983 — Carey v Piphus, 435 US 247 (1978)
- **Strategic Value**: Establishes the violation occurred; preserves right to punitive damages and injunctive relief
- **Important**: A finding of nominal damages can still support punitive damages in § 1983 actions

## VI. Equitable Remedies

| Remedy | Description | When Available | Court Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Injunctive Relief | Court order requiring or prohibiting specific conduct | Irreparable harm + inadequate legal remedy | Inherent equity power |
| Specific Performance | Order to perform contractual obligation | Contract claim + unique subject matter | MCL 600.4401 |
| Declaratory Judgment | Court declaration of rights/obligations | Actual controversy exists | MCR 2.605 |
| Constructive Trust | Equitable ownership imposed on property | Unjust enrichment/fraud | Equity jurisdiction |
| Contempt Sanctions | Penalties for violating court orders | Existing court order + violation | MCR 3.606 (civil contempt) |

## VII. Attorney Fees (Pro Se Limitation)

- **General Rule**: Michigan follows the "American Rule" — each party pays own fees
- **Exceptions**: Fee-shifting statutes, contractual provisions, court rules
- **Critical Pro Se Limitation**: Kay v Ehrler, 499 US 432 (1991) — pro se litigants CANNOT recover attorney fees under fee-shifting statutes (§ 1988, MCPA). You can recover costs but not attorney fees.
- **Workaround**: If you later retain counsel, fees from that point forward may be recoverable under applicable fee-shifting provisions.
