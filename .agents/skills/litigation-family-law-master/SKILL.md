### Custody Modification — MCL 722.27
*Source: litigation-custody-specialist*

### Threshold Requirement
Before court can modify custody, moving party must show either:
1. **Proper cause:** Appropriate grounds for bringing the motion (legal basis)
2. **Change of circumstances:** Material change since last custody order

**Vodvarka v. Grasmeyer, 259 Mich App 499 (2003):** Proper cause means one or more appropriate grounds that have or could have a significant effect on the child's life sufficient to warrant revisiting the custody order. Change of circumstances must be significant enough to warrant revisiting and must have occurred since the entry of the last custody order.

### Procedure
1. File motion alleging proper cause or change of circumstances
2. Court conducts threshold hearing (Vodvarka hearing)
3. If threshold met → full evidentiary hearing on all 12 best interest factors
4. If threshold NOT met → motion denied without reaching best interests

### PPO Violation — MCL 600.2950(23)
*Source: litigation-ppo-specialist*

### Criminal Penalties
Violation of a PPO is a **misdemeanor** punishable by:
- Up to 93 days imprisonment
- Fine up to $500
- Or both

### Contempt Penalties
In addition to criminal prosecution, violation may be punished as contempt:
- Civil contempt (coercive)
- Criminal contempt (punitive)
- These are SEPARATE from criminal prosecution (no double jeopardy if contempt is civil)

### Bond Conditions
After PPO violation:
- Court may impose bond conditions pending hearing
- Bond conditions may include: no contact, GPS monitoring, substance abuse testing
- Violation of bond conditions → bond revocation and potential incarceration

## §2. PPO Strategy & Defense

> MCL 600.2950, petition/response, modification, enforcement

### PPO Modification and Termination
*Source: litigation-ppo-specialist*

### Grounds for Modification — MCL 600.2950(12)

#### Changed Circumstances
- Original basis for PPO no longer exists
- Threat has diminished or been eliminated
- Parties' circumstances have materially changed
- Custody order has been entered providing structured contact
- Time has passed without incident

#### Due Process Challenges
- PPO issued without adequate factual basis
- Petitioner's affidavit contained false statements
- No reasonable person would feel threatened by the alleged conduct
- PPO restrictions are broader than necessary to protect petitioner

#### Overbreadth
- PPO restricts conduct protected by the Constitution (speech, parenting rights)
- Restrictions are not narrowly tailored to address the specific threat
- PPO effectively terminates parenting time without best-interest analysis

#### Vagueness
- PPO terms are so vague that respondent cannot know what is prohibited
- "Stay away" without specifying distance or exceptions
- "No contact" without exception for custody exchanges

### Modification Motion Framework

```
MOTION TO MODIFY / TERMINATE PERSONAL PROTECTION ORDER

I. PROCEDURAL HISTORY
   - Date PPO issued, terms, basis alleged

II. CHANGED CIRCUMSTANCES
   - Material changes since issuance
   - No incidents during compliance period
   - Custody order now governs contact

III. DUE PROCESS CHALLENGE
   - Inadequate factual basis for specific restrictions
   - False statements in petitioner's affidavit
   - Restrictions exceed what is necessary

IV. OVERBREADTH / VAGUENESS
   - Constitutional rights impacted (parenting, speech)
   - Restrictions not narrowly tailored
   - Terms too vague for compliance

V. IMPACT ON PARENTING RIGHTS
   - PPO effectively modifies custody without best-interest analysis
   - MCL 722.23 factors not considered in PPO issuance
   - Parenting time rights under MCL 722.27a impaired

VI. PROPOSED MODIFICATION
   - Specific modifications requested (e.g., exception for custody exchanges)
   - Alternatively: Full termination with supporting basis

VII. PRAYER FOR RELIEF
```

### Support Modification (MCL 552.517)
*Source: litigation-child-support-analyzer*

### Grounds for Modification

| Ground | Standard | Evidence |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Change in income** | Substantial and continuing | Pay stubs, tax returns, W-2s |
| **Change in overnights** | Actual parenting time differs from order | Calendar documentation |
| **Change in child care** | Costs increased or decreased substantially | Receipts, provider statements |
| **Change in health insurance** | Cost change or availability change | Insurance documents |
| **Child's needs changed** | Special needs, medical, educational | Medical records, school records |
| **Emancipation** | Child reaches 18 (or 19.5 if in school) | Birth certificate, school records |

### Modification Threshold

The MCSF provides that a modification is appropriate when the recalculated
amount differs from the current order by **10% or more** (MCL 552.517(1)(a))
OR **$50 or more per month** — whichever is greater.

- [ ] Gather income documentation (both parties)
- [ ] Document change in circumstances
- [ ] File motion on SCAO form FOC 50
- [ ] Serve opposing party
- [ ] Serve FOC office
- [ ] File proof of service

## §3. Parental Alienation Detection

> Gardner 8 criteria, behavioral indicators, counter-strategies

### Gardner Eight Manifestations Framework
*Source: litigation-parental-alienation-detector*

The eight behavioral manifestations of Parental Alienation Syndrome (PAS)
as identified by Dr. Richard Gardner (1985, 1998). Each manifestation
is independently scored on a 3-point severity scale (mild / moderate / severe).

| # | Manifestation | Description | Evidence Types |
|---|---------------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | Campaign of denigration | Child participates in denigrating the targeted parent | Texts, emails, recordings of child's statements |
| 2 | Weak or frivolous rationalizations | Child gives absurd or trivial reasons for hostility | Deposition testimony, FOC interview notes |
| 3 | Lack of ambivalence | Child expresses only negative feelings toward targeted parent | Therapist notes, GAL reports |
| 4 | Independent-thinker phenomenon | Child claims alienation is entirely their own idea | Interview transcripts, child's statements |
| 5 | Reflexive support of alienating parent | Child automatically sides with alienating parent in all disputes | Communication records, witness statements |
| 6 | Absence of guilt | Child shows no guilt for exploitation or cruelty toward targeted parent | Behavioral observations, therapist records |
| 7 | Borrowed scenarios | Child uses phrases or describes events clearly coached by alienating parent | Comparison of parent's language to child's statements |
| 8 | Spread to extended family | Hostility extends to targeted parent's entire family and social circle | Communication records, party invitations, family event exclusions |

### Severity Scoring

```
Mild:     Isolated incidents, child shows some ambivalence
          Score: 1 per manifestation
Moderate: Pattern of behavior, child increasingly rigid
          Score: 2 per manifestation
Severe:   Entrenched behavior, child refuses all contact
          Score: 3 per manifestation

Total Score Range: 0-24
  0-4:   No significant alienation
  5-10:  Mild alienation indicators
  11-16: Moderate alienation — court intervention warranted
  17-24: Severe alienation — emergency intervention required
```

## §4. Child Support Calculation

> Michigan formula, income imputation, deviation, enforcement

### Michigan Child Support Formula (MCSF) Framework
*Source: litigation-child-support-analyzer*

### Overview

The MCSF is the mandatory starting point for all child support calculations
in Michigan. The court MUST apply the formula unless deviation is justified
under MCL 552.605(2).

### Key Formula Components

| Component | Description | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Net income** | Gross income minus allowable deductions | MCSF § 2.01 |
| **Overnights** | Number of overnights per parent per year | Parenting time order |
| **Children** | Number of children of this relationship | Case record |
| **Other children** | Children of other relationships | Disclosure |
| **Child care costs** | Work-related child care | Receipts/estimates |
| **Health care costs** | Insurance premiums for child | Insurance documentation |
| **Ordinary medical** | Annual ordinary medical ($403/child 2024) | MCSF supplement |

### Income Determination (MCSF § 2.01)

#### Gross Income Includes:

| Source | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Wages/salary | All employment compensation |
| Overtime | Regular or predictable overtime |
| Commissions | Sales or performance bonuses |
| Self-employment | Net business income |
| Rental income | Net rental property income |
| Interest/dividends | Investment income |
| Pension/retirement | Retirement income received |
| Social Security | Disability or retirement benefits |
| Workers' compensation | WC benefits received |
| Unemployment | Unemployment benefits |
| Alimony received | From any source |
| Trust income | Distributions from trusts |
| Annuities | Annuity payments |
| Capital gains | Recurring capital gains |

#### Allowable Deductions:

| Deduction | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Federal/state/local taxes | Actual or estimated |
| FICA (Social Security + Medicare) | 7.65% employee share |
| Mandatory retirement contributions | Employer-required only |
| Union dues | If mandatory |
| Prior child support | For other children (actual paid) |
| Alimony paid | Court-ordered alimony to others |
| Health insurance (self) | Employee share for self only |

### Income Imputation (MCSF § 2.01(G))

When a parent is voluntarily unemployed or underemployed, income may be
imputed based on:

| Factor | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| **Employment history** | Prior jobs, earnings trajectory |
| **Education/skills** | Degrees, certifications, training |
| **Local job market** | Available positions, prevailing wages |
| **Physical/mental capacity** | Health limitations |
| **Child care responsibilities** | Custodial parent's availability |
| **Efforts to find employment** | Job search documentation |

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL**: Imputation must be based on real labor market data,
> not aspirational earning capacity. Request Bureau of Labor Statistics
> or Michigan DTMB data to support or challenge imputation.

### Deviation from Formula (MCL 552.605(2))
*Source: litigation-child-support-analyzer*

### Requirements for Deviation

The court may deviate from the MCSF amount only if:

1. Application of the formula would be **unjust or inappropriate**
2. The court states **specific reasons** for deviation on the record
3. The deviation serves the **child's best interests**

### Accepted Deviation Factors

| Factor | Direction | Justification |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Special needs child | Increase | Additional medical, educational, or therapeutic costs |
| Shared physical custody | Decrease | Both parents bear significant direct costs |
| Extraordinary income | Decrease | Formula amount exceeds child's reasonable needs |
| Low income | Decrease | Payer's income below self-support reserve |
| Third-party income | Varies | New spouse's contribution to household |
| Child's own income | Decrease | Minor child has employment or trust income |
| Tax consequences | Varies | Specific tax impact of support arrangement |

### PPO and Custody Intersection
*Source: litigation-ppo-specialist*

### The Custody-PPO Conflict
PPOs can effectively change custody arrangements WITHOUT the best-interest analysis required by MCL 722.23:
- "No contact" PPO prevents parenting time
- "Stay away from residence" prevents custody exchanges
- "No contact with children" modifies custody order without modification hearing

**This is a due process argument:** Using a PPO to accomplish what requires a custody modification hearing violates the respondent's fundamental right to parent.

### Reconciling PPO with Custody Order
| Scenario | Resolution |
|----------|-----------|
| PPO says "no contact" + Custody order says "parenting time" | Custody order generally controls for child contact; PPO should be modified to include custody exchange exception |
| PPO entered AFTER custody order | PPO must be read in harmony with existing custody order; motion to modify PPO to add custody exception |
| PPO entered BEFORE custody order | Custody order should address PPO and include appropriate provisions |
| PPO includes "no contact with children" | Challenge as de facto custody modification without MCL 722.23 analysis |

### Constitutional Argument
- Parental rights are FUNDAMENTAL (Troxel v. Granville, 530 U.S. 57 (2000))
- Depriving a parent of custody/parenting time requires due process
- PPO ex parte procedure provides LESS process than custody modification
- Therefore: PPO cannot be used to accomplish custody change without custody hearing

### Emergency Relief Procedures
*Source: litigation-ppo-specialist*

### Motion for Emergency Modification
When PPO creates immediate crisis (e.g., preventing scheduled parenting time):
1. File emergency motion to modify PPO
2. Request ex parte relief or expedited hearing
3. Argue irreparable harm to parent-child relationship
4. Propose specific, narrow modification (custody exchange exception)
5. Offer safeguards (neutral exchange location, third-party present)

### Timing
- Court MUST hold hearing within 14 days of motion to modify/terminate
- For true emergencies: Request same-day or next-day hearing
- Judge has authority to modify ex parte in emergency circumstances
