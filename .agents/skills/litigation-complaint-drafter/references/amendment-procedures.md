# Amendment Procedures — MCR 2.118 Reference

> Michigan Court Rules for amending pleadings
> Context: Pigors v Watson | 14th Judicial Circuit, Muskegon County

---

## 1. AMENDMENT AS OF RIGHT — MCR 2.118(A)(1)

### When Available:
A party may amend a pleading **once as a matter of course** before:
- Being served with a responsive pleading, **OR**
- Within **21 days** after serving the pleading if a responsive pleading is not required and the action has not been placed on a trial calendar

### No Court Permission Needed:
File the amended pleading directly with the clerk. No motion required. No leave of court needed.

### Procedure:
1. Draft the amended complaint in its entirety (not just the changes)
2. Title it "FIRST AMENDED [VERIFIED COMPLAINT]"
3. File with the clerk
4. Serve on all parties who have appeared — MCR 2.107(A)
5. The amended pleading supersedes the original

### Timing Trap:
Once the defendant files an answer (responsive pleading), the as-of-right window closes. A motion to dismiss under MCR 2.116 is NOT a responsive pleading — the window stays open until the answer is filed.

---

## 2. AMENDMENT BY LEAVE OF COURT — MCR 2.118(A)(2)

### When Required:
Any amendment after the as-of-right period requires a motion for leave to amend.

### Standard:
Leave to amend "shall be freely given when justice so requires." MCR 2.118(A)(2). This mirrors Federal Rule 15(a).

### Court Considers:
1. **Undue delay** — Has the party waited unreasonably long?
2. **Bad faith or dilatory motive** — Is the amendment for improper purpose?
3. **Repeated failure to cure deficiencies** — Has the party been given chances to amend and failed?
4. **Undue prejudice to the opposing party** — Would the amendment unfairly harm the other side at this stage?
5. **Futility** — Would the amended claim be subject to immediate dismissal?

**Key Case:** Ben P. Fyke & Sons v Gunter Co, 390 Mich 649 (1973) — courts should freely grant leave absent the above factors.

### Motion Requirements:
1. **Motion for Leave to Amend** — MCR 2.119 compliant
2. **Brief in Support** — addressing the five factors above
3. **Proposed Amended Complaint** — attached as an exhibit to the motion (complete document, not just changes)
4. **Certificate of Service** — proving service on all parties

### Template Motion:
```
STATE OF MICHIGAN
IN THE CIRCUIT COURT FOR THE COUNTY OF MUSKEGON
14TH JUDICIAL CIRCUIT

[Caption]

PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR LEAVE TO FILE 
FIRST AMENDED VERIFIED COMPLAINT

Plaintiff [Name], appearing pro se, respectfully moves this 
Court for leave to file a First Amended Verified Complaint 
pursuant to MCR 2.118(A)(2). In support, Plaintiff states:

1. The original Verified Complaint was filed on [date].
2. Plaintiff seeks to amend to [add new claims / add new 
   defendants / correct factual allegations / add newly 
   discovered facts].
3. This motion is timely because [explain timing].
4. No party will be unduly prejudiced because [explain].
5. The proposed amendments are not futile because [explain].
6. The proposed First Amended Verified Complaint is attached 
   hereto as Exhibit 1.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court 
grant leave to file the attached First Amended Verified 
Complaint.
```

## 3. RELATION BACK DOCTRINE — MCR 2.118(D)

### Rule:
An amendment relates back to the date of the original pleading when:
- The claim or defense arose out of the **same conduct, transaction, or occurrence** set forth in the original pleading

### Significance:
Relation back defeats statute of limitations defenses on newly added claims or parties, provided the new claims arise from the same transaction as the original.

### Requirements for New Party (MCR 2.118(D)):
An amendment adding a new party relates back only if:
1. The claim arose from the same conduct/transaction as the original
2. Within the service period, the new party:
   - Received notice of the action such that they will not be prejudiced
   - Knew or should have known that but for a mistake, they would have been named originally

### Practical Application:
- Adding a new COUNT against an existing defendant → relates back if same facts
- Adding a new DEFENDANT → must meet the notice + knowledge test
- Changing a legal theory → relates back if same underlying facts

**Key Case:** Miller v Chapman Contracting, 477 Mich 102 (2007)

## 4. SUPPLEMENTAL PLEADING — MCR 2.118(E)

### Distinction from Amendment:
- **Amendment** = changes to allegations about events BEFORE the original filing
- **Supplemental pleading** = allegations about events AFTER the original filing

### When to Use:
When new events occur after the complaint was filed that are relevant to the claims (e.g., additional violations, continued misconduct, new damages).

### Procedure:
File a motion for leave to file supplemental pleading under MCR 2.118(E). Same factors as amendment by leave.

## 5. THIRD-PARTY COMPLAINT — MCR 2.204

### What It Is:
A defending party may bring in a third party "who is or may be liable to the third-party plaintiff for all or part of the plaintiff's claim."

### Timing:
- **Within 21 days after serving the original answer** — as of right
- **After 21 days** — by leave of court

### Purpose:
Contribution, indemnification, or shifting liability to a non-party who should bear responsibility.

### Example in Pigors v Watson:
If a defendant claims a property management company, contractor, or other entity is actually responsible for the conditions, they may file a third-party complaint against that entity.

---

## 6. CROSS-CLAIM — MCR 2.203(B)

### What It Is:
A claim by one party against a co-party arising out of the same transaction or occurrence as the original action.

### Requirements:
- Must arise from the same transaction or occurrence
- Filed with or after the responsive pleading
- Does not require leave of court

### Example:
If multiple defendants are named, one defendant may cross-claim against another for contribution or indemnification.

---

## 7. COUNTERCLAIM — MCR 2.203(A)

### Compulsory Counterclaim:
Any claim that arises out of the same transaction or occurrence as the opposing party's claim **MUST** be filed as a counterclaim or it is **WAIVED**.

### Permissive Counterclaim:
Claims that do not arise from the same transaction may be filed as permissive counterclaims at the party's discretion.

### Procedure:
Filed with the answer/responsive pleading. Uses the same format as a complaint (caption, numbered paragraphs, prayer).

## 8. AMENDMENT TIMING CHART

| Situation | Rule | Permission Required | Deadline |
|-----------|------|-------------------|----------|
| First amendment before answer | MCR 2.118(A)(1) | None (as of right) | Before responsive pleading |
| First amendment within 21 days (no responsive pleading required) | MCR 2.118(A)(1) | None | 21 days after serving pleading |
| Any amendment after answer | MCR 2.118(A)(2) | Leave of court | Motion + hearing |
| Adding new party (relation back) | MCR 2.118(D) | Leave of court | Must meet notice requirements |
| Events after original filing | MCR 2.118(E) | Leave of court | Motion + hearing |
| Third-party complaint (early) | MCR 2.204 | None | Within 21 days of answer |
| Third-party complaint (late) | MCR 2.204 | Leave of court | Motion + hearing |
| Compulsory counterclaim | MCR 2.203(A) | None | With responsive pleading (MANDATORY) |
| Permissive counterclaim | MCR 2.203(A) | None | With responsive pleading |
| Cross-claim | MCR 2.203(B) | None | With or after responsive pleading |

## 9. PRO SE CONSIDERATIONS

### Liberal Construction:
Michigan courts construe pro se pleadings liberally. Decker v Flood, unpub. However, pro se status does not excuse failure to comply with procedural rules.

### Common Pro Se Amendment Mistakes:
1. Filing a "supplement" when an amended complaint is needed
2. Filing only the changed pages instead of the complete amended document
3. Failing to re-serve the amended complaint on all parties
4. Forgetting to re-verify the amended complaint
5. Not updating the exhibit list for the amended version

### Best Practice:
Every amended complaint should be a complete, stand-alone document that could be read without reference to any prior version. Re-number all paragraphs. Re-verify. Re-attach all exhibits.
