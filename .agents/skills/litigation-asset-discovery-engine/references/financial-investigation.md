# Financial Investigation — Michigan Family Law Reference

## Income Analysis for Support Calculations

### MCL 552.517 — Michigan Child Support Formula

#### Income Definition (MCSF Manual)
Income for child support includes:
- Wages, salaries, tips, commissions
- Overtime (if regular pattern)
- Bonuses (averaged over period)
- Self-employment net income
- Rental income (net)
- Interest and dividend income
- Trust income
- Pension/retirement income
- Social Security benefits
- Workers' compensation
- Unemployment benefits
- Imputed income (MCL 552.605(2))

#### Income Exclusions
- Means-tested public assistance (TANF, SSI, food assistance)
- Child support received for other children
- Foster care payments

### Income Imputation Under MCL 552.605(2)

When a party is voluntarily unemployed or underemployed:

#### Imputation Factors
1. **Prior employment history**: What did the party earn previously?
2. **Education and training**: What is the party qualified to earn?
3. **Physical/mental capacity**: Any legitimate limitations?
4. **Job market availability**: Are comparable jobs available?
5. **Age and health**: Relevant limitations
6. **Efforts to find employment**: Good-faith job search evidence

#### Imputation Methodology
```
Step 1: Determine prior earning capacity (highest recent stable income)
Step 2: Assess current job market for comparable positions
Step 3: Apply wage data from Bureau of Labor Statistics or Michigan DTMB
Step 4: Consider legitimate reduction factors (health, child care)
Step 5: Calculate imputed income as reasonable earning capacity
```

#### Key Michigan Cases on Imputation
- *Ghidotti v Barber*, 459 Mich 189 (1998) — imputation standard
- *Stallworth v Stallworth*, 275 Mich App 282 (2007) — burden of proof
- *Peterson v Peterson*, 272 Mich App 511 (2006) — voluntary underemployment

### Property Division Analysis

#### MCL 552.401 — Equitable Distribution Framework

Michigan is an equitable distribution state (NOT community property):

**Factors for Equitable Division:**
1. Duration of the marriage
2. Contributions of the parties to the marital estate
3. Age of the parties
4. Health of the parties
5. Life status of the parties
6. Necessities and circumstances of the parties
7. Earning abilities of the parties
8. Past relations and conduct of the parties (fault)
9. General principles of equity
10. Needs of the children (if applicable)

Per *Sparks v Sparks*, 440 Mich 141 (1992): "The goal is to reach an equitable
division in light of all the circumstances."

#### Asset Classification

| Category | Marital Property | Separate Property |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Wages during marriage | YES | — |
| Pre-marital assets | — | YES (if not commingled) |
| Inheritance | — | Usually YES |
| Gifts between spouses | YES | — |
| Gifts from third parties | — | Usually YES |
| Appreciation of separate property | MAYBE | MAYBE |
| Retirement earned during marriage | YES (coverture fraction) | — |
| Business started during marriage | YES | — |
| Pre-marital business appreciation | MAYBE | MAYBE |

#### Commingling Analysis
Separate property loses its character when commingled with marital property:
- Depositing inheritance into joint account
- Using separate funds for marital expenses
- Adding spouse's name to separate property title

Per *Dart v Dart*, 460 Mich 573 (1999): Once commingled, the burden shifts
to the party claiming separate property to trace the funds.

### Forensic Accounting Techniques

#### Bank Statement Analysis
```
Step 1: Obtain all statements for 24-month period
Step 2: Categorize transactions (income, expenses, transfers)
Step 3: Identify anomalies:
  - Round-number withdrawals ($500, $1000, $5000)
  - New payees appearing near separation
  - Declining balance trends inconsistent with income
  - Transfers to unknown accounts
Step 4: Cross-reference with tax returns
Step 5: Calculate unexplained expenditures
Step 6: Document dissipation claims per Sparks framework
```

#### Tax Return Cross-Reference
| Tax Line Item | Reveals | Cross-Reference With |
|---------------|---------|---------------------|
| W-2 wages | Employment income | Pay stubs, employer records |
| Schedule C | Self-employment | Business bank accounts |
| Schedule D | Capital gains/losses | Brokerage statements |
| Schedule E | Rental income | Property records, leases |
| Schedule B | Interest/dividends | Bank/investment statements |
| Form 8949 | Asset sales | Determine disposition of assets |
| State return | Michigan-specific income | Federal return comparison |

#### Hidden Income Indicators
- Lifestyle inconsistent with reported income
- Cash-intensive business with declining revenue
- Loans from family members (may be disguised income)
- Bartering or in-kind compensation
- Cryptocurrency transactions not reported
- Foreign accounts not disclosed on FBAR
- Multiple business entities with intercompany transfers

### Discovery Sanctions (MCR 2.313)

If opposing party fails to comply with financial discovery:

| Sanction | Authority | When Appropriate |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Order compelling discovery | MCR 2.313(A) | First step — always try this first |
| Reasonable expenses + fees | MCR 2.313(A)(4) | When motion to compel is granted |
| Facts deemed established | MCR 2.313(B)(2)(a) | Willful non-compliance |
| Striking pleadings | MCR 2.313(B)(2)(c) | Repeated non-compliance |
| Default judgment | MCR 2.313(B)(2)(c) | Extreme non-compliance |
| Contempt | MCR 2.313(B)(2)(d) | Willful defiance of court order |
| Adverse inference | General authority | Failure to produce evidence in party's control |

### Practical Tips for Pro Se Financial Discovery

1. **Start with interrogatories** — cheapest method, forces opposing party to identify accounts
2. **Compare answers to tax returns** — inconsistencies reveal concealment
3. **Subpoena early** — bank records take time to compile
4. **Track deadlines** — MCR 2.309(B) allows 28 days for interrogatory responses
5. **Follow up aggressively** — file motion to compel immediately upon non-compliance
6. **Keep a financial timeline** — chronological transaction log for each account
7. **Document lifestyle** — photographs of vehicles, vacations, purchases inconsistent with stated income
