# Daubert/MRE 702 — Expert Witness Standards Reference

## Michigan's Expert Testimony Standard

### MRE 702 — Testimony by Experts
"If the court determines that scientific, technical, or other specialized knowledge will assist the trier of fact to understand the evidence or to determine a fact in issue, a witness qualified as an expert by knowledge, skill, experience, training, or education may testify thereto in the form of an opinion or otherwise if:
(1) the testimony is based on sufficient facts or data,
(2) the testimony is the product of reliable principles and methods, and
(3) the witness has applied the principles and methods reliably to the facts of the case."

### *Gilbert v DaimlerChrysler Corp*, 470 Mich 749 (2004)
Michigan adopted a Daubert-like framework through this decision:

**Four-Factor Reliability Test:**
1. **Testability**: Can the theory/technique be tested?
2. **Peer review**: Has it been subjected to peer review and publication?
3. **Error rate**: What is the known or potential rate of error?
4. **General acceptance**: Is it generally accepted in the relevant scientific community?

**Additional considerations from *Kumho Tire Co v Carmichael*, 526 US 137 (1999):**
- Whether the expert's methodology has been used outside litigation
- Whether the expert exercised appropriate care in applying the methodology
- Whether the expert can account for alternative explanations
- Whether the expert's field of expertise is known to reach reliable results

## Expert Qualification Framework

### Who Qualifies as an Expert?

MRE 702 permits qualification by:
- **Knowledge**: Specialized knowledge beyond common understanding
- **Skill**: Practical ability developed through practice
- **Experience**: Years of practice in the relevant field
- **Training**: Formal instruction in the relevant subject
- **Education**: Academic credentials in the relevant field

## MRE 703-706 — Related Rules

### MRE 703 — Bases of Opinion Testimony
Expert may base opinion on facts/data:
- Perceived firsthand
- Made known to expert at or before hearing
- Of a type reasonably relied upon by experts in the field

### MRE 704 — Opinion on Ultimate Issue
- Expert MAY testify on ultimate issue
- Exception: mental state in criminal cases
- In custody: expert MAY opine on best interests (but court decides)

### MRE 705 — Disclosure of Underlying Data
- Expert may give opinion without first testifying to underlying facts
- Expert may be required to disclose on cross-examination
- Court may require disclosure before opinion

### MRE 706 — Court-Appointed Experts
- Court may appoint its own expert
- Compensation fixed by court, paid as directed
- Parties may still call their own experts
- **Useful for pro se litigants**: request court-appointed expert when unable to afford one

## Daubert Challenge Preparation

### Preparing Your Expert for Challenge
1. Review all four *Gilbert* factors for your expert's methodology
2. Gather peer-reviewed support for methodology
3. Identify and address known error rates
4. Prepare expert to explain methodology in plain language
5. Ensure expert can connect methodology to case facts
6. Review all prior testimony for consistency

### Challenging Opposing Expert
```
DAUBERT CHALLENGE CHECKLIST

□ Has expert been qualified in this specific area before?
□ What is the methodology being used?
□ Has the methodology been tested?
□ Has it been peer-reviewed?
□ What is the error rate?
□ Is it generally accepted?
□ Does the expert have sufficient facts/data?
□ Has the methodology been reliably applied?
□ Are there alternative explanations not considered?
□ Is the expert opinion within the scope of their expertise?

If 3+ factors are weak → file Daubert motion to exclude
```

### Daubert Motion Template
```
MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE EXPERT TESTIMONY

Pursuant to MRE 702 and Gilbert v DaimlerChrysler Corp, 
470 Mich 749 (2004), [Party] moves to exclude the testimony 
of [Expert Name] because:

1. The expert's methodology is [untested/not peer-reviewed/
   has high error rate/not generally accepted]
2. The expert's opinion is not based on sufficient facts or data
3. The expert has not reliably applied the methodology to the 
   facts of this case

[Detailed argument for each challenged element]

RELIEF REQUESTED:
Exclude [Expert]'s testimony in its entirety, or in the 
alternative, limit testimony to [specific scope].
```
