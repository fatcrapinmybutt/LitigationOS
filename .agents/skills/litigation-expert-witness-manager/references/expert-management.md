# Expert Management — Practical Reference

## Expert Retention Process

### Step 1: Identify Need
Before seeking an expert, answer:
- What fact or opinion requires expert testimony?
- Is this beyond the trier of fact's common knowledge?
- Can the claim survive without expert testimony?
- What is the budget constraint?

### Step 2: Search and Screen
| Source | Method | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Bar association referrals | Contact State Bar expert witness section | Free referral service |
| University faculty | Contact relevant department | Often lower fees |
| Professional associations | Search member directories | Specialized expertise |
| Expert witness directories | Online databases | Wide selection |
| Opposing experts | Review prior cases | Know the competition |
| Court appointments | Request MRE 706 appointment | Free if court approves |

### Step 4: Retention Agreement

Key terms to include:
```
EXPERT RETENTION AGREEMENT TERMS

1. SCOPE OF ENGAGEMENT
   - Specific issues expert will address
   - Limitations on scope
   - Whether opinions may change with new facts

2. COMPENSATION
   - Hourly rate: $_____ (review and preparation)
   - Trial rate: $_____ (testimony day)
   - Deposition rate: $_____ 
   - Retainer: $_____
   - Payment schedule
   - Cap on total fees (if agreed)

3. DELIVERABLES
   - Written report (deadline: _____)
   - Deposition availability (dates: _____)
   - Trial testimony (dates: _____)
   - Supplemental reports if facts change

4. CONFIDENTIALITY
   - Work product protection
   - Attorney-client privilege limitations
   - Document retention requirements

5. CANCELLATION
   - Notice period for cancellation
   - Fees for cancelled deposition/trial
   - Refund of unused retainer
```

## Expert Report Requirements

### MCR 2.302(B)(4) — Discovery of Expert Opinions

Required disclosure contents:
1. Complete statement of all opinions to be expressed
2. Basis and reasons for each opinion
3. Data or information considered
4. Exhibits to be used as support
5. Qualifications (including publications)
6. List of other cases testified in (last 4 years)
7. Compensation for study and testimony

### Report Structure Template
```
EXPERT REPORT

I. QUALIFICATIONS
   [Name, credentials, experience summary]
   [CV attached as Appendix A]

II. RETENTION
    [Who retained the expert, when, for what purpose]
    [Fee arrangement — required disclosure]

III. MATERIALS REVIEWED
     [List every document, file, and source reviewed]

IV. METHODOLOGY
    [Description of analytical approach]
    [Why this methodology is appropriate]
    [Peer review and acceptance of methodology]

V. FACTS AND DATA
   [Specific facts relied upon]
   [Data sources and their reliability]

VI. ANALYSIS
    [Application of methodology to facts]
    [Step-by-step reasoning]

VII. OPINIONS
     Opinion 1: [Clear statement]
       Basis: [Specific facts and reasoning]
     
     Opinion 2: [Clear statement]
       Basis: [Specific facts and reasoning]
     
     [Continue for each opinion]

VIII. LIMITATIONS
      [What the expert cannot opine on]
      [Assumptions underlying the opinions]
      [Information that might change the opinions]

IX. SIGNATURE AND CERTIFICATION
    [Sworn statement that opinions are given to a
     reasonable degree of professional certainty]
    Date: ____________
    Signature: ____________
```

## Expert Deposition Preparation

### Preparing Your Expert
1. Review the report in detail — expert must know it cold
2. Identify weak points in the analysis and prepare explanations
3. Practice responding to hostile cross-examination
4. Review all materials the expert reviewed (opposing counsel will ask)
5. Prepare expert for questions about fees and litigation history
6. Remind expert: answer only the question asked, don't volunteer

### Key Cross-Examination Questions to Prepare For
```
"How much are you being paid for your testimony?"
"What percentage of your income comes from litigation work?"
"Have you ever been excluded by a court?"
"Did you consider [alternative methodology]?"
"Would your opinion change if [fact] were different?"
"Are you aware that [opposing expert] reached a different conclusion?"
"Isn't it true that [methodology] has been criticized by [source]?"
"You didn't examine [item/person] yourself, correct?"
```

## Budget Planning for Pro Se Expert Costs

### Cost-Reduction Strategies
1. **MRE 706 court appointment**: Request the court appoint and pay for the expert
2. **University experts**: Faculty and graduate students often charge less
3. **Limited scope**: Retain expert for specific issue only, not full case review
4. **Report only**: Some experts charge less for report without testimony expectation
5. **Pro bono programs**: Some experts offer reduced fees for self-represented parties
6. **Stipulation**: If parties agree on facts, expert testimony may be unnecessary
