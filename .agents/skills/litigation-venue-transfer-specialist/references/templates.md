## Petition for Transfer of Family Case (MCL 552.17a)

```
PETITION FOR TRANSFER OF DOMESTIC RELATIONS CASE

    Plaintiff petitions pursuant to MCL 552.17a for transfer of
this domestic relations case to [COUNTY] County Circuit Court:

    1. The original judgment was entered in Muskegon County Circuit
       Court (14th Judicial Circuit) on [DATE].

    2. Since entry of judgment, the following changes have occurred:
       a. [Party] now resides in [COUNTY] County.
       b. The minor child(ren) now reside in [COUNTY] County.
       c. [Other relevant changes in circumstances].

    3. Continuing jurisdiction in the 14th Circuit is no longer
       practical because: [reasons — distance, convenience, etc.]

    4. Transfer to [COUNTY] County Circuit Court would better serve
       the interests of the parties and the child(ren).

    5. The following matters are currently pending and would transfer:
       a. [Pending motions]
       b. [Pending orders]
       c. [FOC jurisdiction]

RELIEF REQUESTED:
    a. Transfer of the entire case to [COUNTY] County Circuit Court;
    b. Transfer of FOC jurisdiction;
    c. Continuation of all pending orders pending action by the
       transferee court.
```

## Forum Non Conveniens Motion

```
MOTION TO TRANSFER UNDER FORUM NON CONVENIENS

    Plaintiff moves pursuant to MCR 2.223 for transfer to [COUNTY]
County Circuit Court on grounds of forum non conveniens:

PRIVATE INTEREST FACTORS:
    1. Access to proof: [where evidence is located]
    2. Availability of compulsory process for witnesses: [which
       witnesses are in which county]
    3. Cost of attendance for witnesses: [distance / expense analysis]
    4. Practical problems affecting trial: [specify]

PUBLIC INTEREST FACTORS:
    5. Court congestion: [relative docket comparison if known]
    6. Local interest: [which community has stronger interest]
    7. Familiarity with governing law: [same Michigan law applies]

CONCLUSION:
    The balance of private and public interest factors strongly
favors transfer to [COUNTY] County. See Russell v Dep't of
Natural Resources, 185 Mich App 724 (1990).
```

## Workflow: Venue Transfer Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Identify venue issue (improper venue, convenience, or transfer)
    ↓
Step 2: Determine applicable legal basis
    ├── Improper venue → MCR 2.221 / MCL 600.1605+
    ├── Convenience → MCR 2.223 (forum non conveniens)
    ├── Family case transfer → MCL 552.17a
    ↓
Step 3: Prepare motion with supporting affidavit
    ↓
Step 4: File at earliest opportunity (before first responsive pleading)
    ↓
Step 5: Serve on all parties
    ↓
Step 6: Attend hearing on motion
    ↓
    ├── If GRANTED → Clerk transmits case file
    │   ↓ Verify FOC transfer
    │   ↓ Confirm pending orders continue
    ↓
    ├── If DENIED → Proceed in current venue
    │   ↓ Preserve issue for appeal if grounds are strong
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Late venue challenge | Waiver by participation | File before or with first responsive pleading |
| Confusing venue with jurisdiction | Different standards, different remedies | Identify the correct issue and cite correct rules |
| Not supporting with affidavit | MCR 2.221(B) requires factual support | Attach affidavit with specific facts |
| Ignoring continuing jurisdiction | MCL 552.17a controls in family cases | Use family case transfer procedures, not general venue |
| Forgetting FOC transfer | FOC jurisdiction doesn't automatically follow | Specifically request FOC jurisdiction transfer |
| No proposed receiving court | Motion must identify where to transfer | Name the specific court and county |
