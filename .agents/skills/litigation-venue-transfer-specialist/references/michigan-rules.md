# Michigan Rules — Venue Transfer Specialist

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.221 — Change of Venue
- **(A)**: Venue may be changed on motion when: (1) venue is improper, (2) impartial trial cannot be had, (3) convenience of parties and witnesses
- **(B)**: Motion must state facts supporting transfer; supported by affidavit
- **(C)**: Must be filed at **earliest opportunity** — before or with first responsive pleading; late motions require showing of good cause
- **(D)**: Court determines whether transfer is warranted

### MCR 2.222 — Venue Transfer Procedure
- **(A)**: When transfer is ordered, clerk transmits certified copies of all filings
- **(B)**: Transferee court accepts case as if originally filed there
- **(C)**: Pending orders remain in effect until modified by transferee court

### MCR 2.223 — Forum Non Conveniens
- **(A)**: Even if venue is proper, court may dismiss or transfer if another forum is more appropriate
- **(B)**: Factors: private interest (access to proof, cost, compulsory process for witnesses) and public interest (court congestion, local interest, conflicts of law)
- **(C)**: Burden on movant to show transfer is appropriate

### MCR 2.226 — Venue in Domestic Relations
- Domestic relations venue under MCL 552.9 and MCL 552.17a
- Continuing jurisdiction provisions

### MCR 3.211 — Domestic Relations: Transfer Between Courts
- **(A)**: Transfer of domestic relations cases when parties move
- **(B)**: Both sending and receiving courts must agree
- **(C)**: FOC jurisdiction transfers with the case

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.1601-1653 — Venue (General)
- **MCL 600.1605**: Venue in transitory actions — where defendant resides or where cause of action arose
- **MCL 600.1607**: Venue for actions against domestic corporations
- **MCL 600.1611**: Venue for land-related actions — where land is located
- **MCL 600.1621**: Venue in contract actions — where defendant resides or where contract performed
- **MCL 600.1629**: Change of venue for convenience
- **MCL 600.1631**: Change of venue for impartial trial
- **MCL 600.1641**: Transfer procedure between circuit courts
- **MCL 600.1651**: Effect of transfer — transferee court proceeds as if original filing
- **MCL 600.1653**: Costs and fees on transfer

### MCL 552.9 — Domestic Relations: Venue
- **(1)**: Divorce action filed in county where plaintiff or defendant resides
- **(2)**: Residency requirement: at least **180 days** in state, **10 days** in county before filing
- Establishes initial venue for domestic relations

### MCL 552.17a — Continuing Jurisdiction
- Court that enters judgment retains continuing jurisdiction
- Post-judgment matters filed in original court
- Transfer to another circuit requires petition and agreement of both courts

### MCL 600.1651 — Transfer Effect
- Transferred case proceeds as if originally filed in transferee court
- Transferee court has full jurisdiction over all matters
- No need to refile pleadings or motions

### MCL 722.31 — Change of Domicile (Related)
- Not a venue transfer but affects where child resides
- Must petition court before moving child's domicile 100+ miles
- Relevant when venue transfer and domicile change intersect

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Venue Standards
- **Omne Financial Inc v Shacks Inc**, 460 Mich 305 (1999) — Venue is governed by statute; court must apply statutory provisions to determine proper venue
- **Dimmitt & Owens Financial Inc v Deloitte & Touche**, 481 Mich 618 (2008) — Venue provisions are to be strictly applied; improper venue is waivable if not timely challenged
- **Russell v Dep't of Natural Resources**, 185 Mich App 724 (1990) — Forum non conveniens requires balancing private and public interest factors

### Family Law Venue and Transfer
- **Ojo v Ojo**, 197 Mich App 491 (1992) — Continuing jurisdiction under MCL 552.17a means post-judgment matters remain in the original court unless transferred by agreement
- **Reed v Reed**, 265 Mich App 131 (2005) — Transfer of domestic relations cases requires cooperation between sending and receiving circuits

### Waiver of Venue Objection
- **McLemore v Detroit Receiving Hospital**, 196 Mich App 391 (1992) — Failure to timely raise venue objection constitutes waiver; must be raised at the earliest opportunity

## Checklists

### Venue Challenge Checklist
- [ ] Determine whether issue is venue (MCR 2.221) or jurisdiction
- [ ] Identify proper venue under applicable MCL statute
- [ ] File motion before or with first responsive pleading
- [ ] Support motion with affidavit stating facts
- [ ] Identify the correct receiving court/county
- [ ] Address continuing jurisdiction (MCL 552.17a) if family case
- [ ] Prepare proposed order for transfer
- [ ] Serve motion on all parties

### Family Case Transfer Checklist
- [ ] Verify both parties' current county of residence
- [ ] Petition 14th Circuit (sending court) for transfer under MCL 552.17a
- [ ] Identify receiving circuit court and county
- [ ] Address FOC jurisdiction transfer
- [ ] Address pending orders and their continuation
- [ ] Obtain agreement of receiving court
- [ ] Arrange transmission of certified case file

### Filing Deadlines
- Venue objection: Before or with first responsive pleading (MCR 2.221(C))
- Forum non conveniens: At earliest opportunity
- Family case transfer petition: No statutory deadline; file when circumstances warrant
- Response to venue challenge: **21 days** (MCR 2.119(A)(2))
