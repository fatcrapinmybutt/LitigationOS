## Description

Use when the user needs to draft, serve, respond to, or enforce discovery requests under Michigan Court Rules—including interrogatories (MCR 2.309), requests for production (MCR 2.310), requests for admission (MCR 2.312), subpoenas (MCR 2.305/2.506), depositions (MCR 2.306), or when the user needs to craft objections, prepare privilege logs, file motions to compel (MCR 2.313), or seek discovery sanctions. Covers all discovery phases from initial disclosures through sanctions enforcement in the 14th Judicial Circuit, Muskegon County.

- User needs deposition strategy (notice, questions, designations)

## Michigan Discovery Scope — MCR 2.302(B)

**Scope:** Parties may obtain discovery regarding any matter, not privileged, that is relevant to the subject matter involved in the pending action. Information need not be admissible at trial if it appears reasonably calculated to lead to discovery of admissible evidence.

**Limitations (MCR 2.302(C)):**
- Unreasonably cumulative or duplicative
- Obtainable from more convenient/less expensive source
- Party has had ample opportunity to obtain information
- Burden/expense outweighs benefit

## Discovery Tool Selection Matrix

| Need | Tool | MCR | Timing | Limit |
|------|------|-----|--------|-------|
| Facts from parties | Interrogatories | 2.309 | After filing | No Michigan limit (but court may limit) |
| Documents from parties | RFP | 2.310 | After filing | No limit |
| Admissions of fact/genuineness | RFA | 2.312 | After filing | No limit (but reasonably targeted) |
| Documents from non-parties | Subpoena duces tecum | 2.305 | After filing | Must allow reasonable time |
| Testimony under oath | Deposition | 2.306 | After filing | 10 depositions per side (MCR 2.306(A)(2)) |
| Physical/mental examination | IME | 2.311 | After showing cause | Court order required |
| Testimony at trial from non-party | Trial subpoena | 2.506 | Before trial | 100-mile range |

## Discovery Sequencing Strategy

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1-4)
1. **RFAs** first — Lock down undisputed facts, force admissions or denials
2. **Interrogatories** — Identify witnesses, establish positions, discover document locations
3. **RFPs** — Request core documents identified through interrogatories

### Phase 2 — Deep Dive (Weeks 5-10)
4. **Subpoenas to non-parties** — Records from schools, doctors, employers, agencies
5. **Follow-up RFPs** — Based on information learned in Phase 1
6. **Supplemental interrogatories** — Fill gaps

### Phase 3 — Testimony (Weeks 11-16)
7. **Depositions** — Depose parties and key witnesses after document review
8. **Corporate designee depositions** — MCR 2.306(B)(5) for Shady Oaks/HoA/Alden
9. **Expert depositions** — If expert witnesses designated

### Phase 4 — Enforcement (Ongoing)
10. **Motions to compel** — For inadequate responses
11. **Sanctions motions** — For willful non-compliance
12. **RFA consequences** — Cost-shifting for matters that should have been admitted

## Interrogatory Drafting Rules

### Format Requirements (MCR 2.309)
- Written questions
- Served on party (not non-parties—use depositions for them)
- Answers under oath within 28 days
- Each subpart counts as a separate interrogatory (if court imposes limit)

### Effective Drafting Principles
1. **Define terms upfront** — "Communications," "Documents," "Relating to"
2. **Use contention interrogatories strategically** — "State all facts supporting your contention that..."
3. **Request identification of witnesses** — "Identify all persons with knowledge of..."
4. **Pin down document custodians** — "Identify the custodian of records for..."
5. **Force specific denial or admission** — "Do you contend that [specific fact]? If so, state all facts..."

## RFP Drafting Rules

### Format Requirements (MCR 2.310)
- Describe items with reasonable particularity
- Specify reasonable time, place, manner of inspection
- Response within 28 days
- Objections must be specific—general objections are insufficient

### Response Obligations
- Produce documents as kept in ordinary course of business OR organized by request category
- Privilege log required for withheld documents
- Must produce electronically stored information (ESI) in usable format

## RFA Tactical Deployment

### MCR 2.312 Power Moves
- Admitted facts are conclusively established for the pending action
- Failure to respond within 28 days = DEEMED ADMITTED
- Cost-shifting: If requesting party later proves matter that should have been admitted, court SHALL order reasonable expenses including attorney fees (MCR 2.312(D))
- Use RFAs to establish uncontested foundation, then build on admitted facts

### RFA Categories for Pigors
1. **Authenticity RFAs** — "Admit that [document] is a true and correct copy..."
2. **Fact RFAs** — "Admit that on [date], [event occurred]..."
3. **Legal conclusion RFAs** — "Admit that [party] owed a duty to [plaintiff]..."
4. **Contention RFAs** — "Admit that you have no evidence that [plaintiff's claim is false]..."

## Deposition Strategy

### MCR 2.306 Key Rules
- Oral examination by agreement or with reasonable notice (MCR 2.306(A))
- 10 depositions per side without leave of court
- 7-hour limit per deposition (1 day of 7 hours)
- Video depositions permitted with notice
- Corporate designee deposition: Notice describes topics; corporation designates witness(es)

## Subpoena Practice

### MCR 2.305 — Subpoena for Discovery
- Non-party document requests require subpoena
- Must allow at least 14 days for compliance
- Can combine with deposition notice
- Non-party may object in writing before compliance date

### MCR 2.506 — Subpoena for Trial
- 100-mile limit from place of attendance
- Must tender witness fee ($12/day) and mileage
- Failure to attend after service → contempt

## Objection Framework

Standard objections must be specific and cite basis:
1. **Relevance** — MCR 2.302(B)
2. **Privilege** — Attorney-client, work product, spousal, etc.
3. **Overbreadth** — Requests broader than necessary
4. **Burden** — MCR 2.302(C) proportionality
5. **Vague/ambiguous** — Terms undefined or unclear
6. **Compound** — Multiple questions in one
7. **Premature expert opinion** — Seeks expert testimony before designation

**Golden rule:** Object specifically and STILL answer to the extent not objectionable. General "boilerplate" objections waive the objection.

## Motion to Compel — MCR 2.313(A)

### Elements
1. Discovery request properly served
2. Response inadequate, incomplete, or objections without merit
3. Requesting party made good-faith effort to resolve without court intervention
4. Specific identification of deficient responses

### Good Faith Requirement
- MCR 2.313(A)(5): Motion must include certification that movant "in good faith conferred or attempted to confer" with opposing party
- Document ALL meet-and-confer attempts (dates, methods, substance)
- For pro se against pro se: Written correspondence demanding compliance

### Sanctions Available (MCR 2.313(A)(5))
- Reasonable expenses including attorney fees (or equivalent for pro se)
- Unless: Objection was substantially justified OR award would be unjust

1. State the discovery type and governing MCR
2. Include proper caption with case number and judge
3. Draft with definitions and instructions sections
4. Number each request sequentially
5. Include certificate of service
6. Flag timing deadlines (28-day response window)
