# Motion to Compel + MCR 2.313 Sanctions Framework

> Complete framework for enforcing discovery obligations in Michigan courts.
> Covers motion to compel mechanics, good-faith certification, sanctions tiers,
> and practical templates for pro se plaintiff in the 14th Judicial Circuit.

---

## I. Motion to Compel — MCR 2.313(A)

### Prerequisites

1. **Valid discovery request served:** Request must have been properly served per MCR 2.107
2. **Inadequate response:** Response was incomplete, evasive, late, or improperly objected to
3. **Good-faith meet-and-confer:** Moving party must certify efforts to resolve without court intervention
4. **Timely filing:** Filed promptly after failed resolution efforts (not stockpiled)

### Good-Faith Conference Requirement — MCR 2.313(A)(5)

The motion MUST include a certification that the movant:
> "in good faith conferred or attempted to confer with the party or person failing to make the discovery in an effort to secure the information or material without court action"

**What satisfies good faith:**
- Written letter/email specifically identifying deficient responses
- Reasonable time for opposing party to cure (14 days minimum)
- At least TWO documented attempts to resolve
- Offer to narrow or clarify requests if objections have partial merit

**What does NOT satisfy good faith:**
- Single phone call with no follow-up
- Demand letter threatening immediate motion without discussion
- Refusal to consider opponent's legitimate concerns

```

[Date]
```

## II. Sanctions Framework — MCR 2.313

### Sanctions for Failure to Comply with Discovery Order — MCR 2.313(B)(2)

After a court order compelling discovery, if the party STILL fails to comply:

| Sanction Level | MCR Provision | Description | When Appropriate |
|----------------|---------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Level 1: Expenses** | 2.313(A)(5) | Reasonable expenses including attorney fees | First failure; any motion to compel |
| **Level 2: Facts established** | 2.313(B)(2)(a) | Matters addressed in order deemed established | Repeated refusal to answer specific questions |
| **Level 3: Claim/defense stricken** | 2.313(B)(2)(a) | Refusing party cannot support/oppose claims | Persistent obstruction on core issues |
| **Level 4: Evidence excluded** | 2.313(B)(2)(b) | Disobedient party cannot introduce evidence on topics | Refusal to produce documents later sought at trial |
| **Level 5: Pleading stricken** | 2.313(B)(2)(b) | Parts of pleading stricken | Egregious non-compliance |
| **Level 6: Stay proceedings** | 2.313(B)(2)(c) | Action stayed until order obeyed | Pending compliance with prior order |
| **Level 7: Dismissal/Default** | 2.313(B)(2)(c) | Action dismissed or default entered | Willful, contumacious, repeated violation |
| **Level 8: Contempt** | 2.313(B)(2)(d) | Party held in contempt of court | Direct defiance of court order |

### Expense Shifting — MCR 2.313(A)(5)

**Mandatory presumption:** When motion to compel is GRANTED, court SHALL require the losing party to pay movant's reasonable expenses including attorney fees, UNLESS:

1. Movant failed to make good-faith effort to resolve without court action
2. Opposing party's position was **substantially justified**
3. Other circumstances make an award **unjust**

**When motion is DENIED:** Court SHALL require movant to pay respondent's expenses, unless motion was substantially justified or award would be unjust.

**When GRANTED IN PART:** Court may apportion expenses as justice requires.

### Pro Se Fee Equivalent

Michigan courts recognize that pro se litigants can recover reasonable expenses equivalent to attorney fees for discovery sanctions:

- Document hours spent on discovery dispute
- Reasonable hourly rate based on local market (research Muskegon County rates)
- Include: time drafting meet-and-confer letters, reviewing deficient responses, researching law, drafting motion, attending hearing
- Also include: filing fees, copying costs, postage/service costs

## III. Specific Sanction Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Total Failure to Respond

**Problem:** Defendant completely ignores discovery requests.

**Response sequence:**
1. Send meet-and-confer letter (allow 14 days)
2. Send follow-up letter (allow 7 more days)
3. File motion to compel with expense request
4. If order entered and still no compliance → motion for Level 5-7 sanctions

### Scenario 2: Boilerplate Objections Without Substantive Response

**Problem:** Defendant responds with only general objections and no information.

**Response sequence:**
1. Meet-and-confer identifying specific deficient responses
2. Motion to compel arguing boilerplate objections are waived
3. Request court overrule objections and compel complete responses
4. Cite: Objections must be specific (MCR 2.309(B)(4))

### Scenario 3: Incomplete or Evasive Answers

**Problem:** Defendant provides some information but dodges key questions.

**Response sequence:**
1. Identify specific gaps in responses
2. Meet-and-confer with detailed comparison of request vs. response
3. Motion to compel on specific deficiencies
4. Request deeming matters established (Level 2) if pattern persists

### Scenario 4: Late RFA Responses (After 28-Day Deadline)

**Problem:** Defendant fails to timely respond to RFAs.

**Response sequence:**
1. All RFAs deemed admitted per MCR 2.312(B)(1) — automatic
2. If defendant moves to withdraw admissions, oppose unless good cause shown
3. MCR 2.312(D): Court MAY permit withdrawal only if:
   - Presentation of merits will be served AND
   - Party relying on admission will not be prejudiced

### Scenario 5: Spoliation / Document Destruction

**Problem:** Evidence destroyed after litigation obligation attached.

**Response sequence:**
1. Preservation duty attaches when litigation is reasonably anticipated
2. File motion for adverse inference instruction
3. Seek sanctions under MCR 2.313(B)(2)
4. Consider separate tort claim for spoliation (Michigan recognizes this)

## IV. Sanctions Motion Template

```
PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR SANCTIONS UNDER MCR 2.313(B)(2)

1. On [DATE], this Court entered an order compelling Defendant to [describe
   discovery ordered]. [Attach order as Exhibit A.]

2. The Court's order required compliance by [DATE].

3. Defendant has [failed to comply / partially complied as follows:].

4. Plaintiff requests the following sanctions:
   [Select appropriate sanctions from the tiered framework above]

5. Sanctions are warranted because Defendant's non-compliance is [willful /
   without substantial justification / contumacious].

6. Plaintiff has been prejudiced by Defendant's non-compliance in that
   [specify: unable to prepare for trial, unable to prove element of claim,
   delayed proceedings, incurred unnecessary expenses].

EXPENSES INCURRED

Plaintiff has incurred the following reasonable expenses as a result of
Defendant's non-compliance:

| Item | Hours/Units | Rate | Total |
|------|-------------|------|-------|
| Drafting meet-and-confer correspondence | X hrs | $Y/hr | $Z |
| Researching applicable law | X hrs | $Y/hr | $Z |
| Drafting motion for sanctions | X hrs | $Y/hr | $Z |
| Filing fee | 1 | $X | $X |
| Service/mailing costs | 1 | $X | $X |
| **TOTAL** | | | **$Z** |
```

## V. Escalation Decision Tree

```
Discovery request served
│
├─ Response received within 28 days?
│   ├─ NO → Meet-and-confer → Motion to compel
│   └─ YES → Response adequate?
│       ├─ YES → Proceed with case
│       └─ NO → Identify specific deficiencies
│           └─ Meet-and-confer on deficiencies
│               ├─ Resolved → Proceed
│               └─ Not resolved → Motion to compel
│                   ├─ GRANTED → Order entered
│                   │   ├─ Complied → Proceed + collect expenses
│                   │   └─ NOT complied → Motion for sanctions
│                   │       └─ Escalate through sanction tiers
│                   └─ DENIED → Evaluate if appeal warranted
```
