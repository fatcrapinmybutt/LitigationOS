# Complete Objection Library — Michigan Discovery Practice

> Catalog of every recognized discovery objection with MCR/MRE authority.
> Each objection includes when to assert, when NOT to assert, and sample language.

---

## I. Relevance Objections

### 1. Not Relevant to Subject Matter
**Authority:** MCR 2.302(B)(1)
**Standard:** Information must be relevant to the subject matter involved in the action, or reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence.

**When to assert:**
- Request has no conceivable connection to any claim or defense
- Information sought relates to a completely different matter

**When NOT to assert:**
- Request is relevant but embarrassing or inconvenient
- Connection exists but is attenuated—discovery scope is broad

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This request seeks information that is neither relevant to the subject matter of this action nor reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence, as required by MCR 2.302(B)(1). [Specify why the information has no connection to any claim or defense.]"

### 2. Not Proportional to Needs of Case
**Authority:** MCR 2.302(C)
**Standard:** Discovery may be limited if burden or expense outweighs its likely benefit, considering the needs of the case, amount in controversy, parties' resources, importance of issues, and importance of discovery in resolving issues.

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. The burden and expense of responding to this request is not proportional to the needs of the case under MCR 2.302(C). [Specify: e.g., 'The request seeks 10 years of records when the relevant period is 2 years.']"

## II. Privilege Objections

### 3. Attorney-Client Privilege
**Authority:** MRE 501; MCL 767.5a(2)
**Elements:** (1) Communication; (2) made in confidence; (3) between client and attorney; (4) for purpose of obtaining legal advice.

**Critical requirement:** Must produce privilege log identifying each withheld document/communication.

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This request seeks information protected by the attorney-client privilege. Without waiving said privilege, [party] identifies the following privileged communications in the accompanying privilege log."

### 4. Work Product Doctrine
**Authority:** MCR 2.302(B)(3)
**Scope:** Documents and tangible things prepared in anticipation of litigation or for trial by or for a party or representative.

**Qualified vs. Absolute:**
- **Fact work product** (qualified): Can be overcome by showing substantial need + inability to obtain without undue hardship
- **Opinion work product** (absolute): Mental impressions, conclusions, opinions, legal theories of attorney—nearly always protected

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This request seeks documents constituting attorney work product prepared in anticipation of litigation, protected under MCR 2.302(B)(3). The documents reflect [counsel's/party's] mental impressions, conclusions, and legal theories. [Party] asserts both qualified and opinion work product protection."

### 5. Spousal Privilege
**Authority:** MCL 600.2162
**Scope:** Communications between spouses during marriage are privileged.

### 6. Physician-Patient Privilege
**Authority:** MCL 600.2157
**Exception:** Waived when patient puts physical/mental condition at issue.

### 7. Psychotherapist-Patient Privilege
**Authority:** MCL 330.1750
**Scope:** Broader protection than physician-patient; harder to waive.

### 8. Mediation Confidentiality
**Authority:** MCR 2.412
**Scope:** Communications made during court-connected mediation are confidential and not discoverable.

## III. Procedural Objections

### 9. Vague and Ambiguous
**When to assert:** Request uses undefined terms susceptible to multiple interpretations.

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This request is vague and ambiguous in its use of the term '[term],' which is susceptible to multiple interpretations and fails to describe the information sought with the reasonable particularity required by [MCR 2.309 / MCR 2.310(B)(1)]."

### 10. Overbroad
**When to assert:** Request sweeps in irrelevant information along with relevant.
**Distinguish from:** Relevance (overbroad requests may include some relevant information).

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This request is overbroad in that it is not limited to [relevant time period / relevant subject matter / relevant persons] and would require production of vast quantities of information irrelevant to any claim or defense in this action."

### 11. Unduly Burdensome
**Authority:** MCR 2.302(C)
**Requirement:** Must explain the specific burden (cost, hours, disruption) and why it outweighs the benefit.

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This request is unduly burdensome under MCR 2.302(C). Responding would require [specific burden, e.g., 'review of approximately 50,000 documents spanning 15 years'] at an estimated cost of [$X], which is disproportionate to the needs of this case."

### 12. Compound / Multipart
**When to assert:** Single interrogatory contains multiple discrete questions.

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This interrogatory is compound in that it contains [number] discrete questions within a single numbered interrogatory. Answering party responds to each subpart to the extent ascertainable."

### 13. Premature / Seeks Information Not Yet Available
**When to assert:** Request seeks expert opinions before expert designation deadline, or information that does not yet exist.

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This request is premature in that it seeks [expert opinions prior to the expert designation deadline / information that does not yet exist]."

### 14. Assumes Facts Not in Evidence
**When to assert (interrogatories):** Question embeds a factual premise that answering would implicitly admit.

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This interrogatory improperly assumes facts not in evidence, specifically that [identify assumed fact]. Without admitting the assumed fact, [party] responds as follows: ..."

## IV. Duplicative / Harassment Objections

### 15. Unreasonably Cumulative or Duplicative
**Authority:** MCR 2.302(C)

**Sample language:**
> "Objection. This request is unreasonably cumulative and duplicative of [identify prior request, e.g., 'Interrogatory No. 3'] and seeks information already provided in Plaintiff's response to [prior discovery]. See MCR 2.302(C)."

### 16. Harassment / Annoyance
**Authority:** MCR 2.302(C); MCR 2.305(B) (protective order)
**When to assert:** Discovery served for improper purpose—to harass, embarrass, or impose undue burden.

## V. Form Objections

### 17. Improper Form — Interrogatory Calls for Legal Conclusion
**When to assert:** Interrogatory asks for pure legal opinion rather than facts.
**Caveat:** Contention interrogatories ("state all facts supporting...") ARE proper.

### 18. Improper Form — RFP Lacks Reasonable Particularity
**Authority:** MCR 2.310(B)(1)
**When to assert:** Request for production does not describe items sought with sufficient specificity.

### 19. Request for Admission — Not a Statement of Fact
**Authority:** MCR 2.312(A)
**When to assert:** RFA asks for opinion, legal conclusion, or mixed fact/law beyond scope.

## VI. Special Michigan Objections

### 20. MCL 722.27b — Children's Records in Custody Proceedings
**Scope:** Special protections for children's records; may require in camera review.

### 21. MCR 3.218 — FOC Confidentiality
**Scope:** FOC investigation reports and recommendations have limited disclosure rules.
**When to assert:** Opposing party seeks FOC internal deliberative materials.

### 22. MCL 600.2157a — Domestic Violence Counselor Privilege
**When to assert:** Request seeks communications with certified DV counselor.

### 23. MCL 330.1748 — Substance Abuse Treatment Records
**Federal overlay:** 42 CFR Part 2 may also apply to substance abuse records.

## VII. Objection Best Practices

### Do's
- State specific grounds for each objection
- Cite the applicable MCR or MRE
- Answer to the extent not objectionable (MCR 2.309(B)(4))
- Produce privilege log for all privilege claims
- Preserve the objection in writing within 28 days

### Don'ts
- Don't use boilerplate "general objections" at the start of responses
- Don't assert privilege without identifying specific communications
- Don't object on relevance grounds alone without specificity
- Don't assert "overly broad" without explaining the overbreadth
- Don't refuse to answer entirely when only part is objectionable

### Objection Preservation Checklist

```
□ Objection stated within 28-day response window
□ Specific grounds identified for each objection
□ MCR/MRE authority cited
□ Non-objectionable portion answered
□ Privilege log prepared (if privilege asserted)
□ Meet-and-confer offered on disputed objections
□ Copy retained in discovery file with date stamp
```
