# Gotchas — Summary Judgment Engine

## Anti-Rationalization Matrix

| Trap | What It Looks Like | Why It's Wrong | Correct Approach |
|------|-------------------|----------------|------------------|
| Filing before discovery is complete | Moving for summary disposition under MCR 2.116(C)(10) before opposing party has had adequate discovery | MCR 2.116(G)(4) allows the court to deny or defer if the opposing party shows facts essential to justify opposition cannot yet be presented | Complete discovery first; or file under (C)(8) or (C)(7) if the legal basis exists independent of facts |
| Wrong subsection of MCR 2.116 | Filing under (C)(10) when (C)(7) or (C)(8) is the proper ground | Each subsection has different standards; (C)(10) requires "no genuine issue of material fact" while (C)(8) tests legal sufficiency of the pleading; using the wrong one invites confusion and denial | Analyze which subsection fits: (C)(7) for immunity/statute of limitations, (C)(8) for pleading failure, (C)(10) for factual insufficiency |
| Treating all facts as undisputed | Presenting your version of disputed facts as though they are undisputed | Under Maiden v Rozwood, the court views evidence in the light most favorable to the non-moving party; disputed facts preclude summary disposition | Identify genuinely undisputed facts; acknowledge disputed facts; argue that even viewing disputed facts favorably to the other side, you prevail |
| Inadequate response to motion | Responding with conclusory denials or argument rather than specific evidence | MCR 2.116(G)(4) requires the non-moving party to set forth specific facts by affidavit or other documentary evidence showing a genuine issue; bare allegations are insufficient | Attach affidavits, depositions, interrogatory answers, and documentary evidence to the response brief |
| Missing the 21-day response deadline | Failing to respond within 21 days, resulting in summary disposition granted as unopposed | Under MCR 2.119(A)(2), response briefs are due within 21 days; the court may grant the motion without considering the merits | Calendar the deadline immediately; file even a partial response to avoid default |

## Failure Modes
1. **Premature Motion Denied**: Court denies summary disposition as premature under MCR 2.116(G)(4) because discovery is incomplete. Recovery: Complete discovery; refile when factual record is developed; avoid wasting court resources on premature motions.
2. **Genuine Issue Found**: Court finds genuine issues of material fact that preclude summary disposition. Recovery: Proceed to trial on the contested issues; use the motion process to narrow the issues for trial.
3. **Conversion to Different Subsection**: Court converts motion from one (C) subsection to another under MCR 2.116(I)(5). Recovery: Be prepared for conversion by briefing alternative subsections in the original motion.
4. **Affidavit Deficiency**: Supporting affidavits fail to meet MCR 2.116(G)(6) requirements (personal knowledge, specific facts). Recovery: File supplemental affidavits that cure the deficiency; ensure affiants have personal knowledge of the facts stated.

## Integration Gotchas
- When combining with **litigation-interrogatory-specialist**, use interrogatory answers as evidence supporting summary disposition — verified interrogatory answers are admissions that can establish undisputed facts.
- Data flow from **litigation-motion-practice** requires that summary disposition briefs comply with both MCR 2.116 substantive requirements AND MCR 2.119 procedural requirements (notice, timing, brief format).
- Michigan-specific pitfall: In family law cases, summary disposition is rarely appropriate for custody determinations because the best interest factors under MCL 722.23 are inherently fact-intensive. Summary disposition is more useful for ancillary issues (property claims, tort claims joined with the family action). Know when to use it and when trial is unavoidable.
