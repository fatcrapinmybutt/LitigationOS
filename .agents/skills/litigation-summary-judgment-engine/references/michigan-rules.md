# Michigan Rules — Summary Judgment Engine

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.116 — Summary Disposition
- **(C)**: Grounds for summary disposition (ten subsections):
  - **(C)(1)**: Subject matter jurisdiction lacking
  - **(C)(2)**: Personal jurisdiction lacking
  - **(C)(3)**: Venue improper
  - **(C)(4)**: Process or service of process insufficient
  - **(C)(5)**: Same action pending between same parties in another court
  - **(C)(6)**: Claim or defense is barred by prior judgment, res judicata, or statute of limitations
  - **(C)(7)**: Claim barred because of release, immunity, statute of limitations, or other defense that need not be supported by facts
  - **(C)(8)**: Opposing party failed to state a claim on which relief can be granted (equivalent to federal 12(b)(6))
  - **(C)(9)**: Opposing party failed to state a valid defense
  - **(C)(10)**: No genuine issue of material fact; moving party entitled to judgment as a matter of law (equivalent to federal Rule 56)
- **(G)**: Procedures:
  - **(G)(1)**: Timing — may be filed at any time
  - **(G)(2)**: Court must consider all affidavits, depositions, admissions, and documentary evidence
  - **(G)(3)**: Affidavits must be based on personal knowledge and set forth specific facts (not conclusions)
  - **(G)(4)**: Non-moving party must set forth specific facts showing genuine issue for trial; if they cannot, court may grant motion
  - **(G)(5)**: If facts essential to opposition require further discovery, court may adjourn the motion
  - **(G)(6)**: Affidavit requirements — personal knowledge, admissible facts, specific facts
- **(I)**: Court powers:
  - **(I)(1)**: Court may grant on grounds not raised if record supports
  - **(I)(5)**: Court may convert motion from one subsection to another

### MCR 2.119 — Motion Practice (Procedural Overlay)
- **(A)(2)**: Brief required with citations to authority
- **(A)(2)**: Response brief within **21 days**
- **(A)(3)**: Reply brief within **7 days**
- **(C)**: Notice of hearing — **9 days** (14 by mail)

### MCR 2.116 Cross-Reference — Standard of Review on Appeal
- (C)(7): De novo review (legal question)
- (C)(8): De novo review (legal sufficiency of pleading)
- (C)(10): De novo review (viewed in light most favorable to non-moving party)

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.2912d — Affidavit of Merit (Professional Malpractice)
- Required for medical malpractice summary disposition motions
- Cross-reference if malpractice claims are joined with family action

### MCL 600.5805 — Statute of Limitations (General)
- **(1)**: Three-year SOL for personal injury actions
- **(2)**: Six-year SOL for contract actions
- **(8)**: Two-year SOL for malpractice
- Summary disposition under (C)(7) when SOL defense applies

### MCL 600.5807 — Statute of Limitations (Specific Actions)
- Various specific limitation periods
- Discovery rule may toll the limitation period

### MCL 600.2591 — Frivolous Claims
- Summary disposition may be accompanied by request for sanctions
- Frivolous defense may support summary disposition

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Summary Disposition Standards
- **Maiden v Rozwood**, 461 Mich 109 (1999) — Comprehensive standards for summary disposition under all subsections; (C)(10) requires court to view evidence in light most favorable to non-moving party; moving party must specifically identify undisputed issues
- **Quinto v Cross & Peters Co**, 451 Mich 358 (1996) — Non-moving party must present specific evidentiary facts showing genuine issue; speculation is insufficient
- **Skinner v Square D Co**, 445 Mich 153 (1994) — "Give the benefit of reasonable doubt" to non-moving party; summary disposition inappropriate when reasonable minds could differ on material facts

### Timing and Prematurity
- **Peterson v Auto Owners Ins Co**, 274 Mich App 407 (2007) — Summary disposition premature when further discovery would likely produce evidence creating genuine issue
- **Coblentz v City of Novi**, 475 Mich 558 (2006) — Court has discretion to defer or deny motion when discovery is incomplete

### Affidavit Requirements
- **SSC Associates LP v Gen Retirement System**, 192 Mich App 360 (1991) — Affidavits must set forth facts admissible in evidence; legal conclusions and hearsay are insufficient
- **Innovative Adult Foster Care v Ragin**, 285 Mich App 466 (2009) — Personal knowledge requirement is strictly enforced

### Conversion Between Subsections
- **Kefgen v Davidson**, 241 Mich App 611 (2000) — Court may convert C(8) motion to C(10) but must give non-moving party opportunity to present evidence

## Checklists

### Moving Party Checklist (Filing for Summary Disposition)
- [ ] Identify correct MCR 2.116(C) subsection
- [ ] Prepare motion with supporting brief (MCR 2.119(A)(2))
- [ ] Attach affidavits based on personal knowledge (MCR 2.116(G)(6))
- [ ] Attach documentary evidence (depositions, admissions, documents)
- [ ] Identify each issue for which no genuine dispute exists
- [ ] Prepare proposed order
- [ ] Serve with proper notice (9 days / 14 by mail)
- [ ] File proof of service

### Non-Moving Party Checklist (Responding)
- [ ] Calendar **21-day** response deadline
- [ ] Determine which (C) subsection was invoked — tailor response
- [ ] Prepare response brief with contrary authority
- [ ] Attach counter-affidavits with specific facts
- [ ] Identify genuine issues of material fact
- [ ] If discovery needed, file MCR 2.116(G)(5) affidavit requesting adjournment
- [ ] Request oral argument

### Filing Deadlines
- Motion filing: Any time during litigation (MCR 2.116(G)(1))
- Response brief: **21 days** from service (MCR 2.119(A)(2))
- Reply brief: **7 days** from response service (MCR 2.119(A)(3))
- Appeal of summary disposition: **21 days** from order (MCR 7.204(A))
