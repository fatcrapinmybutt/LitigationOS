## Response to Motion for Summary Disposition

```
RESPONSE IN OPPOSITION TO MOTION FOR SUMMARY DISPOSITION

I. COUNTER-STATEMENT OF FACTS:
    1. The following material facts ARE in genuine dispute:
       a. [Disputed fact — cite contrary evidence (affidavit, depo)]
       b. [Disputed fact — cite contrary evidence]

II. ARGUMENT:
    2. Summary disposition is inappropriate because:
       a. Genuine issues of material fact exist (MCR 2.116(G)(4));
       b. Viewing evidence in the light most favorable to Plaintiff
          [or Defendant], reasonable minds could differ;
       c. [Citation to Maiden v Rozwood, 461 Mich 109 (1999)]

    3. [If discovery needed]: Pursuant to MCR 2.116(G)(5), essential
       facts cannot yet be presented because [explain what discovery
       is needed]. See attached affidavit.

III. CONCLUSION:
    The motion should be denied.
```

## Summary Disposition Decision Matrix

| Basis | MCR Subsection | Standard | Evidence Needed | Appellate Review |
|-------|---------------|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Jurisdiction lacking | (C)(1)-(2) | Legal question | None | De novo |
| Improper venue | (C)(3) | Legal question | None | De novo |
| Service deficiency | (C)(4) | Procedural | Service records | De novo |
| Statute of limitations | (C)(7) | Legal defense | Timeline | De novo |
| Failure to state claim | (C)(8) | Pleading sufficiency | Pleadings only | De novo |
| No genuine fact issue | (C)(10) | No material dispute | Full record | De novo |

## Workflow: Summary Disposition Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Identify the issue for summary disposition
    ↓
Step 2: Determine correct MCR 2.116(C) subsection
    ↓
Step 3: Assess whether discovery is sufficient
    ↓
Step 4: Gather supporting evidence (affidavits, documents)
    ↓
Step 5: Draft motion, brief, and proposed order
    ↓
Step 6: Serve with proper notice
    ↓
Step 7: Await response (21 days)
    ↓
Step 8: File reply brief if needed (7 days)
    ↓
Step 9: Attend oral argument
    ↓
    ├── If GRANTED → Enter judgment
    ├── If DENIED → Proceed to trial on those issues
    ├── If DEFERRED → Complete discovery and refile
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Filing before discovery | Court defers or denies as premature | Complete discovery first unless purely legal issue |
| Wrong (C) subsection | Different standards apply; motion may be denied | Analyze which subsection fits your argument |
| Conclusory affidavits | Fail MCR 2.116(G)(6) requirements | Specific facts based on personal knowledge only |
| Ignoring favorable-light standard | Court views facts against movant | Acknowledge disputed facts; argue you still prevail |
| No response to opposition motion | Granted as unopposed; devastating | Always respond within 21 days, even briefly |
| Using for custody disputes | Custody is fact-intensive; rarely granted | Save for ancillary legal issues, not custody itself |
