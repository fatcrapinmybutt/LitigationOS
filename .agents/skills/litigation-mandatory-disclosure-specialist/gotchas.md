# Gotchas — Mandatory Disclosure Specialist

## Anti-Rationalization Matrix

| Trap | What It Looks Like | Why It's Wrong | Correct Approach |
|------|-------------------|----------------|------------------|
| Treating disclosure as optional | Assuming disclosures are only required if the other side requests them | MCR 3.206(B) requires mandatory financial disclosures in domestic relations cases regardless of request | Serve the Verified Financial Information Statement and supporting documents per MCR 3.206(B) timeline |
| Incomplete financial disclosure | Disclosing only W-2 income and ignoring side income, gifts, or benefits | Courts require disclosure of ALL sources of income including cash, barter, gifts, and imputed income under MCL 552.602(f) | Disclose every income source; failure to disclose is sanctionable and destroys credibility |
| Missing the disclosure deadline | Filing the Verified Financial Information Statement weeks late | MCR 3.206(B)(1) requires the statement within 14 days of the scheduling conference or other court-ordered deadline | Calendar the deadline immediately after the scheduling conference; seek extension before expiration if needed |
| Failing to update disclosures | Treating initial disclosure as final even when financial circumstances change | MCR 2.302(E) duty to supplement applies to mandatory disclosures; changed circumstances must be reported | Monitor for material changes (job change, new asset, inheritance) and file supplemental disclosures promptly |
| Not verifying opposing party's disclosures | Accepting the other side's financial disclosures at face value | Non-disclosure and under-disclosure are common in family law; unchallenged false disclosures become the record | Cross-reference disclosures against tax returns, bank statements, employer records; file motion to compel if deficient |

## Failure Modes
1. **Sanction for Non-Disclosure**: Court enters adverse inference or strikes pleadings for failure to disclose. Recovery: File disclosures immediately and move to set aside sanctions under MCR 2.612(C)(1); demonstrate excusable neglect and absence of prejudice.
2. **Hidden Asset Discovery Post-Judgment**: Assets not disclosed during proceedings are discovered after judgment. Recovery: File motion to set aside property division under MCL 552.401 (fraud on the court); hidden assets may warrant reopening the entire financial settlement.
3. **Income Imputation**: Court imputes income based on incomplete disclosures. Recovery: File supplemental disclosure with complete income documentation; move to modify if imputed income was based on incomplete record.
4. **Discovery Sanctions Cascade**: Opposing party moves for sanctions after discovery reveals information that should have been in initial disclosures. Recovery: File amended disclosures immediately; demonstrate good faith effort to comply; argue proportionate sanctions.

## Integration Gotchas
- When combining with **litigation-interrogatory-specialist**, ensure interrogatory answers are consistent with mandatory disclosures — contradictions between the two are devastating at trial.
- Data flow from **litigation-settlement-analyzer** requires complete financial disclosure as input; settlement analysis built on incomplete disclosures produces unreliable results and may constitute fraud on the court.
- Michigan-specific pitfall: The Verified Financial Information Statement (SCAO form FOC 23) requires sworn attestation. A material omission on this form is not just a discovery violation — it is potential perjury under MCL 750.423.
