# Michigan Rules — Mandatory Disclosure Specialist

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 3.206 — Domestic Relations: Interim and Permanent Orders
- **(B) Verified Financial Information**:
  - **(B)(1)**: Each party must serve a Verified Financial Information Statement within the time set by the court or within **14 days** of the scheduling conference
  - **(B)(2)**: Statement must include income, assets, liabilities, and expenses
  - **(B)(3)**: Supporting documentation must include most recent tax returns, pay stubs, bank statements
  - **(B)(4)**: Failure to provide verified financial information may result in sanctions

### MCR 3.201 — Applicability of Civil Rules to Domestic Relations
- Civil discovery rules (MCR 2.301-2.314) apply to domestic relations cases
- Additional disclosure requirements supplement standard discovery

### MCR 2.302 — General Discovery Rules
- **(A)**: Broad scope of discoverable information
- **(E)**: Duty to supplement prior disclosures when information changes
- **(F)**: Agreements regarding discovery modifications

### MCR 2.313 — Failure to Provide Discovery
- **(A)**: Motion to compel when disclosures are inadequate
- **(B)(2)**: Sanctions for non-compliance: adverse findings, dismissal, contempt
- **(C)**: Expenses and attorney fees for successful motions to compel

### MCR 3.208 — Friend of the Court
- FOC reviews financial disclosures for child support calculations
- FOC may request additional documentation
- FOC recommendation based on disclosed financial information

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 552.602 — Income Defined (Child Support)
- **(f)**: Income includes: salaries, wages, commissions, bonuses, overtime, self-employment income, interest, dividends, trust income, annuities, capital gains, Social Security, workers' compensation, unemployment, disability, pensions, spousal support received, and any other gain
- Critical: income is broadly defined to prevent non-disclosure

### MCL 552.605 — Uniform Child Support Formula
- Requires accurate income disclosure for proper calculation
- Both parties' income must be fully disclosed
- Michigan Child Support Formula Manual governs calculations

### MCL 552.401 — Property Division: Fraud
- If a party fraudulently conceals assets, court may reopen property division
- No time limit when fraud is involved
- Hidden assets discovered post-judgment may warrant relief

### MCL 552.23 — Property Division
- Equitable division requires full financial disclosure
- Court considers all property including separately held assets
- Disclosure must cover both marital and separate property

### MCL 552.13 — Spousal Support
- Income disclosure required for alimony determination
- All sources of income relevant to ability to pay and need

### MCL 750.423 — Perjury
- Material false statement under oath in verified financial disclosure
- Felony: up to 15 years imprisonment
- Verified Financial Information Statement is a sworn document

### MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors
- Factor (c): Capacity to provide food, clothing, medical care — requires financial disclosure
- Factor (d): Stability of custodial environment — financial stability relevant
- Financial disclosures feed directly into best interest analysis

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Disclosure Obligations
- **Sands v Sands**, 442 Mich 30 (1993) — Full financial disclosure is essential to equitable property division; failure to disclose may warrant reopening judgment
- **Draggoo v Draggoo**, 223 Mich App 415 (1997) — Court has broad discretion in fashioning remedies for non-disclosure including adverse inferences
- **Hanaway v Hanaway**, 208 Mich App 278 (1995) — Each party has affirmative duty to disclose all assets; passive non-disclosure is still sanctionable

### Hidden Assets
- **Kurz v Kurz**, 178 Mich App 284 (1989) — Concealment of assets constitutes fraud on the court; judgment may be reopened regardless of time elapsed
- **Yeo v Yeo**, 214 Mich App 598 (1995) — Party who conceals assets may forfeit their interest in the concealed property

### Income Calculation
- **Ghidotti v Barber**, 459 Mich 189 (1998) — Income for child support purposes must include all sources defined in MCL 552.602
- **Stallworth v Stallworth**, 275 Mich App 282 (2007) — Court may impute income when party is voluntarily unemployed or underemployed

- **MC 20**: Fee Waiver Request (if applicable)

## Checklists

### Mandatory Disclosure Checklist
- [ ] Complete FOC 23 Verified Financial Information Statement
- [ ] Attach last 3 years of federal and state tax returns
- [ ] Attach last 3 months of pay stubs
- [ ] Attach last 3 months of bank statements (all accounts)
- [ ] Disclose all real property with valuations
- [ ] Disclose all vehicles with valuations
- [ ] Disclose all retirement accounts with current balances
- [ ] Disclose all debts and liabilities
- [ ] Disclose all sources of non-employment income
- [ ] Sign under oath (verification)
- [ ] Serve on opposing party within deadline

### Disclosure Verification Checklist (Reviewing Opposing Party)
- [ ] Cross-reference reported income against tax returns
- [ ] Compare bank deposits to reported income
- [ ] Check for undisclosed accounts
- [ ] Verify asset valuations with independent sources
- [ ] Check for transfers to third parties pre-filing
- [ ] Verify employment status with employer directly if needed
- [ ] Identify lifestyle inconsistent with reported income

### Filing Deadlines
- Verified Financial Information Statement: **14 days** after scheduling conference (MCR 3.206(B)(1))
- Supplemental disclosures: Promptly upon material change (MCR 2.302(E))
- Motion to compel disclosures: After deadline expires and informal request fails
- Motion to reopen for fraud: No time limit (MCL 552.401)
