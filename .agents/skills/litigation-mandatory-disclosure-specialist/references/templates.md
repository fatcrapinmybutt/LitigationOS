## Workflow: Financial Disclosure Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Scheduling conference sets disclosure deadline
    ↓
Step 2: Gather all financial documents (3 years tax, 3 months statements)
    ↓
Step 3: Complete FOC 23 Verified Financial Information Statement
    ↓
Step 4: Sign under oath (verification)
    ↓
Step 5: Serve on opposing party with proof of service
    ↓
Step 6: Review opposing party's disclosures for completeness
    ↓
Step 7: Cross-reference disclosures against independent evidence
    ↓
Step 8: If deficient → informal request → motion to compel
    ↓
Step 9: Monitor for changes requiring supplemental disclosure
    ↓
Step 10: Use verified disclosures in support/property calculations
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Partial disclosure | Court treats as non-disclosure; sanctions follow | Disclose EVERYTHING — even unfavorable information |
| Late filing without extension | May be treated as waiver; sanctions possible | File for extension before deadline if needed |
| Not verifying (signing under oath) | Unverified statement may be stricken | Always sign verification under penalty of perjury |
| Accepting opponent's disclosure at face value | Concealment is common; unchallenged = accepted | Cross-reference against tax returns, bank records, public records |
| Ignoring supplementation duty | Stale disclosures become inaccurate | File supplements promptly when circumstances change |
| Forgetting non-wage income | Side gigs, cash, gifts, crypto not reported | Disclose all income per MCL 552.602(f) broadly |
