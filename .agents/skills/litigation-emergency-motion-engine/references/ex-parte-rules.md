# Ex Parte Rules — Michigan Emergency Motion Reference

## Constitutional Framework

### Due Process Considerations
Ex parte orders are extraordinary because they deprive a party of rights WITHOUT prior notice and hearing. Constitutional due process (14th Amendment) requires:
- The deprivation be temporary
- A prompt post-deprivation hearing be provided
- The circumstances genuinely justify proceeding without notice

### When Ex Parte Is Constitutionally Permissible
Per *Mathews v Eldridge*, 424 US 319 (1976), balancing test:
1. **Private interest** affected by the action
2. **Risk of erroneous deprivation** under current procedures
3. **Government interest** in ex parte action, including burden of providing notice

In custody emergencies: child safety may outweigh notice requirements when delay would expose the child to harm.

## MCR 3.207 — Detailed Requirements

### Pre-Filing Checklist
```
BEFORE FILING EX PARTE IN FAMILY LAW

□ Is there genuinely irreparable harm from delay?
   (Not just inconvenience — actual, provable harm)

□ Have I made efforts to notify the opposing party?
   (Call, text, email — document every attempt)

□ Can I explain why notice would be harmful?
   (E.g., respondent would flee with child, destroy evidence)

□ Do I have specific facts, not just conclusions?
   (Dates, times, witnesses, documents — not "I feel unsafe")

□ Am I requesting specific, narrow relief?
   (Not a wholesale custody change — targeted emergency relief)

□ Have I prepared for the 14-day hearing?
   (Ex parte is not the end — it's the beginning)
```

### What Courts Look For in Ex Parte Motions

**Factors favoring grant:**
- Documented threat to child (police report, CPS report, medical evidence)
- Recent escalation of concerning behavior
- Inability to protect child through less drastic means
- Specific, verifiable factual allegations
- Good faith efforts to provide notice

**Factors favoring denial:**
- Conclusory allegations without supporting evidence
- Long delay between alleged emergency and filing
- Prior court awareness of the same issues (suggests not new)
- Available alternative remedies (e.g., PPO, school notification)
- History of frivolous emergency filings

## MCR 3.206(D) — Related Emergency Provisions

### Attorney Fees for Emergency Motions
- Court may order fee-shifting for emergency motions in family cases
- Moving party who prevails may recover costs of emergency filing
- Party who necessitated emergency may be ordered to pay costs
- Pro se limitation applies: no "attorney fees" but actual costs recoverable

## Practical Emergency Filing Guide

### Same-Day Filing Protocol
1. **8:00 AM**: Draft motion with supporting affidavit and exhibits
2. **10:00 AM**: File with clerk (mark EMERGENCY on face page)
3. **10:30 AM**: Deliver courtesy copy to judge's chambers
4. **11:00 AM**: Await judicial review (may be same day or next morning)
5. **Upon entry**: Immediately serve opposing party
6. **Same day**: File proof of service
7. **Same day**: Calendar 14-day hearing deadline

### What NOT to Do in Emergency Filings
1. **Don't exaggerate** — courts verify facts and remember exaggeration
2. **Don't use emergency for non-emergencies** — burns credibility forever
3. **Don't skip evidence** — unsupported emergency claims fail
4. **Don't ignore the 14-day hearing** — the order lapses
5. **Don't forget to serve** — unserved orders have due process problems
6. **Don't file late in the day** — judges may not review until next day
7. **Don't assume the order is permanent** — it's temporary by definition
