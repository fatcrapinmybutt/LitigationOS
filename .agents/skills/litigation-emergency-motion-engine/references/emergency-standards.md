# Emergency Standards — Michigan Reference

## MCR 2.119(F) — Emergency Motions (General)

### Standard for Emergency Treatment
A motion is treated as an emergency when the moving party demonstrates:
1. **Immediate harm**: Significant and irreparable injury will result from delay
2. **Inadequacy of normal procedures**: Regular motion timeline is insufficient
3. **Specific facts**: Particular circumstances requiring emergency treatment
4. **Specific relief**: Exactly what the court is being asked to do

### Filing Requirements
```
EMERGENCY MOTION REQUIREMENTS

□ Caption marked "EMERGENCY" prominently
□ Certificate of emergency explaining:
  - Nature of the emergency
  - Why normal procedures are inadequate
  - Efforts made to notify opposing party
  - Specific relief requested
□ Supporting brief with legal authority
□ Affidavit or declaration with specific facts
□ Proposed order for court signature
□ Proof of service (or explanation why ex parte)
```

### Court Processing
| Step | Timeline | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| Filing | Same day | File with clerk marked "EMERGENCY" |
| Judicial review | Hours | Judge reviews motion |
| Telephone conference | Optional | Court may call parties |
| Ruling | Hours to 1 day | Grant, deny, or schedule hearing |
| Hearing | 1-14 days | Full hearing if initially granted ex parte |

## MCR 3.207 — Ex Parte Orders in Domestic Relations

### MCR 3.207(A) — When Ex Parte Orders Are Authorized
The court may issue ex parte orders in domestic relations cases when:
- **Irreparable injury, loss, or damage** will result from delay
- **Before** the adverse party is heard
- The order is **temporary** pending a hearing

### Types of Ex Parte Relief Available
| Type | Standard | Duration |
|------|----------|----------|
| Temporary custody | Immediate threat to child's welfare | Until hearing (14 days) |
| Temporary support | Immediate financial need | Until hearing |
| Restraining order | Asset dissipation or party interference | Until hearing |
| Status quo preservation | Prevent irreversible changes | Until hearing |

### MCR 3.207(B) — Hearing Requirement
- **Mandatory hearing within 14 days** of ex parte order entry
- Respondent must be served with:
  - Copy of the ex parte order
  - Copy of the motion and supporting papers
  - Notice of hearing date
- At hearing, moving party bears burden of justifying continuation
- Court may modify, continue, or vacate the ex parte order

### Certification Requirements
The moving party MUST certify:
```
CERTIFICATE OF EFFORTS TO NOTIFY

I hereby certify that:

□ I have made the following efforts to notify the opposing party
  of this motion before filing ex parte:
  [Describe specific efforts — phone calls, emails, texts, visits]

□ OR notice should not be required because:
  [Explain why — e.g., giving notice would enable respondent to
  flee with child, destroy evidence, dissipate assets]

I understand that if the Court grants this ex parte motion,
a hearing must be scheduled within 14 days at which the
opposing party will have the opportunity to be heard.

Date: ____________
Signature: ____________
```

## MCR 3.310 — Temporary Restraining Orders

### MCR 3.310(A) — Grounds for TRO
TRO may be issued to prevent:
- Irreparable injury to property
- Domestic violence or threat thereof
- Interference with custody or parenting time
- Destruction or removal of marital assets
- Other conduct causing irreparable harm

### MCR 3.310(B) — Requirements
- Specific facts showing irreparable harm
- Security bond may be required (court discretion)
- TRO expires in 14 days unless extended
- Hearing on preliminary injunction within 14 days

### MCR 3.310(C) — Preliminary Injunction
After TRO hearing:
- Court may convert TRO to preliminary injunction
- Remains in effect until trial or further order
- Requires showing of:
  1. Likelihood of success on the merits
  2. Irreparable harm without injunction
  3. Balance of equities favors injunction
  4. Injunction serves the public interest

## MCL 600.2950 — Personal Protection Orders (PPO)

### PPO as Emergency Relief
- Ex parte PPO issued without hearing
- Respondent may petition to modify or terminate
- Hearing within 14 days if respondent objects
- Violation is criminal offense (misdemeanor or felony)

### PPO Emergency Modifications
| Situation | Procedure | Timeline |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| New threat | Motion to modify PPO | Same day |
| PPO violation | Police report + motion for contempt | Same day |
| Changed circumstances | Motion to modify/extend | Before expiration |
| Emergency extension | Ex parte motion | Before expiration |

## Emergency Motion Decision Tree

```
Is there IMMEDIATE threat to L.D.W.'s safety?
├── YES → MCR 3.207 (Ex parte custody)
│   ├── File same day
│   ├── Serve immediately
│   └── Hearing within 14 days
│
├── Is there threat to a PARTY's safety?
│   ├── YES → MCL 600.2950 (PPO) or MCR 3.310 (TRO)
│   │   ├── PPO: ex parte, violations are criminal
│   │   └── TRO: ex parte, 14-day hearing
│   │
│   └── Is there threat to ASSETS or STATUS QUO?
│       ├── YES → MCR 3.310 (TRO for asset preservation)
│       │   └── Bond may be required
│       │
│       └── Is there an urgent LEGAL DEADLINE?
│           ├── YES → MCR 2.119(F) (Emergency motion)
│           │   └── General emergency procedure
│           │
│           └── NO → Regular motion calendar
│               └── MCR 2.119(C) (9-day notice)
```

## Post-Emergency Follow-Up Timeline

```
Day 0:  Emergency motion filed + ex parte order entered
Day 0:  Serve opposing party (same day — critical)
Day 1:  File proof of service
Day 1:  Schedule 14-day hearing
Day 7:  Prepare for hearing (evidence, witnesses, brief)
Day 12: File hearing brief (if local rules require)
Day 14: HEARING — court decides to continue, modify, or vacate
Day 15: If continued, comply with all order conditions
Day 30: Review whether permanent relief needed
```
