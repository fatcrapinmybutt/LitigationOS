# Templates — Voir Dire Specialist

## Voir Dire Question Bank — Civil/Family Cases

### General Bias Exploration
```
1. Have you or anyone close to you been involved in a custody
   or divorce proceeding?
2. Do you have any strong feelings about custody arrangements
   that might affect your ability to be impartial?
3. Have you ever had experience with the Friend of the Court
   or family court system?
4. Is there anything about the nature of this case that would
   make it difficult for you to be fair to both sides?
```

### Pro Se Litigant Bias
```
5. The Plaintiff in this case is representing himself without
   an attorney. Would that affect how you evaluate the evidence?
6. Would you hold a self-represented party to the same standard
   as an attorney, or would you expect more or less from them?
7. Do you believe a person is less likely to succeed in court
   without an attorney?
```

### Family Law Specific
```
8. Do you believe mothers are generally better caregivers than
   fathers, or do you believe both parents are equally capable?
9. Have you formed opinions about child custody that you feel
   strongly about?
10. Do you believe children should always live primarily with
    one parent, or are you open to shared custody arrangements?
11. If the evidence showed that one parent was interfering with
    the child's relationship with the other parent, how would
    that affect your view?
```

### Ability to Follow Instructions
```
12. Can you follow the judge's instructions on the law even if
    you personally disagree with those instructions?
13. Can you base your decision solely on the evidence presented
    in court, and not on outside information or assumptions?
14. Will you keep an open mind until you have heard all the
    evidence from both sides?
```

### Financial Matters
```
15. Have you ever been ordered to pay or receive child support?
16. Do you have strong feelings about how child support should
    be calculated?
17. Would you be comfortable evaluating financial evidence and
    making findings about income and expenses?
```

## Challenge for Cause Motion

```
CHALLENGE FOR CAUSE — JUROR NO. [X]

    Plaintiff challenges Juror No. [X] for cause under MCR 2.511(D):

    Ground: [Actual bias / Implied bias / Relationship]

    Basis: During voir dire, Juror No. [X] stated:
    "[Quote juror's statement demonstrating bias]"

    This statement demonstrates that Juror No. [X] cannot be
    impartial because: [explain why the stated bias would affect
    deliberations in this case].

    Even though Juror No. [X] stated they could "try to be fair,"
    the substance of their expressed views reveals an actual bias
    that rehabilitation cannot cure. See People v Walker, 234
    Mich App 299 (1999).
```

## Batson Challenge Protocol

```
BATSON CHALLENGE TO OPPOSING PARTY'S PEREMPTORY STRIKES

Step 1 — Prima Facie Showing:
    Opposing party has used [X] of [3] peremptory challenges to
    strike jurors who are [race/gender]. This pattern gives rise
    to an inference of discriminatory intent.

Step 2 — Demand Race/Gender-Neutral Explanation:
    The Court should require opposing party to state a race/gender-
    neutral reason for each challenged strike.

Step 3 — Court Determination:
    The Court should evaluate whether the stated reasons are
    pretextual. Factors: (a) disparate questioning of struck vs.
    non-struck jurors, (b) similarly situated jurors of different
    race/gender not struck, (c) stated reason applies equally to
    non-struck jurors.

    Authority: Batson v Kentucky, 476 US 79 (1986); People v Knight,
    473 Mich 324 (2005); Gonzalez v Secretary of State, 326 Mich
    App 250 (2018).
```

| Priority | Juror Profile to Strike | Reason | Challenge Type |
|----------|----------------------|--------|---------------|
| 1 (High) | Juror expressing strong bias but not for-cause level | Subtle bias against pro se litigants | Peremptory |
| 2 (High) | Juror with prior negative family court experience | May project experience onto case | Peremptory |
| 3 (Medium) | Juror hostile to shared custody concept | Conflicts with case theory | Peremptory (if available) |

## Workflow: Jury Selection

```
Step 1: Verify jury trial right; file demand + fee (MCR 2.516)
    ↓
Step 2: Develop ideal juror profile from case theory
    ↓
Step 3: Draft voir dire questions (general → case-specific)
    ↓
Step 4: Request written questionnaire (complex cases)
    ↓
Step 5: Court conducts preliminary questioning
    ↓
Step 6: Conduct attorney/party voir dire questioning
    ↓
Step 7: Exercise challenges for cause (unlimited)
    ↓
Step 8: Exercise peremptory challenges (3 per side)
    ↓
Step 9: Monitor for Batson issues; challenge if pattern emerges
    ↓
Step 10: Jury sworn — trial begins
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Arguing during voir dire | Court stops you; jury annoyed | Ask open-ended questions to reveal bias |
| Wasting peremptories on hunches | Run out before reaching truly biased jurors | Challenge for cause first; save peremptories |
| Ignoring Batson obligations | Strike overturned; juror reseated; credibility damaged | Document race-neutral reason for every peremptory |
| Not preparing questions | Ad hoc questioning misses key bias areas | Write and organize questions by topic in advance |
| Assuming custody = no jury | Miss jury right on joined tort/contract claims | Verify jury eligibility for each claim separately |
