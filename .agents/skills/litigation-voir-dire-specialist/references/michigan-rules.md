# Michigan Rules — Voir Dire Specialist

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.511 — Jurors
- **(A)**: Examination of jurors — court and parties may question prospective jurors
- **(B)**: Court may conduct initial questioning; parties then have opportunity
- **(C)**: Scope of examination: bias, prejudice, interest, qualifications, ability to be impartial
- **(D)**: Challenges for cause:
  - **(D)(1)**: Unlimited number of for-cause challenges
  - **(D)(2)**: Grounds: actual bias, implied bias, relationship to party or attorney, financial interest, fixed opinion, inability to follow instructions
  - **(D)(3)**: Court rules on each challenge
- **(E)**: Peremptory challenges:
  - **(E)(1)**: **3 per side** in civil cases with 6-person jury
  - **(E)(2)**: **5 per side** in criminal cases
  - **(E)(3)**: Peremptory challenges may NOT be used to discriminate on basis of race, gender, or ethnicity (Michigan's Batson rule)
  - **(E)(4)**: Additional peremptory challenges when multiple parties on a side
- **(F)**: Alternate jurors — court may impanel alternate jurors
- **(G)**: Juror oath

### MCR 2.513 — Conduct of Jury Trial
- **(A)**: Preliminary instructions before voir dire
- **(B)**: Juror notebooks — court may provide
- **(C)**: Juror questions during trial — court may permit written questions
- **(N)**: Trial court has broad discretion in managing voir dire

### MCR 2.516 — Jury Demand
- **(A)**: Jury demand within **28 days** of service
- **(B)**: Failure to demand = waiver
- **(C)**: Jury fee required with demand

### MCR 6.412 — Criminal Voir Dire (Cross-Reference)
- Criminal voir dire has additional protections
- Not directly applicable to civil/family cases but informative

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.1301 — Right to Jury Trial
- Jury trial as of right in civil actions at law
- Equitable matters (custody) are bench trials — no jury right
- Mixed law/equity may have partial jury rights

### MCL 600.1332 — Jury Size
- Civil cases: **6 jurors** unless parties stipulate otherwise
- Parties may agree to fewer jurors

### MCL 600.1337 — Peremptory Challenges
- **3 peremptory challenges** per side (6-person civil jury)
- When multiple parties on one side, court may allow additional
- Must be exercised before jury is sworn

### MCL 600.1338 — Challenges for Cause
- Actual bias — state of mind that prevents impartial judgment
- Implied bias — relationship, interest, or circumstances creating presumption of bias
- Unlimited in number

### MCL 600.1340 — Oath of Jurors
- Jurors take oath to try the case fairly and impartially
- Violation of oath may be grounds for new trial

### MCL 600.1354 — Juror Qualification
- Citizen, resident, 18+ years old, English speaker
- No felony conviction (or civil rights restored)
- Not under conservatorship

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Voir Dire Standards
- **People v Tyburski**, 445 Mich 606 (1994) — Trial court has broad discretion in conducting voir dire; appellate review is for abuse of discretion
- **Gonzalez v Secretary of State**, 326 Mich App 250 (2018) — Batson protections apply in Michigan; peremptory challenges cannot be exercised on discriminatory grounds

### For-Cause Challenges
- **People v Walker**, 234 Mich App 299 (1999) — Juror who expresses bias but claims ability to be fair may still be challenged for cause; court must evaluate credibility of juror's assurance
- **People v Miller**, 326 Mich App 719 (2019) — Trial court has discretion in evaluating juror responses; clear bias requires excusal

### Batson Challenges
- **Batson v Kentucky**, 476 US 79 (1986) — Peremptory challenges cannot be used to exclude jurors based on race; three-step process: (1) prima facie showing, (2) race-neutral explanation, (3) court determination
- **People v Knight**, 473 Mich 324 (2005) — Michigan applies Batson; extends to gender-based strikes

### Juror Misconduct
- **People v Budzyn**, 456 Mich 77 (1997) — Juror non-disclosure of material information during voir dire may warrant new trial
- **Badalamenti v William Beaumont Hospital**, 237 Mich App 278 (1999) — Post-verdict discovery of juror dishonesty during voir dire is grounds for new trial motion

## Checklists

### Voir Dire Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Verify jury trial right exists for your claims
- [ ] Develop ideal juror profile based on case theory
- [ ] Draft voir dire questions organized by topic area
- [ ] Prepare written jury questionnaire (for complex cases)
- [ ] Research Batson requirements; prepare race-neutral justifications
- [ ] Create juror tracking sheet (name, responses, impression, challenge)
- [ ] Plan allocation of peremptory challenges (3 available)
- [ ] Identify key biases to probe: family law experience, custody opinions, pro se attitudes
- [ ] Prepare for-cause challenge arguments for common bias patterns

### Filing Deadlines
- Jury demand: **28 days** from service of first pleading (MCR 2.516)
- Jury questionnaire request: Per court's pretrial order
- Batson challenge: During voir dire / jury selection
- New trial motion (juror misconduct): **21 days** from verdict (MCR 2.611)
