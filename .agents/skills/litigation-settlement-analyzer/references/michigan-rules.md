# Michigan Rules — Settlement Analyzer

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 3.210 — Settlement in Domestic Relations
- **(A)**: Court encourages settlement in domestic relations cases
- **(B)**: Settlement conferences may be ordered by the court
- **(C)**: Agreements must be placed on the record or reduced to writing

### MCR 2.507 — Consent Judgments and Stipulations
- **(G)**: Agreement made in open court is binding; must be placed on the record
- **(H)**: Written stipulations signed by parties or attorneys are binding
- Court reviews for fairness and compliance with statutory requirements

### MCR 3.206(B) — Financial Disclosure Prerequisite
- Complete financial disclosure required before settlement
- Court may refuse to approve settlement without verified financial information

### MCR 2.410 — Case Evaluation
- **(B)**: Three-member panel evaluates case and assigns value
- **(C)**: Parties have **28 days** to accept or reject
- **(D)**: Rejection consequences — if trial result not more favorable, rejecting party pays costs per MCR 2.403(O)

### MCR 3.216 — Mediation in Domestic Relations
- **(H)(7)**: Mediation agreement signed by both parties is enforceable
- Settlement through mediation follows same enforceability standards

### MCR 2.612(C) — Setting Aside Settlement-Based Judgments
- **(C)(1)(c)**: Fraud, misrepresentation, misconduct — basis to set aside
- **(C)(2)**: Must file within 1 year for fraud grounds

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 552.6 — Settlement of Property Rights
- Parties may agree to property division by written agreement
- Court incorporates agreement into judgment
- Agreement becomes binding court order upon incorporation

### MCL 552.23 — Division of Property
- **(1)**: Court divides property equitably — not necessarily equally
- **(3)**: Factors include: duration of marriage, needs, earning ability, fault, contributions

### MCL 552.391 — Property Division Finality
- Property division in divorce judgment is generally final
- Not modifiable except for fraud or mutual mistake
- Distinguishes from modifiable support provisions

### MCL 552.605 — Michigan Child Support Formula
- **(1)**: Court must apply the Michigan Child Support Formula
- **(2)**: Deviation permitted only with written findings that formula amount is unjust or inappropriate
- **(3)**: Deviation findings must specify: (a) formula amount, (b) ordered amount, (c) reason for deviation

### MCL 552.602 — Income Definition for Support
- **(f)**: Broad definition of income for support calculation
- All sources included: wages, bonuses, benefits, investment income

### MCL 552.13 — Spousal Support
- **(1)**: Court may award spousal support in lump sum or periodic payments
- Factors: parties' financial situation, ability to work, length of marriage, contributions

### MCL 552.17 — Modification of Support
- Support orders are modifiable upon showing of changed circumstances
- Property division is NOT modifiable (MCL 552.391)
- Critical distinction: label provisions correctly in settlement

### MCL 552.101 — Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO)
- Required to divide retirement benefits
- Must comply with federal ERISA requirements
- Must be entered as a separate court order

### MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors in Custody Settlement
- Any custody settlement must serve the child's best interests
- Court reviews custody terms even in consent judgments
- Court is not bound by parties' custody agreement if not in child's best interest

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Settlement Enforceability
- **Vittiglio v Vittiglio**, 297 Mich App 213 (2012) — Settlement agreements in domestic relations are enforceable as contracts when signed by both parties
- **Keystone Const Co v Michigan**, 357 Mich 274 (1959) — Consent judgment is treated as a contract between parties; standard contract interpretation rules apply
- **Walbridge Aldinger Co v Walcon Corp**, 207 Mich App 566 (1994) — Party cannot unilaterally withdraw from settlement agreement

### Property Division Standards
- **Sparks v Sparks**, 440 Mich 141 (1992) — Factors for equitable property division; not required to be equal; court considers all relevant factors
- **Byington v Byington**, 224 Mich App 103 (1997) — Invasion of separate property permitted when equity requires
- **Sands v Sands**, 442 Mich 30 (1993) — Fraudulent concealment of assets justifies reopening property division

### Child Support Settlement
- **Clarke v Clarke**, 297 Mich App 172 (2012) — Child support agreements that deviate from formula require written findings
- **Ghidotti v Barber**, 459 Mich 189 (1998) — Income calculation for support must include all sources

## Checklists

### Pre-Settlement Analysis Checklist
- [ ] All financial disclosures received and verified (MCR 3.206(B))
- [ ] Child Support Formula calculation completed
- [ ] Present value of all assets calculated (including after-tax values)
- [ ] All debts identified and allocated
- [ ] Tax consequences analyzed for each proposed division
- [ ] QDRO requirements identified for retirement accounts
- [ ] Spousal support analysis completed (if applicable)
- [ ] Custody/parenting time terms evaluated for best interest compliance
- [ ] Non-modifiable vs. modifiable provisions distinguished
- [ ] Insurance (health, life, dental) addressed

### Settlement Agreement Review Checklist
- [ ] All assets listed and allocated
- [ ] All debts listed and allocated
- [ ] Child support amount stated with formula worksheet
- [ ] Deviation findings included if non-formula amount
- [ ] Custody and parenting time terms specified
- [ ] Tax exemptions/credits allocated
- [ ] Health insurance responsibility assigned
- [ ] Life insurance requirements (for support security)
- [ ] QDRO provisions for retirement accounts
- [ ] Real property transfer provisions
- [ ] Vehicle transfer provisions
- [ ] Personal property division
- [ ] Spousal support amount, duration, and modifiability
- [ ] Release of claims — appropriately limited
- [ ] Attorney fees allocation

### Filing Deadlines
- Case evaluation response: **28 days** (MCR 2.410(C))
- Motion to set aside settlement (fraud): **1 year** (MCR 2.612(C)(2))
- Support modification: No deadline (file upon changed circumstances)
