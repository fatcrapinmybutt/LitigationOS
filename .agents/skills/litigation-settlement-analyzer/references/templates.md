1. The Michigan Child Support Formula amount is: $[AMOUNT]/month.

## Workflow: Settlement Analysis

```
Step 1: Verify all financial disclosures are complete
    ↓
Step 2: Inventory all marital and separate assets
    ↓
Step 3: Value all assets (appraisals, statements, KBB)
    ↓
Step 4: Calculate after-tax values for each asset
    ↓
Step 5: Run Michigan Child Support Formula calculation
    ↓
Step 6: Analyze spousal support factors (if applicable)
    ↓
Step 7: Present value analysis for all support streams
    ↓
Step 8: Model 3+ settlement scenarios (best/acceptable/minimum)
    ↓
Step 9: Compare scenarios to BATNA (trial outcome projection)
    ↓
Step 10: Present analysis with recommendation
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Settling without discovery | Uninformed decision; may miss hidden assets | Complete all discovery before negotiating |
| Ignoring tax consequences | After-tax value differs from gross; bad deal | Calculate net after-tax value for every asset |
| Lump-sum child support | FOC may reject; cannot modify; disadvantages child | Use monthly formula-based payments |
| General release of all claims | Waives right to address hidden assets later | Narrow releases to known, identified claims only |
| Not distinguishing property from support | Property division is final; support is modifiable | Label each provision clearly; impacts modifiability |
| Rushing to settle | Pressure leads to unfavorable terms | Take time to analyze; 24-48 hour review minimum |
