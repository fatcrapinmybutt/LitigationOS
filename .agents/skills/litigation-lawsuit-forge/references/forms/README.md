# INITIATING FORMS CATALOG

## ALL FORMS REQUIRED TO INITIATE A NEW LAWSUIT IN MICHIGAN

This catalog lists every form needed from complaint filing through service of process, with form numbers, purposes, and where to obtain them.

---

## MICHIGAN COURT FORMS — SCAO (State Court Administrative Office)

Michigan court forms are published by SCAO and available at: https://www.courts.michigan.gov/court-administration/court-forms/

## FORM-BY-FORM DETAIL

### MC 01 — SUMMONS

**Purpose:** Official court document commanding the defendant to file an answer to the complaint within the time allowed by the court rules.

**Key Information Required:**
- Court name and address
- Case number (added by clerk after filing)
- Plaintiff name and address
- Defendant name and address (EXACT legal name)
- Date of filing
- Return date (defendant's answer deadline)
- Clerk's signature and court seal

**Preparation Notes:**
- Prepare ONE summons for EACH defendant
- Leave case number blank — clerk will assign
- Defendant name must match EXACTLY the name used in the complaint caption
- Original stays with court; copies served on defendant

**Answer Deadlines (MCR 2.108):**
| SERVICE METHOD | ANSWER DEADLINE |
|---------------|----------------|
| Personal service in Michigan | 21 days |
| Personal service outside Michigan | 28 days |
| Registered mail | 28 days from date of mailing |
| Publication | 28 days after last publication |

### MC 20 — AFFIDAVIT AND ORDER, SUSPENSION OF FEES/COSTS

**Purpose:** Request waiver of filing fees and costs for litigants who cannot afford them without depriving themselves or dependents of necessities of life.

**Who Qualifies:**
- Persons receiving public assistance (TANF, SSI, Medicaid, food assistance)
- Persons whose income is below 125% of federal poverty guidelines
- Other circumstances showing inability to pay

**Key Information Required:**
- Household size and income
- Monthly expenses
- Assets
- Public assistance received
- Specific fees being requested for waiver
- Signed under oath

**What MC 20 Covers:**
- Filing fees
- Service of process fees
- Transcript costs
- Jury fees
- Other court costs

**Process:**
1. Complete MC 20 affidavit section
2. File with complaint (or in advance)
3. Judge reviews and signs order section
4. If approved, all covered fees are waived
5. If denied, must pay fees to proceed (or appeal denial)

**Attach Supporting Documents:**
- Pay stubs or proof of income
- Public assistance benefit letters
- Tax returns (if available)
- Monthly expense documentation

### MC 12 — PROOF OF SERVICE

**Purpose:** Sworn statement documenting that service of process was completed on a defendant.

**Key Information Required:**
- Case number and caption
- Name of person served
- Date of service
- Time of service
- Location of service
- Method of service (personal, registered mail, substitute, etc.)
- Name and signature of person who served (must NOT be a party — MCR 2.103(A))
- Description of documents served

**Filing Requirements:**
- File with the court AFTER service is completed
- One MC 12 per defendant
- Must be sworn/affirmed (notarized or signed under penalty of perjury)
- File promptly — do not delay

### CIVIL CASE COVER SHEET

**Purpose:** Provides the clerk's office with case classification information for filing and statistical purposes.

**Key Information:**
- Case type (personal injury, contract, real property, etc.)
- Amount in controversy range
- Related cases (if any)
- Jury trial demanded (yes/no)
- Number of parties
- Attorney information (or pro se designation)

**Note:** Format varies by county. Muskegon County Circuit Court may have its own version. Call the clerk's office to confirm requirements.

## FILING PACKAGE ASSEMBLY CHECKLIST

### FOR CIRCUIT COURT FILING

```
ORIGINAL SET (for court file):
[ ] Verified Complaint (signed and notarized)
[ ] Civil Case Cover Sheet (completed)
[ ] MC 20 Fee Waiver (if applicable, signed and notarized)
[ ] Filing fee check/money order (if not using MC 20)
[ ] Exhibit package (original, tabbed and indexed)

PER-DEFENDANT SET (for service):
[ ] Complaint copy
[ ] MC 01 Summons (completed for this specific defendant)
[ ] Exhibit package copy

PLAINTIFF'S SET (for your records):
[ ] Complaint copy (to be file-stamped by clerk)
[ ] All summons copies (to be stamped)
[ ] MC 12 Proof of Service forms (blank, for completion after service)

TOTAL COPIES NEEDED:
= 1 (court original) + 1 per defendant + 1 (plaintiff's copy)
Example: 3 defendants = 5 total sets
```

### FOR FEDERAL COURT FILING (USDC Western District of MI)

```
[ ] Complaint (original, signed)
[ ] Civil Cover Sheet (JS 44 — federal form)
[ ] Summons (form AO 440 — one per defendant)
[ ] Filing fee ($405) or IFP Application (28 USC 1915)
[ ] Exhibits (indexed)
[ ] Corporate Disclosure Statement (FRCP 7.1) if suing corporation
[ ] Consent/Non-consent to Magistrate Judge (per local rule)
```

**E-filing:** USDC Western District uses CM/ECF electronic filing. Pro se litigants may be permitted to file on paper, but check current local rules.

## COUNTY-SPECIFIC INFORMATION — MUSKEGON COUNTY

### Circuit Court Clerk
- **Location:** Michael E. Kobza Hall of Justice, 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, MI 49442
- **Hours:** Check current hours (typically 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM, M-F)
- **Phone:** (231) 724-6241 (verify current)
- **Filing method:** In-person or check local e-filing availability

### What to Bring to the Clerk's Office
1. Complete filing package (see checklist above)
2. Filing fee (cash, check, or money order) OR completed MC 20
3. Photo ID
4. Extra copies of everything (the clerk will file-stamp your copies)

### What the Clerk Will Do
1. Review documents for completeness (not legal sufficiency)
2. Assign a case number
3. File-stamp all copies
4. Accept filing fee or MC 20
5. Return your stamped copies
6. Assign to a judge (random assignment)
