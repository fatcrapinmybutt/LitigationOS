# SERVICE OF PROCESS — COMPLETE MATRIX

## EVERY METHOD OF SERVICE FOR EVERY DEFENDANT TYPE

Improper service = no jurisdiction over the defendant = case dismissed. This matrix covers every permissible method of service under the Michigan Court Rules.

## MCR 2.103 — WHO MAY SERVE PROCESS

| PERMITTED SERVER | REQUIREMENTS |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Any adult (18+) who is NOT a party to the action | Must be legally competent |
| Professional process server | Licensed where applicable |
| Sheriff or deputy sheriff | Official service |
| Court officer | If directed by court |
| Plaintiff's attorney or attorney's agent | Not the plaintiff personally |

**CRITICAL:** The PLAINTIFF CANNOT personally serve process. MCR 2.103(A) prohibits service by a party to the action. This is a common pro se mistake that results in invalid service.

## MCR 2.105 — MANNER OF SERVICE BY DEFENDANT TYPE

### INDIVIDUAL PERSON (MCR 2.105(A))

| METHOD | HOW | ANSWER DEADLINE |
|--------|-----|----------------|
| Personal service | Deliver summons + complaint to defendant personally, anywhere in Michigan | 21 days |
| Service at dwelling | Leave with person of suitable age and discretion at defendant's usual place of abode, THEN mail by first-class mail to last known address | 21 days (from mailing) |
| Registered mail | Send summons + complaint by registered or certified mail, return receipt requested, with delivery restricted to the addressee | 28 days from date of mailing |
| Acknowledgment | Send summons + complaint by first-class mail with acknowledgment form; valid ONLY if defendant returns signed acknowledgment | 28 days |

**PERSONAL SERVICE IS ALWAYS PREFERRED** — it is the most reliable and least challengeable method.

### CORPORATION — DOMESTIC (MCR 2.105(D))

| METHOD | SERVE UPON | NOTES |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Registered agent | The corporation's registered agent on file with LARA | Check LARA for current agent |
| Officer | Any officer of the corporation (president, VP, secretary, treasurer) | Must be a current officer |
| Director | Any director of the corporation | Must be a current director |
| Agent in charge | Any person in charge of a corporate office or facility | If other methods fail |

**HOW TO FIND REGISTERED AGENT:**
Michigan LARA Corporate Entity Search: https://cofs.lara.state.mi.us/corpweb/CorpSearch/CorpSearch.aspx

### LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANY (MCR 2.105(D))

| METHOD | SERVE UPON | NOTES |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Registered agent | The LLC's registered agent on file with LARA | Check LARA for current agent |
| Manager | Any manager of a manager-managed LLC | Check articles/operating agreement |
| Member | Any member of a member-managed LLC | Check articles/operating agreement |
| Agent in charge | Any person in charge of an LLC office or facility | If other methods fail |

### PARTNERSHIP (MCR 2.105(E))

| METHOD | SERVE UPON |
|--------|-----------|
| Any partner | Service on one partner = service on partnership |
| Person in charge | Any person in charge of a partnership office |

### GOVERNMENT — STATE OF MICHIGAN (MCR 2.105(G))

| SERVE UPON | METHOD |
|-----------|--------|
| Michigan Attorney General | Personal service or registered mail to AG's office |
| Specific agency/department head | Also serve the specific agency from which the claim arises |

**BOTH MUST BE SERVED** when suing a state agency or state official in official capacity.

### GOVERNMENT — MUNICIPAL / COUNTY (MCR 2.105(G))

| ENTITY | SERVE UPON |
|--------|-----------|
| City | City clerk or, if none, the chief executive officer |
| Township | Township clerk |
| County | County clerk |
| Village | Village clerk or president |
| Government officer (individual capacity) | The officer personally + the governmental entity's clerk |

### MINOR (UNDER 18) (MCR 2.105(B))

| METHOD | SERVE UPON |
|--------|-----------|
| Personal service on custodial parent or guardian | The person having custody or guardianship of the minor |
| If no parent/guardian | Guardian ad litem appointed by court |

### INCAPACITATED PERSON (MCR 2.105(C))

| METHOD | SERVE UPON |
|--------|-----------|
| Personal service on guardian or conservator | The legally appointed guardian or conservator |
| If no guardian | Court may appoint guardian ad litem |

### OUT-OF-STATE DEFENDANT (MCR 2.105(A), MCL 600.701+)

Michigan's long-arm statute (MCL 600.705, 600.715) provides jurisdiction over out-of-state defendants in certain situations:

| BASIS | STATUTE |
|-------|---------|
| Transacting business in Michigan | MCL 600.705(1) |
| Committing a tort in Michigan | MCL 600.705(2) |
| Owning real property in Michigan | MCL 600.705(3) |
| Contracting to insure person/property in Michigan | MCL 600.705(4) |
| Maintaining a domicile in Michigan | MCL 600.705(5) |
| Property located in Michigan (in rem) | MCL 600.715 |

**SERVICE ON OUT-OF-STATE DEFENDANTS:**
Same methods as in-state (personal, registered mail, etc.) but service must be effected in compliance with the laws of the state where service occurs OR by registered mail from Michigan.

## MCR 2.106 — SERVICE BY PUBLICATION

### WHEN PUBLICATION IS AVAILABLE
Only when the defendant's whereabouts are unknown and cannot be ascertained after diligent inquiry.

### REQUIREMENTS
1. **File affidavit** stating that after diligent inquiry, defendant's address cannot be ascertained
2. **Obtain court order** allowing service by publication
3. **Publish** in a newspaper of general circulation in the county where the defendant was last known to reside, once each week for 3 consecutive weeks
4. **Mail** copy of the order to defendant's last known address (if any)

### DILIGENT INQUIRY INCLUDES
- Check last known address
- Contact known relatives and associates
- Search public records (LARA, Secretary of State, tax records)
- Search social media and internet
- Check USPS forwarding addresses

### ANSWER DEADLINE
28 days after the last date of publication.

## MCR 2.107 — SERVICE OF PAPERS AFTER INITIAL SERVICE

After the initial service of process (summons + complaint), subsequent papers are served differently:

| METHOD | WHEN USED |
|--------|----------|
| Personal delivery | Hand-deliver to party or attorney |
| First-class mail | Mail to party's last known address (or attorney's address if represented) |
| Electronic service | If parties agree or court orders |
| Fax | If parties agree |

**NOTE:** Subsequent papers (motions, discovery, etc.) do NOT require formal process service. First-class mail is sufficient.

## SERVICE TIMELINE REQUIREMENTS

### THE 91-DAY RULE (MCR 2.102(D))

```
FILING DATE: [Date complaint filed with clerk]
         ↓
SERVICE DEADLINE: Filing Date + 91 calendar days
         ↓
IF NOT SERVED BY DEADLINE:
    → Case subject to dismissal without prejudice
    → Must show good cause to extend
    → File motion for extension BEFORE deadline if possible
```

### CALCULATING ANSWER DEADLINES

| SERVICE DATE | SERVICE METHOD | ANSWER DUE |
|-------------|---------------|------------|
| March 1 | Personal service in MI | March 22 (21 days) |
| March 1 | Registered mail | March 29 (28 days from mailing) |
| March 1 | Substituted service | March 29 (28 days) |
| March 1 | Personal service outside MI | March 29 (28 days) |
| Last publication March 15 | Publication | April 12 (28 days) |

**COUNTING DAYS (MCR 1.108):**
- Day of the act (service) is NOT counted
- Every day including weekends and holidays IS counted
- If the last day falls on a weekend or holiday, the deadline extends to the next business day

## SERVICE STRATEGIES BY DEFENDANT TYPE — PIGORS CONTEXT

### LANDLORD LLC
1. Search LARA for registered agent → serve registered agent via personal service
2. If registered agent cannot be located → serve any member or manager
3. If no one can be found → request order for alternate service (MC 303)

### PROPERTY MANAGER (INDIVIDUAL)
1. Personal service at place of business or residence
2. If evading → substituted service at dwelling + first-class mail
3. If cannot be found → registered mail to last known address
4. Last resort → service by publication (MCR 2.106)

### GOVERNMENT OFFICIAL (FOR § 1983)
1. Serve individually at their place of employment or residence
2. ALSO serve the governmental entity's clerk (for official capacity claims)
3. ALSO serve the Michigan Attorney General if state is implicated

### CORPORATE OWNER / PARENT COMPANY
1. Search LARA for registered agent and current officers
2. Serve registered agent via personal service or registered mail
3. If out-of-state corporation → use long-arm statute + serve via registered mail

## PROOF OF SERVICE REQUIREMENTS

### WHAT TO INCLUDE ON MC 12

```
I, [name of server], being duly sworn, state:

1. I am over 18 years of age and am not a party to this action.

2. On [date], at approximately [time], I served:
   [Name of person served]

3. At the following location:
   [Full address where service occurred]

4. By the following method:
   [ ] Personal service — I delivered copies of the Summons and Complaint
       directly to the above-named person.
   [ ] Substituted service — I left copies with [name], a person of
       suitable age and discretion, at the defendant's usual place of
       abode, and mailed copies by first-class mail on [date].
   [ ] Registered/certified mail — I mailed copies by [type] mail,
       return receipt requested, on [date]. [Attach return receipt.]

5. Documents served:
   [ ] Summons
   [ ] Verified Complaint
   [ ] Exhibits A through [X]
   [ ] [Other documents]

Signed under penalty of perjury on [date].

________________________________
[Name of Server]
[Address]
[Phone]
```

### FILING PROOF OF SERVICE
- File MC 12 with the court promptly after service
- Attach return receipt if served by registered mail
- One MC 12 per defendant
- Keep copies for your records

## COMMON SERVICE ERRORS AND FIXES

| ERROR | CONSEQUENCE | FIX |
|-------|------------|-----|
| Plaintiff served process personally | Invalid service | Reserve through non-party server |
| Served wrong person at business | Invalid service | Identify correct person and re-serve |
| Registered mail not restricted delivery | May be challenged | Re-serve with proper restricted delivery |
| Proof of service not filed | Court cannot confirm service | File MC 12 immediately |
| Served after 91 days | Subject to dismissal | File motion for extension showing good cause |
| Missing documents in service package | Potentially invalid | Re-serve with complete package |
| Served minor without serving parent | Invalid for minor | Serve parent/guardian |
