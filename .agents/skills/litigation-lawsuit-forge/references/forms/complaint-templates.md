# COMPLAINT TEMPLATES BY CASE LANE

## STRUCTURAL TEMPLATES FOR EACH LITIGATION LANE

These templates provide the structural framework for complaints in each case lane. Adapt to specific facts and claims.

## LANE A TEMPLATE — CUSTODY / PARENTAL RIGHTS COMPLAINT

```

IN THE 14TH JUDICIAL CIRCUIT COURT FOR THE COUNTY OF MUSKEGON
FAMILY DIVISION

ANDREW PIGORS,                          )
    Plaintiff, Pro Se,                  )
                                        )   Case No. ________________
v.                                      )
                                        )   Hon. ____________________
[DEFENDANT NAME],                       )
    Defendant.                          )
________________________________________)

VERIFIED COMPLAINT FOR DEPRIVATION OF PARENTAL RIGHTS
AND DEMAND FOR JURY TRIAL

    Plaintiff Andrew Pigors ("Plaintiff"), appearing pro se, brings this
Verified Complaint against Defendant [Name] and states as follows:

                            I. JURISDICTION

    1. This Court has jurisdiction over the subject matter of this action
pursuant to MCL 600.601, MCL 600.605, and MCL 722.26 (Child Custody Act).

    2. This Court has jurisdiction over the person of Defendant because
Defendant resides in Muskegon County, Michigan.

    3. [If federal claims included:] Plaintiff reserves the right to remove
this action to federal court or to file a parallel federal action under
28 USC § 1331 for federal question jurisdiction.

                              II. VENUE

    4. Venue is proper in this Court pursuant to MCL 600.1621 because
Defendant resides in Muskegon County and the acts giving rise to this
complaint occurred in Muskegon County.

                             III. PARTIES

    5. Plaintiff Andrew Pigors is an adult individual residing in Muskegon
County, Michigan. Plaintiff is the [father/parent] of [minor child/children]
and brings this action pro se.

    6. Defendant [Name] is [description and residence].

                      IV. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

    [Numbered paragraphs 7+ covering:]
    - Parentage and custody history
    - Specific acts of custody interference (dates, details)
    - Deprivation of parenting time (dates, details)
    - Notice given and responses received
    - Impact on parent-child relationship
    - Impact on children
    - Pattern of conduct
    - Any government actor involvement (for § 1983 claims)

                     V. CAUSES OF ACTION

    COUNT I — DEPRIVATION OF PARENTAL RIGHTS (SUBSTANTIVE DUE PROCESS)
    [Incorporate prior paragraphs. Plead: fundamental right, deprivation,
    no legitimate justification. Cite Troxel, Stanley, Santosky.]

    COUNT II — DEPRIVATION OF PARENTAL RIGHTS (PROCEDURAL DUE PROCESS)
    [Incorporate. Plead: protected interest, deprivation, inadequate process.
    Cite Mathews v Eldridge.]

    COUNT III — INTENTIONAL INFLICTION OF EMOTIONAL DISTRESS
    [Incorporate. Plead: extreme and outrageous conduct, intent/recklessness,
    causation, severe distress.]

    COUNT IV — CIVIL CONSPIRACY
    [If multiple defendants. Incorporate. Plead: agreement, concerted action,
    underlying tort, damages.]

                     VI. PRAYER FOR RELIEF

    WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests:
    a. Compensatory damages;
    b. Restoration of full parental rights;
    c. Injunctive relief ordering compliance with custody orders;
    d. Punitive/exemplary damages;
    e. Costs of suit;
    f. Such other relief as this Court deems just.

                      VII. JURY DEMAND

    Plaintiff demands a trial by jury on all issues so triable.

                      VIII. VERIFICATION

    [Standard verification under oath]

                      IX. SIGNATURE

    Andrew Pigors, Pro Se
    [Address, Phone, Email, Date]
```

## MULTI-DEFENDANT TEMPLATE — KEY MODIFICATIONS

When suing multiple defendants across lanes:

1. **Caption:** List ALL defendants with designation (individually, official capacity, jointly and severally)
2. **Parties section:** Describe each defendant's role and connection to the claims
3. **Factual allegations:** Identify which defendant performed which act in each paragraph
4. **Counts:** Specify which defendant(s) each count applies to
5. **Joint and several liability:** Allege where appropriate (conspiracy, joint tortfeasors)
6. **Alternative pleading:** MCR 2.111(A)(2) — "Defendant A is liable for X, or in the alternative, Defendant B is liable for X"

## AMENDED COMPLAINT TEMPLATE

```
[Same caption, but title reads:]

FIRST AMENDED VERIFIED COMPLAINT AND DEMAND FOR JURY TRIAL

    Plaintiff Andrew Pigors ("Plaintiff"), appearing pro se, files this
First Amended Verified Complaint, which supersedes and replaces the
original Complaint filed on [date], and states as follows:

[Complete complaint — an amended complaint must be complete and
self-contained. It replaces the original entirely.]
```

**Key Rules:**
- MCR 2.118(A)(1): Amend as of right before responsive pleading
- MCR 2.118(A)(2): By leave of court after responsive pleading
- The amended complaint must be served on all parties per MCR 2.107
- If adding new defendants, serve them as original parties (new summons required)
