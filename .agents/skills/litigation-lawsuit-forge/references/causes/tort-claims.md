# MICHIGAN TORT CLAIMS — COMPLETE ELEMENTS

## EVERY ELEMENT FOR EVERY TORT CLAIM IN THE CATALOG

This reference provides the complete element breakdown for each tort cause of action available in Michigan, with Michigan-specific case law and statutory authority.

---

## FRAUD (6 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Clear and convincing evidence
**SOL:** 6 years from accrual, discovery rule applies (MCL 600.5813)
**Authority:** Hi-Way Motor Co v International Harvester Co, 398 Mich 330 (1976)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Material misrepresentation | Defendant made a false representation of material fact (not opinion, puffery, or future promise) |
| 2 | Knowledge of falsity (scienter) | Defendant knew the representation was false, or made it recklessly without knowing whether it was true or false |
| 3 | Intent to induce reliance | Defendant made the representation with the intent that plaintiff would act upon it |
| 4 | Justifiable reliance | Plaintiff actually relied on the representation, and such reliance was reasonable under the circumstances |
| 5 | Causation | Plaintiff's reliance on the misrepresentation was the proximate cause of the damages |
| 6 | Resulting damages | Plaintiff suffered actual damages as a result of the reliance |

**Special Pleading:** MCR 2.112(B)(1) — must be pled with particularity (who, what, when, where, how).
**Damages:** Actual loss (benefit of the bargain or out-of-pocket), consequential damages, punitive damages in egregious cases.

---

## NEGLIGENCE (4 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance of the evidence
**SOL:** 3 years (MCL 600.5805(2))
**Authority:** Moning v Alfono, 400 Mich 425 (1977)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Duty | Defendant owed plaintiff a duty of care (arising from relationship, statute, or common law) |
| 2 | Breach | Defendant failed to conform to the applicable standard of care |
| 3 | Proximate causation | Defendant's breach was the proximate (legal) cause of plaintiff's injury — "but for" test + foreseeability |
| 4 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered actual, compensable damages |

**Comparative fault:** Michigan follows modified comparative negligence — plaintiff's recovery reduced by percentage of fault; barred if plaintiff is >50% at fault (MCL 600.2959).
**Damages:** Economic (medical, lost wages, property) + non-economic (pain, suffering, emotional distress).

---

## INTENTIONAL INFLICTION OF EMOTIONAL DISTRESS (4 ELEMENTS + SEVERITY)

**Standard:** Preponderance of the evidence
**SOL:** 3 years (MCL 600.5805(2))
**Authority:** Roberts v Auto-Owners Ins Co, 422 Mich 594 (1985)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Extreme and outrageous conduct | Defendant's conduct was "so outrageous in character, and so extreme in degree, as to go beyond all possible bounds of decency, and to be regarded as atrocious, and utterly intolerable in a civilized community" |
| 2 | Intent or recklessness | Defendant intended to cause emotional distress, or acted in reckless disregard of the probability of causing such distress |
| 3 | Causation | Defendant's conduct caused plaintiff's emotional distress |
| 4 | Severe emotional distress | The resulting emotional distress was severe — "so severe that no reasonable person could be expected to endure it" |

**Severity threshold is HIGH.** Mere insults, indignities, annoyances, and petty oppressions do not qualify. Tevis v Amex Assurance Co, 283 Mich App 76 (2009). Evidence of physical manifestation or clinical diagnosis strengthens the claim.

---

## NEGLIGENT INFLICTION OF EMOTIONAL DISTRESS (NIED)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 3 years (MCL 600.5805(2))
**Authority:** Daley v LaCroix, 384 Mich 4 (1970)

**Zone of Danger Theory (3 Elements):**
| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Negligent conduct | Defendant acted negligently |
| 2 | Zone of danger | Plaintiff was in the zone of physical danger created by defendant's negligence |
| 3 | Definite physical injury or severe distress | Plaintiff suffered a definite, objective physical injury from the distress OR severe emotional distress with physical manifestation |

**Bystander Theory (4 Elements):**
| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Close relationship | Plaintiff had a close familial relationship with the victim |
| 2 | Contemporaneous perception | Plaintiff witnessed or contemporaneously perceived the injury-producing event |
| 3 | Serious injury | The victim suffered a serious injury or death |
| 4 | Severe distress | Plaintiff suffered severe emotional distress with physical manifestation |

## CONVERSION (3 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 3 years (MCL 600.5805(2))
**Authority:** Foremost Ins Co v Allstate Ins Co, 439 Mich 378 (1992)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Plaintiff's property right | Plaintiff had an ownership interest or right to possession of specific, identifiable personal property |
| 2 | Wrongful dominion | Defendant exercised dominion or control over the property inconsistent with plaintiff's rights |
| 3 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered damages (typically the fair market value of the property at time of conversion) |

---

## TRESPASS TO LAND (3 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 3 years (MCL 600.5805(2))

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Plaintiff's possessory interest | Plaintiff had a possessory interest in the land |
| 2 | Unauthorized entry | Defendant intentionally entered or caused entry upon plaintiff's land without authorization |
| 3 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered damages (actual damages, or nominal damages suffice to establish the tort) |

## DEFAMATION — PER SE (4 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance (clear and convincing if public figure/public concern)
**SOL:** 1 year (MCL 600.5805(2)) — VERY SHORT
**Authority:** Mitan v Campbell, 474 Mich 21 (2005)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | False statement of fact | Defendant made a false and defamatory statement concerning plaintiff |
| 2 | Publication | The statement was communicated to at least one third party |
| 3 | Fault | Defendant was at least negligent regarding the truth (private plaintiff) or acted with actual malice (public figure) |
| 4 | Per se category | The statement falls into a recognized per se category: (a) criminal conduct, (b) loathsome disease, (c) business/profession unfitness, (d) serious sexual misconduct |

**Damages presumed** in per se cases — no need to prove special (economic) damages.

---

## DEFAMATION — PER QUOD (5 ELEMENTS)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | False statement of fact | Same as per se |
| 2 | Publication | Same as per se |
| 3 | Fault | Same as per se |
| 4 | Defamatory meaning | The statement, with extrinsic facts, carries a defamatory meaning |
| 5 | Special damages | Plaintiff must prove actual, specific economic damages |

## INVASION OF PRIVACY (4 TYPES)

Michigan recognizes four types. Each is a separate cause of action.

### TYPE 1: Intrusion Upon Seclusion (3 Elements)
| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Intentional intrusion | Defendant intentionally intruded upon plaintiff's solitude, seclusion, or private affairs |
| 2 | Highly offensive | The intrusion would be highly offensive to a reasonable person |
| 3 | Private matter | The matter intruded upon was private |

### TYPE 2: Public Disclosure of Private Facts (3 Elements)
| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Public disclosure | Defendant publicly disclosed private facts about plaintiff |
| 2 | Highly offensive | The disclosure would be highly offensive to a reasonable person |
| 3 | Not newsworthy | The matter disclosed is not of legitimate public concern |

### TYPE 3: False Light (3 Elements)
| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Publicity | Defendant gave publicity to a matter placing plaintiff in a false light |
| 2 | Highly offensive | The false light would be highly offensive to a reasonable person |
| 3 | Knowledge or recklessness | Defendant had knowledge of or acted in reckless disregard of the false light |

### TYPE 4: Appropriation of Name or Likeness (3 Elements)
| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Use of identity | Defendant used plaintiff's name, likeness, or identity |
| 2 | Without consent | The use was without plaintiff's consent |
| 3 | For defendant's advantage | The use was for defendant's commercial advantage or benefit |

## CIVIL CONSPIRACY (4 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** Tied to the underlying tort
**Authority:** Admiral Ins Co v Columbia Casualty Ins Co, 194 Mich App 300 (1992)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Concerted action | Two or more persons acted in concert |
| 2 | Combination or agreement | A combination or agreement to accomplish an act existed |
| 3 | Wrongful act or wrongful means | The purpose was unlawful, or the means used were unlawful (REQUIRES UNDERLYING TORT) |
| 4 | Resulting damages | Plaintiff suffered damages as a result of the conspiracy |

**CRITICAL:** Civil conspiracy is NOT a standalone tort in Michigan. Must plead an underlying tortious or unlawful act.

---

## ABUSE OF PROCESS (3 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 3 years (MCL 600.5805(2))
**Authority:** Friedman v Dozorc, 412 Mich 1 (1981)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Use of process | An ulterior purpose existed in the use of legal process (e.g., filing a motion, using discovery, invoking court authority) |
| 2 | Improper act | Defendant committed an act in the use of process that was improper in the regular conduct of the proceeding — some definite act or threat NOT authorized by the process |
| 3 | Resulting damages | Plaintiff suffered damages from the abuse |

**Distinguished from malicious prosecution:** Abuse of process = misuse of legitimately issued process for an improper purpose. Malicious prosecution = initiating process without probable cause.

---

## MALICIOUS PROSECUTION (5 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 3 years from favorable termination (MCL 600.5805(2))
**Authority:** Friedman v Dozorc, 412 Mich 1 (1981)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Prior proceeding | Defendant initiated or continued a prior proceeding against plaintiff |
| 2 | Lack of probable cause | The prior proceeding was brought without probable cause |
| 3 | Malice | The prior proceeding was initiated with malice (improper purpose) |
| 4 | Favorable termination | The prior proceeding terminated in plaintiff's favor |
| 5 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered damages (costs, fees, emotional distress, reputational harm) |

**FAVORABLE TERMINATION:** Must be on the merits or at least not inconsistent with innocence. Voluntary dismissal may or may not qualify depending on circumstances.

---

## WRONGFUL EVICTION — STATUTORY (MCL 600.2918)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 3 years (MCL 600.5805(2))
**Authority:** MCL 600.2918

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Tenancy | Plaintiff was a tenant with a possessory right |
| 2 | Wrongful removal | Defendant removed or excluded plaintiff from possession other than by lawful court process |
| 3 | No legal authority | Defendant lacked legal authority (no court order, no proper eviction proceedings) |
| 4 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered damages |

**TREBLE DAMAGES:** MCL 600.2918(2) provides for treble the amount of actual damages or $200, whichever is greater. This is AUTOMATIC, not discretionary.

---

## WRONGFUL EVICTION — COMMON LAW (4 ELEMENTS)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Tenancy | Valid landlord-tenant relationship existed |
| 2 | Wrongful act | Defendant engaged in conduct that effectively deprived plaintiff of possession (self-help eviction, lockout, utility shutoff, removal of belongings) |
| 3 | Lack of legal process | Defendant did not use proper legal eviction process |
| 4 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered actual damages (moving costs, temporary housing, emotional distress, property damage/loss) |

## BREACH OF CONTRACT (4 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 6 years (MCL 600.5807(8))

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Valid contract | A valid contract existed between the parties |
| 2 | Contract terms | The contract contained the terms plaintiff claims were breached |
| 3 | Breach | Defendant breached one or more terms of the contract |
| 4 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered damages as a result of the breach |

---

## TORTIOUS INTERFERENCE WITH CONTRACT (5 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 3 years (MCL 600.5805(2))
**Authority:** Feldman v Green, 138 Mich App 360 (1984)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Existing contract | A valid contract or business relationship existed between plaintiff and a third party |
| 2 | Knowledge | Defendant knew of the contract or relationship |
| 3 | Intentional interference | Defendant intentionally and unjustifiably interfered with the contract or relationship |
| 4 | Breach or termination | The interference caused a breach or termination of the contract or relationship |
| 5 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered damages resulting from the interference |

---

## UNJUST ENRICHMENT (3 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 6 years

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Benefit conferred | Plaintiff conferred a benefit on defendant |
| 2 | Knowledge and retention | Defendant knew of the benefit and retained it |
| 3 | Inequity | Under the circumstances, it would be inequitable for defendant to retain the benefit without paying for it |

---

## CONSTRUCTIVE EVICTION (4 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 3 years

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Landlord's action/inaction | Landlord substantially interfered with tenant's use and enjoyment (by act or failure to act) |
| 2 | Notice | Tenant gave landlord notice of the conditions |
| 3 | Reasonable time | Landlord failed to remedy within reasonable time |
| 4 | Tenant vacated | Tenant vacated the premises within reasonable time after the interference |

**NOTE:** Tenant MUST vacate to claim constructive eviction. Remaining in possession may waive this claim. Consider habitability claim (MCL 554.139) as alternative that does not require vacating.

---

## PREMISES LIABILITY (4 ELEMENTS)

**Standard:** Preponderance
**SOL:** 3 years
**Authority:** Stitt v Holland Abundant Life Fellowship, 462 Mich 591 (2000)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Duty of care | Landowner owed a duty of care to plaintiff based on plaintiff's status (invitee, licensee, trespasser) |
| 2 | Condition or hazard | A dangerous condition existed on the premises |
| 3 | Knowledge | Landowner knew or should have known of the condition |
| 4 | Damages | Plaintiff was injured and suffered damages as a proximate result |

**Invitee status:** Tenant is an invitee of the landlord as to common areas. Highest duty of care owed.
