# MICHIGAN STATUTORY CLAIMS — COMPLETE ELEMENTS

## CLAIMS CREATED BY MICHIGAN STATUTE

These claims provide specific statutory remedies, often with enhanced damages (treble, double) and fee-shifting. They are powerful tools in the Pigors litigation.

---

## MICHIGAN CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT (MCL 445.901+)

**SOL:** 6 years (MCL 445.911(7))
**Standard:** Preponderance
**Authority:** MCL 445.901–.922
**Remedies:** Actual damages or $250 (whichever greater) + attorney fees + treble damages if willful/knowing

### ELEMENTS (4)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Trade or commerce | The transaction occurred in "trade or commerce" — broadly defined to include rental of property |
| 2 | Unfair, unconscionable, or deceptive act | Defendant engaged in one or more practices prohibited by MCL 445.903 |
| 3 | Person aggrieved | Plaintiff was directly affected by the prohibited practice |
| 4 | Loss or damage | Plaintiff suffered a loss as a result of the prohibited practice |

### THE 29 ENUMERATED UNFAIR PRACTICES (MCL 445.903)

Key practices applicable to housing/landlord-tenant context:

| SUBSECTION | PRACTICE | APPLICATION |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| (c) | Representing goods are of a particular standard, quality, or grade when they are not | Misrepresenting property condition |
| (e) | Representing that goods have characteristics, uses, benefits, or quantities they do not have | False claims about property amenities, safety features |
| (g) | Advertising goods with intent not to sell as advertised | Bait-and-switch with rental property |
| (n) | Causing confusion as to the source, sponsorship, or certification of goods or services | Misrepresenting property ownership or management |
| (q) | Representing that goods are needed when they are not | Charging for unnecessary "repairs" or "services" |
| (s) | Failing to reveal a material fact, if the omission tends to mislead | Concealing known defects, hazards, violations |
| (t) | Entering into a consumer transaction where the consumer waives rights | Illegal lease provisions that waive tenant rights |
| (w) | Gross discrepancies between oral representations and written agreement | Promising repairs that don't appear in lease |
| (x) | Failing to provide promised benefits | Failing to deliver promised property condition |
| (y) | Gross discrepancies between actual condition and advertised condition | Property condition far worse than advertised |
| (z) | Making material misrepresentation that is relied upon in a transaction | Misrepresenting habitability, code compliance |
| (bb) | Making a representation of fact that is misleading | Overstating property condition, amenities |
| (cc) | Failing to reveal facts material to the transaction | Concealing mold, lead, structural defects |

### TREBLE DAMAGES ANALYSIS
MCL 445.911(2): "If the court finds that the [violation] was willful or knowing, the court may award... treble damages..." This is discretionary with the court but should ALWAYS be pled when evidence supports willful or knowing conduct.

### EXEMPTIONS TO WATCH
MCL 445.904 lists exemptions — particularly:
- (1)(a): Transaction specifically authorized under federal/state law
- (2): Transaction regulated by a regulatory board/officer may be exempt IF the board/officer actually regulates that specific practice

**NOTE:** Michigan courts have held that landlord-tenant transactions are NOT generally exempt from the MCPA. Dix v American Bankers Life Assurance Co, 429 Mich 410 (1987) (MCPA applies broadly to consumer transactions).

## TRUTH IN RENTING ACT (MCL 554.631+)

**SOL:** 6 years (MCL 554.636)
**Standard:** Preponderance
**Remedies:** $250 per violation + actual damages + reasonable attorney fees (MCL 554.636)

### ELEMENTS (3)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Residential lease | A written residential lease agreement exists |
| 2 | Prohibited provision | The lease contains one or more provisions prohibited by the Act |
| 3 | Damages | Tenant suffered damages or was adversely affected by the prohibited provision |

### PROHIBITED LEASE PROVISIONS (MCL 554.633)

The following provisions are VOID AND UNENFORCEABLE if included in a residential lease:

| TYPE | DESCRIPTION | STATUTORY BASIS |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Waiver of habitability | Provision waiving landlord's duty to maintain premises | MCL 554.633(1)(a) |
| Waiver of code compliance | Provision waiving landlord's duty to comply with building codes | MCL 554.633(1)(a) |
| Confession of judgment | Provision authorizing confession of judgment | MCL 554.633(1)(b) |
| Waiver of notice | Provision waiving requirement of notice or legal process for eviction | MCL 554.633(1)(c) |
| Limitation of liability | Provision limiting landlord's liability for negligence or willful misconduct | MCL 554.633(1)(d) |
| Acceleration clause | Unreasonable acceleration of rent upon breach | MCL 554.633(1)(e) |
| Waiver of redemption | Provision waiving tenant's right to redeem | MCL 554.633(1)(f) |
| Penalty provision | Provision imposing penalty for exercise of tenant's legal rights | MCL 554.633(1)(g) |
| Waiver of jury trial | Provision waiving right to jury trial | MCL 554.633(1)(h) |

### PER-VIOLATION DAMAGES
Each prohibited provision = $250 minimum. A lease with 4 prohibited provisions = minimum $1,000. Plus actual damages. Plus attorney fees.

## BREACH OF WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY (MCL 554.139)

**SOL:** 6 years (contract-based)
**Standard:** Preponderance
**Authority:** MCL 554.139; Trentadue v Gorton, 479 Mich 378 (2007)

### ELEMENTS (5)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Residential lease | A residential lease agreement exists between the parties |
| 2 | Unfit premises | The premises were not reasonably fit for their intended use as a residence, or were not kept in reasonable repair during the term of the lease |
| 3 | Covenants | MCL 554.139(1) creates two implied covenants: (a) premises and all common areas are fit for the use intended by the parties; and (b) premises are kept in reasonable repair during the lease term |
| 4 | Notice and failure to repair | Tenant notified landlord of defective conditions and landlord failed to repair within a reasonable time |
| 5 | Damages | Tenant suffered damages (diminished value of tenancy, out-of-pocket costs, consequential damages) |

### NON-WAIVABLE
MCL 554.139(3): "The provisions of this section shall be applicable... notwithstanding any agreement or covenant to the contrary." This warranty CANNOT be waived by the lease. Any waiver provision is void.

### WHAT CONSTITUTES "UNFIT"
Michigan courts have found these conditions render premises unfit:
- Lack of heat, hot water, or running water
- Broken plumbing, sewage backup
- Mold or hazardous materials (lead, asbestos)
- Structural defects (roof leaks, foundation problems)
- Pest infestations (rats, roaches, bedbugs)
- Non-functional locks, doors, or windows (security)
- Electrical hazards
- Failure to comply with housing/building codes

### DAMAGES CALCULATION
- **Diminished value:** Difference between rent paid and value of premises in defective condition
- **Out-of-pocket:** Repair costs, temporary housing, moving expenses
- **Consequential:** Medical costs from hazardous conditions, property damage

## SECURITY DEPOSIT VIOLATIONS (MCL 554.601+)

**SOL:** 6 years
**Standard:** Preponderance
**Remedies:** Double the amount wrongfully withheld (MCL 554.613)

### ELEMENTS (3)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Security deposit paid | Tenant paid a security deposit to landlord |
| 2 | Violation of Act | Landlord violated one or more provisions of the Security Deposit Act |
| 3 | Damages | Tenant was damaged by the violation (or entitled to statutory damages) |

### LANDLORD OBLIGATIONS (MCL 554.601–.616)

| OBLIGATION | REQUIREMENT | VIOLATION |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Maximum amount | Cannot exceed 1.5 months' rent (MCL 554.602) | Collecting more = violation |
| Deposit account | Must hold in regulated financial institution (MCL 554.603) | Commingling = violation |
| Move-in inventory | Must provide inventory checklist at move-in (MCL 554.608) | Failure = presumption in tenant's favor |
| Itemized list | Within 30 days of termination, must provide itemized list of damages claimed (MCL 554.609) | Failure = forfeiture of right to withhold |
| Return deadline | Unused portion must be returned within 30 days (MCL 554.609) | Failure = double damages |
| Normal wear and tear | Cannot deduct for normal wear and tear (MCL 554.610) | Deducting = violation |

### DOUBLE DAMAGES
MCL 554.613: If landlord fails to comply with the return/itemization requirements, tenant may recover DOUBLE the amount of the security deposit wrongfully withheld. This is MANDATORY, not discretionary.

## RETALIATORY ACTION (MCL 600.5720)

**SOL:** Defense to eviction (affirmative defense); also basis for damages claim
**Authority:** MCL 600.5720

### ELEMENTS (3)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Protected activity | Tenant engaged in protected activity: (a) complained to governmental authority about code violations, (b) complained to landlord about habitability, (c) joined a tenant organization |
| 2 | Retaliatory action | Landlord took adverse action: raised rent, decreased services, threatened/commenced eviction, or other retaliation |
| 3 | Causal connection | The adverse action was in retaliation for the protected activity (timing and circumstances create inference) |

### PRESUMPTION
MCL 600.5720(2): If adverse action occurs within 90 days of protected activity, a REBUTTABLE PRESUMPTION of retaliation arises. The burden shifts to the landlord to prove a legitimate, non-retaliatory reason.

## HOUSING DISCRIMINATION — ELLIOTT-LARSEN CIVIL RIGHTS ACT (MCL 37.2501+)

**SOL:** 3 years (MCL 37.2801)
**Remedies:** Compensatory damages, injunctive relief, attorney fees
**Administrative:** MDCR complaint must be filed within 180 days (MCL 37.2602)

### ELEMENTS (4)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Protected class | Plaintiff is a member of a protected class (religion, race, color, national origin, age, sex, familial status, marital status, disability) |
| 2 | Housing-related action | Defendant engaged in a housing-related action (rental, sale, terms, conditions, services) |
| 3 | Discriminatory treatment | Defendant treated plaintiff differently because of protected class membership |
| 4 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered damages from the discriminatory treatment |

### PROTECTED ACTIVITIES (MCL 37.2502)
It is unlawful to:
- Refuse to sell or rent
- Discriminate in terms, conditions, or privileges
- Make statements indicating preference or limitation based on protected class
- Represent that dwelling is not available when it is
- Discriminate in providing services or facilities

## BUILDING CODE VIOLATIONS

**Authority:** Michigan Building Code Act (MCL 125.1501+), local codes
**Remedies:** Injunctive relief, damages, penalties

### ELEMENTS (3)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Applicable code | A building, housing, fire, or health code applies to the property |
| 2 | Violation | The property violates one or more code requirements |
| 3 | Damages | The violation caused damages to plaintiff (or plaintiff seeks injunctive relief) |

### RELEVANCE TO OTHER CLAIMS
Building code violations serve as powerful evidence for:
- Negligence (violation = evidence of breach of duty — negligence per se)
- Habitability (code violation = strong evidence premises are unfit)
- MCPA (concealing code violations = unfair practice)
- Fraud (misrepresenting code compliance)

## LEAD PAINT DISCLOSURE (42 USC 4852d)

**Authority:** Residential Lead-Based Paint Hazard Reduction Act (Title X)
**Applies to:** Housing built before 1978
**Remedies:** Treble damages, costs, attorney fees

### ELEMENTS (3)

| # | ELEMENT | DESCRIPTION |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Pre-1978 housing | The rental property was built before 1978 |
| 2 | Failure to disclose | Landlord failed to: (a) disclose known lead-based paint hazards, (b) provide EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family From Lead in Your Home," or (c) include lead warning in lease |
| 3 | Damages | Tenant suffered damages (or statutory treble damages apply regardless) |

**TREBLE DAMAGES:** 42 USC 4852d(b)(3) authorizes treble damages for knowing violations. This is a powerful federal statutory claim.
