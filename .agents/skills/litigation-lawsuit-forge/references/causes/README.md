## ORGANIZATION BY TYPE

### TORTS (1-29, 53-62)
Common law claims based on wrongful conduct causing harm. See **tort-claims.md** for complete elements.

### STATUTORY CLAIMS (30-38)
Claims created by Michigan statute with specific requirements and remedies. See **statutory-claims.md** for complete elements.

### CONSTITUTIONAL CLAIMS (39-44, 52)
Claims based on violations of constitutional rights. Typically brought via § 1983. See **constitutional-claims.md** for complete elements.

### FEDERAL CLAIMS (45-51)
Claims arising under federal statutes. Filed in federal court or state court with concurrent jurisdiction. See **federal-claims.md** for complete elements.

## STRATEGIC NOTES

### HIGH-VALUE CLAIMS (FEE-SHIFTING / MULTIPLIED DAMAGES)
These claims deserve priority because they increase recovery and deter defendants:
- **MCPA:** Treble damages for willful violations (MCL 445.911(2))
- **Wrongful eviction:** Treble damages (MCL 600.2918(2))
- **Security deposit:** Double damages (MCL 554.613)
- **§ 1983:** Attorney fees (42 USC 1988) — even for pro se? Limited, but preserve the argument
- **Fair Housing Act:** Attorney fees, punitive damages
- **Truth in Renting:** $250 per violation + actual damages + attorney fees

### CLAIMS REQUIRING SPECIAL PLEADING
These claims have heightened pleading requirements under MCR 2.112 or federal equivalents:
- **Fraud** (all types): Particularity required
- **Mistake:** Particularity required
- **Conditions precedent:** Must specifically allege
- **RICO:** If asserted, pattern requirement + predicate acts with specificity

### CLAIMS WITH SHORT SOL
Watch these carefully — they expire fastest:
- **Defamation:** 1 year (MCL 600.5805(2))
- **42 USC 1986:** 1 year from when action could have been brought
- **Fair Housing Act (admin):** 1 year for HUD complaint
- **Whistleblower:** 90 days (MCL 15.362)
