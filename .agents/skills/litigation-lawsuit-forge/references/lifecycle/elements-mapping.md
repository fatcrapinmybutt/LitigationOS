# ELEMENT-BY-ELEMENT EVIDENCE MAPPING

## PHASE 3 — MAPPING EVERY ELEMENT TO PROOF

No element without evidence. No count without complete elements. This is the bridge between legal theory and courtroom proof.

---

## ELEMENT MAP STRUCTURE

For each cause of action, build this matrix:

```
CAUSE OF ACTION: [Name]
DEFENDANT(S): [Who]
BURDEN OF PROOF: [Preponderance / Clear and Convincing]
SOL STATUS: [Safe / Warning / Critical]

| # | ELEMENT | REQUIRED SHOWING | AVAILABLE EVIDENCE | GAP STATUS |
|---|---------|-----------------|-------------------|------------|
| 1 | [Element text] | [What must be proven] | [Specific evidence] | [MET/PARTIAL/GAP] |
| 2 | ... | ... | ... | ... |

OVERALL POSTURE: [STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK]
```

---

## BURDEN OF PROOF REFERENCE

| CLAIM TYPE | BURDEN | STANDARD |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Negligence | Preponderance | More likely than not (>50%) |
| Fraud | Clear and convincing | Highly probable, firm belief |
| IIED | Preponderance | More likely than not |
| Breach of contract | Preponderance | More likely than not |
| MCPA | Preponderance | More likely than not |
| § 1983 | Preponderance | More likely than not |
| Punitive damages | Clear and convincing | Highly probable (MCL 600.6304) |
| Habitability | Preponderance | More likely than not |
| Wrongful eviction | Preponderance | More likely than not |

**Note:** Clear and convincing is a HIGHER standard. Fraud claims require stronger evidence than negligence claims for the same underlying facts.

## EVIDENCE POSTURE REQUIREMENTS

### WHAT COUNTS AS EVIDENCE

| EVIDENCE TYPE | EXAMPLES | STRENGTH | ADMISSIBILITY NOTES |
|---------------|----------|----------|-------------------|
| Documentary | Leases, emails, texts, letters, receipts | HIGH | Authenticate per MRE 901 |
| Photographic | Photos of conditions, damage, communications | HIGH | Authenticate, timestamp |
| Testimonial | Plaintiff testimony, witness statements | MEDIUM | Subject to cross-examination |
| Expert | Building inspector, psychologist, appraiser | HIGH | Qualified per MRE 702 |
| Public records | Court filings, government records, LARA records | HIGH | Self-authenticating (MRE 902) |
| Electronic | Social media, GPS data, call logs, metadata | MEDIUM-HIGH | Foundation required (MRE 901(b)(9)) |
| Physical | Property damage, hazardous materials | HIGH | Chain of custody required |
| Circumstantial | Patterns, timing, relationships | MEDIUM | Must support reasonable inference |

### EVIDENCE GAP STATUS

- **MET:** Element fully supported by available admissible evidence
- **PARTIAL:** Some evidence exists but may be insufficient standing alone
- **GAP:** No evidence currently available — must be obtained through discovery or other means

### GAP-FILLING STRATEGIES

| GAP TYPE | STRATEGY | TOOL |
|----------|----------|------|
| Missing documents | Request for production | Discovery (MCR 2.310) |
| Unknown facts | Interrogatories | Discovery (MCR 2.309) |
| Witness testimony needed | Deposition | Discovery (MCR 2.306) |
| Government records | FOIA request | MCL 15.231+ |
| Expert opinion needed | Retain expert | Expert witness engagement |
| Adverse party admission | Request for admission | Discovery (MCR 2.312) |
| Third-party records | Subpoena duces tecum | MCR 2.305 |

## ELEMENT MAPS BY CAUSE OF ACTION

### FRAUD (6 ELEMENTS — CLEAR AND CONVINCING)

| # | ELEMENT | WHAT TO PROVE | EVIDENCE SOURCES |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Material misrepresentation | Defendant made a false statement of fact (not opinion) | Written statements, advertisements, lease terms, recorded conversations |
| 2 | Knowledge of falsity (scienter) | Defendant knew statement was false or made it recklessly | Prior complaints, inspection reports, defendant's communications showing knowledge |
| 3 | Intent to induce reliance | Defendant intended plaintiff to act on the false statement | Context of statement, relationship, timing of statement relative to transaction |
| 4 | Justifiable reliance | Plaintiff actually relied on the statement, and reliance was reasonable | Plaintiff testimony, transaction records, timeline showing reliance preceded discovery |
| 5 | Resulting damages | Plaintiff suffered actual damages from the reliance | Financial records, repair costs, medical bills, lost wages, moving costs |
| 6 | Causation | The reliance on the misrepresentation caused the damages | Timeline, but-for analysis, chain of causation documentation |

**SPECIAL PLEADING:** MCR 2.112(B)(1) — must plead with particularity. State the WHO, WHAT, WHEN, WHERE, and HOW of the fraud.

### NEGLIGENCE (4 ELEMENTS — PREPONDERANCE)

| # | ELEMENT | WHAT TO PROVE | EVIDENCE SOURCES |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Duty | Defendant owed plaintiff a duty of care | Relationship (landlord-tenant, etc.), statutory duty, contractual duty |
| 2 | Breach | Defendant failed to meet the standard of care | Expert testimony, building codes, industry standards, comparable conduct |
| 3 | Causation (proximate) | Breach was the proximate cause of plaintiff's injuries | Medical records, timeline, expert testimony, but-for analysis |
| 4 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered actual, compensable damages | Medical bills, repair costs, lost wages, property damage, pain and suffering |

### IIED (4 ELEMENTS + SEVERITY — PREPONDERANCE)

| # | ELEMENT | WHAT TO PROVE | EVIDENCE SOURCES |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Extreme and outrageous conduct | Conduct "so outrageous in character, and so extreme in degree, as to go beyond all possible bounds of decency" | Documentation of conduct, witness testimony, pattern evidence |
| 2 | Intent or recklessness | Defendant intended to cause distress or acted with reckless disregard | Defendant's statements, pattern of conduct, knowledge of plaintiff's vulnerability |
| 3 | Causation | Defendant's conduct caused the emotional distress | Timeline, medical/psychological records, testimony |
| 4 | Severe emotional distress | Distress is severe — not mere annoyance, embarrassment, or hurt feelings | Psychological/medical records, therapy records, testimony about impact on daily life |

**SEVERITY THRESHOLD:** Michigan requires distress be "severe" — Roberts v Auto-Owners Ins Co, 422 Mich 594 (1985). Must be "so severe that no reasonable person could be expected to endure it."

### § 1983 (4 ELEMENTS — PREPONDERANCE)

| # | ELEMENT | WHAT TO PROVE | EVIDENCE SOURCES |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Person acting under color of state law | Defendant is a state actor or acting jointly with state actors | Employment records, authority exercised, joint action evidence |
| 2 | Deprivation of rights | A constitutional or federal statutory right was violated | Nature of the right, how it was deprived |
| 3 | Causation | The state actor's conduct caused the deprivation | Direct evidence, policies, orders, actions |
| 4 | Damages | Plaintiff suffered compensable injury | Same as underlying constitutional violation |

**ADDITIONAL FOR MONELL CLAIMS:**
| # | ELEMENT | WHAT TO PROVE |
|---|---------|---------------|
| 5 | Policy, custom, or practice | The violation resulted from an official policy, widespread custom, or deliberate indifference |
| 6 | Moving force | The policy/custom was the "moving force" behind the violation |

### BREACH OF WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY (MCL 554.139)

| # | ELEMENT | WHAT TO PROVE | EVIDENCE SOURCES |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Residential lease exists | Valid lease agreement between parties | Lease document, rental history, payment records |
| 2 | Premises not fit for intended use | Conditions rendered premises unfit for habitation | Photos, inspection reports, code violations, expert testimony |
| 3 | Landlord had notice | Landlord was informed of the defective conditions | Written repair requests, emails, texts, certified mail, 311 reports |
| 4 | Reasonable time to repair elapsed | Sufficient time passed for landlord to make repairs | Timeline of requests vs. repairs, nature of defect (emergency vs. routine) |
| 5 | Damages | Tenant suffered damages from unfit conditions | Diminished value of tenancy, medical costs, relocation costs, property damage |

### MCPA VIOLATION (MCL 445.903)

| # | ELEMENT | WHAT TO PROVE | EVIDENCE SOURCES |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Transaction in trade or commerce | The transaction was a commercial transaction (landlord-tenant qualifies) | Lease, payments, business records |
| 2 | Unfair, unconscionable, or deceptive practice | Defendant engaged in one of the 29 enumerated practices under MCL 445.903 | Specific facts matching the enumerated practice |
| 3 | Person aggrieved | Plaintiff was the victim of the practice | Direct connection between practice and plaintiff |
| 4 | Loss/damages | Plaintiff suffered actual damages | Financial records, costs, lost value |

**TREBLE DAMAGES:** MCL 445.911(2) — court may award treble damages if violation was willful or knowing. PLEAD THIS.

### CIVIL CONSPIRACY (4 ELEMENTS — PREPONDERANCE)

| # | ELEMENT | WHAT TO PROVE | EVIDENCE SOURCES |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Concerted action | Two or more persons acted in concert | Communications, meetings, coordinated timing, shared interests |
| 2 | Combination of persons | A combination or agreement existed | Direct evidence of agreement or circumstantial (parallel conduct + motive) |
| 3 | Unlawful act or unlawful means | The purpose or means were unlawful (underlying tort required) | Evidence of the underlying tortious conduct |
| 4 | Resulting damages | Plaintiff suffered damages from the conspiracy | Same damages as underlying tort |

**CRITICAL:** Civil conspiracy is NOT a standalone claim in Michigan. Must plead an underlying tort. Admiral Ins Co v Columbia Casualty Ins Co, 194 Mich App 300 (1992).

## GAP ANALYSIS PROTOCOL

After mapping all elements, run this analysis:

### FOR EACH CLAIM WITH GAPS:

1. **Is the gap fatal?** Can the claim survive summary judgment without this evidence?
2. **Is the gap fillable?** Can discovery, FOIA, or investigation obtain the missing evidence?
3. **Timeline for filling:** How long will it take to obtain the evidence?
4. **Cost of filling:** What resources are needed?
5. **Risk assessment:** What if the gap cannot be filled?

### DECISION MATRIX

| GAP STATUS | FILLABLE? | ACTION |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| Single non-critical gap | Yes | Proceed, fill via discovery |
| Single critical gap | Yes | Proceed cautiously, prioritize gap-filling |
| Single critical gap | No | Consider dropping claim |
| Multiple gaps | Yes | Proceed only if claim has high strategic value |
| Multiple gaps | No | Drop claim — do not waste court resources |
| No gaps | N/A | Proceed with confidence |

## EVIDENCE STRENGTH SCORING

For each piece of evidence mapped to an element, score its strength:

| SCORE | MEANING | DESCRIPTION |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| 5 | Conclusive | Document/record that directly proves the element beyond dispute |
| 4 | Strong | Clear evidence that strongly supports the element |
| 3 | Moderate | Reasonable evidence, may face challenge but holds up |
| 2 | Weak | Some evidence, but vulnerable to attack or alternative interpretation |
| 1 | Minimal | Barely relevant, circumstantial at best |
| 0 | None | No evidence available |

**CLAIM STRENGTH = (Sum of element scores) / (Max possible score) × 100%**

Example: Fraud (6 elements) with scores [4, 3, 3, 4, 5, 4] = 23/30 = 77% = MODERATE-STRONG

**MINIMUM THRESHOLD:** 60% to include in complaint. Below 60% = exclude unless strategic value is overwhelming.
