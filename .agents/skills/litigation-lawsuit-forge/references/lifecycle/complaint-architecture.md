# VERIFIED COMPLAINT ARCHITECTURE

## MCR-COMPLIANT COMPLAINT STRUCTURE — SECTION BY SECTION

This reference provides the complete structural blueprint for drafting a verified complaint in Michigan courts, compliant with the Michigan Court Rules.

---

## GOVERNING RULES

| RULE | SUBJECT | REQUIREMENT |
|------|---------|-------------|
| MCR 2.111 | General rules of pleading | Numbered paragraphs, concise statements, separate counts |
| MCR 2.112 | Special pleading | Fraud particularity, conditions precedent, official acts |
| MCR 2.113 | Form of pleadings | Caption, title, signature, paper size |
| MCR 2.114 | Signatures | Must sign every filing; certifies good faith basis |
| MCR 2.508 | Jury trial | Demand in first filing or risk waiver |
| MCR 2.111(B)(1) | Jurisdictional facts | Must plead facts showing jurisdiction |
| MCR 2.111(A)(1) | Format | Clear, concise, and direct statements |

---

## SECTION-BY-SECTION GUIDE

### 1. CAPTION (MCR 2.113)

The caption is the heading of the complaint. It must contain:

```
STATE OF MICHIGAN
IN THE [14TH] JUDICIAL CIRCUIT COURT FOR THE COUNTY OF [MUSKEGON]

ANDREW PIGORS,                          )
    Plaintiff, Pro Se,                  )
                                        )   Case No. ________________
v.                                      )
                                        )   Hon. ____________________
[DEFENDANT 1 FULL LEGAL NAME],         )
[DEFENDANT 2 FULL LEGAL NAME],         )
    jointly and severally,              )
    Defendants.                         )
________________________________________)

VERIFIED COMPLAINT AND JURY DEMAND
```

**REQUIREMENTS:**
- Full legal name of court
- Full names of all parties (legal names — no nicknames, no abbreviations)
- Designation of each party (plaintiff/defendant)
- Case number (left blank — clerk assigns)
- Title of document
- "JURY DEMAND" in the title if jury trial requested

**PRO SE DESIGNATION:**
Add "Pro Se" after plaintiff name and include pro se information block:

```
ANDREW PIGORS, Pro Se
[Address]
[City, State ZIP]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

### 2. INTRODUCTION

Brief introductory paragraph identifying what the complaint is about:

```
Plaintiff Andrew Pigors ("Plaintiff"), appearing pro se, brings this Verified
Complaint against Defendants [names] and alleges as follows:
```

### 3. JURISDICTION (MCR 2.111(B)(1))

Must plead facts establishing the court's jurisdiction:

**Circuit Court Civil:**
```
1. This Court has jurisdiction over the subject matter of this action pursuant
   to MCL 600.601 and MCL 600.605 because the amount in controversy exceeds
   $25,000.00, exclusive of interest and costs.

2. This Court has jurisdiction over the person of each Defendant because
   [Defendant resides in Muskegon County / Defendant transacts business in
   Muskegon County / the cause of action arose in Muskegon County].
```

**Federal Court (§ 1983):**
```
1. This Court has original jurisdiction over this action pursuant to 28 USC
   § 1331 (federal question) and 28 USC § 1343(a)(3) (civil rights).

2. This Court has supplemental jurisdiction over the state law claims pursuant
   to 28 USC § 1367(a) because the state claims arise from the same case or
   controversy as the federal claims.
```

### 4. VENUE

Must establish that the chosen court is the proper venue:

```
3. Venue is proper in this Court pursuant to MCL 600.1629 because the acts
   and omissions giving rise to this action occurred in Muskegon County,
   Michigan.
```

**Alternative bases:**
- MCL 600.1621: Defendant resides in the county
- MCL 600.1625: Contract was to be performed in the county
- MCL 600.1605: Real property is located in the county
- MCL 600.5714: Landlord-tenant — property located in the county

### 5. PARTIES

Identify each party with full legal name, status, and relevant details:

```
4. Plaintiff Andrew Pigors ("Plaintiff") is an adult individual residing in
   Muskegon County, Michigan. Plaintiff appears pro se in this action.

5. Defendant [Name] ("Defendant [Short Name]") is [an adult individual
   residing in / a Michigan limited liability company with its principal
   place of business in] Muskegon County, Michigan.

6. Defendant [Name] ("Defendant [Short Name]") is [description]. At all
   times relevant hereto, Defendant [Short Name] was acting as [agent/
   employee/manager] of [entity] and [individually / within the scope of
   employment].
```

**FOR CORPORATE/LLC DEFENDANTS:**
- State exact legal name as registered with Michigan LARA
- State type of entity (LLC, corporation, partnership)
- State principal place of business
- State registered agent if relevant

**FOR GOVERNMENT DEFENDANTS (§ 1983):**
- Name individual AND specify capacity (individual and/or official)
- State title and role at time of relevant conduct
- If suing municipality under Monell, name the governmental entity

### 6. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

The heart of the complaint. Every fact supporting your claims, in numbered paragraphs.

**MCR 2.111 REQUIREMENTS:**
- Each allegation in a separately numbered paragraph (MCR 2.111(A)(2))
- Paragraphs contain concise statements of fact (MCR 2.111(A)(1))
- Statements are "simple, concise, and direct" — no argument, no conclusions of law disguised as facts
- Organize chronologically or thematically (chronological preferred for narrative clarity)
- Include dates, participants, locations, and key details
- Reference documentary evidence ("As shown in Exhibit A, attached hereto...")

**STRUCTURE:**
```
FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

7. On or about [date], Plaintiff entered into a written residential lease
   agreement with Defendant [Name] for the property located at [address],
   Muskegon County, Michigan. A true and correct copy of the lease is
   attached hereto as Exhibit A.

8. The lease term was from [date] to [date], at a monthly rental rate of
   $[amount].

9. At the time of lease execution, Defendant [Name] represented to Plaintiff
   that the premises were in habitable condition and complied with all
   applicable building codes.

[Continue with numbered paragraphs covering all relevant facts...]
```

**DO:**
- State facts, not conclusions
- Be specific about dates, amounts, and participants
- Reference exhibits
- Include facts supporting EVERY element of EVERY claim you will assert

**DON'T:**
- Argue your case in the fact section
- Include legal conclusions ("Defendant was negligent" — that's for the count)
- Editorialize or express opinions
- Include irrelevant facts that don't support any claim

### 7. COUNTS

Each cause of action gets its own count. Structure:

```
COUNT I — FRAUD
(Against Defendant [Name])

27. Plaintiff incorporates by reference paragraphs 1 through 26 as if fully
    set forth herein.

28. Defendant [Name] made material misrepresentations to Plaintiff regarding
    [specific representations], specifically: [who said what, when, where,
    how].

29. At the time of making said representations, Defendant [Name] knew the
    representations were false, or made them recklessly without knowledge of
    their truth.

30. Defendant [Name] made the representations with the intent that Plaintiff
    would rely upon them in [entering the lease / remaining in the premises /
    specific action].

31. Plaintiff justifiably relied on Defendant's representations by [specific
    acts of reliance].

32. As a direct and proximate result of Plaintiff's reliance on Defendant's
    false representations, Plaintiff has suffered damages including but not
    limited to [specific damages].

33. Defendant's conduct was willful, wanton, and in conscious disregard of
    Plaintiff's rights, entitling Plaintiff to exemplary damages.
```

**COUNT RULES:**
- Title format: "COUNT [Roman numeral] — [CAUSE OF ACTION]"
- Specify which defendant(s) the count is against
- Incorporate prior paragraphs by reference
- Plead EVERY element of the cause of action
- Specify damages
- If fraud or special pleading applies, plead with particularity (MCR 2.112)
- Continue paragraph numbering sequentially (do not restart at 1)

**ORDERING COUNTS:**
1. Strongest claims first
2. Federal claims before state claims (if in federal court)
3. Group related claims together
4. End with alternative or derivative claims

### 8. PRAYER FOR RELIEF

The "WHEREFORE" clause specifying what you want the court to do:

```
PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Andrew Pigors respectfully requests that this Court
enter judgment in Plaintiff's favor and against Defendants, jointly and
severally, and grant the following relief:

a. Compensatory damages in an amount to be proven at trial, but in no event
   less than $[amount];

b. Statutory damages as provided under [MCL 445.911 / MCL 554.613 / etc.];

c. Treble damages pursuant to MCL 445.911(2) for Defendants' willful and
   knowing violations of the Michigan Consumer Protection Act;

d. Punitive/exemplary damages for Defendants' willful, wanton, and malicious
   conduct;

e. Costs and disbursements of this action;

f. Pre-judgment and post-judgment interest as provided by law;

g. Injunctive relief [specify if applicable];

h. Attorney fees [under applicable fee-shifting statutes — 42 USC 1988,
   MCL 445.911(2), etc.] (Note: pro se litigants generally cannot recover
   attorney fees, but preserve the argument);

i. Such other and further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.
```

**SPECIFIC DEMANDS BY CLAIM TYPE:**
| CLAIM | AVAILABLE RELIEF |
|-------|-----------------|
| Fraud | Actual damages, rescission, punitive damages |
| Negligence | Compensatory damages (economic + non-economic) |
| IIED | Compensatory + punitive damages |
| Habitability | Rent abatement, repair costs, consequential damages |
| MCPA | Actual damages or $250 (whichever greater), treble if willful |
| Truth in Renting | $250 per violation, actual damages, attorney fees |
| § 1983 | Compensatory, punitive (against individuals), attorney fees (42 USC 1988) |
| Wrongful eviction | Treble damages (MCL 600.2918) |
| Security deposit | Double damages (MCL 554.613) |

### 9. JURY DEMAND

```
DEMAND FOR JURY TRIAL

Plaintiff demands a trial by jury on all issues so triable.
```

**MCR 2.508(B):** The demand MUST be in the first paper filed by the party, or it may be waived. Always include it.

### 10. VERIFICATION

For a verified complaint, the verification is a sworn statement:

```
VERIFICATION

STATE OF MICHIGAN  )
                   ) ss.
COUNTY OF MUSKEGON )

I, Andrew Pigors, being duly sworn, state that I have read the foregoing
Verified Complaint and that the facts stated therein are true to the best
of my knowledge, information, and belief, except as to those matters stated
on information and belief, and as to those matters, I believe them to be true.

________________________________
Andrew Pigors, Plaintiff, Pro Se

Subscribed and sworn to before me this _____ day of _____________, 20___.

________________________________
Notary Public, Muskegon County, Michigan
My Commission Expires: ___________
```

**"INFORMATION AND BELIEF" LANGUAGE:**
Use "upon information and belief" for facts you believe true but cannot personally verify. Example: "Upon information and belief, Defendant [LLC] is a Michigan limited liability company organized under the laws of the State of Michigan." This protects you from perjury for facts within the defendant's knowledge.

### 11. SIGNATURE BLOCK

```
Respectfully submitted,

________________________________
Andrew Pigors, Pro Se
[Street Address]
[City, MI ZIP]
[Phone Number]
[Email Address]

Dated: _____________, 20___
```

**MCR 2.114 — EFFECT OF SIGNATURE:**
Your signature certifies:
1. The document is well grounded in fact
2. It is warranted by existing law or good faith argument for extension/modification of law
3. It is not filed for an improper purpose (harassment, delay, cost increase)

Violation → sanctions under MCR 2.114(E).

### 12. EXHIBITS

```
INDEX OF EXHIBITS

Exhibit A — Residential Lease Agreement dated [date]
Exhibit B — Written Repair Request dated [date]
Exhibit C — Photographs of Premises Conditions dated [date]
Exhibit D — [Description]
...
```

**EXHIBIT RULES:**
- Each exhibit is lettered sequentially (A, B, C...)
- Referenced in the body of the complaint ("as shown in Exhibit A")
- Attached to the complaint or filed simultaneously
- Authenticated if possible (cover sheet noting what it is and where it came from)

## MCR 2.112 — SPECIAL PLEADING REQUIREMENTS

### (B)(1) FRAUD, MISTAKE, BREACH OF TRUST
Must plead "with particularity the circumstances constituting fraud or mistake." This means:
- **WHO** made the representation
- **WHAT** was represented
- **WHEN** the representation was made
- **WHERE** the representation was made
- **HOW** the representation was false
- **WHY** plaintiff relied on it

### (B)(2) CONDITIONS PRECEDENT
Must specifically allege either:
- All conditions precedent have been performed or have occurred, OR
- Which specific conditions have NOT been performed and why

### (B)(3) OFFICIAL DOCUMENT OR ACT
Sufficient to allege document was issued or act done in compliance with law.

### (B)(5) TIME AND PLACE
When material to the claim, time and place must be specifically alleged.
