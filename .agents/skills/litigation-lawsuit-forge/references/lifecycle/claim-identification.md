# SYSTEMATIC CLAIM IDENTIFICATION

## PHASE 2 — FROM FACTS TO VIABLE CAUSES OF ACTION

This reference provides the systematic framework for identifying every viable cause of action from a given fact pattern. The goal is comprehensive identification — miss nothing, then strategically select which claims to pursue.

---

## FACT PATTERN ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

### STEP 1: CATEGORIZE THE HARM
For each harmful event in the timeline, classify it:

| HARM CATEGORY | EXAMPLES | PRIMARY LEGAL THEORIES |
|---------------|----------|----------------------|
| Physical harm to person | Assault, unsafe conditions, injury | Negligence, assault/battery, premises liability |
| Emotional/psychological harm | Harassment, threats, intimidation | IIED, NIED, stalking, harassment |
| Financial harm | Fraud, theft, overcharging, lost wages | Fraud, conversion, unjust enrichment, MCPA |
| Property harm | Damage, destruction, wrongful taking | Conversion, trespass, negligence |
| Housing harm | Uninhabitable conditions, wrongful eviction | MCL 554.139, wrongful eviction, MCPA |
| Rights deprivation | Due process, parental rights, equal protection | § 1983, constitutional torts |
| Discrimination | Based on protected class | ELCRA, Fair Housing Act, § 1983 |
| Court/legal system abuse | False filings, perjury, improper orders | Abuse of process, malicious prosecution |
| Contractual harm | Breach, fraud in the inducement | Breach of contract, fraud, promissory estoppel |
| Conspiracy/coordination | Multiple actors, coordinated misconduct | Civil conspiracy, § 1985, RICO |

### STEP 2: RIGHTS VIOLATED → APPLICABLE LAW → ELEMENTS

For each categorized harm, trace the legal path:

```
FACT: Landlord refused to repair heating system for 3 months despite written requests
  │
  ├── RIGHT VIOLATED: Habitability (right to safe, fit dwelling)
  │     └── APPLICABLE LAW: MCL 554.139(1)(a) — implied warranty of habitability
  │           └── ELEMENTS: (1) residential lease, (2) premises not fit for intended use,
  │                         (3) landlord had notice, (4) reasonable time to repair elapsed, (5) damages
  │
  ├── RIGHT VIOLATED: Consumer protection (unfair/deceptive practice)
  │     └── APPLICABLE LAW: MCL 445.903(1)(s) — failing to reveal material fact
  │           └── ELEMENTS: (1) transaction in trade or commerce, (2) unfair/deceptive act,
  │                         (3) enumerated violation, (4) damages
  │
  └── RIGHT VIOLATED: Contractual obligations
        └── APPLICABLE LAW: Common law breach of contract
              └── ELEMENTS: (1) valid contract, (2) terms of contract, (3) breach, (4) damages
```

### STEP 3: VIABILITY ASSESSMENT

For each potential claim, assess:

| FACTOR | ASSESSMENT | WEIGHT |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| SOL status | Expired / Expiring / Safe | FATAL if expired |
| Element completeness | All elements provable / Gaps exist | HIGH |
| Evidence strength | Strong / Moderate / Weak | HIGH |
| Damages available | Actual / Statutory / Treble / Punitive | MEDIUM |
| Defendant solvency | Collectible / Uncollectible | MEDIUM |
| Strategic value | Leverage / Settlement / Precedent | LOW |

**Scoring:**
- STRONG (80-100%): Pursue aggressively
- MODERATE (60-79%): Pursue if strategic value justifies
- WEAK (40-59%): Consider including for settlement leverage only
- NON-VIABLE (<40%): Do not pursue — exclude from complaint

## CROSS-CLAIM AND COUNTERCLAIM IDENTIFICATION

### CROSS-CLAIMS
When multiple defendants are named, analyze whether any defendant has a claim against a co-defendant:
- **Contribution:** Defendant A seeks contribution from Defendant B for shared liability
- **Indemnification:** Defendant A contracted for Defendant B to hold harmless
- **Direct claims:** Defendant A has independent claims against Defendant B

### COUNTERCLAIM IDENTIFICATION
Anticipate defendant counterclaims and prepare responses:

| LIKELY COUNTERCLAIM | DEFENSE | PREEMPTIVE ACTION |
|--------------------|---------|-------------------|
| Unpaid rent | Habitability defense (MCL 554.139) | Document conditions, repair requests |
| Breach of lease | Constructive eviction | Document uninhabitable conditions |
| Frivolous lawsuit | MCR 2.114 compliance | Ensure every claim has good faith basis |
| Abuse of process | Legitimate purpose | Document proper purpose for every filing |

### COMPULSORY COUNTERCLAIMS (MCR 2.203(A))
If plaintiff has been sued by the defendant in another action, any claim arising from the same transaction or occurrence MUST be raised as a compulsory counterclaim. Failure = waiver.

## THIRD-PARTY CLAIM ANALYSIS

### WHEN TO ADD THIRD PARTIES
- Person or entity not originally named who shares liability
- Indemnification or contribution rights
- Joint tortfeasors not yet in the case
- "Empty chair" defense prevention — name everyone now

### MICHIGAN THIRD-PARTY PRACTICE (MCR 2.204)
- Defendant may file third-party complaint against non-party
- Must be within "reasonable time" after filing answer
- Third-party plaintiff must serve third-party complaint like original process

### COMMON THIRD PARTIES IN PIGORS CONTEXT
- Property management companies (if landlord uses manager)
- Contractors (if repairs were delegated and botched)
- Insurance companies (if coverage triggers apply)
- Government inspectors (if inspections were missed or falsified)
- Social workers or GAL (if custody recommendations were improper)

## DERIVATIVE CLAIMS

### CLAIMS THAT DERIVE FROM PRIMARY CLAIMS

**Civil Conspiracy (requires underlying tort)**
- NOT a standalone claim in Michigan
- Must identify the underlying tortious act
- Elements: (1) concerted action, (2) by two or more persons, (3) to accomplish an unlawful purpose or lawful purpose by unlawful means, (4) resulting in damages
- Available here: Landlord + property manager + contractor coordinating to evade habitability obligations

**Aiding and Abetting**
- One who aids and abets a tortious act is jointly liable
- Elements: (1) tortious act by primary actor, (2) knowledge of the tort, (3) substantial assistance or encouragement
- Available here: Parties who helped conceal housing code violations

**Loss of Consortium**
- Derivative of primary personal injury claim
- Available to spouse/family members
- Must plead in same action as primary claim

**Respondeat Superior / Vicarious Liability**
- Employer liable for employee acts within scope of employment
- LLC liable for member/manager acts in course of LLC business
- Government entity liable for official acts of employees

## SOL AUDIT PROTOCOL

For EVERY identified claim, complete this audit:

```
CLAIM: [Name]
ACCRUAL DATE: [When the cause of action accrued]
SOL PERIOD: [Statutory limitation period]
SOL EXPIRY: [Exact date]
TOLLING APPLICABLE?: [Yes/No — if yes, explain basis]
DISCOVERY RULE?: [Yes/No — if yes, when discovered or should have discovered]
CONTINUING VIOLATION?: [Yes/No — if yes, latest act date]
STATUS: [SAFE / WARNING / CRITICAL / EXPIRED]
```

**STATUS DEFINITIONS:**
- **SAFE:** > 6 months remaining
- **WARNING:** 1-6 months remaining — prioritize this claim
- **CRITICAL:** < 30 days remaining — EMERGENCY FAST-TRACK
- **EXPIRED:** Cannot file — document and exclude

## STRATEGIC CLAIM SELECTION

After identifying all viable claims, strategically select which to include:

### INCLUDE CRITERIA
- Every element is provable or reasonably discoverable
- SOL is unexpired
- Damages are meaningful and collectible
- Claim adds strategic value (leverage, precedent, fee-shifting)

### EXCLUDE CRITERIA
- Fatal evidence gap with no discovery remedy
- SOL expired or expiring before filing is possible
- Duplicative of stronger claim (don't clutter the complaint)
- Damages are negligible
- Claim weakens overall case credibility

### CLAIM GROUPING STRATEGY
- **Lead claims:** Strongest claims that anchor the complaint (put first)
- **Supporting claims:** Claims that reinforce the narrative
- **Alternative claims:** Pled in the alternative where facts support multiple theories
- **Statutory claims:** Claims with fee-shifting or treble damages (strategic value)
- **Federal claims:** If filing in federal court, federal claims first, state claims via supplemental jurisdiction
