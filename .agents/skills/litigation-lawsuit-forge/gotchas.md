# GOTCHAS — ANTI-RATIONALIZATION GUIDE

## THE LAWSUIT-KILLING MISTAKES

This file exists to prevent the catastrophic errors that destroy lawsuits before they begin. Every item here has ended a real case. Do not rationalize away any of these checks.

## 1. STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS MISSED

**THE KILL SHOT.** Nothing saves a time-barred claim. Not equity, not ignorance, not sympathy.

### Michigan SOL Reference (MCL 600.5805 et seq.)
| CLAIM TYPE | SOL | STATUTE |
|------------|-----|---------|
| Fraud | 6 years (discovery rule applies) | MCL 600.5813 |
| Negligence | 3 years | MCL 600.5805(2) |
| IIED / NIED | 3 years | MCL 600.5805(2) |
| Civil conspiracy | Tied to underlying tort | Varies |
| Abuse of process | 3 years | MCL 600.5805(2) |
| Malicious prosecution | 3 years from favorable termination | MCL 600.5805(2) |
| Wrongful eviction | 3 years (statutory) | MCL 600.5805(2) |
| Breach of habitability | 6 years (contract) | MCL 600.5807(8) |
| Consumer protection (MCPA) | 6 years | MCL 445.911(7) |
| Truth in Renting | 6 years | MCL 554.636 |
| § 1983 | 3 years (borrows MI personal injury SOL) | MCL 600.5805(2) |
| Fair Housing Act | 2 years (admin: 1 year) | 42 USC 3613(a)(1)(A) |
| Housing discrimination (ELCRA) | 3 years | MCL 37.2801 |

### DISCOVERY RULE TRAPS
- Fraud SOL runs from date plaintiff DISCOVERED or SHOULD HAVE DISCOVERED the fraud — not the date of the fraudulent act.
- Continuing violation doctrine may extend SOL if wrongful conduct is ongoing, but do NOT rely on this without clear precedent.
- Tolling for minors: SOL tolled until age 18 (MCL 600.5851).
- Mental incapacity tolling: Narrow, requires adjudication or clear evidence (MCL 600.5851).

### ANTI-RATIONALIZATION
- "We still have time" → CALCULATE THE EXACT DATE. Write it down. Calendar it.
- "The discovery rule will save us" → Maybe. But file anyway. Let the court decide.
- "It's a continuing violation" → The first act may still be time-barred. Plead both theories.

## 2. WRONG DEFENDANT NAMED

**YOU CANNOT SUE SOMEONE WHO DID NOT CAUSE YOUR HARM.**

### Common Mistakes
- Suing the property manager when the LLC is the landlord
- Suing an individual when they acted in corporate capacity
- Suing a dissolved entity
- Suing "John Doe" without a plan to identify and substitute
- Suing a government employee without specifying individual vs. official capacity
- Naming a parent company when only the subsidiary is liable

### How To Get It Right
1. **Landlords:** Check the lease. Who signed? Check LARA for LLC/corporate records. The entity on the lease is your defendant.
2. **Corporations/LLCs:** Search Michigan LARA (Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs) for exact legal name, registered agent, and status (active/dissolved).
3. **Government Officials:** Name them individually AND in official capacity. Individual = personal liability (qualified immunity defense). Official = entity liability (Monell analysis for municipalities).
4. **Multiple Defendants:** Name ALL parties in the chain. Landlord LLC + managing member + property manager + contractor. Let discovery sort out who did what.

### ANTI-RATIONALIZATION
- "Close enough" → NO. The legal name must be EXACT. "Shady Oaks Homes" ≠ "Shady Oaks Homes of America, LLC."
- "They're all the same company" → Prove it. Plead alter ego / veil piercing if needed.
- "We can amend later" → Relation-back doctrine (MCR 2.118(D)) has limits. New defendants added after SOL = possible dismissal.

## 3. WRONG VENUE / JURISDICTION

**FILE IN THE WRONG COURT = START OVER (AT BEST) OR LOSE FOREVER.**

### Michigan Venue Rules (MCL 600.1621 et seq.)
- **General rule:** County where defendant resides or has principal place of business (MCL 600.1621).
- **Tort actions:** County where cause of action arose OR where defendant resides (MCL 600.1629).
- **Contract actions:** County where defendant resides or where contract was to be performed (MCL 600.1625).
- **Landlord-tenant:** County where property is located (MCL 600.5714).
- **Real property:** County where property is located (MCL 600.1605).

### Jurisdiction Mistakes
- Filing a federal claim in state court without concurrent jurisdiction analysis
- Filing a state claim in federal court without diversity or supplemental jurisdiction
- Filing in circuit court when amount in controversy is under $25,000 (district court territory)
- Filing custody matters outside the home county of the child (UCCJEA analysis required)

### ANTI-RATIONALIZATION
- "Muskegon County is fine for everything" → Only if the cause of action arose there or defendant resides there. Verify EACH claim.
- "Federal court is better" → Only if you have a federal question or diversity. Supplemental jurisdiction for state claims can be declined.
- "The court will transfer it" → Maybe. But you lose time, credibility, and potentially your SOL.

## 4. MISSING ELEMENTS IN COUNTS

**A COUNT MISSING AN ELEMENT IS A COUNT THAT GETS DISMISSED.**

### MCR 2.116(C)(8) — Failure to State a Claim
The defendant WILL file a motion to dismiss if any element is missing. The court WILL grant it if the complaint, taken as true, fails to state every element.

### How Elements Get Missed
- Drafting fraud without pleading reliance or damages
- Pleading negligence without establishing duty (no relationship alleged)
- IIED without alleging conduct was "extreme and outrageous" (the magic words)
- § 1983 without alleging "under color of state law"
- Consumer protection without identifying which of the 29 enumerated unfair practices was violated
- Conspiracy without alleging an underlying tort (conspiracy is not a standalone claim in Michigan)

### THE ELEMENT CHECKLIST PROTOCOL
Before finalizing any count:
1. List every element of the cause of action
2. Find the paragraph number(s) in the complaint that plead each element
3. If ANY element lacks a corresponding paragraph → ADD ONE
4. Cross-reference with the elements-mapping.md reference

### ANTI-RATIONALIZATION
- "The court can infer it" → No. Plead it explicitly. Leave nothing to inference.
- "It's obvious from the facts" → The legal standard is whether the element is PLED, not whether it is inferrable.
- "We'll add it in an amendment" → That signals weakness. Get it right the first time.

## 5. SERVICE DEFECTS

**IMPROPER SERVICE = NO JURISDICTION OVER THE DEFENDANT.**

### Common Service Failures
- Serving the wrong person at a business address
- Mailing to the wrong address (must be defendant's last known address)
- Failing to include all required documents (summons + complaint + exhibits)
- Process server who is a party to the action (prohibited — MCR 2.103(A))
- Serving a minor without serving parent/guardian
- Failing to file proof of service

### Michigan Service Requirements (MCR 2.105)
- **Personal service:** Deliver to defendant personally. Always valid.
- **Registered mail:** Return receipt requested, restricted delivery. 28 days to answer (not 21).
- **Substitution:** Service on person of suitable age at defendant's dwelling. Followed by first-class mail.
- **Corporations/LLCs:** Serve registered agent, officer, or director. Check LARA for registered agent.
- **Government:** Serve the attorney general AND the specific agency/official.

### 91-DAY RULE (MCR 2.102(D))
Complaint MUST be served within 91 days of filing. Failure = dismissal without prejudice. The clock starts on the FILING DATE, not the date you get the stamped copies back.

### ANTI-RATIONALIZATION
- "They know about the lawsuit" → Knowledge is NOT service. Due process requires proper notice.
- "We mailed it" → Regular mail is NOT service. Must be registered mail, return receipt requested.
- "The process server said it was fine" → Get the affidavit. Verify every detail.

## 6. FILING FEE ISSUES

### Michigan Filing Fees (Current)
| COURT | FEE | NOTES |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Circuit Court | ~$175 | Varies by county |
| District Court | ~$65 | Varies by district |
| Small Claims | ~$30-$70 | Based on amount |
| Federal Court (USDC) | $405 | Fixed |

### Fee Waiver (MC 20 — Affidavit and Order, Suspension of Fees/Costs)
- Available for indigent litigants
- Must demonstrate inability to pay without depriving self or dependents of necessities
- File MC 20 WITH the complaint — do not file complaint without fee or waiver
- Court may deny waiver — have a backup plan to pay
- Fee waiver covers filing fee, service fees, and transcript costs

### ANTI-RATIONALIZATION
- "I'll pay later" → No. The clerk will not accept an unfiled complaint. Fee or waiver at filing.
- "I qualify for a waiver" → Maybe. Complete the MC 20 fully and honestly. Attach proof of income.
- "The fee is wrong" → Call the clerk's office before filing. Confirm the exact amount.

## 7. SPECIAL PLEADING FAILURES (MCR 2.112)

### What Requires Special Pleading
Michigan Court Rule 2.112 requires heightened specificity for certain allegations:

**(B)(1) FRAUD, MISTAKE, BREACH OF TRUST**
- Must plead "with particularity the circumstances constituting fraud or mistake"
- Who made the representation? What was said? When? Where? How was it false? How did plaintiff rely?
- Vague fraud = dismissed fraud. Period.

**(B)(2) CONDITIONS PRECEDENT**
- Must specifically allege that conditions precedent have been performed or occurred
- OR specifically allege which conditions precedent have NOT been performed

**(B)(3) OFFICIAL DOCUMENT OR ACT**
- Sufficient to allege that the document was issued or the act done in compliance with law

**(B)(4) JUDGMENT OR DECISION OF COURT**
- Must plead with sufficient particularity to identify the judgment

**(B)(5) TIME AND PLACE**
- When material, must be specifically alleged

### ANTI-RATIONALIZATION
- "General allegations are enough" → NOT for fraud. MCR 2.112(B)(1) is explicit.
- "We pled it in spirit" → The rule requires PARTICULARITY, not spirit.
- "Details will come out in discovery" → The complaint must survive a 12(b)(6) motion BEFORE discovery.

## 8. ADDITIONAL TRAPS

### CLAIM SPLITTING
- You cannot sue the same defendant on the same facts in multiple lawsuits
- All claims arising from the same transaction or occurrence must be brought in one action
- Failure to join = res judicata bars the omitted claims later

### COMPULSORY COUNTERCLAIMS
- If you have a claim against someone who sues you first, you MUST raise it as a counterclaim or lose it (MCR 2.203(A))

### INDISPENSABLE PARTIES
- MCR 2.205 — parties who must be joined if feasible
- Failure to join an indispensable party = potential dismissal

### DEMAND FOR JURY TRIAL
- MCR 2.508(B) — must demand in the first paper filed or risk waiver
- Include "PLAINTIFF DEMANDS TRIAL BY JURY" in the complaint

### VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
- If you title it "Verified Complaint," every factual allegation must be sworn
- Do not include allegations you cannot personally verify in a verified complaint
- Use "on information and belief" for facts you believe true but cannot personally verify
