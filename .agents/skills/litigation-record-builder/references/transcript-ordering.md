# Transcript Ordering — Court Reporter Logistics

Complete procedural guide for ordering, receiving, reviewing, and utilizing court transcripts in Pigors v Watson proceedings. Covers Muskegon County (14th Circuit), Court of Appeals, and federal court.

## I. Court Reporter System in Michigan

### How Court Reporting Works

Michigan circuit courts use a combination of court reporters and electronic recording systems:

- **Court Reporters (Stenographic)**: Live reporters who create stenographic records. Most common for substantive hearings (trials, evidentiary hearings, contested motions).
- **Electronic Recording (FTR/CourtSmart)**: Audio/video recording systems. Used for routine hearings, status conferences, some motion hearings.
- **Per Diem Reporters**: Freelance reporters hired for specific proceedings when staff reporters are unavailable.

## II. Ordering Process — Step by Step

### Step 1: Identify All Hearings Requiring Transcription

Create a comprehensive hearing list:

| Hearing Date | Type | Judge | Duration (Est.) | Priority | Reporter |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Date] | [Hearing type] | [Judge name] | [Hours] | [1-5] | [If known] |

**Priority System:**
- **Priority 1**: Hearings producing the order being appealed
- **Priority 2**: Evidentiary hearings where testimony was taken
- **Priority 3**: Motion argument hearings on disputed legal issues
- **Priority 4**: Status/scheduling conferences with substantive discussions
- **Priority 5**: Administrative hearings (usually not needed)

### Step 2: Contact Court Reporter(s)

**Initial Contact Script:**
```
"My name is Andrew Pigors. I am the Plaintiff in Case No. [XXXX],
Pigors v Watson, before Judge [Name] in the 14th Judicial Circuit.
I need to order transcripts for appeal purposes. I would like to
order transcripts of the following hearing dates: [dates]. Could you
please provide me with a cost estimate and timeline?"
```

**Information to Provide:**
- Your full name and contact information
- Case number and case name
- Court and judge
- Specific hearing dates
- Whether you need full or partial transcripts
- Whether this is for an appeal (triggers MCR timelines)

**Information to Request:**
- Per-page rate
- Estimated page count for each hearing
- Total cost estimate
- Deposit requirements
- Estimated delivery timeline
- Payment methods accepted

### Step 3: Negotiate Cost and Timeline

**Standard Rates (Michigan):**

| Service | Rate Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Original transcript (per page) | $3.25 – $4.50 | Standard rate; varies by reporter |
| Copy rate (per page) | $0.50 – $1.00 | Additional copies for parties |
| Expedited (14-day) | $5.00 – $7.50 per page | Significant premium |
| Super-expedited (7-day) | $7.50 – $10.00+ per page | Available from some reporters |
| Electronic copy (in addition to paper) | $0.25 – $0.50 per page | PDF or text file |
| Certification fee | Included or $10–$25 | Required for appeal |
| Rough draft/real-time | Not typically available after the fact | Only during proceeding |

**Cost Estimation Formula:**
```
Estimated Cost = (Hearing Duration in Hours) × (Pages per Hour) × (Per-Page Rate)
Typical Pages/Hour = 30–40 pages (for normal-speed proceedings)
```

**Example:**
- 2-hour evidentiary hearing × 35 pages/hour = 70 pages
- 70 pages × $3.75/page = $262.50

### Step 4: Fee Waiver Coverage for Transcripts

If you have an MC 20 fee waiver on file:

- **Circuit Court MC 20**: May cover transcript costs — verify with the clerk and the order
- **COA MC 20**: File separately at the COA level for appeal transcript costs
- **Federal IFP**: Under 28 USC § 1915, IFP status may cover transcript costs; file a separate motion for transcript at government expense

**If Fee Waiver Doesn't Cover Transcripts:**
- Request a payment plan from the court reporter
- File a motion with the trial court requesting transcript at public expense (particularly in cases involving constitutional rights)
- Explore whether the transcript cost can be waived as part of the appeal fee waiver
- Consider ordering partial transcripts to reduce cost (see Section III below)

### Step 6: Monitor Delivery

**Standard Delivery Timeline:**
- Normal: 56 days from order (or from payment of deposit)
- Expedited: 14 days (additional cost)
- Court reporters frequently run behind schedule — follow up at:
  - 30 days: Polite inquiry on progress
  - 45 days: Firmer follow-up with reference to MCR timeline
  - 56 days: If not received, consider filing Motion for Transcript Production Order

### Step 7: Transcript Production Order

If the court reporter fails to deliver within the required timeframe:

**File in trial court** (not COA):
- Motion for Order Compelling Transcript Production
- Explain: transcript ordered on [date], payment made, deadline passed
- Request: order reporter to produce within [14 days]
- Authority: MCR 7.210(B)(1); court's inherent supervisory authority

## III. Partial Transcripts

### MCR 7.210(B)(1)(b) — Partial Transcript Procedure

If you do not need the entire proceedings transcribed:

1. **In the claim of appeal or transcript order**, describe which portions you're ordering
2. **State why the remainder is unnecessary** — e.g., "The proceedings on [dates] addressed only discovery scheduling and are not relevant to the issues on appeal."
3. **Serve notice on appellee** — appellee may order additional portions within 21 days
4. **Risk**: Court may presume omitted portions support the lower court's ruling

### When Partial Transcripts Are Appropriate

| Appropriate | Inappropriate |
|---|---|
| Routine status conferences unrelated to merits | Any hearing where the judge made factual findings |
| Discovery scheduling hearings | Evidentiary hearings (even partial testimony) |
| Adjournment discussions | Hearings where the judge stated reasoning for order |
| Administrative matters | Settlement conferences that affected outcome |

## IV. Reviewing Transcripts for Accuracy

### MCR 7.210(B)(2) — Correction of Transcript

After receiving transcripts, review within 21 days for:

1. **Incorrect words**: Reporter misheard or mistranscribed testimony
2. **Missing exchanges**: Off-the-record discussions that were actually on the record
3. **Wrong attributions**: Testimony attributed to wrong speaker
4. **Inaudible notations**: Where reporter wrote "[inaudible]" but you recall the words
5. **Spelling errors**: Names of people, places, and technical terms

### Correction Process

1. Identify specific errors by page and line number
2. File motion in trial court to correct transcript
3. Attach proposed corrections with:
   - Page number and line number
   - Current (incorrect) text
   - Proposed corrected text
   - Basis for correction (other record evidence, notes, etc.)
4. Serve on court reporter and opposing party
5. Court reporter responds; trial judge resolves disputes
6. Corrected transcript filed with COA

## V. Settled Statements — Alternative to Transcripts

### MCR 7.210(B)(3) — When Transcript Is Unavailable

When a transcript cannot be obtained (reporter unavailable, recording destroyed, no reporter present):

**Process:**
1. Appellant prepares a proposed statement of proceedings
2. Statement describes what occurred at the hearing in narrative form
3. Serve on appellee — appellee has 21 days to file objections or proposed amendments
4. Submit to trial court for settlement (approval)
5. Trial court may hold a hearing, modify the statement, or approve as submitted
6. Settled statement becomes part of the record

**Draft settled statements conservatively.** Include facts favorable to both sides. Overreach invites challenge and modification by the trial court.

### MCR 7.210(B)(4) — Agreed Statement

If the parties can agree on the relevant facts:
1. Parties jointly prepare a statement of facts
2. Both parties sign the agreed statement
3. File with the trial court and COA
4. Agreed statement substitutes for transcript

**This is rare in contested cases** but worth proposing if it could save transcript costs on uncontested hearings.

## VI. Transcript Reference Tools

### Transcript Index Template

After receiving transcripts, create an index for brief-writing:

| Page:Line | Speaker | Topic | Key Quote/Summary | Brief Section |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 15:3-12 | Watson | Parenting time | "I didn't think it was necessary to..." | Arg. I.A |
| 23:18-24:5 | Pigors | Interference | Description of March 3 incident | Facts ¶12 |
| 45:7-15 | Court | Ruling | "The court finds..." | Standard/Review |

### Transcript Citation Format

In briefs, cite transcripts as:
```
(Tr. [Date], [Page]:[Line].)
or
([Hearing Type] Hr'g Tr., [Page]:[Line]-[Line], [Date].)
```

**Examples:**
- `(Evidentiary Hr'g Tr., 45:7-15, Mar. 15, 2025.)`
- `(Mot. Hr'g Tr., 12:3-8, Apr. 22, 2025.)`
- `(Trial Tr., Vol. II, 156:22-157:14.)`
