# Record Designation — MCR 7.210 Requirements

Complete reference for designating the record on appeal in Michigan Court of Appeals proceedings. Covers automatic record contents, designation procedures, timing, and common issues for Pigors v Watson.

## I. What Constitutes the Record on Appeal

### MCR 7.210(A) — Definition

The record on appeal consists of the original papers filed in the trial court, the transcript of proceedings (or an approved substitute), and the exhibits introduced in evidence or considered by the court or tribunal.

### Automatic Record Contents

The following items are automatically part of the record without separate designation:

| Item | Description | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Register of actions | Complete docket of all entries | Trial court clerk |
| Filed pleadings | All complaints, answers, motions, responses | Trial court file |
| Court orders | All orders entered by the trial court | Trial court file |
| Judgments | Final judgment and any interlocutory judgments | Trial court file |
| Filed exhibits | All exhibits admitted or offered into evidence | Trial court file |
| Jury instructions | Given and refused (if jury trial) | Trial court file |
| Written opinions | Any opinion of the trial court | Trial court file |
| Filed stipulations | All stipulations entered in the record | Trial court file |

### Items Requiring Designation

Some items are NOT automatically part of the record and must be affirmatively designated:

| Item | How to Include | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Unfiled documents referenced at hearing | Motion to supplement under MCR 7.210(C) | MCR 7.210(C)(1) |
| Sealed documents | Motion to unseal or motion to include under seal | Court order |
| Documents from companion cases | Separate designation or motion to consolidate records | MCR 7.210(A)(3) |
| Demonstrative exhibits not formally offered | May not be includable; motion to supplement | MCR 7.210(C) |
| Electronic recordings not transcribed | Order transcription or seek settled statement | MCR 7.210(B) |
| Documents filed after notice of appeal | Supplement motion or clerk's certificate | MCR 7.210(C) |

## II. Timing Requirements

### Critical Deadlines

| Event | Deadline | Authority | Consequence of Missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| File Claim of Appeal | 21 days from entry of final order (right) | MCR 7.204(A)(1) | Jurisdictional — appeal barred |
| File Application for Leave | 21 days (by right) or 42 days (by leave) | MCR 7.205(A) | Jurisdictional — application barred |
| Order transcripts | Within 21 days of filing claim of appeal | MCR 7.210(B)(1)(a) | Show cause order; possible dismissal |
| File proof of transcript ordering | Same 21-day period | MCR 7.210(B)(1)(a) | Clerk may issue show cause |
| Appellant's brief | 56 days after claim of appeal filed (or transcript filing, whichever is later) | MCR 7.212(A)(1) | Extension required or brief stricken |
| Appellee's brief | 35 days after appellant's brief served | MCR 7.212(A)(2) | Extension required or right waived |
| Reply brief | 21 days after appellee's brief served | MCR 7.212(A)(4) | No extension usually needed; deadline firm |

### Timeline Flowchart

```
Day 0:   Final Order/Judgment entered
         └─ CLOCK STARTS
Day 1-21: File Claim of Appeal (MCR 7.204)
          ├─ Order transcripts from court reporter
          ├─ File proof of ordering with COA clerk
          └─ Pay filing fee or file MC 20 fee waiver at COA
Day 21-77: Court reporter prepares transcript (56-day standard)
           └─ If reporter is late, file motion for transcript production order
Day 77+:  Transcript filed → triggers brief deadline
          └─ 56 days from transcript filing for Appellant's brief
```

## III. Record Completeness Checklist

### Pre-Designation Audit

Before the record is transmitted to the COA, verify every item:

```
RECORD COMPLETENESS AUDIT
==========================

PLEADINGS
□  Original Complaint / Petition
□  Answer / Response
□  All Amended Pleadings
□  Counterclaims / Cross-claims (if any)
□  Third-party complaints (if any)

MOTIONS AND RESPONSES
□  All motions filed by all parties
□  All responses / oppositions
□  All reply briefs
□  All proposed orders (granted and denied)

ORDERS AND JUDGMENTS
□  All court orders (signed copies, not just minute entries)
□  Final judgment or order appealed from
□  Any interlocutory orders
□  Any orders from companion cases referenced in proceedings

TRANSCRIPTS
□  All hearing transcripts ordered
□  Proof of transcript ordering filed with COA
□  Transcripts received and reviewed for accuracy
□  Corrections filed under MCR 7.210(B)(2) if needed

EXHIBITS
□  All exhibits admitted into evidence
□  All exhibits offered but excluded (important for appeal)
□  All exhibits physically accounted for with clerk
□  Demonstrative exhibits (if included in record)

MISCELLANEOUS
□  Register of actions — complete and current
□  Written opinions of the trial court
□  Jury instructions (if applicable)
□  Referee/mediator reports (if applicable)
□  FOC (Friend of Court) reports and recommendations
□  Guardian ad Litem reports (if applicable)
□  Fee waiver orders (for transcript cost coverage)
```

## IV. Certified Copies

### When Certified Copies Are Required

- **Judgment or order appealed from**: Must be a certified copy in the appendix
- **Prior orders referenced in appeal**: Certified copies preferred
- **Orders from other courts**: Certified copies required (cannot self-certify)

### How to Obtain Certified Copies

1. Request from trial court clerk's office
2. Pay certification fee (typically $10–$20 per document, waivable under MC 20)
3. Clerk stamps "CERTIFIED COPY" with seal and signature
4. Allow 5–10 business days for processing

## V. The Record Appendix (MCR 7.212(C))

### Required Contents

The appellant's brief MUST include an appendix containing:

1. **The judgment, order, or decision appealed from** (certified copy)
2. **Any opinion of the trial court** (written or transcribed oral opinion)
3. **The register of actions** for the trial court case
4. **Any jury instructions** given or refused (if jury trial)
5. **Any relevant findings of fact and conclusions of law**
6. **Other portions of the record** specifically referenced in the brief

### Appendix Format

```
APPENDIX TO PLAINTIFF-APPELLANT'S BRIEF

Contents:

Appendix Page

A.    Order [Granting/Denying] [Description], dated [Date] ........ A-1
B.    Register of Actions .......................................... A-5
C.    Trial Court Opinion [or oral ruling transcript] ............. A-10
D.    [Relevant Order #2] ......................................... A-15
E.    [Key Exhibit Referenced in Brief] ........................... A-18
```

### Appendix Best Practices

- Number appendix pages separately (A-1, A-2, etc.) or continuously with the brief
- Tab each appendix item for easy reference
- Include only items directly referenced in the brief (don't duplicate the entire record)
- Ensure every document in the appendix is legible and complete
- Cross-reference appendix page numbers in the brief text

## VI. Common Record Designation Issues

### Issue 1: Missing Documents

**Problem**: Documents you know were filed are not in the register of actions.
**Solution**: Check with the clerk. If the document was filed but not entered, request correction. If the document was not filed, you cannot include it in the record. Consider whether a motion to supplement (MCR 7.210(C)) applies.

### Issue 2: Sealed Records

**Problem**: Relevant proceedings or documents are sealed.
**Solution**: File a motion to unseal for purposes of appeal, or file a motion to transmit sealed records to the COA under seal. The COA can review sealed materials in camera.

### Issue 3: FOC/Referee Reports

**Problem**: Friend of Court reports or referee recommendations may not be automatically in the record.
**Solution**: Verify with the clerk that all FOC reports are in the file. If relevant to the appeal, designate them specifically. These are often critical in custody appeals.

### Issue 4: Electronic Records

**Problem**: Some documents exist only electronically in MiFile and may not have physical counterparts.
**Solution**: MiFile documents are part of the record. Ensure the COA clerk can access the MiFile record or request printed copies of critical electronic filings for the appendix.

### Issue 5: Multi-Judge Cases

**Problem**: Different hearings were before different judges (reassignment, visiting judges).
**Solution**: Transcripts must be ordered from each court reporter who covered each hearing. Verify which reporter covered which hearing by checking with the clerk or register of actions.

## VII. Preserving Issues for Appeal

The record must contain the foundation for every issue raised on appeal:

| To Raise This Issue | The Record Must Contain |
|---|---|
| Abuse of discretion in custody | Transcript of hearing + evidence presented + order |
| Legal error in applying statute | Briefing on the legal issue + court's ruling + transcript of argument |
| Evidentiary error | Objection on the record + offer of proof (if excluded) + ruling |
| Due process violation | Request for hearing/notice + court's response + evidence of impact |
| Factual finding error | Transcript showing evidence + finding + demonstration that finding is against great weight of evidence |

**Critical Rule**: An issue not preserved at the trial court level generally cannot be raised on appeal. The record must show you raised the issue, the court ruled on it, and you objected (if the ruling was adverse).
