# Sanctions Calculation — MCR 2.403(O) Reference

## Sanctions Framework

### Statutory Basis
MCR 2.403(O) provides that when a party rejects case evaluation and fails to improve their position at trial by at least 10%, they must pay the opposing party's "actual costs" incurred from the date of rejection.

### What Constitutes "Actual Costs"

Per MCR 2.403(O)(6), costs include:
1. **Reasonable attorney fees** — hours × reasonable rate
2. **Expert witness fees** — testimony and preparation
3. **Actual costs** — filing fees, deposition costs, copies, service fees

### What Is Excluded
- Costs incurred BEFORE the rejection date
- Costs not reasonably related to the litigation
- Excessive or duplicative billing
- Costs the party would have incurred regardless of evaluation

## Lodestar Calculation Method

### Step 1: Determine Reasonable Hours

Per *Smith v Khouri*, 481 Mich 519, 531 (2008):
- Hours must be documented with contemporaneous time records
- Duplicative hours are excluded
- Excessive research time is reduced
- Block billing may be reduced by 10-30%
- Travel time may be reduced to 50%

### Step 2: Determine Reasonable Rate

Per *Smith v Khouri*, factors include:
1. Professional standing and experience
2. Skill, time, and labor involved
3. Amount in question and results obtained
4. Difficulty of the case
5. Expenses incurred
6. Nature and length of professional relationship
7. Likelihood of preclusion from other employment
8. Fee customarily charged in the locality

### Step 3: Calculate Lodestar

```
Lodestar = Reasonable Hours × Reasonable Rate

Example:
Attorney hours post-rejection:     120 hours
Reasonable hourly rate:             $300/hour
Base lodestar:                      $36,000

Adjustments:
- Block billing reduction (15%):    -$5,400
- Excessive research reduction:     -$2,100
- Travel time reduction:            -$900
Adjusted lodestar:                  $27,600

Plus expert fees:                   $5,000
Plus actual costs:                  $2,400
TOTAL SANCTIONS:                    $35,000
```

## The 10% Threshold Calculation

### For Plaintiff Who Rejects

```
Case Evaluation Award:           $50,000
10% Threshold (award × 1.10):   $55,000
Trial Verdict:                   $52,000

$52,000 < $55,000 → Plaintiff FAILED to improve by 10%
→ Plaintiff pays defendant's costs from rejection to trial
```

### For Defendant Who Rejects

```
Case Evaluation Award:           $50,000
10% Threshold (award × 0.90):   $45,000
Trial Verdict:                   $48,000

$48,000 > $45,000 → Defendant FAILED to improve by 10%
→ Defendant pays plaintiff's costs from rejection to trial
```

### Special Cases

#### Both Parties Reject
If both parties reject and both fail to improve by 10%:
- Each party pays the other's costs from rejection
- Net result may be a wash, or significant disparity

#### Non-Monetary Awards (Family Law)
In custody cases where the award is non-monetary:
- "More favorable" is determined by the court
- Subjective assessment of custody/parenting time outcome vs. evaluation
- Courts have discretion in determining the 10% equivalent
- *Loutts v Loutts*, 298 Mich App 21 (2012) — family law sanctions analysis

## Practical Sanctions Scenarios

### Scenario A: Custody Case (Lane A)
```
Evaluation Award: Joint legal custody, primary physical to Mother
                  Standard parenting time to Father
                  Child support per formula

Trial Result:     Same custody arrangement, minor parenting time increase

Analysis: Father rejected evaluation but trial result is only marginally
better. Court likely finds less than 10% improvement.
Sanctions exposure: Mother's attorney fees from rejection (~$15,000-$30,000)
```

### Scenario B: Housing Case (Lane B)
```
Evaluation Award: $25,000 damages for habitability violations

Plaintiff rejects seeking $50,000

Trial Result: $30,000 verdict

Analysis: $30,000 > $27,500 (10% threshold) → Plaintiff IS safe
No sanctions because trial result exceeded evaluation by more than 10%
```

### Scenario C: Combined Claims
```
Evaluation Award: $75,000 total (custody + housing + damages)

Plaintiff rejects

Trial Result: $70,000 total

Analysis: $70,000 < $82,500 (10% threshold) → Plaintiff FAILED
Sanctions: Defendant's attorney fees from rejection date (~$25,000-$50,000)
```

## Pro Se Sanctions Considerations

### *Kay v Ehrler* Impact
- Pro se litigants generally cannot recover attorney fees (no attorney)
- But pro se litigants CAN be ordered to PAY opposing counsel's fees
- This creates asymmetric sanctions exposure
- Pro se party bears full risk of MCR 2.403(O) sanctions with no comparable recovery

### Practical Implications for Andrew James Pigors
1. **Carefully evaluate any case evaluation award** — sanctions are asymmetric
2. **Calculate worst-case sanctions** before rejecting
3. **Document trial improvement potential** with evidence strength analysis
4. **Consider partial acceptance** if available under local rules
5. **Budget for potential sanctions** in case strategy

## Sanctions Defense Strategies

If sanctions are sought against you:

1. **Challenge reasonableness of hours** — request detailed billing records
2. **Challenge hourly rate** — compare to local market rates
3. **Challenge necessity of work** — duplicative or excessive
4. **Argue good faith rejection** — basis for rejection was reasonable
5. **Argue changed circumstances** — new evidence or law post-rejection
6. **Request hearing** — sanctions should not be entered without opportunity to contest
7. **Document pre-rejection costs** — only post-rejection costs are recoverable
