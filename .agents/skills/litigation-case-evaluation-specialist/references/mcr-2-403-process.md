# MCR 2.403 Case Evaluation Process — Michigan Reference

## Overview

Case evaluation (formerly mediation) is a form of ADR under MCR 2.403 where a panel of three evaluators reviews the case and issues a monetary or non-monetary award. The critical feature is the sanctions mechanism under MCR 2.403(O) that penalizes parties who reject the evaluation and fail to improve their position at trial.

## Process Timeline

| Step | Rule | Timeline | Description |
|------|------|----------|-------------|
| Court orders evaluation | MCR 2.403(A) | Court discretion | May be on motion or sua sponte |
| Evaluator selection | MCR 2.403(C) | Per local rule | ADR clerk assigns from approved list |
| Summary submission | MCR 2.403(H) | Per scheduling order | Written summary to evaluators |
| Evaluation hearing | MCR 2.403(H) | Scheduled date | Each side presents (usually 15-30 min) |
| Award issued | MCR 2.403(J) | Day of hearing | Panel issues written award |
| Accept/reject deadline | MCR 2.403(K) | 28 days from service | Written acceptance or rejection filed |
| Deemed rejection | MCR 2.403(K)(2) | Day 29 if no response | Silence = rejection |
| Trial | — | After rejection | Case proceeds to trial |
| Sanctions determination | MCR 2.403(O) | Post-trial | If rejecting party fails 10% threshold |

## MCR 2.403 Detailed Provisions

### MCR 2.403(A) — Scope
- Applies to civil actions in circuit court
- Court may order evaluation on its own initiative or on motion
- Parties may stipulate to case evaluation
- Not available in certain actions (criminal, small claims)

### MCR 2.403(C) — Evaluator Selection
- Panel of three evaluators
- Selected from ADR clerk's list of approved evaluators
- Parties may stipulate to specific evaluators
- Objections for cause within 14 days of appointment
- Grounds for objection: conflict of interest, bias, lack of expertise

### MCR 2.403(D) — Evaluator Qualifications
- Licensed attorneys with specified years of experience
- Experience in relevant area of law
- Completed ADR training per court rule
- No conflict of interest with parties or counsel

### MCR 2.403(H) — Hearing Procedures
- Informal proceeding — rules of evidence do not strictly apply
- Each side presents a summary of claims, defenses, and evidence
- Evaluators may ask questions
- Typical format:
  - Plaintiff/Petitioner presentation (15-30 minutes)
  - Defendant/Respondent presentation (15-30 minutes)
  - Evaluator questions (10-15 minutes)
  - Deliberation (private — parties excused)

### MCR 2.403(J) — The Award
- Panel issues a monetary award (or non-monetary in custody/equitable cases)
- Award states the amount each party would recover
- Award is NOT a judgment — it's a benchmark
- Award is confidential and not admissible at trial
- Award may include allocation among multiple defendants

### MCR 2.403(K) — Accept/Reject
- **28 days** from service of award to accept or reject
- Acceptance must be in writing, filed with court, served on all parties
- Rejection must be in writing, filed with court, served on all parties
- If ALL parties accept: award becomes the judgment
- If ANY party rejects: case proceeds to trial
- **Failure to respond = deemed rejection**

### MCR 2.403(L) — Effect of Acceptance
- If all parties accept, award entered as judgment
- Same force and effect as any other judgment
- No further proceedings needed

### MCR 2.403(O) — Sanctions for Rejection

This is the critical provision:

**Triggering Conditions:**
1. A party rejects the case evaluation award
2. The case goes to trial
3. The rejecting party's verdict is NOT more favorable than the evaluation by at least 10%

**Sanctions Calculation:**
```
If Rejecting Party = Plaintiff:
  Trial verdict must exceed evaluation award by 10%
  If not: plaintiff pays defendant's costs from rejection date

If Rejecting Party = Defendant:
  Trial verdict must be at least 10% less than evaluation award
  If not: defendant pays plaintiff's costs from rejection date

Costs include:
  - Reasonable attorney fees (at actual rates)
  - Expert witness fees
  - Actual costs incurred after rejection date
  - NOT discovery costs incurred before rejection
```

**Key Case: *Haliw v City of Sterling Heights*, 471 Mich 700 (2005)**
- Established framework for calculating reasonable fees under MCR 2.403(O)
- Reasonableness determined by same factors as general fee petitions
- Court must evaluate whether hours were reasonable and rates were appropriate

## Strategic Considerations

### Accept/Reject Decision Matrix

| Scenario | Likely Action | Rationale |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Award is favorable | Accept | Lock in favorable result |
| Award is close to expected trial result | Accept | Avoid sanctions risk |
| Award is significantly unfavorable | Reject | Trial improvement likely exceeds 10% |
| Weak trial evidence | Accept | Can't beat the award |
| Strong trial evidence | Reject | Confident in 10%+ improvement |
| Opposing party likely to reject | Strategic accept | Forces sanctions exposure on opponent |

### Sanctions Exposure Calculator
```
Evaluation Award:        $___________
10% Threshold:           $_________ (award × 1.10 if plaintiff; award × 0.90 if defendant)
Estimated Trial Result:  $___________
Delta (trial - threshold): $___________
Estimated Opposing Fees: $___________

If Delta > 0: SAFE — beat the threshold
If Delta < 0 AND < opposing fees: REJECT IS RISKY
If Delta < 0 AND > opposing fees: ACCEPT IS BETTER
```
