# Question Techniques — Deposition Strategy Reference

## Question Categories and Strategic Purpose

### Category 1: Background Questions (10-15% of time)

**Purpose**: Establish witness identity, qualifications, and relationship to the case.

**Examples:**
```
- "Please state your full name for the record."
- "What is your current address?"
- "What is your relationship to [party name]?"
- "How long have you been in your current position?"
- "What is your educational background?"
```

**Strategic value**: Creates a baseline; inconsistencies with known facts are immediately useful.

### Category 2: Foundation Questions (20-25% of time)

**Purpose**: Establish what the witness knows, how they know it, and the basis for their testimony.

**Examples:**
```
- "Were you present on [date] at [location]?"
- "What did you personally observe?"
- "Who else was present?"
- "How do you know that? Did someone tell you, or did you see it yourself?"
- "How far away were you when this occurred?"
```

**Strategic value**: Separates personal knowledge from hearsay; establishes or undermines credibility.

### Category 3: Lock-In Questions (25-30% of time)

**Purpose**: Get the witness to commit to specific facts under oath that can be used at trial.

**Technique — The Funnel:**
1. **Open**: "Tell me about the incident on January 15th."
2. **Narrow**: "You said [X] happened. What time was that?"
3. **Lock**: "So your testimony is that at approximately 3:00 PM on January 15, 2025, you saw [specific fact]. Is that correct?"

**Examples for Pigors v Watson:**
```
Lane A (Custody):
- "On how many occasions in the last 6 months has [party] been
   late for the custody exchange?"
- "So your testimony is that you were late [X] times in 6 months?"

Lane B (Housing):
- "When did you first notice the [defect] in the property?"
- "Did you report it to the landlord? When?"
- "What was the landlord's response?"
```

### Category 4: Impeachment Questions (15-20% of time)

**Purpose**: Expose contradictions between deposition testimony and prior statements, documents, or other evidence.

**The MRE 613 Protocol:**
```
Step 1: Commit — "You testified that [X], correct?"
Step 2: Confront — "I'm showing you [Document Y]. Please read 
         paragraph [Z] aloud."
Step 3: Contrast — "So the document says [opposite of X]. 
         Can you explain the difference?"
```

**Key impeachment sources:**
| Source | MRE Authority | Method |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Prior sworn testimony | MRE 613, 801(d)(1) | Read prior statement |
| Signed documents | MRE 901 | Show authenticated document |
| Text messages/emails | MRE 901(b)(4) | Authenticate, then confront |
| Social media posts | MRE 901(b)(4) | Screenshot with metadata |
| Prior deposition | MRE 613 | Read from transcript |
| Police reports | MRE 803(8) | Public record |

### Category 5: Admission Questions (10-15% of time)

**Purpose**: Get the witness to admit facts that support your claims or undermine their defenses.

**Technique — Yes/No Progression:**
```
"You were responsible for maintaining the property, correct?"
    → "Yes."
"You received written notice of the defect on [date], correct?"
    → "Yes."
"You did not repair the defect within 30 days, correct?"
    → "That's correct."
"The defect was still present when [event] occurred?"
    → "Yes."
```

Each "yes" locks in an element of the claim. String enough admissions together and you have a case built from the opposing party's own testimony.

## Topic-Specific Question Banks

### Custody Depositions (Lane A)

**Best Interest Factor (j) — Facilitation:**
```
1. How many times in the past year has the other parent
   requested a schedule change?
2. How many times did you agree to the change?
3. How many times did you deny the request? What were
   your reasons?
4. Do you encourage L.D.W. to have a positive relationship
   with the other parent?
5. Have you ever made negative comments about the other
   parent in L.D.W.'s presence?
```

**Change of Circumstances (MCL 722.27):**
```
1. What has changed since the current custody order was entered?
2. When did this change occur?
3. How has this change affected L.D.W.?
4. What evidence supports your position on the change?
5. Have you discussed this change with any professionals
   (therapist, teacher, doctor)?
```

### Housing Depositions (Lane B)

**Habitability (MCL 554.139):**
```
1. When did you become aware of the [specific defect]?
2. How did you learn about it?
3. Did the tenant report it to you? When?
4. What did you do in response to the report?
5. When was the repair completed?
6. During the period the defect existed, was the property
   safe for habitation?
```

## Time Management Template

```
DEPOSITION TIME ALLOCATION (7-hour limit)

Background/Foundation:     1 hour    (14%)
Topic 1 — [Primary issue]: 2 hours   (29%)
Topic 2 — [Secondary]:     1.5 hours (21%)
Topic 3 — [Tertiary]:      1 hour    (14%)
Impeachment material:       1 hour    (14%)
Clean-up/follow-up:        0.5 hours (7%)
                          ──────────
TOTAL:                     7 hours   (100%)

RULE: If running behind schedule, skip Topic 3 — 
      never skip impeachment.
```

## Post-Deposition Checklist

- [ ] Order transcript (expedited if trial is approaching)
- [ ] Review transcript for errata within 30 days (MCR 2.306(F))
- [ ] Extract key admissions and impeachment material
- [ ] Update evidence analysis with new information
- [ ] Identify follow-up discovery needs
- [ ] Brief the case strategy team on findings
- [ ] Index testimony by topic for trial preparation
