# Damage Categories — Michigan Litigation Reference

## Economic Damages

### Definition
Quantifiable monetary losses that can be calculated with reasonable certainty from documentary evidence.

### Categories by Lane

#### Lane A: Custody (2024-001507-DC)
| Category | Examples | Evidence |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Attorney fees (if represented) | Counsel fees for custody proceedings | Billing records |
| Filing costs | Motion fees, transcript costs | Receipts |
| Transportation | Extra travel for parenting time compliance | Mileage log |
| Child-related expenses | Unreimbursed medical, child care | Bills, receipts |
| Lost wages | Time off work for court appearances | Pay records |
| Communication costs | Required parenting apps, phone plans | Invoices |

#### Lane B: Housing (2025-002760-CZ)
| Category | Examples | Evidence |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Repair costs | Fixing habitability defects (MCL 554.139) | Contractor invoices |
| Alternative housing | Temporary housing during uninhabitable period | Lease/hotel receipts |
| Property damage | Personal property damaged by housing defects | Replacement costs |
| Moving expenses | Costs to relocate due to conditions | Moving company invoice |
| Medical expenses | Health costs from unsafe conditions (mold, lead) | Medical bills |
| Rent overpayment | Rent paid for substandard premises | Rent receipts + defect evidence |

#### Lane D: PPO (2023-5907-PP)
| Category | Examples | Evidence |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Security costs | Locks, cameras, security systems | Receipts |
| Relocation costs | Moving to escape violator | Moving expenses |
| Medical/therapy | Treatment for PPO-related trauma | Medical bills |
| Lost wages | Time off for court, police reports | Pay records |

#### Lane E: Judicial Misconduct / Federal (42 USC § 1983)
| Category | Examples | Evidence |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Constitutional deprivation | Monetary value of rights violated | Expert testimony |
| Costs of additional litigation | Appeals, motions caused by misconduct | Filing records |
| Lost custody time | Value of parent-child relationship time lost | Calendar, orders |

### Calculation Methodology for Economic Damages
```
Step 1: Identify each category of economic loss
Step 2: Compile documentary evidence for each
Step 3: Calculate total per category with dates
Step 4: Subtract any insurance recovery or other offsets
Step 5: Add prejudgment interest (MCL 600.6013)
Step 6: Total economic damages = sum of all categories
```

## Non-Economic Damages

### Definition
Non-quantifiable harms that require subjective valuation — pain, suffering, emotional distress, loss of companionship, dignitary harm.

### Categories
| Type | Description | Applicable Lanes |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Emotional distress | Anxiety, depression, fear caused by defendant's conduct | All |
| Pain and suffering | Physical and mental anguish | B, D |
| Loss of consortium | Impaired parent-child relationship | A, D |
| Humiliation/embarrassment | Public exposure or dignitary harm | E |
| Loss of enjoyment of life | Inability to enjoy normal activities | B, D |
| Mental anguish | Severe psychological impact | A, D, E |

### Proving Non-Economic Damages
- Plaintiff testimony about impact
- Therapist/counselor testimony or records
- Before/after comparisons of functioning
- Witness testimony about observable changes
- Duration and severity of impact
- Whether professional treatment was sought

## Statutory Damages and Multipliers

### Michigan Consumer Protection Act (Lane B)
**MCL 445.911**: Private right of action for MCPA violations
- Actual damages OR $250 (whichever is greater)
- Court MAY award treble damages for knowing violations
- Reasonable attorney fees
- Class action provisions

### Civil Rights (Lane E)
**42 USC § 1983**: Compensatory + punitive damages
- No damage cap for § 1983 claims
- Compensatory: actual harm caused by constitutional violation
- Punitive: if defendant acted with evil motive or callous indifference
- **42 USC § 1988**: Prevailing party attorney fees

### Housing Habitability (Lane B)
**MCL 554.139**: Implied warranty of habitability
- Difference in value: rent paid vs. value of defective premises
- Consequential damages: property damage, medical costs, moving
- Potential MCPA treble damages if landlord's conduct was knowing

## Prejudgment Interest

### MCL 600.6013 — Statutory Framework
- Available in tort and contract actions
- Accrues from date of filing (tort) or date of loss (contract)
- Rate set annually by State Treasurer
- Must be REQUESTED in the complaint or prayer for relief

### Calculation
```
Prejudgment Interest = Principal × Annual Rate × (Days / 365)

Example:
  Damages:        $50,000
  Annual Rate:     3.278% (example — verify current rate)
  Days Pending:    730 (2 years)
  Interest:        $50,000 × 0.03278 × (730/365) = $3,278

  Total with Interest: $53,278
```

## Per-Defendant Allocation

### MCL 600.6304 — Joint and Several Liability

**Economic Damages**: Full joint and several liability applies
- Can collect 100% from any defendant

**Non-Economic Damages**: Modified joint and several
- Defendant ≥50% at fault: joint and several for non-economic
- Defendant <50% at fault: several liability only (proportional share)

### Allocation Template
```
DEFENDANT ALLOCATION MATRIX

                     Defendant A    Defendant B    Total
Economic Damages      $30,000        $30,000      $30,000 (J&S)
Non-Economic (>50%)   $20,000        —            $20,000 (J&S)
Non-Economic (<50%)   —              $8,000       $8,000 (several)
Statutory Multiplier  $60,000        —            $60,000
Prejudgment Interest  $3,000         $3,000       $3,000 (J&S)
TOTAL PER DEFENDANT   $113,000       $41,000      —
GRAND TOTAL                                       $121,000
```
