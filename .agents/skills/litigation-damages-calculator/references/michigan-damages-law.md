# Michigan Damages Law — Reference

## Statutory Framework

### MCL 600.6013 — Prejudgment Interest
- Applicable to complaints filed in circuit court
- Rate determined annually by State Treasurer (published January 1)
- Tort claims: interest accrues from date complaint filed
- Contract claims: interest accrues from date of loss
- Must be specifically requested in prayer for relief
- Compounds annually unless court orders otherwise

### MCL 600.6304 — Joint and Several Liability (Modified)
Michigan's modified joint and several liability statute:

**Economic Damages (MCL 600.6304(1)):**
- Joint and several liability applies to ALL defendants
- Plaintiff can collect full economic damages from any defendant
- Defendants can seek contribution from each other

**Non-Economic Damages (MCL 600.6304(2)):**
- Defendant found ≥50% at fault: joint and several liability
- Defendant found <50% at fault: several liability only
- Each defendant pays only their proportional share
- Reallocation permitted if a defendant is insolvent

### MCL 600.6304(4) — Allocation of Fault
- Trier of fact allocates fault percentages to each party
- Includes plaintiff's comparative negligence
- Includes non-parties if properly identified
- Fault must total 100% across all parties

### MCL 600.2911 — Defamation Damages
If applicable in any lane:
- Actual damages provable with evidence
- Presumed damages for defamation per se (limited categories)
- Punitive damages available for actual malice
- Single publication rule applies

## Family Law Damages Framework

### Attorney Fees in Custody Cases
**MCR 3.206(D)**: Court may order fee-shifting in family cases
- Must show inability to pay AND ability of other party to pay
- Not automatic — requires motion and showing
- Pro se limitation: *Kay v Ehrler*, 499 US 432 (1991) — generally no attorney fees for pro se

### MCL 722.28 — Appellate Review in Custody
- Not a damages statute, but controls the cost of appeal
- Great weight standard means high bar for reversal
- Losing appellate party may face cost sanctions

### MCL 552.13 — Costs in Divorce Actions
- Court has discretion to award costs to either party
- Includes filing fees, service costs, transcript costs
- Does NOT include attorney fees (separate analysis)

## Civil Rights Damages (42 USC § 1983)

### Compensatory Damages
Available for violation of constitutional rights:
- **Out-of-pocket losses**: Concrete economic harm
- **Emotional distress**: Anxiety, humiliation, mental anguish
- **Constitutional deprivation**: Intrinsic value of the right violated
- **Consequential damages**: Downstream effects of the violation
- No statutory cap on § 1983 compensatory damages

### Punitive Damages
Available when defendant acted with:
- Evil motive or intent to violate rights
- Reckless or callous indifference to constitutional rights
- Per *Smith v Wade*, 461 US 30 (1983)

**Not available against:**
- Municipal entities (§ 1983 municipal immunity from punitive damages)
- State actors in their official capacity

### Attorney Fees — 42 USC § 1988
- Prevailing party may recover reasonable attorney fees
- *Hensley v Eckerhart*, 461 US 424 (1983) — lodestar method
- Pro se exception: *Kay v Ehrler* — generally cannot recover "fees"
- BUT: can recover litigation costs (filing, service, copies)

## Housing Damages (Lane B)

### MCL 554.139 — Implied Warranty of Habitability
Damages for breach:
1. **Rent differential**: Value of habitable premises minus value received
2. **Consequential damages**: Property damage, medical costs, moving costs
3. **Incidental damages**: Inspection costs, temporary housing

### MCL 445.911 — Michigan Consumer Protection Act
If housing conditions involve unfair/deceptive practices:
- Actual damages OR $250 minimum
- Treble damages for knowing violations
- Reasonable attorney fees (available even to pro se for costs)
- Court costs

### MCL 125.534-540 — Housing Code Violations
Municipal code enforcement damages:
- Costs of code compliance
- Relocation assistance in some jurisdictions
- Potential criminal penalties for landlord

## Key Michigan Damages Cases

| Case | Holding | Application |
|------|---------|-------------|
| *Pirgu v United Servs Auto Ass'n*, 499 Mich 269 (2016) | Attorney fee calculation framework | Fee component of damages |
| *Smith v Khouri*, 481 Mich 519 (2008) | Lodestar method for reasonable fees | Fee petition methodology |
| *Hensley v Eckerhart*, 461 US 424 (1983) | Federal lodestar framework | § 1983 fee recovery |
| *Sparks v Sparks*, 440 Mich 141 (1992) | Equitable division factors | Property damages allocation |
| *Haliw v City of Sterling Heights*, 471 Mich 700 (2005) | MCR 2.403(O) sanctions calculation | Case evaluation damages |
