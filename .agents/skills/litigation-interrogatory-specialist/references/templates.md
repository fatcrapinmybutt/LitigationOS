# Templates — Interrogatory Specialist

## Standard Interrogatory Set — Custody Case

```
STATE OF MICHIGAN
IN THE 14TH CIRCUIT COURT FOR THE COUNTY OF MUSKEGON
FAMILY DIVISION

ANDREW JAMES PIGORS,
    Plaintiff,                          Case No. 2024-001507-DC
                                        Hon. Jenny L. McNeill
v.

EMILY A. WATSON,
    Defendant.
_________________________________/

PLAINTIFF'S FIRST SET OF INTERROGATORIES TO DEFENDANT

    Pursuant to MCR 2.309, Plaintiff propounds the following interrogatories.
Answers must be served within 28 days under oath.

DEFINITIONS:
    "You" / "Your" means Defendant Emily A. Watson.
    "Child" means the minor child(ren) of the parties.
    "Relevant Period" means [DATE] through the present.

INTERROGATORIES:

    1. State your current residential address and all persons residing there.
    2. State your current employer, position, rate of pay, and weekly hours.
    3. Identify all sources of income during the Relevant Period.
    4. Name the child's current school, daycare, and primary care physician.
    5. Identify each occasion you denied Plaintiff's parenting time.
    6. Identify all mental health professionals who treated you.
    7. State any arrests, charges, or convictions during the Relevant Period.
    8. Identify all witnesses you intend to call at trial.

    [Continue up to 20 total including subparts]

VERIFICATION
I declare under the penalties of perjury that the answers are true and
complete to the best of my knowledge, information, and belief.

____________________________        Date: _______________
Emily A. Watson
```

## Motion to Compel Interrogatory Answers

```
MOTION TO COMPEL ANSWERS TO INTERROGATORIES

    Plaintiff moves pursuant to MCR 2.313(A)(2) for an order compelling
Defendant to answer Plaintiff's First Set of Interrogatories:

    1. Interrogatories served on [DATE].
    2. 28-day response period expired on [DATE].
    3. Defendant has [not responded / provided incomplete responses].
    4. Plaintiff conferred with Defendant on [DATE] per MCR 2.313(A)(1).
    5. Deficiencies were not resolved.

SPECIFIC DEFICIENCIES:
    a. Interrogatory No. [X]: [Describe deficiency]
    b. Interrogatory No. [Y]: [Describe deficiency]

    Plaintiff requests complete answers within 14 days and expenses
under MCR 2.313(A)(4).
```

```
    RESPONSE: Should be overruled because:
    (a) Within scope of MCR 2.302(A);
    (b) Objection is boilerplate / non-specific / waived;

```

## Workflow: Interrogatory Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Draft interrogatories (max 20 including subparts)
    ↓
Step 2: Serve with proof of service
    ↓
Step 3: Calendar 28-day response deadline
    ↓
Step 4: Receive and review for completeness
    ↓
Step 5: Confer on deficiencies (MCR 2.313(A)(1))
    ↓
Step 6: File Motion to Compel if unresolved
    ↓
Step 7: Cross-reference answers against other evidence
    ↓
Step 8: Flag inconsistencies for impeachment
    ↓
Step 9: Monitor supplementation duty
    ↓
Step 10: Use verified answers as admissions at trial
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Exceeding 20 limit | Court strikes excess | Stay within 20; seek leave if needed |
| "Any and all" phrasing | Objectionable as overbroad | Specific, targeted questions |
| No definitions section | Ambiguity provides escape routes | Comprehensive definitions |
| Not calendaring deadlines | Waived objections | Calendar immediately upon service |
| Form-book copying | Misses case-specific issues | Customize to your facts |
| Ignoring supplementation | Stale answers miss new evidence | Track and update proactively |
