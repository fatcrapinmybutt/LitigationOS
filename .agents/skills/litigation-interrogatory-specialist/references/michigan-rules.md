# Michigan Rules — Interrogatory Specialist

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.309 — Interrogatories
- **(A)(1)**: Any party may serve written interrogatories on any other party
- **(A)(2)**: Interrogatories are limited to **20 (including subparts)** unless the court permits more for good cause
- **(B)(1)**: Interrogatories must be answered separately and fully under oath
- **(B)(2)**: Answers must be served within **28 days** after service of the interrogatories
- **(B)(3)**: The party, not the attorney, must sign the answers under oath
- **(B)(4)**: Objections must state the reason with specificity; boilerplate objections are waived
- **(B)(5)**: If business records provide the answer, the responding party may specify the records and make them available

### MCR 2.302 — General Rules Governing Discovery
- **(A)**: Scope — any non-privileged matter relevant to the subject matter
- **(B)(1)**: Not limited to admissible evidence if reasonably calculated to lead to admissible evidence
- **(C)**: Protective orders to limit discovery from annoyance or undue burden
- **(D)**: Sequence and timing — may be used in any sequence
- **(E)**: Supplementation — duty to supplement prior responses

### MCR 2.313 — Failure to Provide Discovery
- **(A)(2)**: Motion to compel answers
- **(A)(3)**: Evasive or incomplete answers = failure to answer
- **(A)(4)**: Reasonable expenses awarded to prevailing party
- **(B)(2)**: Sanctions: facts established, claims/defenses barred, pleadings struck, dismissal/default, contempt

### MCR 3.206 — Domestic Relations: Discovery
- Standard discovery rules apply to domestic relations
- Verified financial information statement required

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.2163a — Privilege Against Self-Incrimination
- Party may invoke Fifth Amendment in civil interrogatories
- Court may draw adverse inference in civil case

### MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors
- Interrogatories in custody cases target specific best interest factors
- Discovery scope includes information relevant to all 12 factors

### MCL 722.27a — Parenting Time
- Interrogatories may address parenting time compliance history
- Relevant to factor (j) — willingness to facilitate relationship

### MCL 552.6 — Financial Discovery in Domestic Relations
- Financial interrogatories authorized in all domestic relations cases
- Scope includes income, assets, debts, expenses

### MCL 600.1701-1741 — Contempt
- Failure to answer after court order may constitute contempt
- Civil contempt sanctions available to compel compliance

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Interrogatory Scope and Limits
- **Domako v Rowe**, 184 Mich App 137 (1990) — Discovery scope is broad; interrogatories need only be reasonably calculated to lead to admissible evidence
- **Hamed v Wayne County**, 271 Mich App 106 (2006) — Trial court has broad discretion in managing discovery disputes
- **Cabana v Hutchison**, 197 Mich App 595 (1992) — Objections must be specific; general objections are deemed waived

### Sanctions for Discovery Abuse
- **Dean v Tucker**, 182 Mich App 27 (1990) — Court may impose sanctions including dismissal for persistent failure to answer
- **Bass v Combs**, 238 Mich App 16 (1999) — Sanctions should be proportionate; lesser sanctions first
- **Traxler v Ford Motor Co**, 227 Mich App 276 (1998) — Willful failure to supplement may warrant severe sanctions

### Privilege in Discovery
- **Leibel v General Motors Corp**, 250 Mich App 229 (2002) — Privilege must be specifically asserted with privilege log
- **Reed Dairy Farm v Consumers Power Co**, 227 Mich App 614 (1998) — Work product doctrine applies to interrogatory responses

## Checklists

### Drafting Interrogatories Checklist
- [ ] Total count (including subparts) does not exceed 20
- [ ] Each interrogatory asks a single, discrete question
- [ ] Definitions section included for key terms
- [ ] Time period specified
- [ ] Verification block included for party signature under oath
- [ ] Proof of service prepared

### Answering Interrogatories Checklist
- [ ] Calendar 28-day deadline from date of service
- [ ] Answer each interrogatory separately and fully
- [ ] Assert specific objections (not boilerplate)
- [ ] Provide privilege log for any withheld information
- [ ] Party (not attorney) signs under oath
- [ ] Track answers for supplementation

### Filing Deadlines
- Response deadline: **28 days** from service (MCR 2.309(B)(2))
- Motion to compel: After response deadline expires
- Extension request: Before original deadline expires
- Supplementation: Ongoing duty (MCR 2.302(E))
