# Purge Conditions — Michigan Contempt Reference

## What Are Purge Conditions

Purge conditions are the specific, affirmative actions a contemnor must take to "purge" (remedy) their contempt and avoid sanctions. They are the hallmark of CIVIL contempt — the contemnor "carries the keys to their own cell."

### Legal Standard

Per *In re Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81, 99 (1987):
- Purge conditions must be achievable
- The contemnor must have present ability to comply
- Conditions that are impossible to satisfy = improper punishment disguised as civil contempt
- Court must find that the contemnor CAN do what is being required

### Requirements for Valid Purge Conditions

1. **Specific**: Exactly what must be done (not "obey the order")
2. **Measurable**: Court can objectively determine compliance
3. **Achievable**: Contemnor has present ability to satisfy
4. **Time-bound**: Clear deadline for compliance
5. **Related**: Connected to the underlying violation

## Purge Condition Categories

### Category 1: Custody/Parenting Time Compliance
For violations of custody or parenting time orders:

| Violation Type | Sample Purge Condition |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Missed parenting time | Provide makeup time on [specific dates] and comply with all scheduled parenting time for 90 days |
| Late dropoff/pickup | Arrive at exchange location within 15 minutes of scheduled time for 60 days |
| Denial of communication | Allow daily phone/video contact at [time] for 60 days |
| Failure to return child | Immediately return L.D.W. to [parent] at [location] |
| Interference with school | Notify school of [parent]'s rights; provide school access within 7 days |

### Category 2: Support Payment
For violations of support orders:

| Violation Type | Sample Purge Condition |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Non-payment of support | Pay current obligation plus $[amount] toward arrears within 14 days |
| Irregular payments | Enroll in income withholding within 7 days |
| Hidden income | Provide complete financial disclosure within 14 days |

**Important**: Purge conditions for support must account for ability to pay.
Per *Harvey v Harvey*, 470 Mich 186, 194 (2004): "Incarceration for civil contempt
is improper when the contemnor does not have the present ability to pay."

### Category 3: Document/Information Production
For violations of discovery or disclosure orders:

| Violation Type | Sample Purge Condition |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Failure to produce documents | Produce all documents identified in [order] within 14 days |
| Incomplete financial disclosure | Provide complete sworn financial statement within 14 days |
| Failure to authorize releases | Sign and deliver all required releases within 7 days |

### Category 4: Behavioral Compliance
For violations of no-contact or behavioral orders:

| Violation Type | Sample Purge Condition |
|---------------|----------------------|
| PPO violation | Strictly comply with all PPO terms for 120 days |
| Disparagement | Refrain from disparaging [parent] in L.D.W.'s presence for 90 days |
| Third-party interference | Ensure [third party] has no contact with L.D.W. for 60 days |

## Drafting Effective Purge Conditions

### Template
```
PROPOSED PURGE CONDITIONS

The Court should order that Respondent may purge contempt by:

1. [SPECIFIC ACTION]:
   Within [timeframe] of this Order, Respondent shall [exact action].
   Compliance shall be verified by [method].

2. [ONGOING COMPLIANCE]:
   For a period of [duration] following this Order, Respondent shall
   [ongoing obligation]. Any violation during this period shall
   constitute a separate finding of contempt without further hearing.

3. [COMPENSATORY PAYMENT]:
   Within [timeframe], Respondent shall pay Petitioner $[amount]
   representing [attorney fees / costs / damages] incurred as a
   direct result of Respondent's contempt.

SANCTIONS IF PURGE NOT SATISFIED:

If Respondent fails to satisfy the above purge conditions:
- [Amount] fine per day of non-compliance, OR
- [Number] days imprisonment (stayed pending purge), OR
- Other sanctions as the Court deems appropriate
```

### Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Make conditions specific to the violation
- Include a compliance verification mechanism
- Set realistic timeframes
- Calculate compensatory damages with documentation
- Propose stepped sanctions (warning → fine → jail)

**DON'T:**
- Propose conditions the contemnor cannot meet
- Use vague language ("obey the order" "behave appropriately")
- Set impossibly short deadlines
- Request incarceration without showing ability to comply
- Propose conditions unrelated to the violation

## Defending Against Purge Conditions

If purge conditions are proposed against Andrew James Pigors:

### Step 1: Challenge Achievability
- Document any inability to comply (financial, logistical, health)
- Present evidence of good-faith efforts to comply
- Show that conditions are more punitive than remedial

### Step 2: Propose Alternatives
- Offer alternative purge conditions you CAN meet
- Demonstrate willingness to comply
- Propose graduated compliance timeline

### Step 3: Protect Constitutional Rights
- Object to incarceration without ability finding per *Harvey*
- Request appointed counsel if facing jail
- Assert right to hearing on ability to comply

## Michigan Case Law on Purge Conditions

| Case | Holding | Relevance |
|------|---------|-----------|
| *Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81 (1987) | Four-element test for civil contempt | Foundational |
| *Sword v Sword*, 399 Mich 367 (1976) | Civil vs. criminal contempt distinction | Procedural |
| *Harvey v Harvey*, 470 Mich 186 (2004) | Cannot jail for inability to pay | Support contempt |
| *Arbour v Arbour*, 477 Mich 894 (2006) | Purge must be achievable | Standard |
| *Brandt v Brandt*, 250 Mich App 68 (2002) | Compensatory contempt sanctions | Damages |
| *Peters v Peters*, 307 Mich App 407 (2014) | Parenting time contempt | Lane A relevant |

## Documentation for Contempt Proceedings

### Evidence Preservation Protocol
For each violation, contemporaneously document:
1. **Date and time** of violation
2. **Specific order provision** violated
3. **What happened** (factual description)
4. **What should have happened** (order requirement)
5. **Evidence** (text messages, photos, witnesses, calendar entries)
6. **Impact** on L.D.W. and the non-violating parent
7. **Communication** — any attempts to resolve before filing
