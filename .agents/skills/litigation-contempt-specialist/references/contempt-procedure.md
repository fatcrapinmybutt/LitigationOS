# Contempt Procedure — Michigan Reference

## Statutory Framework

### MCL 600.1701 — Civil Contempt
Civil contempt is remedial in nature — designed to coerce compliance with a court order.

**Elements (all must be proven by preponderance):**
1. Valid court order existed
2. Respondent had knowledge of the order
3. Respondent had ability to comply
4. Respondent willfully failed to comply

**Key Cases:**
- *In re Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81, 96 (1987) — four-element test
- *Sword v Sword*, 399 Mich 367 (1976) — civil vs. criminal distinction
- *Harvey v Harvey*, 470 Mich 186 (2004) — ability to comply analysis

### MCL 600.1715 — Criminal Contempt
Criminal contempt is punitive — designed to punish past disobedience.

**Procedural Requirements (heightened):**
- Proof beyond a reasonable doubt
- Right to jury trial if potential sentence exceeds 6 months
- Right to appointed counsel if indigent
- Constitutional protections apply (5th, 6th Amendment)
- Must be initiated by the court or prosecutor

### MCR 3.606 — Show Cause Procedure (Family Law)

#### Filing the Motion
```
Required contents of show cause motion:
1. Case caption with case number
2. Citation to specific order provision(s) violated
3. Specific dates and descriptions of each violation
4. Evidence supporting each violation
5. Statement that respondent had ability to comply
6. Request for specific relief (purge conditions, sanctions)
7. Proposed order to show cause
8. Certified copy of violated order (attached as exhibit)
```

#### Service Requirements
- Personal service on respondent required
- MCR 2.105 service methods apply
- Include order to show cause and supporting motion
- Minimum 9 days before hearing per MCR 2.119(C)(1)

#### The Show Cause Hearing
| Phase | Description | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Petitioner's case | Present evidence of violation | Must prove all 4 elements |
| Respondent's response | Present defenses | Inability, ambiguity, excuse |
| Rebuttal | Petitioner addresses defenses | Anticipate and prepare |
| Court ruling | Finding of contempt or not | May be from bench |
| Purge conditions | If contempt found | Must be achievable |

### Burden-Shifting Framework

1. **Petitioner**: Proves valid order, knowledge, violation
2. **Burden shifts to respondent**: To show inability to comply
3. **Petitioner**: Can rebut inability defense with evidence of ability
4. **Court decides**: Based on totality of evidence

## Types of Relief Available

### In Civil Contempt
| Relief | Authority | Description |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Jail (conditional) | MCL 600.1701 | Stayed pending purge compliance |
| Fine | MCL 600.1701 | Compensatory or coercive |
| Attorney fees | MCR 3.206(D) | Reasonable fees caused by contempt |
| Makeup parenting time | MCL 722.27a | Compensate missed visits |
| Modified order | MCR 3.606 | Adjust provisions to prevent future violations |
| Supervised compliance | Court discretion | Third-party monitoring |

### In Criminal Contempt
| Relief | Authority | Description |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Jail (definite term) | MCL 600.1715 | Not stayed — punitive |
| Fine | MCL 600.1721 | Up to $7,500 |
| Probation | Court discretion | Conditions set by court |

## Motion Template Structure

### Part 1: Background
- Case history and relevant orders
- Relationship of parties to L.D.W.
- Relevant custody/support provisions

### Part 2: Violations
```
VIOLATION 1:
  Order Provision: Paragraph 3(a) of Custody Order dated [date]
  Requirement: [Specific obligation from order]
  Date of Violation: [Specific date]
  Description: [What happened/didn't happen]
  Evidence: [Document, photograph, witness, text message]

VIOLATION 2:
  [Same format]

VIOLATION 3:
  [Same format]
```

### Part 3: Ability to Comply
- Evidence that respondent had ability
- Anticipate and address inability defenses
- Financial records, health records, schedule

### Part 4: Requested Relief
- Specific purge conditions proposed
- Attorney fees and costs
- Makeup parenting time dates
- Any order modifications needed

### Part 5: Supporting Documents
- [ ] Certified copy of violated order
- [ ] Evidence for each violation (exhibits)
- [ ] Financial ability documentation
- [ ] Proposed order to show cause
- [ ] Proof of service

## Hearing Preparation Checklist

- [ ] All exhibits organized and copies prepared
- [ ] Testimony outline for each violation
- [ ] Ability-to-comply evidence ready
- [ ] Responses to anticipated defenses prepared
- [ ] Purge conditions drafted (specific, measurable)
- [ ] Calculation of compensatory damages
- [ ] Proposed order prepared for court signature
