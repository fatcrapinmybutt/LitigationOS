# PPO Statutes — MCL 600.2950 and MCL 600.2950a Complete Reference

> Full statutory framework for Personal Protection Orders in Michigan,
> including elements, procedure, prohibited conduct, and forms.

---

## I. MCL 600.2950 — Domestic Personal Protection Order

### Eligibility — Who Can Petition
A person may petition for a domestic PPO if respondent is:
1. **Spouse or former spouse**
2. **Individual with whom petitioner has a child in common**
3. **Individual who resides or has resided in same household as petitioner**
4. **Individual in or who has been in a dating relationship with petitioner**

**Pigors v Watson:** Amy Watson qualifies as petitioner under subsection (2)—individual with whom respondent has a child in common.

### Prohibited Conduct — MCL 600.2950(1)
The court may order respondent to refrain from:

| Prohibited Act | Subsection | Notes |
|---------------|-----------|-------|
| Entering onto premises | (a) | Petitioner's residence, property |
| Assaulting, attacking, beating, molesting, wounding | (b) | Physical violence |
| Threatening to kill or physically injure | (c) | Verbal/written threats |
| Removing minor children | (d) | **Except as authorized by custody order** |
| Purchasing or possessing firearms | (e) | Triggers federal 18 USC § 922(g)(8) |
| Interfering with at place of employment/education | (f) | Workplace/school protection |
| Engaging in specifically prohibited conduct | (g) | Catch-all for court-specified restrictions |
| Accessing info revealing petitioner's location in minor child records | (h) | Address confidentiality |
| Any other specific act or conduct | (i) | Broad discretion |

### Ex Parte Issuance — MCL 600.2950(9)
Court may issue PPO without notice to respondent if:
- Petition alleges facts showing **immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage**
- **No security/bond required** from petitioner
- PPO effective immediately upon signing by judge
- Must be served on respondent promptly

### Service — MCL 600.2950(16)
- PPO must be served on respondent by:
  - Personal service (preferred)
  - First-class mail to last known address
  - Any other method deemed appropriate by court
- PPO is enforceable upon service (or actual knowledge)

### Duration — MCL 600.2950(11)
- Domestic PPO has **NO automatic expiration date**
- Effective until **modified or terminated by court order**
- Respondent may file motion to modify/terminate at any time

### Modification/Termination — MCL 600.2950(12)
- Either party may file motion to modify or terminate
- Court must hold hearing within **14 days** of motion
- At hearing: **Petitioner bears burden** of showing PPO should continue
- Court considers totality of circumstances including:
  - Original basis for PPO
  - Whether circumstances have changed
  - Whether PPO remains necessary for protection
  - Impact on parties and children

### Violation — MCL 600.2950(23)
- Violation is a **misdemeanor**
- Punishable by imprisonment up to **93 days**, fine up to **$500**, or both
- Law enforcement may arrest WITHOUT warrant upon probable cause
- Violation may ALSO be prosecuted as contempt of court
- Subsequent violations: Enhanced penalties

### Firearm Provisions — MCL 600.2950(1)(e)
- Court SHALL prohibit purchase/possession of firearms if PPO includes assault/threat provisions
- Court MAY prohibit firearms in other circumstances
- **Federal overlay:** 18 USC § 922(g)(8) — possession of firearm while subject to qualifying domestic PPO is a **federal felony** (up to 10 years)
- **United States v. Rahimi, 602 U.S. ___ (2024):** Upheld constitutionality of § 922(g)(8) under Second Amendment

## II. MCL 600.2950a — Non-Domestic (Stalking) PPO

### Eligibility
Any person who is a victim of stalking behavior, where the respondent is NOT in a domestic relationship with petitioner.

### Stalking Defined — MCL 750.411h
"Willful course of conduct involving repeated or continuing harassment of another individual that would cause a reasonable person to feel terrorized, frightened, intimidated, threatened, harassed, or molested."

### Elements for Non-Domestic PPO
1. Two or more unconsented contacts
2. Contacts would cause reasonable person to feel threatened/harassed
3. Respondent knew or should have known contacts were unwelcome
4. Respondent intended to cause fear or harm

### Key Differences from Domestic PPO

| Feature | Domestic (2950) | Non-Domestic (2950a) |
|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| Relationship required | Yes (domestic) | No |
| Burden for issuance | Reasonable cause | Stalking elements |
| Firearm prohibition | Mandatory/discretionary | Discretionary |
| Federal firearm ban (§ 922(g)(8)) | Yes | May not qualify |
| Duration | Indefinite | Typically 182 days (may be extended) |
| Violation penalty | 93 days / $500 | 93 days / $500 |

## III. Michigan PPO Forms

### Required Court Forms (SCAO)
- **CC 375:** Petition for Personal Protection Order (Domestic)
- **CC 375a:** Petition for PPO (Non-Domestic Stalking)
- **CC 376:** Personal Protection Order (Domestic)
- **CC 376a:** PPO (Non-Domestic Stalking)
- **CC 377:** Motion to Modify/Terminate PPO
- **CC 378:** Notice of Hearing on PPO
- **CC 380:** Proof of Service of PPO

### Filing Requirements
1. Petition filed in circuit court (14th Judicial Circuit for Muskegon County)
2. **No filing fee** for PPO petitions (MCL 600.2950(2))
3. Petition must contain specific factual allegations
4. Affidavit or verified petition under oath

## IV. PPO Process Flow

```
PETITIONER files petition (CC 375)
│
├─ Judge reviews ex parte
│   ├─ GRANTED → PPO issued; must be served on respondent
│   │   ├─ Respondent served → PPO enforceable
│   │   │   ├─ Respondent complies → PPO remains in effect indefinitely
│   │   │   ├─ Respondent files Motion to Modify/Terminate (CC 377)
│   │   │   │   └─ Hearing within 14 days
│   │   │   │       ├─ MODIFIED → Revised PPO issued
│   │   │   │       ├─ TERMINATED → PPO dissolved
│   │   │   │       └─ CONTINUED → PPO unchanged
│   │   │   └─ Respondent VIOLATES → Criminal charge (misdemeanor)
│   │   │       └─ Bond conditions imposed
│   │   └─ Respondent not served → PPO not enforceable
│   └─ DENIED → Petitioner may request hearing
│       └─ Hearing → PPO granted or denied after both parties heard
│
└─ Judge sets hearing instead of ex parte issuance
    └─ Both parties appear → PPO granted, modified, or denied
```

## V. Federal Implications

### 18 USC § 922(g)(8) — Firearm Prohibition
A qualifying domestic PPO triggers federal prohibition on:
- Purchasing firearms
- Possessing firearms or ammunition
- Receiving firearms

**Qualifying order requirements (federal):**
1. Issued after hearing where respondent had notice and opportunity to participate
   - **NOTE:** Ex parte PPOs may not qualify until after a contested hearing
2. Restrains respondent from harassing, stalking, or threatening intimate partner or child
3. Includes a finding of credible threat or explicitly prohibits use/threatened use of physical force

**Penalty:** Federal felony — up to 10 years imprisonment

### Violence Against Women Act (VAWA) — Full Faith and Credit
- 18 USC § 2265: PPOs from one state must be enforced by other states
- Valid Michigan PPO is enforceable nationwide
- Violation in another state can be prosecuted federally

### Lautenberg Amendment — 18 USC § 922(g)(9)
Separate from PPO: Conviction of misdemeanor crime of domestic violence permanently prohibits firearm possession (no PPO needed).

## VI. PPO in Custody Context — Special Considerations

### MCL 600.2950(1)(d) — Child Removal Exception
PPO may prohibit removing minor children "except as provided by court order granting custody or parenting time."

**This means:** A custody/parenting time order can OVERRIDE the PPO's child-related restrictions. If you have a valid custody order granting parenting time, the PPO should not prevent exercise of that parenting time.

### Arguing PPO/Custody Conflict
When a PPO effectively prevents court-ordered parenting time:
1. File motion to modify PPO to add explicit custody exchange exception
2. Argue: Custody order was entered after full best-interest analysis; PPO was entered ex parte
3. Argue: Child's best interests (MCL 722.23) include relationship with both parents
4. Propose safeguards: Neutral exchange location, third-party present, no direct contact between parents

### PPO's Effect on Factor (k) — Domestic Violence
The EXISTENCE of a PPO is relevant to MCL 722.23(k) (domestic violence factor) in custody proceedings:
- Even if PPO was based on false allegations, its existence creates a record
- Terminating the PPO removes this factor from the other party's arsenal
- **This is why PPO termination/modification should be pursued aggressively**

### Documentation of PPO Abuse as Custody Weapon
If the PPO was filed to gain custody advantage rather than for genuine protection:
- Timeline showing PPO filed simultaneously with custody filing
- Absence of any police reports or prior complaints before custody dispute
- Petitioner's continued voluntary contact with respondent
- PPO terms that specifically target custody/parenting rather than safety
- Pattern of PPO filings around custody hearing dates
