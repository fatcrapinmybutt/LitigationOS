# Contempt Triggers — Court Order Compliance Reference

## Automatic Contempt Trigger Matrix

### Purpose
This reference defines specific, documented conditions under which a violation rises to the level of contempt action. Not every violation warrants a contempt motion — this matrix helps determine when to escalate.

### Escalation Levels

| Level | Description | Action | Threshold |
|-------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **1 — Notice** | Minor, isolated violation | Informal communication | First occurrence, minimal impact |
| **2 — Warning** | Repeated minor or single significant violation | Written notice to party | 2nd minor or 1st significant |
| **3 — Demand** | Pattern forming or serious single violation | Formal demand letter | 3+ minor or 2nd significant |
| **4 — Contempt** | Established pattern or severe violation | MCR 3.606 motion | Pattern or severe impact on L.D.W. |
| **5 — Emergency** | Immediate threat to child safety | MCR 2.119(F) + contempt | Safety risk |

### Custody Order Violations (Lane A)

#### Parenting Time Violations
| Violation | Impact Level | Escalation |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| 15-minute late exchange | LOW | Level 1 — notice, document |
| Missed exchange (no notice) | HIGH | Level 3 first time; Level 4 if pattern |
| Refusal to allow parenting time | CRITICAL | Level 4 immediately |
| Failure to return child | CRITICAL | Level 5 — emergency |
| Withholding child from school pickup | HIGH | Level 3 first time; Level 4 if pattern |
| Denying phone/video contact | MEDIUM | Level 2 first; Level 3 pattern |
| Interfering with holiday schedule | HIGH | Level 3 first; Level 4 pattern |

#### Communication Violations
| Violation | Impact Level | Escalation |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Refusing to respond to scheduling requests | MEDIUM | Level 2 first; Level 3 pattern |
| Blocking co-parent's phone number | HIGH | Level 3 |
| Disparaging other parent in L.D.W.'s presence | MEDIUM | Level 2 + document for MCL 722.23(j) |
| Sharing private communications publicly | MEDIUM | Level 2 |

#### Decision-Making Violations
| Violation | Impact Level | Escalation |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Unilateral major decision (education) | HIGH | Level 3 |
| Unilateral medical decision (non-emergency) | HIGH | Level 3 |
| Enrolling child in activities without consultation | MEDIUM | Level 2 |
| Relocating without court approval | CRITICAL | Level 5 — MCL 722.31 |

### Support Order Violations (Lane A)

| Violation | Impact Level | Escalation |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| 1 missed payment | MEDIUM | Level 2 — written notice |
| 2 consecutive missed payments | HIGH | Level 3 — demand |
| 3+ missed payments or $1000+ arrears | CRITICAL | Level 4 — contempt |
| Failure to maintain insurance | HIGH | Level 3 |
| Hiding income to reduce support | CRITICAL | Level 4 + fraud referral |
| Refusing income withholding | HIGH | Level 3 |

### PPO Violations (Lane D)

| Violation | Impact Level | Escalation |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Direct contact in violation of PPO | CRITICAL | Level 4 — criminal referral + contempt |
| Third-party contact on behalf of violator | HIGH | Level 3 first; Level 4 pattern |
| Proximity violation | HIGH | Level 4 if intentional |
| Social media contact | MEDIUM | Level 3 |
| Violation involving L.D.W. | CRITICAL | Level 5 — emergency |

### Discovery Order Violations (Any Lane)

| Violation | Impact Level | Escalation |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Late response (< 7 days) | LOW | Level 1 — good faith extension |
| Late response (7-14 days) | MEDIUM | Level 2 — meet and confer |
| No response (14+ days) | HIGH | Level 3 — motion to compel (MCR 2.313) |
| Evasive/incomplete response | HIGH | Level 3 — motion to compel |
| Destruction of evidence | CRITICAL | Level 4 — sanctions + adverse inference |

## Pattern Recognition Guidelines

### Defining a "Pattern"
A pattern exists when:
- 3+ similar violations within 90 days
- 2+ violations of the same provision within 30 days
- Any combination of violations suggesting intentional non-compliance
- Escalating severity of violations over time

### Pattern Documentation
```
PATTERN ANALYSIS

Subject: [Respondent name]
Order: [Case number, date]
Period Analyzed: [Start date] to [End date]

VIOLATION TIMELINE:
Date 1: [Violation description] — Level [X]
Date 2: [Violation description] — Level [X]
Date 3: [Violation description] — Level [X]
...

PATTERN ASSESSMENT:
□ Isolated incidents (no pattern)
□ Emerging pattern (2 violations, similar nature)
□ Established pattern (3+ violations, consistent behavior)
□ Escalating pattern (increasing severity over time)

RECOMMENDED ACTION:
Based on the above analysis, recommend: Level [X] response
Rationale: [Why this level is appropriate]
```

### Judicial Considerations for Contempt Filing

Before filing contempt motion, evaluate:
1. **Strength of evidence**: Can we prove all four *Dougherty* elements?
2. **Pattern**: Is this isolated or part of a pattern?
3. **Impact on L.D.W.**: Has the child been harmed?
4. **Good faith**: Did respondent attempt to comply?
5. **Judicial receptivity**: How does Judge McNeill typically handle contempt?
6. **Strategic timing**: Does filing now serve the overall case strategy?
7. **Cost-benefit**: Is the relief sought worth the effort and potential backlash?

### MCL 722.23(j) Documentation Value

Every documented violation, even at Level 1-2, supports Factor (j) analysis:
- Factor (j): willingness to facilitate parent-child relationship
- Documented pattern of interference → negative Factor (j) finding
- This is often the MOST IMPORTANT factor in modification proceedings
- Maintain the violation log even for minor issues — the pattern matters more than individual incidents
