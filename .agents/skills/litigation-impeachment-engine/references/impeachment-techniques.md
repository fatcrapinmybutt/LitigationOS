# Impeachment Techniques — litigation-impeachment-engine

## Michigan Impeachment Technique Library

This reference provides a comprehensive library of impeachment techniques
applicable in Michigan courts under the Michigan Rules of Evidence (MRE),
tailored for Pigors v Watson litigation in the 14th Judicial Circuit.

---

## Technique 1: Prior Inconsistent Statement (MRE 613)

### Foundation
MRE 613 governs impeachment by prior inconsistent statements. It requires:
1. The witness be told the substance of the prior statement
2. The circumstances under which it was made (time, place, to whom)
3. The witness be given an opportunity to explain or deny

### Execution Protocol

**Step 1: COMMIT** (Lock the witness into current testimony)
```
Q: "Mr./Ms. [Witness], you testified today that [current claim], correct?"
Q: "And that testimony is based on your personal knowledge?"
Q: "And you're certain about that?"
```
*Note: Get a clear, unequivocal commitment. If witness hedges, repeat until firm.*

**Step 2: CREDIT** (Establish the reliability of the prior statement)
```
Q: "Do you recall giving a [deposition/statement/affidavit] on [date]?"
Q: "You were [under oath / signing under penalty of perjury]?"
Q: "You understood the importance of being truthful?"
Q: "You had the opportunity to review and correct that statement?"
Q: "And you [signed/affirmed] it as accurate?"
```

**Step 3: CONFRONT** (Present the contradiction)
```
Q: "I'm going to show you what's been marked as Exhibit [X]."
Q: "Do you recognize this as your [deposition/affidavit/statement]?"
Q: "Please turn to page [X], line [Y]."
Q: "Please read the highlighted portion aloud."
   [Witness reads contradicting statement]
Q: "So on [prior date], your testimony was [prior statement], correct?"
```

**Step 4: CONTRAST** (Highlight the inconsistency — do NOT ask "why")
```
Q: "So on [prior date], you said [A], and today you say [B]."
   [Statement, not question — let the contradiction speak]
Q: "I have nothing further on this topic."
```

### Critical Rules
- **NEVER ask "Why did you change your story?"** — This gives the witness a platform to explain away the contradiction
- **Stop after the contrast** — Let the contradiction sit. Silence is powerful.
- **Use the witness's own words** — Quote exactly from the prior statement

## Technique 2: Contradiction by Document (MRE 901 + 613)

### When to Use
When a document (email, letter, report, record) contradicts the witness's
testimony, but the witness did not author the document.

### Foundation Requirements
1. Authenticate the document (MRE 901)
2. Establish the witness's knowledge of the document
3. Present the contradicting content

### Execution Protocol
```
Q: "Are you familiar with [document description]?"
Q: "You [received/reviewed/responded to] this document?"
Q: "I'm showing you Exhibit [X]. Do you recognize it?"
Q: "Please read the section highlighted in yellow."
   [Witness reads]
Q: "This document says [contradicting fact], but you testified [different fact]?"
```

## Technique 3: Omission Impeachment

### When to Use
When a witness failed to mention a critical fact in a prior statement that
they now claim in testimony. The absence of the fact is the impeachment.

### Execution Protocol
```
Q: "You gave a statement on [date] about [event], correct?"
Q: "That statement was [X] pages long?"
Q: "You had the opportunity to include everything important?"
Q: "You were trying to be thorough and accurate?"
Q: "Nowhere in that statement did you mention [critical fact], did you?"
Q: "But today, for the first time, you're telling us about [critical fact]?"
```

## Technique 4: Bias / Motive Impeachment (MRE 616)

### When to Use
When the witness has a reason to testify favorably for one party.

### Common Bias Sources
| Bias Type | Lane A Application | Lane B Application |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Family relationship | Witness related to Watson | N/A |
| Financial interest | Child support implications | Tenant-landlord financial dispute |
| Friendship | Personal relationship with party | Employee loyalty to Shady Oaks |
| Fear / intimidation | Domestic situation dynamics | Employment consequences |
| Pending litigation | Related cases | Related complaints |

### Execution Protocol
```
Q: "You're [relationship] to [party], correct?"
Q: "You [financial/personal interest in outcome]?"
Q: "If [party] wins, you benefit because [specific benefit]?"
Q: "You want us to believe your testimony is unaffected by this?"
```

## Technique 5: Perception / Memory Impeachment

### When to Use
When attacking the witness's ability to perceive or remember the events.

### Factors to Explore
- **Distance**: How far was witness from the event?
- **Lighting**: What were visibility conditions?
- **Duration**: How long did the witness observe?
- **Attention**: Was the witness focused on the event or distracted?
- **Time elapsed**: How much time between event and testimony?
- **Stress**: Was the witness under stress during observation?

## Technique 6: Character for Truthfulness (MRE 608/609)

### MRE 608 — Opinion/Reputation Evidence
- May attack witness's character for truthfulness through opinion or reputation testimony
- May NOT use specific instances of conduct (except on cross of the character witness)

### MRE 609 — Prior Convictions
| Conviction Type | Admissible? | Foundation |
|----------------|-------------|------------|
| Crime with dishonesty element | Yes (automatic) | Establish conviction |
| Felony (no dishonesty) | Yes if probative > prejudice | Court balances under MRE 609(a) |
| Conviction > 10 years old | Yes only if probative substantially outweighs prejudice | Heightened standard |
| Juvenile adjudications | Generally no | Exceptions exist |

## Technique 7: Learned Treatise Impeachment (MRE 803(18))

### When to Use
When an expert witness's opinion contradicts an established authority.

### Foundation
1. Establish the treatise is authoritative (through the expert or judicial notice)
2. Read the contradicting passage into evidence
3. Treatise may be read but not received as an exhibit

## Michigan-Specific Impeachment Notes

### Deposition Use at Trial — MCR 2.308(A)
- **Party deposition**: Admissible for any purpose by adverse party
- **Non-party deposition**: Admissible if witness unavailable or for impeachment
- Deposition of FOC worker: Treat as non-party unless FOC is a party

### Affidavit vs. Deposition
- Affidavits are sworn but not subject to cross-examination when made
- Depositions are sworn AND subject to cross-examination
- Both qualify under MRE 801(d)(1)(A) for substantive use

### Transcript Impeachment in Family Court (Lane A)
- Prior hearing transcripts from same case are powerful impeachment tools
- McNeill conducts evidentiary hearings — testimony is recorded
- Request expedited transcripts after every hearing for impeachment database

### Corporate Representative Impeachment (Lane B)
- MCR 2.307(A)(1)(f) designee speaks for the corporation
- Contradictions between designee testimony and corporate documents are devastating
- Shady Oaks corporate records vs. corporate representative testimony = key target
