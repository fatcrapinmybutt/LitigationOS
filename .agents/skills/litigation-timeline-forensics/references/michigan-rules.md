# Michigan Rules — Timeline Forensics

## Michigan Rules of Evidence (MRE)

### MRE 901 — Authentication and Identification
- **(a)**: General provision — authentication required as condition precedent to admissibility
- **(b)(1)**: Testimony of witness with knowledge
- **(b)(3)**: Comparison by trier or expert witness
- **(b)(4)**: Distinctive characteristics (appearance, contents, substance)
- **(b)(7)**: Public records
- **(b)(9)**: Process or system — evidence describing a process or system and showing it produces an accurate result (relevant for digital forensics)
- **(b)(10)**: Methods provided by statute or rule

### MRE 902 — Self-Authentication
- **(1)**: Domestic public documents under seal
- **(2)**: Domestic public documents not under seal (with certification)
- **(4)**: Certified copies of public records
- **(5)**: Official publications
- **(11)**: Certified domestic records of regularly conducted activity (business records)

### MRE 803(6) — Business Records Exception
- Records of regularly conducted activity
- Made at or near the time by a person with knowledge
- Kept in the course of regularly conducted business
- Foundation: custodian or qualified witness testimony
- Applicable to phone records, bank records, medical records in timelines

### MRE 803(8) — Public Records Exception
- Records of public offices or agencies
- Court records, police reports, government filings
- Strong foundation for timeline entries based on official records

### MRE 1001-1008 — Best Evidence Rule
- **(1001)**: Definitions — originals and duplicates
- **(1003)**: Duplicate admissible unless genuine question of authenticity
- **(1006)**: Summaries of voluminous records (charts, timelines as summaries)
- Timeline exhibits may qualify as MRE 1006 summaries of voluminous evidence

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.302 — Discovery for Timeline Building
- **(A)**: Broad discovery scope — documents, electronically stored information
- **(B)**: ESI (electronically stored information) — text messages, emails, social media
- Discovery is the primary tool for obtaining timeline evidence from opposing parties

### MCR 2.310 — Request for Production
- **(A)**: Requests for documents including ESI
- **(B)**: Response within **28 days**
- Use production requests to obtain dated documents for timeline

### MCR 2.506 — Subpoena (Third-Party Records)
- Subpoena third-party records (phone companies, banks, social media platforms)
- Essential for corroborating or building independent timeline

### MCR 2.316 — ESI Discovery
- Electronic discovery provisions for text messages, emails, social media
- Preservation obligations once litigation is anticipated
- Proportionality in ESI discovery

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.2136-2139 — Documentary Evidence
- Authentication requirements for documentary evidence in Michigan courts
- Business record foundation requirements

### MCL 750.539c — Eavesdropping (One-Party Consent)
- Michigan is a **one-party consent** state for recordings
- Recordings made by a party to the conversation are admissible
- Relevant for audio/video evidence in timelines

### MCL 445.72 — Financial Records
- Bank and financial records obtained through proper legal process
- Foundation through custodian of records affidavit

### MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors (Timeline Context)
- Timeline evidence supports findings on multiple factors
- Factor (j): Pattern of facilitation/interference over time
- Factor (k): History of domestic violence with dates and progression

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Authentication of Electronic Evidence
- **People v Smith**, 336 Mich App 79 (2021) — Text messages authenticated by testimony of recipient who recognized the sender's phone number and the substance of the conversation
- **People v Mahone**, 294 Mich App 208 (2011) — Electronic evidence requires same authentication as other evidence; distinctive characteristics can establish authenticity

### Summaries of Voluminous Evidence
- **Michigan Millers Mutual Ins Co v Bronson Plating Co**, 197 Mich App 482 (1992) — Summary exhibits (charts, timelines) are admissible under MRE 1006 when underlying documents are voluminous and available for inspection
- Timelines qualify as MRE 1006 summaries when built from documented evidence

### Spoliation
- **Brenner v Kolk**, 226 Mich App 149 (1997) — Failure to preserve evidence when litigation is anticipated may result in adverse inference instruction
- Relevant when timeline gaps suggest evidence destruction

### Digital Evidence Reliability
- **Duray Dev LLC v Perrin**, 288 Mich App 143 (2010) — Computer-generated records admissible when foundational requirements are met

## Checklists

### Timeline Source Inventory
- [ ] Court filings (all cases — custody, PPO, housing)
- [ ] Text messages (preserved with metadata)
- [ ] Emails (with full headers)
- [ ] Phone call logs (from carrier records)
- [ ] Financial records (bank statements with dates)
- [ ] Social media posts (with timestamps and URLs)
- [ ] Medical records (with treatment dates)
- [ ] School records (attendance, events, reports)
- [ ] Police reports (with incident dates)
- [ ] Photographs (with EXIF data / creation dates)
- [ ] Video recordings (with timestamps)
- [ ] Witness statements (dated and signed)
- [ ] FOC records and correspondence
- [ ] Employment records (relevant events)

### Timeline Quality Checklist
- [ ] Every entry has a date and source citation
- [ ] Sources are authenticated or authenticatable per MRE 901
- [ ] Multiple sources corroborate key events
- [ ] Both favorable and unfavorable events included
- [ ] No internal contradictions
- [ ] Metadata preserved for digital evidence
- [ ] Chain of custody documented for critical items
- [ ] Summary version prepared for court (2-5 pages)
- [ ] Detailed version maintained for preparation (comprehensive)

### Filing Deadlines
- ESI preservation: Immediately upon anticipation of litigation
- Discovery requests for timeline documents: Per scheduling order
- Exhibit exchange deadline: Per scheduling order (typically 14 days before trial)
- Summary exhibit disclosure: Before trial per scheduling order
