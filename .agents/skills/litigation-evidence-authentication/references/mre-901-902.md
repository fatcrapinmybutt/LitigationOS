# MRE 901-902 — Evidence Authentication Reference

## MRE 901 — Requirement of Authentication

### MRE 901(a) — General Provision
"The requirement of authentication or identification as a condition precedent to admissibility is satisfied by evidence sufficient to support a finding that the matter in question is what its proponent claims."

**Burden**: Preponderance of the evidence (more likely than not)

### MRE 901(b) — Illustrations of Authentication

| Method | MRE | Description | Common Use |
|--------|-----|-------------|------------|
| Testimony of witness with knowledge | 901(b)(1) | "I was there; I saw this" | Photographs, events |
| Non-expert opinion on handwriting | 901(b)(2) | Familiarity not acquired for litigation | Signatures, letters |
| Comparison by trier or expert | 901(b)(3) | Expert compares questioned item to known | Handwriting, forensics |
| Distinctive characteristics | 901(b)(4) | Content, context, substance, pattern | Texts, emails, social media |
| Voice identification | 901(b)(5) | Familiarity with person's voice | Phone recordings |
| Telephone conversation | 901(b)(6) | Call to listed number + identification | Phone calls |
| Public records or reports | 901(b)(7) | Authorized by law to be recorded/filed | Government records |
| Ancient documents | 901(b)(8) | 20+ years old, fair condition, found where expected | Historical records |
| Process or system | 901(b)(9) | System produces accurate results | Computer records, databases |
| Methods provided by statute | 901(b)(10) | Authentication prescribed by law | Varies by statute |

### Digital Evidence Authentication Protocols

#### Text Messages (MRE 901(b)(4))
```
Authentication Steps:
1. Identify the phone number associated with sender
   - Phone records showing subscriber info
   - Testimony that you know the number belongs to [person]

2. Establish distinctive characteristics
   - Content references known facts
   - Conversational context (reply to your message)
   - Writing style consistent with known communications
   - Nicknames, inside references

3. Preserve the evidence
   - Screenshot with timestamp, phone number visible
   - Export full thread (not cherry-picked messages)
   - Preserve on original device if possible
   - Certified forensic extraction (best practice)

4. Present at hearing
   - Testify: "This is a text conversation I had with [person]"
   - "I know this is [person] because [distinctive characteristics]"
   - Offer screenshot or extraction as exhibit
```

#### Email Authentication (MRE 901(b)(4) and (b)(9))
```
Authentication Steps:
1. Full email headers (From, To, Date, Subject, server routing)
2. Content consistent with sender's known communications
3. Reply chain showing back-and-forth
4. Email account ownership (testimony or records)
5. System reliability (email server is functional)
```

#### Social Media Posts (MRE 901(b)(4))
```
Authentication Steps:
1. Identify the account holder (profile matches known person)
2. Content references personal information
3. Screenshot with URL, profile info, timestamp
4. Testimony connecting account to person
5. Metadata preservation (print to PDF with URL visible)
```

#### Photographs (MRE 901(b)(1) and (b)(9))
```
Authentication Steps:
1. Testimony from photographer: when, where, what depicted
2. EXIF metadata (date, time, GPS coordinates, device)
3. "Fair and accurate representation" testimony
   - "This photo fairly and accurately depicts [scene] as it
     appeared on [date]"
4. Chain of custody from capture to courtroom
```

## MRE 902 — Self-Authentication

Certain documents require NO extrinsic evidence of authenticity:

| Type | MRE 902 | Requirement |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Domestic public documents under seal | 902(1) | Seal + signature of authorized officer |
| Domestic public documents not under seal | 902(2) | Certification by officer with seal |
| Foreign public documents | 902(3) | Attestation + final certification |
| Certified copies of public records | 902(4) | Certification by custodian |
| Official publications | 902(5) | Issued by public authority |
| Newspapers and periodicals | 902(6) | Purporting to be as described |
| Trade inscriptions | 902(7) | Labels, signs, tags in normal course |
| Acknowledged documents | 902(8) | Notarized per law |
| Commercial paper | 902(9) | Per commercial code |
| Business records with certification | 902(11) | Custodian certification |
| Certified domestic records | 902(12) | Certified by custodian |

### Obtaining Certified Copies

**From Court Clerk:**
- Request certified copy of court order/filing
- Clerk stamps with court seal and certification
- Cost: typically $1/page + $10 certification fee
- Processing time: 1-5 business days

**From Government Agency:**
- FOIA request (MCL 15.231) if not publicly available
- Request "certified copy" specifically
- May require fee payment in advance
- Processing time: 5-15 business days

## Hearsay Exceptions Relevant to Authentication

Even authenticated documents may contain hearsay. Common exceptions:

| Exception | MRE | Application |
|-----------|-----|-------------|
| Present sense impression | 803(1) | Statement during or immediately after event |
| Excited utterance | 803(2) | Statement under stress of startling event |
| State of mind | 803(3) | Statement of intent, plan, motive |
| Medical diagnosis | 803(4) | Statement for medical treatment |
| Business records | 803(6) | Records kept in regular course of business |
| Public records | 803(8) | Government agency records and reports |
| Absence of record | 803(10) | Proving something didn't happen |
| Party admission | 801(d)(2) | Statement by opposing party |
| Prior inconsistent | 801(d)(1) | Under oath at prior proceeding |
