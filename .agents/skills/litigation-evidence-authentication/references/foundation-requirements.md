# Foundation Requirements — Evidence Authentication Reference

## What Is "Foundation"?

Foundation is the preliminary evidence establishing that an exhibit is what its proponent claims it is. Without proper foundation, evidence is inadmissible regardless of its relevance or probative value.

### The Foundation Formula
```
Foundation = Authentication + Relevance + No Applicable Exclusion

Authentication: MRE 901/902 (what is this thing?)
Relevance: MRE 401/402 (does it matter?)
No Exclusion: MRE 403/802/etc. (is there a rule keeping it out?)
```

## Foundation Requirements by Evidence Type

### Type 1: Documents

#### Business Records (MRE 803(6))
Foundation elements:
1. Record made at or near the time of the event
2. Made by or from information transmitted by a person with knowledge
3. Kept in the course of a regularly conducted business activity
4. It was the regular practice to make such a record
5. Testified to by the custodian or other qualified witness

**Foundation questions:**
```
Q: Are you familiar with the record-keeping practices of [organization]?
Q: Is it the regular practice of [organization] to create this type of record?
Q: Who creates these records?
Q: When are they created in relation to the events they document?
Q: How is the information in these records obtained?
Q: I'm showing you Exhibit [X]. Do you recognize this document?
Q: Is this document kept in the regular course of [organization]'s business?
```

#### Medical Records
Foundation elements:
1. All business records elements (MRE 803(6))
2. Plus: records made for purposes of medical diagnosis/treatment (MRE 803(4))
3. Custodian certification (MRE 902(11)) OR testimony

#### School Records
Foundation elements:
1. Business records elements
2. Certified copy from school administration
3. Or testimony from records custodian

### Type 2: Photographs and Videos

#### Still Photographs
Foundation elements:
1. Witness with knowledge of the scene photographed
2. Testimony that the photo "fairly and accurately depicts" the scene
3. Identification of when and where photo was taken
4. Testimony about who took the photo (or that it accurately reflects what they saw)

**Foundation questions:**
```
Q: I'm showing you Exhibit [X]. Do you recognize what is depicted?
Q: What is depicted in this photograph?
Q: When was this photograph taken?
Q: Does this photograph fairly and accurately depict [scene/object]
   as it appeared on [date]?
Q: Has this photograph been altered in any way?
```

#### Video Recordings
Additional foundation beyond photographs:
1. Testimony about recording device and its operation
2. Whether video is continuous or edited
3. Audio component authentication (if applicable)
4. Storage and chain of custody

#### Surveillance/Security Camera Footage
Foundation elements:
1. Testimony about camera system installation and maintenance
2. Camera location and field of view
3. Date/time stamp accuracy verification
4. Chain of custody of recording media
5. Testimony that system was functioning properly

### Type 3: Electronic Communications

#### Text Messages
Foundation elements (detailed):
1. **Device identification**: Which device captured the messages?
2. **Account identification**: Whose phone number is involved?
3. **Sender identification**: How do you know who sent the message?
   - Phone records linking number to person
   - Content references known facts (MRE 901(b)(4))
   - Reply doctrine (response to your message)
4. **Completeness**: Is the thread complete or selectively presented?
5. **Integrity**: Has the content been altered?
6. **Preservation**: How was the evidence captured (screenshot, export, forensic)?

#### Emails
Foundation elements:
1. Sender email address identification
2. Email header information (routing data)
3. Content consistency with sender's known communications
4. System reliability (email service functioning normally)
5. Completeness of thread

#### Social Media
Foundation elements:
1. Account belongs to the person (profile verification)
2. Content was posted by that person (not hacked/impersonated)
3. Screenshot includes URL, timestamp, profile information
4. Content has not been altered since capture

### Type 4: Audio Recordings

#### Recording Admissibility (Michigan Law)
Michigan is a ONE-PARTY consent state (MCL 750.539c):
- A party to the conversation may record without the other's knowledge
- Third-party recording without consent of any party = illegal wiretap
- Exception: law enforcement with warrant

Foundation elements:
1. Identity of speakers
2. Accuracy and completeness of recording
3. No alterations or tampering
4. Equipment was functioning properly
5. Recording was made legally

### Type 5: Physical Evidence

#### General Foundation
```
Q: I'm showing you Exhibit [X]. Do you recognize it?
Q: How do you recognize it?
Q: Is it in substantially the same condition as when you last saw it?
Q: Has anyone altered, modified, or tampered with it?
```

#### Chain of Custody
For evidence susceptible to tampering:
```
Person 1 → obtained evidence on [date] at [location]
         → stored evidence at [location] under [conditions]
         → transferred to Person 2 on [date]
Person 2 → received evidence on [date]
         → stored at [location]
         → brought to court on [date]
```

## Common Foundation Objections and Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Lacks foundation" | Offer additional foundational testimony |
| "Not authenticated" | Identify MRE 901(b) method being used |
| "Hearsay" | Identify applicable exception (MRE 803/804) |
| "Best evidence rule" | Produce original or establish MRE 1004 exception |
| "Relevance" | Connect exhibit to specific claim/defense element |
| "Prejudicial" | Argue probative value outweighs prejudice (MRE 403) |
| "Altered/incomplete" | Testify to completeness and integrity |

## Pre-Hearing Foundation Preparation

For EVERY exhibit:
1. Identify foundation method
2. Prepare foundation witness
3. Write out foundation questions
4. Anticipate objections and prepare responses
5. Have backup authentication if primary method fails
6. Organize exhibits in order of presentation
7. Provide copies to court and opposing party
