# Service Methods — Complete MCR 2.103-2.107 Reference

> Michigan Court Rules for service of process
> Context: Pigors v Watson | 14th Judicial Circuit, Muskegon County

---

## MCR 2.102 — SUMMONS

### Issuance:
- Summons is issued by the clerk upon filing of the complaint — MCR 2.102(A)
- Must bear the court seal, case number, and filing date
- Must command the defendant to answer within 21 days (MI) or 28 days (out-of-state)
- Plaintiff is responsible for delivering summons to the person who will make service

### Validity Period:
- Summons is valid for **91 days** from the date of issuance — MCR 2.102(D)
- After 91 days, summons expires and a new one must be requested
- No limit on the number of reissues

### Alias Summons:
If the original summons expires before service is completed:
1. Request an alias (new) summons from the clerk
2. No additional filing fee required
3. New 91-day period begins from the date of the alias summons

## MCR 2.103 — WHO MAY SERVE PROCESS

### Authorized Servers — MCR 2.103(A):
Process may be served by any person who is:
1. **18 years of age or older** (legally competent adult)
2. **NOT a party to the action**

### Types of Servers:
| Server Type | Pros | Cons |
|------------|------|------|
| **Professional process server** | Experienced, reliable, sworn affidavit | Costs $50-$150 per serve |
| **County sheriff/deputy** | Official authority, familiar with courts | May be slow, limited availability |
| **Friend/family member (non-party)** | Free, available | Must be 18+, must complete affidavit |
| **Attorney's office staff** | Convenient if represented | N/A for pro se |

### Pro Se Critical Rule:
The pro se plaintiff **CANNOT** serve their own process. This is the most common pro se error. Designate a non-party server before the summons is issued.

## MCR 2.105 — MANNER OF SERVICE

### MCR 2.105(A) — Individuals:

#### Method 1: Personal Service — MCR 2.105(A)(1)
The process server physically hands the summons and complaint to the defendant.

**Procedure:**
1. Locate the defendant
2. Confirm identity ("Are you [defendant's name]?")
3. Hand the defendant the summons, complaint, and all attachments
4. If defendant refuses to accept, place documents at their feet or as close as practicable
5. State: "You are being served with a lawsuit"

**Best for:** Defendants with known location. Most bulletproof method.

#### Method 2: Service at Dwelling — MCR 2.105(A)(2)
Leave copies at defendant's usual place of abode with a person of suitable age and discretion who resides there.

**Requirements:**
- Must be the defendant's USUAL place of abode (not workplace, not a relative's house)
- Person receiving must RESIDE there (not a visitor)
- Person must be of SUITABLE AGE (adult) and DISCRETION (mentally competent)
- Process server must identify the person receiving service

**Best for:** Defendants who are rarely home but have household members.

#### Method 3: Registered/Certified Mail — MCR 2.105(A)(2)
Send by registered or certified mail, return receipt requested, restricted delivery.

**Procedure:**
1. Place summons + complaint in envelope addressed to defendant
2. Send by USPS certified mail, return receipt requested
3. Request "restricted delivery" (only defendant can sign)
4. When green card (return receipt) comes back SIGNED by defendant → service is complete
5. If returned unsigned, unclaimed, or "refused" → service FAILED, use another method

**Best for:** Out-of-area defendants. Least confrontational method.

#### Method 4: Tacking and Mailing — MCR 2.105(A)(3)
Attach to the defendant's door AND mail by first-class mail.

**Requirements:**
- Only after diligent attempts at personal and substitute service
- Securely attach to defendant's door in a conspicuous place
- ALSO mail by first-class mail to defendant's last known address
- Document at least 3 prior failed service attempts

**Best for:** Evasive defendants, last resort before publication.

### MCR 2.105(D) — Corporations and LLCs:

#### Service on Corporation:
Serve any of the following:
1. An officer of the corporation
2. The resident agent designated by the corporation
3. A director of the corporation
4. If none can be found: the Michigan Department of LARA (Licensing and Regulatory Affairs)

#### Service on LLC:
Serve any of the following:
1. A member or manager of the LLC
2. The registered agent designated with LARA
3. If no agent: LARA itself

#### Finding the Registered Agent:
1. Go to LARA Corporate Entity Search: https://cofs.lara.state.mi.us/SearchApi/Search/Search
2. Search by entity name
3. Record: registered agent name, registered office address, entity status
4. If entity is "dissolved" or "inactive" — serve the last known agent or officer

### MCR 2.105(E) — Partnerships:
Serve any partner or agent authorized to receive process.

### MCR 2.105(G) — Government Entities:

#### State of Michigan — MCR 2.105(G)(1):
Serve the **Attorney General** or a deputy/assistant AG.
- Address: Michigan Attorney General, P.O. Box 30212, Lansing, MI 48909

#### Local Government (City, Township, County) — MCR 2.105(G)(2):
Serve the **chief executive officer** (mayor, township supervisor, county administrator) or the **clerk**.

### MCR 2.105(I) — Alternative Service:
When standard methods fail, file a motion requesting the court authorize alternative service.

**Motion must show:**
1. Specific efforts made to serve by standard methods
2. Why standard methods have failed or will fail
3. Proposed alternative method
4. Why the alternative is reasonably calculated to provide notice

**Examples of court-approved alternative methods:**
- Service by email (with confirmation of active email address)
- Service by posting at workplace
- Service through a known associate
- Service at a location defendant is known to frequent

### MCR 2.105(J) — Out-of-State Defendants:
Use Michigan's long-arm statute (MCL 600.701-745) to serve out-of-state defendants.

**Requirements:**
1. Long-arm jurisdiction must exist (minimum contacts with Michigan)
2. Serve by any method authorized for in-state service
3. OR by registered mail to defendant's last known address
4. OR as directed by court order

## MCR 2.106 — SERVICE BY PUBLICATION

### When Authorized:
- Defendant cannot be found within Michigan after diligent search
- Defendant's address is unknown
- Defendant is an unknown party ("John Doe")

### Procedure:

#### Step 1: Affidavit of Diligent Search
File an affidavit detailing:
- All efforts to locate the defendant
- Addresses checked, databases searched, people contacted
- Why standard service methods cannot succeed

**The affidavit must be specific and detailed.** Courts reject vague "I couldn't find them" affidavits. Include:
- Dates of each search effort
- Specific databases searched (LARA, SOS, property records, voter registration, social media)
- Specific addresses visited
- Specific people contacted

#### Step 2: Court Order
File a motion for service by publication with the affidavit. Obtain court order authorizing publication.

#### Step 3: Publish
- Publish in a newspaper of general circulation in the county where the defendant was last known to reside — MCR 2.106(D)
- Publish **once per week for 3 consecutive weeks**
- The publication must contain: case caption, brief statement of the nature of the action, and deadline for defendant to answer

#### Step 4: Mail
- Also mail a copy to the defendant's last known address by registered mail — MCR 2.106(E)
- If no address is known, document this in the affidavit

#### Step 5: File Proof of Publication
- Obtain an affidavit of publication from the newspaper
- File with the court along with a copy of the published notice

### Answer Deadline After Publication:
Defendant has **28 days after the last publication date** to answer — MCR 2.106(F)

## MCR 2.107 — SERVICE AFTER FILING (Non-Initial Service)

### Applies To:
All documents served AFTER the initial complaint — motions, briefs, discovery, amended pleadings.

### Methods:
1. **First-class mail** to the party's last known address
2. **Hand delivery** to the party or their attorney
3. **Electronic service** if agreed or ordered by the court

### Certificate of Service:
Every filed document (after the initial complaint) must include a certificate of service:

```
CERTIFICATE OF SERVICE

I hereby certify that on [date], I served a copy of the foregoing 
[document name] upon all parties by [first-class mail / hand 
delivery / electronic service] addressed to:

[Defendant Name]
[Address]

____________________________
ANDREW PIGORS, Pro Se
```

### Timing:
- Service must be made on the same day as filing, or within a reasonable time
- For motions: serve at least 9 days before the hearing (MCR 2.119(C)(1)) unless the court shortens time
