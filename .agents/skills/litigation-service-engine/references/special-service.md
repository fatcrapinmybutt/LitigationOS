# Special Service Situations — Michigan Reference

> Service on corporations, LLCs, government, out-of-state, and unknown defendants
> Context: Pigors v Watson | 14th Judicial Circuit, Muskegon County

---

## 1. SERVICE ON CORPORATIONS — MCR 2.105(D)

### Michigan Corporations:

**Authorized Recipients (in order of preference):**
1. **Registered agent** at the registered office address (filed with LARA)
2. **Officer** of the corporation (president, VP, secretary, treasurer)
3. **Director** of the corporation
4. **LARA** — if the corporation has no registered agent or the agent cannot be found

### Finding the Registered Agent:
1. Visit LARA Corporate Entity Search: https://cofs.lara.state.mi.us/SearchApi/Search/Search
2. Search by entity name (try variations: "Watson Properties," "Watson Properties LLC," etc.)
3. Record:
   - Entity ID number
   - Entity status (active, dissolved, inactive)
   - Registered agent name
   - Registered office address
   - Date of formation
   - Resident agent's acceptance date

### Service on LARA (Last Resort):
If no registered agent can be located:
1. File duplicate copies of the summons and complaint with LARA
2. LARA will forward to the corporation's last known address
3. Include the processing fee (check current fee schedule)
4. Address: Michigan LARA, Corporations Division, P.O. Box 30054, Lansing, MI 48909

### Dissolved Corporations:
A dissolved corporation can still be served:
- Serve the last known officer or director
- Serve the registered agent (agent authority survives dissolution for purposes of service)
- If no agent, serve through LARA

**Key:** Dissolution does not extinguish liability for pre-dissolution conduct. A dissolved corporation can be sued and its assets (or its officers) held liable.

### Foreign Corporations (Incorporated Outside Michigan):
**If authorized to do business in Michigan:**
- Serve the registered agent filed with LARA
- OR serve an officer at a Michigan office

**If NOT authorized to do business in Michigan but doing business here:**
- Long-arm jurisdiction applies — MCL 600.715
- Serve by any method authorized under MCR 2.105
- OR serve by registered mail to the corporation's principal office in the home state

## 2. SERVICE ON LLCs — MCL 450.4207; MCR 2.105(D)

### Michigan LLCs:

**Authorized Recipients:**
1. **Registered agent** — MCL 450.4207 requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent
2. **Any member** — for member-managed LLCs
3. **Any manager** — for manager-managed LLCs
4. **LARA** — if no registered agent can be found

### Member-Managed vs. Manager-Managed:
| Type | Who Has Authority | Service Target |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| Member-managed | All members manage | Any member |
| Manager-managed | Designated managers only | Any manager |

Check the LLC's Articles of Organization (filed with LARA) to determine governance structure.

### Multiple LLCs Under Common Ownership:
In cases like Pigors v Watson, the defendant may operate through multiple LLCs:
- Each LLC is a separate legal entity requiring separate service
- Serving one LLC does NOT constitute service on another
- The registered agent may be the same person for multiple LLCs — still serve separately for each entity

### Series LLCs:
Michigan recognizes series LLCs under MCL 450.4207a. Each series within a series LLC may need to be separately named and served if it has separate assets and liabilities.

## 3. SERVICE ON PARTNERSHIPS — MCR 2.105(E)

**Authorized Recipients:**
1. Any general partner
2. Any agent authorized to receive process
3. Person in charge at a place of business of the partnership

**Note:** Limited partners in a limited partnership are NOT proper recipients unless also general partners or authorized agents.

## 4. SERVICE ON GOVERNMENT ENTITIES — MCR 2.105(G)

### State of Michigan — MCR 2.105(G)(1):

**Serve the Attorney General:**
- Michigan Attorney General
- P.O. Box 30212
- Lansing, MI 48909
- Phone: (517) 335-7622

**AND serve the specific state agency** if suing a department or agency:
- Serve the head of the agency or the person designated to accept service

### Local Government — MCR 2.105(G)(2):

| Entity Type | Serve | Alternative |
|------------|-------|------------|
| **City** | Mayor or city manager | City clerk |
| **Township** | Township supervisor | Township clerk |
| **County** | County administrator/executive | County clerk |
| **Village** | Village president | Village clerk |
| **School district** | Superintendent | Board secretary |

### Government Immunity Considerations:
Before suing a government entity, check:
- **Governmental immunity** — MCL 691.1407 (broad immunity with narrow exceptions)
- **Notice requirements** — MCL 691.1404 (120-day notice for highway defect claims)
- **Exceptions** — Proprietary function, motor vehicle, highway maintenance, public building, hospital
- **Federal § 1983 claims bypass state immunity** but municipality must have an unconstitutional policy or custom (Monell v Dept of Social Services)

## 5. SERVICE ON OUT-OF-STATE DEFENDANTS — MCR 2.105(J)

### Long-Arm Jurisdiction — MCL 600.701-745:

Michigan's long-arm statute grants jurisdiction over out-of-state defendants who:
- **MCL 600.705**: Transact any business in Michigan
- **MCL 600.705**: Commit a tortious act in Michigan
- **MCL 600.705**: Own, use, or possess real property in Michigan
- **MCL 600.705**: Contract to insure any person, property, or risk in Michigan
- **MCL 600.715** (limited personal jurisdiction): Narrower grounds for specific actions

### Due Process Requirements:
Long-arm jurisdiction must also satisfy the Due Process Clause:
1. **Minimum contacts** — defendant purposefully availed itself of Michigan (International Shoe Co v Washington, 326 US 310 (1945))
2. **Relatedness** — the claim arises from or relates to defendant's Michigan contacts
3. **Reasonableness** — exercising jurisdiction doesn't offend fair play and substantial justice

### Service Methods for Out-of-State Defendants:

**Option 1: Same as In-State**
Any method authorized under MCR 2.105 can be used for out-of-state defendants. Personal service in another state is valid if long-arm jurisdiction exists.

**Option 2: Registered Mail**
Send by registered or certified mail, return receipt requested, to the defendant's out-of-state address.

**Option 3: As Directed by Court**
If standard methods fail, move for alternative service under MCR 2.105(I).

### Answer Timeline:
Out-of-state defendants have **28 days** to answer (vs. 21 days for Michigan residents) — MCR 2.108(A)(2).

## 6. SERVICE ON UNKNOWN DEFENDANTS — MCR 2.106

### "John Doe" Defendants:
When the identity of a defendant is unknown:
1. Caption the complaint: "[Name], individually, and JOHN DOE 1 through JOHN DOE 10, whose identities are presently unknown"
2. After filing, move for service by publication — MCR 2.106
3. Publication must describe the nature of the action and the property/transaction involved so the unknown party can recognize the claim

### Procedure:
See MCR 2.106 publication procedures in `service-methods.md`.

### Replacing "John Doe" with Named Defendants:
Once identity is discovered (through investigation or discovery):
1. File a motion to amend the complaint under MCR 2.118
2. Replace "John Doe" with the actual name
3. Serve the newly named defendant with summons and amended complaint
4. Relation back doctrine — MCR 2.118(D) — may preserve the original filing date

## 7. FEDERAL DEFENDANTS

### United States Government:
Service under Federal Rule 4(i):
1. Serve the United States Attorney for the Eastern or Western District of Michigan
2. AND send by registered or certified mail to the Attorney General of the United States in Washington, D.C.
3. AND serve the specific federal agency or officer named

### Federal Officers/Employees:
Service under Federal Rule 4(i)(3):
1. Serve the individual officer/employee
2. AND serve the United States as described above

### Federal Agencies:
Service under Federal Rule 4(i)(2):
1. Serve the agency by registered or certified mail to the agency head
2. AND serve the United States Attorney

## 8. WAIVER OF SERVICE

### Voluntary Waiver:
Under MCR 2.105(A)(4), a defendant may waive formal service by:
- Signing a written waiver acknowledging receipt of the summons and complaint
- The waiver gives the defendant 63 days to answer (instead of 21)

### Benefits of Requesting Waiver:
- Saves the cost of process server
- Less adversarial
- Extended answer time may encourage cooperation

### Template Waiver Request:
```
[Date]

[Defendant Name]
[Defendant Address]

RE: Pigors v [Defendant] — Muskegon County Circuit Court

Dear [Defendant]:

I have filed a lawsuit against you in the Muskegon County Circuit 
Court. Enclosed please find a copy of the Summons and Verified 
Complaint.

I am requesting that you waive formal service of process. If you 
agree, please sign the enclosed Waiver of Service form and return 
it to me within 30 days. By waiving service, you will have 63 days 
(instead of 21) to file your response.

If I do not receive the signed waiver within 30 days, I will 
arrange for formal service at your expense.

Respectfully,
Andrew Pigors
[Address]
[Phone]
```
