# gotchas — litigation-service-engine

## Anti-Rationalization Table: Improper Service

| # | The Agent Wants To Say | Why It's Wrong | What To Do Instead |
|---|----------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| 1 | "Just mail it to them — that's good enough" | Regular first-class mail is NOT sufficient for initial service of process in Michigan. MCR 2.105(A)(2) requires REGISTERED or CERTIFIED mail with return receipt requested. Ordinary mail gives no proof of delivery. Without a signed return receipt, service by mail is not accomplished and the court has no personal jurisdiction over the defendant. | Use ONLY registered or certified mail with return receipt requested. If the receipt comes back unsigned, unclaimed, or "refused," service is NOT accomplished by mail — escalate to personal service or alternative service. |
| 2 | "The plaintiff can serve the papers personally" | MCR 2.103(A) requires that the person serving process be a legally competent adult who is NOT a party to the action. A pro se plaintiff CANNOT serve their own process. Service by a party is void and confers no jurisdiction. The defendant can move to dismiss for insufficient service. | Hire a process server, ask the county sheriff, or have any non-party adult (friend, family member over 18) serve the papers. The server must then sign the proof of service affidavit. |
| 3 | "I served the defendant's spouse — close enough" | Serving a spouse is only valid as substitute service at the defendant's dwelling under MCR 2.105(A)(2), and only if the spouse is a person of "suitable age and discretion" residing at the same address. Handing papers to a spouse at a different location, or to a spouse who does not reside with the defendant, is not valid service. | For substitute service at the dwelling: confirm it's the defendant's actual residence, confirm the person receiving service lives there, and confirm they are of suitable age (adult) and discretion. Document all of this in the proof of service. |
| 4 | "The summons has been out for 4 months but I'll just serve it now" | A summons is valid for only 91 days from issuance — MCR 2.102(D). After 91 days, the summons expires and any service attempted with the expired summons is void. The defendant can challenge jurisdiction based on service with an expired summons. | Check the summons issuance date before EVERY service attempt. If 91 days have passed, request a new summons from the clerk before attempting service. Keep a calendar reminder for the expiration date. |
| 5 | "I served the complaint but forgot the summons" | Both the summons AND the complaint must be served together. MCR 2.102(A) requires that the summons accompany the complaint. Serving the complaint without the summons (or vice versa) is defective service. | Always serve: (1) the original summons, (2) a copy of the complaint with all exhibits, and (3) any other required documents (e.g., ADR notice if required by local rules). Create a service packet checklist. |
| 6 | "I taped the papers to the door — that counts as substitute service" | Posting on the door (tacking) under MCR 2.105(A)(3) is only valid as a LAST RESORT after diligent attempts at personal and substitute service, AND it must be accompanied by a first-class mailing to the defendant's address. Tacking alone, without the mailing component, is insufficient. Additionally, you must be able to demonstrate prior diligent attempts. | Document at least 3 attempts at personal service on different days/times. If all fail, then tack AND mail. The proof of service must detail all prior attempts, the date/time of tacking, and the date of the first-class mailing. |
| 7 | "I can serve the LLC by serving any employee at the business" | An LLC must be served through its members, managers, or registered agent — MCL 450.4207; MCR 2.105(D). Serving a random employee is not valid unless that employee is authorized to accept service (e.g., as designated agent). Most employees have no authority to accept process on behalf of the entity. | Look up the LLC's registered agent on the LARA Corporate Entity Search. Serve the registered agent at the registered office address. If the registered agent cannot be found, serve a member or manager. As a last resort, serve through LARA. |
| 8 | "I don't need to file proof of service — the court knows I served them" | Proof of service must be filed with the court. Without it, the court has no record that service was accomplished and cannot enter default judgment if the defendant fails to respond. MCR 2.104 requires filing proof of service. | File proof of service (MC 12 form or affidavit) immediately after service is accomplished. Keep a copy for your records. If seeking default, the court will require proof of service as a prerequisite. |

## Anti-Rationalization Table: Timing and Procedural Errors

| # | The Agent Wants To Say | Why It's Wrong | What To Do Instead |
|---|----------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| 1 | "I'll serve them eventually — there's no rush" | The defendant's time to answer runs from the date of service (21 days for Michigan residents, 28 days for out-of-state defendants — MCR 2.108). But the summons expires 91 days from issuance. Delaying service wastes the summons period and risks dismissal for failure to prosecute. | Serve as soon as possible after filing. Ideally within 7-14 days. Track the 91-day summons expiration. If service proves difficult, begin alternative service efforts early — don't wait until day 85. |
| 2 | "The defendant answered so the service defect doesn't matter" | Generally true — a defendant who files an answer without challenging service waives the objection. But this is DEFENDANT'S choice, not yours. If you rely on this and the defendant DOES challenge service (MCR 2.116(C)(1)), you lose. Never plan around hoping the defendant won't notice your defective service. | Do service correctly the first time. Don't rely on waiver. If you discover a service defect, correct it immediately — re-serve properly and file new proof of service. |
| 3 | "I served all the individual defendants — I don't need to serve the LLC separately" | Each named defendant must be separately served. An LLC is a separate legal entity from its members. Serving John Watson individually does NOT constitute service on Watson Properties LLC. The entity has separate existence and must be independently served through its registered agent or authorized person. | Serve EVERY named defendant individually. Create a service tracking chart: defendant name, service method, server identity, date served, proof filed. Do not consider service complete until every defendant is checked off. |
| 4 | "Service by publication is easy — just put it in the paper" | Publication requires a COURT ORDER first. MCR 2.106(B). You cannot just publish and claim service. You must: (1) file an affidavit of diligent search detailing all efforts to find the defendant, (2) obtain a court order authorizing publication, (3) publish for 3 consecutive weeks, (4) also mail to last known address. Skip any step and the service is void. | Follow the publication procedure exactly: affidavit → order → publish 3 weeks → mail → file proof. The affidavit must show genuine diligent effort — courts reject boilerplate "I looked but couldn't find them" affidavits. Detail every search method used. |
| 5 | "I served the amended complaint on the existing defendants — done" | If the amended complaint adds NEW defendants, those new defendants must be served with a NEW summons plus the amended complaint. Existing defendants who have already appeared can be served the amended complaint under MCR 2.107 (service after filing). But new parties need full initial service under MCR 2.105. | For amended complaints: (1) New defendants → full service under MCR 2.105 with new summons, (2) Existing defendants who have appeared → service under MCR 2.107, (3) File proof of service for all defendants. |
| 6 | "The process server said they served them — that's enough proof" | The process server must complete a sworn affidavit or the MC 12 form detailing: who was served, when, where, how (method), and the server's identity. A verbal assurance from the server is not proof of service. If the defendant challenges service, you need the written, sworn documentation. | Get the completed MC 12 or affidavit from the process server BEFORE paying them. Review it for completeness: correct defendant name, correct date/time, correct address, correct method, server's signature under oath. File immediately. |

## Critical Deadlines

| Deadline | Calculation | Authority |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Summons expiration | 91 days from issuance | MCR 2.102(D) |
| Defendant's answer (MI resident) | 21 days from service | MCR 2.108(A)(1) |
| Defendant's answer (out-of-state) | 28 days from service | MCR 2.108(A)(2) |
| Default entry | After answer deadline passes | MCR 2.603 |
| Proof of service filing | "Promptly" after service | MCR 2.104 |
