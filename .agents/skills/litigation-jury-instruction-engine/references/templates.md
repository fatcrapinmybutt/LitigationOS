## Special Jury Instruction Request

```
PLAINTIFF'S PROPOSED SPECIAL INSTRUCTION NO. [X]

    Plaintiff requests the following special instruction be given to
the jury:

    "INSTRUCTION TEXT: [Precise wording of proposed instruction]"

LEGAL BASIS:
    This instruction is supported by:
    (a) [Statute: MCL section]
    (b) [Case law: Citation]
    (c) The evidence at trial, specifically [describe evidence]

    This instruction is necessary because the standard M Civ JI
instructions do not adequately cover [specific legal theory or
factual issue], and the jury requires guidance on this point to
fairly decide the case. See Jaworski v Kiernan, 241 Mich App 697
(2000) (party entitled to instruction on their theory of the case
when supported by evidence).
```

## Objection to Jury Instructions — On the Record

```
PLAINTIFF'S OBJECTIONS TO JURY INSTRUCTIONS
(Stated on the Record Before Jury Retires — MCR 2.512(C))

THE COURT: Does Plaintiff have objections to the instructions?

PLAINTIFF: Yes, Your Honor. Plaintiff states the following objections:

    OBJECTION 1: Plaintiff objects to Instruction No. [X] regarding
    [subject] because [specific legal error]. The correct instruction
    should state [correct legal standard]. This is supported by
    [citation].

    OBJECTION 2: Plaintiff objects to the Court's refusal to give
    Plaintiff's Proposed Special Instruction No. [Y] regarding
    [subject]. This instruction is supported by the evidence presented
    through [witness/exhibit] and is a correct statement of law under
    [citation].

    OBJECTION 3: Plaintiff objects to Instruction No. [Z] because it
    misstates the burden of proof. The correct burden for [claim type]
    is [preponderance / clear and convincing], not [what was stated].

    Plaintiff requests these objections be preserved for the record.
```

## Workflow: Jury Instruction Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Determine jury trial right for each claim
    ↓
Step 2: File jury demand + fee within 28 days (MCR 2.516)
    ↓
Step 3: Identify applicable M Civ JI for claims/defenses
    ↓
Step 4: Draft special instructions for unique theories
    ↓
Step 5: Submit proposed instructions per scheduling order
    ↓
Step 6: Attend charge conference
    ↓
Step 7: Review court's proposed final charge
    ↓
Step 8: State specific objections on record (MCR 2.512(C))
    ↓
Step 9: Monitor jury questions during deliberation
    ↓
Step 10: If adverse verdict → evaluate instruction error for appeal
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Submitting instructions at trial | Too late; court may refuse | Submit per scheduling order, well before trial |
| General objection to charge | Does not preserve error (Elezovic) | State specific objection for each instruction |
| Using criminal instructions in civil case | Wrong burden of proof; wrong standards | Use M Civ JI exclusively for civil matters |
| Not requesting special instructions | Standard charge may miss your theory | Draft special instructions for unique theories |
| Assuming custody cases get juries | Custody is equitable; no jury right | Only demand jury for claims that carry jury rights |
| Ignoring juror questions | Missed opportunity to clarify | Propose supplemental instructions when questions arise |
