# Michigan Rules — Jury Instruction Engine

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.512 — Instructions to Jury
- **(A)**: At any time during trial, the court may instruct the jury on applicable law
- **(B)(1)**: Before closing arguments, court must give parties opportunity to submit proposed instructions
- **(B)(2)**: Court must advise parties of instructions it intends to give before closing arguments
- **(C)**: After instructions, parties must state specific objections before jury retires; failure to object waives error on appeal
- **(D)**: Court must use Michigan Model Civil Jury Instructions (M Civ JI) where applicable; departure requires stating reasons on the record
- **(E)**: Jurors may take written instructions to deliberation room

### MCR 2.513 — Conduct of Jury Trial
- **(A)**: Jury selection, oath, and preliminary instructions
- **(B)**: Order of trial proceedings
- **(C)**: Juror questions — court may permit jurors to submit written questions
- **(D)**: Juror notebooks — court may provide notebooks for juror notes
- **(E)**: Final instructions given after closing arguments
- **(F)**: Deliberations — court must instruct on deliberation procedures
- **(G)**: Verdict forms — court provides appropriate verdict forms with instructions

### MCR 2.514 — Verdicts
- **(A)**: General and special verdicts
- **(B)**: General verdict with written interrogatories
- **(C)**: Inconsistent verdicts — procedures for handling

### MCR 2.516 — Jury Demand
- **(A)**: Jury demand must be filed with the first responsive pleading or within **28 days** of service
- **(B)**: Failure to demand constitutes waiver
- **(C)**: Jury fee must accompany the demand

### MCR 2.511 — Jurors
- **(A)**: Qualification and selection of jurors
- **(D)**: Challenges for cause (unlimited)
- **(E)**: Peremptory challenges (number varies by case type)
- **(F)**: Alternate jurors

### MCR 6.414 — Criminal Jury Instructions (cross-reference only)
- Criminal instructions use M Crim JI, NOT M Civ JI
- Different burden of proof (beyond reasonable doubt vs. preponderance)

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.1301 — Right to Jury Trial
- Jury trial as of right in civil actions at law
- Equity matters (including most custody disputes) are bench trials
- Mixed law/equity cases may have partial jury rights

### MCL 600.1320 — Jury Demand Timing
- Must be filed within statutory timeframe
- Failure to file timely demand waives right

### MCL 600.1332 — Number of Jurors
- Civil cases: 6 jurors unless parties stipulate otherwise
- Agreement for fewer jurors permitted

### MCL 600.1337 — Peremptory Challenges
- 3 peremptory challenges per side in civil cases (6-person jury)
- Additional challenges when multiple parties on a side

### MCL 600.2157 — Competency of Witnesses (Jury Context)
- Instructions on witness competency and credibility
- Dead Man's Statute implications for jury instructions

### MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors (No Jury Right)
- Custody determinations are equitable — no jury trial right
- Court makes findings on all 12 factors without jury

### MCL 600.6304 — Comparative Fault (If Applicable)
- Jury instructions on comparative negligence in tort claims
- Relevant if related tort claims are joined with family matter

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Jury Instruction Standards
- **Case v Consumers Power Co**, 463 Mich 1 (2000) — Jury instructions must fairly present the issues and applicable law; erroneous instructions that affect outcome require reversal
- **Jimkoski v Shoney's Inc**, 250 Mich App 335 (2002) — Court must use M Civ JI when applicable; departure requires stated reasons on the record
- **Heaton v Benton Construction Co**, 286 Mich App 528 (2009) — Jury instructions are read as a whole; a single imperfect instruction does not require reversal if the overall charge is accurate

### Preservation of Error
- **Hilgendorf v St John Hospital**, 245 Mich App 670 (2001) — Failure to object to jury instructions before jury retires waives issue for appeal; plain error review only
- **Elezovic v Ford Motor Co**, 472 Mich 408 (2005) — Specific objection required; general objection does not preserve the issue

### Special Instructions
- **Jaworski v Kiernan**, 241 Mich App 697 (2000) — Party is entitled to special instruction on their theory of the case when supported by the evidence

## Checklists

### Jury Instruction Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Determine whether jury trial right exists for each claim
- [ ] File jury demand within 28 days with jury fee (MCR 2.516)
- [ ] Identify applicable M Civ JI for each claim/defense
- [ ] Draft special instructions for case-specific theories
- [ ] Submit proposed instructions before charge conference deadline
- [ ] Review court's proposed charge before closing arguments
- [ ] State specific objections on record before jury retires

### Filing Deadlines
- Jury demand: **28 days** from service of first pleading (MCR 2.516(A))
- Proposed instructions: Per scheduling order; before charge conference
- Objections to instructions: Before jury retires to deliberate (MCR 2.512(C))
- Motion for new trial (instruction error): **21 days** after judgment (MCR 2.611)
