# Gotchas — Witness Preparation

## Anti-Rationalization Matrix

| Trap | What It Looks Like | Why It's Wrong | Correct Approach |
|------|-------------------|----------------|------------------|
| Coaching witnesses on what to say | Telling a witness "say it happened on Tuesday" or scripting specific testimony | Coaching is suborning perjury (MCL 750.424); witnesses must testify from their own memory and knowledge | Prepare witnesses on HOW to testify (courtroom procedure, question format, demeanor) — never on WHAT to say |
| Failing to prepare witnesses at all | Assuming witnesses will perform fine without any preparation | Unprepared witnesses give rambling, non-responsive, or damaging answers; they may volunteer harmful information or freeze under cross | Review anticipated questions; practice direct examination flow; explain courtroom procedures and expectations |
| Over-preparing: creating a scripted witness | Running through testimony so many times the witness sounds rehearsed | Judges and juries recognize rehearsed testimony; it appears manufactured and undermines credibility | Prepare witnesses on topics and key points, not scripted answers; practice answering unanticipated questions naturally |
| Not preparing for cross-examination | Only practicing direct examination without anticipating opposing counsel's attacks | Cross-examination is where cases are won or lost; an unprepared witness may make devastating admissions or become combative | Practice likely cross-examination questions; teach the witness to answer only what is asked, stay calm, and say "I don't know" when appropriate |
| Ignoring hostile or reluctant witnesses | Not subpoenaing witnesses who are necessary but unwilling to testify voluntarily | Critical evidence may be unavailable without their testimony; hoping they show up is not a strategy | Subpoena all necessary witnesses per MCR 2.506; prepare to establish hostile witness status under MRE 611(c) if needed |

## Failure Modes
1. **Witness Recantation on Stand**: Witness changes their story during testimony. Recovery: Impeach with prior inconsistent statement under MRE 613; have the prior statement ready to show the witness; request to treat as hostile under MRE 611(c).
2. **Volunteered Harmful Information**: Witness provides damaging information beyond the scope of the question. Recovery: On redirect, provide context and explanation; file motion to strike if the testimony was non-responsive; in future preparation, train witnesses to answer only the question asked.
3. **Witness Emotional Breakdown**: Witness becomes emotional and cannot continue testifying. Recovery: Request a brief recess; the court will typically accommodate; resume when the witness is composed. Prevention: discuss emotional topics during preparation so the witness processes them before trial.
4. **Expert Witness Credibility Attack**: Expert's methodology or qualifications challenged under Daubert/MRE 702. Recovery: Prepare the expert for qualification challenges; have CV and publications ready; prepare the expert to defend methodology in plain language.

## Integration Gotchas
- When combining with **litigation-trial-preparation-specialist**, witness preparation is step 2 AFTER trial strategy is set — you cannot prepare witnesses until you know which elements each witness must establish and in what order.
- Data flow from **litigation-subpoena-engine**: all witnesses who are not voluntary must be subpoenaed with fees tendered at least 14 days before trial — a prepared witness who doesn't appear is worthless.
- Michigan-specific pitfall: In Muskegon County family division, bench trials are common and the judge is the sole trier of fact. Witness preparation should focus on credibility and clarity rather than jury persuasion techniques. The judge will see through theatrical preparation — straightforward, honest testimony is far more effective than polished performance.
