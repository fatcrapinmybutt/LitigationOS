# Templates — Witness Preparation

## Witness Preparation Session Outline

```
WITNESS PREPARATION — [WITNESS NAME]
Trial Date: [DATE]
Preparation Date: [DATE]

I. INTRODUCTION
   - Explain your role in the case
   - Review courtroom layout and procedures
   - Discuss oath and obligation to tell the truth
   - Explain direct examination vs. cross-examination

II. COURTROOM RULES
   - Address the judge as "Your Honor"
   - Wait for the full question before answering
   - Answer only the question asked — do not volunteer
   - "I don't know" and "I don't remember" are acceptable
   - If you don't understand a question, ask for clarification
   - Stop speaking immediately if the judge or an attorney says "Objection"
   - Do not look at me for guidance — answer from your own knowledge
   - Remain calm and polite, especially during cross-examination

III. DIRECT EXAMINATION TOPICS
   - Topic 1: [What you will ask about]
     Key facts: [What the witness knows]
   - Topic 2: [What you will ask about]
     Key facts: [What the witness knows]
   - Topic 3: [What you will ask about]
     Key facts: [What the witness knows]

IV. ANTICIPATED CROSS-EXAMINATION
   - They may ask about: [topic]
     Your honest answer: [what the witness actually knows]
   - They may try to: [tactic — e.g., get you to speculate]
     Response: [e.g., "I can only tell you what I personally saw"]
   - They may challenge: [prior statement or inconsistency]
     Response: [e.g., acknowledge and explain]

V. DOCUMENTS
   - You may be shown Exhibit [X] — this is [describe]
   - If shown a document you don't recognize, say so
   - Read carefully before answering any question about a document

VI. FINAL REMINDERS
   - Tell the truth — always
   - Take your time — there is no rush
   - Stay calm — cross-examination is designed to rattle you
   - After you leave the stand, do not discuss your testimony
     with other witnesses (MRE 615)
```

## Direct Examination Outline Template

```
DIRECT EXAMINATION — [WITNESS NAME]
Purpose: Establish [specific elements/factors]

FOUNDATION:
Q: Please state your name.
Q: How do you know the Plaintiff / the parties?
Q: How long have you known them?

TOPIC 1 — [FACTOR/ELEMENT]:
Q: Were you present on [date]?
Q: What did you observe?
Q: Who else was present?
Q: What happened next?

TOPIC 2 — [FACTOR/ELEMENT]:
Q: Have you observed [specific subject]?
Q: How many times?
Q: Can you describe what you saw?

EXHIBIT INTRODUCTION:
Q: I am showing you what has been marked as Exhibit P-[X].
   Do you recognize this?
Q: What is it?
Q: How do you recognize it?
MOVE: Plaintiff offers Exhibit P-[X].
Q: [Question about exhibit content]

CONCLUSION:
Q: Is there anything else relevant to this matter that you
   would like to tell the Court?
```

## Cross-Examination Defense Preparation

| Expected Question | Honest Answer | Trap to Avoid | Coached Response |
|------------------|--------------|---------------|-----------------|
| "Isn't it true that..." | [Truthful answer] | Don't agree just to be agreeable | "That's not exactly right. What I saw was..." |
| "You don't really know, do you?" | [State what you do know] | Don't concede more than accurate | "I know what I personally observed." |
| "Didn't you say something different in your [statement]?" | [Acknowledge if accurate] | Don't deny documented statements | "Let me see the statement. [Review]. I may have said that, but what I recall is..." |
| "Are you being paid to testify?" | [Truthful answer] | Don't be defensive | "No, I received a witness fee as required by law." |

## Workflow: Witness Preparation Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Identify what each witness must establish
    ↓
Step 2: Review witness's prior statements and depositions
    ↓
Step 3: Schedule preparation session (ideally 1-2 weeks before trial)
    ↓
Step 4: Conduct preparation session (see outline above)
    ↓
Step 5: Practice direct examination flow (topics, not scripts)
    ↓
Step 6: Rehearse likely cross-examination scenarios
    ↓
Step 7: Review any exhibits the witness will identify/discuss
    ↓
Step 8: Confirm logistics: date, time, courtroom, parking
    ↓
Step 9: Day-before check: remind witness of time and location
    ↓
Step 10: Pre-trial briefing: review key points, calm nerves
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Scripting testimony | Sounds rehearsed; undermines credibility | Prepare on topics and key points, not exact words |
| Coaching on facts | Suborning perjury (MCL 750.424); felony | Only prepare on HOW to testify, never WHAT to say |
| No preparation at all | Witness rambles, volunteers harmful information | Always conduct at least one preparation session |
| Ignoring cross-exam prep | Witness falls apart under opposing questions | Anticipate and practice likely cross-exam questions |
| Not reviewing prior statements | Impeached with own prior inconsistent statement | Review all prior statements for consistency |
| Failing to subpoena | Witness doesn't show; testimony lost | Subpoena all necessary witnesses, even friendly ones |
