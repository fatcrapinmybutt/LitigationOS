# Michigan Rules — Witness Preparation

## Michigan Rules of Evidence (MRE)

### MRE 601 — General Rule of Competency
- Every person is competent to be a witness unless otherwise provided
- Court determines competency as a preliminary question under MRE 104

### MRE 602 — Lack of Personal Knowledge
- Witness may not testify unless evidence shows personal knowledge of the matter
- Foundation must be laid showing witness perceived the events they describe
- Exception: expert witnesses under MRE 703

### MRE 607 — Who May Impeach
- Any party may impeach any witness, including their own
- Opens door for using prior inconsistent statements

### MRE 608 — Evidence of Character and Conduct of Witness
- **(a)**: Credibility may be attacked/supported by opinion or reputation evidence for truthfulness
- **(b)**: Specific instances of conduct related to truthfulness — cross-examination only

### MRE 611 — Mode and Order of Interrogation
- **(a)**: Court exercises control over questioning to make it effective for truth, avoid wasting time, protect witnesses from harassment
- **(b)**: Cross-examination limited to scope of direct and credibility
- **(c)**: Leading questions:
  - Not allowed on direct examination (generally)
  - Allowed on cross-examination
  - May lead on direct when witness is hostile, adverse party, or identified with adverse party

### MRE 612 — Writing Used to Refresh Memory
- Witness may use a writing to refresh recollection while testifying
- Opposing party may inspect the writing, cross-examine, and introduce relevant portions
- Prepare witnesses for this — any notes reviewed may be discoverable

### MRE 613 — Prior Statements of Witnesses
- **(a)**: Examining witness about prior inconsistent statement — must first give witness opportunity to explain
- **(b)**: Extrinsic evidence of prior inconsistent statement admissible if witness given opportunity to explain

### MRE 615 — Exclusion of Witnesses ("The Rule")
- Court may order witnesses excluded from courtroom during other testimony
- Exception: party who is a natural person (cannot be excluded from own trial)
- Prevents witnesses from tailoring testimony based on what others say

### MRE 701 — Lay Witness Opinion
- Lay witness may give opinion rationally based on perception and helpful to understanding
- Must be based on personal observation, not speculation

### MRE 702 — Expert Witness Testimony
- Expert testimony permitted if specialized knowledge assists the trier of fact
- Daubert standard applies in Michigan (Craig v Oakwood Hospital)
- Expert must be qualified by knowledge, skill, experience, training, or education

### MRE 703 — Bases of Expert Opinion
- Expert may base opinion on facts perceived, admitted in evidence, or made known at trial
- Underlying data need not be independently admissible if reasonably relied upon

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.506 — Subpoenas for Witnesses
- **(A)**: Court-issued subpoenas compel attendance
- **(D)**: Witness fee ($12/day) and mileage required at time of service
- **(E)**: Contempt for failure to comply

### MCR 2.302(B)(4) — Expert Witness Discovery
- Expert reports must be disclosed per scheduling order
- Depositions of experts permitted
- Expert preparation materials may be discoverable

### MCR 2.401(B) — Pretrial Witness Disclosure
- Witness lists must be exchanged per scheduling order
- Witnesses not disclosed may be excluded from testifying

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.2157 — Physician-Patient Privilege
- Treating physicians cannot testify about privileged communications without waiver
- Prepare medical witnesses on privilege limitations

### MCL 600.2163a — Child Witness Competency
- **(1)**: Child witness must be able to describe events, distinguish truth from lies
- **(2)**: Court determines competency
- **(3)**: Special accommodations may be ordered (support person, closed-circuit TV)

### MCL 750.424 — Subornation of Perjury
- Procuring or inducing false testimony is a felony
- Up to life imprisonment
- Witness preparation must NEVER cross into coaching on what facts to state

### MCL 750.423 — Perjury
- Material false statement under oath is a felony
- Witnesses must understand the consequences of untruthful testimony

### MCL 750.539c — Recording Conversations (One-Party Consent)
- Michigan allows one-party consent for recordings
- Relevant when recording preparation sessions (not recommended)

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Witness Preparation Standards
- **People v Rose**, 289 Mich App 499 (2010) — Attorneys may prepare witnesses by reviewing anticipated questions and areas of inquiry; this does not constitute improper coaching
- **Craig v Oakwood Hospital**, 471 Mich 67 (2004) — Michigan adopted Daubert standard for expert witness qualification and methodology

### Impeachment
- **People v Layher**, 464 Mich 756 (2001) — Prior inconsistent statements are powerful impeachment tools; witnesses must be prepared for confrontation with prior statements
- **People v Solmonson**, 261 Mich App 657 (2004) — Witness credibility determinations are left to the trier of fact

### Witness Exclusion
- **People v Meconi**, 277 Mich App 651 (2008) — Sequestration (MRE 615) prevents witnesses from hearing each other's testimony; violation may result in exclusion or contempt
- **In re Archer**, 325 Mich App 227 (2018) — Party witness cannot be excluded under MRE 615; but other witnesses must comply

## Checklists

### Witness Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Identify what this witness must establish (which elements/factors)
- [ ] Review witness's prior statements, depositions, and affidavits
- [ ] Prepare direct examination outline (topics, not scripts)
- [ ] Anticipate cross-examination questions and prepare witness
- [ ] Explain courtroom procedures (oath, judge, objections, breaks)
- [ ] Practice key direct examination questions
- [ ] Instruct: answer only the question asked; don't volunteer
- [ ] Instruct: "I don't know" and "I don't remember" are acceptable answers
- [ ] Ensure witness understands MRE 615 (cannot discuss testimony with other witnesses)
- [ ] Subpoena served with fees if witness is not voluntary
- [ ] Review any documents the witness may be asked about

### Expert Witness Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Review and update CV
- [ ] Prepare for qualification examination
- [ ] Review report and all opinions to be offered
- [ ] Prepare for Daubert challenge on methodology
- [ ] Anticipate cross-examination on qualifications and opinions
- [ ] Practice explaining complex concepts in plain language
- [ ] Review all materials relied upon (may be discoverable)
- [ ] Confirm disclosure was made per scheduling order

### Filing Deadlines
- Witness list disclosure: Per scheduling order
- Expert report disclosure: Per scheduling order
- Subpoena service: **14+ days** before trial (recommended)
- Witness fee tender: At time of service (MCR 2.506(D))
