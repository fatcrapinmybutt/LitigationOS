# Gotchas — Guardian Ad Litem Specialist

## Anti-Rationalization Matrix

| Trap | What It Looks Like | Why It's Wrong | Correct Approach |
|------|-------------------|----------------|------------------|
| Treating GAL as neutral | Assuming GAL recommendations are unbiased because they are court-appointed | GALs can have conflicts of interest, limited investigation time, or bias from ex parte contact with one party | Scrutinize GAL methodology, document all contacts, challenge inadequate investigation under MCR 3.916 |
| Failing to object to GAL scope | Allowing GAL to exceed their appointed role without challenge | GAL authority is limited to the court's appointment order; exceeding scope violates due process | File motion to clarify/limit GAL role; object on record when GAL acts beyond appointment order |
| Accepting GAL report at face value | Not requesting underlying interview notes, records reviewed, or contact logs | GAL reports may omit contradictory evidence or reflect incomplete investigation | Subpoena GAL file; depose GAL; cross-examine on methodology and completeness |
| Missing objection deadlines | Failing to file timely objections to GAL report before hearing | Under MCR 3.916(C), objections must be filed before the hearing or the report may be admitted without challenge | Calendar the report receipt date; file written objections within the court-specified timeframe |
| Confusing GAL with attorney for child | Treating GAL and Lawyer-Guardian Ad Litem (LGAL) as interchangeable | GAL investigates and reports to court; LGAL represents the child's legal interests — different roles under MCL 722.24 | Identify which role was appointed; tailor challenges to the specific role and its statutory duties |

## Failure Modes
1. **Inadequate Investigation Challenge**: GAL spent minimal time, interviewed only one parent's witnesses. Recovery: File motion to strike GAL report under MCR 3.916; request new GAL or supplemental investigation citing specific deficiencies.
2. **Ex Parte Communication Exposure**: GAL had undisclosed communications with opposing party or counsel. Recovery: Subpoena GAL phone records and email; file motion to disqualify GAL and strike report; request evidentiary hearing.
3. **Best Interest Factor Mismatch**: GAL report does not address all 12 best interest factors under MCL 722.23. Recovery: File objection identifying missing factors; argue report is incomplete and cannot support judicial findings on all factors.
4. **Fee Dispute Escalation**: GAL fees become excessive or are allocated unfairly. Recovery: File motion to reallocate fees under MCL 722.24(3); request itemized billing; object to charges for work outside appointment scope.

## Integration Gotchas
- When combining with **litigation-motion-practice**, ensure motions to strike GAL reports cite both MCR 3.916 procedural deficiencies AND MCL 722.23 substantive best-interest-factor gaps — courts require both.
- Data flow from **litigation-timeline-forensics** must feed GAL contact logs — if your timeline shows GAL met with opposing party 5 times but you only once, that asymmetry is your strongest challenge argument.
- Michigan-specific pitfall: In Muskegon County 14th Circuit, GAL recommendations carry heavy weight with the bench. A pro se litigant who fails to file written objections before the hearing effectively waives the right to challenge — there is no "I'll object at trial" fallback in family division practice.
