## Objection to GAL Report

```
OBJECTION TO GUARDIAN AD LITEM REPORT

    Plaintiff objects to the Guardian Ad Litem report filed [DATE]:

PROCEDURAL DEFICIENCIES:
    1. The report fails to comply with MCR 3.916(B) because [specify].
    2. The GAL did not [interview both parties / visit both homes / etc.].

SUBSTANTIVE DEFICIENCIES:
    3. The report fails to address best interest factor(s) [list factors]
       under MCL 722.23.
    4. The GAL's finding on factor [X] is not supported by the evidence.
    5. The GAL relied on [inadmissible hearsay / unverified claims].

REQUESTED RELIEF:
    a. The Court strike the GAL report or give it diminished weight;
    b. The Court order a supplemental investigation;
    c. Alternatively, the Court appoint a new GAL.
```

## GAL Cross-Examination Outline

### Investigation Methodology
1. How many hours did you spend on this investigation?
2. How many times did you meet with each parent?
3. Did you visit both parents' homes? If not, why?
4. What records did you review?
5. Who were your collateral contacts?

### Best Interest Factor Coverage
6. Which of the 12 factors under MCL 722.23 did you specifically investigate?
7. For factor [X], what evidence did you rely on?
8. Did you consider [contradictory evidence]?

### Potential Bias
9. How many times did you communicate with opposing counsel?
10. Were there any ex parte communications with either party?
11. Have you worked with opposing counsel on prior cases?

### Report Accuracy
12. Is every factual statement based on personal observation or verified records?
13. Are there corrections or additions you would make today?

## Workflow: Challenging a GAL Recommendation

```
Step 1: Receive GAL report
    ↓
Step 2: Catalog all 12 factors — mark addressed/unaddressed
    ↓
Step 3: Compare GAL factual claims against your evidence
    ↓
Step 4: Identify discrepancies, omissions, unsupported conclusions
    ↓
Step 5: Subpoena GAL's complete file (notes, emails, call logs)
    ↓
Step 6: File written objections (BEFORE hearing date)
    ↓
Step 7: Prepare cross-examination (methodology → bias → accuracy)
    ↓
Step 8: Present independent evidence on contested factors
    ↓
Step 9: Argue court must make independent findings (Dehring standard)
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Attacking GAL personally | Alienates court; appears hostile | Focus on methodology and completeness |
| Ignoring favorable findings | Misses reinforcement opportunity | Embrace favorable; challenge only adverse |
| Waiving cross-examination | Loses chance to expose flaws on record | Always cross-examine, even briefly |
| Filing late objections | Court may refuse to consider them | Calendar deadline immediately |
| Requesting new GAL without cause | Courts rarely grant absent clear conflict | Build record showing bias or inadequacy first |
