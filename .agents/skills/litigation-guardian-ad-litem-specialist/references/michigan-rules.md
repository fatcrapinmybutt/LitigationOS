# Michigan Rules — Guardian Ad Litem Specialist

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 3.916 — Guardian Ad Litem / Lawyer-Guardian Ad Litem
- **(A) Appointment**: Court may appoint GAL or LGAL to represent the child's best interests
- **(B) Duties**: GAL must investigate, interview parties and child, review relevant records, and file written report
- **(C) Report Filing**: GAL report must be filed with court and served on parties sufficiently in advance of hearing to allow review and objection
- **(D) Cross-Examination**: Parties have the right to cross-examine the GAL on methodology and findings
- **(E) Fees**: Court determines allocation of GAL fees between parties

### MCR 3.904 — Definitions (Child Protective Proceedings)
- Defines GAL vs. LGAL roles in protective proceedings
- LGAL must be an attorney; GAL need not be

### MCR 3.915 — Hearings and Trials
- GAL report is evidence but not dispositive
- Court must make independent best interest findings under MCL 722.23

### MCR 3.210 — Domestic Relations Actions: Mediation
- GAL involvement in mediation contexts
- Confidentiality limitations when GAL is also mediator (prohibited dual role)

### MCR 2.507 — Referee Hearings
- GAL participation in referee hearings
- Objections to referee recommendations when GAL testified

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 722.24 — Child Custody: Investigation and Report
- **(1)**: Court may order investigation and report concerning custodial arrangements
- **(2)**: Investigator shall consult with parties, observe child with each parent, and investigate relevant information
- **(3)**: Court may allocate investigation costs between the parties
- **(4)**: Report must address the best interest factors of MCL 722.23

### MCL 722.23 — Best Interest of the Child Factors
The 12 factors a GAL must address:
1. (a) Love, affection, emotional ties
2. (b) Capacity to provide love, affection, guidance
3. (c) Capacity to provide food, clothing, medical care
4. (d) Length of time in stable environment
5. (e) Permanence of family unit
6. (f) Moral fitness of parties
7. (g) Mental and physical health of parties
8. (h) Home, school, community record
9. (i) Reasonable preference of child (if sufficient age)
10. (j) Willingness to facilitate relationship with other parent
11. (k) Domestic violence
12. (l) Any other relevant factor

### MCL 722.27 — Modification of Custody Orders
- GAL may be re-appointed for modification proceedings
- Proper cause or change of circumstances threshold applies

### MCL 722.27b — Parenting Time
- GAL role in parenting time disputes
- Supervised parenting time recommendations

### MCL 712A.17d — LGAL Duties (Child Protective)
- LGAL must make an independent investigation
- Must determine child's wishes and report to court

## Key Michigan Case Law

### GAL Investigation Standards
- **Dehring v Dehring**, 220 Mich App 163 (1996) — Court cannot adopt GAL recommendation without making independent findings on each best interest factor
- **Daniels v Daniels**, 165 Mich App 726 (1988) — GAL report is one piece of evidence; court must weigh it against all other evidence
- **Grew v Knox**, 265 Mich App 333 (2005) — Parties have due process right to challenge GAL findings through cross-examination

### GAL Fee Allocation
- **Kosch v Kosch**, 233 Mich App 346 (1999) — Court must consider parties' respective financial positions when allocating GAL fees
- **Stallworth v Stallworth**, 275 Mich App 282 (2007) — GAL fee allocation is reviewed for abuse of discretion

### GAL Scope and Limitations
- **Brown v Brown**, 332 Mich App 1 (2020) — Trial court erred by relying on GAL opinion on legal questions rather than factual investigation
- **Berger v Berger**, 277 Mich App 700 (2008) — GAL must provide sufficient detail to allow meaningful cross-examination

## Checklists

### GAL Report Review Checklist
- [ ] Report addresses all 12 best interest factors (MCL 722.23(a)-(l))
- [ ] Sources of information are identified (who was interviewed, what records reviewed)
- [ ] Contact log provided (dates, duration, participants for each interaction)
- [ ] Child's stated preferences documented (if age-appropriate)
- [ ] Both parents' homes visited
- [ ] Collateral contacts made (teachers, doctors, therapists)
- [ ] Recommendation is supported by factual findings (not conclusory)
- [ ] Report filed within court-ordered timeframe

### Objection to GAL Report — Decision Tree
1. Was the report timely served on all parties? → If NO: Motion to strike for untimely service
2. Does the report address all 12 factors? → If NO: Objection for incompleteness
3. Did GAL interview both parties? → If NO: Objection for inadequate investigation
4. Did GAL visit both homes? → If NO: Objection for incomplete assessment
5. Are there undisclosed ex parte contacts? → If YES: Motion to disqualify GAL
6. Are fees reasonable and properly allocated? → If NO: Motion to reallocate fees

### Filing Deadlines
- Objections to GAL report: Must be filed before the scheduled hearing (MCR 3.916(C))
- Motion to appoint GAL: May be filed at any time during custody proceedings
- Motion to disqualify GAL: File immediately upon discovering disqualifying conflict
- GAL fee objections: Must be raised before fee order becomes final (21-day appeal window)
