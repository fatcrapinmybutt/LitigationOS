# Michigan Rules — Void Judgment Engine

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.612 — Relief from Judgment or Order
- **(C)(1)**: Grounds for relief:
  - **(a)**: Mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect
  - **(b)**: Newly discovered evidence
  - **(c)**: Fraud, misrepresentation, or other misconduct
  - **(d)**: **The judgment is void** — NO TIME LIMIT
  - **(e)**: The judgment has been satisfied, released, or discharged
  - **(f)**: Any other reason justifying relief from the operation of the judgment
- **(C)(2)**: Motions under (a), (b), (c) must be filed within **1 year**; motions under (d), (e), (f) within a "reasonable time"
- **(C)(3)**: This rule does NOT limit a court's power to: (a) entertain an independent action to relieve from judgment, (b) set aside a judgment for fraud on the court, (c) set aside a void judgment
- **(D)**: Writs abolished — the rule provides adequate remedies

### MCR 2.116(C)(1) — Lack of Subject Matter Jurisdiction
- Subject matter jurisdiction defect renders judgment void
- Can be raised at any time, including on appeal
- Never waivable by the parties

### MCR 2.116(C)(2) — Lack of Personal Jurisdiction
- Personal jurisdiction defect renders judgment void as to that party
- Must be raised promptly; can be waived by participation in proceedings
- Distinguished from venue (waivable) vs. jurisdiction (may or may not be waivable)

### MCR 2.105 — Service of Process
- **(A)**: Proper methods of service on individuals
- **(B)**: Service on corporations, partnerships, government
- **(D)**: Substituted service when personal service cannot be made
- **(J)**: Service by publication — last resort, strict requirements
- Defective service may deprive court of personal jurisdiction → void judgment

### MCR 2.603 — Default and Default Judgment
- **(A)**: Entry of default
- **(B)**: Setting aside default — if judgment entered without proper service, it is void
- **(D)**: Default judgment entered without jurisdiction is void ab initio

### MCR 7.204(A) — Appeal from Void Judgment
- Appeal of right from final judgment within **21 days**
- But void judgment may also be attacked collaterally at any time

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.701 — Circuit Court Jurisdiction
- Subject matter jurisdiction of circuit courts
- Family division jurisdiction under MCL 600.1021

### MCL 600.701-703 — Jurisdiction Defined
- **(701)**: Circuit courts have original jurisdiction in all civil claims except as limited by law
- **(703)**: Subject matter jurisdiction cannot be conferred by consent; absence renders proceedings void

### MCL 600.1905 — Service of Process Requirements
- Statutory service requirements for establishing personal jurisdiction
- Failure to comply may render judgment void for lack of personal jurisdiction

### MCL 552.6 — Divorce Jurisdiction
- Residency requirements: **180 days** in state, **10 days** in county
- Failure to meet residency requirements = lack of jurisdiction = void judgment

### MCL 552.9 — Domestic Relations Venue and Jurisdiction
- Venue and jurisdiction requirements for divorce/custody
- Jurisdictional defects distinguish void from merely irregular proceedings

### MCL 600.2932 — Collateral Attack on Judgments
- Void judgments may be collaterally attacked at any time
- Voidable judgments must be attacked directly (motion or appeal)

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Void vs. Voidable Distinction
- **Bowie v Arder**, 441 Mich 23 (1992) — A judgment is void only if the rendering court lacked subject matter jurisdiction, lacked personal jurisdiction, or acted in a manner inconsistent with due process; all other defects make judgment voidable, not void
- **Barrow v Barrow**, 327 Mich App 338 (2019) — Reaffirms that void judgment can be attacked at any time; does not require motion within any specific timeframe under MCR 2.612(C)(1)(d)

### Lack of Jurisdiction
- **Altman v Nelson**, 197 Mich App 467 (1992) — Judgment entered without personal jurisdiction is void and may be set aside at any time
- **In re Hatcher**, 443 Mich 426 (1993) — Subject matter jurisdiction is a prerequisite to any valid judicial action; its absence renders all proceedings void

### Service of Process Defects
- **Krueger v Williams**, 410 Mich 144 (1981) — Defective service of process deprives court of personal jurisdiction; resulting judgment is void
- **Stott v Meridian Mut Ins Co**, 200 Mich App 540 (1993) — Service substantially complying with the rule may be sufficient; technical defects do not always void the judgment

### Fraud on the Court
- **Averill v Dauterman**, 284 Mich App 18 (2009) — Fraud on the court is an independent basis for vacating judgment with no time limit; requires showing of fraud that prevented fair adjudication
- **Heugel v Heugel**, 237 Mich App 471 (1999) — Concealment of assets in divorce constitutes fraud justifying relief

### Collateral Attack
- **Laird v Laird**, 115 Mich App 227 (1982) — Void judgment subject to collateral attack in any proceeding; parties need not wait for direct challenge
- **Sprague v Buhagiar**, 213 Mich App 310 (1995) — Burden on party attacking judgment to prove jurisdictional defect

## Checklists

### Void Judgment Analysis Checklist
- [ ] Identify the alleged defect in the judgment
- [ ] Classify: void (jurisdictional) or voidable (non-jurisdictional)
- [ ] If void — verify one of three grounds:
  - [ ] Lack of subject matter jurisdiction
  - [ ] Lack of personal jurisdiction (service defect)
  - [ ] Action in excess of jurisdiction / due process violation
- [ ] Gather evidence of the jurisdictional defect
- [ ] Determine remedy: motion under MCR 2.612(C)(1)(d) or independent action
- [ ] Address collateral consequences of vacating the judgment
- [ ] File motion with supporting brief and evidence

### Void vs. Voidable Decision Tree
1. Was there a defect in service of process? → Void (personal jurisdiction)
2. Did the court lack authority over this type of case? → Void (subject matter jurisdiction)
3. Did the court exceed its jurisdictional authority? → Void (excess of jurisdiction)
4. Was there a legal error in applying the law? → Voidable (not void)
5. Was there a factual error? → Voidable (not void)
6. Was there fraud by a party? → Voidable under (c); fraud on the court may be independent

### Filing Deadlines
- Void judgment (MCR 2.612(C)(1)(d)): **No time limit** — but "reasonable time" expected
- Fraud on the court: **No time limit** (independent action under MCR 2.612(C)(3))
- Voidable judgment (MCR 2.612(C)(1)(a)-(c)): **1 year** from judgment
- Other grounds (MCR 2.612(C)(1)(f)): Within a "reasonable time"
- Appeal: **21 days** from judgment entry (MCR 7.204(A))
