# Brief Structure — Michigan Appellate Brief Reference

## MCR 7.212 Brief Requirements

### Required Sections (in order)

Per MCR 7.212(C), an appellant's brief must contain:

#### 1. Table of Contents
- List all sections with page numbers
- List all issues presented with page references
- Must be accurate — COA clerk verifies

#### 2. Index of Authorities
- All cases cited, alphabetically, with page references
- All statutes cited, numerically, with page references
- All court rules cited with page references
- All secondary sources with page references
- Format: Authority name — page(s) where cited

#### 3. Jurisdictional Statement
Per MCR 7.212(C)(1):
```
Statement of jurisdiction:
- Basis for appellate jurisdiction
- Date of judgment/order appealed
- Date of any post-judgment motions
- Date of claim of appeal filing
- Whether timely under MCR 7.204(A) (21 days for claim of appeal)
```

#### 4. Statement of Questions Presented
Per MCR 7.212(C)(4):
- Each issue stated as a question answerable yes or no
- Each question immediately followed by the trial court's answer
- Each question immediately followed by the appellant's answer
- Format:
```
I. Did the trial court abuse its discretion in [specific ruling]?

   Trial court's answer: No.
   Appellant's answer: Yes.
```

#### 5. Statement of Facts
Per MCR 7.212(C)(6):
- Clear, concise statement of material facts
- EVERY factual assertion must cite the record
- Citation format: (Tr Vol II, p 45) or (Ex 12, p 3) or (Register of Actions, Entry 45)
- Present facts favorably but accurately — do not misrepresent the record
- Include procedural history relevant to appeal

#### 6. Argument
Per MCR 7.212(C)(7):
- Each issue under a separate heading
- Standard of review stated for each issue
- Legal argument with pinpoint authority citations
- Application of law to facts with record citations
- Request for specific relief

#### 7. Relief Requested
Per MCR 7.212(C)(9):
- Specific statement of relief sought
- "Wherefore, Appellant requests this Court..."
- Must specify: reversal, remand, modification, or other specific relief

#### 8. Appendix
Per MCR 7.212(C)(8):
Required contents:
- [ ] Judgment or order appealed from
- [ ] Any written opinion of the trial court
- [ ] Register of actions
- [ ] Relevant portions of the record not in the transcript
- [ ] Any order granting/denying leave to appeal

### Page and Format Requirements

| Requirement | Rule | Specification |
|-------------|------|---------------|
| Maximum pages | MCR 7.212(B) | 50 pages (argument section) |
| Font | MCR 7.212(B) | 12-point proportional or equivalent |
| Margins | MCR 7.212(B) | 1-inch minimum all sides |
| Spacing | MCR 7.212(B) | Double-spaced (footnotes may be single) |
| Cover | MCR 7.212(A) | Blue for appellant, red for appellee |
| Copies | MCR 7.212(A) | Original + copies per COA requirements |
| Filing deadline | MCR 7.212(A)(1) | 56 days after claim of appeal or transcript |

### Standard of Review by Issue Type

#### Custody-Specific Standards (Lane A — COA 366810)

| Issue Type | Standard | Authority | Burden |
|------------|----------|-----------|--------|
| Best-interest findings | Great weight | MCL 722.28 | Clear error — findings against great weight of evidence |
| Change of circumstances | Abuse of discretion | MCL 722.27(1)(c) | Palpable abuse |
| Legal interpretation | De novo | General appellate standard | Correctness |
| Procedural rulings | Abuse of discretion | MCR 7.216(A)(7) | Outside range of reasonable outcomes |
| Evidentiary rulings | Abuse of discretion | MRE 103(a) | Substantial rights affected |
| Due process claims | De novo | US Const Amend XIV | Constitutional question |
| Judicial disqualification | Abuse of discretion | MCR 2.003(D) | Reasonable person standard |

### Brief-Writing Best Practices for Pro Se Appellants

1. **Lead with your strongest issue** — judges pay most attention to the first argument
2. **State the standard of review explicitly** — don't make the court guess
3. **Cite the record for every fact** — uncited facts will be ignored
4. **Use headings and subheadings** — make the brief scannable
5. **Keep sentences short** — complex sentences obscure clear arguments
6. **Address counterarguments** — acknowledge and distinguish opposing authority
7. **Request specific relief** — "reverse and remand" is better than "grant relief"
8. **Proofread citations** — wrong page numbers destroy credibility
9. **Respect page limits** — going over gets your brief rejected
10. **File on time** — late filing = dismissal under MCR 7.212(A)(1)
