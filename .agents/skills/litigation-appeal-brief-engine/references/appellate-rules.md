# Appellate Rules — Michigan Court of Appeals Reference

## Jurisdiction and Filing

### MCR 7.203 — Jurisdiction of the Court of Appeals
- **MCR 7.203(A)**: Appeal of right from final judgments and certain interlocutory orders
- **MCR 7.203(B)**: Appeal by leave from orders not appealable by right
- **MCR 7.203(C)**: Original proceedings (superintending control, habeas corpus)

### MCR 7.204 — Filing Requirements (Appeal of Right)
- **MCR 7.204(A)**: Claim of appeal must be filed within 21 days of entry of judgment
- **MCR 7.204(A)(2)**: If post-judgment motion filed, 21 days from order deciding last motion
- **MCR 7.204(B)**: Claim filed with COA clerk AND served on all parties
- **MCR 7.204(D)**: Filing fee required ($375 as of 2024; fee waiver available)

### MCR 7.205 — Application for Leave to Appeal
- **MCR 7.205(A)**: Filed within 21 days of order or judgment
- **MCR 7.205(B)**: Must include: questions presented, statement of facts, argument, relief requested
- **MCR 7.205(E)**: If leave granted, treated as appeal of right from that point

## Brief Requirements

### MCR 7.212 — Briefs (Complete Requirements)

#### MCR 7.212(A) — Filing Deadlines
| Brief Type | Deadline | Extension |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Appellant's brief | 56 days after claim of appeal or transcript filing | MCR 7.212(A)(4) motion required |
| Appellee's brief | 35 days after service of appellant's brief | MCR 7.212(A)(2) |
| Reply brief | 21 days after service of appellee's brief | MCR 7.212(A)(3) |

#### MCR 7.212(B) — Format Requirements
- Maximum 50 pages (exclusive of tables, appendix)
- 12-point type minimum
- Double-spaced (footnotes may be single-spaced)
- 1-inch margins minimum
- Pages numbered consecutively

#### MCR 7.212(C) — Content Requirements
Detailed in brief-structure.md reference.

### MCR 7.215 — Opinions and Orders

#### MCR 7.215(C)(1) — Unpublished Opinions
- NOT precedentially binding
- May be cited as persuasive authority
- MUST be identified as unpublished when cited
- Format: *Name v Name*, unpublished per curiam opinion of the Court of Appeals, issued [date] (Docket No. [number])

#### MCR 7.215(E)(1) — Conflict Resolution
- When two published COA opinions conflict, the first controls
- Exception: special panel convened under MCR 7.215(J) to resolve conflict

#### MCR 7.215(J) — Special Panel
- Required when panel disagrees with prior published COA opinion
- Must follow prior published opinion OR convene special panel

## Record on Appeal

### MCR 7.210 — Record on Appeal
- **MCR 7.210(A)(1)**: Record consists of original papers filed in trial court, transcript, and exhibits
- **MCR 7.210(B)(1)**: Appellant responsible for ordering transcript within 14 days of filing claim
- **MCR 7.210(B)(3)**: If transcript unavailable, appellant may prepare statement of proceedings
- **MCR 7.210(H)**: Appellant responsible for ensuring complete record; issues not supported by the record cannot be reviewed

### Transcript Requirements
| Hearing Type | Transcript Required? | Notes |
|-------------|---------------------|-------|
| Evidentiary hearing | YES — mandatory | Must include all testimony |
| Motion hearing | YES if argued | Record of arguments and ruling |
| Settlement conference | Usually NO | Confidential under MCR 2.403 |
| Bench trial | YES — mandatory | Complete transcript required |
| Jury trial | YES — mandatory | All proceedings on record |

## Michigan Supreme Court (if further appeal)

### MCR 7.302 — Application for Leave to Appeal to Supreme Court
- **MCR 7.302(B)**: Filed within 42 days of COA decision
- **MCR 7.302(B)(3)**: Must specify why Supreme Court review is warranted
- Grounds for leave:
  - Conflict with Supreme Court or other COA decisions
  - Significant public interest
  - Important question of state/federal law
  - Need for supervision of lower courts

### MCR 7.305 — Supreme Court Proceedings
- If leave granted, full briefing schedule set
- Oral argument typically scheduled
- Supreme Court review is de novo on legal questions

## Pro Se Appellate Practice Notes

### Key Advantages
- Michigan courts construe pro se filings liberally: *Estelle v Gamble*, 429 US 97 (1976)
- No requirement to hire attorney for appeal
- Fee waivers available under MCR 2.002

### Key Risks
- Same substantive standards apply — liberal construction does not change the law
- Technical defects can result in dismissal
- Deadlines are strictly enforced — no extensions for being pro se
- Must comply with all MCR 7.212 formatting requirements

### Critical Deadlines for COA 366810 (Pigors v Watson)
- Monitor all deadlines via `litigation-filing-countdown` agent
- 21-day claim of appeal deadline is jurisdictional — missing it = no appeal
- 56-day brief deadline runs from transcript filing, not claim of appeal
- Extensions require motion with good cause BEFORE deadline expires
- Late filings require motion to accept late filing with explanation
