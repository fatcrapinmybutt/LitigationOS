# GOTCHAS — ANTI-RATIONALIZATION TABLES

## PURPOSE

This file exists to PREVENT rationalization of errors. Every table below documents
a real failure mode in MSC practice. If you are tempted to skip a step, CHECK HERE FIRST.

---

## TABLE 1 — JURISDICTION ERRORS

| Gotcha | Rule | What Goes Wrong | How to Prevent |
|--------|------|-----------------|----------------|
| Filing in MSC before COA decides | MCR 7.305(C) | MSC dismisses — no jurisdiction without COA final order (unless bypass) | Confirm COA has entered final order before filing leave application |
| Assuming MSC has original jurisdiction over private disputes | MCR 7.303 | MSC original jurisdiction is LIMITED — constitutional officers, superintending control, specific constitutional matters | Verify the respondent/matter falls within Art 6 §4 scope |
| Confusing COA interlocutory order with final order | MCR 7.302(A) | Interlocutory COA orders are NOT "decisions" triggering 56-day clock unless they dispose of the entire case | Check whether COA order is final disposition or interlocutory |
| Filing bypass without showing extraordinary circumstances | MCR 7.305(B)(3) | MSC denies — bypass requires showing COA delay causes irreparable harm AND issue is of major significance | Draft bypass petition with explicit irreparable-harm argument |
| Treating superintending control as ordinary appeal | MCR 7.306 | MSC denies — superintending control requires showing NO adequate legal remedy exists | Exhaust all ordinary remedies before seeking writ |
| Filing habeas in MSC without exhausting state remedies | MCR 7.306(B)(3) | MSC dismisses — must first exhaust trial court and COA remedies | Document full exhaustion chain before filing |
| Assuming MSC must accept certified questions | MCR 7.308 | MSC has DISCRETION — may decline certified questions from federal courts | Prepare for possibility MSC declines certification |
| Filing motion for rehearing without new grounds | MCR 7.309 | MSC denies — rehearing requires material error or significant omission, not mere disagreement | Identify specific factual or legal error in MSC decision |

## TABLE 2 — DEADLINE TRAPS

| Gotcha | Rule | Deadline | Consequence of Missing |
|--------|------|----------|----------------------|
| 56-day leave application deadline | MCR 7.305(C)(2) | 56 days from COA decision date | JURISDICTIONAL — MSC cannot accept late application without showing fraud/irregularity |
| Counting from wrong date | MCR 7.305(C)(2) | Runs from DATE OF DECISION, not date of receipt or date motion for reconsideration denied | Verify exact date on COA order |
| COA motion for reconsideration does NOT toll MSC deadline | MCR 7.305(C)(2)(a) | If you file motion for reconsideration in COA, 56 days runs from THAT ruling | Track both dates — original order AND reconsideration ruling |
| 21-day rehearing deadline | MCR 7.309(B) | 21 days from MSC order | JURISDICTIONAL — no extensions absent extraordinary circumstances |
| Extension of time — must file BEFORE deadline expires | MCR 7.311(F) | Must file motion for extension before original deadline | Calendar the deadline and file extension motion early |
| Cross-application deadline | MCR 7.305(C)(3) | 28 days after applicant's application is filed | Opposing party has separate clock — track both |
| Response deadline | MCR 7.305(C)(4) | 28 days after application served | Must serve answer within 28 days or waive right to respond |
| Reply brief deadline | MCR 7.305(C)(5) | 14 days after answer is served | Optional but must be timely if filed |
| Stay pending application | MCR 7.311(E) | Must be filed promptly — no fixed deadline but delay undercuts urgency argument | File stay motion simultaneously with or immediately after leave application |

## TABLE 3 — FORM ERRORS

| Gotcha | Rule | Error | Fix |
|--------|------|-------|-----|
| Wrong number of copies | MCR 7.302(B) | Filing rejected — clerk returns | Check current clerk requirements — typically original + copies for all parties |
| Missing cover page | MCR 7.212(A) | Non-compliant — may be rejected | Include cover page with case caption, filing type, lower court info |
| Missing TOC or TOA | MCR 7.212(C)(2)–(3) | Non-compliant — brief rejected | Generate TOC and TOA for any brief exceeding 5 pages |
| Wrong caption format | MCR 7.212(A) | Confuses clerk — potential misfiling | Use MSC caption format: MSC case number, party names, lower court numbers |
| Missing statement of jurisdiction | MCR 7.212(C)(5) | Court cannot confirm jurisdiction — may dismiss | Include explicit jurisdictional statement citing MCR 7.305 and Const Art 6 §4 |
| Missing questions presented | MCR 7.212(C)(4) | FATAL — MSC reviews by QUESTION, not by case | Draft precise, answerable questions presented — this is the most important section |
| No certificate of service | MCR 2.107(C) | Filing not effective — service defective | Attach certificate of service to every filed document |
| Fee not included | MCR 7.302(B) | Filing held — not processed until fee paid or waiver granted | Include $375 fee or MC 20 fee waiver with filing |
| Appendix missing required items | MCR 7.212(C)(8) | Court lacks necessary record — may dismiss | Include COA opinion, trial court orders, key transcript excerpts |
| Exceeding page limits | MCR 7.212(B) | Brief rejected or portions stricken | Verify: application brief ≤ 50 pages; reply ≤ 10 pages |

## TABLE 4 — RECORD FAILURES

| Gotcha | Consequence | Prevention |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Transcript not ordered from court reporter | MSC has nothing to review — claims of trial error unsupported | Order transcripts IMMEDIATELY after trial court proceedings |
| Transcript not filed in COA | COA record incomplete — MSC cannot review what COA didn't have | Verify transcript is part of COA record before filing MSC application |
| Exhibits not included in record | MSC cannot consider evidence not in the record | File exhibit list and ensure all referenced exhibits are in the record |
| Register of Actions incomplete | MSC missing procedural history | Obtain certified Register of Actions from trial court clerk |
| Relying on documents not in the lower court record | MSC WILL NOT consider new evidence | Cite only documents that are in the trial or COA record |
| Failing to include COA briefs | MSC lacks context of COA arguments | Include COA briefs in appendix to MSC application |
| Missing trial court opinion/order being challenged | MSC cannot identify error | Include the specific order(s) being challenged in appendix |
| Settling record disputes before COA, not before MSC filing | MCR 7.210(C) record disputes must be resolved at COA level | Resolve any record disputes in COA BEFORE filing MSC application |

## TABLE 5 — GROUNDS FAILURES (MCR 7.305(B))

| Gotcha | Ground | Why It Fails | How to Fix |
|--------|--------|--------------|------------|
| Claiming "conflict" without identifying specific conflicting decisions | MCR 7.305(B)(1) | Must cite specific COA or trial court decisions that conflict | Identify published COA decisions reaching opposite conclusions on same legal issue |
| Asserting "public interest" without showing breadth of impact | MCR 7.305(B)(2) | Must show issue affects persons beyond this case | Document how many Michigan residents / cases affected by the legal question |
| Bypass without extraordinary circumstances | MCR 7.305(B)(3) | Must show BOTH that issue has major significance AND that delay causes harm | Draft separate sections for (a) significance and (b) irreparable harm from delay |
| Federal question without showing it is dispositive | MCR 7.305(B)(4) | Federal question must CONTROL the outcome, not merely be relevant | Show that resolution of federal question determines case outcome |
| Relying solely on "catch-all" ground (B)(5) | MCR 7.305(B)(5) | Weakest ground — MSC rarely grants on (B)(5) alone | Always pair (B)(5) with at least one other ground |
| Presenting factual disputes as legal questions | MCR 7.305(B) | MSC does not retry facts — it reviews legal questions | Frame every argument as a legal error, not a factual dispute |
| Too many questions presented | Practice | Dilutes strength — MSC sees scattershot approach | Limit to 2-3 strongest questions |
| Not connecting grounds to questions presented | Practice | Court cannot see why leave should be granted | Each question presented should map to a specific MCR 7.305(B) ground |

## TABLE 6 — IMMUNITY ASSUMPTIONS

| Gotcha | Reality | Authority |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| "Judges have absolute immunity so I cannot challenge judicial actions" | WRONG — Judicial immunity has EXCEPTIONS: non-judicial acts, clear absence of jurisdiction, declaratory/injunctive relief | Mireles v Waco, 502 US 9 (1991); Stump v Sparkman, 435 US 349 (1978) |
| "I cannot sue a judge under §1983" | PARTIALLY WRONG — Cannot get damages, but CAN get declaratory and injunctive relief | Pulliam v Allen, 466 US 522 (1984); FSIA amendments limit but don't eliminate |
| "Judicial immunity protects all court staff" | WRONG — Quasi-judicial immunity applies only to functions integral to judicial process | Antoine v Byers & Anderson, 508 US 429 (1993) |
| "Filing a JTC complaint is the same as suing a judge" | WRONG — JTC complaint is administrative, not litigation. Different standards, different process | Const 1963 Art 6 §30; MCR 9.200 et seq. |
| "Prosecutorial immunity protects FOC/caseworkers" | DEPENDS — Absolute immunity only for advocacy; qualified immunity for investigation/admin | Buckley v Fitzsimmons, 509 US 259 (1993); Van de Kamp v Goldstein, 555 US 335 (2009) |
| "Sovereign immunity bars all claims against county" | WRONG in Michigan — Governmental immunity has broad exceptions for torts, constitutional violations | MCL 691.1407; Ross v Consumers Power Co, 420 Mich 567 (1984) |
| "I need to overcome immunity to file in MSC" | WRONG — Immunity is an affirmative defense. File the claim; respondent must raise and prove immunity | Scott v Clay County, 205 F3d 867 (6th Cir 2000) |
| "Administrative acts by judges are protected" | WRONG — Non-judicial administrative acts (scheduling manipulation, ex parte communications about admin matters) are NOT protected | Forrester v White, 484 US 219 (1988) |

## MASTER ANTI-RATIONALIZATION RULE

```
BEFORE EVERY MSC FILING, ASK:
  1. Do I have jurisdiction? (Table 1)
  2. Am I within deadline? (Table 2)
  3. Are my forms correct? (Table 3)
  4. Is the record complete? (Table 4)
  5. Do my grounds actually satisfy MCR 7.305(B)? (Table 5)
  6. Am I making false immunity assumptions? (Table 6)

If ANY answer is uncertain → STOP and resolve before filing.
```
