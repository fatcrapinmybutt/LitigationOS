# APPLICATION TEMPLATES — MSC FILINGS

## PURPOSE

Ready-to-use templates for every MSC filing type. Fill in bracketed fields,
verify compliance with MCR 7.212(C), and switch to FILE_READY mode.

---

## TEMPLATE 2: BYPASS APPLICATION — ADDITIONAL SECTION

Insert this section BEFORE the standard Argument section when filing
a bypass application under MCR 7.305(B)(3):

```
STATEMENT OF EXTRAORDINARY CIRCUMSTANCES
WARRANTING BYPASS OF THE COURT OF APPEALS

A. Status of Court of Appeals Proceedings

[Describe current status — case pending, not yet filed, etc.]

B. Issue of Major Significance to Michigan Jurisprudence

[Explain why the legal issue has statewide significance. Cite
cases, statutes, constitutional provisions showing the scope
of the issue. Quantify impact if possible — number of cases,
persons affected, areas of unsettled law.]

C. Substantial Harm From Delay

[Explain specific, concrete harm that will occur if MSC waits
for COA to decide. Must show irreparable harm — harm that
cannot be remedied after COA proceedings conclude.]

[For Lane A custody context: ongoing deprivation of parental
rights, harm to child welfare, constitutional rights violations
that compound daily.]

D. Bypass Is the Only Adequate Remedy

[Explain why COA proceedings cannot provide adequate or timely
relief. Show that only MSC's immediate intervention can prevent
the identified harm.]
```

## TEMPLATE 3: PETITION FOR EXTRAORDINARY WRIT

### MANDAMUS
```
IN THE SUPREME COURT OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN

ANDREW PIGORS,                       Supreme Court No. ___________
     Petitioner,
                                     Related Lower Court:
v                                    [Court and Case No.]

[RESPONDENT — e.g., HON. [JUDGE NAME],
Judge of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court],
     Respondent.
__________________________________________/

COMPLAINT FOR WRIT OF MANDAMUS

VERIFIED PETITION

Petitioner Andrew Pigors, appearing In Propria Persona, states
under penalty of perjury:

1. PARTIES AND JURISDICTION

[Identify petitioner and respondent. State jurisdictional basis
under Const 1963 Art 6 §4 and MCR 7.306.]

2. STATEMENT OF FACTS

[Verified facts showing clear legal right in petitioner and
clear legal duty in respondent. Every fact must be stated under
oath. Cite supporting documents attached as exhibits.]

3. DEMAND AND REFUSAL

[State that petitioner demanded respondent perform the duty.
State that respondent refused or failed to act.]

4. NO ADEQUATE LEGAL REMEDY

[Explain why appeal is not an adequate remedy. Show that only
mandamus can provide the needed relief.]

5. RELIEF REQUESTED

Petitioner requests that this Court issue a writ of mandamus
commanding Respondent to [specific action demanded].

VERIFICATION

I, Andrew Pigors, state under penalty of perjury that the
foregoing statements are true and accurate to the best of my
knowledge, information, and belief.

                              _________________________
                              Andrew Pigors, In Propria Persona
                              Date: ___________________
```

```
Plaintiff-Appellant moves for a stay of [specific order/judgment]
pending this Court's consideration of the accompanying Application
for Leave to Appeal, based on the following:

1. LIKELIHOOD OF SUCCESS ON THE MERITS
[Summarize strongest argument for reversal.]

2. IRREPARABLE HARM ABSENT STAY
[Specific harm that will occur if order is not stayed.]

[Why stay serves justice — harm to applicant vs. harm to respondent.]

[Why stay serves broader public interest.]

A proposed order is attached.
```

## PRO SE FILING NOTES

```
CRITICAL PRO SE REMINDERS:
1. Sign EVERYTHING — unsigned documents are rejected
2. Include "In Propria Persona" after your name — not "Pro Se"
   (Michigan convention)
3. Include full contact information on every document
4. Keep copies of EVERYTHING you file
5. Use certified mail for service — keep receipts
6. Call the MSC clerk with procedural questions: (517) 373-0120
7. MSC clerks CANNOT give legal advice but CAN answer procedural questions
8. File early — do not wait until the deadline day
9. Review EVERY document before filing for typos, missing pages, wrong dates
10. When in doubt about form, look at published MSC opinions for formatting
```
