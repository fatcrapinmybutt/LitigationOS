# MSC BRIEF ARCHITECTURE

## PURPOSE

Definitive structure for every Michigan Supreme Court brief, per MCR 7.212(C)
as applied to MSC practice. This is the architectural blueprint — follow it
exactly for FILE_READY compliance.

---

## GOVERNING RULES

- **MCR 7.212(C)** — Content requirements for appellate briefs
- **MCR 7.212(B)** — Page/word limits
- **MCR 7.300(B)** — MCR 7.212 applies to MSC briefs where applicable
- **Michigan Appellate Opinion Manual** — Citation format guidance

---

## BRIEF STRUCTURE — REQUIRED SECTIONS IN ORDER

### 1. COVER PAGE

```
IN THE SUPREME COURT OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN

[APPLICANT NAME],                    Supreme Court No. ___________
     Plaintiff-Appellant,            Court of Appeals No. _________
                                     Circuit Court No. ___________
v                                    Circuit Judge: Hon. __________

[RESPONDENT NAME],
     Defendant-Appellee.
__________________________________________/

APPLICATION FOR LEAVE TO APPEAL

[Name], In Propria Persona
[Address]
[City, State ZIP]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

### 2. TABLE OF CONTENTS (TOC)

- Required for briefs exceeding 5 pages
- List every section heading with page number
- Include subsections for argument headings
- Include appendix items with tab/page references
- Generate AFTER brief is complete (page numbers must be accurate)

### 3. TABLE OF AUTHORITIES (TOA)

- Required for briefs exceeding 5 pages
- Organized by category:
  1. **Cases** — alphabetical by first-named party
  2. **Constitutional Provisions** — by jurisdiction (federal, then state)
  3. **Court Rules** — numerical order
  4. **Statutes** — by jurisdiction, then numerical
  5. **Other Authorities** — treatises, law review articles, etc.
- Page numbers where each authority is cited

### 4. STATEMENT OF QUESTIONS PRESENTED

**THE MOST CRITICAL SECTION OF THE ENTIRE BRIEF.**

MSC justices decide whether to grant leave primarily based on questions presented.

**Requirements:**
- State each question in a single sentence (may have subparts)
- Frame as YES/NO answerable questions
- Include the LEGAL PRINCIPLE at issue
- Include enough FACTS to show the question arises from this case
- Maximum 2-3 questions for MSC application (LESS IS MORE)

**Format:**
```
QUESTIONS PRESENTED

I. Whether the Court of Appeals erred in holding that [legal principle]
   when [relevant facts], in conflict with this Court's decision in
   [authority], thereby [consequence].

   Trial court answered: [Yes/No]
   Court of Appeals answered: [Yes/No]
   Plaintiff-Appellant answers: [Yes/No]

II. Whether [second question in same format]...
```

**Pro Se Note:**
Questions presented must demonstrate legal sophistication. Poor framing = denial.
Each question should implicitly show why MSC review is warranted (conflict,
public significance, constitutional dimension).

### 5. STATEMENT OF JURISDICTION

**Required by MCR 7.212(C)(5).**

Must establish MSC's authority to hear the case.

**Required Elements:**
1. Constitutional basis: Const 1963 Art 6 §4
2. Rule basis: MCR 7.302, MCR 7.305
3. Lower court decision details: date, case number, judges
4. Timeliness: demonstrate filing within 56-day deadline
5. Grounds: identify MCR 7.305(B) subsection(s)

**Template:**
```
STATEMENT OF JURISDICTION

This Court has jurisdiction under Const 1963 Art 6 §4 and MCR 7.302.
The Court of Appeals issued its [opinion/order] on [date] in Case
No. [number]. This application is filed within the 56-day period
prescribed by MCR 7.305(C)(2).

Leave should be granted under MCR 7.305(B)([subsection(s)]) because
[brief statement of grounds].
```

### 6. STATEMENT OF PROCEEDINGS AND FACTS

**Two distinct components — do not combine them.**

**Statement of Proceedings** (procedural history):
- Chronological procedural history from trial court through COA
- Every significant filing, hearing, ruling, and order
- Dates, judges, outcomes
- Neutral tone — let the facts show the problems

**Statement of Facts** (substantive facts):
- Facts relevant to the questions presented
- Supported by record citations (Appendix tab and page)
- EVERY factual assertion must cite the record
- No argument in this section — save it for Argument
- Adverse facts must be included (credibility)

**Record Citation Format:**
```
On [date], the trial court entered an order [description]. (App. A at 3.)
The witness testified that [fact]. (Tr. at 45-46.)
```

### 7. ARGUMENT

**Structure — one section per question presented:**

```
ARGUMENT

I. [HEADING RESTATING QUESTION PRESENTED I AS ARGUMENT]

   A. Standard of Review
      [State applicable standard — de novo, abuse of discretion, etc.]

   B. The Court of Appeals erred because [argument]
      [Legal analysis with authority citations]
      [Application to facts with record citations]

   C. This error warrants Supreme Court review because [MCR 7.305(B) ground]
      [Why this issue transcends this case]

II. [HEADING RESTATING QUESTION PRESENTED II AS ARGUMENT]
    [Same structure as above]
```

**Argument Requirements:**
- Begin each section with standard of review
- Cite controlling authority for every legal proposition
- Apply law to specific facts of this case
- Distinguish unfavorable authority — don't ignore it
- Connect EVERY argument to an MCR 7.305(B) ground
- Show why the issue matters beyond this case

**Pro Se Argument Tips:**
- Do NOT include emotional appeals — legal arguments only
- Do NOT argue facts the lower court resolved against you (unless clearly erroneous)
- DO acknowledge and distinguish adverse authority
- DO focus on legal error, not disagreement with outcome

### 8. RELIEF REQUESTED

**Be specific about what you want MSC to do.**

**Options:**
1. Grant leave to appeal and reverse COA decision
2. Grant leave and remand for specific proceedings
3. Issue peremptory order (reverse without full briefing)
4. Grant other appropriate relief

**Template:**
```
RELIEF REQUESTED

For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff-Appellant respectfully requests
that this Court:

1. Grant this application for leave to appeal;
2. Reverse the Court of Appeals' [opinion/order] dated [date];
3. [Specific relief — e.g., remand for new hearing, reinstate trial
   court order, declare rights, etc.]; and
4. Grant such other relief as this Court deems just and appropriate.

                              Respectfully submitted,

                              _________________________
                              Andrew Pigors, In Propria Persona
                              [Address]
                              [Phone]
                              [Email]
                              Date: ___________________
```

### 9. APPENDIX

**Required contents:**
| Tab | Document |
|-----|----------|
| A | Court of Appeals opinion/order |
| B | Trial court order(s) being challenged |
| C | Trial court opinion (if any) |
| D | Key transcript excerpts |
| E | Critical exhibits referenced in brief |
| F | Register of Actions from trial court |
| G | Other relevant orders |

**Appendix Rules:**
- Tab each document for easy reference
- Include appendix index at beginning
- Cross-reference appendix tabs in brief citations
- Only include documents that ARE IN THE RECORD
- Do NOT include new evidence not presented to lower courts

## PAGE LIMITS AND FORMATTING

### Page/Word Limits
| Document | Page Limit | Word Limit (Alternative) |
|----------|-----------|-------------------------|
| Application brief | 50 pages | 16,000 words |
| Reply brief | 10 pages | 3,200 words |
| Response/answer | 50 pages | 16,000 words |
| Motion brief | 20 pages | 6,400 words |
| Amicus brief | 25 pages | 8,000 words |

### Formatting Requirements
- **Paper**: 8.5 x 11 inches, white
- **Margins**: 1 inch on all sides
- **Font**: 12-point, proportional (Times New Roman preferred) or 11-point monospace (Courier)
- **Spacing**: Double-spaced text; single-spaced for quotations > 50 words and footnotes
- **Page Numbers**: Centered at bottom
- **Line Numbers**: Not required for MSC briefs
- **Binding**: Stapled or bound at left margin; no spiral binding
- **Color**: Black ink only; no color printing except in exhibits

### Pro Se Formatting Considerations
- Handwritten briefs are technically accepted but STRONGLY discouraged
- Type/word-process all filings
- Use consistent formatting throughout
- Number every page
- Double-check spelling of case names and rule citations
