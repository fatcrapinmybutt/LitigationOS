# MCR 7.300 SERIES — COMPLETE RULE-BY-RULE MAP

## PURPOSE

This is the COMPLETE mapping of every Michigan Court Rule governing
Michigan Supreme Court practice: MCR 7.300 through MCR 7.311.
Each rule is broken down by subsection with practical annotations.

---

## MCR 7.300 — APPLICABILITY

### Scope
- This subchapter governs practice and procedure in the Michigan Supreme Court
- Applies to ALL proceedings in MSC: appeals, original actions, writs, motions
- Where MCR 7.300 is silent, COA rules (MCR 7.200 series) apply to the extent
  applicable — MCR 7.300(B)

### Cross-References
- MCR 7.200 series (COA rules) — incorporated by reference where applicable
- MCR 1.105 — general provisions
- MCR 2.107 — service of process (incorporated for service requirements)

### Practical Note
When in doubt about MSC procedure, check both MCR 7.300 series AND MCR 7.200
series. The COA rules fill gaps in the MSC rules.

---

## MCR 7.301 — ORGANIZATION AND POWERS

### (A) Composition
- Seven justices elected at large on nonpartisan ballot
- Eight-year terms
- Quorum: majority of justices (4 of 7)
- Chief Justice designated by majority vote of justices

### (B) Powers
1. **Appellate review** of COA decisions and orders
2. **Original jurisdiction** — constitutionally granted
3. **General superintending control** over all Michigan courts
4. **Rulemaking authority** — all Michigan Court Rules
5. **Administrative authority** through SCAO
6. **Power to issue extraordinary writs**

### (C) Sessions
- MSC sits in Lansing — Hall of Justice, 925 W. Ottawa Street
- Regular session: First Wednesday in October through July
- Special sessions may be called by Chief Justice

### (D) Clerk
- Clerk of the Supreme Court maintains all filings
- Clerk has authority to reject non-compliant filings
- Electronic filing available through TrueFiling system

### Practical Notes
- MSC typically considers leave applications on the briefs without oral argument
- Oral argument is ordered ONLY after leave is granted (MCR 7.307)
- MSC decides approximately 80-100 cases per year on the merits

## MCR 7.302 — APPLICATION FOR LEAVE TO APPEAL

### (A) When Available
- Application for leave may be filed after COA has issued a final decision
  or order disposing of the case
- Not available for interlocutory COA orders (must use different vehicle)

### (B) Filing Requirements
1. **Original** — one signed original of the application
2. **Copies** — sufficient copies for all parties plus one for the court
3. **Cover Page** — must include:
   - MSC case number (assigned by clerk)
   - Full case caption
   - Lower court numbers (COA and trial court)
   - Names and addresses of all attorneys/parties
4. **Fee** — $375 filing fee OR MC 20 fee waiver
5. **Proof of Service** — MC 12 form

### (C) Content of Application
Must contain all items required by MCR 7.212(C):
1. Cover page
2. Table of Contents
3. Table of Authorities
4. Statement of Questions Presented
5. Statement of Jurisdiction
6. Statement of Proceedings and Facts
7. Argument
8. Relief Requested
9. Appendix (COA opinion, trial court orders, key record items)

### (D) Length
- Application brief: not to exceed 50 pages (double-spaced)
- Or 16,000 words (if using word count)
- Reply brief: 10 pages or 3,200 words

### (E) Service
- Must serve all parties simultaneously with filing
- Service by mail, personal delivery, or electronic means if consented
- Proof of service required — MC 12 form

### Practical Notes
- The Questions Presented section is the MOST IMPORTANT part of the application
- MSC justices often decide whether to grant leave based primarily on questions presented
- Frame questions to show the issue transcends this case
- DO NOT repeat entire COA brief — focus on why MSC review is warranted

## MCR 7.303 — ORIGINAL PROCEEDINGS

### (A) Scope
- Original proceedings in the MSC — not appeals from lower courts
- Constitutional basis: Const 1963 Art 6 §4

### (B) Types of Original Proceedings
1. **Superintending control** over lower courts
2. **Mandamus** against state officers / constitutional officers
3. **Quo warranto** — challenging right to hold public office
4. **Advisory opinions** — on request of Governor

### (C) Procedure
1. File complaint/petition directly in MSC
2. Include brief in support
3. Serve respondent
4. MSC may:
   a. Dismiss without response
   b. Order response
   c. Order oral argument
   d. Grant relief
   e. Refer to COA or trial court for proceedings

### (D) Standard
- Must demonstrate the matter is appropriate for MSC original jurisdiction
- Must show no adequate remedy in lower courts
- Must show urgency or constitutional significance

### Practical Notes for Pigors Lane C
- If seeking superintending control over 14th Circuit procedures:
  file under MCR 7.303 + MCR 7.306
- Must demonstrate SYSTEMIC failure, not merely unfavorable rulings
- Document pattern of conduct, not isolated incidents

## MCR 7.305 — APPLICATION FOR LEAVE TO APPEAL

### (A) Applicability
- Governs all applications for leave to appeal to the MSC
- Works in conjunction with MCR 7.302 (filing requirements)

### (B) Grounds for Granting Leave — THE CRITICAL SECTION

**MCR 7.305(B)(1) — Conflict**
- COA decision conflicts with MSC decision
- COA decision conflicts with another COA panel decision
- COA decision conflicts with federal court decision
- Must be on the SAME legal issue
- Must identify specific conflicting holdings

**MCR 7.305(B)(2) — Public Significance**
- Legal principle of major significance to state jurisprudence
- Issue affects persons beyond the parties
- Unsettled or developing area of law
- Need for uniform statewide rule

**MCR 7.305(B)(3) — Bypass**
- Before COA decision (bypass provision)
- TWO requirements:
  1. Issue of major significance to Michigan jurisprudence
  2. Delay in COA would cause substantial harm
- MSC grants bypass RARELY — extraordinary circumstances required
- Must show WHY waiting for COA is inadequate

**MCR 7.305(B)(4) — Federal/Constitutional Question**
- Substantial question about validity of legislative act
- Federal constitutional question that is dispositive
- Not yet finally determined by MSC

**MCR 7.305(B)(5) — Clear Error / Material Injustice**
- Decision is clearly erroneous
- Will cause material injustice
- Weakest ground — supplement with others
- Essentially a "catch-all" for exceptional cases

### (C) Timing

**MCR 7.305(C)(1)** — Application filed in MSC
**MCR 7.305(C)(2)** — Deadline: **56 days from COA decision**
  - Runs from date of COA decision/order
  - If motion for reconsideration filed in COA: 56 days from ruling on that motion
  - JURISDICTIONAL — late applications rejected absent fraud/irregularity
**MCR 7.305(C)(3)** — Cross-application: 28 days after applicant's application filed
**MCR 7.305(C)(4)** — Response/answer: 28 days after service of application
**MCR 7.305(C)(5)** — Reply: 14 days after service of answer

### (D) Action by the Court
- MSC may grant, deny, or defer action
- May grant with limitations (specific issues only)
- May issue peremptory order without full briefing
- May remand to COA or trial court

### Practical Notes
- Peremptory reversal is possible — MSC can reverse without granting full leave
- MSC sometimes issues "mini-opinions" with leave denial — watch for these
- If MSC grants leave, full briefing and oral argument scheduled (MCR 7.307)

## MCR 7.306 — EXTRAORDINARY WRITS

### (A) Types Available
1. **Mandamus** — compel performance of clear legal duty
2. **Superintending Control** — compel lower court to act within jurisdiction
3. **Habeas Corpus** — challenge unlawful custody/detention
4. **Prohibition** — prevent lower court from exceeding jurisdiction
5. **Certiorari** — review for jurisdictional excess (largely superseded)

### (B) Requirements

**MCR 7.306(B)(1) — Mandamus**
- Clear legal right in petitioner
- Clear legal duty in respondent
- No adequate legal remedy (appeal insufficient)
- Discretion: only where duty is ministerial, not discretionary

**MCR 7.306(B)(2) — Superintending Control**
- Lower court exceeded jurisdiction or failed to perform duty
- No adequate appellate remedy
- Petitioner exhausted lower court remedies
- Urgency: continued lower court action would cause irreparable harm

**MCR 7.306(B)(3) — Habeas Corpus**
- Person held in custody/restraint
- Custody is unlawful (no legal basis)
- Exhaustion of lower court remedies
- In family law: may apply to wrongful custody deprivation

### (C) Procedure
1. File complaint/petition directly in MSC
2. Include verified statement of facts
3. Include brief in support with legal authorities
4. Serve respondent
5. MSC may issue order to show cause or dismiss
6. If order to show cause: respondent files answer, petitioner may reply

### (D) Standard of Review
- Extraordinary relief — not routine
- Petitioner bears heavy burden
- Must show clear entitlement, not mere probability of success

### Practical Application to Pigors
- Lane A: Habeas / mandamus if custody rights being violated without due process
- Lane C: Superintending control if 14th Circuit systematically exceeding authority

## MCR 7.307 — CALENDAR CASES / ORAL ARGUMENT

### (A) Calendar Cases
- After leave is granted, case is placed on calendar
- Full briefing ordered: appellant's brief → appellee's brief → reply

### (B) Oral Argument
- Scheduled by MSC after briefing complete
- Each side typically allocated 30 minutes
- Justices ask questions — preparation is essential
- Pro se parties receive same time allocation

### (C) Amicus Curiae
- May file brief with leave of court — MCR 7.312
- Amicus may request oral argument time (rarely granted)

---

## MCR 7.308 — CERTIFIED QUESTIONS

### (A) Source
- Federal courts (6th Circuit, EDMI, WDMI) may certify unsettled questions
  of Michigan law to the MSC

### (B) Procedure
1. Federal court issues certification order
2. MSC clerk receives and dockets
3. MSC decides whether to accept certification
4. If accepted: briefing schedule issued
5. Parties and amici file briefs
6. MSC may order oral argument
7. MSC issues opinion answering certified question

### (C) Discretion
- MSC may DECLINE to answer certified questions
- Acceptance depends on whether question is truly unsettled and significant

### (D) Application to Pigors
- If Lane A or B involves federal claims (§1983, Fair Housing), and
  federal court encounters unsettled Michigan law question, federal court
  may certify to MSC
- Party role: file brief addressing certified question per MSC schedule

## MCR 7.309 — REHEARINGS

### (A) Availability
- Motion for rehearing after MSC decision on the merits

### (B) Deadline
- 21 days from MSC decision date
- JURISDICTIONAL — no late filings

### (C) Grounds
- Material error of fact
- Material error of law
- Significant point overlooked
- NOT for re-argument of points already considered

### (D) Procedure
- File motion + brief (limited length)
- Opposing party may file response
- MSC decides on papers — no oral argument on rehearing

---

## MCR 7.310 — EXECUTION OF JUDGMENTS

### (A) MSC Judgment
- After final decision, MSC issues judgment
- Judgment transmitted to lower courts for execution

### (B) Stay
- Execution automatically stayed for 21 days (rehearing period)
- Further stay by motion under MCR 7.311

## MCR 7.311 — MISCELLANEOUS MOTIONS AND PROCEDURES

### (A) General Motion Practice
- Motions filed in MSC for various relief
- Must include: motion + brief in support + proposed order

### (B) Types of Motions
1. **Stay pending appeal** — MCR 7.311(E)
2. **Extension of time** — MCR 7.311(F)
3. **Consolidation** — MCR 7.311(G)
4. **Intervention** — MCR 7.311(H)
5. **Amicus curiae** — MCR 7.312
6. **Disqualification of justice** — MCR 7.311(B)
7. **Voluntary dismissal**

### (C) Response Time
- 14 days to respond to motions unless otherwise ordered

### (D) Emergency Motions
- May request expedited consideration
- Must show irreparable harm from delay
- Contact clerk's office for emergency procedures

### (E) Stay Pending Application for Leave
- Must show:
  1. Likelihood of success on the merits
  2. Irreparable harm absent stay
  3. Balance of equities favors stay
  4. Stay serves public interest
- Same standard as federal preliminary injunction

### (F) Extension of Time
- Must be filed BEFORE deadline expires
- Must show good cause for extension
- MSC grants extensions rarely for jurisdictional deadlines

### Practical Notes
- Stay motions should be filed simultaneously with leave application
- Extension motions should be filed well before deadline — not the day of
- Emergency motions require separate cover sheet marked "EMERGENCY"
