# FILING PATHS — EVERY MSC FILING PATH DETAILED

## PURPOSE

This document provides the complete specification for every available filing
path in the Michigan Supreme Court. For each path: prerequisites, timing,
required documents, service requirements, fees, and grounds.

---

## PATH 1: APPLICATION FOR LEAVE TO APPEAL

### Governing Rules
- MCR 7.302 (procedure)
- MCR 7.305 (grounds and timing)
- MCR 7.212(C) (brief format — incorporated by reference)

### Prerequisites
1. COA has entered a FINAL decision or order
2. All COA remedies exhausted (or deliberately foregone)
3. If motion for reconsideration filed in COA — must wait for ruling
4. Standing: party aggrieved by COA decision
5. Justiciable controversy exists (not moot)

### Timing
| Event | Deadline |
|-------|----------|
| COA issues decision | Day 0 |
| Application for leave due | Day 56 (MCR 7.305(C)(2)) |
| Cross-application due | 28 days after applicant's filing |
| Response/answer due | 28 days after service of application |
| Reply brief due | 14 days after service of answer |

### Required Documents
1. **Application brief** — per MCR 7.212(C):
   - Cover page (case caption, parties, lower court info)
   - Table of Contents
   - Table of Authorities
   - Statement of Questions Presented
   - Statement of Jurisdiction
   - Statement of Proceedings and Facts
   - Argument (organized by question presented)
   - Relief Requested
2. **Appendix**:
   - COA opinion/order
   - Trial court opinion/order(s) being challenged
   - Key transcript excerpts (if relevant)
   - Critical exhibits (if relevant)
   - Register of Actions from trial court
3. **CC 298** — Application for Leave to Appeal form
4. **MC 12** — Proof of Service
5. **Fee**: $375 OR **MC 20** fee waiver

### Service Requirements
- Serve ALL parties or their attorneys
- Service simultaneous with filing
- Method: first-class mail, personal delivery, or e-service if consented
- Complete MC 12 with:
  - Names and addresses of all served parties
  - Date of service
  - Method of service
  - Signature of server

### Fee
- $375 filing fee payable to State of Michigan
- OR MC 20 Affidavit of Indigency for fee waiver
- Fee waiver: must demonstrate inability to pay without depriving self/dependents of necessities

### Grounds — Must Satisfy at Least One MCR 7.305(B) Ground
See references/strategy/grounds-matrix.md for detailed mapping.

## PATH 2: BYPASS APPLICATION

### Governing Rules
- MCR 7.305(B)(3) (bypass provision)
- MCR 7.302 (filing procedure — same as leave application)

### Prerequisites
1. Case is either pending in COA OR has not yet been filed in COA
2. Issue of major significance to Michigan jurisprudence exists
3. Delay in COA proceedings would cause substantial harm
4. BOTH prongs must be met — significance AND harm from delay

### Timing
- No fixed deadline — but delay undercuts urgency argument
- File as soon as extraordinary circumstances become apparent
- Practical: file within 21 days of triggering event

### Required Documents
All documents required for standard leave application PLUS:
1. **Separate bypass section** in brief:
   - Why issue has major significance to state jurisprudence
   - Why delay in COA will cause substantial/irreparable harm
   - Why MSC bypass is the only adequate remedy
2. **Evidence of urgency** — declarations, exhibits showing time-sensitive harm
3. **Explanation of COA status** — what is pending, expected timeline

### Service Requirements
- Same as standard leave application
- ALSO serve COA clerk if case is pending in COA

### Fee
- Same as standard leave application: $375 or MC 20 waiver

### Grounds
- MCR 7.305(B)(3) specifically
- Should ALSO argue at least one other ground from (B)(1), (2), or (4)

### Strategic Notes
- MSC grants bypass in < 5% of requests
- Strongest bypass cases: constitutional rights being violated pending appeal,
  ongoing harm to children, imminent irreversible government action
- Lane A bypass: ongoing custody deprivation + child welfare concerns
- Must convincingly show COA CANNOT address the harm quickly enough

## PATH 3: ORIGINAL PROCEEDING

### Governing Rules
- MCR 7.303
- Const 1963 Art 6 §4

### Prerequisites
1. Matter falls within MSC original jurisdiction:
   - Superintending control over lower courts
   - Actions against constitutional officers
   - Quo warranto proceedings
2. No adequate remedy in lower courts
3. Constitutional significance or systemic importance
4. Exhaustion of available lower court remedies

### Timing
- No fixed deadline — but laches doctrine may bar stale claims
- Equitable defenses (laches, estoppel) may apply
- File promptly after triggering event

### Required Documents
1. **Complaint** — verified statement of facts and claims
2. **Brief in support** — legal authorities, constitutional basis
3. **Appendix** — relevant lower court orders, administrative records
4. **Proposed order** — specific relief requested
5. **MC 12** — Proof of Service
6. **Fee**: $375 or MC 20 waiver

### Service Requirements
- Serve named respondent(s)
- Serve all interested parties
- Serve Attorney General if state interest implicated (MCL 14.101)
- Service method: personal service preferred for original proceedings

### Fee
- $375 or MC 20 fee waiver

### Grounds
- Constitutional grant of superintending control
- Clear legal right + clear legal duty + no adequate remedy
- Demonstrate systemic nature (not isolated ruling)

## PATH 4: EXTRAORDINARY WRITS

### Governing Rules
- MCR 7.306
- Const 1963 Art 6 §4

### PATH 4A: MANDAMUS

**Prerequisites:**
1. Clear legal right in petitioner
2. Clear legal duty in respondent (ministerial, not discretionary)
3. No other adequate legal remedy
4. Petitioner has demanded performance and been refused

**Required Documents:**
1. Petition for Writ of Mandamus (verified)
2. Brief in support
3. Evidence of demand and refusal
4. Proposed writ
5. MC 12 Proof of Service
6. Fee: $375 or MC 20

**Standard:** Petitioner must show CLEAR entitlement. Court will not compel
exercise of discretion — only performance of ministerial duty.

### PATH 4B: SUPERINTENDING CONTROL

**Prerequisites:**
1. Lower court exceeded jurisdiction OR failed to perform clear duty
2. No adequate appellate remedy available
3. Petitioner exhausted available remedies in lower court
4. Continued lower court action would cause irreparable harm

**Required Documents:**
1. Complaint for Superintending Control (verified)
2. Brief in support citing Const Art 6 §4
3. Evidence of jurisdictional excess or failure of duty
4. Complete record from lower court proceedings
5. Proposed order
6. MC 12 + Fee/$375/MC 20

**Application to Pigors Lane C:**
- Document specific instances of judicial overreach in 14th Circuit
- Show pattern, not just isolated unfavorable rulings
- Demonstrate how ordinary appeal cannot remedy systemic issues

### PATH 4C: HABEAS CORPUS

**Prerequisites:**
1. Person held in custody or restraint
2. Custody/restraint is unlawful
3. Exhaustion of lower court remedies (trial court + COA)
4. In family law: wrongful deprivation of custody constitutes "restraint"

**Required Documents:**
1. Petition for Writ of Habeas Corpus (verified)
2. Brief in support
3. Evidence of unlawful custody/restraint
4. Copy of custody order (if any)
5. Evidence of exhaustion of lower court remedies
6. MC 12 + Fee/$375/MC 20

**Application to Pigors Lane A:**
- If custody deprivation amounts to unlawful restraint
- Must show prior attempts to remedy through trial court and COA
- Strong vehicle if emergency custody situation exists

## PATH 5: CERTIFIED QUESTIONS

### Governing Rules
- MCR 7.308

### Prerequisites
1. A federal court (6th Circuit, EDMI, WDMI) identifies an unsettled
   question of Michigan law
2. Federal court issues certification order to MSC
3. MSC accepts the certified question (discretionary)

### Party Role
- NOT initiated by parties — initiated by federal court
- Parties file briefs as directed by MSC scheduling order
- Parties may file motion requesting federal court to certify
  (filed in federal court, not MSC)

### Timing
- Per MSC scheduling order after accepting certification
- Typically 30-60 days for party briefs
- Amicus briefs may be permitted

### Required Documents
- Party brief addressing the certified question
- Comply with MCR 7.212(C) formatting
- Any appendix materials relevant to the state-law question

### Fee
- No fee for parties responding to certified question
- Federal court pays certification fee

### Application to Pigors
- If Lane A or B generates federal litigation (§1983 in federal court),
  AND federal court encounters novel Michigan law question,
  parties may request certification
- Useful for getting MSC to address Michigan-specific legal standards
  that lack clear precedent

## PATH 6: MOTIONS (MCR 7.311)

### Governing Rules
- MCR 7.311

### Motion Types and Requirements

| Motion Type | Specific Rule | Key Showing |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| Stay pending appeal | MCR 7.311(E) | Likelihood of success + irreparable harm + balance of equities + public interest |
| Extension of time | MCR 7.311(F) | Good cause — must file BEFORE deadline |
| Consolidation | MCR 7.311(G) | Common questions of law/fact |
| Intervention | MCR 7.311(H) | Interest in subject matter + impaired interest |
| Disqualification | MCR 7.311(B) | Bias, conflict, prior involvement |
| Voluntary dismissal | MCR 7.311 | Party's right (with limitations) |
| Emergency relief | MCR 7.311 | Irreparable harm from delay |

### Required Documents (All Motions)
1. Motion — specific relief requested
2. Brief in support — legal authorities
3. Proposed order — exact language of requested relief
4. MC 12 — Proof of Service
5. Fee: varies (some motions have no separate fee)

### Service Requirements
- All parties or attorneys of record
- 14 days for response unless otherwise ordered
- Emergency motions: may request shortened response time

### Timing
- Response: 14 days after service (default)
- Emergency: court may shorten or eliminate response period
- Extension motions: MUST be filed before underlying deadline expires
