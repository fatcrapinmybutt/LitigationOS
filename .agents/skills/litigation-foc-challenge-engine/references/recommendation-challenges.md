# Recommendation Challenges — FOC Challenge Reference

## Challenge Framework

### Grounds for Challenging FOC Recommendations

#### Ground 1: Factual Error
The FOC's findings of fact are incorrect or unsupported.

**Examples:**
- FOC states parent is unemployed when documentation shows employment
- FOC reports no domestic violence history when police reports exist
- FOC miscounts parenting time overnights
- FOC misstates child's age, school, or living situation

**Challenge approach:**
```
1. Identify the specific factual error
2. Present contradicting evidence (documents, testimony)
3. Show how the error affected the recommendation
4. Request the court make a corrected finding
```

#### Ground 2: Legal Error
The FOC applied the wrong legal standard or misapplied the correct standard.

**Examples:**
- FOC failed to analyze all 12 MCL 722.23 best-interest factors
- FOC applied wrong income definition for child support calculation
- FOC used incorrect overnight count methodology for MCSF
- FOC failed to consider MCL 722.27 change-of-circumstances threshold

**Challenge approach:**
```
1. Identify the legal standard that should apply
2. Show how the FOC deviated from that standard
3. Cite MCR/MCL authority for the correct standard
4. Demonstrate how correct application changes the result
```

#### Ground 3: Incomplete Investigation
The FOC failed to consider material evidence or investigate relevant facts.

**Examples:**
- FOC didn't interview available witnesses
- FOC didn't review submitted documents
- FOC didn't conduct a home visit when warranted
- FOC didn't contact school, doctor, or other third parties
- FOC spent minimal time on investigation (FOIA time logs reveal)

**Challenge approach:**
```
1. Identify what evidence was available but not reviewed
2. Present that evidence at the de novo hearing
3. Show how the missing evidence changes the analysis
4. Argue that the recommendation is unreliable due to incomplete investigation
```

#### Ground 4: Investigator Bias
The FOC investigator demonstrated bias for or against a party.

**Examples:**
- Investigator spent significantly more time with one party
- Investigator asked leading questions of one party
- Investigator discounted evidence without explanation
- Investigator had prior relationship with one party
- Investigator's recommendation contradicts the evidence they reviewed

**Challenge approach:**
```
1. Document specific instances of bias with dates and details
2. Request disqualification of investigator for future proceedings
3. Request independent investigation (MCR 2.003 analogy)
4. Present your own evidence directly to the court at hearing
```

## De Novo Hearing Preparation

### What "De Novo" Means
The court considers the issues fresh, as if the FOC recommendation doesn't exist. However, in practice:
- The FOC recommendation is in the court's file
- The judge has likely read it
- Your challenge must be strong enough to overcome the recommendation's influence

### Hearing Preparation Checklist
```
DE NOVO HEARING PREPARATION

□ EVIDENCE ORGANIZATION
  □ All exhibits marked and organized
  □ Copies for court, opposing party, and self
  □ Authentication prepared for each exhibit
  □ Evidence indexed by MCL 722.23 factor

□ TESTIMONY PREPARATION
  □ Your testimony outline (factor-by-factor)
  □ Witness list with expected testimony
  □ Subpoenas served on witnesses (if needed)

□ LEGAL AUTHORITY
  □ MCL 722.23 factor citations
  □ Case law supporting your position per factor
  □ MCR 3.218 procedural authority
  □ Response to anticipated opposing arguments

□ FOC CHALLENGE SPECIFICS
  □ Point-by-point rebuttal of FOC recommendation
  □ FOIA results showing investigation deficiencies
  □ Alternative analysis per best-interest factors
  □ Alternative support calculation (if support at issue)

□ LOGISTICS
  □ Hearing date, time, courtroom confirmed
  □ All documents filed and served per deadlines
  □ Childcare arranged (for L.D.W. during hearing)
  □ Work schedule accommodated
```

### Hearing Testimony Framework
```
DIRECT TESTIMONY OUTLINE

1. Introduction (2 minutes)
   - Name, relationship to L.D.W., current living situation

2. FOC Investigation Deficiencies (5 minutes)
   - What the investigator missed or got wrong
   - FOIA evidence of limited investigation

3. Factor-by-Factor Presentation (20-30 minutes)
   - Focus on factors where FOC erred
   - Present evidence for each challenged factor
   - Connect evidence to legal standard

4. Relief Requested (3 minutes)
   - Specific custody/support modification sought
   - How requested relief serves L.D.W.'s best interests
```

## Key Michigan Cases on FOC Challenges

| Case | Holding | Relevance |
|------|---------|-----------|
| *Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499 (2003) | Proper cause/change of circumstances standard | When FOC recommendation addresses modification |
| *Fletcher v Fletcher*, 447 Mich 871 (1994) | Best-interest factor weighting | How to challenge factor analysis |
| *Demski v Petlick*, 309 Mich App 404 (2015) | Factor (j) importance | Challenging facilitation findings |
| *Pierron v Pierron*, 282 Mich App 222 (2009) | Child's preference weight | Challenging Factor (i) findings |
| *Ireland v Smith*, 451 Mich 457 (1996) | Established custodial environment | Challenging status quo findings |
