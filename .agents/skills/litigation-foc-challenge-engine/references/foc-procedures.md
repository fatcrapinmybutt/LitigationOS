# FOC Procedures — Michigan Friend of Court Reference

## What Is the Friend of the Court?

The Friend of the Court (FOC) is a Michigan court agency that assists the family court in domestic relations cases. It operates under MCL 552.501 et seq. and provides:
- Investigation and recommendations on custody and parenting time
- Child support calculation and enforcement
- Mediation services
- Compliance monitoring

## MCR 3.218 — Objections to FOC Recommendations

### MCR 3.218(A) — Filing Objections
- **Deadline**: Within **21 days** after service of the recommendation
- **Filing**: Written objection filed with the court clerk
- **Service**: Copy served on all parties AND the FOC
- **Content**: Must specify the grounds for objection

### MCR 3.218(B) — De Novo Hearing
Upon timely objection:
- Court schedules a de novo hearing
- Parties present evidence as if the recommendation didn't exist
- Court makes independent findings
- FOC recommendation is evidence but not binding
- Court may adopt, modify, or reject the recommendation

### Objection Timeline
```
Day 0:   FOC recommendation served on parties
Day 1:   BEGIN DRAFTING OBJECTION (do not wait)
Day 7:   FOIA request to FOC for investigation file
Day 14:  Objection draft complete; review and finalize
Day 18:  FILE objection (3-day buffer before deadline)
Day 21:  *** DEADLINE — objection must be filed ***
Day 28:  FOC responds to FOIA request
Day 35+: De novo hearing scheduled
Day 42+: De novo hearing conducted
```

## MCR 3.224 — Domestic Relations Referees

### Referee Hearings
- FOC referee conducts hearings on certain matters
- Referee issues recommended order
- Parties may object within 21 days
- Circuit judge reviews de novo if objection filed

### Objection to Referee Recommendation
Same 21-day deadline and process as MCR 3.218:
- File written objection within 21 days
- Request de novo hearing before the judge
- Judge reviews independently

## FOC Investigation Process

### What the FOC Investigates
| Area | Investigation Methods | Documents Reviewed |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Custody | Parent interviews, home visits | School records, medical records |
| Parenting time | Schedule review, compliance check | Calendar, communication logs |
| Child support | Income verification, expense review | Tax returns, pay stubs |
| Modifications | Changed circumstances assessment | Current orders, new evidence |

### Typical Investigation Timeline
1. **Assignment**: FOC assigns investigator after motion/petition filed
2. **Interviews**: Investigator contacts both parties (phone or in-person)
3. **Document review**: Investigator reviews submitted documents
4. **Home visit**: May conduct home visit (not always)
5. **Third-party contact**: May contact school, doctor, counselor
6. **Analysis**: Investigator applies MCL 722.23 factors
7. **Recommendation**: Written recommendation issued to court and parties
8. **Objection period**: 21 days for parties to object

### Common Investigation Deficiencies
```
DEFICIENCY CHECKLIST (for challenge preparation)

□ Investigator failed to interview key witnesses
□ Investigator did not review critical documents
□ Investigator relied on one party's narrative without verification
□ Home visit was superficial or not conducted
□ Investigator applied wrong legal standard
□ Support calculation contains mathematical errors
□ Investigator did not consider all MCL 722.23 factors
□ Investigation was rushed due to caseload pressure
□ Investigator showed bias in questioning or conclusions
□ Recommendation conflicts with undisputed evidence
```

## FOIA Requests to the FOC

### MCL 15.231 — Freedom of Information Act

**What to request:**
1. Complete investigation file for case number [X]
2. Investigator's notes and communications
3. All documents reviewed during investigation
4. Internal correspondence about the case
5. Time logs showing investigation effort

**FOIA request template:**
```
[Date]

Friend of the Court
[County] County
[Address]

RE: FOIA Request — Case No. [X]

Dear FOC Records Coordinator:

Pursuant to MCL 15.231, I request copies of the following
records relating to Case No. [X]:

1. The complete investigation file, including all notes,
   memoranda, and correspondence
2. All documents submitted by either party and reviewed
   by the investigator
3. The investigator's work product, analysis, and draft
   recommendations
4. Communication logs between the investigator and either
   party or third parties

I request a fee waiver as this information is in the public
interest and I am the subject of the investigation.

If a fee waiver is not granted, please provide a cost estimate
before proceeding.

Sincerely,
[Name]
[Address]
[Phone]
```

**Timeline**: FOC must respond within 5 business days (may extend to 10)
**Cost**: May charge for copies ($0.10/page typical) — fee waiver possible

## MCL 552.505b — Opt-Out of FOC Services

### Eligibility
Parties may opt out if:
- Both parties agree in writing
- No minor child receives public assistance
- Court approves the opt-out agreement

### What Opt-Out Covers
- FOC no longer collects/distributes support payments
- FOC no longer monitors compliance
- FOC no longer provides enforcement services
- Court retains jurisdiction for modifications

### What Opt-Out Does NOT Cover
- Court's authority to modify orders
- Contempt proceedings (still available)
- Party's right to file motions
- Child's right to support
