# Motion Practice Checklist — litigation-supreme-commander

## Universal Motion Components

Every motion filed in Michigan court requires these components.
Check ALL items before marking a motion as complete.

### Required for ALL Motions
- [ ] Caption with correct court, case number, judge, and parties
- [ ] Document title clearly identifying the motion type
- [ ] Statement of relief requested
- [ ] Legal authority supporting the motion
- [ ] Certificate of service (MCR 2.107)
- [ ] Signature block with name, address, phone, bar number (or "Pro Se")
- [ ] Date of filing

### Required for MOST Motions
- [ ] Brief in support (MCR 2.119(A)(2)) — may be combined with motion
- [ ] Proposed order (required by many judges, always recommended)
- [ ] Notice of hearing (if oral argument requested)
- [ ] Verification (MCR 1.109(D)(3)) — required when motion relies on facts outside the record

### Required for SPECIFIC Motions
- [ ] Affidavit(s) in support (for motions based on factual allegations)
- [ ] Exhibits with index and Bates numbers
- [ ] Fee waiver motion (if applicable)

## Motion-Specific Checklists

### Motion for Disqualification (MCR 2.003)
- [ ] Timely filed (within 14 days of discovery of grounds)
- [ ] Specific factual allegations of bias or prejudice
- [ ] Affidavit supporting factual claims
- [ ] Legal standard: MCR 2.003(C)(1) — bias or prejudice
- [ ] Alternative: MCR 2.003(C)(3)(a) — judge would not be fair and impartial
- [ ] Proposed order for disqualification
- [ ] Brief in support citing *Cain v Dep't of Corrections*, 451 Mich 470 (1996)
- [ ] Service on all parties AND the judge's chambers

### Motion for Contempt (MCR 3.606)
- [ ] Specify which court order was violated
- [ ] Attach certified copy of the order alleged to have been violated
- [ ] State specific facts of noncompliance (dates, actions, witnesses)
- [ ] Distinguish civil vs criminal contempt (different burdens of proof)
- [ ] For civil contempt: show respondent has ability to comply
- [ ] Request specific sanctions (fine, jail, attorney fees, makeup parenting time)
- [ ] Proposed order with purge conditions

### Motion for Summary Disposition (MCR 2.116)
- [ ] Identify subsection: (C)(7) immunity, (C)(8) no genuine issue, (C)(10) no factual support
- [ ] Statement of material facts not in dispute
- [ ] Affidavits or documentary evidence supporting undisputed facts
- [ ] Brief addressing each element of each claim/defense
- [ ] 21-day response period for opposing party
- [ ] Proposed order of dismissal

### Motion for Reconsideration (MCR 2.119(F))
- [ ] Filed within 21 days of the order
- [ ] Demonstrate palpable error
- [ ] Show different disposition would result if error corrected
- [ ] Must NOT simply present same issues already ruled upon
- [ ] Brief in support with specific identification of the error
- [ ] Proposed order

### Emergency / Ex Parte Motion (MCR 2.119(F))
- [ ] Demonstrate irreparable injury will result without immediate relief
- [ ] Explain why notice to opposing party was not possible or would defeat purpose
- [ ] Include certification of efforts to notify opposing party
- [ ] Proposed order with expiration date / return hearing date
- [ ] Fee for emergency motion (if applicable)

### Motion to Modify Custody (MCL 722.27)
- [ ] Allege proper cause or change of circumstances (threshold)
- [ ] Address established custodial environment
- [ ] Address all 12 best-interest factors (MCL 722.23(a)-(l))
- [ ] Attach supporting evidence for each factor claimed to favor modification
- [ ] Proposed parenting time schedule
- [ ] Friend of the Court recommendation (if available)

## Common Deficiencies (from prior session audit)

### Deficiency 1: Missing Verification
Motions relying on facts not in the court record require verification per MCR 1.109(D)(3).
The verification is a sworn statement that the facts are true to the best of the signer's knowledge.

### Deficiency 2: Conclusory Statements Without Evidence
"Defendant has repeatedly violated the court order" is conclusory.
Correct: "Defendant failed to make child available for parenting time on [specific dates],
in violation of the [date] Order, paragraph [X]."

### Deficiency 3: Wrong Legal Standard
Applying "abuse of discretion" when the court is making an initial determination (which is de novo).
Always verify the standard of review / legal standard for the specific motion type.

### Deficiency 4: Service on Wrong Party
After Barnes' withdrawal, service to Barnes Law Firm is ineffective.
All service must go directly to Emily A. Watson.

### Deficiency 5: Missing Proposed Order
Many 14th Circuit judges return motions without proposed orders.
Always include one as a default practice.

## Briefing Schedule (MCR 2.119(A)(2))

| Action | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| Motion + Brief filed | Day 0 |
| Response brief due | 21 days after service |
| Reply brief due | 7 days after response |
| Hearing (if requested) | Per court scheduling |

**Time computation per MCR 1.108**: If the period is less than 7 days, exclude
Saturdays, Sundays, and legal holidays. For 7+ day periods, include all days
but if the last day falls on a weekend/holiday, extend to next business day.
