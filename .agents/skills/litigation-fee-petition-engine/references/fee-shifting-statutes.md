# Fee-Shifting Statutes — Michigan Reference

## Michigan Fee-Shifting Provisions

### MCR 2.403(O) — Case Evaluation Sanctions
**Trigger**: Party rejects case evaluation and fails to improve by 10% at trial
**Recovery**: Reasonable attorney fees + costs from rejection date to trial
**Scope**: All circuit court civil actions where evaluation occurred
**Pro se impact**: Pro se pays opposing counsel fees if sanctions apply
**Key case**: *Haliw v City of Sterling Heights*, 471 Mich 700 (2005)

### MCR 2.625 — Taxation of Costs
**Trigger**: Prevailing party in civil action
**Recovery**: Filing fees, service costs, transcript costs, witness fees, copy costs
**Scope**: All civil actions in Michigan courts
**Pro se recovery**: YES — costs (not attorney fees) are recoverable
**Deadline**: 28 days after final judgment

### MCR 2.625(B) — Specific Recoverable Costs
| Cost Item | Authority | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Filing fees | MCR 2.625(B)(1) | All court filing fees |
| Service costs | MCR 2.625(B)(2) | Process server fees |
| Witness fees | MCR 2.625(B)(3) | MCL 600.2552 per diem + mileage |
| Transcript costs | MCR 2.625(B)(4) | Court reporter charges |
| Discovery costs | MCR 2.625(B)(5) | Deposition costs, copies |
| Expert witness fees | MCR 2.625(B)(6) | If authorized by statute or rule |

### MCR 3.206(D) — Attorney Fees in Domestic Relations
**Trigger**: Party unable to pay own fees; other party able to pay
**Recovery**: Reasonable attorney fees in family law proceedings
**Standard**: Need-based — requesting party must show inability to pay
**Pro se impact**: Generally not available — no attorney to pay
**Key limitation**: Court discretion, not automatic

### MCR 2.313 — Discovery Sanctions
**Trigger**: Party fails to comply with discovery obligations
**Recovery**: Reasonable expenses including attorney fees for motion to compel
**Scope**: Discovery disputes in any civil action
**Escalation**: Progressive sanctions up to dismissal/default
**Pro se impact**: Can recover motion costs (filing fees, service)

### MCR 2.114 — Frivolous Filing Sanctions
**Trigger**: Filing frivolous claims, defenses, or motions
**Recovery**: Reasonable attorney fees + costs + sanctions
**Standard**: Not well-grounded in fact, not warranted by law, filed for improper purpose
**Pro se impact**: Can be SANCTIONED for frivolous filings even as pro se

## Federal Fee-Shifting Statutes

### 42 USC § 1988 — Civil Rights Attorney Fees
**Trigger**: Prevailing party in civil rights action (42 USC § 1983)
**Recovery**: Reasonable attorney fees as part of costs
**Scope**: All § 1983, § 1985, § 1986, § 1988 actions
**Pro se impact**: Per *Kay v Ehrler*, pro se generally cannot recover "attorney fees"
**Exception**: Actual litigation costs ARE recoverable under § 1988
**Key case**: *Hensley v Eckerhart*, 461 US 424 (1983) — lodestar method

### 42 USC § 1988 — What Pro Se CAN Recover
Even under *Kay v Ehrler*, pro se prevailing parties can recover:
1. Filing fees
2. Service costs
3. Expert witness fees
4. Travel expenses (for court appearances)
5. Copy and printing costs
6. Postage (certified mail)
7. Transcript costs
8. Other litigation costs

### 15 USC § 1692k — Fair Debt Collection
**Trigger**: Violation of FDCPA
**Recovery**: Statutory damages + attorney fees + costs
**Relevance**: If debt collection involved in any lane

### MCL 445.911 — Michigan Consumer Protection Act
**Trigger**: Violation of MCPA
**Recovery**: Actual damages or $250 + reasonable attorney fees
**Pro se impact**: "Attorney fees" may include costs; court discretion
**Lane B relevance**: Housing-related unfair trade practices

## Fee-Shifting Decision Matrix

### When to Request Fees
| Situation | Statute | Recoverable | Priority |
|-----------|---------|-------------|----------|
| Won after case evaluation | MCR 2.403(O) | Opposing counsel's post-rejection fees | HIGH |
| Won civil rights claim | 42 USC § 1988 | Litigation costs | HIGH |
| Won after discovery abuse | MCR 2.313 | Motion to compel costs | MEDIUM |
| Won domestic relations motion | MCR 3.206(D) | Only if represented | LOW (pro se) |
| Won on MCPA claim | MCL 445.911 | Actual costs + statutory | MEDIUM |
| General prevailing party | MCR 2.625 | Taxable costs only | STANDARD |

### Fee Petition Filing Checklist
```
PRE-FILING CHECKLIST

□ Identify fee-shifting statute(s) that apply
□ Confirm prevailing party status
□ Compile contemporaneous time records
□ Calculate lodestar (if applicable)
□ Compile cost documentation with receipts
□ Segregate recoverable vs. non-recoverable items
□ Apply self-adjustments (block billing, unsuccessful claims)
□ Draft fee petition with required attachments
□ File within deadline (28 days for MCR 2.625)
□ Serve on opposing party

ATTACHMENTS REQUIRED:
□ Itemized time records
□ Rate justification (affidavit or market evidence)
□ Cost receipts and invoices
□ Summary calculation sheet
□ Supporting legal authority for fee request
```
