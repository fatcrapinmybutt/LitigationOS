# Lodestar Method — Fee Petition Reference

## Overview

The lodestar method is the standard approach for calculating reasonable attorney fees in Michigan. It applies to fee-shifting statutes, sanctions, and cost recovery motions.

**Formula**: Lodestar = Reasonable Hours × Reasonable Hourly Rate

## Step 1: Determine Reasonable Hours

### What Counts as Reasonable
Per *Smith v Khouri*, 481 Mich 519 (2008) and *Hensley v Eckerhart*, 461 US 424 (1983):

**Compensable time:**
- Legal research directly related to the case
- Drafting motions, briefs, and pleadings
- Court appearances and preparation
- Discovery (propounding and responding)
- Client meetings and communication
- Deposition preparation and attendance
- Settlement negotiation
- Fee petition preparation (if court awards)

**Non-compensable time (exclude):**
- Duplicative work (two people doing same task)
- Excessive research (more time than reasonably needed)
- Administrative/clerical tasks
- Time on unsuccessful claims (unless interrelated)
- Block-billed entries without task descriptions
- Unreasonable travel time
- Internal conferences without specific purpose

### Time Record Requirements
```
ACCEPTABLE TIME ENTRY:
  Date: 01/15/2025
  Task: Research MCL 722.27 modification standards; reviewed 
        Vodvarka v Grasmeyer and Fletcher v Fletcher for 
        change-of-circumstances analysis
  Time: 2.5 hours
  Claim: Custody modification (Lane A)

UNACCEPTABLE TIME ENTRY:
  Date: 01/15/2025
  Task: Legal research
  Time: 4.0 hours
```

### Block Billing Reduction
If time entries combine multiple tasks without individual time allocation:
- Courts typically reduce block-billed entries by 10-30%
- Better practice: break each task into separate entries
- If block billing is unavoidable, provide percentage estimates

## Step 2: Determine Reasonable Rate

### *Smith v Khouri* Factors (Michigan)
1. **Professional standing and experience** of the attorney
2. **Skill, time, and labor** involved in the case
3. **Amount in question and results obtained**
4. **Difficulty of the case**
5. **Expenses incurred**
6. **Nature and length** of the professional relationship
7. **Likelihood of preclusion** from other employment
8. **Fee customarily charged** in the locality

### Rate Evidence
To establish a reasonable rate:
- Survey of local attorney rates for similar work
- Published fee surveys (State Bar, legal publications)
- Rates charged by opposing counsel (discovery if needed)
- Rates approved in similar cases in the same court

### Rate Ranges by Practice Area (Michigan, approximate)
| Practice Area | Junior (0-5 yrs) | Mid (5-15 yrs) | Senior (15+ yrs) |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Family law | $200-$300 | $300-$400 | $400-$550 |
| Civil litigation | $225-$350 | $350-$475 | $475-$650 |
| Civil rights | $250-$375 | $375-$500 | $500-$700 |
| Appellate | $250-$400 | $400-$550 | $550-$750 |

## Step 3: Apply Adjustments

### Upward Adjustments (Rare)
- Exceptional results justifying enhancement
- Case required extraordinary expertise
- Attorney took case on contingency with significant risk
- Per *Perdue v Kenny A.*, 559 US 542 (2010): enhancements should be rare

### Downward Adjustments (Common)
| Adjustment | Typical Reduction | Reason |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| Block billing | 10-30% | Inability to evaluate individual tasks |
| Excessive research | Varies | More time than reasonably necessary |
| Duplicative work | 100% of duplicate | Two people doing same task |
| Unsuccessful claims | Pro rata | Time on claims that lost |
| Travel time | 50% | Less productive time |
| Clerical tasks | 100% | Not attorney work |

## Pro Se Cost Recovery

### What Pro Se Litigants CAN Recover
Per *Kay v Ehrler*, 499 US 432 (1991) and Michigan law:

| Item | Recoverable? | Authority | Documentation |
|------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| Filing fees | YES | MCR 2.625 | Receipts |
| Service costs | YES | MCR 2.625 | Process server invoice |
| Copy/printing costs | YES | MCR 2.625 | Receipts or log |
| Transcript costs | YES | MCR 2.625 | Court reporter invoice |
| Mileage | YES | MCR 2.625(F) | Mileage log + IRS rate |
| Postage | YES | MCR 2.625 | Receipts |
| Expert witness fees | YES | Statute-dependent | Expert invoice |
| Attorney fees (own time) | NO | *Kay v Ehrler* | N/A |
| Paralegal fees (own time) | NO | *Kay v Ehrler* | N/A |
| Lost wages | MAYBE | Case-dependent | Pay records |

### MCR 2.625 Cost Bill Requirements
- File within **28 days** of final judgment
- Itemize each cost with description and amount
- Attach receipts or other documentation
- Serve on opposing party (who may object within 7 days)
- Court decides disputed items
