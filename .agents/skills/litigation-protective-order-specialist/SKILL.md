## Michigan PPO Statutory Framework

### MCL 600.2950 — Domestic Relationship PPO

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Eligible petitioner** | Person in domestic relationship with respondent |
| **Domestic relationship** | Spouse, former spouse, person with child in common, resident/former resident |
| **Standard** | Reasonable cause to believe respondent may commit prohibited acts |
| **Prohibited acts** | Assault, threats, harassment, stalking, entry of premises, removal of child, interference with custody |
| **Duration** | Not less than 182 days; may be extended |
| **Enforcement** | Violation is criminal contempt; also criminal under MCL 750.411h |

### MCL 600.2950a — Non-Domestic Stalking PPO

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Eligible petitioner** | Any individual |
| **Standard** | Reasonable cause to believe respondent engaged in stalking |
| **Stalking definition** | Willful course of conduct involving repeated harassment causing fear |
| **Duration** | As ordered by court |

### MCR 3.703-3.708 — PPO Proceedings

| Rule | Subject | Key Provision |
|------|---------|---------------|
| MCR 3.703 | Commencement of action | Filing petition, SCAO form CC 375 |
| MCR 3.704 | Ex parte PPO | Court may issue without hearing if immediate danger |
| MCR 3.705 | Service | Personal service required for ex parte PPO |
| MCR 3.706 | Motion to terminate/modify | Respondent's right to hearing within 14 days |
| MCR 3.707 | Hearing | Evidentiary hearing, burden on petitioner |
| MCR 3.708 | Violation proceedings | Arrest, contempt, criminal prosecution |

## Ex Parte PPO Challenge Strategy

### Timeline After Service of Ex Parte PPO

| Day | Action | Authority |
|-----|--------|-----------|
| 0 | Ex parte PPO served | MCR 3.705 |
| 0-14 | **File motion to terminate/modify** | MCR 3.706(A) |
| Within 14 days | **Hearing must be held** | MCR 3.706(C) |
| At hearing | Petitioner bears burden of proof | MCR 3.707(A) |

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL**: File motion to terminate IMMEDIATELY upon service.
> The 14-day hearing window is mandatory — the court MUST hold a hearing.
> The PPO remains in effect until the hearing unless the court modifies earlier.

### Motion to Terminate Grounds

| Ground | Description | Evidence Needed |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **No domestic relationship** | Parties don't meet MCL 600.2950(1) definition | Factual documentation |
| **No reasonable cause** | Insufficient basis for PPO issuance | Rebuttal evidence |
| **False allegations** | Petitioner fabricated claims | Counter-evidence, witness statements |
| **Changed circumstances** | Conditions no longer warrant PPO | Current situation documentation |
| **Overly broad** | PPO restricts more than necessary | Comparison to statutory requirements |
| **Custody interference** | PPO used to gain custody advantage | Timeline showing PPO filed near custody hearing |
| **Retaliatory filing** | PPO filed in retaliation for legitimate action | Timing evidence, motive |

### Hearing Preparation Checklist

- [ ] File written motion to terminate (SCAO form CC 381)
- [ ] Request evidentiary hearing (not just arguments)
- [ ] Prepare witness list and subpoenas
- [ ] Gather counter-evidence for each PPO allegation
- [ ] Prepare timeline showing PPO context (filed near custody events?)
- [ ] Document any PPO violations of respondent's constitutional rights
- [ ] Brief on burden of proof (petitioner must prove, not respondent disprove)
- [ ] Prepare cross-examination of petitioner
- [ ] File proof of service

## PPO-Custody Intersection Analysis

### How PPOs Affect Custody Proceedings

| PPO Impact | Custody Effect | Legal Basis |
|------------|---------------|-------------|
| PPO restricts contact with child | Effectively changes custody | MCL 722.27(1)(c) |
| PPO excludes from marital home | Disrupts established custodial environment | MCL 722.27 |
| PPO issued without hearing | No fact-finding on DV allegations | Due process concern |
| PPO used as custody tool | Bad faith litigation tactic | MCL 722.23(j) — factor (j) |
| PPO violation allegation | Arrest risk during parenting time | MCL 750.411h |

### Strategic Considerations

1. **Document the timing**: If PPO was filed within 30 days of a custody
   filing or hearing, document this as evidence of litigation motive.
2. **Separate the forums**: PPO proceedings (2023-5907-PP) are separate
   from custody (2024-001507-DC), but judges may consider both.
3. **Protect parenting time**: If PPO restricts contact, immediately
   move for parenting time order that addresses PPO constraints.
4. **Factor (j) impact**: False PPO filings undermine factor (j) —
   willingness to facilitate parent-child relationship.

## False PPO Documentation Protocol

When a PPO is believed to be filed in bad faith:

### Evidence to Collect

| Category | Items | Purpose |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Timing | Filing date vs. custody events | Show tactical motivation |
| Prior history | Communication records before filing | Contradict "fear" claim |
| Witness accounts | Third-party observations | Rebut specific allegations |
| Digital evidence | Texts/emails showing normal interaction | Contradict harassment claims |
| Pattern evidence | Prior false reports or dismissed claims | Show pattern of false accusations |
| Motive evidence | Custody filings, property disputes | Explain alternative motivation |

### Bad Faith Indicators

```
Score each indicator (0-3):
  0 = Not present
  1 = Possible
  2 = Probable
  3 = Documented

Indicators:
□ PPO filed within 30 days of custody action
□ PPO allegations contradict prior communications
□ Petitioner continued voluntary contact after alleged incidents
□ No police reports for alleged incidents
□ PPO scope targets custody/parenting time specifically
□ Prior dismissed PPO or false report history
□ Witnesses contradict petitioner's account
□ Petitioner made admissions undermining PPO basis

Total Score:
  0-6:   Standard PPO — defend on merits
  7-14:  Suspicious — argue bad faith at hearing
  15-24: Strong bad faith case — seek sanctions under MCR 1.109(E)
```

## PPO Violation Defense

### MCL 750.411h — Criminal Violation

| Element | Requirement | Defense |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Valid PPO | PPO was properly issued and served | Challenge validity |
| Knowledge | Respondent knew of PPO | Challenge notice/service |
| Willful violation | Intentional act violating PPO terms | Show no willful intent |
| Specific term violated | Identify exact PPO provision | Challenge vagueness |

### Defense Strategies

1. **Challenge PPO validity** — Was it properly issued? Proper jurisdiction?
2. **Challenge service** — Was respondent properly served? Personal service required.
3. **No willful violation** — Accidental contact, unavoidable encounter.
4. **PPO terms ambiguous** — If PPO language is unclear, violation requires specific intent.
5. **Constitutional challenge** — PPO restricts protected speech or association.
6. **Entrapment** — Petitioner initiated contact to create violation.
