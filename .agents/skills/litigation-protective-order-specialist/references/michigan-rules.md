# Michigan Rules — Protective Order Specialist

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 3.701-3.709 — Personal Protection Orders
- **MCR 3.701**: Scope — applies to domestic relationship and non-domestic PPOs
- **MCR 3.703**: Issuance of PPO
  - **(A)**: Ex parte issuance without hearing when petitioner shows immediate danger
  - **(B)**: Court reviews petition and determines whether statutory criteria are met
  - **(C)**: PPO must specify prohibited conduct
- **MCR 3.705**: Service of PPO
  - **(A)**: Must be served on respondent by law enforcement or other authorized person
  - **(B)**: PPO is effective upon signing; respondent bound upon service or actual notice
- **MCR 3.707**: Modification and Termination
  - **(A)**: Either party may move to modify or terminate
  - **(B)**: Hearing required before modification
- **MCR 3.708**: Violation and Contempt
  - **(A)**: PPO violation is criminal contempt, not civil
  - **(B)**: Independent criminal charges may also apply
- **MCR 3.706**: Hearing on PPO
  - **(A)**: Respondent may request hearing within **14 days** of service
  - **(B)**: Hearing scheduled within **14 days** of request
  - **(C)**: Petitioner bears burden of proof by preponderance of evidence

### MCR 3.206(D) — Emergency Ex Parte Orders (Domestic Relations)
- Temporary custody/parenting time orders when PPO affects access
- Emergency provisions when child safety is at issue

### MCR 2.506 — Subpoenas (for PPO Hearing Evidence)
- Subpoena witnesses for PPO hearing
- Subpoena documents and records

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.2950 — Domestic Relationship PPO
- **(1)**: Petition may be filed if petitioner has "reasonable cause to believe" respondent may commit one or more of the following:
  - Assault or battery (MCL 750.81, 750.82)
  - Stalking (MCL 750.411h, 750.411i)
  - Threatening behavior
  - Removal of minor child in violation of custody order
  - Purchase or possession of firearms
  - Interference with petitioner's efforts to remove children or personal property
  - Entry onto petitioner's property or into petitioner's dwelling
- **(4)**: "Domestic relationship" defined: spouse, former spouse, dating relationship, cohabitant, parent of common child
- **(10)**: Duration — PPO remains in effect until modified or rescinded
- **(12)**: Respondent's right to request hearing within **14 days** of service
- **(13)**: Hearing on whether PPO should continue, modify, or terminate

### MCL 600.2950a — Non-Domestic Stalking PPO
- **(1)**: Petition based on stalking (MCL 750.411h) or aggravated stalking (MCL 750.411i)
- No domestic relationship required
- Same procedural framework as domestic PPO

### MCL 600.2950h — Minor Children PPO
- Petition filed on behalf of minor child
- Parent or guardian may petition

### MCL 764.15b — Arrest for PPO Violation
- Law enforcement shall arrest without warrant for PPO violation
- Criminal contempt: up to **93 days** imprisonment and/or $500 fine
- Aggravated violation: felony charges possible

### MCL 750.411h — Stalking
- Definition: willful course of conduct involving repeated or continuing harassment
- Misdemeanor: up to 1 year imprisonment and/or $1,000 fine
- Relevant to both domestic and non-domestic PPO petitions

### MCL 750.411i — Aggravated Stalking
- Stalking with credible threat or in violation of court order
- Felony: up to 5 years imprisonment and/or $10,000 fine

### MCL 722.23(k) — Best Interest Factor: Domestic Violence
- Court must consider domestic violence regardless of whether PPO exists
- PPO history is relevant but not dispositive of factor (k)
- Absence of PPO does not mean absence of domestic violence

### MCL 722.27a(6) — Parenting Time and PPO
- PPO may restrict or condition parenting time
- Court must balance child safety with parenting time rights
- Supervised visitation may be ordered

## Key Michigan Case Law

### PPO Standards
- **Hayford v Hayford**, 279 Mich App 324 (2008) — PPO must be based on reasonable cause to believe enumerated conduct will occur; generalized fear is insufficient
- **Pickering v Pickering**, 253 Mich App 694 (2002) — Court must consider the totality of circumstances in evaluating PPO petition
- **Maldonado v Ford Motor Co**, 476 Mich 372 (2006) — Stalking requires willful course of conduct; isolated incidents may not suffice

### PPO and Custody
- **Brandt v Brandt**, 250 Mich App 68 (2002) — PPO restrictions must be considered in custody and parenting time determinations
- **Brown v Brown**, 332 Mich App 1 (2020) — Court must make independent findings on domestic violence factor; cannot rely solely on existence or absence of PPO

### PPO Violations
- **In re Contempt of Dougherty**, 429 Mich 81 (1987) — Criminal contempt for PPO violation requires proof beyond reasonable doubt
- **People v Coones**, 216 Mich App 721 (1996) — PPO violation is a criminal offense; respondent entitled to due process protections

## Checklists

### PPO Petition Checklist
- [ ] Identify correct form: CC 375 (domestic) or CC 376 (non-domestic)
- [ ] Verify domestic relationship under MCL 600.2950(4)
- [ ] Document specific incidents with dates and details
- [ ] Identify which enumerated conduct applies (MCL 600.2950(1))
- [ ] Attach supporting evidence (police reports, messages, photos)
- [ ] State whether minor children are involved
- [ ] Request specific prohibited conduct (contact, proximity, firearms)
- [ ] File with court — no filing fee for PPO petitions

### PPO Defense Checklist (If Served with PPO)
- [ ] Calendar **14-day** deadline to request hearing
- [ ] File motion requesting hearing (MCR 3.706)
- [ ] Gather evidence contradicting petitioner's claims
- [ ] Identify witnesses for hearing
- [ ] Prepare cross-examination of petitioner
- [ ] Understand: burden is on petitioner (preponderance standard)

### Filing Deadlines
- PPO petition: No deadline (file when reasonable cause exists)
- Hearing request by respondent: **14 days** from service (MCL 600.2950(12))
- Hearing scheduled: Within **14 days** of request (MCR 3.706(B))
- Motion to modify/terminate: No fixed deadline; file when grounds exist
