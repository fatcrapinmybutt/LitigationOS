## Motion to Modify/Terminate PPO

```
MOTION TO MODIFY/TERMINATE PERSONAL PROTECTION ORDER

    [Party] moves pursuant to MCR 3.707 and MCL 600.2950 to
[modify / terminate] the Personal Protection Order entered [DATE]:

GROUNDS:
    1. [If modifying]: The PPO should be modified to [specific change]
       because [changed circumstances, new facts, practical need].

    2. [If terminating]: The PPO should be terminated because:
       a. The conditions giving rise to the PPO no longer exist;
       b. [Specific facts showing change in circumstances];
       c. Continued enforcement is no longer necessary.

    3. Supporting evidence: [attach exhibits]

RELIEF REQUESTED:
    a. Hearing on this motion per MCR 3.707(B);
    b. [Modification of specific provisions / termination of PPO];
    c. Such other relief as the Court deems just.
```

## PPO Impact on Custody — Analysis Template

| PPO Provision | Custody Impact | Parenting Time Impact | Mitigation |
|--------------|---------------|---------------------|------------|
| No contact | Cannot co-parent directly | Exchange logistics complicated | Use parenting app (e.g., OFW) |
| Distance restriction | Cannot attend same events | Drop-off/pick-up locations limited | Designate neutral exchange site |
| No firearms | None directly | N/A | Verify compliance |
| No third-party contact | Cannot use mutual friends | Communication limited | Court-approved communication method |

## Workflow: PPO Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Document specific incidents meeting statutory criteria
    ↓
Step 2: Complete SCAO form CC 375 (domestic) or CC 376 (non-domestic)
    ↓
Step 3: Write supporting statement with specific facts
    ↓
Step 4: File with court (no filing fee)
    ↓
Step 5: Court reviews and enters ex parte PPO (if criteria met)
    ↓
Step 6: PPO served on respondent by law enforcement
    ↓
Step 7: Respondent has 14 days to request hearing
    ↓
    ├── If no hearing requested → PPO remains in effect
    ↓
    ├── If hearing requested → Prepare for evidentiary hearing
    ↓
Step 8: Hearing — petitioner bears burden (preponderance)
    ↓
    ├── PPO continued → remains in effect
    ├── PPO modified → new terms apply
    ├── PPO dissolved → no longer in effect
    ↓
Step 9: Document any violations; report to police
    ↓
Step 10: Coordinate with custody case (factor (k) implications)
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Vague allegations | Court cannot enter PPO without specific facts | Document exact dates, times, conduct, evidence |
| Using PPO as custody tactic | Court will see through it; damages all credibility | File only when genuine safety concern exists |
| Violating your own PPO | Undermines the order; may lead to dissolution | Maintain strict compliance with all PPO terms |
| Not preparing for hearing | PPO dissolved because petitioner unprepared | Treat hearing like trial — witnesses, exhibits, testimony |
| Ignoring PPO in custody case | Missing factor (k) argument opportunity | Address PPO and DV in custody proceedings explicitly |
| Not reporting violations | Pattern not documented; enforcement impossible | Report every violation to police immediately |
