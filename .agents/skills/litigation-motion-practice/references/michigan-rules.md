# Michigan Rules — Motion Practice

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.119 — Motion Practice
- **(A)(1)**: Motions must be in writing unless made during hearing or trial
- **(A)(2)**: Each motion must be accompanied by a brief citing supporting authorities; response brief due within **21 days** after service
- **(A)(3)**: Reply brief permitted within **7 days** after service of response
- **(B)**: Ex parte orders — only when irreparable injury would result from waiting for notice; must show by specific facts why notice should not be given
- **(C)(1)**: Notice of hearing must be served at least **9 days** before hearing
- **(C)(2)**: If served by mail, add **5 days** (total: **14 days** before hearing)
- **(D)**: Proposed order must accompany every motion
- **(E)(1)**: Court may decide motion on briefs without oral argument
- **(E)(2)**: Party may request oral argument; court has discretion to grant
- **(F)**: Adjournment of motions — stipulated or for good cause

### MCR 2.116 — Summary Disposition
- **(C)(1)-(10)**: Ten grounds for summary disposition
- **(C)(7)**: No genuine issue of material fact — moving party's burden
- **(C)(8)**: Opposing party failed to state a claim
- **(C)(10)**: No genuine issue of material fact (with documentary evidence)
- **(G)(1)**: Timing — may be filed any time, but typically after discovery
- **(G)(4)**: Court may convert motion under one subsection to another
- **(I)**: If motion under (C)(10), court must give non-moving party opportunity to present evidence

### MCR 2.612 — Relief from Judgment or Order
- **(C)(1)**: Grounds: (a) mistake, (b) newly discovered evidence, (c) fraud, (d) void judgment, (e) satisfied judgment, (f) other reason justifying relief
- **(C)(2)**: Motion must be filed within reasonable time, and for (a)-(c) within **1 year** of the judgment
- **(C)(3)**: Does not limit court's power to set aside void judgments

### MCR 2.611 — New Trial: Amendment of Judgment
- **(A)(1)**: Grounds: (a)-(h) including irregularity, misconduct, surprise, newly discovered evidence, excessive damages, verdict against weight of evidence, error of law
- **(B)**: Motion must be filed within **21 days** after entry of judgment
- **(D)**: Motion for rehearing in non-jury cases

### MCR 2.119 Cross-References for Domestic Relations
### MCR 3.206 — Interim and Permanent Orders
- **(C)**: Temporary orders may be entered on motion
- **(D)**: Ex parte temporary support orders in emergency

### MCR 3.207 — Domestic Relations: Motions for Change of Domicile
- Special motion requirements for parental domicile change
- 100-mile rule (MCL 722.31)
- Burden of proof on moving party

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.2591 — Frivolous Claims and Defenses
- **(1)**: Court shall award costs and fees if claim or defense is frivolous
- **(3)**: "Frivolous" defined: (a) not reasonably supported by law, (b) no reasonable basis in fact, (c) filed for improper purpose

### MCL 722.27 — Modification of Custody
- **(1)(c)**: Motion to modify custody requires proper cause or change of circumstances
- Threshold determination before best interest analysis

### MCL 722.27a — Parenting Time
- Motion to modify parenting time
- Clear and convincing evidence standard if established custodial environment affected

### MCL 722.31 — Change of Domicile
- Motion required before moving child's domicile more than 100 miles
- Five statutory factors for court consideration
- Burden on moving parent

### MCL 600.1701 — Contempt
- Motion for contempt when party violates court order
- Show cause order procedure
- Civil vs. criminal contempt distinction

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Motion Practice Standards
- **Maiden v Rozwood**, 461 Mich 109 (1999) — Summary disposition standards under MCR 2.116(C)(10); moving party must identify issues with no genuine dispute; court views evidence in light most favorable to non-moving party
- **Quinto v Cross & Peters Co**, 451 Mich 358 (1996) — Non-moving party must set forth specific facts showing genuine issue of material fact; mere speculation insufficient
- **Ben P Fyke & Sons v Gunter Co**, 390 Mich 649 (1973) — Court should exercise caution in granting summary disposition; purpose is to test factual support, not determine issues of fact

### Motion Timing
- **Peterson v Auto Owners Ins Co**, 274 Mich App 407 (2007) — Summary disposition premature when discovery not yet complete and relevant facts remain to be established
- **Coblentz v City of Novi**, 475 Mich 558 (2006) — Trial court has broad discretion in managing motion practice

### Contempt Motions
- **In re Contempt of Henry**, 282 Mich App 656 (2009) — Civil contempt requires clear and convincing evidence of ability to comply and willful failure
- **Porter v Porter**, 285 Mich App 450 (2009) — In domestic relations, contempt is often the appropriate remedy for parenting time violations

## Checklists

### Motion Filing Checklist
- [ ] Motion in writing (MCR 2.119(A)(1))
- [ ] Brief with citations to MCR, MCL, and case law (MCR 2.119(A)(2))
- [ ] Proposed order attached (MCR 2.119(D))
- [ ] Proof of service on all parties
- [ ] Proper notice period: 9 days (14 if mailed) (MCR 2.119(C))
- [ ] Request for oral argument if desired
- [ ] Filing fee paid (if applicable)
- [ ] Exhibits attached and properly referenced

### Motion Response Checklist
- [ ] Calendar 21-day response deadline (MCR 2.119(A)(2))
- [ ] Draft response brief with counter-authorities
- [ ] Attach exhibits supporting your position
- [ ] File proposed counter-order if applicable
- [ ] Request oral argument if desired
- [ ] Serve on all parties with proof of service

### Filing Deadlines
- Response brief: **21 days** after service (MCR 2.119(A)(2))
- Reply brief: **7 days** after service of response (MCR 2.119(A)(3))
- Notice of hearing: **9 days** before (14 by mail) (MCR 2.119(C))
- Motion for new trial: **21 days** after judgment (MCR 2.611(B))
- Motion for relief from judgment: within **1 year** for (a)-(c) (MCR 2.612(C)(2))
- Motion for reconsideration: **21 days** after order (MCR 2.119(F)(1))
