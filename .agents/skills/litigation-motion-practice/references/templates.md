# Templates — Motion Practice

## General Motion Template

```
STATE OF MICHIGAN
IN THE 14TH CIRCUIT COURT FOR THE COUNTY OF MUSKEGON
FAMILY DIVISION

ANDREW JAMES PIGORS,
    Plaintiff,                          Case No. 2024-001507-DC
                                        Hon. Jenny L. McNeill
v.

EMILY A. WATSON,
    Defendant.
_________________________________/

PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR [TITLE]

    Plaintiff, Andrew James Pigors, respectfully moves this Court
pursuant to [MCR section] for an order [specific relief sought]:

FACTUAL BASIS:
    1. [Fact 1 — with dates and specifics]
    2. [Fact 2]
    3. [Fact 3]

LEGAL BASIS:
    4. Under [MCR/MCL section], [legal standard applies because...].
    5. [Additional legal authority and application to facts]

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff requests this Court:
    a. [Specific relief item 1];
    b. [Specific relief item 2];
    c. Such other relief as the Court deems just and equitable.

    Plaintiff requests oral argument on this motion.

Respectfully submitted,

____________________________
Andrew James Pigors, Pro Se
[Address]
[Phone]
Date: _______________
```

## Supporting Brief Template

```
BRIEF IN SUPPORT OF PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR [TITLE]

I. STATEMENT OF FACTS
    [Concise factual narrative with record citations]

II. LEGAL STANDARD
    [Standard of review / applicable legal standard]

III. ARGUMENT
    A. [First Argument Heading]
       [Analysis with citations to MCR, MCL, and case law]

    B. [Second Argument Heading]
       [Analysis]

    C. [Third Argument Heading if needed]
       [Analysis]

IV. CONCLUSION
    For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff requests [specific relief].

Respectfully submitted,

____________________________
Andrew James Pigors, Pro Se
Date: _______________
```

## Response Brief Template

```
BRIEF IN OPPOSITION TO DEFENDANT'S MOTION FOR [TITLE]

I. COUNTER-STATEMENT OF FACTS
    [Correct/supplement the movant's factual presentation]

II. LEGAL STANDARD
    [The proper standard, if different from movant's characterization]

III. ARGUMENT
    A. [Why movant's first argument fails]
    B. [Why movant's second argument fails]
    C. [Affirmative arguments for denial]

IV. CONCLUSION
    Defendant's motion should be denied.
```

## Ex Parte Motion Template

```
EX PARTE MOTION FOR [EMERGENCY RELIEF]

    Plaintiff moves ex parte under MCR 2.119(B) because:

    1. IRREPARABLE INJURY: [Describe the immediate, irreparable harm
       that will occur if relief is not granted before notice can be
       provided to the opposing party]

    2. SPECIFIC FACTS: [Detail the emergency circumstances with dates]

    3. EFFORTS TO GIVE NOTICE: [Describe what efforts were made to
       notify the opposing party, or why notice would itself cause
       the irreparable injury]

    4. RELIEF SOUGHT: [Precise, limited relief requested]

    This motion is supported by the attached affidavit.
```

## Workflow: Motion Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Identify need for motion and correct MCR basis
    ↓
Step 2: Research legal authority (MCR, MCL, case law)
    ↓
Step 3: Draft motion with supporting brief and exhibits
    ↓
Step 4: Prepare proposed order
    ↓
Step 5: Calculate notice period (9 days + 5 for mail)
    ↓
Step 6: Serve on all parties with proof of service
    ↓
Step 7: File with court clerk
    ↓
Step 8: Attend hearing (if oral argument granted)
    ↓
Step 9: If granted → obtain signed order → serve
    ↓
Step 9a: If denied → evaluate appeal / reconsideration
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Motion without brief | Court may deny without consideration | Always attach supporting brief with citations |
| Late response | Motion may be granted as unopposed | Calendar deadline immediately; file timely |
| Insufficient notice | Hearing vacated; motion delayed | Calculate notice from hearing date backward |
| No proposed order | Court cannot act even if inclined to grant | Attach proposed order to every motion |
| Citing only cases, no rules | Missing the procedural foundation | Always cite the MCR/MCL first, then cases |
| Overly long briefs | Courts have limited time; key points buried | Concise briefs: 10 pages max unless complex |
