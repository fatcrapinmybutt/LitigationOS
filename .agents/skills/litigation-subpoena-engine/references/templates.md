```
    The following documents are requested for production:

    1. All records pertaining to [subject] for the period [DATE]
       through [DATE], including but not limited to:
       a. [Specific record type]
       b. [Specific record type]
       c. [Specific record type]

    2. All records of [specific category] between [parties/accounts]
       for the period [DATE] through [DATE].

    3. Certified copies of [specific documents].

INSTRUCTIONS:
    - If any requested record does not exist, provide a sworn statement
      to that effect.
    - If any record is withheld on privilege grounds, provide a privilege
      log identifying: the document, its date, author, recipients, and
      the privilege claimed.
    - Production may be made by mail to the address below at least
      [X] days before the appearance date.

____________________________
Andrew James Pigors, Pro Se
[Address]
```

## Motion to Quash Subpoena (Defense Template)

```
MOTION TO QUASH SUBPOENA

    [Party/Non-Party] moves to quash the subpoena issued [DATE]
pursuant to MCR 2.506(F):

GROUNDS:
    1. The subpoena is unduly burdensome because: [specifics]
    2. The requested records are protected by [privilege type]
       because: [explanation]
    3. The subpoena is overbroad because: [specifics — suggest
       narrowed scope that would be acceptable]

ALTERNATIVE RELIEF:
    If the Court does not quash, [Party] requests:
    a. Narrowing the scope to: [proposed narrower scope]
    b. Protective order limiting use of records to this case
    c. In camera review of disputed records
```

## Workflow: Subpoena Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Identify needed records/testimony
    ↓
Step 2: Check for privilege issues (medical, attorney-client)
    ↓
Step 3: Obtain authorization/court order if privileged
    ↓
Step 4: Request subpoena from clerk (pro se) / issue (attorney)
    ↓
Step 5: Complete SCAO form MC 11 or MC 11a
    ↓
Step 6: Calculate and prepare witness fee + mileage
    ↓
Step 7: Arrange personal service on witness
    ↓
Step 8: Tender fees at time of service
    ↓
Step 9: File proof of service (MC 11b)
    ↓
Step 10: Monitor for compliance or objection
    ↓
    ├── If compliance → receive and review records
    ├── If objection → respond to motion to quash
    ├── If no response → move for contempt (MCR 2.506(E))
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Self-issuing subpoena (pro se) | Only attorneys can issue; unenforceable | Request issuance from court clerk |
| No witness fee tender | Subpoena is unenforceable | Calculate and tender fee at service |
| Overbroad document request | Gets quashed for undue burden | Be specific about records needed |
| Ignoring HIPAA for medical records | Records custodian won't comply | Follow HIPAA procedures with authorization |
| Last-minute service | Witness can't comply; gets quashed | Serve at least 14 days before trial |
| Subpoenaing privileged records | Quashed; may invite sanctions | Research privileges before issuing |
