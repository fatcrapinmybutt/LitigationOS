# Michigan Rules — Subpoena Engine

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.506 — Subpoenas
- **(A)(1)**: Subpoenas may be issued by: (a) court clerk, (b) attorney of record
- **(A)(2)**: Pro se litigants must request subpoena issuance from the clerk
- **(B)**: Types of subpoenas:
  - Subpoena to testify (ad testificandum)
  - Subpoena to produce documents/things (duces tecum)
  - Combined subpoena (testify and produce)
- **(C)**: Service requirements:
  - Personal service on the witness
  - Reasonable time before appearance required
  - Must include date, time, and place of appearance
- **(D)**: Witness fees and mileage:
  - **$12.00 per day** witness fee (MCL 600.2552)
  - **Mileage** at current IRS rate per mile (round trip)
  - Fees must be tendered at time of service
- **(E)**: Contempt for failure to comply:
  - Court may hold non-compliant witness in contempt
  - Court may issue bench warrant for arrest
- **(F)**: Motion to quash or modify:
  - Witness/party may move to quash for undue burden, expense, or privilege
  - Court balances need for evidence against burden on witness
- **(G)**: Subpoena for deposition — must comply with MCR 2.305 and 2.306

### MCR 2.305 — Subpoena for Taking Deposition
- **(A)**: Deposition subpoenas for testimony or document production
- **(B)**: Non-party deposition subpoena requirements
- **(C)**: Location restrictions — must be within the state or within 100 miles

### MCR 2.310 — Production of Documents from Non-Parties
- Production requests to non-parties require subpoena duces tecum
- Non-party has same rights to object as a party

### MCR 2.302(C) — Protective Orders
- Court may limit subpoena scope to protect from annoyance, embarrassment, oppression
- Protective order for sensitive or confidential information
- In camera review of disputed documents

### MCR 3.206 — Domestic Relations: Subpoena Use
- Subpoenas available in domestic relations discovery
- Third-party financial records often subpoenaed in family cases

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.1455 — Subpoena Power of Courts
- All Michigan courts have subpoena power
- Geographic limitations on service (within Michigan)
- Interstate subpoenas require Uniform Interstate Depositions Act

### MCL 600.2552 — Witness Fees
- **(1)**: Witnesses entitled to **$12.00 per day** for attendance
- **(2)**: Mileage at statutory rate (currently matches IRS rate)
- **(3)**: Expert witnesses — reasonable fee as set by court

### MCL 600.1701 — Contempt for Subpoena Violation
- Civil contempt for non-compliance with subpoena
- Court may fine and/or imprison until compliance
- Criminal contempt for willful defiance

### MCL 600.2157 — Physician-Patient Privilege
- Medical records require patient authorization or court order
- Subpoena alone is insufficient for privileged medical records
- Patient must be notified before records are released

### MCL 333.16281 — Mental Health Records
- Stricter protections than general medical records
- Requires specific authorization or court order
- HIPAA and state law both apply

### MCL 600.2164 — Spousal Privilege
- Privilege against testifying against spouse in certain situations
- Limited applicability in family law (does not protect financial matters)

### MCL 600.2163 — Attorney-Client Privilege
- Communications with attorney are privileged
- Privilege belongs to client, not attorney
- Crime-fraud exception applies

### MCL 445.72 — Financial Records Privacy
- Bank records require proper legal process
- Customer notification may be required
- Follow financial institution's subpoena compliance procedures

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Subpoena Scope and Enforcement
- **Domako v Rowe**, 184 Mich App 137 (1990) — Subpoena scope is broad; records must be relevant to claims or defenses
- **Hamed v Wayne County**, 271 Mich App 106 (2006) — Court has discretion to limit subpoena scope; balances relevance against burden
- **In re Subpoena Duces Tecum**, 144 Mich App 750 (1985) — Non-party subpoena duces tecum must be specific enough to identify the requested records

### Privilege in Subpoena Context
- **Leibel v General Motors Corp**, 250 Mich App 229 (2002) — Privilege must be asserted specifically; blanket objection is insufficient
- **Swickard v Wayne County Medical Examiner**, 438 Mich 536 (1991) — Balancing test for privileged records: relevance and necessity weighed against privacy interest

### Contempt for Non-Compliance
- **In re Contempt of Henry**, 282 Mich App 656 (2009) — Contempt requires clear and convincing evidence of ability to comply and willful refusal
- **Porter v Porter**, 285 Mich App 450 (2009) — Court has broad discretion in enforcing subpoenas through contempt proceedings

## SCAO Forms
- **MC 11**: Subpoena (general)
- **MC 11a**: Subpoena — Order to Produce (duces tecum)
- **MC 11b**: Return of Service of Subpoena

## Checklists

### Subpoena Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Identify specific records or testimony needed
- [ ] Determine if privilege applies; obtain authorization if needed
- [ ] Request subpoena issuance from court clerk (pro se)
- [ ] Complete SCAO form MC 11 or MC 11a
- [ ] Calculate witness fee ($12/day) and mileage
- [ ] Arrange for personal service on witness
- [ ] Allow reasonable time before appearance
- [ ] Prepare proof of service form (MC 11b)
- [ ] Retain copy of served subpoena

### Medical Records Subpoena Checklist
- [ ] Obtain patient authorization (HIPAA compliant) OR
- [ ] Obtain court order for release OR
- [ ] Serve subpoena with HIPAA-compliant notice to patient
- [ ] Include qualified protective order if required
- [ ] Allow 14+ days for records custodian to respond
- [ ] Comply with MCL 600.2157 (physician-patient privilege)

### Filing Deadlines
- Trial subpoena service: At least **14 days** before hearing (recommended)
- Deposition subpoena: Per MCR 2.305 timing requirements
- Motion to quash: Before the date of compliance
- Witness fee tender: At time of service (MCR 2.506(D))
