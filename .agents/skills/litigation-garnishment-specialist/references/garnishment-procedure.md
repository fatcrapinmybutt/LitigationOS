# Garnishment Procedure — Michigan Reference

## Statutory Framework

### MCL 600.4001 — General Garnishment Authorization
Michigan allows garnishment of debts owed to and property held for a judgment debtor by a third party (the garnishee).

### Types of Garnishment

| Type | Statute | Target | Duration |
|------|---------|--------|----------|
| Non-periodic (one-time) | MCL 600.4011 | Bank accounts, one-time debts | One-time sweep |
| Periodic (wage) | MCL 600.4012 | Wages, salary, commissions | Ongoing per pay period |
| Continuing periodic | MCL 600.4061 | Same as periodic, automatic renewal | Continuous until satisfied |
| Child support withholding | MCL 552.625f | Income of support obligor | Continuous |

## Step-by-Step Garnishment Process

### Step 1: Obtain a Judgment
- Must have a final, enforceable money judgment
- Judgment must be entered by the court (MCR 2.602)
- Judgment amount includes: principal + interest + costs

### Step 2: Request for Garnishment (Verified Statement)
```
REQUIREMENTS FOR VERIFIED STATEMENT (SCAO MC 14):
□ Judgment creditor's name and address
□ Judgment debtor's name and address
□ Garnishee's name and address
□ Court and case number where judgment entered
□ Date of judgment
□ Amount of judgment (including interest and costs)
□ Amount previously collected
□ Amount remaining due
□ Verified (sworn) by judgment creditor
```

### Step 3: Obtain Writ of Garnishment
- File verified statement with court clerk
- Pay garnishment filing fee ($15-$25)
- Clerk issues writ of garnishment
- Writ specifies: maximum amount to garnish, garnishee obligations

### Step 4: Serve the Writ
Per MCR 3.101(D):
- Serve writ on garnishee (employer, bank, etc.)
- Serve copy on judgment debtor
- File proof of service with court

**Service methods:**
| Garnishee Type | Service Method |
|---------------|----------------|
| Corporation | Registered agent or officer (MCR 2.105(D)) |
| Individual | Personal service (MCR 2.105(A)) |
| Bank | Branch manager or designated agent |
| Government employer | Designated payroll office |

### Step 5: Garnishee Response
Per MCR 3.101(G):
- Garnishee must file disclosure within **28 days** of service
- Disclosure states: whether garnishee holds property/owes debt to debtor
- If yes: amount held/owed
- If no: denial of liability
- Garnishee must also:
  - Freeze non-exempt funds (bank garnishment)
  - Begin withholding (wage garnishment)

### Step 6: Collection and Distribution
- Garnishee remits funds to court or creditor (per local practice)
- Creditor applies payment to judgment
- File satisfaction of judgment when fully paid

## Federal Exemption Limits

### 15 USC § 1673 — Consumer Credit Protection Act (CCPA)

**Regular judgment garnishment:**
Maximum garnishment per pay period is the LESSER of:
- 25% of disposable earnings, OR
- Amount by which disposable earnings exceed 30 × federal minimum wage

```
CALCULATION EXAMPLE (2024):
  Gross wages:              $3,000/biweekly
  Mandatory deductions:     $750 (tax, FICA, mandatory retirement)
  Disposable earnings:      $2,250

  25% of disposable:        $562.50
  30 × $7.25 × 2 weeks:    $435 (federal minimum × 30 × 2)
  Disposable minus threshold: $2,250 - $435 = $1,815

  Maximum garnishment:      $562.50 (lesser of $562.50 and $1,815)
```

### Child Support Garnishment (Exception to CCPA)
Higher limits apply:
| Situation | Maximum Garnishment |
|-----------|-------------------|
| No current spouse/dependents + less than 12 weeks arrears | 60% of disposable |
| No current spouse/dependents + 12+ weeks arrears | 65% of disposable |
| Current spouse/dependents + less than 12 weeks arrears | 50% of disposable |
| Current spouse/dependents + 12+ weeks arrears | 55% of disposable |

### Fully Exempt Income (Cannot Be Garnished)

| Income Type | Exemption Statute | Amount Exempt |
|-------------|------------------|---------------|
| Social Security | 42 USC § 407 | 100% |
| SSI | 42 USC § 1383(d)(1) | 100% |
| VA benefits | 38 USC § 5301 | 100% |
| ERISA pension/retirement | 29 USC § 1056(d) | Generally 100% |
| Railroad retirement | 45 USC § 231m | 100% |
| Federal employee retirement | 5 USC § 8346 | 100% (with exceptions) |
| Military active duty pay | 50 USC § 3931 (SCRA) | Special protections |
| Workers' compensation | MCL 418.821 | 100% |
| Unemployment benefits | MCL 421.30 | Generally exempt |
| Public assistance | Various | 100% |

## Michigan-Specific Garnishment Rules

### MCR 3.101 — Garnishment Procedure

**MCR 3.101(D)**: Service of writ on garnishee
**MCR 3.101(G)**: Garnishee's 28-day disclosure obligation
**MCR 3.101(H)**: Default against garnishee for failure to disclose
**MCR 3.101(I)**: Objections and hearing process

### Objection Process
Either the judgment debtor or garnishee may object:
- File objection within 14 days of disclosure
- Court schedules hearing
- Debtor may claim exemptions at hearing
- Court determines exempt and non-exempt amounts

### SCAO Garnishment Forms
| Form | Use |
|------|-----|
| MC 12 | Request and Writ of Garnishment (Periodic) |
| MC 13 | Request and Writ of Garnishment (Non-Periodic) |
| MC 14 | Garnishee Disclosure |
| MC 15 | Objection to Garnishment |
| MC 52 | Request and Writ for Installment Payment Order |

## Garnishment Filing Checklist
```
PRE-FILING:
□ Judgment entered and enforceable
□ Judgment debtor's employer/bank identified
□ Exemption analysis completed
□ Correct garnishment type selected
□ SCAO form completed (MC 12 or MC 13)

FILING:
□ Verified statement sworn and signed
□ Filing fee paid ($15-$25)
□ Writ issued by clerk
□ Service arranged on garnishee
□ Copy prepared for judgment debtor

POST-FILING:
□ Proof of service filed
□ 28-day response deadline calendared
□ Monitor garnishee compliance
□ Follow up on missed payments
□ Track amounts collected toward judgment
□ File satisfaction when judgment paid in full
```
