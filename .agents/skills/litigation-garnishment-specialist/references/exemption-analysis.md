# Exemption Analysis — Garnishment Reference

## Federal Exemptions

### Consumer Credit Protection Act (15 USC § 1673)

The CCPA sets the maximum amount of an individual's disposable earnings that may be garnished. This is the FLOOR of protection — states can provide MORE protection but not less.

**Key Definitions:**
- **Earnings**: Compensation paid or payable for personal services (wages, salary, commissions, bonuses)
- **Disposable earnings**: Earnings minus deductions required by law (taxes, FICA, mandatory retirement — NOT voluntary deductions)
- **Garnishment**: Any legal or equitable procedure where earnings are required to be withheld by employer

### CCPA Calculation Worksheet
```

Employee: ___________________
Pay period: ____ (weekly/biweekly/monthly)

B. Mandatory deductions:
   Federal income tax withheld:             $________
   State income tax withheld:               $________
   FICA (Social Security + Medicare):       $________
   Mandatory retirement:                    $________
   Total mandatory deductions:              $________
C. Disposable earnings (A - B):             $________

REGULAR JUDGMENT:
D. 25% of disposable earnings (C × 0.25):  $________

   ($7.25 × 30 = $217.50/week)
F. Disposable minus threshold (C - E):      $________
G. Maximum garnishment (lesser of D and F): $________
   If F is negative or zero: EXEMPT — no garnishment

CHILD SUPPORT JUDGMENT:
H. Applicable percentage (50/55/60/65%):    ___%
   □ Current spouse/dependents?  Y / N
   □ Over 12 weeks in arrears?   Y / N

```

### Multiple Garnishment Priority
When multiple garnishments exist:
1. **First priority**: Child support (MCL 552.625f)
2. **Second priority**: Federal tax levies (26 USC § 6331)
3. **Third priority**: Student loan garnishments (20 USC § 1095a)
4. **Fourth priority**: Regular judgment garnishments (first in time)

**CCPA aggregate cap**: Total garnishment cannot exceed 25% of disposable earnings (except support). If support garnishment already takes the maximum, other garnishments cannot attach.

## Michigan State Exemptions

### MCL 600.6023 — Personal Property Exemptions
These apply to execution on personal property and may affect bank account garnishment:

| Property | Exemption Amount | Statute |
|----------|-----------------|---------|
| Household goods | $3,775 per item | MCL 600.6023(1)(b) |
| Provisions (food, fuel) | 6 months supply | MCL 600.6023(1)(a) |
| Professional tools | $2,525 total | MCL 600.6023(1)(d) |
| Family pictures/books | Unlimited | MCL 600.6023(1)(c) |
| Life insurance policies | Up to $400/yr premium | MCL 600.6023(1)(f) |
| One vehicle | $3,775 value | MCL 600.6023(1)(g) |
| Burial plots | Family plot | MCL 600.6023(1)(h) |
| IRA/retirement | Per federal/state limits | Various |

### MCL 600.6023a — Homestead Exemption
- **Amount**: $40,475 (adjusted periodically)
- **Application**: Equity in primary residence
- **Note**: Protects equity from judgment execution, not from mortgage foreclosure

## Bank Account Garnishment — Special Rules

### Exempt Funds in Bank Accounts
When a garnishment writ reaches a bank account, certain funds are automatically exempt:

**Federal benefit protection (31 CFR § 212):**
- Banks MUST review accounts for direct-deposited federal benefits
- If federal benefits were deposited in the last 2 months, the protected amount equals the sum of federal benefit payments in that period
- Bank cannot freeze the protected amount

### Bank Account Garnishment Procedure
```
1. Writ served on bank
2. Bank freezes non-exempt funds in debtor's accounts
3. Bank reviews for federal benefits (2-month lookback)
4. Bank sends disclosure (28 days)
5. Debtor may claim additional exemptions (14 days)
6. If no objection: bank remits frozen funds to court/creditor
7. If objection: hearing on exemptions
```

## Challenging Improper Garnishment

### Grounds for Objection
| Ground | Statute | Remedy |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Exempt income garnished | 42 USC § 407 et al. | Release of exempt funds |
| Excessive garnishment | 15 USC § 1673 | Recalculation to CCPA limit |
| Improper service | MCR 3.101(D) | Void garnishment |
| Wrong debtor | MCR 3.101 | Release all funds |
| Judgment satisfied | MCR 3.101 | Release and terminate |
| Calculation error | MCR 3.101 | Correct and refund excess |

### Objection Timeline
- **14 days** from service of garnishee disclosure to file objection
- Court schedules hearing within 14 days of objection
- At hearing: debtor proves exemption, creditor proves entitlement
- Court enters order: release exempt funds, adjust garnishment

## Post-Judgment Collection Strategy Matrix

| Debtor Asset | Collection Tool | Priority |
|-------------|----------------|----------|
| Wages/salary | Periodic garnishment (MC 12) | HIGH — consistent income stream |
| Bank accounts | Non-periodic garnishment (MC 13) | HIGH — immediate access |
| Real property | Judgment lien (MCL 600.2801) | MEDIUM — secures claim, slow |
| Personal property | Execution (MCL 600.6001) | LOW — expensive, limited value |
| Unknown assets | Creditor's exam (MCR 2.621) | FIRST — discover assets before garnishing |
| Business income | Periodic garnishment on business | MEDIUM — if debtor is self-employed |
| Accounts receivable | Non-periodic garnishment | MEDIUM — debts owed to debtor |
| Tax refunds | Garnishment of tax refund | ANNUAL — timing dependent |

### Creditor's Examination (MCR 2.621)
Before garnishing, consider a creditor's examination to discover assets:
- File motion for order requiring debtor to appear
- Question debtor under oath about: employment, bank accounts, property, income sources
- Use answers to target garnishment precisely
- If debtor fails to appear: contempt sanctions available
