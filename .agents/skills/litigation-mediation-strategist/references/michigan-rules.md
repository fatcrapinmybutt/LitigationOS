# Michigan Rules — Mediation Strategist

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 3.216 — Alternative Dispute Resolution (Domestic Relations)
- **(A)**: Court may order mediation in domestic relations cases
- **(B)**: Mediator qualifications — must be approved by the court
- **(C)**: Exemptions — mediation not ordered when there is domestic violence history (MCL 600.1035)
- **(D)**: Scope — mediation may address custody, parenting time, property, support
- **(E)**: Selection of mediator — parties may agree; court appoints if no agreement
- **(F)**: Confidentiality — mediation communications are confidential per MCR 2.412
- **(G)**: Cost allocation — court determines allocation between parties
- **(H)**: Good Faith Participation:
  - **(H)(1)**: Parties must attend in person
  - **(H)(2)**: Parties must participate in good faith
  - **(H)(7)**: Agreement reached in mediation, when signed, is enforceable as a contract
- **(I)**: Sanctions for failure to participate in good faith — may include costs and fees

### MCR 2.410 — Case Evaluation
- Case evaluation as alternative to mediation
- Acceptance/rejection within **28 days**
- Rejection consequences: if result at trial is not more favorable, rejecting party pays costs

### MCR 2.411 — Case Evaluation: Procedure
- Three-member panel evaluates case
- Panel provides monetary evaluation
- Not applicable to equitable custody determinations

### MCR 2.412 — Mediation Communications: Confidentiality
- **(A)**: All communications in mediation are confidential
- **(B)**: Not admissible in any court proceeding
- **(C)**: Exceptions: signed agreement, threat of bodily harm, professional malpractice
- **(D)**: Mediator cannot be compelled to testify about mediation communications

### MCR 3.210 — Domestic Relations: Mediation
- Court may order mediation at any stage of the proceeding
- Must be completed within time set by court or scheduling order
- Provisions for domestic violence screening before mediation

### MCR 3.208 — Friend of the Court
- FOC may facilitate mediation or refer to outside mediator
- FOC mediation is often free; private mediation costs are allocated by court

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.1035 — Domestic Violence Exception to Mediation
- Mediation shall not be ordered if party or child has been subjected to domestic violence by other party
- Court must screen for domestic violence before ordering mediation
- Party may waive the exception

### MCL 600.1040-1050 — Michigan Mediation Act
- **(1040)**: Definitions — mediation, mediator, mediation communication
- **(1045)**: Confidentiality of mediation communications
- **(1050)**: Mediator immunity from civil liability
- **(1055)**: Exceptions to confidentiality

### MCL 552.505 — Child Support: Mediation
- Mediation may be used for child support disputes
- Michigan Child Support Formula applies regardless of mediation outcome
- Agreement cannot deviate from formula without court approval and findings

### MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors in Mediation Context
- Mediated custody agreements must serve best interests of child
- Court reviews mediated agreements for best interest compliance
- Court not bound by mediated agreement if contrary to child's best interests

### MCL 722.27a — Parenting Time Mediation
- Parenting time disputes may be mediated
- FOC may provide parenting time mediation services
- Mediated parenting time plans subject to court approval

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Mediation Agreement Enforceability
- **Vittiglio v Vittiglio**, 297 Mich App 213 (2012) — Mediation agreements signed by both parties are enforceable as contracts; courts may enter them as consent judgments
- **Walbridge Aldinger Co v Walcon Corp**, 207 Mich App 566 (1994) — Courts favor enforcement of mediation agreements; parties cannot unilaterally withdraw after signing

### Confidentiality
- **Groulx v Carleson**, 176 Mich App 484 (1989) — Mediation communications are privileged and inadmissible absent statutory exception
- **Mich State Employees Assn v Dep't of Mental Health**, 421 Mich 152 (1984) — Confidentiality provisions serve important public policy of encouraging candid mediation participation

### Good Faith Participation
- **Vyletel-Rivard v Rivard**, 286 Mich App 13 (2009) — Party who refuses to make any proposals or engage in discussion may be found to have participated in bad faith
- **MacIntyre v MacIntyre**, 267 Mich App 449 (2005) — Good faith requires genuine engagement, not mere physical presence

### Domestic Violence Exception
- **Harvey v Harvey**, 257 Mich App 278 (2003) — Court must conduct domestic violence screening before ordering mediation; failure to screen is reversible error

## Checklists

### Pre-Mediation Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Complete financial disclosure (MCR 3.206(B)) before mediation
- [ ] Calculate BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)
- [ ] Identify ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement)
- [ ] Prepare mediation summary with key facts and proposals
- [ ] Gather supporting evidence (organized, not voluminous)
- [ ] Identify priorities and trade-offs (what you need vs. want)
- [ ] Review child support formula calculations
- [ ] Screen for domestic violence issues (MCL 600.1035)
- [ ] Understand confidentiality rules (MCR 2.412)
- [ ] Prepare multiple settlement proposals at different levels

### Post-Mediation Checklist
- [ ] If agreement reached: review all terms before signing
- [ ] Ensure agreement is reduced to writing at the session
- [ ] File signed agreement with court for entry as order
- [ ] If impasse: prepare trial position statement
- [ ] Do NOT reference mediation communications in any filing

### Filing Deadlines
- Mediation completion: Per court order or scheduling order
- Agreement filing: Promptly after signing
- Good faith dispute: File brief within time set by court
- Case evaluation acceptance/rejection: **28 days** (MCR 2.410)
