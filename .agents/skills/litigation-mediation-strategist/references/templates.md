## Workflow: Mediation Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Receive mediation order / scheduling
    ↓
Step 2: Complete financial disclosure (prerequisite)
    ↓
Step 3: Calculate BATNA for each issue
    ↓
Step 4: Identify ZOPA — is settlement possible?
    ↓
Step 5: Prepare mediation position statement
    ↓
Step 6: Prepare multiple settlement proposals (A, B, C)
    ↓
Step 7: Attend mediation — present position
    ↓
Step 8: Negotiate — track concessions and reciprocation
    ↓
    ├── If AGREEMENT → Review terms → Sign → File with court
    ↓
    ├── If IMPASSE → Document good faith → Prepare for trial
    ↓
Step 9: File agreement or report impasse to court
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| No preparation | Reactive instead of strategic; miss opportunities | Complete BATNA/ZOPA analysis before session |
| Adversarial posturing | Creates impasse; mediator loses patience | Interest-based approach; focus on underlying needs |
| Signing under pressure | Binding agreement you regret later | Never sign at the table; take 24 hours to review |
| Conceding without reciprocity | Anchors expectations; invites more demands | Every concession tied to a reciprocal ask |
| Disclosing mediation in filings | Violates MCR 2.412 confidentiality; motion to strike | Never reference what was said or offered in mediation |
| Skipping mediation or showing up unprepared | Bad faith finding; possible sanctions (MCR 3.216(I)) | Attend prepared; engage genuinely even if skeptical |
