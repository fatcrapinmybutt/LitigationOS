# Gotchas — Trial Preparation Specialist

## Anti-Rationalization Matrix

| Trap | What It Looks Like | Why It's Wrong | Correct Approach |
|------|-------------------|----------------|------------------|
| Inadequate exhibit preparation | Arriving at trial with loose documents and no exhibit list | MCR 2.511(A)(7) requires pre-marked, organized exhibits; disorganized presentation wastes court time and undermines credibility | Pre-mark all exhibits; prepare exhibit list; organize in binders with tabs; exchange with opposing party per scheduling order |
| No witness preparation | Calling witnesses without discussing their testimony or the flow of examination | Unprepared witnesses give rambling, non-responsive answers that fail to establish required elements | Prepare each witness on direct examination topics; practice key questions; ensure witnesses understand courtroom procedure |
| Failing to comply with pretrial order | Missing pretrial deadlines for exhibit lists, witness lists, or pretrial statements | Non-compliance may result in exclusion of witnesses or exhibits; prejudices your entire case | Calendar every pretrial deadline immediately upon receiving scheduling order; comply early |
| Not making an opening statement | Waiving or fumbling the opening statement because "the evidence will speak for itself" | Opening statement frames the case for the judge; without it, evidence is received without context; the judge must understand your theory | Prepare a concise opening (5-10 minutes in family court) that tells your story and previews key evidence |
| Ignoring motions in limine | Failing to file motions in limine to exclude prejudicial evidence before trial | Without pre-trial rulings, objectionable evidence may be heard before you can object; even a sustained objection cannot un-ring the bell | File motions in limine at least 14 days before trial for all foreseeable evidentiary issues |

## Failure Modes
1. **Exhibit Exclusion**: Key exhibit excluded because it was not on the pretrial exhibit list or not exchanged timely. Recovery: Move for admission arguing no prejudice and good cause for late disclosure; success is uncertain — prevention is the only reliable strategy.
2. **Witness Unavailability**: Key witness fails to appear at trial. Recovery: If subpoenaed, move for contempt and adjournment; if not subpoenaed, request a brief adjournment to secure attendance; have a backup plan to introduce the evidence through other means.
3. **Evidentiary Foundation Failure**: Court sustains objection to key evidence because the proper foundation was not laid. Recovery: Request opportunity to recall the foundation witness or offer the evidence through an alternative foundation; know MRE requirements for every exhibit.
4. **Time Limit Exceeded**: Court imposes time limits and your case is incomplete when time expires. Recovery: Prioritize the most important witnesses and exhibits; have a triage plan; request additional time with good cause shown before it runs out.

## Integration Gotchas
- When combining with **litigation-timeline-forensics**, the trial timeline exhibit must be pre-marked, authenticated, and disclosed on the exhibit list — a timeline created for preparation that was never disclosed cannot be used at trial.
- Data flow from **litigation-subpoena-engine** must be completed well before trial — all subpoenas for trial witnesses should be served at least 14 days in advance, with witness fees tendered.
- Michigan-specific pitfall: In the 14th Circuit Family Division, bench trials on custody are conducted differently than jury trials. The judge is the finder of fact. Accordingly, focus on clearly presenting evidence organized by the 12 best interest factors rather than jury-style storytelling. The judge needs a factor-by-factor roadmap.
