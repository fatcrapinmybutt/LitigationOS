# Michigan Rules — Trial Preparation Specialist

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.501 — Scheduling and Pretrial Conferences
- **(A)**: Court may schedule pretrial conference at any time
- **(B)**: Pretrial conference topics: simplifying issues, amending pleadings, obtaining admissions, limiting witnesses, settlement
- **(C)**: Pretrial order controls subsequent course of action; modification only to prevent manifest injustice

### MCR 2.511 — Jury Trial Procedure (Also Applicable to Bench Trials)
- **(A)**: Order of trial proceedings
  - **(1)**: Opening statements
  - **(2)**: Plaintiff's case-in-chief
  - **(3)**: Defendant's motion for directed verdict (if applicable)
  - **(4)**: Defendant's case-in-chief
  - **(5)**: Rebuttal
  - **(6)**: Closing arguments
  - **(7)**: Exhibits must be pre-marked and organized

### MCR 2.507 — Referee Hearings (Domestic Relations)
- **(A)**: Many family division matters heard by referees
- **(B)**: Same rules of evidence apply at referee hearings
- **(C)**: Objections to referee recommendations within **21 days**

### MCR 2.401 — Pretrial Procedures
- **(A)**: Scheduling conference to set trial date and pretrial deadlines
- **(B)**: Pretrial statement requirements (witness list, exhibit list, issues)
- **(C)**: Settlement conference may be ordered
- **(D)**: Final pretrial order — binding on parties

### MCR 2.404 — Assignment and Adjournment of Cases
- **(A)**: Cases assigned for trial per court scheduling
- **(B)**: Adjournment only for good cause shown
- **(C)**: Court may impose conditions on adjournment

### MCR 2.509 — Nonsuit and Dismissal
- **(A)**: Voluntary dismissal before trial
- **(B)**: Involuntary dismissal for failure to prosecute
- **(C)**: Directed verdict at close of plaintiff's case

## Michigan Rules of Evidence (MRE)

### Foundation Rules for Trial
- **MRE 104**: Preliminary questions of admissibility (judge decides)
- **MRE 401-403**: Relevance and exclusion of prejudicial evidence
- **MRE 602**: Personal knowledge requirement for witnesses
- **MRE 611**: Mode and order of interrogation (direct, cross, redirect)
- **MRE 613**: Prior inconsistent statements for impeachment
- **MRE 701**: Lay witness opinion testimony
- **MRE 702**: Expert witness testimony (Daubert standard in Michigan)
- **MRE 801-807**: Hearsay rules and exceptions
- **MRE 901-902**: Authentication requirements

### MCR 2.302(B) — Discovery for Trial Preparation
- Work product doctrine protections
- Expert witness disclosure requirements

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors (Trial Framework)
- All 12 factors must be addressed at trial
- Court makes findings on each factor
- Evidence organized by factor is most effective presentation

### MCL 722.27(1)(c) — Burden of Proof at Trial
- If established custodial environment affected: **clear and convincing evidence**
- If no established custodial environment change: **preponderance of evidence**
- Must know which standard applies before trial

### MCL 600.2552 — Witness Fees for Trial
- $12/day witness fee plus mileage
- Must be tendered with subpoena

### MCL 600.2157 — Competency of Witnesses
- General competency rules
- Child witness competency (relevant in custody trials)

### MCL 600.2163a — Child Testimony Protections
- Special procedures for children testifying
- Video testimony options
- In camera interview by judge

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Trial Procedure
- **Fletcher v Fletcher**, 447 Mich 871 (1994) — Trial court must make findings on each best interest factor; failure to address each factor is reversible error
- **Vodvarka v Grasmeyer**, 259 Mich App 499 (2003) — Threshold determination (proper cause/change of circumstances) must be made before proceeding to best interest trial
- **Pierron v Pierron**, 282 Mich App 222 (2009) — Trial court's factual findings must be supported by the evidence; conclusory findings insufficient

### Evidence at Trial
- **Craig v Oakwood Hospital**, 471 Mich 67 (2004) — Michigan adopted Daubert standard for expert testimony; applies to all expert witnesses including custody evaluators
- **People v Lukity**, 460 Mich 484 (1999) — Harmless error standard; prejudicial evidentiary errors at trial require reversal only if they affected the outcome

### Pretrial and Scheduling
- **Dean v Tucker**, 182 Mich App 27 (1990) — Court may impose sanctions for failure to comply with pretrial orders, including dismissal
- **Vicencio v Ramirez**, 211 Mich App 501 (1995) — Pretrial orders are binding; late amendments require good cause

## Checklists

### Trial Preparation Master Checklist
- [ ] Review and comply with all pretrial order deadlines
- [ ] Prepare and exchange witness list per scheduling order
- [ ] Prepare and exchange exhibit list per scheduling order
- [ ] Pre-mark all exhibits (Plaintiff's: P-1, P-2, etc.)
- [ ] Organize exhibits in binders with tabs (3 copies: court, witness, self)
- [ ] Subpoena all witnesses with fees tendered (14+ days before trial)
- [ ] Prepare direct examination outlines for each witness
- [ ] Prepare cross-examination outlines for opposing witnesses
- [ ] Draft opening statement (5-10 minutes, organized by best interest factors)
- [ ] Draft closing argument outline
- [ ] File motions in limine (14+ days before trial)
- [ ] Prepare proposed findings of fact and conclusions of law
- [ ] Review all deposition transcripts and prior testimony for impeachment
- [ ] Confirm trial date, time, and courtroom with clerk

### Filing Deadlines
- Pretrial statement: Per scheduling order (typically 14-28 days before trial)
- Exhibit/witness exchange: Per scheduling order
- Motions in limine: 14+ days before trial (recommended)
- Subpoena service: 14+ days before trial
- Proposed findings of fact: Per court direction (often day of trial)
