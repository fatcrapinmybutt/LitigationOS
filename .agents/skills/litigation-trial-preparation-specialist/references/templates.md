## Opening Statement Outline

```
OPENING STATEMENT OUTLINE — BENCH TRIAL

May it please the Court:

I. INTRODUCTION (1 minute)
   - This case is about [central theme]
   - The Court will hear evidence that [preview conclusion]

II. FACTUAL OVERVIEW (3-4 minutes)
   - The parties: [brief context]
   - The child: [relevant facts]
   - The history: [key events]

III. BEST INTEREST FACTORS PREVIEW (3-4 minutes)
   Factor (j): The evidence will show [preview]
   Factor (a): The evidence will show [preview]
   Factor (k): The evidence will show [preview]
   [Additional priority factors]

IV. WHAT THE EVIDENCE WILL SHOW (1-2 minutes)
   - You will hear from [key witnesses]
   - You will see [key exhibits]
   - Together, this evidence demonstrates [conclusion]

V. REQUESTED OUTCOME (30 seconds)
   - At the conclusion, Plaintiff will ask the Court to [specific relief]
```

## Direct Examination Template

```
DIRECT EXAMINATION OUTLINE — [WITNESS NAME]

Foundation:
Q: State your name for the record.
Q: How do you know the Plaintiff?
Q: How long have you known the family?

Factor (j) — Facilitation of Relationship:
Q: Have you observed how each parent interacts with the child?
Q: Can you describe what you observed on [specific date]?
Q: How did the child react?
Q: Have you observed [opposing party] discussing [plaintiff] in
   front of the child?

Factor (a) — Emotional Ties:
Q: Describe the child's relationship with the Plaintiff.
Q: What specific interactions have you witnessed?
Q: How does the child behave when with the Plaintiff?

[Continue per factor and witness knowledge]

Exhibits:
Q: I'm showing you what has been marked as Exhibit P-[X].
   Do you recognize this?
Q: What is it?
Q: How do you know what it is?
MOVE: Plaintiff offers Exhibit P-[X] into evidence.
```

## Workflow: Trial Day Preparation

```
Day Before Trial:
    ↓ Confirm courtroom, time, and judge assignment with clerk
    ↓ Organize exhibit binders (3 sets: court, witness stand, counsel)
    ↓ Review witness outlines one final time
    ↓ Prepare personal notes for opening and closing

Trial Day:
    ↓ Arrive 30 minutes early
    ↓ Set up exhibit binders at counsel table
    ↓ Check in with clerk; confirm witness order
    ↓ Opening statement
    ↓ Case-in-chief (witnesses + exhibits by BIF factor)
    ↓ Cross-examination of opposing witnesses
    ↓ Rebuttal if needed
    ↓ Closing argument
    ↓ Submit proposed findings of fact

Post-Trial:
    ↓ Calendar deadline for post-trial briefs if ordered
    ↓ Calendar 21-day appeal deadline from judgment entry
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| No exhibit organization | Judge frustrated; evidence lost; looks unprepared | Pre-marked binders with tabs and index |
| Unprepared witnesses | Rambling, non-responsive testimony | Practice key questions; explain courtroom procedure |
| Missing pretrial deadlines | Exhibits/witnesses excluded | Calendar all deadlines immediately |
| No opening statement | Judge hears evidence without context | Prepare concise, compelling opening |
| Skipping motions in limine | Prejudicial evidence heard before you can object | File MILs 14+ days before trial |
| Not organized by BIF factors | Judge struggles to map evidence to required findings | Structure entire case around 12 factors |
