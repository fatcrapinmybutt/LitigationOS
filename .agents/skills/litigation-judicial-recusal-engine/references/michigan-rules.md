# Michigan Rules — Judicial Recusal Engine

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.003 — Disqualification of Judge
- **(A)**: Applicability — applies to all Michigan courts
- **(C)**: Grounds for Disqualification:
  - **(C)(1)(a)**: Personal bias or prejudice concerning a party or attorney
  - **(C)(1)(b)**: Personal knowledge of disputed evidentiary facts
  - **(C)(1)(c)**: Prior involvement as attorney, witness, or judge in same matter
  - **(C)(1)(d)**: Financial interest in the subject matter or a party
  - **(C)(1)(e)**: Related within third degree to a party, attorney, or their spouse
  - **(C)(1)(f)**: Spouse or close family member is a party, attorney, or has financial interest
  - **(C)(1)(g)**: Former law partner/associate of an attorney in the matter (within 2 years)
- **(C)(2)**: Judge must disqualify when judge cannot impartially hear the case
- **(D)(1)**: Motion must be filed at **earliest practicable opportunity** after discovering grounds
- **(D)(2)**: The challenged judge decides the motion first; if denied, party may request chief judge review
- **(D)(3)**: Motion must be verified and supported by affidavits or other proof
- **(E)**: Voluntary disqualification — judge may recuse sua sponte
- **(F)**: Chief judge assignment when disqualification is granted

### MCR 7.203 — Jurisdiction of Court of Appeals
- **(A)**: Appeals of right from final orders
- **(B)(1)**: Interlocutory appeals by leave — includes denial of recusal motion
- **(C)(4)**: Superintending control (mandamus) for judicial misconduct/bias

### MCR 7.211 — Motions in Court of Appeals
- Emergency motions for immediate disqualification pending appeal
- Stay of proceedings pending recusal appeal

### MCR 9.104 — Judicial Tenure Commission Referral
- When recusal-triggering conduct also constitutes judicial misconduct
- Parallel JTC complaint may be appropriate

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 600.151 — Disqualification of Judge
- Statutory basis for judicial disqualification
- Substantially mirrors MCR 2.003 grounds

### MCL 600.225 — Assignment of Visiting Judge
- Procedure when disqualification leaves no available judge in the circuit
- Chief judge of circuit assigns or requests SCAO assignment

### MCL 169.254 — Judicial Campaign Finance
- Requires financial disclosure that may reveal disqualifying interests
- Campaign contributions from parties/attorneys may trigger disqualification

### MCL 600.1621 — Contempt Power Limitations
- Judges cannot use contempt to punish recusal motions made in good faith
- Filing a recusal motion is protected conduct

### MCL 168.409 — Judicial Conduct Standards
- Cross-reference with Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct
- Canons 1-7 inform disqualification analysis

## Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct

### Canon 2 — Avoiding Impropriety and Appearance of Impropriety
- Rule 2.2: Impartiality and fairness
- Rule 2.3: Bias, prejudice, and harassment
- Rule 2.9: Ex parte communications (disqualifying if substantive)
- Rule 2.11: Disqualification (mirrors MCR 2.003)

### Canon 3 — Personal and Extrajudicial Activities
- Rule 3.1: Extrajudicial activities generally
- Rule 3.7: Participation in organizations (potential conflicts)
- Rule 3.13: Financial disclosures

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Disqualification Standards
- **Cain v Michigan Dept of Corrections**, 451 Mich 470 (1996) — The test is whether a reasonable person would perceive a significant risk of actual bias; subjective feelings of bias are insufficient
- **Armstrong v Ypsilanti Township**, 248 Mich App 573 (2001) — Adverse rulings alone are insufficient grounds for disqualification absent other evidence of bias
- **Bayati v Bayati**, 264 Mich App 595 (2004) — Disqualification motion must be timely; filing after adverse ruling creates inference of forum shopping

### Timeliness Requirements
- **Kern v Kern-Koskela**, 320 Mich App 212 (2017) — "Earliest practicable opportunity" means promptly after discovering disqualifying facts, not after suffering an adverse outcome
- **In re MKK**, 286 Mich App 546 (2009) — Delay in filing recusal motion may constitute waiver

### Appellate Review
- **People v Jackson**, 292 Mich App 583 (2011) — Denial of recusal is reviewed for abuse of discretion
- **Schellenberg v Rochester**, 172 Mich App 476 (1988) — Mandamus may issue when trial judge clearly should have recused

## Checklists

### Recusal Motion Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Identify specific MCR 2.003(C) subsection(s) applicable
- [ ] Gather factual evidence (transcripts, documents, declarations)
- [ ] Prepare verified motion with specific factual allegations
- [ ] Attach supporting affidavits or certified transcripts
- [ ] Confirm timeliness — filed at earliest practicable opportunity
- [ ] Prepare for chief judge review if initial motion denied (MCR 2.003(D)(2))
- [ ] Research whether interlocutory appeal is available
- [ ] Consider parallel JTC complaint if conduct warrants

### Disqualification Grounds Decision Tree
1. Does judge have financial interest in outcome? → MCR 2.003(C)(1)(d)
2. Is judge related to a party or attorney? → MCR 2.003(C)(1)(e)-(f)
3. Was judge previously involved as attorney/witness? → MCR 2.003(C)(1)(c)
4. Does judge have personal knowledge of disputed facts? → MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b)
5. Has judge demonstrated personal bias/prejudice? → MCR 2.003(C)(1)(a)
6. Has judge had ex parte communications? → Canon 2, Rule 2.9
7. None of the above but "reasonable person" would see bias risk? → MCR 2.003(C)(2)

### Filing Deadlines
- Recusal motion: **Earliest practicable opportunity** after discovering grounds (MCR 2.003(D)(1))
- Chief judge review: Promptly after denial by challenged judge
- Interlocutory appeal to COA: **21 days** from order denying recusal
- Mandamus petition: File when no adequate remedy at law exists
- JTC complaint: No statutory deadline, but file promptly
