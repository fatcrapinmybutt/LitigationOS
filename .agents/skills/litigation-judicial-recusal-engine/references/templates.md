## Motion for Disqualification of Judge

```
STATE OF MICHIGAN
IN THE 14TH CIRCUIT COURT FOR THE COUNTY OF MUSKEGON
FAMILY DIVISION

ANDREW JAMES PIGORS,
    Plaintiff,                          Case No. 2024-001507-DC
                                        Hon. Jenny L. McNeill
v.

EMILY A. WATSON,
    Defendant.
_________________________________/

VERIFIED MOTION FOR DISQUALIFICATION OF JUDGE

    Plaintiff, Andrew James Pigors, respectfully moves this Court for
disqualification of the Hon. Jenny L. McNeill pursuant to MCR 2.003(C),
and in support states:

GROUNDS FOR DISQUALIFICATION:
    1. This motion is filed at the earliest practicable opportunity after
       Plaintiff learned of the disqualifying facts, as required by
       MCR 2.003(D)(1).
    2. Disqualification is required under MCR 2.003(C)(1)([subsection])
       because: [specific factual basis].
    3. [Detailed factual allegations with dates, transcript references,
       and specific conduct at issue]
    4. [Additional grounds if applicable]

FACTUAL SUPPORT:
    5. Attached hereto as Exhibit A is [transcript excerpt / document /
       declaration] supporting the above grounds.
    6. [Additional exhibits]

LEGAL STANDARD:
    7. Under Cain v Michigan Dept of Corrections, 451 Mich 470 (1996),
       the test is whether a reasonable, objective person would perceive
       a significant risk of actual bias.
    8. The facts set forth above satisfy this standard because [explain].

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff requests this Court enter an order:
    a. Disqualifying the Hon. Jenny L. McNeill from further proceedings;
    b. Transferring this matter to another judge.

VERIFICATION
I declare under the penalties of perjury that the facts stated in this
motion are true and correct to the best of my knowledge.

____________________________
Andrew James Pigors, Pro Se
Date: _______________
```

## Request for Chief Judge Review (After Denial)

```
REQUEST FOR CHIEF JUDGE REVIEW OF DENIAL
OF MOTION FOR DISQUALIFICATION

    Pursuant to MCR 2.003(D)(2), Plaintiff requests that the Chief
Judge of the 14th Circuit Court review the denial of Plaintiff's
Motion for Disqualification entered on [DATE]:

    1. Plaintiff filed a Motion for Disqualification on [DATE].
    2. The challenged judge denied the motion on [DATE] (Order attached
       as Exhibit A).
    3. The denial was erroneous because [specify reasons].
    4. Plaintiff respectfully requests the Chief Judge:
       a. Review the motion de novo;
       b. Grant disqualification;
       c. Assign a substitute judge.
```

## Recusal Motion Workflow

```
Step 1: Identify disqualifying conduct
    ↓
Step 2: Determine MCR 2.003(C) subsection
    ↓
Step 3: Gather evidence (transcripts, documents, declarations)
    ↓
Step 4: Draft verified motion with specific facts
    ↓
Step 5: File at earliest practicable opportunity
    ↓
Step 6: Challenged judge rules (MCR 2.003(D)(2))
    ↓
    ├── If GRANTED → New judge assigned
    ↓
    ├── If DENIED → Step 7
    ↓
Step 7: Request Chief Judge review (MCR 2.003(D)(2))
    ↓
    ├── If GRANTED → New judge assigned
    ↓
    ├── If DENIED → Step 8
    ↓
Step 8: Interlocutory appeal to COA (MCR 7.203)
    ↓ (or)
Step 8a: Mandamus petition to COA (MCR 7.203(C)(4))
    ↓
Step 9: Parallel JTC complaint if conduct warrants
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Waiting until after bad ruling | Creates inference of forum shopping; likely waived | File immediately upon discovering disqualifying facts |
| Citing only adverse rulings | Rulings alone never establish bias (Armstrong standard) | Focus on extrajudicial conduct, personal relationships, ex parte contact |
| Unverified motion | MCR 2.003(D)(3) requires verification | Always verify under oath and attach supporting affidavits |
| Skipping to COA | COA will deny without exhaustion of trial-court remedies | File with trial judge first → chief judge → then COA |
| Emotional tone | Undermines credibility; judge may view as vexatious | Present facts objectively; let the evidence speak |
| No transcript support | Bare allegations without proof are denied routinely | Obtain and cite specific transcript pages |
