# Templates — Post-Judgment Specialist

## Motion to Modify Custody

```
STATE OF MICHIGAN
IN THE 14TH CIRCUIT COURT FOR THE COUNTY OF MUSKEGON
FAMILY DIVISION

ANDREW JAMES PIGORS,
    Plaintiff,                          Case No. 2024-001507-DC
                                        Hon. Jenny L. McNeill
v.

EMILY A. WATSON,
    Defendant.
_________________________________/

MOTION TO MODIFY CUSTODY ORDER

    Plaintiff moves pursuant to MCL 722.27(1)(c) to modify the
custody order entered [DATE]:

I. PROPER CAUSE / CHANGE OF CIRCUMSTANCES (Vodvarka Threshold):
    1. Since the current order was entered, the following changes
       have occurred: [specific facts with dates]
    2. These changes are significant because: [explain significance]
    3. These changes warrant reevaluation of the custody arrangement.

II. ESTABLISHED CUSTODIAL ENVIRONMENT:
    4. An established custodial environment [exists / does not exist]
       with [Plaintiff / Defendant / both].
    5. The applicable burden of proof is [clear and convincing /
       preponderance of the evidence].

III. BEST INTEREST FACTORS (MCL 722.23):
    6. The following factors favor modification:
       (a) [Factor analysis with evidence]
       (j) [Factor analysis — willingness to facilitate]
       [Additional factors]

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff requests modification of custody to [specify].
```

## Motion for Relief from Judgment

```
MOTION FOR RELIEF FROM JUDGMENT

    Plaintiff moves pursuant to MCR 2.612(C)(1)([subsection]):

    1. The judgment entered on [DATE] should be set aside because:
       [specific grounds under applicable subsection]

    2. This motion is timely: [explain timing — within 1 year for
       (a)-(c), or explain why no time limit applies for (d)]

    3. Supporting facts: [detailed factual basis]

    4. Relief requested: [specific modification or vacation of judgment]
```

## Post-Judgment Enforcement — Show Cause Motion

```
MOTION AND ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE

    Plaintiff moves for an order requiring Defendant to show cause
why Defendant should not be held in contempt for violating the
Court's order dated [DATE]:

VIOLATIONS:
    1. The Court ordered: [quote specific provision]
       Defendant violated this provision by: [specific conduct, date]

    2. The Court ordered: [quote specific provision]
       Defendant violated this provision by: [specific conduct, date]

    Defendant's non-compliance is willful because: [evidence of
ability to comply].

RELIEF REQUESTED:
    a. Order to show cause with hearing date;
    b. Finding of contempt;
    c. [Specific remedy: makeup time, fine, modification];
    d. Reasonable expenses and attorney fees.
```

## Post-Judgment Decision Matrix

| Situation | Correct Vehicle | MCR/MCL | Deadline |
|-----------|---------------|---------|----------|
| Order not being followed | Show cause / contempt | MCR 3.213 | No fixed deadline |
| Circumstances have changed | Motion to modify | MCL 722.27(1)(c) | No fixed deadline |
| Legal error in judgment | Motion for reconsideration | MCR 2.119(F) | 21 days |
| Want appellate review | Claim of appeal | MCR 7.204(A) | 21 days |
| Newly discovered evidence | Relief from judgment | MCR 2.612(C)(1)(b) | 1 year |
| Fraud/misconduct | Relief from judgment | MCR 2.612(C)(1)(c) | 1 year |
| Judgment is void | Relief from judgment | MCR 2.612(C)(1)(d) | No time limit |
| Property division unfair | Generally NOT modifiable | MCL 552.391 | N/A |

## Workflow: Post-Judgment Motion Lifecycle

```
Step 1: Identify the problem (enforcement vs. modification vs. appeal)
    ↓
Step 2: Determine correct procedural vehicle (see matrix above)
    ↓
Step 3: Check deadlines — is your motion timely?
    ↓
Step 4: Verify jurisdiction — file in original court (MCL 552.17a)
    ↓
Step 5: For modification — brief Vodvarka threshold
    ↓
Step 6: Identify established custodial environment and burden of proof
    ↓
Step 7: Draft motion with supporting brief and exhibits
    ↓
Step 8: Serve all parties; file with court
    ↓
Step 9: Attend hearing; present evidence
    ↓
Step 10: If denied → evaluate appeal or alternative remedy
```

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Skipping Vodvarka threshold | Motion denied without reaching merits | Always brief change of circumstances first |
| Relitigating original issues | Res judicata bars; court annoyed | Focus exclusively on post-judgment changes |
| Filing in wrong court | Dismissed for lack of jurisdiction | File in court that entered original judgment |
| Confusing enforcement with modification | Wrong remedy applied; wasted time | Enforcement = existing order adequate but violated; modification = order itself needs changing |
| Missing 21-day deadlines | Forfeit appeal of right or reconsideration | Calendar every deadline from day of judgment entry |
| Seeking property redistribution | Property division is generally final | Only challenge via fraud (MCR 2.612(C)(1)(c)) |
