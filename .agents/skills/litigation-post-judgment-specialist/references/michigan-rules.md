# Michigan Rules — Post-Judgment Specialist

## Michigan Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.612 — Relief from Judgment or Order
- **(C)(1)**: Grounds for relief:
  - **(a)**: Mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect
  - **(b)**: Newly discovered evidence that could not have been discovered with due diligence
  - **(c)**: Fraud, misrepresentation, or other misconduct of adverse party
  - **(d)**: Judgment is void
  - **(e)**: Judgment has been satisfied, released, or discharged
  - **(f)**: Any other reason justifying relief
- **(C)(2)**: Motion under (a), (b), (c) must be filed within **1 year**
- **(C)(3)**: Does not limit court's power to set aside void judgments or entertain independent action for relief

### MCR 2.611 — New Trial; Amendment of Judgment
- **(A)(1)**: Grounds: irregularity, misconduct, accident, surprise, newly discovered evidence, excessive damages, against weight of evidence, error of law
- **(B)**: Motion within **21 days** after entry of judgment
- **(D)**: Rehearing in non-jury cases — same standards apply
- **(E)**: Court may order new trial on its own initiative within **21 days**

### MCR 7.204 — Filing Appeal of Right
- **(A)(1)**: Claim of appeal within **21 days** of entry of final judgment
- **(A)(2)**: Cross-appeal within **21 days** of service of claim of appeal
- **(B)**: Late appeal — within **6 months** with showing of excusable neglect

### MCR 3.213 — Domestic Relations: Show Cause
- Post-judgment enforcement of domestic relations orders
- Show cause hearings for contempt of custody/support orders
- Must show willful non-compliance and ability to comply

### MCR 3.206 — Domestic Relations: Post-Judgment Motions
- **(C)**: Temporary orders pending post-judgment proceedings
- **(D)**: Emergency temporary orders

### MCR 2.119(F) — Motion for Reconsideration
- **(1)**: Must be filed within **21 days** of entry of order
- **(2)**: Must demonstrate palpable error by which the court was misled
- **(3)**: May not present new evidence or raise new arguments

## Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL)

### MCL 722.27 — Custody Modification
- **(1)(c)**: Court may modify custody if: (1) proper cause shown or change of circumstances, AND (2) modification is in child's best interest
- **(1)(c)**: If established custodial environment would be changed, burden of proof is clear and convincing evidence
- **(1)(c)**: If no established custodial environment change, preponderance of evidence standard

### MCL 722.27(1)(c) — Established Custodial Environment
- Exists when child naturally looks to the custodian for guidance, discipline, necessities of life, and parental comfort
- Determined by the child's age, physical environment, stability of arrangement

### MCL 552.17a — Continuing Jurisdiction
- Court that enters divorce judgment retains continuing jurisdiction over post-judgment matters
- Support, custody, and parenting time modifications filed in original court

### MCL 552.28 — Enforcement of Support Orders
- Income withholding is primary enforcement mechanism
- Contempt for willful non-payment
- License suspension (driver's, professional, recreational) for arrears

### MCL 552.602 — Income (Child Support Context)
- Modification requires showing change in income or circumstances
- Income broadly defined per statute

### MCL 552.391 — Property Division: Finality
- Property division in divorce judgment is generally final and not modifiable
- Exception: fraud or mutual mistake — MCR 2.612(C)(1)(c)

### MCL 722.31 — Post-Judgment Change of Domicile
- Must seek court permission before moving child's domicile more than 100 miles
- Five factors court considers: proximity, quality of life, intent, domestic violence, compliance

## Key Michigan Case Law

### Modification Standards
- **Vodvarka v Grasmeyer**, 259 Mich App 499 (2003) — Establishes threshold requirement: must show proper cause or change of circumstances before court reaches best interest analysis; change must be significant enough to warrant reevaluation
- **Shade v Wright**, 291 Mich App 17 (2010) — Change of circumstances must have occurred since the last custody order; anticipated changes at time of original order do not qualify
- **Lieberman v Orr**, 319 Mich App 68 (2017) — Normal life changes (child aging, school changes) generally do not constitute sufficient change of circumstances

### Established Custodial Environment
- **Baker v Baker**, 411 Mich 567 (1981) — Defined established custodial environment; can exist with one or both parents
- **Berger v Berger**, 277 Mich App 700 (2008) — ECE analysis is fact-specific; court must make detailed findings

### Relief from Judgment
- **Dimmitt & Owens Financial Inc v Deloitte & Touche**, 481 Mich 618 (2008) — MCR 2.612(C)(1)(f) is extraordinary relief; requires more than simple disagreement with the judgment
- **Heugel v Heugel**, 237 Mich App 471 (1999) — Fraud on the court justifies reopening property division

## Checklists

### Post-Judgment Motion Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Verify continuing jurisdiction (MCL 552.17a) — correct court
- [ ] Identify correct procedural vehicle (modification vs. enforcement vs. appeal)
- [ ] For modification: brief the Vodvarka threshold
- [ ] Identify whether established custodial environment exists
- [ ] Apply correct burden of proof (clear and convincing vs. preponderance)
- [ ] Attach evidence of changed circumstances
- [ ] File in the court that entered the original judgment
- [ ] Serve all parties with proper notice

### Filing Deadlines
- Appeal of right: **21 days** from judgment entry (MCR 7.204(A)(1))
- Motion for new trial: **21 days** from judgment entry (MCR 2.611(B))
- Motion for reconsideration: **21 days** from order entry (MCR 2.119(F)(1))
- Relief from judgment (mistake/fraud): **1 year** (MCR 2.612(C)(2))
- Relief from void judgment: No time limit (MCR 2.612(C)(3))
- Late appeal: **6 months** with excusable neglect (MCR 7.204(B))
