# FOC Procedures — Complete Reference

> Friend of the Court objection procedures, referee recommendations,
> de novo hearings, and timeline under MCR 3.218.

---

## I. Friend of the Court — Overview

### Role of the FOC
The Friend of the Court (FOC) is a court agency in every Michigan county that:
1. Investigates custody, parenting time, and support disputes
2. Makes recommendations to the court
3. Enforces court orders (support, parenting time)
4. Mediates disputes between parties
5. Conducts referee hearings on contested matters

### Muskegon County FOC
- **14th Judicial Circuit Court**
- **Location:** Muskegon County Hall of Justice
- **Jurisdiction:** All domestic relations cases with minor children
- **Key authority:** MCL 552.501 et seq.; MCR 3.208-3.224

## II. FOC Investigation Process

### Triggering an Investigation
An FOC investigation may be triggered by:
1. Court referral (judge orders investigation)
2. Party request (motion for investigation)
3. FOC's own initiative (if concerned about child welfare)
4. Initial custody/parenting time petition (automatic in some counties)

### Investigation Steps
1. **Intake:** FOC receives referral or request
2. **Interviews:** Both parents interviewed separately
3. **Home study:** Investigator visits both homes (if ordered)
4. **Collateral contacts:** School, doctors, therapists, references
5. **Record review:** Court file, police reports, DHS records
6. **Child interview:** If age-appropriate
7. **Report preparation:** Written report with findings and recommendation

### Your Rights During Investigation
- Right to be interviewed
- Right to provide documents and evidence
- Right to identify witnesses for the investigator to contact
- Right to know what allegations are being investigated
- Right to submit a written statement
- **No right** to see the investigator's notes during investigation
- **No right** to cross-examine during investigation phase

### Maximizing Your Investigation Outcome
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Be cooperative and responsive | Be hostile or uncooperative with investigator |
| Provide organized documentation | Dump piles of disorganized papers |
| Focus on child's needs, not your grievances | Make the interview about attacking the other parent |
| Identify specific witnesses with contact info | Give vague references without contact information |
| Follow up in writing after interview | Pester the investigator with daily calls |
| Present a clean, child-ready home | Have unsafe conditions during home visit |

## III. FOC Referee Hearings

### Authority
**MCR 3.215:** Domestic relations referees may conduct hearings on:
- Support (temporary and permanent)
- Parenting time disputes
- Custody disputes (initial and modification)
- Show cause / contempt for order violations
- Other matters referred by the court

### Referee Hearing Procedure
1. **Scheduling:** FOC schedules hearing; notice sent to all parties
2. **Evidence:** Sworn testimony, documents, exhibits admitted
3. **Rules of evidence:** Generally applied but less formal than trial
4. **Cross-examination:** Right to cross-examine witnesses
5. **Recommendation:** Referee issues written recommendation
6. **Timeframe:** Recommendation typically within 14-28 days of hearing

### Preparing for Referee Hearing
```
□ Organize all documentary evidence with exhibit list
□ Prepare witness list with expected testimony summaries
□ Write outline of key points for direct testimony
□ Prepare cross-examination questions for opposing witnesses
□ Have copies of all exhibits for referee, opposing party, and yourself
□ Dress professionally; arrive early
□ Bring a notebook for contemporaneous notes
□ Know which MCL/MCR sections support your position
```

## IV. Objection to FOC Recommendation — MCR 3.218

### The Critical 21-Day Deadline

**MCR 3.218(C)(1):** A party may file a written objection to the referee's recommendation within **21 days** after the recommendation is served.

**THIS DEADLINE IS JURISDICTIONAL.**
- Failure to object within 21 days = recommendation becomes the court order
- No extensions unless extraordinary circumstances
- Calendar this deadline IMMEDIATELY upon receiving the recommendation

### Objection Requirements

**MCR 3.218(C)(2):** The objection must:
1. Be in writing
2. Be filed with the court within 21 days
3. Identify the specific findings or recommendations objected to
4. State the basis for each objection
5. Be served on the other party

## V. De Novo Hearing — MCR 3.218(D)

### What "De Novo" Means
- Fresh hearing before the judge
- Judge makes independent findings — NOT bound by referee's recommendation
- Both parties present evidence and testimony
- Full Rules of Evidence apply (more formally than referee hearing)
- Judge weighs credibility independently

### De Novo Hearing Procedure
1. **Scheduling:** Court schedules hearing after objection filed (typically 30-60 days)
2. **Burden:** The party who requested the original hearing generally bears the burden
3. **Evidence:** May present evidence presented to referee PLUS new evidence
4. **Witnesses:** Subpoena power available; bring your witnesses
5. **Legal argument:** Present legal authorities supporting your position
6. **Order:** Judge enters order after hearing

### De Novo Hearing Preparation Checklist
```
□ Review referee's recommendation line by line
□ Identify specific errors of fact (what referee got wrong)
□ Identify specific errors of law (what legal standard referee misapplied)
□ Prepare additional evidence not available at referee hearing
□ Subpoena witnesses (MCR 2.506)
□ Prepare exhibit binder (organized, tabbed, copies for all parties + judge)
□ Draft proposed findings of fact and conclusions of law
□ Prepare opening statement (brief, focused on key issues)
□ Prepare closing argument with citations to testimony and exhibits
```

### Scope of De Novo Review
- Court reviews ONLY the matters specifically objected to
- Unobjected findings/recommendations stand as the court's order
- This is why objections must be specific and comprehensive
- If you missed an issue in your objection, you cannot raise it at de novo hearing

## VI. FOC Enforcement

### Parenting Time Enforcement
FOC can:
1. Investigate complaints of parenting time interference
2. Recommend makeup parenting time
3. Recommend modification of parenting time order
4. Recommend contempt proceedings
5. Mediate disputes between parents

### Support Enforcement
FOC can:
1. Income withholding orders
2. Tax refund intercept
3. License suspension (driver's, professional, recreational)
4. Contempt proceedings
5. Referral to prosecuting attorney for felony non-support

### Filing an FOC Complaint
1. Contact FOC office (in person, phone, or online if available)
2. Provide specific information about the violation
3. Include documentary evidence (text messages, calendars, photos)
4. Be specific about dates, times, and witnesses
5. Follow up regularly—FOC handles high volume and cases fall through cracks

## VII. FOC Opt-Out — MCL 552.505d

### Limited Right to Opt Out
Parties may opt out of FOC services in certain circumstances:
- Both parties must agree in writing
- Court must approve the opt-out
- Court retains authority to reinstate FOC involvement
- Opt-out does NOT apply to child support collection
- Opt-out allows parties to manage custody/parenting time without FOC oversight

### When to Opt Out
- Both parties are cooperative and can manage disputes directly
- Privacy concerns with FOC involvement
- FOC investigation was biased or inadequate

### When NOT to Opt Out
- Opposing party is non-compliant (you NEED FOC enforcement)
- History of domestic violence (FOC provides protection)
- Complex custody issues requiring investigation
- **Generally: Pro se plaintiff should NOT opt out—FOC enforcement tools are valuable**
