# Parenting Time — MCL 722.27a Complete Reference

> Modification, enforcement, makeup time, contempt for violations,
> and reasonable parenting time standards under Michigan law.

---

## I. Statutory Framework — MCL 722.27a

### Core Principle
**MCL 722.27a(1):** "Parenting time shall be granted in accordance with the best interests of the child."

### Right to Parenting Time
**MCL 722.27a(3):** A child has a RIGHT to parenting time with both parents. The court shall not deny parenting time to a parent unless:
- Parenting time would endanger the child's physical, mental, or emotional health
- The specific basis for the finding is documented

**This is a PRESUMPTION in favor of parenting time.** The burden is on the party seeking to restrict parenting time to show endangerment.

## II. Parenting Time Modification

### Threshold for Modification

Unlike custody modification (which requires proper cause/change of circumstances under Vodvarka), parenting time modification has a LOWER threshold:

**Shade v. Wright, 291 Mich App 17 (2010):**
- Proper cause or change of circumstances must be shown
- But the standard is less stringent than custody modification
- Court evaluates whether modification serves the child's best interests

### Grounds for Modification
1. **Change in work schedule** — parent's availability changed
2. **Child's age and needs** — older children have different scheduling needs
3. **Violation of current order** — non-compliance justifies restructuring
4. **Geographic change** — within 100-mile radius
5. **Safety concerns** — new information about risk
6. **Child's preference** — as child matures, preference may warrant change
7. **Parenting time interference** — systematic denial warrants restructuring

### Modification Procedure
1. File motion alleging grounds for modification
2. Court may refer to FOC for investigation (MCR 3.218)
3. FOC investigation and recommendation
4. Objection hearing if either party objects to FOC recommendation
5. Court order modifying parenting time

## III. Reasonable Parenting Time — MCL 722.27a(7)

### What Constitutes "Reasonable" Parenting Time

Michigan does not have a statutory definition of "reasonable," but case law and practice establish:

**Standard parenting time (non-custodial parent):**
- Every other weekend (Friday after school to Sunday evening)
- One evening per week (midweek dinner)
- Alternating holidays
- Extended summer time (2-6 weeks)
- Shared school breaks

**Liberal/expanded parenting time:**
- Week on/week off (50/50)
- 5-2-2-5 schedule
- 3-4-4-3 schedule
- Extended midweek overnight

**Restricted parenting time:**
- Supervised visitation
- Daytime only (no overnights)
- Specific location requirements
- Third-party supervised exchanges

### Factors for Determining Parenting Time Schedule
MCL 722.27a(7) lists factors including:
1. Existence of special circumstances or need for reasonable accommodations
2. Whether the child is a nursing child (under 6 months)
3. Whether parenting time would endanger child's health
4. Likelihood each parent will comply with the schedule
5. Any history of domestic violence
6. Distance between parents' homes
7. Each parent's work schedule
8. Child's school schedule
9. Any other relevant factor

## IV. Makeup Parenting Time

### MCL 722.27a(8)
When parenting time has been wrongfully denied, the aggrieved parent is entitled to makeup parenting time:

**Elements to establish:**
1. A parenting time order exists
2. The order was violated (time was denied or interfered with)
3. The denial was unjustified (no legitimate emergency or excuse)
4. The aggrieved parent requested makeup time

**How makeup time works:**
- Must be requested within a reasonable time after the denial
- Court should order makeup time equivalent to what was lost
- Makeup time should be at a time convenient for the child
- Cannot be used to stockpile—should be exercised reasonably soon

### Documentation of Denied Parenting Time

Maintain a contemporaneous log:

| Date | Scheduled Time | What Happened | Communication Evidence | Witnesses |
|------|---------------|---------------|----------------------|-----------|
| MM/DD/YYYY | Fri 6pm - Sun 6pm | Mother refused exchange; claimed child was sick | Text messages (attached) | [Name] |
| MM/DD/YYYY | Wed 5pm - 8pm | Mother did not appear at exchange location | Dashcam/timestamp photo | [Name] |
| MM/DD/YYYY | Fri 6pm - Sun 6pm | Mother took child out of town without notice | Social media posts | None |

## V. Parenting Time Interference

### MCL 722.27a(7)(c) — Court's Remedies

When the court finds parenting time has been wrongfully denied, it may:

1. **Makeup parenting time** — Additional time equivalent to what was denied
2. **Modification of custody** — Interference is a basis for custody modification (factor (j))
3. **Civil fine** — Up to $100 per incident
4. **Contempt** — Civil contempt under MCR 3.606
5. **Counseling/mediation** — At interfering parent's expense
6. **Attorney fees / pro se equivalent** — Reasonable costs of enforcement

### Building the Interference Case

**Pattern evidence is critical.** A single denial can be excused; a pattern cannot.

| Month | Scheduled Occasions | Actual Occasions Exercised | Denied | Interference Rate |
|-------|--------------------|-----------------------------|--------|-------------------|
| Jan 2024 | 4 | 2 | 2 | 50% |
| Feb 2024 | 4 | 1 | 3 | 75% |
| Mar 2024 | 4 | 3 | 1 | 25% |
| **Cumulative** | **12** | **6** | **6** | **50%** |

A cumulative interference rate above 20% creates a strong presumption of systematic denial.

### Types of Interference
1. **Direct denial:** Refusing to allow exchange
2. **Passive interference:** "Forgetting" the schedule, being unavailable
3. **Schedule manipulation:** Creating conflicts (enrolling in activities during parenting time)
4. **Alienation:** Causing child to resist parenting time
5. **False allegations:** Claiming abuse/neglect to block parenting time
6. **Geographic interference:** Moving to make parenting time impractical
7. **Communication blocking:** Preventing phone/video contact during non-parenting time

## VI. Contempt for Parenting Time Violations

### Elements
1. Clear and definite parenting time order exists
2. Contemnor had knowledge of the order
3. Contemnor violated the order (denied or interfered with parenting time)
4. Violation was willful (no legitimate excuse)
5. Contemnor has present ability to comply

### Common Defenses to Parenting Time Contempt
| Defense | Validity | Response |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Child was sick | May be valid if genuine and documented | Demand medical records; sick child still needs both parents |
| Child refused to go | Not valid—parent must make child available | Parent has obligation to ensure compliance; child does not choose |
| Schedule conflict | Not valid unless order allows flexibility | Order takes priority over voluntary activities |
| Safety concern | May be valid if documented | Demand documentation; unfounded safety claims = bad faith |
| Transportation issue | Partially valid if genuine | Offer alternatives; court may modify exchange logistics |
| Misunderstanding of order | Rarely valid for clear orders | Order should be unambiguous; willful misreading is not excusable |

## VII. Special Parenting Time Issues

### Supervised Parenting Time
- Court may order if safety concerns exist
- Supervised by professional agency, agreed third party, or FOC
- Costs typically borne by supervised parent (but court has discretion)
- Goal is to transition to unsupervised—request review date

### Holiday and Vacation Scheduling
- Holiday schedule takes priority over regular schedule
- Federal holidays, school breaks, birthdays, and parent-specific holidays
- Even/odd year alternation is standard
- First right of refusal: If custodial parent is unavailable, offer time to other parent first

### Long-Distance Parenting Time
- MCL 722.31 (100-mile rule): Cannot move child more than 100 miles without court permission
- If parents live far apart: Extended blocks rather than frequent short visits
- Transportation costs: Typically shared or borne by moving parent
- Virtual parenting time: Supplemental, not replacement

### Third-Party Interference
- Watson family members (Tony Sr., Diana, Tony Jr.) interfering with exchanges
- Document specific incidents of third-party interference
- Motion to modify exchange conditions (neutral location, police standby)
- If criminal conduct: File police report + contempt motion

## VIII. Parenting Time Orders — Essential Provisions

Every parenting time order should address:
```
□ Regular schedule (weekdays, weekends)
□ Holiday schedule (specific holidays, alternation)
□ Summer schedule (extended time, notification deadlines)
□ School break schedule (spring, winter, fall)
□ Birthday provisions (child's birthday, parent's birthday)
□ Exchange logistics (location, time, who transports)
□ Right of first refusal (threshold: X hours)
□ Communication provisions (phone, video, frequency)
□ Travel notification requirements
□ Makeup time provisions
□ Dispute resolution mechanism (FOC, mediation)
□ Modification procedure
```
