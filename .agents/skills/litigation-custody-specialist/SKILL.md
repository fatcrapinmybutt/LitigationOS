## 12-Factor Analysis Framework — MCL 722.23

The "best interests of the child" means the sum total of the following factors, each evaluated and weighed by the court:

### Factor (a) — Love, Affection, Emotional Ties

**Statute:** "The love, affection, and other emotional ties existing between the parties involved and the child."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Child's statements of preference for time with each parent | Child interview, therapist, GAL | High |
| Parent's demonstrated affection (cards, gifts, involvement) | Documentary evidence | Medium |
| Child's emotional reaction to transitions | Witnesses, therapist notes | High |
| History of parent's emotional availability | Testimony, school/daycare records | Medium |

### Factor (b) — Capacity to Give Love, Affection, Guidance

**Statute:** "The capacity and disposition of the parties involved to give the child love, affection, and guidance and to continue the education and raising of the child in his or her religion or creed, if any."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Parenting classes completed | Certificates, course records | Medium |
| Involvement in child's education | School records, teacher testimony | High |
| Religious/cultural continuation | Church attendance, community involvement | Medium |
| Daily routine and structure provided | Testimony, schedule documentation | High |

### Factor (c) — Capacity to Provide Food, Clothing, Medical Care

**Statute:** "The capacity and disposition of the parties involved to provide the child with food, clothing, medical care or other remedial care recognized and permitted under the laws of this state in place of medical care, and other material needs."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Income and employment stability | Pay stubs, tax returns, employer verification | High |
| Housing adequacy | Lease, photos, inspection | High |
| Health insurance coverage | Insurance cards, plan documents | Medium |
| Track record of meeting child's material needs | Receipts, testimony | Medium |

### Factor (d) — Length of Time in Stable Environment

**Statute:** "The length of time the child has lived in a stable, satisfactory environment, and the desirability of maintaining continuity."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Residence history | Lease records, utility bills, school enrollment | High |
| Frequency of moves or disruptions | Testimony, documentary evidence | High |
| Child's adjustment to current environment | School performance, therapist reports | High |
| Stability of household composition | Testimony, records | Medium |

### Factor (e) — Permanence of Family Unit

**Statute:** "The permanence, as a family unit, of the existing or proposed custodial home or homes."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Marriage/cohabitation stability | Records, testimony | Medium |
| Household member stability | Testimony, records | Medium |
| Extended family involvement | Testimony | Low |
| Plans for future stability | Testimony, evidence of commitment | Medium |

### Factor (f) — Moral Fitness

**Statute:** "The moral fitness of the parties involved."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Criminal history | ICHAT records, court records | High (if relevant to child) |
| Substance abuse history | Treatment records, testimony | High |
| Domestic violence history | Police reports, PPO records | Very High |
| Honesty with the court | Prior testimony, filings vs. evidence | Medium |
| Parental alienation behavior | Communications, testimony | High |

### Factor (g) — Mental and Physical Health

**Statute:** "The mental and physical health of the parties involved."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Mental health treatment records | With consent or subpoena | High |
| Physical health limitations | Medical records | Medium (if affects parenting) |
| Substance abuse treatment | Treatment records | High |
| Fitness to parent evaluation | Court-ordered evaluation | Very High |

### Factor (h) — Home, School, Community Record

**Statute:** "The home, school, and community record of the child."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| School grades and attendance | School records | High |
| Extracurricular activities | Registration records, testimony | Medium |
| Community involvement | Testimony, records | Low |
| Behavioral issues at school | School discipline records | High |
| Friendships and social adjustment | Testimony, therapist | Medium |

### Factor (i) — Reasonable Preference of Child

**Statute:** "The reasonable preference of the child, if the court considers the child to be of sufficient age to express preference."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| In camera interview by judge | Court record | Very High |
| Child's statements to GAL | GAL report | High (if unbiased) |
| Child's statements to therapist | Therapist records/testimony | High |
| Child's age and maturity | Observable factors | Threshold determination |

**Note:** No specific age threshold in Michigan; judge determines if child is "of sufficient age." Generally considered meaningful at age 6+, significant at 12+.

### Factor (j) — Willingness to Facilitate Relationship

**Statute:** "The willingness and ability of each of the parties to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent or the child and the parents."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| Parenting time interference instances | Calendar, texts, police reports | Very High |
| Disparaging remarks about other parent | Witnesses, child's statements | High |
| Flexibility in scheduling | Communications | Medium |
| Support for child's relationship with other parent | Testimony, evidence | High |
| Gatekeeping behavior | Pattern evidence | Very High |

**THIS IS THE CRITICAL FACTOR IN PIGORS v WATSON.** Parenting time interference, alienation behavior, and gatekeeping by Amy Watson should be documented exhaustively.

### Factor (k) — Domestic Violence

**Statute:** "Domestic violence, regardless of whether the violence was directed against or witnessed by the child."

**Evidence mapping:**
| Evidence Type | Source | Weight |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| PPO history (any party) | Court records | Very High |
| Police reports | Law enforcement records | Very High |
| DV shelter records | With consent | High |
| Witness statements | Testimony | Medium |
| Pattern of coercive control | Documentary evidence | High |

### Factor (l) — Any Other Relevant Factor

**Statute:** "Any other factor considered by the court to be relevant to a particular child custody dispute."

**Common "other factors":**
- Parental alienation
- False allegations
- Interference with parent-child bond
- Willingness to cooperate with court orders
- Pro se litigant accommodation needs

## Established Custodial Environment — MCL 722.27(1)(c)

### Definition
An established custodial environment exists if "over an appreciable time the child naturally looks to the custodian in that environment for guidance, discipline, the necessities of life, and parental comfort."

### Burden of Proof
| Situation | Burden | Standard |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Change would alter ECE | Moving party | Clear and convincing evidence |
| Change would NOT alter ECE | Moving party | Preponderance of evidence |
| ECE exists with BOTH parents | Either party | Preponderance |

### Application to Pigors
- Does ECE exist with Amy Watson? Analyze duration, child's attachment, routine
- Does ECE exist with Pigors? Analyze parenting time consistency, child's reliance
- If ECE is only with Amy Watson: Pigors must show by CLEAR AND CONVINCING evidence that change serves best interests
- Strategy: Argue ECE exists with BOTH parents (if supported by facts) to lower burden to preponderance

## Custody Modification — MCL 722.27

### Threshold Requirement
Before court can modify custody, moving party must show either:
1. **Proper cause:** Appropriate grounds for bringing the motion (legal basis)
2. **Change of circumstances:** Material change since last custody order

**Vodvarka v. Grasmeyer, 259 Mich App 499 (2003):** Proper cause means one or more appropriate grounds that have or could have a significant effect on the child's life sufficient to warrant revisiting the custody order. Change of circumstances must be significant enough to warrant revisiting and must have occurred since the entry of the last custody order.

### Procedure
1. File motion alleging proper cause or change of circumstances
2. Court conducts threshold hearing (Vodvarka hearing)
3. If threshold met → full evidentiary hearing on all 12 best interest factors
4. If threshold NOT met → motion denied without reaching best interests

## Output Format

When generating custody analysis:
1. Address the threshold requirement (proper cause / change of circumstances)
2. Analyze all 12 factors with available evidence
