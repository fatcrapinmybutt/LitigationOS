## Remedy Type Explanations

### Compensatory Damages
The primary remedy for most causes of action. Includes:
- **Economic damages**: Medical bills, lost wages, repair costs, moving expenses, rental differential, storage fees
- **Non-economic damages**: Pain and suffering, emotional distress, loss of use and enjoyment, inconvenience
- **Consequential damages**: Foreseeable downstream losses caused by the wrong

### Punitive / Exemplary Damages
Michigan has largely abolished common law punitive damages (Veselenak v Smith, 414 Mich 567 (1982)). Available only where:
- A specific statute authorizes them (Fair Housing Act punitive damages)
- Characterized as "exemplary" under specific statutory scheme
- NOT available for common law tort claims in Michigan

### Injunctive Relief
Available when:
1. Irreparable harm — money damages insufficient
2. Likelihood of success on the merits
3. Balance of equities favors plaintiff
4. Public interest is served
**Authority:** Thermatool Corp v Borzym, 227 Mich App 366 (1998)

### Declaratory Relief
Available under MCR 2.605 when:
1. An actual controversy exists
2. Declaratory judgment will resolve the controversy
3. No other adequate remedy at law
**Use cases:** Lease validity, rights under contract, constitutional questions

### Specific Performance
Extraordinary equitable remedy. Available only when:
1. Valid enforceable contract exists
2. Plaintiff performed or tendered performance
3. Money damages are inadequate
4. Contract terms are sufficiently definite
**Primarily used for:** Real property transactions, unique goods

### Treble / Double Damages
Statutory multipliers — available ONLY under specific statutes:

| Statute | Multiplier | Trigger | Authority |
|---------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| MCPA (knowing violation) | 3x actual damages | Defendant knew practice was deceptive | MCL 445.911(2) |
| Security Deposit Act | 2x wrongful retention | Failure to return/itemize within 30 days | MCL 554.613 |
| Forcible Entry/Detainer | 3x damages | Illegal self-help eviction | MCL 600.2918 |
| Timber Trespass | 3x timber value | Unlawful cutting/removal of trees | MCL 600.2919 |

### Attorney Fees
Michigan follows the American Rule — fees awarded only where:
- Statute explicitly provides (MCPA, Elliott-Larsen, § 1983/§ 1988, security deposit)
- Contract contains fee-shifting clause
- MCR 2.625 allows costs (but NOT attorney fees without statutory basis)
- Court sanctions under MCR 2.114 (frivolous pleading) or MCR 2.313 (discovery abuse)

## Damages Calculation Framework

### Step 1 — Identify All Viable Claims
List every cause of action with supporting elements.

### Step 2 — Calculate Per-Claim Damages
For each claim, compute:
- Direct economic loss
- Consequential economic loss
- Non-economic damages (where available)
- Statutory minimums (MCPA $250 floor)

### Step 3 — Apply Multipliers
For claims with treble/double provisions:
- Verify the statutory trigger is met (e.g., "knowing" for MCPA)
- Apply multiplier to actual damages only
- Do NOT double-count overlapping damages across claims

### Step 4 — Add Fee-Shifting
For claims with attorney fee provisions:
- Track time and expenses separately per claim
- Apportion fees to fee-eligible claims
- Lodestar method: reasonable hours × reasonable rate

### Step 5 — Total Demand Calculation
```
Total = Σ (Per-Claim Compensatory)
      + Σ (Statutory Multipliers where applicable)
      + Attorney Fees (on eligible claims)
      + Costs (filing fees, service fees, expert fees)
      - Overlap Reduction (no double recovery for same loss)
```

### Step 6 — Prayer for Relief Structure
Each count in the complaint gets its own prayer. The general prayer aggregates:
- All compensatory damages
- All statutory enhancements
- Attorney fees and costs
- Pre-judgment interest — MCL 600.6013 (commercial) / MCL 600.6455 (tort)
- Post-judgment interest — MCL 600.6013
- "Such other and further relief as this Court deems just and equitable"

## Pre-Judgment Interest

| Claim Type | Rate | Authority |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Contract / commercial | Statutory rate or contract rate | MCL 600.6013 |
| Tort (personal injury) | Statutory rate from filing date | MCL 600.6455 |
| Wrongful death | Statutory rate | MCL 600.6455 |

Pre-judgment interest compensates plaintiff for the time value of money between injury and judgment. Always include in prayer for relief.
