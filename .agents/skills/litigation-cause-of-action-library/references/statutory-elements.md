# Michigan Statutory Claims — Complete Elements Reference

> Jurisdiction: Michigan | Context: Pigors v Watson | 14th Judicial Circuit, Muskegon County

---

## 1. MICHIGAN CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT (MCPA) — MCL 445.903

### Overview
Prohibits unfair, unconscionable, or deceptive methods, acts, or practices in trade or commerce. Provides private right of action with treble damages for knowing violations.

### Elements (3):
1. Defendant engaged in trade or commerce
2. Defendant committed an unfair, unconscionable, or deceptive act or practice listed in MCL 445.903
3. Plaintiff suffered loss as a result

### 29 Prohibited Practices (MCL 445.903 selections most relevant to housing/consumer):
| # | Practice | Subsection |
|---|---------|------------|
| (c) | Representing goods/services have characteristics they do not have | 445.903(1)(c) |
| (e) | Representing goods/services are of a particular quality when they are not | 445.903(1)(e) |
| (n) | Causing coercion or duress as a means of effecting a transaction | 445.903(1)(n) |
| (s) | Failing to reveal a material fact that deceives or tends to deceive | 445.903(1)(s) |
| (t) | Entering unconscionable contracts | 445.903(1)(t) |
| (y) | Gross discrepancies between oral and written representations | 445.903(1)(y) |
| (z) | Making misleading statements about availability of products/services | 445.903(1)(z) |
| (bb) | Making a representation intended to create undue anxiety | 445.903(1)(bb) |
| (cc) | Taking advantage of consumer's inability to protect their interests | 445.903(1)(cc) |

### Burden: Preponderance of the evidence
### SOL: 6 years — MCL 445.911(7)
### Remedies:
- Actual damages or $250 minimum — MCL 445.911(2)
- **Treble damages** for knowing violations — MCL 445.911(2)
- Attorney fees — MCL 445.911(2)
- Injunctive relief — MCL 445.911(1)
- Class action available — MCL 445.911(3)

### Key Case: Dix v American Bankers Life Assurance Co, 429 Mich 410 (1987)
### Note: MCPA has a "transaction" exemption for conduct specifically authorized by regulatory scheme. Landsfield v State Farm, unpub. Verify the transaction doesn't fall under a regulated exemption.

## 2. TRUTH IN RENTING ACT — MCL 554.631 et seq.

### Elements (3):
1. A residential rental agreement exists
2. The agreement contains a provision that violates MCL 554.633 (prohibited clauses)
3. Plaintiff suffered loss from the inclusion or enforcement of the prohibited provision

### Prohibited Lease Provisions (MCL 554.633):
- Waiver of landlord's liability for negligence
- Waiver of tenant's right to habitable premises
- Acceleration clauses for entire remaining rent
- Confession of judgment clauses
- Waiver of right to jury trial
- Waiver of notice requirements
- Provisions exceeding statutory late fees
- Provisions authorizing self-help eviction

### Burden: Preponderance of the evidence
### SOL: 6 years — MCL 600.5807(8) (contract theory)
### Remedies: Void the offending provision, actual damages, $250 statutory penalty per violation — MCL 554.636
### Key Case: Tuscany Grove Dev Co v Perrotta (distinguishing void vs voidable lease provisions)

## 3. HOUSING DISCRIMINATION — ELLIOTT-LARSEN CIVIL RIGHTS ACT (MCL 37.2101 et seq.)

### Elements (4):
1. Plaintiff is a member of a protected class (or perceived as such)
2. Plaintiff applied for or occupied housing
3. Defendant took adverse housing action against plaintiff
4. Protected class status was a motivating factor in the adverse action

### Protected Classes (MCL 37.2502):
Religion, race, color, national origin, age, sex, familial status, marital status, disability

### Prohibited Acts (MCL 37.2502):
- Refuse to sell/rent/lease
- Discriminate in terms or conditions
- Publish discriminatory statements
- Misrepresent availability
- Deny access to MLS or related services

### Burden: Preponderance using McDonnell Douglas burden-shifting framework
### SOL: 3 years — MCL 600.5805(2) (personal injury)
### Remedies: Actual damages, injunctive relief, civil fines, attorney fees — MCL 37.2801
### Key Case: Fair Housing Center of West Michigan v Woodland Mall, 2002 WL 31307484

## 4. WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY / FITNESS — MCL 554.139

### Elements (3):
1. A lease agreement exists for residential premises
2. The premises are not reasonably fit for their intended use OR the landlord failed to keep premises and common areas in reasonable repair
3. Plaintiff suffered damages from the defective condition

### Two Covenants (MCL 554.139(1)):
- **(a)** Premises and all common areas are fit for the use intended by the parties
- **(b)** Premises are kept in reasonable repair during the term of the lease

### Burden: Preponderance of the evidence
### SOL: 6 years — MCL 600.5807(8) (statutory/contract)
### Remedies: Rent abatement, repair costs, consequential damages, lease termination, damages for diminished value of tenancy
### Key Case: Trentadue v Gorton, 479 Mich 378 (2007) (affirming MCL 554.139 cannot be waived)
### Note: The covenant CANNOT be waived by lease terms. Any lease provision purporting to waive habitability is void. Applies to all residential leases regardless of what the lease says.

## 5. SECURITY DEPOSIT ACT — MCL 554.601 et seq.

### Elements for Wrongful Retention:
1. A security deposit was collected — MCL 554.602
2. Landlord failed to comply with statutory procedures upon lease termination:
   - Itemized list of damages within 30 days — MCL 554.609
   - Return of remaining deposit within 30 days — MCL 554.609
3. Plaintiff suffered loss (unreturned deposit or improper deductions)

### Landlord Obligations:
- Cannot demand deposit > 1.5 months' rent — MCL 554.602
- Must provide written notice of deposit holder within 14 days — MCL 554.603
- Must provide inventory checklist at move-in — MCL 554.608
- Must return deposit (or itemized deductions) within 30 days of termination — MCL 554.609
- Cannot deduct normal wear and tear — MCL 554.609

### Burden: Preponderance; landlord bears burden of proving damages justify retention
### SOL: 6 years — MCL 600.5807(8)
### Remedies: Double the amount wrongfully withheld — MCL 554.613; attorney fees; costs
### Key Case: Etter v Roper, unpub; Tumbarella v Kroger Co, 85 Mich App 482 (1978)

## 6. RETALIATORY ACTION — MCL 600.5720

### Elements (3):
1. Tenant exercised a legal right (complained to code enforcement, withheld rent per statute, organized tenants)
2. Landlord took adverse action within 90 days of the tenant's protected activity
3. The adverse action was retaliatory (presumed retaliatory if within 90 days)

### Protected Tenant Activities:
- Complaining to government agency about code violations
- Exercising rights under the lease or law
- Organizing or joining a tenant organization
- Testifying in court proceedings related to housing

### Retaliatory Actions Include:
- Eviction proceedings
- Rent increase
- Decrease in services
- Threats of eviction
- Refusal to renew lease

### Burden: Once tenant shows protected activity + adverse action within 90 days, burden shifts to landlord to prove non-retaliatory motive
### SOL: 3 years — MCL 600.5805(2)
### Remedies: Defense to eviction, actual damages, injunctive relief
### Key Case: Silberstein v Silberstein, unpub (discussing scope of protected activities)

## 7. BUILDING CODE VIOLATIONS — MCL 125.1501 et seq. (State Construction Code)

### Elements for Civil Claim:
1. Property is subject to Michigan building/housing code
2. Specific code provisions are violated
3. The violations create unsafe or uninhabitable conditions
4. Plaintiff suffered damages as a result

### Relevance to Litigation:
- Establishes negligence per se for safety violations
- Supports habitability claims under MCL 554.139
- Creates grounds for code enforcement complaints
- Can establish constructive knowledge of dangerous conditions

### Burden: Preponderance; code violation establishes standard of care (negligence per se)
### SOL: 3 years (negligence per se) — MCL 600.5805(10)
### Remedies: Compensatory damages, repair orders, occupancy restrictions
### Key Authority: International Property Maintenance Code (IPMC) as adopted by Michigan

## 8. MANUFACTURED HOUSING — MCL 125.2301 et seq.

### Overview:
Michigan Manufactured Housing Commission Act governs manufactured home communities (mobile home parks). Creates additional rights and obligations beyond general landlord-tenant law.

### Key Provisions:
- **MCL 125.2312d**: Prohibited lease terms in manufactured housing communities
- **MCL 125.2312e**: Required lease disclosures
- **MCL 125.2312f**: Eviction procedures specific to manufactured housing
- **MCL 125.2310**: License requirements for community operators

### Relevance to Pigors v Watson:
If the property involves a manufactured/mobile home community, this Act creates separate causes of action for violations of community operator obligations.

### Remedies: Actual damages, injunctive relief, license revocation for community operator

## 9. FEDERAL — 42 USC § 1983 (Civil Rights)

### Elements (3):
1. Defendant acted under color of state law
2. Defendant deprived plaintiff of a right secured by the Constitution or federal law
3. Plaintiff suffered damages

### "Under Color of State Law" Doctrine:
State action required. Private parties qualify ONLY through:
- **Public function test**: Performing a traditionally exclusive government function
- **State compulsion test**: State compelled the private conduct
- **Joint action/symbiotic relationship**: State and private party so intertwined that private action is effectively state action
- **Nexus test**: Sufficiently close nexus between state and private conduct

### Burden: Preponderance of the evidence
### SOL: 3 years (borrowed from Michigan personal injury) — MCL 600.5805(2); Owens v Okure, 488 US 235 (1989)
### Remedies: Compensatory damages, injunctive/declaratory relief, attorney fees — 42 USC § 1988
### Key Case: Lugar v Edmondson Oil Co, 457 US 922 (1982) (state action framework); Monroe v Pape, 365 US 167 (1961)

## 10. FEDERAL — FAIR HOUSING ACT (42 USC § 3601 et seq.)

### Elements (4):
1. Plaintiff is a member of a protected class
2. Plaintiff applied for or occupied a dwelling
3. Defendant engaged in a prohibited discriminatory practice
4. Protected class status was a motivating factor

### Protected Classes (federal):
Race, color, religion, sex, familial status, national origin, disability

### Prohibited Practices:
- Refusal to sell/rent — 42 USC § 3604(a)
- Discrimination in terms/conditions — 42 USC § 3604(b)
- Discriminatory advertising — 42 USC § 3604(c)
- Blockbusting — 42 USC § 3604(e)
- Discriminatory lending — 42 USC § 3605

### Burden: Preponderance; burden-shifting per McDonnell Douglas
### SOL: 2 years — 42 USC § 3613(a)(1)(A)
### Remedies: Actual damages, injunctive relief, punitive damages, attorney fees — 42 USC § 3613(c)
### Key Case: Texas Dept of Housing v Inclusive Communities Project, 576 US 519 (2015) (disparate impact cognizable)
