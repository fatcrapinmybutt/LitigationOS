## Local Civil Rules (Key Provisions)

### LCivR 5.7 — Electronic Filing (CM/ECF)

- **Mandatory e-filing** for all represented parties
- **Pro se exception:** Pro se parties may file in paper unless granted CM/ECF access
- **Pro se e-filing option:** May request CM/ECF login from Clerk's Office
  - Requires signing the CM/ECF Registration Form
  - Training available from Clerk's Office
  - Recommended if filing multiple documents
- **Paper filing:** Submit original + one copy to Clerk's Office
- **Service on non-CM/ECF parties:** Must serve by U.S. mail or other permitted means

### LCivR 7.1 — Motion Practice

| Step | Deadline | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Motion + Brief** | Filed together | Brief required for all non-routine motions |
| **Response** | 28 days after service | Must include concise statement of issues + argument |
| **Reply** | 14 days after response | Optional — only new issues raised in response |
| **Page limits** | 28 pages (brief) | Excluding tables/certificates |
| **Oral argument** | At court's discretion | Request in brief if desired |
| **Concurrence statement** | Required | State whether opposing party concurs; if no response after good-faith effort, state so |

**Concurrence Requirement (LCivR 7.1(d)):**
Before filing any non-dispositive motion, movant must seek concurrence from opposing party. State in motion:
- "Undersigned has sought concurrence. [Opposing party] [concurs/does not concur/has not responded]."

### LCivR 7.2 — Dispositive Motions (MSJ, MTD, etc.)

- **Same deadlines as LCivR 7.1** for briefing
- **Statement of Material Facts** required with summary judgment motions
  - Numbered paragraphs, each citing specific record evidence
  - Opposing party must respond to each numbered paragraph: admit, deny, or qualify
- **Discovery deadline:** All dispositive motions must be filed by the discovery cutoff or as specified in scheduling order

### LCivR 10.6 — Pro Se Filings

- Pro se litigants held to same filing requirements but given liberal construction
- **Signature requirement:** All filings must be signed with name, address, phone, email
- **Paper format:** 8.5" × 11", one-sided, double-spaced text, 1" margins
- **Case number:** Must appear on all filings
- **Copies:** Original + one copy for paper filings

### LCivR 16.1 — Case Management

**Initial Scheduling Conference:**
- Typically held within 120 days of service on defendants
- Magistrate judge usually conducts
- **Pro se parties:** Must attend (telephonic appearance may be permitted)

**Case Management Order typically includes:**
| Phase | Typical Deadline |
|-------|-----------------|
| Initial disclosures (FRCP 26(a)(1)) | 14 days after FRCP 26(f) conference |
| FRCP 26(f) planning conference | At least 21 days before scheduling conference |
| Fact discovery cutoff | 6-9 months from case management order |
| Expert disclosure | Per FRCP 26(a)(2) schedule in CMO |
| Expert discovery cutoff | 30 days after expert disclosures |
| Dispositive motion deadline | 30-60 days after discovery cutoff |
| Final pretrial conference | 30-60 days before trial |
| Trial | 12-18 months from filing (typical) |

### LCivR 26.1 — Discovery

- **Initial disclosures:** Required under FRCP 26(a)(1) — 14 days after FRCP 26(f) conference
- **Interrogatories:** Max 25 (including subparts) per FRCP 33
- **Depositions:** Max 10 per side, 7 hours each per FRCP 30
- **Discovery disputes:** Meet and confer REQUIRED before filing motion to compel
- **Privilege log:** Required for all withheld documents — must be sufficiently detailed

### LCivR 37.1 — Discovery Motions

**Meet-and-confer requirement:**
1. Attempt good-faith resolution by phone or in person
2. Document the attempt in the motion
3. State specifically what was requested, what was refused, and why it matters
4. File motion to compel only after genuine effort fails

### LCivR 41.1 — Dismissal for Want of Prosecution

- Court may dismiss sua sponte for failure to prosecute or comply with rules/orders
- **Pro se warning:** Maintain active case management — respond to ALL court orders promptly
- Failure to appear at scheduled conferences = potential dismissal

## Pro Se Filing Procedures (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Prepare Your Complaint
- Use numbered paragraphs throughout
- Include all required sections (parties, jurisdiction, venue, facts, claims, relief)
- Sign and date
- See `frcp-templates.md` for complete templates

### Step 2: Prepare Civil Cover Sheet
- **Form JS-44** (available at uscourts.gov)
- Check: "Civil Rights — Other" (Nature of Suit: 440) or "Civil Rights — Due Process" (Nature of Suit: 441)
- Identify: basis of jurisdiction (Federal Question, 28 USC § 1331)
- Origin: 1 (Original Proceeding)

### Step 3: IFP Application (if applicable)
- Complete the Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis
- Attach financial documentation if available
- See `frcp-templates.md` Template 3

### Step 4: File
**Paper filing:**
1. Bring original + one copy to Clerk's Office, Room 399
2. Gerald R. Ford Federal Building, 110 Michigan St NW, Grand Rapids
3. Office hours: 8:30 AM — 5:00 PM, Monday-Friday
4. Clerk stamps "Filed" with date on both copies, returns one to you

**E-filing (if registered):**
1. Log into CM/ECF
2. Select "Civil" → "Complaints" or appropriate event
3. Upload PDF documents
4. Pay filing fee or submit IFP application electronically

### Step 5: Summons and Service
- **Request summons** from Clerk's Office for each defendant
- **90 days to serve** under FRCP 4(m)
- **Service methods by defendant type:**

| Defendant Type | Service Method | Authority |
|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Individual (state officer)** | Certified mail + copy to MI Attorney General | FRCP 4(e), MCL 600.1910 |
| **Individual (private person)** | Personal service or certified mail to last known address | FRCP 4(e)(2) |
| **Municipality (County)** | Serve chief executive officer or clerk | FRCP 4(j)(2) |
| **State judge (official capacity)** | Certified mail + copy to MI Attorney General | FRCP 4(j)(2), per 11th Amend. considerations |

**Pro se service tip:** If IFP is granted, you may request the Court to order service by the U.S. Marshal under 28 USC § 1915(d). This eliminates the cost and complexity of service.

### Step 6: After Filing
1. Monitor docket for IFP ruling (if applicable)
2. Watch for 28 USC § 1915(e)(2) screening — court reviews IFP complaints
3. Respond promptly to any deficiency orders
4. Prepare for FRCP 26(f) conference once defendants are served

## Judge Assignment and Magistrate Procedures

### Random Assignment
- Cases randomly assigned to a District Judge
- Magistrate Judge also assigned automatically

### Magistrate Judge Role
- **Consent jurisdiction:** If ALL parties consent, magistrate may handle entire case (28 USC § 636(c))
- **Without consent:** Magistrate handles:
  - Discovery disputes
  - Scheduling conferences
  - Settlement conferences
  - Non-dispositive motions
  - Preliminary review of dispositive motions (Report & Recommendation)
- **Consent form:** Filed within 14 days of service on all defendants
- **Pro se note:** You are NOT required to consent to magistrate jurisdiction

### Objecting to Magistrate R&R
- **14 days** to file written objections to Report & Recommendation
- **Failure to object = waiver** of appellate review
- Objections must be specific — general objections treated as no objection
- District Judge reviews de novo any portion objected to

## IFP Screening Process (28 USC § 1915)

### What Happens After IFP is Granted

The Court **must** screen the complaint and dismiss if:
1. **Frivolous** — lacks arguable basis in law or fact (Neitzke v. Williams, 490 U.S. 319 (1989))
2. **Fails to state a claim** — under Iqbal/Twombly standard
3. **Seeks damages from immune defendant** — e.g., damages against judge for judicial acts

### Surviving Screening (Pro Se Strategy)

- **Anticipate screening dismissal** — draft complaint as if responding to an MTD
- **Address immunity in complaint** — explain why each defendant is not immune to the specific claim
- **Rooker-Feldman preemptive paragraph** — include standard language distinguishing conduct from judgment
- **Plead with particularity** — specific facts, specific dates, specific defendants, specific rights
- **Cite controlling law** — show the court you understand the elements
- **Do NOT:**
  - Assert rambling, conclusory allegations
  - Conflate multiple defendants' conduct
  - Challenge state court judgments instead of process
  - Seek damages where only injunctive relief is available

## W.D. Michigan–Specific Practice Tips

### 1. Pro Se Litigant Resources
- **Federal Pro Se Clinic:** Check with Western Michigan Legal Aid for availability
- **Law Library:** Gerald R. Ford Federal Building (limited access)
- **Self-help guides:** Available on miwd.uscourts.gov under "Pro Se Information"

### 2. Civil Rights Case Patterns in W.D. Michigan
- § 1983 prisoner cases are the majority of pro se filings — your case (family court due process) is less common but well-recognized
- The court is familiar with qualified immunity analysis
- Judges generally follow 6th Circuit qualified immunity framework closely
- Monell claims against counties require strong factual pleading — conclusory "custom" allegations will not survive screening

### 3. Settlement and ADR
- Court may order mediation or facilitative case evaluation
- Pro se parties may participate
- Settlement discussions are confidential and cannot be used at trial
- Consider: federal settlement could resolve federal claims while preserving state-court rights

### 4. Emergency Filings
- TRO motions may be presented ex parte under FRCP 65(b) in "true emergencies"
- Call Clerk's Office to arrange emergency filing and hearing
- After hours: Contact duty judge through Clerk's Office emergency line
- Must demonstrate: (1) specific irreparable harm, (2) why notice is impractical, (3) efforts to notify

### 5. Electronic Filing Tips (if registered)
- File documents as PDF (text-searchable preferred)
- Name files descriptively (e.g., "Complaint.pdf", "Exhibit_A_Court_Order.pdf")
- Maximum file size varies — check current limit on CM/ECF
- Proposed orders: Upload as separate attachment
- Sealed documents: File motion to seal first, then file sealed document upon court order

## FRCP 2025 Amendments Awareness

### Rule 16.1 — Multidistrict Litigation Management
- New centralized management procedures for MDL cases
- Limited direct applicability to single-plaintiff § 1983 case
- Awareness item only — monitor for indirect effects on scheduling

### Rule 26(f) — Discovery Planning Conference Updates
- Enhanced obligation to discuss privilege log format early
- Must address ESI preservation and production format
- Pro se tip: Bring a proposed discovery plan to the conference

### Rule 26 — Discovery Scope
- Proportionality remains central: cost, burden, and benefit analysis
- Pro se litigants: courts may be more flexible on format but expect substantive compliance
- Electronically stored information (ESI): state what formats you can accept

### E-Filing Requirements
- W.D. Michigan continues transition to mandatory e-filing
- Pro se exemption remains available but e-filing encouraged
- CM/ECF training sessions offered periodically — check court website
