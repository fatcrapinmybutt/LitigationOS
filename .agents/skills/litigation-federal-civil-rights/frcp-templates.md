## Template 4: Opposition to Motion to Dismiss

### Structure — Addressing Rooker-Feldman + Immunity

```
UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
WESTERN DISTRICT OF MICHIGAN
SOUTHERN DIVISION

ANDREW JAMES PIGORS,
    Plaintiff,
                                        Case No. ___________
v.                                      Hon. _______________

[DEFENDANTS],
    Defendants.
___________________________________/

     PLAINTIFF'S RESPONSE IN OPPOSITION TO DEFENDANTS' MOTION TO DISMISS

    Plaintiff Andrew James Pigors respectfully opposes Defendants'
Motion to Dismiss and states as follows:

I. INTRODUCTION

    Defendants seek dismissal on grounds of [Rooker-Feldman / judicial
immunity / qualified immunity / failure to state a claim / Younger
abstention]. Each ground fails because [summary of argument].

II. STANDARD OF REVIEW

    On a Rule 12(b)(1) motion (subject matter jurisdiction), the
plaintiff bears the burden to establish jurisdiction. On a Rule 12(b)(6)
motion, the court must accept all well-pleaded facts as true and draw all
reasonable inferences in plaintiff's favor. Ashcroft v. Iqbal, 556 U.S.
662, 678 (2009).

III. ROOKER-FELDMAN DOES NOT BAR PLAINTIFF'S CLAIMS

    A. The Narrowed Doctrine

    After Exxon Mobil Corp. v. Saudi Basic Industries Corp., 544 U.S. 280
(2005), Rooker-Feldman is "confined to cases brought by state-court losers
complaining of injuries caused by state-court judgments rendered before the
district court proceedings commenced and inviting district court review and
rejection of those judgments." Id. at 284.

    B. Plaintiff Challenges Conduct, Not Judgments

    Plaintiff does not ask this Court to reverse any custody order, PPO,
or other state court judgment. Rather, Plaintiff challenges the
unconstitutional CONDUCT of Defendants during the course of state
proceedings:

    [For each claim, distinguish conduct from judgment:]
    - Ex parte communications are not judicial acts subject to review —
      they are independent constitutional violations.
    - Denial of notice and hearing is a process deficiency, not a
      challenge to the hearing's outcome.
    - Retaliation for filing complaints is an independent federal wrong
      regardless of any state judgment.

    C. Independent Federal Injuries

    Plaintiff's injuries exist independently of any state court judgment.
Even if every state court order were affirmed on appeal, the constitutional
violations would remain actionable. See Skinner v. Switzer, 562 U.S. 521
(2011).

    D. Ongoing State Proceedings

    [If applicable:] The state court proceedings remain pending. Rooker-
Feldman requires a final state judgment rendered before federal proceedings.
Because no final judgment exists, the doctrine is inapplicable. Exxon
Mobil, 544 U.S. at 291.

IV. JUDICIAL IMMUNITY DOES NOT BAR ALL CLAIMS

    A. Non-Judicial Acts Are Not Protected

    Mireles v. Waco, 502 U.S. 9, 11-12 (1991). Defendant McNeill's
ex parte communications, scheduling manipulation, and retaliatory conduct
are not "functions normally performed by a judge" and therefore fall outside
judicial immunity.

    B. Injunctive and Declaratory Relief Remain Available

    Pulliam v. Allen, 466 U.S. 522 (1984). Judicial immunity does not
bar prospective injunctive or declaratory relief. Plaintiff's claims for
such relief survive regardless of immunity.

    [For each non-judicial act, cite specific DB evidence:]
    [Source: litigation_context.db → judicial_violations table]

V. QUALIFIED IMMUNITY IS OVERCOME

    A. Constitutional Rights Were Clearly Established

    [For each defendant claiming qualified immunity:]
    Step 1 — Violation: [specific constitutional right violated]
    Step 2 — Clearly established: [cite specific 6th Circuit or SCOTUS
    precedent with analogous facts]

    [Source: litigation_context.db → authority_chains, claims tables]

VI. PLAINTIFF STATES PLAUSIBLE CLAIMS

    [For each challenged count, demonstrate Iqbal/Twombly plausibility
    with specific factual allegations from the Complaint]

denied in its entirety.
```

## Template 5: Summary Judgment Motion (FRCP 56)

### Standard

```
UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
WESTERN DISTRICT OF MICHIGAN
SOUTHERN DIVISION

ANDREW JAMES PIGORS,
    Plaintiff,
                                        Case No. ___________
v.                                      Hon. _______________

[DEFENDANTS],
    Defendants.
___________________________________/

    PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT UNDER FRCP 56

    Plaintiff moves for summary judgment on [Counts ___] because there
is no genuine dispute as to any material fact and Plaintiff is entitled to
judgment as a matter of law. Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(a).

I. STANDARD

    Summary judgment is appropriate when "the movant shows that there is
no genuine dispute as to any material fact and the movant is entitled to
judgment as a matter of law." Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(a). The evidence must
be viewed in the light most favorable to the nonmoving party. Matsushita
Elec. Indus. Co. v. Zenith Radio Corp., 475 U.S. 574, 587 (1986).

    Once the movant demonstrates the absence of a genuine issue, the
nonmoving party must present "specific facts showing that there is a
genuine issue for trial." Celotex Corp. v. Catrett, 477 U.S. 317, 324
(1986).

II. UNDISPUTED MATERIAL FACTS

    [Numbered statements of undisputed fact, each citing record evidence]

    1. On [DATE], [FACT]. (Exhibit _, p. _; Deposition of _, p. _:_)
    2. [Continue with chronological facts]

    [Source: litigation_context.db → master_evidence_timeline,
    evidence_quotes — each fact must be traceable to a DB record]

III. ARGUMENT

    A. Count [X] — [Constitutional Violation]

    Element 1: [State element] — [Cite undisputed facts establishing it]
    Element 2: [State element] — [Cite undisputed facts establishing it]
    [Continue for all elements]

    Because the undisputed facts establish every element of [claim],
Plaintiff is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.

    B. Qualified Immunity Cannot Save Defendants at Summary Judgment

    At summary judgment, qualified immunity requires the court to
determine whether the facts, viewed favorably to the plaintiff, show a
violation of clearly established rights. Saucier v. Katz, 533 U.S. 194
(2001). Here, the undisputed record establishes [violation] and the right
was clearly established by [cite precedent].

IV. CONCLUSION

    There is no genuine dispute of material fact on [Counts ___].
Plaintiff is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.

    Dated: _______________

    ___________________________
    Andrew James Pigors, Pro Se
```
