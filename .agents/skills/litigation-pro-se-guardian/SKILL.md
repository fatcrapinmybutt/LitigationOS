## Procedural Safety Checklist

Before filing ANY document, run through this checklist:

```
PRE-FILING SAFETY CHECK
========================
□  1. DEADLINE:     Is this filing timely? What rule sets the deadline?
□  2. COURT:        Correct court and division? (Circuit, COA, Federal?)
□  3. CAPTION:      MCR 2.113 compliant? Case number correct?
□  4. FORMAT:       Meets local formatting rules? (See style-guide)
□  5. SIGNATURE:    Signed with name, address, phone, email? (MCR 2.114)
□  6. VERIFICATION: Does this filing require verification? (sworn statement)
□  7. CERTIFICATE:  Certificate of Service included and accurate?
□  8. SERVICE:      Served on ALL parties simultaneously with filing?
□  9. COPIES:       Correct number of copies? (Original + copies per court rule)
□ 10. FEE:          Filing fee paid or fee waiver on file?
□ 11. PROPOSED ORDER: Required for motions? Attached?
□ 12. EXHIBITS:     All referenced exhibits attached? Properly indexed?
□ 13. PROOF:        Kept personal copy with timestamp/confirmation?
```

## Michigan Pro Se Leniency Doctrine

### The Core Protection

Michigan courts hold pro se litigants to a "less stringent standard" than licensed attorneys, but this leniency has hard limits:

**Federal Foundation**: Haines v Kerner, 404 US 519 (1972) — pro se complaints are held to "less stringent standards than formal pleadings drafted by lawyers."

**Michigan Adoption**: Estelle v Gamble, 429 US 97 (1976) and progeny, adopted in Michigan practice. The Michigan Court of Appeals has repeatedly stated: "We review a pro se litigant's pleadings with leniency and endeavor to understand them."

### What Leniency Covers

| Protected (Leniency Applies) | NOT Protected (No Leniency) |
|---|---|
| Imperfect pleading format | Missing filing deadlines |
| Non-technical language | Failure to state a claim entirely |
| Minor procedural deviations | Ignoring court orders |
| Informal citation style | Discovery abuse or obstruction |
| Imprecise legal terminology | Frivolous filings (MCR 2.114(D)) |
| Organizational deficiencies | Failure to serve opposing party |
| Missing non-critical attachments | Failure to appear for hearings |

### The Hard Limits

Courts will NOT excuse:
1. **Jurisdictional deadlines** — claim of appeal timing (MCR 7.204), statute of limitations
2. **Failure to prosecute** — MCR 2.502; dismissal for no progress
3. **Contempt of court orders** — compliance is non-negotiable regardless of representation status
4. **Discovery obligations** — MCR 2.302 et seq. apply equally
5. **Ethical obligations** — MCR 2.114 signature requirements (no frivolous filings)

### Key Michigan Cases on Pro Se Leniency

- **Haines v Kerner**, 404 US 519 (1972) — foundational federal pro se protection
- **Erickson v Pardus**, 551 US 89 (2007) — reinforced Haines in federal context
- **Mich Ct App Standing Order** — COA will liberally construe pro se filings
- **Marceau v Int'l Bhd of Elec Workers**, 618 F2d 1198 (6th Cir 1980) — pro se leniency does not exempt from procedural rules
- **Jourdan v Jabe**, 951 F2d 108 (6th Cir 1991) — pro se litigants must follow basic procedural rules

## Filing Procedures

### E-Filing via MiFile

Michigan's mandatory electronic filing system (most circuit courts, including 14th Circuit):

**MiFile Portal**: https://mifile.courts.michigan.gov

```
E-FILING STEPS:
1. Create/login to MiFile account
2. Select court (14th Circuit — Muskegon County)
3. Select case number
4. Choose filing type (motion, response, etc.)
5. Upload document as PDF (text-searchable preferred)
6. Upload exhibits as separate PDFs (labeled)
7. Add Certificate of Service
8. Pay filing fee or select fee waiver
9. Submit — receive confirmation number and timestamp
10. SAVE confirmation email and screenshot submission
```

**MiFile Technical Requirements:**
- PDF format (not Word, not scanned images if avoidable)
- Maximum file size: 25 MB per document
- File naming: descriptive (e.g., "Motion_to_Modify_Custody_2025-03-15.pdf")
- Separate uploads for main document and each exhibit
- Text-searchable PDFs preferred (OCR scanned documents)

**Pro Se MiFile Issues:**
- System sometimes requires attorney bar number — leave blank or enter "PRO SE"
- If system rejects, call clerk's office for guidance
- Keep screenshots of every submission and confirmation
- Check filing status 24–48 hours after submission

### Paper Filing (Backup)

When MiFile fails or for courts not on MiFile:

1. Print original + required copies (typically original + 2)
2. Bring to clerk's office at 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, MI 49442
3. Have clerk stamp "FILED" with date on all copies
4. Keep one stamped copy for your records
5. Pay filing fee at window or present fee waiver order

**Clerk's Office Hours**: Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM (verify current hours)

### Service of Process Requirements

After filing, you **must** serve all other parties:

| Service Method | When Allowed | Proof Required |
|---|---|---|
| E-service via MiFile | When all parties are on MiFile | System generates proof |
| First-class mail | Default for most filings | Certificate of Service (in filing) |
| Personal delivery | Always allowed | Certificate of Service with details |
| Email | Only if parties have stipulated | Certificate + email confirmation |

**Certificate of Service Template:**

## Fee Waivers

### MC 20 — Motion and Affidavit to Waive Fees

If you cannot afford filing fees:

**Eligibility** (MCR 2.002): Show one of the following:
1. Currently receiving public assistance (TANF, SSI, Medicaid, Food Assistance)
2. Income below 125% of federal poverty guidelines
3. Other circumstances showing inability to pay

**Process:**
1. Complete SCAO Form MC 20 (Affidavit and Order — Suspension of Fees/Costs)
2. Complete MC 20a (Attachment — listing income, expenses, assets)
3. File with the court before or simultaneously with your first filing
4. Court reviews — may grant without hearing or schedule brief hearing
5. If granted, applies to filing fees, transcript fees, and some service costs
6. **Must be renewed** for each court level (circuit, COA, MSC — separate orders)

**Critical**: Fee waiver at circuit court does NOT automatically carry to COA. File a new MC 20 with the Court of Appeals.

### Fees to Know

| Filing | Fee (Approx.) | Waivable? |
|---|---|---|
| Circuit Court motion | $20 | Yes (MC 20) |
| Circuit Court complaint | $175 | Yes (MC 20) |
| COA Claim of Appeal | $375 | Yes (MC 20 at COA) |
| COA Application for Leave | $375 | Yes (MC 20 at COA) |
| MSC Application | $375 | Yes (MC 20 at MSC) |
| Transcript (per page) | $3.25–$4.50 | Partially (MC 20 may cover) |
| Federal Court complaint | $405 | Yes (IFP — 28 USC § 1915) |

## Common Pro Se Pitfalls

### The Deadly Seven

1. **Missing deadlines** — No excuse will save a missed jurisdictional deadline
2. **Failure to serve** — Filing without service = filing not effective
3. **Ex parte contact** — NEVER contact the judge directly outside of court
4. **Emotional filings** — Anger in writing undermines credibility permanently
5. **Over-filing** — Filing too many motions annoys courts; be strategic
6. **Ignoring orders** — Even wrong orders must be obeyed until reversed on appeal
7. **Waiving rights silently** — If you don't object on the record, you've waived it

### Court Clerk Interactions

**Clerks CAN:**
- Accept filings, provide forms, stamp copies
- Tell you filing fees and deadlines listed on court website
- Provide case register of actions
- Give you SCAO form numbers

**Clerks CANNOT:**
- Give legal advice (what to file, how to argue)
- Tell you whether your filing is "good enough"
- Recommend whether to appeal
- Interpret court orders for you

**Always be polite to clerks.** They control access to the system and can make your life easier or harder within their discretion.

1. File DISQUALIFICATION first — before any substantive motion
