# Filing Procedures — E-Filing, Paper Filing, and Service

Complete procedural reference for filing documents in all courts relevant to Pigors v Watson: 14th Judicial Circuit (Muskegon County), Michigan Court of Appeals, Michigan Supreme Court, and Western District of Michigan (Federal).

## I. E-Filing via MiFile

### System Overview

MiFile is Michigan's statewide electronic filing system, mandated for most circuit courts including the 14th Judicial Circuit (Muskegon County).

**URL**: https://mifile.courts.michigan.gov
**Support**: (517) 373-4835 or MiFileSupport@courts.mi.gov
**Hours**: 24/7 for filing; support staff available business hours

### Account Setup

1. Navigate to MiFile portal
2. Click "Register" — create account with email and password
3. Verify email address
4. Complete profile: name, address, phone, email
5. Select "Self-Represented Litigant" (not Attorney)
6. Accept terms of service
7. **Note**: No bar number required; system may have a field for it — leave blank or enter "PRO SE"

### Filing Steps — Detailed

```
STEP 1: LOG IN
- Navigate to MiFile portal
- Enter credentials
- Select court: "14th Circuit Court — Muskegon County"

STEP 2: FIND YOUR CASE
- Enter case number in search field
- Verify case name and parties match
- If new case, select "New Case Filing" instead

STEP 3: SELECT FILING TYPE
- Choose document category (Motion, Response, Brief, etc.)
- Select specific document type from dropdown
- If your document type isn't listed, use "Other" and describe

STEP 4: UPLOAD DOCUMENTS
- Main document: Upload as PDF
  • Naming: "Motion_to_Modify_Custody_20250315.pdf"
  • Format: Text-searchable PDF preferred
  • Size limit: 25 MB per file
- Exhibits: Upload each as separate PDF
  • Naming: "Exhibit_A_Custody_Order.pdf"
  • If multiple exhibits: upload each separately with labels
- Proposed Order: Upload separately if required

STEP 5: ADD SERVICE INFORMATION
- System may auto-populate parties for e-service
- For parties not on MiFile: note that you will serve by mail
- Complete Certificate of Service information

STEP 6: PAYMENT
- Enter payment method (credit/debit card)
- OR select "Fee Waiver" if MC 20 has been granted
- Some filings have no fee (responses, replies typically)

STEP 7: REVIEW AND SUBMIT
- Review all uploaded documents for completeness
- Verify file names and descriptions
- Click "Submit"

STEP 8: CONFIRM AND SAVE
- System generates confirmation number and timestamp
- SAVE the confirmation page (screenshot + print to PDF)
- You will receive email confirmation — SAVE this email
- Check filing status 24-48 hours later to confirm acceptance
```

### Common MiFile Issues for Pro Se Filers

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| System asks for bar number | Leave blank, enter "PRO SE," or enter "0000" |
| Document type not in dropdown | Select "Other" and describe in the text field |
| File too large (>25 MB) | Compress PDF; split into volumes; reduce image resolution |
| Filing rejected by clerk | Read rejection reason carefully; fix and re-file promptly |
| Can't find case number | Verify number format; contact clerk's office |
| E-service not reaching opponent | File certificate of service noting alternative service method |
| System down/unavailable | Document outage (screenshot); paper-file as backup; notify court |

### MiFile Filing Timestamps

- Filing is timestamped when submitted, not when accepted by clerk
- If submitted before 11:59 PM, it counts as filed that day
- Weekend/holiday submissions: filed as of submission time but processed next business day
- **Save your confirmation timestamp** — it's your proof of timely filing

## II. Paper Filing — Backup Procedures

### When to Paper-File

- MiFile system is down or inaccessible
- Court has not adopted mandatory e-filing for your filing type
- Judge has specifically requested paper copies (courtesy copies)
- You cannot resolve a MiFile technical issue before a deadline
- Initial filing in a new case (some courts require paper for the first filing)

### Paper Filing Steps

1. **Print documents**: Original + required copies
   - Circuit Court: Original + 2 copies (typically)
   - COA: Original + 4 copies (MCR 7.212(A))
   - MSC: Original + 11 copies (MCR 7.305)
   - Federal: Check LCivR — typically original only (CM/ECF is mandatory)

2. **Organize the filing**:
   - Main document on top
   - Exhibits in order behind main document
   - Proposed order (if required) — separate or attached per court preference
   - Certificate of Service — last page of main document or separate

3. **Visit clerk's office**:
   - Muskegon County: 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, MI 49442
   - Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
   - Bring all copies and the original

4. **At the counter**:
   - Present documents to the clerk
   - Request "FILED" stamp with date on all copies
   - Pay filing fee or present fee waiver order
   - Ask for one stamped copy back for your records

5. **After filing**:
   - Keep your stamped copy in a safe location
   - Note the filing in your personal docket
   - Proceed with service on all parties

### Courtesy Copies

Some judges request "courtesy copies" of e-filed documents — paper copies delivered directly to chambers:

- Ask the judge's scheduling clerk whether courtesy copies are expected
- Label courtesy copies: "COURTESY COPY — Filed via MiFile on [Date]"
- Deliver to chambers or place in judge's mailbox at clerk's office
- A courtesy copy is NOT a substitute for filing

## III. Federal Court Filing — CM/ECF (PACER)

### Western District of Michigan

**CM/ECF Portal**: https://ecf.miwd.uscourts.gov
**PACER (for viewing)**: https://pacer.uscourts.gov
**Clerk's Office**: 410 W. Michigan Ave., Kalamazoo, MI 49007 (or Grand Rapids division)
**Phone**: (269) 337-5650 (Kalamazoo); (616) 456-2381 (Grand Rapids)

### Pro Se E-Filing in Federal Court

As of recent changes, most federal courts allow (and some require) pro se e-filing via CM/ECF:

1. **Request CM/ECF access**: Contact the clerk's office; request pro se e-filing credentials
2. **Complete training**: Some courts require a brief training session or video
3. **Receive login**: Username and password for the CM/ECF system
4. **File electronically**: Similar to MiFile but different interface

**If CM/ECF access is not available:**
- Paper-file at the clerk's office
- Clerk will scan and enter into the electronic docket
- Service still required on all parties

### Federal Filing Differences from State

| Element | Michigan State | Federal (W.D. Mich) |
|---|---|---|
| Filing system | MiFile | CM/ECF |
| Viewing system | MiFile | PACER ($0.10/page, cap per document) |
| Brief page limit | Varies (50 pages COA) | 25 pages (LCivR 7.1(d)(2)) |
| Motion practice | File motion + brief together | File motion + brief + proposed order |
| Response time | 14-21 days typical | 14 days (LCivR 7.1(d)(1)) |
| Reply time | Varies | 7 days (LCivR 7.1(d)(1)) |
| IFP application | MC 20 (state form) | 28 USC § 1915 + AO 240 form |

## IV. Service of Process — Detailed Requirements

### Initial Service (Complaint/Summons)

For new cases, the original complaint must be served under MCR 2.105:

| Method | Description | Who Can Serve | Proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| Personal service | Hand-delivery to defendant | Any adult (not a party) | Affidavit of Service |
| Substituted service | Delivery to person of suitable age at defendant's dwelling | Any adult (not a party) | Affidavit of Service |
| Registered/certified mail | Mailed with return receipt | Plaintiff may mail | Return receipt + Affidavit |
| Publication | For defendants who cannot be found | Plaintiff files motion | Proof of Publication |

### Subsequent Service (Motions, Briefs, etc.)

After initial service, subsequent filings are served under MCR 2.107:

| Method | When Allowed | Proof |
|---|---|---|
| MiFile e-service | Both parties on MiFile | Automatic system record |
| First-class mail | Default method | Certificate of Service in filing |
| Personal delivery | Always allowed | Certificate of Service |
| Email | Only if stipulated or ordered | Certificate of Service + email record |
| Fax | If stipulated | Certificate of Service + fax confirmation |

### Service Timing Rules

| Filing Type | Service Deadline | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Motions | At least 9 days before hearing (14 days for mail) | MCR 2.119(C) |
| Responses | At least 5 days before hearing (mail: 9 days) | MCR 2.119(C)(2) |
| Replies | At least 3 days before hearing (mail: 7 days) | MCR 2.119(C)(2) |
| Appellate briefs | Same day as filing | MCR 7.212(A) |
| Discovery requests | Within discovery period | MCR 2.301 |

### Computing Service Deadlines

Under MCR 1.108:
- Count from the day after service, not the day of service
- Exclude the last day if it falls on a weekend or holiday
- Add 3 days when service is by mail (MCR 2.107(C)(3))
- "Days" means calendar days unless "business days" is specified

## V. Fee Waiver Procedures — Detailed

### MC 20 — Affidavit and Order: Suspension of Fees and Costs

**SCAO Form Number**: MC 20
**Available at**: courts.michigan.gov/scao-forms or court clerk's office

### Completing MC 20

**Section 1 — Case Information:**
- Case number, court name, party names
- Check the box for the fees you're requesting waiver of

**Section 2 — Basis for Request:**
Check all that apply:
- □ Currently receiving public assistance (TANF, SSI, Medicaid, Food Assistance)
- □ Income below 125% of federal poverty guidelines
- □ Unable to pay fees due to other circumstances (explain)

### MC 20a — Attachment (Financial Details)

**Section 1 — Monthly Income:**
- List all sources: employment, self-employment, government benefits, other
- Include household income if relevant to the analysis

**Section 2 — Monthly Expenses:**
- Rent/mortgage, utilities, food, transportation, medical, childcare, insurance, debt payments

**Section 3 — Assets:**
- Bank accounts, vehicles, real property, investments
- Note: Having a modest bank balance or owning a car does not disqualify you

**Section 4 — Explanation:**
- Explain why you cannot pay fees
- Be specific: "After paying rent ($XXX), utilities ($XXX), food ($XXX), and childcare ($XXX), I have $XX remaining monthly income, which is insufficient to pay the $375 COA filing fee."

### Filing the Fee Waiver

1. Complete MC 20 and MC 20a
2. File with the court BEFORE or SIMULTANEOUSLY with your first fee-generating filing
3. If filing at COA or MSC, file a NEW MC 20 at that court level
4. Court reviews — may grant without hearing or schedule a brief hearing
5. If granted, order covers: filing fees, transcript costs (usually), service costs (sometimes)
6. If denied, you must pay fees or file a motion for reconsideration

### Federal IFP (In Forma Pauperis)

For federal court (W.D. Michigan) under 28 USC § 1915:

1. Complete AO 240 (Application to Proceed in District Court Without Prepaying Fees)
2. Attach financial affidavit with income, expenses, and asset information
3. File with the federal complaint or first appearance
4. Magistrate judge reviews; may grant conditionally
5. **Note**: Even with IFP, the $405 filing fee may be assessed in installments from any income
6. **Prisoner exception**: Additional screening under § 1915(e) — does not apply to non-prisoner pro se
