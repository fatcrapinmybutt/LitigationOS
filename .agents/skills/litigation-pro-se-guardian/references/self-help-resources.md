### Court Facilitator's Office — Muskegon County

**What Court Facilitators Do:**
Court facilitators assist self-represented litigants in domestic relations matters (custody, parenting time, child support, divorce).

**Location**: Muskegon County Circuit Court, 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, MI 49442
**Phone**: (231) 724-6241 (main clerk, ask for facilitator's office)
**Hours**: By appointment; walk-ins may be available

**Facilitators CAN:**
- Provide court-approved forms for domestic relations matters
- Explain general court procedures and filing steps
- Help fill out standard forms (entry of information, not legal strategy)
- Provide information about mediation services
- Explain Friend of Court processes
- Direct you to additional resources

**Facilitators CANNOT:**
- Provide legal advice (what to file, whether to appeal, what arguments to make)
- Recommend strategic decisions
- Review your filings for legal sufficiency
- Represent you or advocate for you
- Tell you whether your case has merit
- Contact the judge on your behalf

## II. Legal Aid Organizations

### Michigan Legal Help

**Website**: https://michiganlegalhelp.org
**Coverage**: Statewide

**Services:**
- Guided interviews that generate completed court forms (automated document assembly)
- Legal information organized by topic (family law, housing, consumer, employment)
- Self-help toolkits for common legal issues
- Court directory with addresses, phone numbers, and local rules
- Resource finder connecting users to local legal aid offices

**Key Toolkits Relevant to Pigors v Watson:**
1. **Custody and Parenting Time**: Forms, guides, and information about Michigan custody law
2. **Landlord-Tenant Issues**: Security deposit disputes, eviction defense, habitability
3. **Consumer Protection**: Michigan Consumer Protection Act claims
4. **Appeals**: How to appeal a circuit court decision

### Legal Aid of Western Michigan (LAWM)

**Website**: https://www.legalaidwestmich.net
**Phone**: (888) 783-8190
**Muskegon Office**: 104 W. 4th Street, Suite 200, Muskegon, MI 49440
**Phone (Muskegon)**: (231) 726-4831

**Services:**
- Free legal representation for eligible individuals (income-qualified)
- Legal advice clinics (brief consultations)
- Self-help workshops on common legal issues
- Referrals to other resources

**Eligibility**: Generally 125% of federal poverty guidelines or below; some programs have higher thresholds. Apply regardless — they may have special programs.

**Priority Areas**: Family law (custody, divorce), housing (eviction, conditions), consumer, public benefits

### Michigan State Bar Lawyer Referral Service

**Phone**: (800) 968-0738
**Website**: https://www.michbar.org/public/lawyerreferral

**Services:**
- Referral to attorneys by practice area and location
- Initial consultation (fee varies, some offer free initial consult)
- Can find attorneys willing to take limited-scope representation (help with one motion or hearing)

### Limited Scope Representation (Unbundled Legal Services)

Michigan allows attorneys to provide limited scope representation under MRPC 1.2(b):

- An attorney can help with one specific task (draft a brief, appear at one hearing) without taking on the entire case
- Useful for complex motions or critical hearings where pro se assistance isn't enough
- Ask the State Bar referral service specifically about "limited scope" or "unbundled" services
- Cost: Typically $500–$2,000 for a single motion or hearing appearance

## V. Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)

### Mediation Services — Muskegon County

The 14th Circuit Court maintains a mediation program:

**Community Dispute Resolution Center:**
- Provides mediation for custody and parenting time disputes
- Low-cost or sliding-scale fees
- Court may order mediation in custody cases (MCR 3.216)
- Voluntary settlement through mediation is binding if reduced to a court order

### FOC Mediation

The Friend of Court offers mediation services for custody and parenting time:
- Included as part of FOC services (no additional fee)
- Mediators are trained FOC staff
- Agreements reached in FOC mediation are submitted to the court as recommendations

## VIII. Emergency Resources

### After-Hours Filing

If a deadline falls after business hours:
- **MiFile**: Available 24/7 — file electronically
- **Federal CM/ECF**: Available 24/7
- **Paper filing**: Not available after hours; plan ahead
- **Emergency motions**: Call the court's emergency number (available through the clerk's recording or judge's chambers)
