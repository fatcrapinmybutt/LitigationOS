# Appellate Brief Requirements — litigation-appellate-supreme

## Michigan Court of Appeals (COA) — MCR 7.212

### Brief Format Requirements

**Page Limits (MCR 7.212(B))**:
| Brief Type | Page Limit |
|------------|-----------|
| Appellant's brief | 50 pages |
| Appellee's brief | 50 pages |
| Reply brief | 25 pages |
| Cross-appeal brief | 50 pages |

Page limits EXCLUDE: table of contents, table of authorities, signature block,
certificate of service, and appendix.

**Margins and Font**:
- Margins: 1 inch on all sides
- Font: 12-point proportional or 10-point monospaced
- Double-spaced text (single-space permitted for block quotes and footnotes)
- Pages numbered at bottom center

### Required Brief Components (MCR 7.212(D))

Every appellant's brief MUST contain, in this order:

1. **Table of Contents** — with page references for each section
2. **Index of Authorities** — alphabetical list of cases, statutes, rules, other authorities
3. **Statement of Jurisdiction** — basis for COA jurisdiction, key dates
4. **Statement of Questions Presented** — each question on a separate page
   - Format: "Whether [specific ruling/action] constitutes [specific error] requiring [specific relief]"
   - Must fairly present the issues — not argumentative
   - Answer "yes" or "no" below each question with one-sentence explanation
5. **Statement of Facts** — fair and accurate, with record citations
   - Must be supported by the record (transcript pages, exhibit numbers)
   - Must include facts favorable to BOTH parties
   - Must NOT be argumentative
6. **Argument** — organized by issue, with headings matching questions presented
   - Each section begins with the standard of review for that issue
   - Michigan authority cited before out-of-state authority
   - Binding authority addressed first (Michigan Supreme Court → published COA)
7. **Relief Requested** — specific relief sought (reverse, remand, modify, etc.)
8. **Signature Block** — name, address, phone, bar number (or "Pro Se")

### Appendix Requirements (MCR 7.212(C))

The appendix must contain:
- [ ] Register of actions from the lower court
- [ ] Opinion or order being appealed (with date)
- [ ] Any other orders relevant to the issues on appeal
- [ ] Relevant portions of the transcript (NOT the entire transcript)
- [ ] Relevant exhibits from the lower court record
- [ ] Relevant statutory and rule provisions

The appendix must NOT contain:
- Entire trial transcript (only relevant portions)
- Documents not part of the lower court record
- New evidence not presented below

## Michigan Supreme Court (MSC) — MCR 7.305

### Application for Leave to Appeal

**Page Limit**: 50 pages (MCR 7.305(A))

**Filing Deadline**: 42 days from COA decision (MCR 7.305(C)(2))
- This deadline is JURISDICTIONAL — no extensions

**Required Components (MCR 7.305(B))**:
1. Statement of questions presented
2. Summary of the basis for jurisdiction
3. Statement of facts (with record citations)
4. Argument — why the Supreme Court should grant leave
5. Relief requested
6. Appendix containing: COA opinion, trial court opinion/order, relevant pleadings

**Grounds for Granting Leave**:
The MSC has discretionary review. Arguments for granting leave should address:
- Conflict with other COA panels or MSC precedent
- Issue of significant public interest
- Need to clarify an area of law
- Substantial question about the validity of a legislative act

## Claim of Appeal Filing Requirements (MCR 7.204)

### Deadlines (JURISDICTIONAL)
| Filing | Deadline | Authority |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Claim of appeal (final order) | 21 days | MCR 7.204(A)(1) |
| Claim of appeal (interlocutory by right) | 21 days | MCR 7.204(A)(2) |
| Application for leave (interlocutory) | 21 days | MCR 7.205(A) |
| Cross-appeal | 21 days after claim of appeal served | MCR 7.207(A) |
| Transcript order | 14 days after claim filed | MCR 7.210(B)(1) |
| Appellant's brief | 56 days after claim (or transcript filed) | MCR 7.212(A)(1) |
| Appellee's brief | 35 days after appellant's brief | MCR 7.212(A)(2) |
| Reply brief | 21 days after appellee's brief | MCR 7.212(A)(3) |

### Required Filing Components
- [ ] Claim of appeal form (completed)
- [ ] Filing fee ($375 as of 2025; fee waiver if applicable)
- [ ] Copy of judgment or order being appealed
- [ ] Certificate of service on all parties
- [ ] Docket statement
- [ ] Transcript order filed with court reporter

## TrueFiling Requirements (COA/MSC E-Filing)

- **Format**: PDF only (text-searchable / OCR)
- **File size**: Maximum 25 MB per document
- **Bookmarks**: Required for documents exceeding 20 pages
- **Separate PDFs**: Brief, appendix, and motion must be separate files
- **Naming convention**: [Party]-[Document Type]-[Date] (e.g., "Appellant-Brief-2025-06-15")
- **Signatures**: "/s/ Andrew James Pigors" accepted for electronic filing
- **Service**: TrueFiling can serve registered users electronically; unregistered
  parties must be served by traditional means and proof filed separately

## Common Appellate Brief Errors (Avoid These)

1. **Argumentative statement of facts** — must be neutral and record-based
2. **Missing standard of review** — each argument section must state the standard
3. **Failing to address adverse authority** — MRPC 3.3(a)(2) requires disclosure
4. **Raising issues not preserved below** — limited to plain error review
5. **Exceeding page limits** — brief will be rejected by TrueFiling
6. **Missing appendix items** — register of actions frequently omitted
7. **Citing unpublished opinions without notation** — must note as "unpublished"
   per MCR 7.215(C)(1) and include copy in appendix
8. **Wrong court rules applied** — using MCR 2.xxx instead of MCR 7.xxx
