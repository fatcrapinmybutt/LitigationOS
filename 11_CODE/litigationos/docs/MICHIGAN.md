## 1. Michigan Court Structure

Michigan's court system has five levels, from highest to lowest:

### Michigan Supreme Court

- **Justices:** 7, elected to 8-year terms
- **Jurisdiction:** Discretionary review of Court of Appeals decisions; original jurisdiction for mandamus, habeas corpus, superintending control
- **Key:** Governed by MCR 7.301-7.306
- **Address:** 925 W. Ottawa St, Lansing, MI 48915
- **Phone:** (517) 373-0120
- **Website:** https://courts.michigan.gov/courts/supremecourt

### Michigan Court of Appeals (COA)

- **Judges:** 25, sitting in panels of 3
- **Jurisdiction:** Appeals from Circuit Court, administrative agencies
- **Districts:** 4 districts covering all 83 Michigan counties
- **Key:** Governed by MCR 7.201-7.219
- **Address (Detroit):** Cadillac Place, 3020 W. Grand Blvd, Detroit, MI 48202
- **Phone:** (313) 972-5700
- **Website:** https://courts.michigan.gov/courts/coa

### Circuit Court

- **Jurisdiction:** General trial court with the broadest jurisdiction
  - Civil cases over ,000
  - All felony criminal cases
  - Family division (divorce, custody, parenting time, child support, PPOs)
  - Appeals from District Court and administrative agencies
- **Circuits:** 57 circuits covering all 83 counties
- **Key divisions:**
  - **Family Division** -- Divorce, custody, support (MCR 3.201-3.219)
  - **Civil Division** -- Torts, contracts, property disputes
  - **Criminal Division** -- Felony cases
- **Common rules:** MCR 2.101-2.621 (general civil procedure)

### District Court

- **Jurisdiction:**
  - Civil cases up to ,000
  - Misdemeanor criminal cases (up to 1 year)
  - Landlord-tenant disputes
  - Small claims (up to ,500)
  - Traffic offenses
- **Courts:** 105 district courts across Michigan
- **Key:** Small claims division governed by MCR 4.301-4.306

### Probate Court

- **Jurisdiction:**
  - Wills, estates, and trusts
  - Guardianships and conservatorships
  - Mental health commitments
  - Juvenile matters (in some counties)
- **Courts:** One per county (83 total)
- **Key:** Governed by MCR 5.101-5.991

## 2. Key Court Rules (MCR)

### MCR 2.105 -- Summons; Expiration, Reissuance

- Summons expires **91 days** after the complaint is filed
- May be reissued or replaced on motion and payment of fee
- Reissued summons expires 91 days after reissuance

### MCR 2.107 -- Service and Filing of Pleadings

- Every pleading after the original complaint must be served on each party
- Service methods: personal delivery, mail (to last known address), or electronic service
- Proof of service required for all filed documents

### MCR 2.108 -- Time to Answer or Respond

- Defendant must answer or respond within **21 days** after service of summons + complaint
- Different time may be prescribed by specific statute or court rule

### MCR 2.119 -- Motion Practice

| Action | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| Serve motion + brief + notice | At least **9 days** before hearing |
| Serve response + brief | At least **5 days** before hearing |
| Serve reply brief | At least **3 days** before hearing |

- Written motions must include a brief or affidavit
- Notice of hearing required (unless ex parte motion)

### MCR 3.206 -- Initiating Domestic Relations Actions

- Requires filing complaint **and** verified statement
- Serve summons + complaint + verified statement per MCR 2.105
- **Waiting periods:**
  - No minor children: **60 days** before judgment of divorce
  - Minor children involved: **6 months (180 days)** before judgment of divorce

### MCR 3.310 -- Parenting Time and Conciliation

- Parenting time disputes must be submitted to FOC for conciliation/mediation
- Modification requires proper cause or change of circumstances
- MCR 3.310 governs parenting time enforcement procedures

### MCR 7.212 -- Briefs in Court of Appeals

| Brief | Deadline | Page Limit |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Appellant's brief | **56 days** after claim of appeal filed | 50 pages |
| Appellee's brief | **35 days** after service of appellant's brief | 50 pages |
| Reply brief | **21 days** after service of appellee's brief | -- |

**Format requirements:**
- Font: Times New Roman 12pt or similar proportional font
- Margins: 1 inch on all sides
- Spacing: Double-spaced
- Must include table of contents and table of authorities

### MCR 7.302 -- Application for Leave to Appeal (Supreme Court)

- File within **42 days** of Court of Appeals decision
- Must include: questions presented, concise statement of facts, argument

### MCR 7.303 -- Cross-Application for Leave to Appeal

- File within **21 days** after application is served on cross-applicant

### MCR 7.304 -- Responses and Replies (Supreme Court)

- Response to application: **28 days** after service
- Reply to response: **14 days** after service

### MCR 7.305 -- Application for Leave to Appeal; Bypass

- File within **56 days** of order/judgment being appealed
- Must demonstrate significant public question or material injustice from delay

## 3. Key Statutes (MCL)

### MCL 722.23 -- Best Interest of the Child Factors

The "best interest of the child" factors for custody determinations:

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| (a) | Love, affection, and emotional ties between the child and each parent |
| (b) | Capacity and disposition to give love, affection, and guidance |
| (c) | Capacity and disposition to provide food, clothing, and medical care |
| (d) | Length of time the child has lived in a stable, satisfactory environment |
| (e) | Permanence as a family unit of the existing or proposed custodial home |
| (f) | Moral fitness of the parties |
| (g) | Mental and physical health of the parties |
| (h) | Home, school, and community record of the child |
| (i) | Reasonable preference of the child (if of sufficient age) |
| (j) | Willingness and ability to facilitate a close relationship with the other parent |
| (k) | Domestic violence |
| (l) | Any other relevant factor |

### MCL 722.27a -- Parenting Time

- Each parent is entitled to reasonable parenting time unless it would endanger the child's well-being
- Parenting time disputes must be submitted to FOC for conciliation/mediation
- Modification requires **proper cause** or **change of circumstances**

### MCL 600.2950 -- Personal Protection Orders (PPO)

- Types of PPOs:
  - **Domestic relationship PPO** -- Between spouses, former spouses, household members, persons with children in common, or dating relationship
  - **Non-domestic (stalking) PPO** -- Against stalking behavior regardless of relationship
- Standard: Petitioner must show reasonable cause to believe respondent may commit listed prohibited acts
- **Duration:** Effective immediately upon entry; typically valid for up to 182 days (renewable)
- **Enforcement:** Violation is a criminal offense (contempt, misdemeanor, or felony depending on conduct)

## 5. E-Filing Systems

### MiFILE

- **URL:** https://mifile.courts.michigan.gov
- **Coverage:** All Michigan trial courts (Circuit, District, Probate)
- **Accepts:** PDF documents up to 25 MB per filing
- **Fee payment:** Integrated online payment
- **Hours:** Available 24/7 (filings timestamped when submitted)
- **Registration:** Free account required; register with email and create a password
- **Note:** E-filing is **mandatory** in most Michigan courts as of 2024

### TrueFiling

- **URL:** https://www.truefiling.com
- **Coverage:** Michigan Court of Appeals and Michigan Supreme Court
- **Accepts:** PDF documents
- **Fee payment:** Credit card, debit card, or trust account
- **Registration:** Separate account from MiFILE
- **Note:** Required for all appellate court filings

### Filing Tips for Pro Se Litigants

1. **PDF format:** Convert all documents to PDF before uploading
2. **File naming:** Use descriptive names (e.g., Motion_to_Compel_2025-03-15.pdf)
3. **Page limits:** Ensure documents meet MCR page limits before filing
4. **Proof of service:** Must be filed separately alongside the main document
5. **Fees:** Fee waiver requests can be filed simultaneously if applicable
6. **Confirmation:** Save the e-filing confirmation receipt for your records
7. **Service:** E-filing on MiFILE serves registered parties automatically; non-registered parties must be served separately

## 7. Complaint Filing Procedures

This section covers filing complaints with Michigan regulatory agencies and federal bodies.

### 7.1 Michigan Department of Civil Rights (MDCR)

**Purpose:** Discrimination complaints based on race, color, religion, national origin, age, sex, height, weight, familial status, marital status, disability, or arrest record.

**Filing:**
- **Online:** https://www.michigan.gov/mdcr
- **Mail:** Michigan Department of Civil Rights, Capitol Tower Building, 110 W. Michigan Ave, Suite 800, Lansing, MI 48933
- **Phone:** (517) 335-3165 or (800) 482-3604
- **Deadline:** File within **180 days** of the discriminatory act
- **Required:** Description of discrimination, respondent identification, dates, witnesses

### 7.2 Michigan Attorney General (AG)

**Purpose:** Consumer protection complaints, fraud, unfair business practices, and certain public interest matters.

**Filing:**
- **Online:** https://www.michigan.gov/ag (Consumer Complaint Form)
- **Mail:** Michigan Attorney General, Consumer Protection Division, P.O. Box 30213, Lansing, MI 48909
- **Phone:** (877) 765-8388
- **Required:** Description of the problem, copies of relevant documents, respondent business information, amount of loss

### 7.3 Judicial Tenure Commission (JTC)

**Purpose:** Complaints about judicial misconduct, disability, or conduct that is clearly prejudicial to the administration of justice.

**Filing:**
- **Mail:** Judicial Tenure Commission, 3034 W. Grand Blvd, Suite 8-450, Detroit, MI 48202
- **Phone:** (313) 875-5110
- **Website:** https://jtc.courts.michigan.gov
- **Required:** Written description of the judge's conduct, case number, dates, names of witnesses
- **Note:** The JTC investigates complaints confidentially. They do not handle appeals of court decisions.

### 7.4 HIPAA / HHS Office for Civil Rights

**Purpose:** Health information privacy violations under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act.

**Filing:**
- **Online:** https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/filing-a-complaint/index.html (OCR Complaint Portal)
- **Mail:** U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights, 200 Independence Ave SW, Washington, DC 20201
- **Phone:** (800) 368-1019
- **Deadline:** File within **180 days** of the violation (extensions possible for good cause)
- **Required:** Name of entity that violated HIPAA, description of violation, dates, any documentation

### 7.5 Michigan State Bar (Attorney Grievance Commission)

**Purpose:** Complaints about attorney misconduct or incompetence.

**Filing:**
- **Online:** https://www.agcmi.com (Request for Investigation form)
- **Mail:** Attorney Grievance Commission, 535 Griswold, Suite 1700, Detroit, MI 48226
- **Phone:** (313) 961-6585
- **Required:** Attorney name and bar number, description of misconduct, supporting documentation

### 7.6 Friend of the Court (FOC)

**Purpose:** Enforcement of support orders, parenting time orders, and custody provisions.

**Filing:**
- **In person:** At the FOC office in the county where the case is filed
- **Forms:** FOC 100 (Parenting Time Motion), FOC 89 (Support Motion), FOC 115 (Custody Motion)
- **Required:** Motion form, current order, description of violation or change in circumstances
- **Process:** FOC reviews complaint, may schedule conciliation/mediation, may recommend enforcement

## 8. ICS Calendar Integration

LitigationOS can export court deadlines as standard ICS (iCalendar) files compatible with all major calendar applications.

### How It Works

1. Open the **Calendar View** in LitigationOS
2. Click **Export ICS**
3. The system generates an .ics file containing:
   - VCALENDAR wrapper with LitigationOS product identifier
   - VEVENT blocks for each deadline
   - ISO 8601 dates in YYYYMMDD format
   - Priority levels mapped from urgency (Critical/High/Medium/Low)
   - Deadline descriptions and associated case information

### Importing ICS Files

| Calendar App | Import Method |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Google Calendar** | Settings --> Import & Export --> Import |
| **Microsoft Outlook** | File --> Open & Export --> Import/Export |
| **Apple Calendar** | File --> Import --> select .ics file |
| **Thunderbird** | Import from the Events and Tasks menu |

### Automatic Updates

Export a new ICS file after adding or modifying deadlines to keep your external calendar in sync.

## 9. E-Filing Workflow

### 9.1 Trial Court Filing via MiFILE

**Step-by-step workflow:**

1. **Prepare documents** in LitigationOS:
   - Build filing stack with all required components
   - Run compliance validation (score >= 80)
   - Run pre-filing QA (must receive GO decision)
   - Export stack as PDF documents

2. **Log into MiFILE** at https://mifile.courts.michigan.gov

3. **Create or select your case:**
   - For new cases: select court, case type, enter party information
   - For existing cases: search by case number

4. **Upload documents:**
   - Main filing document (motion, brief, complaint, etc.)
   - Exhibits as separate PDFs
   - Proof of service (MC 11 or equivalent)
   - Proposed order (if applicable)

5. **Pay filing fee** (or submit fee waiver MC 20)

6. **Review and submit:**
   - Verify all documents are attached
   - Confirm case and party information
   - Submit filing
   - Save confirmation receipt

7. **Service:**
   - MiFILE automatically serves registered parties
   - Manually serve non-registered parties per MCR 2.107

### 9.2 Appellate Filing via TrueFiling

**Step-by-step workflow:**

1. **Prepare appellate documents** in LitigationOS:
   - Build brief with MCR 7.212 compliance (50-page limit, TOC, TOA)
   - Run brief compliance checker
   - Export as PDF

2. **Log into TrueFiling** at https://www.truefiling.com

3. **Select the appellate court:**
   - Michigan Court of Appeals
   - Michigan Supreme Court

4. **Create or select your case:**
   - Enter COA/MSC case number
   - Verify party information

5. **Upload documents:**
   - Appellant's/Appellee's brief
   - Appendix (if applicable)
   - Supporting motions

6. **Pay filing fee** ( for appeals,  for motions)

7. **Submit and verify:**
   - Review uploaded documents
   - Submit filing
   - Save TrueFiling confirmation and receipt number
