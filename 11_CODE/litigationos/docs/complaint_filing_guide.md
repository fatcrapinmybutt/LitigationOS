## 1. Michigan Department of Civil Rights (MDCR)

### What MDCR Handles

Discrimination complaints based on:
- Race, color, national origin
- Religion or creed
- Age (employment)
- Sex, gender identity, sexual orientation
- Height, weight
- Familial status, marital status
- Disability
- Arrest record (employment)

### Step-by-Step Filing

1. **Gather information:**
   - Your contact information
   - Respondent's name, address, and phone number
   - Dates of the discriminatory act(s)
   - Detailed description of what happened
   - Names and contact info of witnesses
   - Any supporting documentation

2. **Choose filing method:**
   - **Online:** https://www.michigan.gov/mdcr
   - **Mail:** MDCR, Capitol Tower Building, 110 W. Michigan Ave, Suite 800, Lansing, MI 48933
   - **Phone intake:** (517) 335-3165 or toll-free (800) 482-3604
   - **In person:** Visit a regional MDCR office

3. **Complete the complaint form:**
   - Identify yourself as the complainant
   - Identify the respondent (person/organization that discriminated)
   - Select the basis of discrimination
   - Describe the discriminatory acts in detail
   - Provide dates and locations
   - List witnesses

4. **Submit supporting evidence:**
   - Copies of relevant communications
   - Employment records (if employment discrimination)
   - Photos, screenshots, or recordings
   - Any written policies or rules that were violated

5. **Deadline:** File within **180 days** of the most recent discriminatory act

6. **After filing:**
   - MDCR reviews the complaint for jurisdiction
   - Investigation may include interviews, document requests, site visits
   - Possible outcomes: mediation, finding of probable cause, dismissal
   - If probable cause found, may proceed to public hearing

In LitigationOS, use the **complaint** filing type with the following stack:

| Main document | MDCR complaint narrative |
| Exhibit A | Supporting communications/documents |
| Exhibit B | Witness list with contact information |
| Exhibit C | Timeline of discriminatory acts |
| Cover letter | Letter to MDCR with case summary |

## 2. Michigan Attorney General (AG)

### What the AG Handles

- Consumer protection complaints
- Fraud and deceptive business practices
- Utility complaints
- Identity theft
- Charitable trust violations
- Environmental violations
- Antitrust matters

### Step-by-Step Filing

1. **Gather information:**
   - Your contact information
   - Business/company name, address, phone, website
   - Description of the problem
   - Dates of transactions or events
   - Dollar amount of loss or damage
   - Copies of contracts, receipts, correspondence

2. **Choose filing method:**
   - **Online:** https://www.michigan.gov/ag (Consumer Complaint Form)
   - **Mail:** Michigan Attorney General, Consumer Protection Division, P.O. Box 30213, Lansing, MI 48909
   - **Phone:** (877) 765-8388

3. **Complete the complaint:**
   - Describe the transaction or interaction
   - Explain what the business did wrong
   - State what resolution you seek
   - Attach all supporting documents

4. **Submit supporting evidence:**
   - Copies of contracts and agreements
   - Receipts and invoices
   - Email/letter correspondence
   - Photos of defective products or services
   - Bank/credit card statements showing charges

5. **After filing:**
   - AG office reviews complaint
   - May contact the business on your behalf
   - May investigate for pattern of violations
   - Cannot provide legal representation for individuals
   - May refer you to other agencies or resources

### LitigationOS Filing Stack

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Main document | AG consumer complaint narrative |
| Exhibit A | Contract/agreement copies |
| Exhibit B | Correspondence with business |
| Exhibit C | Financial records (receipts, statements) |
| Exhibit D | Photos/evidence of harm |

## 3. Judicial Tenure Commission (JTC)

### What the JTC Handles

- Judicial misconduct
- Persistent failure to perform judicial duties
- Conduct clearly prejudicial to the administration of justice
- Habitual intemperance
- Disability that prevents performance of duties

### What the JTC Does NOT Handle

- Appeals of court decisions (use the appellate process)
- Disagreement with a judge's ruling (file an appeal)
- Complaints about court staff (contact the court administrator)
- Requests for legal advice

### Step-by-Step Filing

1. **Gather information:**
   - Judge's full name and court
   - Case number(s) involved
   - Dates of the judicial conduct at issue
   - Detailed description of the misconduct
   - Names of witnesses
   - Copies of court orders or transcripts showing the conduct

2. **Prepare the complaint:**
   - Write a clear, factual narrative of the judge's conduct
   - Focus on specific actions, not disagreement with rulings
   - Include dates, times, and locations
   - Reference any court rules or ethical canons violated

3. **Submit:**
   - **Mail:** Judicial Tenure Commission, 3034 W. Grand Blvd, Suite 8-450, Detroit, MI 48202
   - **Phone:** (313) 875-5110
   - **Website:** https://jtc.courts.michigan.gov

4. **Required documents:**
   - Written complaint (letter or form)
   - Copies of relevant court orders
   - Transcripts of proceedings (if available)
   - Any evidence of the misconduct

5. **After filing:**
   - JTC staff reviews the complaint
   - Investigation is confidential
   - JTC may dismiss, investigate further, or file a formal complaint
   - Formal complaints may lead to public proceedings before the Michigan Supreme Court
   - Possible sanctions: private censure, public censure, suspension, removal

## 4. HIPAA / HHS Office for Civil Rights

### What OCR Handles

Health information privacy violations under HIPAA, including:
- Unauthorized disclosure of protected health information (PHI)
- Failure to provide access to medical records
- Failure to implement required safeguards
- Retaliation for filing a HIPAA complaint
- Failure to provide notice of privacy practices

### Step-by-Step Filing

1. **Gather information:**
   - Name and address of the covered entity (hospital, doctor, insurance company, etc.)
   - Description of the privacy violation
   - Date(s) of the violation
   - How you learned of the violation
   - Any communications with the entity about the violation

2. **Choose filing method:**
   - **Online (preferred):** https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/filing-a-complaint/index.html (OCR Complaint Portal)
   - **Mail:** U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights, 200 Independence Ave SW, Room 509F HHH Building, Washington, DC 20201
   - **Phone:** (800) 368-1019; TDD: (800) 537-7697
   - **Regional office:** Contact the HHS regional office for your area

3. **Complete the complaint:**
   - Identify the covered entity
   - Describe specifically what privacy rule was violated
   - Provide dates and details
   - Explain how you were harmed

4. **Deadline:** File within **180 days** of the violation (or when you became aware)
   - Extensions may be granted for good cause

5. **After filing:**
   - OCR reviews complaint for jurisdiction and timeliness
   - May investigate the covered entity
   - May resolve through voluntary compliance or corrective action
   - Can impose civil monetary penalties for violations
   - Does not provide compensation to individuals (file civil suit separately)

### LitigationOS Filing Stack

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Main document | HIPAA complaint narrative |
| Exhibit A | Medical records release/denial documentation |
| Exhibit B | Privacy notice from the covered entity |
| Exhibit C | Correspondence about the violation |
| Exhibit D | Evidence of unauthorized disclosure |

## 5. Michigan State Bar (Attorney Grievance Commission)

### What AGC Handles

- Attorney misconduct or ethical violations
- Incompetent legal representation
- Mishandling of client funds
- Conflicts of interest
- Failure to communicate with clients
- Abandonment of cases
- Dishonesty or fraud by attorneys

### Step-by-Step Filing

1. **Gather information:**
   - Attorney's full name and P-number (bar number)
   - Law firm name and address
   - Your relationship (client, opposing party, etc.)
   - Dates of the misconduct
   - Description of the attorney's conduct
   - Supporting documentation

2. **Choose filing method:**
   - **Online:** https://www.agcmi.com (Request for Investigation form)
   - **Mail:** Attorney Grievance Commission, 535 Griswold, Suite 1700, Detroit, MI 48226
   - **Phone:** (313) 961-6585

3. **Complete the Request for Investigation:**
   - Provide your contact information
   - Identify the attorney by name and bar number
   - Describe the misconduct factually and specifically
   - Attach supporting evidence

4. **Required documents:**
   - Signed Request for Investigation form
   - Copies of retainer agreement or engagement letter
   - Correspondence with the attorney
   - Court filings showing missed deadlines (if applicable)
   - Financial records (if mishandling of funds)

5. **After filing:**
   - AGC staff reviews the complaint
   - Attorney may be asked to respond
   - Investigation may lead to: dismissal, admonishment, reprimand, suspension, or disbarment
   - Serious cases referred to Attorney Discipline Board for hearing

### LitigationOS Filing Stack

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Main document | AGC Request for Investigation narrative |
| Exhibit A | Retainer/engagement agreement |
| Exhibit B | Correspondence with attorney |
| Exhibit C | Court filings showing misconduct |
| Exhibit D | Financial records (if applicable) |

## 6. Friend of the Court (FOC)

### What FOC Handles

- Enforcement of child support orders
- Enforcement of parenting time/visitation orders
- Custody investigations and recommendations
- Mediation/conciliation for disputes
- Income withholding for support
- Show cause proceedings for violations

### Step-by-Step Filing

1. **Determine the issue:**
   - **Support enforcement:** Other parent not paying ordered support
   - **Parenting time enforcement:** Other parent denying court-ordered parenting time
   - **Modification:** Changed circumstances warrant modifying existing order

2. **Obtain the correct form:**
   - **FOC 100** -- Motion Regarding Parenting Time
   - **FOC 101** -- Response to Motion Regarding Parenting Time
   - **FOC 89** -- Motion Regarding Support
   - **FOC 90** -- Response to Motion Regarding Support
   - **FOC 115** -- Motion Regarding Custody
   - Forms available at: https://courts.michigan.gov/scao-forms

3. **Complete the form:**
   - Fill in case number and party names
   - Describe the violation or changed circumstances
   - State what relief you are requesting
   - Sign and date the form

4. **File with FOC:**
   - File in person at the FOC office in the county where the case is pending
   - Some counties accept e-filing via MiFILE
   - File the motion and serve it on the other party per MCR 2.107

5. **Required documents:**
   - Completed motion form
   - Copy of current court order
   - Proof of service on opposing party
   - Supporting documentation (payment records, communication logs, etc.)

6. **After filing:**
   - FOC reviews the motion
   - May schedule conciliation or mediation (MCR 3.310)
   - If unresolved, FOC may recommend enforcement action
   - Court may schedule a hearing
   - Possible outcomes: modified order, contempt finding, make-up parenting time

### Using LitigationOS to File

1. **Create a case** for the complaint (if not already tracking)
2. **Open Filing Wizard** and select "complaint" filing type
3. **Choose target agency** from the dropdown
4. **Fill in required fields** (respondent, dates, facts)
5. **Attach evidence** from your evidence library
6. **Run compliance check** -- validates all required components
7. **Run pre-filing QA** -- ensures GO decision
8. **Export the packet** as PDF documents
9. **Submit** via the agency's preferred method (online, mail, etc.)
