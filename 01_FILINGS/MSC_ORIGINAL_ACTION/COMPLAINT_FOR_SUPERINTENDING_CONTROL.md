## I. JURISDICTIONAL STATEMENT

1. This Honorable Court has original and plenary jurisdiction over this action pursuant to the Michigan Constitution of 1963, Article 6, Section 4, which provides:

   > "The supreme court shall have general superintending control over all courts; power to issue, hear and determine prerogative and remedial writs; and appellate jurisdiction as provided by rules of the supreme court."

2. MCR 7.304(A) authorizes the filing of original actions in this Court, including complaints for superintending control under MCR 7.306.

3. MCR 7.306(A) provides that a complaint for superintending control may be filed when a lower court has failed to perform a duty, has exceeded its jurisdiction, or has otherwise acted in a manner requiring correction by this Court.

4. MCL 600.1701 provides additional statutory authority for superintending control proceedings.

5. This Court's superintending control power permits it to "correct abuses of judicial power, to compel the performance of judicial duties, and to ensure the orderly administration of justice" when no other remedy is adequate. *In re Hatcher*, 443 Mich 426, 441–42 (1993).

6. Plaintiff-Relator further invokes this Court's original jurisdiction to hear claims arising under the United States Constitution, Amendment XIV, Section 1, and 42 USC § 1983, where the structural compromise of the lower court renders it incapable of adjudicating the constitutional claims presented herein.

7. **No adequate remedy exists in the 14th Circuit Court.** The presiding judge (McNeill) has an undisclosed familial conflict. The Chief Judge (Hoopes) was McNeill's former law partner at the same firm. A third judge from the same firm (Maria Ladas-Hoopes) serves on the 60th District Court in the same county. The entire 14th Circuit bench is structurally compromised by the Ladas-Hoopes-McNeill pipeline from a single law firm. Only this Court has the authority and independence to provide impartial relief.

8. Plaintiff-Relator has a related appeal pending before the Michigan Court of Appeals, Case No. 366810. That appeal cannot provide adequate or timely relief for the extraordinary circumstances presented here, including the ongoing separation of a young child from his father for 290+ days and counting.

The County's acquiescence in the Ladas-Hoopes-McNeill judicial monopoly constitutes such a deliberate choice.

100. Under *Gregory v. City of Louisville*, 444 F.3d 725 (6th Cir. 2006), and *Cady v.
