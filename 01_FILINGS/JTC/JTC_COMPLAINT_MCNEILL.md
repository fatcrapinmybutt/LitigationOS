## III. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

### A. Failure to Disclose Conflicts of Interest

17. Judge McNeill has failed to disclose her prior law partnership with Chief Judge Kenneth Hoopes at the firm of Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill. This former professional association creates, at minimum, an appearance of impropriety given that Chief Judge Hoopes has supervisory authority over case assignments and court administration in the 14th Circuit.

18. Judge McNeill has failed to acknowledge or address the Berry family connection to the proceedings. Cavan Berry's position as an attorney/magistrate at the 60th District Court, in the same building as the Friend of the Court, combined with Ronald Berry's status as the domestic partner of the opposing party, creates a conflict that should have been disclosed and addressed.

19. Despite being informed of these connections, Judge McNeill has not voluntarily recused herself, nor has she placed any disclosure on the record.

### B. Pattern of One-Sided Rulings

20. The litigation database for this case documents **471 descriptions** of judicial violations attributed to Judge McNeill's conduct, **478 source-quoted violations**, and a pattern of **3,697 ex parte-related entries** and **1,076 bias-related entries** across the case record.

21. The case timeline reflects **15 documented McNeill events** spanning from April 26, 2024 through September 5, 2025, including violations, contested orders, and hearings where procedural irregularities occurred.

22. The docket reflects a persistent pattern of rulings and orders that disproportionately favor the Watson/Berry interests while restricting or penalizing the Complainant's rights, including but not limited to:

    a. Restriction or suspension of parenting time without adequate evidentiary basis;

    b. Entry of orders based on proceedings where Complainant was not properly noticed or served;

    c. Hostile treatment of Complainant as a pro se litigant, including refusal to allow adequate time to present evidence or argument;

    d. Acceptance of unverified allegations from the opposing party while demanding heightened proof from Complainant;

    e. Failure to enforce court orders against the opposing party with the same rigor applied to Complainant.

### C. Ex Parte Communications and Proceedings

23. The record contains evidence suggesting ex parte communications or proceedings occurred without proper notice to Complainant, in violation of MCR 2.119 and due process requirements.

24. Orders have been entered in circumstances where Complainant was not given adequate notice or opportunity to be heard, raising concerns about ex parte influences on judicial decision-making.

### D. Hostile Treatment of Pro Se Litigant

25. Judge McNeill has exhibited a pattern of hostility toward Complainant as a self-represented party, including:

    a. Dismissive treatment during hearings;

    b. Failure to afford the same procedural courtesies extended to represented parties;

    c. Imposition of requirements not equally applied to the opposing party;

    d. Characterization of Complainant's legitimate legal arguments as frivolous or vexatious.

### E. Incarceration and Extreme Sanctions

26. Complainant has been subjected to incarceration in connection with these family court proceedings, raising serious due process concerns about the proportionality of sanctions and whether proper procedures were followed.

## IV. JUDICIAL CONDUCT RULES VIOLATED

### A. Canon 1 — Integrity and Independence of the Judiciary

27. Canon 1 of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct requires that a judge shall uphold the integrity and independence of the judiciary. Judge McNeill has violated this Canon by:

    a. Failing to disclose her prior partnership with Chief Judge Hoopes at Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill, thereby undermining public confidence in the independence of the judiciary;

    b. Presiding over a case involving the Berry family's interests without transparency, given the Berry connection to both the opposing party and the court system;

    c. Creating an appearance that the judicial outcome is influenced by institutional relationships rather than the merits of the case.

### B. Canon 2 — Avoiding Impropriety and the Appearance of Impropriety

28. Canon 2 requires that a judge shall avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety in all activities. The judicial bias chronology documents **severity-5 incidents** (the highest severity level) related to Canon 2 violations. Judge McNeill has violated this Canon by:

    a. Maintaining undisclosed professional and institutional connections to individuals related to the proceedings;

    b. Creating an appearance of bias through a pattern of one-sided rulings;

    c. Failing to take steps to address or remedy the appearance of impropriety once the connections were raised.

### C. Canon 3 — Performing the Duties of Judicial Office Impartially

29. Canon 3 requires that a judge shall perform judicial duties without bias or prejudice and shall maintain professional competence in the law. Judge McNeill has violated this Canon by:

    a. **Canon 3(A)(4)** — Failing to accord pro se Complainant full right to be heard according to law;

    b. **Canon 3(A)(5)** — Disposing of matters without providing adequate notice and opportunity to be heard;

    c. **Canon 3(B)(2)** — Failing to be patient, dignified, and courteous to litigant appearing pro se;

    d. **Canon 3(B)(5)** — Engaging in or permitting ex parte communications concerning pending matters.

### D. Canon 3(C)(1) — Disqualification

30. Canon 3(C)(1) requires disqualification in proceedings where the judge's impartiality might reasonably be questioned. Judge McNeill should have disqualified herself because:

    a. Her prior law partnership with Chief Judge Hoopes at Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill creates a reasonable question about impartiality;

    b. The Berry family connection (Ronald Berry as opposing party's domestic partner, Cavan Berry as attorney/magistrate in the same court complex) creates a reasonable question about impartiality;

    c. The cumulative effect of these connections — combined with the one-sided pattern of rulings — satisfies the objective standard for disqualification.

### E. MCR 9.104 — Grounds for Discipline

31. MCR 9.104 provides that the following constitute misconduct and grounds for discipline:

    a. **MCR 9.104(1)** — Misconduct in office, demonstrated by the pattern of biased rulings and failure to disclose conflicts;

    b. **MCR 9.104(2)** — Conduct prejudicial to the proper administration of justice, demonstrated by the three-court nexus of undisclosed relationships;

    c. **MCR 9.104(4)** — Conduct that violates the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, as documented in Sections IV.A through IV.D above.

33. Judge Jenny L. McNeill, Chief Judge Kenneth Hoopes, and Judge Maria Ladas-Hoopes all trace their professional origins to the same law firm: **Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill**.

### B. The Berry Connection

35. The Berry family creates a secondary nexus:
    - **Ronald Berry** lives with and is the domestic partner of the opposing party, Emily A. Watson;
    - **Cavan Berry** is an attorney/magistrate at the 60th District Court (990 Terrace Street);
    - **Pamela Rusco** (Friend of the Court) works at 990 Terrace Street — the same address as Cavan Berry;

### C. Combined Effect

36. The combined effect of these connections is that three courts in Muskegon County — the 14th Circuit Court, the 60th District Court, and the Friend of the Court — are linked by former law firm partnerships, spousal relationships, and the Berry family's dual connection to both the opposing party and the court system.

37. Whether or not actual bias has occurred, these undisclosed connections create an overwhelming appearance of impropriety that undermines public confidence in the fairness of the proceedings.
