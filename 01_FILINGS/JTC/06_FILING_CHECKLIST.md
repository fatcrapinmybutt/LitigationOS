## WHAT TO EXPECT: JTC INVESTIGATION PROCESS

### Stage 1: Preliminary Screening (2–6 weeks)

After receiving the complaint, the JTC staff will:

1. **Confirm receipt** — You should receive an acknowledgment letter
2. **Screen for jurisdiction** — Verify that the complaint is against a sitting Michigan judge and alleges conduct within the JTC's authority
3. **Initial review** — Staff attorneys review the complaint for sufficiency

**What you should do:**
- If no acknowledgment received within 30 days, call (313) 875-5110 to confirm receipt
- Do not contact the JTC repeatedly — they will contact you if they need additional information

### Stage 2: Investigation (If Opened — 3–12 months)

If the JTC determines the complaint warrants investigation:

1. **Investigator assigned** — A JTC investigator will review the court record
2. **Court records requested** — The JTC has authority to obtain all court files, transcripts, and records
3. **Judge's response** — The respondent judge may be asked to respond to the allegations
4. **Complainant interview** — You may be asked to provide additional testimony or documentation
5. **Witness interviews** — The JTC may contact witnesses identified in the complaint

**What you should do:**
- Cooperate fully with the investigator
- Provide additional documentation promptly upon request
- Be truthful and accurate in all communications
- Keep records of all communications with the JTC

### Stage 3: Commission Decision

After investigation, the JTC may take one of the following actions:

| Action | Description | Public? |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Dismiss** | Insufficient evidence or not within jurisdiction | Confidential |
| **Admonish** (private) | Private letter of admonishment to the judge | Confidential |
| **Conditional Dismissal** | Dismiss with conditions (e.g., education, counseling) | Confidential |
| **File Formal Complaint** | File a formal Request for Investigation with the Michigan Supreme Court | **PUBLIC** |

### Stage 4: Formal Proceedings (If Formal Complaint Filed)

If the JTC files a Request for Investigation with the Michigan Supreme Court:

1. **Public hearing** before a Master appointed by the Supreme Court
2. **Full trial-type proceedings** — both sides present evidence and witnesses
3. **Master's report** filed with the Michigan Supreme Court
4. **Supreme Court review** — final decision on discipline
5. **Possible sanctions:** Public censure, suspension (with or without pay), removal from the bench, retirement

## CONFIDENTIALITY RULES

### MCR 9.261 — Confidentiality of JTC Proceedings

- **All JTC proceedings are CONFIDENTIAL** until the Commission files a formal complaint with the Michigan Supreme Court
- **Do NOT publicly disclose** that you have filed a complaint with the JTC
- **Do NOT discuss the JTC investigation** on social media, in court filings, or with the press
- **Exception:** You may discuss the underlying facts of your case (the misconduct itself) — just not the fact that you filed with the JTC
- **Violation of confidentiality** could adversely affect your complaint

### What You CAN Say

- ✅ You can discuss the facts of your case (the orders, the hearings, the conduct)
- ✅ You can discuss the legal standards (the canons, the MCR, the MCL)
- ✅ You can pursue appellate remedies (COA 366810) simultaneously
- ✅ You can file motions in your pending cases based on the same underlying facts

### What You Should NOT Say

- ❌ Do not state publicly that you "filed a JTC complaint"
- ❌ Do not name the JTC as having "your case under investigation"
- ❌ Do not discuss any communications you receive from the JTC
- ❌ Do not threaten the judge with "I've filed with the JTC" in court proceedings

## YOUR RIGHTS DURING THE INVESTIGATION

Under MCR 9.200 et seq., as a complainant you have the following rights:

1. **Right to file:** Any person may file a complaint (MCR 9.205/9.207)
2. **Right to submit evidence:** You may submit additional evidence at any time during the investigation
3. **Right to be heard:** You may be asked to provide testimony during the investigation
4. **Right to notification:** You will be notified of the disposition of your complaint
5. **Right to supplement:** You may supplement your complaint with additional violations or evidence as they are discovered
6. **Right to pursue other remedies:** Filing with the JTC does not preclude appellate relief (COA 366810), federal civil rights claims (42 USC §1983), or motions in pending cases
7. **Protection from retaliation:** Filing a complaint with the JTC is a protected activity
