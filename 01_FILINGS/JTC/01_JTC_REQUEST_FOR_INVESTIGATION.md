## I. SUMMARY OF MISCONDUCT ALLEGATIONS

The enclosed materials document **1,127 discrete judicial violations** committed by Judge McNeill in the above-referenced domestic relations and personal protection order proceedings. These violations are supported by an impeachment database of **15,171 items**, including 545 documented deviations from Michigan Judicial Institute Benchbook standards and 381 instances of PPO weaponization.

The violations fall into five principal categories:

| Category | Count | Severity |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **Ex Parte Violations** — Orders entered without hearing or notice, constituting 44% of all substantive orders | 150 | CRITICAL |
| **Bias & Prejudice (MCR 2.003 Disqualification Violations)** — Including derogatory language ("crazy," "liar") in official orders, one-sided application of legal standards, and predetermined outcomes | 167 | CRITICAL |
| **Procedural Misconduct** — Failure to comply with mandatory MCR/MCL requirements, including 126 violations of MCL 600.2950/2950a (PPO statutes) and 545 benchbook deviations | 161 | CRITICAL |
| **Due Process Denial** — Complete failure to apply 12 mandatory MCL 722.23 best-interest factors (0 of 12 analyzed) while depriving me of custody; disregard of exculpatory evidence including negative drug screen | 175+ | CRITICAL |
| **Retaliation** — $250 sanctions imposed for filing motions; escalating adverse orders following exercise of legal rights; PPO weaponization (27 documented instances) | Documented pattern | HIGH–CRITICAL |

More than one-third of all violations (377 of 1,127 — 33.5%) are classified as CRITICAL, meaning they involve deprivation of constitutional rights or fundamental procedural protections. Over 55% are CRITICAL or HIGH severity — an inverted distribution inconsistent with normal judicial error and indicative of systematic misconduct.

## V. JURISDICTIONAL BASIS

This Request for Investigation is properly before the Commission under:

1. **Michigan Constitution of 1963, Article 6, §30** — Granting the Judicial Tenure Commission authority to investigate judicial misconduct and recommend discipline, including censure, suspension, or removal from office.

2. **MCR 9.200 et seq.** — Establishing the procedural framework for investigation, prosecution, and adjudication of complaints of judicial misconduct and disability.

3. **MCR 9.205** — Authorizing any person to file a complaint alleging that a judge has engaged in misconduct in office.

4. **MCR 9.207** — Providing that complaints may be filed by any person and setting forth the initial screening process.

## VI. REQUEST

I respectfully request that the Commission:

1. **Open an investigation** into the conduct of Judge Jenny L. McNeill as documented in the enclosed complaint and attachments;

2. **Assign an investigator** to review the complete court record in Cases 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP, including all orders, transcripts, docket entries, and filed documents;
