### VII. LEGAL AUTHORITY

18. Under **MCL 600.2529(5)**, the Court may waive fees for a party who demonstrates inability to pay.

19. Under **MCR 2.002**, a party may file a verified statement of inability to pay fees. The Court must consider: (a) the party's income and assets; (b) the party's expenses; (c) the nature and complexity of the case; and (d) the party's litigation history.

20. The United States Supreme Court has held that due process prohibits states from conditioning access to courts on the payment of fees that an indigent party cannot afford:
    - *Boddie v. Connecticut*, 401 U.S. 371 (1971) — Court access cannot be conditioned on fees in family law matters
    - *M.L.B. v. S.L.J.*, 519 U.S. 102 (1996) — Extends *Boddie* to parental rights cases
    - *Turner v. Rogers*, 564 U.S. 431 (2011) — Due process protections in civil contempt proceedings
