### COUNT II — INTENTIONAL INFLICTION OF EMOTIONAL DISTRESS
### (Against All Defendants)

52. Plaintiff incorporates by reference each and every allegation set forth in Paragraphs 1 through 46 as if fully restated herein.

**Elements Under Michigan Law** (*Roberts v Auto-Owners Ins. Co.*, 422 Mich 594, 602 (1985)):

To establish IIED, Plaintiff must prove: (1) extreme and outrageous conduct; (2) intent or recklessness; (3) causation; and (4) severe emotional distress. Each element is satisfied here:

**(1) Extreme and Outrageous Conduct:** The conduct "must be so outrageous in character, and so extreme in degree, as to go beyond all possible bounds of decency, and to be regarded as atrocious and utterly intolerable in a civilized community." *Roberts*, 422 Mich at 602–03; *Restatement (Second) of Torts* § 46, cmt. d.

53. Defendants engaged in extreme and outrageous conduct that was intentional and/or reckless, including but not limited to:

   a. Deliberately separating Plaintiff from his child for 329 or more days;
   b. Fabricating an arsenic poisoning allegation designed to destroy the father-child bond;
   c. Fabricating a physical assault allegation — police found nothing;
   d. Fabricating a drug use/hallucination allegation — HealthWest cleared Plaintiff;
   e. Fabricating a cocaine residue claim (October 15, 2023);
   f. Fabricating a stalking allegation — police found no stalking;
   g. Albert Watson's physical assault of Plaintiff (November 2023);
   h. Albert Watson's forcible removal of a toddler from his father's arms (October 20, 2024);
   i. Cody Watson's road harassment endangering L.D.W. (October 20, 2024);
   j. Lori Watson's trespass threats and ambush PPO service;
   k. Denying Plaintiff access to L.D.W. on Halloween 2024 and denying the child an ice cream outing with his father;
   l. Albert Watson's threat that the "FBI is after your family"; and
   m. Causing Plaintiff to be jailed for contempt for asking to see his son.

54. Defendants' conduct was so extreme and outrageous that no reasonable person in a civilized society would tolerate it. The deliberate, prolonged separation of a loving parent from his young child — accomplished through violence, fraud, and systematic abuse of legal processes — exceeds all possible bounds of decency and is utterly intolerable.

**(2) Intent or Recklessness:** Defendants knew their conduct was substantially certain to cause severe emotional distress. Albert Watson admitted to police that the purpose of filing the August 7, 2025 report was so "Emily can go tomorrow to get an Ex Parte order" — proving deliberate intent to deprive Father of custody. *See Lewis v LeGrow*, 258 Mich App 175, 196 (2003) (recklessness sufficient where defendant "knew or should have known" distress was substantially certain).

**(3) Causation:** But for Defendants' coordinated campaign — the false police reports, fabricated allegations (arsenic, assault, drugs, stalking), ambush PPO service, physical intimidation, and weaponized court filings — Plaintiff would not have lost custody, been jailed, or suffered 329+ days of separation from L.D.W.

**(4) Severe Emotional Distress:**

55. As a direct and proximate result of Defendants' extreme and outrageous conduct, Plaintiff has suffered severe emotional distress, including but not limited to:

   a. Severe and ongoing anxiety;
   b. Clinical depression;
   c. Disrupted sleep;
   d. Disrupted employment and loss of income;
   e. Loss of the parent-child bond with L.D.W.; and
   f. Need for counseling and medical treatment.

56. Plaintiff's emotional distress is severe, ongoing, and of a nature that no reasonable person should be expected to endure.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against all Defendants, jointly and severally, for compensatory damages, punitive damages, costs, and such further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.
