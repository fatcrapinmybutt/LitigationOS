## PURPOSE

This memorandum sets forth the undisputed material facts establishing ongoing irreparable harm to the parent-child relationship between Plaintiff-Father Andrew J. Pigors and the minor child L.D.W. (minor child, born November 9, 2022) (DOB: November 9, 2022), supporting the emergency designation of this filing under MCR 2.119(B) and MCR 3.207(A).

---

## IRREPARABLE HARM FACTS

### A. Duration of Separation

1. Plaintiff-Father has been separated from his minor child L.D.W. for **571+ cumulative days** across the pendency of this case.

2. The most recent continuous period of complete deprivation has been running since **July 29, 2025** — the last date Plaintiff-Father had any contact whatsoever with his son.

3. Since July 29, 2025, Plaintiff-Father has had **zero contact** with Lincoln — no visits, no telephone calls, no video calls, no communication of any kind.

4. Lincoln is currently **three years old** — a critical developmental period for attachment formation, language acquisition, emotional regulation, and social development.

---

### B. Negative Drug Screen

5. Plaintiff-Father submitted to a narcotics screening in connection with the allegations raised in the ex parte motion.

6. The results of the narcotics screening were **NEGATIVE**. Plaintiff-Father did not test positive for any controlled substance, including methamphetamine. (*See* Exhibit D.)

7. The allegation of drug use — the primary factual basis for the suspension of all parenting time — has been **affirmatively disproved**.

---

### C. Ex Parte Orders of August 8, 2025

8. On **August 8, 2025**, this Court entered ex parte orders suspending all of Plaintiff-Father's parenting time with Lincoln.

9. Plaintiff-Father was **not given notice** prior to the entry of these orders.

10. Plaintiff-Father was **not given an opportunity to be heard** before the deprivation occurred.

11. Upon information and belief, the ex parte orders were based on a motion filed by Defendant Emily A. Watson that was:
    - **Not sworn** — no oath or affirmation of truth
    - **Not notarized** — no jurat or notarial certification
    - **Not verified** — in violation of MCR 3.207(B)(1) requiring "specific facts in a verified pleading or affidavit"

12. Upon information and belief, the unsworn motion relied on **hearsay statements** from:
    - An unnamed "officer" who allegedly "believed this was a result of meth use" — unsworn, unidentified, constituting inadmissible double hearsay under MRE 801(c) and MRE 802
    - An unnamed "friend" who was allegedly "concerned" about an "eviction" — anonymous, unsworn hearsay with no authenticated supporting documentation

---

### D. No Post-Deprivation Hearing

13. As of the date of this filing, **no evidentiary hearing on the merits** of the parenting time suspension has been conducted.

14. MCR 3.206(B)(6) mandates that a hearing be scheduled "as soon as possible" after entry of an ex parte order. More than **seven months** have elapsed without a hearing.

15. The prolonged deprivation without a hearing has created a **de facto permanent custody arrangement** imposed without the procedural protections required even for temporary orders.

---

### E. Milestones Permanently Lost

16. During the 571+ days of separation, Plaintiff-Father has been unable to be present for:

    a. **Lincoln's 3rd birthday** (November 9, 2025)
    b. **Labor Day 2025**
    c. **Thanksgiving 2025**
    d. **Christmas 2025**
    e. **New Year's 2026**
    f. **Valentine's Day 2026**
    g. Every ordinary day of Lincoln's development between ages two and three

17. These milestones **cannot be recaptured**. The developmental window is closing.

---

### F. Irreparable Nature of Harm

18. The harm to the parent-child bond is **ongoing and irreparable**. At three years of age, Lincoln's memory and attachment bonds are actively forming. A separation of this duration during this developmental period risks **permanent damage** to the parent-child bond.

19. Developmental research consistently demonstrates that prolonged separation from a bonded parent during ages 2–4 causes measurable harm to attachment security, emotional regulation, and social development.

20. Every additional day of separation **deepens the harm** and makes restoration of the bond more difficult.

21. No monetary remedy can compensate for the permanent loss of a parent-child relationship during these critical years. The harm is, by definition, **irreparable**.

---

## CONCLUSION

The undisputed facts establish that Plaintiff-Father is suffering ongoing, irreparable harm: 571+ days of total separation from his three-year-old son, based on ex parte orders entered on unsworn hearsay, with a negative drug screen disproving the sole factual basis, and no hearing conducted in over seven months. Emergency relief is warranted.
