### I. DISCOVERY DATE LOGIC

**Applicable Rule:** MCR 2.003(D)(1) — A motion to disqualify must be filed "within 14 days of the party's discovery of the grounds for disqualification."

**Discovery Analysis:**

The grounds for disqualification in this case arise from a **cumulative pattern** of judicial conduct, not a single discrete event. While individual incidents (ex parte orders, derogatory language, retaliatory sanctions) occurred at various points throughout the proceeding, the **full scope and pattern** of bias was not discoverable until a comprehensive review of the complete case record was conducted.

- **Individual incident dates:** Various dates throughout 2024–2025 proceedings
- **Most recent triggering event:** Ex parte order entered on or about August 8, 2025, affecting Father's custodial rights without notice or hearing
- **Discovery date of cumulative pattern:** The date on which Father completed systematic review of all docket entries, orders, and transcripts, revealing the full extent of 1,127 documented judicial violations across multiple categories
- **Discovery date for filing purposes:** ______________________, 2025

**Continuing violation doctrine:** Where judicial bias manifests as a continuing pattern rather than a discrete act, the discovery date is the date on which the movant becomes aware of the pattern's full scope. *See* MCR 2.003(D)(1); *Cain v. Dep't of Corrections*, 451 Mich. 470, 497 (1996).

### VI. IF DENIED → REQUEST DE NOVO REFERRAL

**Applicable Rule:** MCR 2.003(D)(3)(a)–(b)

If the trial judge (Hon. Jenny L. McNeill) denies this Motion for Disqualification, the following procedure applies automatically under MCR 2.003(D):

> **REQUEST FOR DE NOVO REVIEW**
>
> In the event this Motion for Disqualification is denied by the trial judge, Plaintiff-Father hereby requests, pursuant to MCR 2.003(D)(3)(a), that the motion be referred to the Chief Judge of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court for de novo review.
>
> If the Chief Judge is unable to hear the motion (including if the Chief Judge is the subject of the motion), Plaintiff-Father requests referral to the State Court Administrator for reassignment to another judge for de novo review, pursuant to MCR 2.003(D)(3)(b).

**De Novo Review Framework:**

1. **Step 1 — Trial Judge Decision:** The challenged judge (McNeill) rules on the motion. MCR 2.003(D)(2).
2. **Step 2 — If Denied → Chief Judge Referral:** If the challenged judge denies the motion, the motion is automatically referred to the Chief Judge of the 14th Judicial Circuit for a de novo hearing. MCR 2.003(D)(3)(a).
3. **Step 3 — If Chief Judge Cannot Hear → State Court Administrator:** If the Chief Judge is disqualified or otherwise unable to hear the motion, referral is made to the State Court Administrator, who shall assign another judge. MCR 2.003(D)(3)(b).
4. **Step 4 — De Novo Standard:** The reviewing judge conducts a de novo hearing and makes an independent determination. The reviewing judge is not bound by the challenged judge's findings or conclusions.

**Preservation language:**

> Plaintiff-Father preserves his right to de novo review at each level of the MCR 2.003(D) procedure. Denial by the trial judge does not terminate this motion but triggers mandatory referral for independent review.
