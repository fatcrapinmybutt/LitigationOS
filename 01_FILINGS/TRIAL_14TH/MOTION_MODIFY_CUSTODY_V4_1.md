# PART III: ESTABLISHED CUSTODIAL ENVIRONMENT ANALYSIS

**Issue:** Whether an established custodial environment (ECE) exists and what burden of proof applies.

**Rule:** MCL 722.27(1)(c) provides that if an ECE exists, the party seeking modification must prove by clear and convincing evidence that the modification is in the child's best interest. An ECE is established when "over an appreciable time the child naturally looks to the custodial parent for guidance, discipline, the necessities of life, and parental comfort." *Id.*; *Hunter v. Hunter*, 484 Mich 247, 265; 771 NW2d 694 (2009).

**Application:**

36. Prior to the August 2025 ex parte orders, an ECE may have existed with both parents, as L.D.W. had regular contact with Father and Mother. The court-imposed 329+ day separation has artificially disrupted this dual ECE.

37. To the extent an ECE now exists solely with Mother, it was created by the very ex parte orders that are being challenged as procedurally deficient. The Court should not allow a court-manufactured status quo — created without proper findings, without an evidentiary hearing, and without compliance with MCR 3.206(B)(5)-(6) — to be weaponized as an obstacle to correction.

38. Even under the heightened clear and convincing evidence standard, the best interest analysis above — particularly the overwhelming evidence under Factor (j) and the documented parental alienation campaign — satisfies this burden.

**Conclusion:** Whether the standard is preponderance or clear and convincing, the evidence demonstrates that custody modification is in L.D.W.'s best interests.
