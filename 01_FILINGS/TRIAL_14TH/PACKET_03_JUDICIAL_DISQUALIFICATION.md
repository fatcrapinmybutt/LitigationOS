MOTION AND AFFIDAVIT FOR DISQUALIFICATION OF JUDGE
Pursuant to MCR 2.003(C) and (D)

NOW COMES Plaintiff Andrew J. Pigors, appearing pro se, and pursuant
to MCR 2.003(C)(1)(a), (b), and (d), and the Michigan Code of Judicial
Conduct, Canons 1, 2, and 3, respectfully moves for the disqualification
of the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill from all proceedings in the above-
captioned matters, and in support thereof submits the following Motion
and Affidavit of Prejudice:

                    I. LEGAL STANDARD

1. MCR 2.003(C)(1) provides grounds for disqualification of a judge,
including:

    (a) "The judge is biased or prejudiced for or against a party or
    attorney."

    (b) "The judge, based on objective and reasonable perceptions, has
    either (i) a serious risk of actual bias impacting the due process
    rights of a party as enunciated in Caperton v Massey, 556 US 868;
    129 S Ct 2252; 173 L Ed 2d 1208 (2009), or (ii) has failed to
    adhere to the appearance of impropriety standard set forth in Canon
    2 of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct."

    (d) "The judge has a personal bias or prejudice concerning a party
    or a party's lawyer."

2. The party seeking disqualification need not prove actual prejudice
or that the judge would rule against them. Rather, the test is whether
"a reasonable person, knowing all the circumstances, would harbor a
justified doubt about the judge's impartiality." In re MCI Telecomm
Complaint, 460 Mich 396, 436 (1999).

3. Canon 2 of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct requires: "A judge
should avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety in all
activities."

4. Canon 3(A)(4) provides: "A judge should accord to every person who
has a legal interest in a proceeding, or that person's lawyer, the
right to be heard according to law."

5. Canon 3(C)(1) states: "A judge should disqualify himself or herself
in a proceeding in which the judge's impartiality might reasonably be
questioned."

            II. GROUNDS FOR DISQUALIFICATION

A. Pattern of Ex Parte Proceedings

6. Judge McNeill has conducted proceedings and entered orders in these
matters without proper notice to Plaintiff-Father and without affording
Plaintiff-Father an opportunity to be heard, in violation of MCR 2.119
and Canon 3(A)(4).

7. Specific instances include:
    a. Issuance of the PPO (Case No. 2023-5907-PP) ex parte without
       subsequent timely hearing;
    b. Entry of orders affecting custody and parenting time without
       evidentiary hearing;
    c. Denial of Plaintiff-Father's motions without oral argument or
       hearing;
    d. Failure to schedule hearings on properly filed motions within
       the timeframes required by MCR 2.119(F).

B. Failure to Apply Mandatory Legal Standards

8. Judge McNeill has repeatedly failed to apply mandatory legal
standards in these proceedings:

    a. Failure to conduct a best interest analysis under MCL 722.23
       before modifying custody or parenting time;
    b. Failure to apply the clear and convincing evidence standard
       required by MCL 722.27a before suspending parenting time;
    c. Failure to make findings of fact on the record as required by
       MCR 3.207 and MCL 722.27;
    d. Failure to follow the procedural requirements of MCR 3.705 and
       MCR 3.707 in PPO proceedings.

C. Demonstrated Bias Against Plaintiff-Father

9. The totality of the record demonstrates a pattern of bias against
Plaintiff-Father:

    a. Consistently ruling in Defendant-Mother's favor on virtually
       every contested matter;
    b. Crediting Defendant-Mother's allegations without evidentiary
       support while dismissing Plaintiff-Father's documented evidence;
    c. Applying different procedural standards to the two parties;
    d. Demonstrating hostility or impatience toward Plaintiff-Father
       during proceedings;
    e. Permitting opposing counsel (Rusco) latitude not afforded to
       Plaintiff-Father as a pro se litigant;
    f. Failure to ensure Plaintiff-Father's pro se status did not
       result in denial of fundamental rights, as required by Haines v.
       Kerner, 404 U.S. 519, 520 (1972).

D. Violation of Canon 3(C)(1): Failure to Self-Disqualify

10. Despite the foregoing pattern of conduct, Judge McNeill has failed
to self-disqualify as required by Canon 3(C)(1), which provides that
"a judge should disqualify himself or herself in a proceeding in which
the judge's impartiality might reasonably be questioned."

11. The cumulative effect of these actions creates, at minimum, an
appearance of impropriety that undermines public confidence in the
impartiality of these proceedings, in violation of Canon 2.

E. Caperton Standard: Serious Risk of Actual Bias

12. Under the standard articulated in Caperton v. A.T. Massey Coal Co.,
556 U.S. 868 (2009), and adopted in MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b)(i), the question
is whether, "under a realistic appraisal of psychological tendencies
and human weakness," the judge's conduct "poses such a risk of actual
bias or prejudgment that the practice must be forbidden if the guarantee
of due process is to be adequately implemented."

13. The prolonged pattern of conduct described herein — including 329+
days of parental separation without an evidentiary hearing, repeated
denial of motions without hearing, ex parte proceedings, and failure to
apply mandatory legal standards — creates a serious risk of actual bias
that impacts Plaintiff-Father's due process rights.
