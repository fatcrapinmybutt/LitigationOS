## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This analysis examines 755 documents spanning custody proceedings, personal protection orders, judicial orders, psychological evaluations, court transcripts, and correspondence in two intertwined family law matters. The evidence reveals a systematic pattern of judicial misconduct, procedural violations, fraudulent allegations, parental alienation, and constitutional deprivations affecting a father's fundamental right to parent his child.

**Key Findings:**
- **192+ days** of wrongful parental deprivation (July 29, 2025 - present)
- **5 ex parte suspension orders** issued without affidavits or evidentiary hearings
- **Unauthenticated mental health evaluation** relied upon as basis for custody modification
- **Favorable HealthWest evaluation** systematically excluded from record
- **Police reports** contradicting mother's allegations ignored by court
- **Fraudulent PPO** weaponized to control custody and parenting time
- **Systematic judicial bias** by Judge Jenny L. McNeill
- **Multiple constitutional violations** including due process and liberty interest deprivations

## I. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

### A. Pre-Litigation Status (Birth - June 2024)

Andrew J. Pigors ("Father") has been a consistent, primary caregiver for his son, L.D.W. (born 2019), since birth. Before any court involvement, the parties maintained a de facto shared-parenting arrangement with Father exercising frequent and substantial parenting time, providing daily care, transportation, and financial support.

**Key Facts:**
- No history of criminal convictions against Father
- No CPS findings or substantiated abuse allegations
- No mental health diagnoses or hospitalizations
- Informal but stable co-parenting arrangement
- Father's caregiving relationship recognized as appropriate by court's own July 2024 order awarding equal legal custody and 182.5 overnights per year

### B. Escalation Phase (October 2023 - June 2024)

Emily A. Watson ("Mother") initiated a campaign to portray Father as mentally unstable and dangerous, employing a coordinated strategy of false allegations, weaponized police welfare checks, and fraudulent legal filings.

**Timeline of False Allegations:**

| Date | Allegation | Police Finding | Evidence Status |
|------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 10/15/2023 | Suicidal/mentally unstable | No evidence found; not hospitalized | Contradicted by medical records |
| 11/12/2023 | Suicidal/mentally unstable (2nd) | No evidence found; cleared | Contradicted by medical records |
| 11/17/2023 | Arsenic poisoning attempt | No toxicology; no forensic evidence; no charges | Contradicted by ER records |
| 11/2023 | Physical assault/pushing | No police report; no witnesses | Unsubstantiated |
| 11/2023 | Surveillance/stalking | Based on ordinary devices (webcam) | Contradicted by technical analysis |
| 3/28-5/5/2024 | (First withholding window) | N/A | Unilateral deprivation acknowledged by court |

**Critical Finding:** Mother's allegations escalated in severity and frequency precisely when Father sought to formalize his parenting rights through custody litigation, suggesting a strategic litigation tactic rather than genuine safety concerns.

### C. July 17, 2024 Custody Trial: Abrupt Reversal Without Evidentiary Basis

**Pre-Trial Status:** Father exercised 182.5 overnights per year (50/50 arrangement) with equal legal custody.

**Trial Conditions:**
- Father appeared pro se (without counsel)
- Mother appeared with counsel (Attorney Jennifer Barnes)
- Father was not allowed to present prepared documentary evidence
- Father was not allowed to present recordings
- Father's witness list was restricted
- Mother's counsel given wide latitude in narrative and argument
- Father confined to limited oral answers

**Post-Trial Outcome:** Parenting time reduced from 182.5 overnights to 79 overnights per year; Father stripped of legal custody.

**Legal Violation:** This reduction, absent substantiated harm findings and complete evidentiary hearing, violates:
- MCL 722.27a (presumption favoring parenting time)
- MCR 3.210 (custody modification procedures requiring clear and convincing evidence)
- Pierron v. Pierron, 486 Mich 81 (2010) (requiring best-interest analysis when modifying established custodial environment)
- Due process (fundamental liberty interest in parent-child relationship)

### D. Ex Parte Suspension Phase (August 2025 - Present)

**August 8, 2025:** Judge McNeill issued an ex parte order suspending ALL of Father's parenting time based on an unsworn motion without:
- Affidavit or verified pleading
- Evidentiary hearing
- Child-specific findings of immediate and irreparable harm
- Clear and convincing proof of endangerment
- Opportunity for Father to be heard

**Subsequent Continuations:** The suspension was repeatedly continued at hearings on:
- September 4, 2025
- October 29, 2025
- November 26, 2025
- (Additional continuations through present)

**Legal Violations:**
- MCR 3.207(B) (ex parte relief requires affidavit showing immediate/irreparable harm)
- MCL 722.27a(3) (clear and convincing endangerment standard)
- MCR 3.210(D) (written findings required)
- Due process (fundamental liberty interest)

## II. JUDICIAL MISCONDUCT: FIVE SYSTEMATIC BIAS PATTERNS

### Pattern 1: Ex Parte Handling of Evidence Without Procedural Safeguards

**Evidence:**
- Judge McNeill reviewed USB audio recordings in chambers without Father's knowledge or participation
- Court relied on alleged "second" mental health evaluation that Father never completed
- Court excluded favorable HealthWest evaluation that Father completed at court's direction
- No authentication, foundation, or expert testimony regarding "second" evaluation
- No opportunity for cross-examination or rebuttal

**Legal Standard Violated:**
- MRE 901 (authentication requirement)
- MRE 702-703 (expert testimony foundation)
- Due process (right to confront witnesses/evidence)
- Santosky v. Kramer, 455 US 745 (1982) (heightened reliability for parental rights restrictions)

### Pattern 2: Selective Application of Evidence

**Evidence Excluded:**
- HealthWest psychological evaluation (favorable to Father)
- AppClose co-parenting app logs
- Police reports contradicting Mother's allegations
- Father's documentary evidence and recordings
- Exculpatory medical records

**Evidence Admitted/Relied Upon:**
- Mother's unsworn allegations
- Unauthenticated "second" mental health evaluation
- Mother's curated recordings
- Mother's subjective characterizations

**Legal Violation:** This selective evidentiary approach violates:
- Due process (fundamental fairness)
- MCR 2.517 (requirement for findings addressing all evidence)
- Rules of evidence (authentication, foundation, relevance)

### Pattern 3: Procedural Barriers to Motion Practice

**Evidence:**
- Judge McNeill stated on record: "Do not file any more. I will not look at it."
- Court imposed $250 per-motion bond requirement without vexatious litigant finding
- Court refused to consider Father's written objections
- Court limited Father's oral argument time
- Court muted Father during remote hearings when he attempted to testify

**Legal Violations:**
- MCR 2.002 (access to courts; indigency considerations)
- Due process (right to be heard)
- MCR 2.003(C) (appearance of bias/impropriety)

### Pattern 4: Mischaracterization and Stigmatization

**Evidence:**
- Judge called Father "crazy" on the record
- Judge called Father a "liar" on the record
- Judge characterized Father's mental health without diagnostic basis
- Judge made assumptions about drug use without evidence or testing
- Judge relied on family hearsay and speculation

**Legal Violation:**
- MCR 2.003(C) (appearance of bias and loss of impartiality)
- Due process (fundamental fairness)
- Caperton v. AT Massey Coal Co., 556 US 868 (2009) (appearance of bias standard)

### Pattern 5: Failure to Sanction Documented Gatekeeping and Parental Alienation

**Evidence:**
- Mother withheld child for 39 days (March 28 - May 5, 2024) without court intervention
- Mother withheld child for 26 days (October 20 - November 15, 2024) without court intervention
- Maternal grandmother sent written trespass threats to Father
- Mother prevented Father from contacting child through co-parenting app
- Court acknowledged withholding but stated it could not "hold it against her" because it occurred pre-filing
- No sanctions imposed on Mother for documented gatekeeping

**Legal Violation:**
- MCL 722.27a (presumption favoring parenting time; gatekeeping violates this presumption)
- MCL 722.23 (best-interest factors; parental alienation is relevant)
- Equal protection (Father sanctioned for disputed communication; Mother not sanctioned for documented withholding)

## III. EMILY WATSON'S PERJURY AND FALSE STATEMENTS

### Documented False Statements Under Oath

**Statement 1: Arsenic Poisoning**
- **Claim:** Father attempted to poison Mother with arsenic using a mason jar
- **Evidence:** Mother produced photographs of jar
- **Medical Records:** ER visit showed no objective findings consistent with arsenic poisoning
- **Forensic Evidence:** No toxicology confirmation; no forensic testing; no criminal charges
- **Status:** Completely unsubstantiated; PPO granted despite lack of evidence
- **Legal Consequence:** Perjury under MCL 750.423

**Statement 2: Physical Assault**
- **Claim:** Father hit Mother or pushed her to her knees
- **Evidence:** Mother's testimony only
- **Police Reports:** No police report filed; no third-party witnesses; no corroboration
- **Status:** Unsubstantiated
- **Legal Consequence:** Perjury under MCL 750.423

**Statement 3: Surveillance/Stalking**
- **Claim:** Father surveilled Mother using cameras and technology
- **Evidence:** Ordinary devices (computer webcam) reframed as sinister; staged/curated recordings
- **Technical Analysis:** Devices were standard computer equipment, not surveillance equipment
- **Status:** Mischaracterization and fabrication
- **Legal Consequence:** Perjury under MCL 750.423

**Statement 4: Mental Health/Drug Use**
- **Claim:** Father was using drugs and suffering from hallucinations
- **Evidence:** Family hearsay; Mother's subjective reports
- **Medical Records:** No testing; no diagnoses; no medical findings
- **HealthWest Evaluation:** Contradicts these claims
- **Status:** Unsubstantiated speculation
- **Legal Consequence:** Perjury under MCL 750.423

**Statement 5: Threat to Child**
- **Claim:** Father posed a threat to the child
- **Evidence:** None presented
- **Court Finding:** Court awarded Father equal legal custody and 182.5 overnights (50/50) in July 2024, contradicting any genuine threat
- **Status:** Contradicted by court's own custody order
- **Legal Consequence:** Perjury under MCL 750.423

### Pattern of Perjury

Mother's false statements share common characteristics:
- **Escalation:** Allegations became increasingly severe over time
- **Timing:** Allegations intensified when Father sought to formalize parenting rights
- **Lack of Evidence:** No corroborating evidence, police reports, medical findings, or witness testimony
- **Strategic Purpose:** Each allegation served to restrict Father's parenting time
- **Judicial Acceptance:** Judge McNeill accepted allegations despite lack of evidence

## VII. PROCEDURAL VIOLATIONS

### A. Ex Parte Order Violations (MCR 3.207)

**Requirement:** Ex parte relief only when affidavit or verified pleading shows specific facts demonstrating immediate and irreparable harm from notice delay.

**Violations:**
- August 8, 2025 order issued on unsworn motion
- No affidavit or verified pleading
- No specific facts showing immediate/irreparable harm
- No child-specific findings
- Continued without evidentiary hearing

### B. Custody Modification Violations (MCR 3.210)

**Requirements:**
- Clear and convincing evidence of endangerment (MCL 722.27a)
- Best-interest analysis under MCL 722.23
- Written findings addressing statutory factors
- Consideration of established custodial environment (Pierron)

**Violations:**
- No written findings addressing MCL 722.23 factors
- No clear and convincing evidence of endangerment
- No Pierron analysis of established environment
- Abrupt reversal from 50/50 to 79 overnights without evidentiary basis

### C. Evidence Rule Violations (MRE)

**Violations:**
- MRE 901 (authentication): Unauthenticated "second" evaluation relied upon
- MRE 702-703 (expert testimony): No foundation for "second" evaluation
- MRE 401-403 (relevance): Favorable HealthWest evaluation excluded
- MRE 611 (control of presentation): Father excluded from presenting evidence

### D. Findings Requirement Violations (MCR 2.517)

**Requirement:** Orders must include findings of fact and conclusions of law.

**Violation:** Orders lack findings addressing:
- Specific facts supporting suspension
- Best-interest factors
- Clear and convincing endangerment
- Consideration of Father's evidence
