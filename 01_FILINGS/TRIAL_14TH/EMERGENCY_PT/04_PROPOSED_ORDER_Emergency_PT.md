              PROPOSED ORDER RESTORING PARENTING TIME


    At a session of said Court, held in the Muskegon County
Courthouse, 990 Terrace St., Muskegon, Michigan 49442, on the
______ day of _________________, 20___.

PRESENT: HON. JENNY L. McNEILL, Circuit Court Judge


    The Court having considered Plaintiff's Emergency Motion for
Expedited Restoration of Parenting Time and Makeup Parenting Time,
the accompanying Verified Affidavit and Brief in Support, and any
response filed by Defendant; and the Court having considered the
factors set forth in MCL 722.27a(7) and MCL 722.23; and the Court
being otherwise fully advised in the premises;

    IT IS HEREBY ORDERED AS FOLLOWS:


               I. IMMEDIATE RESTORATION OF PARENTING TIME

    1. Plaintiff's parenting time with the minor child, L.D.W.,
is IMMEDIATELY RESTORED effective upon entry of this Order.

    2. The ex parte order dated on or about August 8, 2025,
suspending Plaintiff's parenting time, is VACATED.


                    II. INTERIM PARENTING TIME SCHEDULE

    3. Pending a full evidentiary hearing, Plaintiff shall have
parenting time with L.D.W. as follows:

        a. SUPERVISED VISITS: A minimum of four (4) hours, three
           (3) times per week — specifically Wednesday from 4:00 PM
           to 8:00 PM, Saturday from 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM, and
           Sunday from 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM;

        b. SUPERVISION: Supervised by a court-approved professional
           supervisor (NOT a family member of either party);

        c. TELEPHONE/VIDEO CONTACT: Plaintiff shall have daily
           telephone or video contact with L.D.W. for a minimum of
           thirty (30) minutes per day, at a consistent time agreed
           upon by the parties or, absent agreement, at 7:00 PM
           daily;

        d. EXCHANGE LOCATION: A neutral, public location — the
           Muskegon County courthouse lobby or such other location
           as the Court may designate;

        e. COMMENCEMENT: The first supervised visit shall occur
           within seventy-two (72) hours of the entry of this Order.


                III. TRANSITION TO UNSUPERVISED PARENTING TIME

    4. Upon satisfactory completion of sixty (60) days of
supervised parenting time with no documented incidents or concerns,
Plaintiff may petition this Court for transition to unsupervised
parenting time on the following schedule:

        a. Every other weekend from Friday at 6:00 PM to Sunday at
           6:00 PM;
        b. One evening per week (Wednesday) from 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM;
        c. Alternating holidays per the standard Muskegon County
           holiday schedule;
        d. Two (2) weeks of summer parenting time with fourteen
           (14) days' advance notice.


                  IV. MAKEUP PARENTING TIME

    5. In recognition of the approximately 571+ days of denied
parenting time, Plaintiff is awarded makeup parenting time pursuant
to MCL 722.27a(7)(c) and MCL 552.644(2)(c), to be scheduled as
follows:

        a. An additional full weekend per month (Friday 6:00 PM to
           Sunday 6:00 PM) for a period of twelve (12) months;
        b. An additional two (2) weeks of summer parenting time in
           the first year;
        c. The makeup schedule shall not be subject to modification
           absent a showing of clear and convincing evidence that
           it endangers the child.


               V. ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE — CONTEMPT

    6. Defendant Emily A. Watson is ORDERED to appear before this
Court on _________________ at _________ __.m., to show cause why
she should not be held in contempt of court pursuant to MCR 3.208
and MCL 552.644 for the wrongful denial of court-ordered parenting
time.

    7. Should the Court find Defendant in contempt, the Court may
impose any or all of the remedies set forth in MCL 552.644(2),
including fines, jail, community service, modification of parenting
time, and mandatory sanctions under MCL 552.644(6) if bad faith is
found.


              VI. COMMUNICATION AND CONDUCT PROVISIONS

    8. Neither party shall make disparaging remarks about the other
party in the presence of L.D.W. or within L.D.W.'s hearing.

    9. Neither party shall discuss the details of this litigation
with or in the presence of L.D.W.

    10. Neither party shall allow third parties (including but not
limited to Tony Watson Sr., Diana Watson, Tony Watson Jr., and Lori
Watson) to make disparaging remarks about the other parent in the
presence of L.D.W. or to interfere with parenting time exchanges.

    11. Both parties shall use the OurFamilyWizard or AppClose
application for all non-emergency communication regarding parenting
time scheduling.

    12. Defendant shall ensure that L.D.W. is available for all
scheduled telephone/video contact and parenting time exchanges.
Failure to do so without good cause shall constitute contempt.


                    VII. GUARDIAN AD LITEM

    13. The Court appoints _____________________________ as Guardian
Ad Litem to represent the independent interests of L.D.W. The GAL
shall file a report within thirty (30) days of appointment and
before the evidentiary hearing.


                  VIII. EXPEDITED HEARING

    14. An expedited evidentiary hearing is SCHEDULED for
_________________ at _________ __.m., to address:

        a. Permanent restoration and expansion of parenting time;
        b. Transition from supervised to unsupervised parenting time;
        c. Contempt findings and sanctions;
        d. All pending motions in this matter.

    15. This hearing shall be scheduled within fourteen (14) days
of the entry of this Order.


                 IX. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

    16. Defendant is WARNED that any further denial, interference
with, or obstruction of Plaintiff's parenting time — including
coaching, alienating statements, or permitting third-party
interference — may result in:

        a. Contempt of court with potential jail sanctions;
        b. Mandatory fines under MCL 552.644(6) ($250 first
           offense, $500 second, $1,000 each subsequent);
        c. Payment of Plaintiff's costs and expenses
           (MCL 552.644(8));
        d. Modification of the custody arrangement;
        e. License suspension under MCL 552.645.

    17. Both parties shall comply with this Order in all respects.
The Friend of the Court is authorized to monitor compliance and
to bring any violations to the Court's attention.


                  X. COSTS AND EXPENSES

    18. Defendant shall pay Plaintiff's reasonable costs and
expenses incurred in bringing this emergency motion, including
filing fees, service costs, and copying expenses, in the amount
of $__________, to be paid within thirty (30) days.


                  XI. ADDITIONAL PROVISIONS

    19. This Order is effective immediately upon entry and shall
remain in effect until further order of this Court.

    20. This Order is without prejudice to Plaintiff's right to
seek additional relief, including modification of custody, at the
evidentiary hearing.

    21. The Friend of the Court shall serve a copy of this Order
upon both parties.


IT IS SO ORDERED.
