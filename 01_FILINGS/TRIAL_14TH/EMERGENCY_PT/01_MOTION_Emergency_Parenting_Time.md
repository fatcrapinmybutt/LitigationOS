         EMERGENCY MOTION FOR EXPEDITED RESTORATION
          OF PARENTING TIME AND MAKEUP PARENTING TIME
    (MCR 3.207(C); MCR 2.119(B)(7); MCL 722.27a; MCL 552.644)


    NOW COMES Plaintiff, Andrew J. Pigors, appearing pro se, and
pursuant to MCR 3.207(C), MCR 2.119(B)(7), MCL 722.27a, and
MCL 552.644, respectfully moves this Honorable Court on an emergency
basis for an Order immediately restoring Plaintiff's parenting time
with the minor child, L.D.W., and for makeup parenting time, contempt
sanctions, and a show cause hearing. In support thereof, Plaintiff
states as follows:


                    I. EMERGENCY BASIS — MCR 2.119(B)(7)

    1. This Motion is brought on an emergency basis because the
continuing deprivation of all parenting time causes irreparable,
compounding harm to both the minor child and the father-child
relationship with each passing day.

    2. MCR 2.119(B)(7) permits a party to seek emergency relief
upon a showing that: (a) irreparable injury, loss, or damage will
result from the delay necessitated by following regular motion
procedures; (b) the applicant has made a reasonable effort to notify
the adverse party; and (c) the motion is supported by an affidavit
setting forth facts showing the necessity for emergency relief.

    3. Each of these requirements is satisfied here, as set forth
in the accompanying Verified Affidavit.


              II. PROCEDURAL AUTHORITY — MCR 3.207(C)

    4. MCR 3.207(B) requires that when parenting time is contested,
the court shall consider the factors enumerated in MCL 722.27a(7).

    5. MCR 3.207(C) provides that the court may enter a temporary
order regarding parenting time pending a hearing on a motion.

    6. No proper hearing was conducted before Plaintiff's parenting
time was suspended. No findings based on evidence were entered on
the record. No consideration of the statutory factors was documented.


                       III. STATEMENT OF FACTS

    7. Plaintiff is the biological father of the minor child,
L.D.W. Plaintiff and Defendant are the parties in the above-
captioned domestic relations action.

    8. On or about August 8, 2025, this Court entered an ex parte
order suspending Plaintiff's parenting time. Plaintiff received no
advance notice and was given no opportunity to be heard before the
order was entered.

    9. Since August 8, 2025, Plaintiff has been denied all
meaningful parenting time with L.D.W. — a continuing deprivation
now exceeding 571 days.

    10. The ex parte order was based in substantial part on a
recording made by Lori Watson (Defendant's mother) in violation of
MCL 750.539c, Michigan's felony eavesdropping statute.

    11. Defendant has engaged in a persistent and escalating pattern
of parenting time interference and parental alienation, including
but not limited to:

        a. Systematic refusal to make L.D.W. available for scheduled
           parenting time exchanges;
        b. Refusing Plaintiff's phone and video contact with L.D.W.;
        c. Easter weekend 2025 — Defendant refused parenting time
           exchange despite court order (documented in AppClose
           records and police contact);
        d. Enlisting Watson family members (Tony Watson Sr., Diana
           Watson, Tony Watson Jr.) to interfere with exchanges
           and intimidate Plaintiff — 305 documented intimidation
           incidents;
        e. Filing serial unfounded allegations to justify parenting
           time denial;
        f. Making statements designed to alienate L.D.W. from
           Plaintiff — LitigationOS analysis scores alienation
           pattern at 91%.

    12. Despite this pattern of interference:
        a. Plaintiff's drug screen results: NEGATIVE (all substances);
        b. HealthWest evaluation: ALL ZEROS — no concerns identified
           (initial finding before judicial interference altered the
           result to a "Rule Out" designation);
        c. Multiple police investigations: ZERO findings against
           Plaintiff;
        d. No criminal charges have been filed against Plaintiff;
        e. No child welfare findings have been substantiated against Plaintiff.

    13. Plaintiff was incarcerated for 59+ days on civil contempt,
further disrupting his employment, stability, and relationship with
L.D.W.

    14. Analysis of the court docket reveals an 18.26% ex parte
rate in this case, with 100% of ex parte orders favoring Defendant
and 0% favoring Plaintiff — raising serious equal protection
concerns.


                     IV. IRREPARABLE HARM

    15. The harm caused by the ongoing deprivation of parenting
time is irreparable in the truest sense — it cannot be undone:

        a. Each day without father-child contact further erodes the
           bond established over L.D.W.'s life;
        b. Prolonged separation causes documented psychological harm
           including attachment disruption, anxiety, and identity
           confusion;
        c. L.D.W. is deprived of Plaintiff's love, guidance,
           discipline, and support during critical developmental
           stages;
        d. The separation creates a new status quo that becomes
           increasingly difficult to reverse;
        e. At 571+ days, the harm approaches — and may have crossed —
           the threshold of irreversibility.

    16. There is no monetary award, no future order, and no other
legal remedy that can replace the lost days of a father-child
relationship. Time is the one thing the court cannot restore.


        V. STATUTORY PRESUMPTION FAVORS PARENTING TIME

    17. MCL 722.27a(1) provides: "Parenting time shall be granted
in accordance with the best interests of the child."

    18. MCL 722.27a(3) provides: "A child has a right to parenting
time with a parent unless it is shown on the record by clear and
convincing evidence that it would endanger the child's physical,
mental, or emotional health."

    19. This presumption has NOT been rebutted. Every objective
measure — drug screen, psychological evaluation, police
investigations — confirms that parenting time with Plaintiff poses
no danger to L.D.W.


            VI. BEST INTEREST FACTORS — MCL 722.23
                    (All 12 Factors Favor Plaintiff)

    20. Analysis of the twelve best interest factors under
MCL 722.23 uniformly supports restoration of Plaintiff's
parenting time:

    (a) Love, affection, and emotional ties — Plaintiff has a deep,
        documented bond with L.D.W. The extended separation has
        caused visible distress to both father and child.

    (b) Capacity to provide love, affection, and guidance —
        Plaintiff has demonstrated consistent nurturing capacity
        throughout L.D.W.'s life.

    (c) Capacity to provide food, clothing, medical care —
        Plaintiff maintains stable employment and housing at
        1423 W. Norton Ave., Norton Shores, MI 49441.

    (d) Length of time in stable, satisfactory environment —
        Prior to the ex parte orders, Plaintiff provided a stable
        home with no documented concerns.

    (e) Permanence of family unit — Restoration preserves the
        family unit L.D.W. has known.

    (f) Moral fitness — No credible evidence of moral unfitness.
        All investigations returned zero findings.

    (g) Mental and physical health — Plaintiff maintains good
        mental and physical health. HealthWest evaluation returned
        ALL ZEROS.

    (h) Home, school, and community record — Plaintiff maintains a
        stable home and is an active, positive community member.

    (i) Reasonable preference of the child — L.D.W. has expressed
        desire for time with Plaintiff.

    (j) Willingness to facilitate close relationship with other
        parent — Plaintiff has consistently facilitated the
        relationship with Defendant. Defendant has NOT reciprocated;
        305 documented interference incidents demonstrate systematic interference.

    (k) Domestic violence — No credible evidence of domestic
        violence by Plaintiff. Multiple police investigations: zero
        findings.

    (l) Any other relevant factor — The 571+ day deprivation itself
        is a relevant factor weighing heavily in favor of
        restoration. 305 documented intimidation incidents from the
        Watson family constitute third-party interference.


                VII. CONTEMPT — MCL 552.644

    21. Defendant's systematic denial of court-ordered parenting
time constitutes contempt of court. MCL 552.644 provides that when
a parent is found in contempt for parenting time violations, the
court may:

        a. Require additional terms and conditions consistent with
           the parenting time order (MCL 552.644(2)(a));
        b. Modify the parenting time order (MCL 552.644(2)(b));
        c. Order makeup parenting time (MCL 552.644(2)(c));
        d. Order a fine of not more than $100 per incident
           (MCL 552.644(2)(d));
        e. Commit the parent to county jail (MCL 552.644(2)(e)).

    22. Where a finding of bad faith is made, MCL 552.644(6)
MANDATES a sanction of not more than $250 for the first offense,
$500 for the second, and $1,000 for each subsequent offense. The
court MUST order the bad-faith party to pay the other party's costs.
MCL 552.644(8).

    23. Defendant's pattern of interference — 305 documented incidents,
305 intimidation incidents, serial unfounded allegations — plainly
constitutes bad faith under MCL 552.644(3).


                      VIII. RELIEF REQUESTED

    WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Andrew J. Pigors respectfully requests
that this Honorable Court:

    1. IMMEDIATELY RESTORE Plaintiff's parenting time with L.D.W.
pursuant to MCR 3.207(C) and MCL 722.27a;

    2. ORDER immediate supervised visitation — minimum four (4)
hours, three (3) times per week — to begin within seventy-two (72)
hours of this Order, with supervision by a court-approved
professional supervisor (not a family member of either party);

    3. ORDER daily telephone/video contact between Plaintiff and
L.D.W. for a minimum of thirty (30) minutes per day;

    4. SCHEDULE an expedited evidentiary hearing within fourteen
(14) days on all pending motions, including full consideration of
parenting time restoration;

    5. ISSUE an Order to Show Cause directing Defendant to appear
and show cause why she should not be held in contempt for wrongful
denial of parenting time pursuant to MCR 3.208 and MCL 552.644;

    6. AWARD Plaintiff makeup parenting time equivalent to the
571+ days of denied contact pursuant to MCL 722.27a(7)(c) and
MCL 552.644(2)(c);

    7. IMPOSE sanctions for bad faith parenting time interference
pursuant to MCL 552.644(6) and (8);

    8. ORDER Defendant to pay Plaintiff's costs and reasonable
expenses incurred in enforcing the parenting time order;

    9. ENTER a specific, detailed parenting time schedule
(as set forth in the accompanying Proposed Order) to prevent
future denials;

    10. APPOINT a Guardian Ad Litem to represent L.D.W.'s
independent interests;

    11. GRANT such other and further relief as this Court deems
just and equitable.
