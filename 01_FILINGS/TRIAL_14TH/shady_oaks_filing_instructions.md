## C. Fee Waiver (MC 20 -- Fee Waiver Request)

If filing fees are a barrier:

1. **Obtain Form MC 20** (Fee Waiver Request) from the court clerk or Michigan Courts website
2. Complete all sections showing:
   - Current income (if any)
   - Monthly expenses
   - Assets and liabilities
   - Number of dependents
   - Receipt of public assistance (if any -- Medicaid, food assistance, SSI)
3. File MC 20 with the complaint or motion
4. If receiving any form of public assistance, fee waiver is typically granted automatically
5. If not receiving assistance, the court evaluates ability to pay

**Key Points:**
- Plaintiff's current housing instability and homelessness strongly supports a fee waiver
- Loss of employment due to Defendants' conduct further supports inability to pay
- The court cannot deny access to justice based on inability to pay (Boddie v. Connecticut, 401 U.S. 371 (1971))

## B. Methods of Service

### Personal Service (MCR 2.105(A))
- **Most reliable method**
- Hire a process server ($50-$100 per defendant)
- Process server delivers summons + complaint to defendant personally
- File Proof of Service (MC 04) with the court

### Certified Mail (MCR 2.105(A)(2))
- Send by certified mail, return receipt requested
- Must be restricted delivery (to named addressee only)
- Keep green card (return receipt) as proof
- File Proof of Service with return receipt attached

### Michigan Secretary of State Service (for out-of-state entities)
- MCL 600.715 -- long-arm jurisdiction
- File two copies of summons + complaint with MI SOS
- SOS forwards to defendant
- Pay SOS service fee ($10)

# VI. CLASS ACTION POTENTIAL ANALYSIS

## A. Potential Class Definition

"All current and former residents of Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community who, since March 05, 2026, have been subjected to unauthorized rent increases, unauthorized fees, ledger manipulation, blocked home sales, or retaliatory eviction practices by Defendants."

## B. Rule 23 / MCR 3.501 Analysis

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Numerosity** | Shady Oaks has 2024-001507-DC lots -- if a substantial percentage affected, satisfied |
| **Commonality** | Common questions: ledger practices, fee structures, sales policies, entity fraud |
| **Typicality** | Plaintiff's claims are typical of class if other residents faced similar treatment |
| **Adequacy** | Pro se plaintiff may face adequacy challenges -- consider co-counsel |
| **Predominance** | Common questions likely predominate over individual issues |
| **Superiority** | Class action superior to individual suits for pattern claims |

## C. Class Action Considerations

**Advantages:**
- Multiplies damages across all affected residents
- Greater leverage against corporate defendants
- Attracts attorney interest (contingency fee on larger pool)
- Addresses systemic practices, not just individual harm
- Greater media attention and regulatory interest

**Challenges:**
- Pro se plaintiff typically cannot represent a class (MCR 3.501 may require counsel)
- Certification process is complex and resource-intensive
- May slow individual claims
- Defendants will vigorously oppose certification

**Recommendation:** File individual complaint NOW; explore class certification later with co-counsel or after attracting attorney interest through individual case success.

## D. How to Attract Attorney Interest

1. File the Mega-Complaint as drafted (shows sophistication of claims)
2. Win early motions (motion to amend, discovery motions)
3. Obtain key evidence (ledger fraud, Partridge check, manager texts)
4. Contact:
   - Michigan Legal Services
   - Michigan Poverty Law Program
   - National Consumer Law Center
   - Michigan mobile home advocacy organizations
   - Private attorneys who handle manufactured housing cases
5. RICO treble damages and conversion treble damages create strong contingency-fee incentive
