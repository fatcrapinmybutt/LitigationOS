## 1. OVERVIEW OF SERVICE REQUIREMENTS {#1-overview}

### Governing Rules

| Rule | Application |
|------|-------------|
| **MCR 2.105(A)** | Individuals — personal hand delivery |
| **MCR 2.105(D)** | LLCs and corporations — serve registered agent, officer, or authorized agent |
| **MCR 2.105(E)** | Partnerships/unincorporated entities — serve a partner or managing agent |
| **MCR 2.105(I)** | Dissolved entities — serve last known officer, director, or registered agent |
| **MCR 2.105(J)** | Alternative/substituted service — by court order when personal service fails |
| **MCL 450.4209** | Michigan LLC registered agent requirements |
| **MCL 450.4802** | Dissolved LLC — winding up and service during dissolution |
| **MCL 600.705** | Personal jurisdiction — individuals |
| **MCL 600.715** | Personal jurisdiction — corporations and LLCs |

### General Requirements

1. **Who may serve:** Any person 18+ who is NOT a party (MCR 2.103(A))
2. **Time limit:** Summons expires **91 days** after issuance (MCR 2.102(D)) — request reissue if expired
3. **What to serve:** Summons + copy of the Complaint (all 22 counts)
4. **Proof of service:** File affidavit/proof of service with the Court within the time for service
5. **Number of defendants:** 8 — each requires separate service

## 4. ALTERNATIVE SERVICE PROCEDURES {#4-alternative-service}

### When Alternative Service Is Needed

Alternative service under **MCR 2.105(J)** is required when:
- Personal service cannot be completed after diligent attempts
- Registered agent cannot be located or refuses service
- Entity is dissolved and no agent/officer can be found

### Motion for Alternative Service — Requirements

1. **File a motion** with the Court requesting permission for substituted service
2. **Attach an affidavit** establishing:
   - All attempts at personal service (dates, times, locations, results)
   - Steps taken to locate the defendant (LARA search, skip tracing, etc.)
   - Why personal service is impracticable
3. **Propose a method** of alternative service:
   - **Service by mail** (certified mail, return receipt requested)
   - **Service by posting** (tacking at last known address + mailing)
   - **Service by publication** (if defendant cannot be found at all)
   - **Service by email** (rare — must demonstrate defendant actively uses the email)
4. **Court must approve** the alternative method before it is used

### Service by Publication (Last Resort)

- Governed by **MCR 2.106**
- Requires publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the county
- Must publish once each week for 3 consecutive weeks
- Cost: approximately $150–$400 depending on newspaper
- **Only use when all other methods have been exhausted**

## 7. COMMON PITFALLS {#7-pitfalls}

1. **Serving the wrong person at an LLC** — Must serve registered agent, officer, or authorized
   agent. Serving a receptionist or random employee is NOT valid under MCR 2.105(D).

2. **Expired summons** — If 91 days pass without service, must request reissuance. Service with
   an expired summons is VOID.

3. **No proof of service filed** — Cannot obtain default judgment without filed proof of service.

4. **Dissolved LLC confusion** — A dissolved LLC can still be sued under MCL 450.4802. It continues
   to exist for winding-up purposes. Do NOT assume dissolution bars the claim.

5. **Out-of-state service without jurisdiction basis** — Must establish Michigan long-arm jurisdiction
   under MCL 600.705/715 before serving out-of-state defendants.

6. **Serving "Shady Oaks" at the park address** — The park address is NOT necessarily the registered
   agent address. Must serve the proper agent/officer, not just leave papers at the park office.

7. **Individual defendants — leaving with a housemate** — MCR 2.105(A) requires personal hand delivery
   to the named individual. Leaving with another person at the residence is NOT valid personal service
   (it may support a motion for alternative service, but is not service itself).
