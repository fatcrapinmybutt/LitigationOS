# ORDER RESTORING PARENTING TIME

At a session of said Court held in the Muskegon County Courthouse, 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, Michigan 49442, on the _____ day of _________________, 2026.

**PRESENT: HON. JENNY L. McNEILL, Circuit Court Judge**

---

This matter having come before the Court on Plaintiff's Emergency Motion to Restore Parenting Time, and the Court having reviewed the motion, supporting affidavit, exhibits, and the record in this case, and the Court being otherwise fully advised in the premises;

**THE COURT FINDS:**

A. Plaintiff-Father Andrew J. Pigors has been separated from the minor child, L.D.W. (DOB: November 9, 2022), for in excess of 329 days without a full evidentiary hearing on the merits of the parenting time suspension.

B. The ex parte orders entered August 8, 2025 suspended all parenting time without findings of endangerment as required by MCL 722.27a(3).

C. Continued deprivation of parenting time causes irreparable harm to both the minor child and Plaintiff-Father.

D. MCL 722.27a(1) establishes a statutory presumption that parenting time is in the best interests of the child.

E. A prompt hearing on this matter is necessary to protect the rights of both the minor child and Plaintiff-Father.

---

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

**1.** The ex parte orders entered on August 8, 2025, suspending Plaintiff's parenting time are hereby **VACATED**.

**2.** Plaintiff-Father Andrew J. Pigors shall have parenting time with the minor child, L.D.W. (DOB: November 9, 2022), on the following schedule, effective immediately:

   a. **Supervised parenting time** at a neutral location approved by the Friend of the Court, for a minimum of two (2) hours, twice per week, pending further order of this Court;

   b. Within thirty (30) days of the date of this Order, the parenting time schedule shall transition to **unsupervised parenting time** consistent with the schedule established in the Final Judgment of Custody and Parenting Time entered August 23, 2024, including alternating weekends (Friday 6:00 PM to Sunday 6:00 PM) and one weekday evening (Wednesday 4:00 PM to 7:00 PM), unless cause is shown at an evidentiary hearing;

   c. Plaintiff's **holiday parenting time** shall be restored per the existing parenting time order.

**3.** Defendant Emily A. Watson (fka Pigors) **shall not** interfere with, obstruct, or deny Plaintiff's parenting time as established by this Order. Any interference with parenting time may constitute a violation of MCL 722.27a and may be grounds for contempt proceedings under MCL 600.1701 and MCR 3.606.

**4.** **Makeup parenting time** is hereby granted to Plaintiff pursuant to MCL 722.27a(7)(a) to compensate for the parenting time wrongfully denied since the entry of the ex parte orders on August 8, 2025. The specific makeup schedule shall be determined by the Friend of the Court or by further order of this Court, and shall include additional weekends and extended holiday time.

**5.** The **Friend of the Court** shall monitor and enforce this Order. The Friend of the Court is directed to facilitate the transition from supervised to unsupervised parenting time and to report to this Court any violations of this Order by either party.

**6.** A **full evidentiary hearing** on all issues of custody and parenting time shall be scheduled within fourteen (14) days of the date of this Order.

**7.** Violation of any provision of this Order may be punishable as **contempt of court** pursuant to MCL 600.1701 and MCR 3.606, including but not limited to fines, costs, attorney fees, and/or incarceration.

**8.** This Order is effective **immediately** upon entry and shall remain in effect until further order of this Court.

---

IT IS SO ORDERED.
