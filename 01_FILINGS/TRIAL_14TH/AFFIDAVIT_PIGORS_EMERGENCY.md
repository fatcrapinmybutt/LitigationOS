## II. LAST PHYSICAL CONTACT WITH CHILD (¶¶ 4–6)

**4.** My last physical contact with my son, L.D.W., occurred on **July 29, 2025**. On that date, I exercised parenting time that had been scheduled. Since that date, I have had **ZERO** contact of any kind with my child — no physical contact, no telephone calls, no video calls, no written communication.

**5.** As of the date of this Affidavit (February 28, 2026), I have been forcibly separated from my three-year-old son for **214 consecutive days** since my last physical contact on July 29, 2025, and in excess of **329 cumulative days** of parent-child separation since the inception of this litigation.

**6.** During this 214-day period since last contact, L.D.W. has passed through critical developmental milestones without his father's presence, including his **third birthday** (November 9, 2025), Thanksgiving 2025, Christmas 2025, New Year's 2026, and Valentine's Day 2026. These milestones are irrecoverable.

## III. THE EX PARTE ORDERS AND SEPARATION (¶¶ 7–12)

**7.** On or about August 7, 2025, Defendant Emily A. Watson filed an Emergency Ex Parte Motion to Suspend my parenting time. This motion was filed **without prior notice to me** and without affording me any opportunity to be heard.

**8.** On **August 8, 2025**, this Court entered **five (5) separate ex parte orders** on a single day, suspending all of my parenting time with L.D.W. I was not served with these orders before they took effect. I discovered the suspension of my parenting time only after arriving at an exchange point on August 8, 2025, expecting to exercise my regularly scheduled parenting time, and being unable to pick up my child.

**9.** The ex parte motion filed by Defendant was **not verified under oath** — it contained no notarization, no jurat, and no sworn affirmation of truth, in violation of MCR 3.207(B)(1).

**10.** The ex parte motion relied on inadmissible double hearsay: an unnamed "officer" who allegedly "believed this was a result of meth use" and an unnamed "friend" who was allegedly "concerned" about an "eviction." Neither allegation was supported by a sworn affidavit from any percipient witness.

**11.** I submitted to the narcotics screening referenced in the ex parte motion. **My narcotics screen was NEGATIVE.** This directly contradicts the sole substantive allegation supporting the suspension of my parenting time.

**12.** The HealthWest mental health assessment — ordered by this Court and received on September 12, 2025 — found that I "report[] no issues with employment," "report[] no issues with others with the exception of [my] ex," and "ha[ve] support of friends." These clinical findings further contradict the ex parte motion's portrayal of me as a danger to L.D.W.

## IV. ATTEMPTS TO CONTACT AND VISIT CHILD (¶¶ 13–17)

**13.** Since the entry of the ex parte orders on August 8, 2025, I have made every effort within the legal system to restore contact with my son. I have filed the following motions, all of which were denied:
   - Motion to Restore Parenting Time (January 15, 2025);
   - Motion for Joint Custody and equal parenting time (February 14, 2025);
   - Objection to the Ex Parte Order suspending parenting time;
   - Multiple requests for hearing dates.

**14.** I appeared at every hearing scheduled by this Court, including:
   - The evidentiary hearing on September 4, 2025, which was adjourned for a mental health assessment;
   - The continued hearing on October 29, 2025;
   - The hearing on November 26, 2025, at which the Court denied my objection to the ex parte order.

**15.** At no point during these proceedings was any interim parenting time restored, even on a supervised basis with conditions.

**16.** At the November 26, 2025 hearing, the Court stated that it had "offered supervised parenting time to the Father and he categorically rejected that." This characterization is inaccurate. My position, as stated on the record, was: "there is no need for suspension, and I am not any danger to my son in any way, shape, or form." I did not refuse to see my son; I objected to the legal and factual basis for imposing supervision absent any evidence of danger. This distinction is critical: I challenged the Court's authority to condition parenting time on unfounded safety concerns, not my desire to see my child.

**17.** I have exhausted every available remedy at the trial court level. I filed a Claim of Appeal with the Michigan Court of Appeals (COA Docket No. 366810) on June 1, 2025, and filed a Judicial Misconduct Complaint with the Judicial Tenure Commission on September 1, 2025. Despite these efforts, no parenting time has been restored.

This employment provides Defendant with specialized knowledge of family court procedures, judicial tendencies, and professional relationships with individuals who may influence proceedings in this case.

## VI. IMPACT ON CHILD (¶¶ 22–25)

**22.** L.D.W. is three (3) years old. The period between ages two and four is the critical window for secure attachment formation. The forced separation of L.D.W. from his father during this developmental window is causing harm to his attachment security, emotional regulation, and sense of identity.

**23.** Prior to the suspension, L.D.W. and I had a strong, healthy, loving bond. This Court itself found at the July 17, 2024 custody trial that "both parents have a strong bond with the minor child" and that "both parties are capable of providing the minor child with love, affection, and guidance." The Court found Factors (a) and (b) under MCL 722.23 to be "equal between the parties."

**24.** I maintained over **100 gigabytes** of photographs documenting L.D.W.'s healthy, happy time in my care — evidence I attempted to present to this Court but which was refused. These photographs show a child who is loved, well-cared-for, and thriving in his father's care.

**25.** At age three, L.D.W. is approaching the developmental threshold beyond which attachment disruptions become significantly more difficult to remediate. Every additional day of separation deepens the harm and makes recovery of the father-child bond more difficult. The urgency of restoring contact cannot be overstated.

The evidentiary record uniformly supports my fitness as a parent.

## VIII. PATTERN OF ALIENATION UNDER MCL 722.23(j) (¶¶ 30–33)

**30.** The record establishes a persistent pattern of conduct by Defendant consistent with parental alienation, as recognized under MCL 722.23(j) — "the willingness and ability of each of the parties to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent."

**31.** Defendant's conduct demonstrates a deliberate, sustained effort to destroy the father-child bond:
   - Filed an unsworn ex parte motion seeking total suspension of all parenting time;
   - Made no effort to propose intermediate remedies;
   - Withheld L.D.W. during holidays and scheduled parenting time on multiple occasions;
   - Has maintained the complete separation for 329+ days without offering any alternative contact;
   - Leveraged her institutional knowledge as a family court caseworker to navigate the legal system to Father's disadvantage.

**32.** *Harvey v Harvey*, 470 Mich 186, 192; 680 NW2d 835 (2004), recognized Factor (j) as "an important consideration" in custody disputes. Defendant's conduct is the antithesis of the willingness to facilitate the parent-child relationship that Factor (j) requires.

**33.** The court has applied zero (0) of the five (5) available remedies under MCL 722.27a(9) for parenting time violations — including make-up time (§9(a)), modification of custody (§9(c)), civil contempt (§9(b)), costs (§9(d)), and additional conditions (§9(e)). The statutory word "shall" is mandatory. *Walters v. Nadell*, 481 Mich 377, 383 (2008).

## IX. URGENCY — EVERY DAY CAUSES FURTHER HARM (¶¶ 34–36)

**34.** Every additional day of separation between a three-year-old child and his bonded parent causes further, irremediable harm to the attachment bond, emotional development, and sense of security. This harm is cumulative and accelerating. At L.D.W.'s age, every week is a developmental epoch.

**35.** The cumulative effect of 329+ days of separation may already be causing L.D.W. to question or forget his relationship with his father. The longer this separation continues, the more difficult it will be to restore the bond — and the more Defendant's position is strengthened by the passage of time itself.

**36.** MCL 722.27a(1) establishes a statutory presumption that "it is in the best interests of a child for the child to have a strong relationship with both of his or her parents." The current state of this case — 329+ days of total separation imposed without an evidentiary hearing on the merits — is a direct and ongoing violation of this statutory presumption and of L.D.W.'s right to know and love his father.

---

## CONCLUSION

**37.** The facts set forth in this Affidavit demonstrate that: (a) Father has had zero contact with his three-year-old son for 214 consecutive days since July 29, 2025, and 329+ cumulative days of separation; (b) the ex parte orders were entered without proper verification, without adequate evidence, and without the required findings; (c) Father's drug screen was negative and his mental health assessment was favorable; (d) Father has exhausted all available remedies; and (e) every day of continued separation causes irremediable harm to both Father and child.

**38.** I respectfully request that this Court grant the Emergency Motion to Restore Parenting Time, vacate the August 8, 2025 ex parte orders, and restore Father's parenting time immediately, at minimum on a supervised basis pending a full evidentiary hearing.
