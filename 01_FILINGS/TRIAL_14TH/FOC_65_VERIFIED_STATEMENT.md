## 4. REASON FOR MODIFICATION — PROPER CAUSE / CHANGE OF CIRCUMSTANCES

Plaintiff-Father seeks modification of the current parenting time arrangement (complete suspension) for the following reasons constituting proper cause and/or change of circumstances:

a. The ex parte orders of August 8, 2025 were entered based on an unsworn motion containing inadmissible hearsay, in violation of MCR 3.207(B)(1).

b. No finding of endangerment was made as required by MCL 722.27a(3) before suspending all parenting time.

c. No findings were made on any of the twelve MCL 722.23 best interest factors, in violation of *Pierron v. Pierron*, 486 Mich 81 (2010).

d. Plaintiff-Father's narcotics screening returned NEGATIVE, affirmatively disproving the primary allegation supporting the suspension.

e. Plaintiff-Father has been separated from the minor child for 571+ cumulative days, causing ongoing irreparable harm to the parent-child bond during a critical developmental period.

f. No evidentiary hearing on the merits of the suspension has been conducted since the ex parte orders were entered on August 8, 2025, in violation of MCR 3.206(B)(6).

g. The statutory presumption of MCL 722.27a(1) — that it is in the best interests of a child to have a strong relationship with both parents — has not been rebutted.

h. Less restrictive alternatives to total suspension were available and were not considered. *See Shade v. Wright*, 291 Mich App 17 (2010).
