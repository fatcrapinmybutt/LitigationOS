## II. LEGAL STANDARD FOR CIVIL CONTEMPT IN MICHIGAN

### A. Four Elements of Civil Contempt

Under Michigan law, civil contempt is established when the moving party demonstrates:

> **(1)** A court order existed that was clear and unambiguous;
>
> **(2)** The contemnor had knowledge of the order;
>
> **(3)** The contemnor violated the order; and
>
> **(4)** The violation was willful.

*See* *Sword v Sword*, 399 Mich 367, 381-82 (1976); *In re Contempt of Henry*, 282 Mich App 656, 668 (2009).

### B. The Purpose of Civil Contempt

Civil contempt is **coercive, not punitive**; its purpose is to compel compliance with the court's order. The contemnor "carries the keys to the jail in [her] own pocket"—she can purge the contempt by complying with the court's order. *In re Contempt of Henry*, 282 Mich App at 668.

### C. Burden of Proof

The movant must establish contempt by a preponderance of the evidence. Once the movant shows a prima facie case of violation, the burden shifts to the alleged contemnor to show that the violation was not willful or that compliance was impossible. *In re Contempt of Henry*, 282 Mich App at 669.

### D. Parenting Time Enforcement Statute

MCL 722.27a(7) provides:

> "If a court finds that parenting time has been wrongfully denied, the court **shall** order that additional parenting time be provided to make up for the parenting time that was denied."

This provision is **mandatory**, not discretionary. *DeRose v DeRose*, 469 Mich 320, 332-33 (2003). The Legislature's use of "shall" imposes an affirmative obligation on the court to award make-up parenting time when wrongful denial is established.

## VI. PREEMPTIVE RESPONSE TO ANTICIPATED COUNTERARGUMENTS

### 1. "Mother Was Following the Ex Parte Order"

**Anticipated Defense:** Mother complied with the August 8, 2025 ex parte order suspending parenting time, so her denial of parenting time after that date is not contemptuous.

**Rebuttal:** This defense fails because (a) the ex parte order was obtained through fraud (Albert Watson admitted to police the report was filed specifically to obtain the order — NSPD Case NS2505044), and compliance with a fraudulently obtained order does not excuse contempt; (b) it does not excuse the three prior episodes of denial (March–May 2024: 39 days; various 2024: 27 days; May–July 2025: 50 days), all of which occurred before the August 8, 2025 order; and (c) Mother had an independent obligation to facilitate parenting time under MCL 722.27a, which she never attempted to do. *See Rains v Rains*, 301 Mich App 313, 328 (2013) (party may not create conditions that prevent compliance and then claim impossibility).

### 2. "Father Was Arrested, So He Is Dangerous"

**Anticipated Defense:** Father's arrest and jailing for contempt shows he is dangerous to the child.

**Rebuttal:** Father was jailed for sending a message through the co-parenting app asking to see his son. Seven police investigations found zero evidence of violence, threats, or danger. HealthWest's first clinical evaluation cleared Father with Psychosis = 0, Substance Abuse = 0, Danger to Self/Others = 0, LOCUS 12 (Level One — routine outpatient). An arrest caused by the very party now resisting enforcement cannot be used as justification for continued denial. *See In re Contempt of Dorsey*, 306 Mich App 571, 585 (2014) (party cannot benefit from own wrongful conduct).

### 3. "Father Is Vexatious / Filing Too Many Motions"

**Anticipated Defense:** Father's multiple filings show he is harassing the court and Mother.

**Rebuttal:** No vexatious-litigant finding has been entered under MCL 600.5701. Every filing addresses the ongoing denial of Father's fundamental constitutional rights. A parent facing 329+ days of separation from his child and 27+ unenforced violations has not only the right but the obligation to seek judicial relief. *See Boddie v Connecticut*, 401 U.S. 371, 374 (1971) (access to courts is a fundamental right); *In re Hague*, 412 Mich 532 (1982) (filing motions is a constitutionally protected activity).

### 4. "Make-Up Time Is Impractical After This Long"

**Anticipated Defense:** The denied time is too great for make-up time to be meaningful.

**Rebuttal:** The Legislature made make-up time mandatory ("shall"), not discretionary, through MCL 722.27a(7). *DeRose v DeRose*, 469 Mich 320, 332–33 (2003). The impracticality of restoring 310+ hours is itself the strongest argument for immediate and comprehensive enforcement, not a reason to reward continued denial.
