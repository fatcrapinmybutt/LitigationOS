## V. LEGAL STANDARD

Under MCL 722.23, the court must consider each of the twelve enumerated factors 
when determining the best interests of the child. *Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 
259 Mich App 499 (2003). No single factor is dispositive, but the court must 
make specific findings on each factor. *Fletcher v Fletcher*, 447 Mich 871 (1994).

Where an established custodial environment exists, the party seeking to change 
custody bears the burden of proving by clear and convincing evidence that the 
change is in the child's best interests. MCL 722.27(1)(c); *Pierron v Pierron*, 
486 Mich 81 (2010).
