## FILING PROCEDURES

### Option A: In-Person Filing (Recommended for Emergency)

1. **Location**:  
   14th Circuit Court  
   Muskegon County Hall of Justice  
   990 Terrace Street  
   Muskegon, MI 49442

2. **Bring with you**:
   - All motions (originals + copies)
   - All proposed orders (originals + copies)
   - All exhibit binders
   - Photo ID
   - Filing fees (if applicable - may be waived if indigent)
   - Check or money order (court may not accept cash or credit)

3. **At the clerk's window**:
   - Request emergency filing/scheduling
   - Ask clerk to file-stamp all your copies
   - Request hearing dates (emphasize emergency nature)
   - Ask for judge's availability for emergency hearing
   - Get case assignment information

4. **Immediately after filing**:
   - Serve opposing party/attorney per Certificate of Service
   - Complete Certificate of Service with date and method
   - File completed Certificate of Service with court (within 24 hours)

### Option B: E-Filing (If Available in 14th Circuit)

Check with court clerk whether e-filing is available for your case type.

If e-filing:
- Upload PDF versions of all documents
- Still serve opposing party separately
- File Certificate of Service after service

### Filing Fees

- **Motion filing fees**: Check with clerk (typically $20-80 per motion)
- **Fee waiver**: If you cannot afford fees, file "Motion for Waiver of Fees" or "Affidavit of Indigency"

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

### ❌ Don't Do This:

1. **Don't file without notarization** - Verifications MUST be notarized
2. **Don't skip service** - Motions won't be heard without proof of service
3. **Don't miss placeholders** - Fill in ALL bracketed information
4. **Don't exaggerate** - Stick to facts you can prove with evidence
5. **Don't file angry** - Keep tone professional and respectful
6. **Don't ignore deadlines** - Track all response and hearing deadlines
7. **Don't go alone** - Consider consulting with attorney even if pro se
8. **Don't surprise opposing party** - Follow all notice requirements

### ✅ Do This:

1. **Triple-check all information** before filing
2. **Keep multiple copies** of everything
3. **Document everything** - dates, times, communications
4. **Stay organized** - Use binders, tabs, labels
5. **Be professional** - With court staff, judge, opposing counsel
6. **Follow court rules** - MCR compliance is critical
7. **Meet deadlines** - File on time, every time
8. **Seek help** - Free legal aid, law library, court facilitator
