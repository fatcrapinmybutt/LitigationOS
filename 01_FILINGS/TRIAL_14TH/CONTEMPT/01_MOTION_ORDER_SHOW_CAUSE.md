## VI. MANDATORY STATUTORY FRAMEWORK — MCL 552.644

18. MCL 552.644 establishes a **mandatory** enforcement framework that this Court is required to follow:

### A. Mandatory Finding of Violation — MCL 552.644(3)

19. MCL 552.644(3) provides that in a parenting time dispute, the court **"SHALL"** find that parenting time was wrongfully denied if the respondent **fails to prove by a preponderance of the evidence** that she complied with or had good cause for violating the parenting time order.

20. The word "SHALL" is mandatory, not permissive. *Brausch v. Brausch*, 283 Mich App 339, 348 (2009) (holding that the statutory language in the parenting time enforcement provisions is mandatory). Defendant **cannot** establish compliance or good cause for 571+ days of complete parenting time denial.

### B. Mandatory Sanctions — MCL 552.644(2)

21. Upon a finding of contempt for parenting time violation, MCL 552.644(2) authorizes the court to impose one or more of the following:
    - (a) Additional terms and conditions consistent with the parenting time order
    - (b) Modification of the parenting time order
    - (c) **Makeup parenting time** for wrongfully denied parenting time
    - (d) Fine of not more than $100
    - (e) Commitment to county jail
    - (f) Commitment with work-release privileges
    - (g) Suspension of occupational, driver's, recreational, or sporting license
    - (h) Community corrections participation
    - (i) Supervision with conditions

22. If a finding of contempt is entered and **NO sanctions** are imposed, the court **MUST** state on the record the reason why. MCL 552.644(3).

### C. Mandatory Bad Faith Sanctions — MCL 552.644(6)

23. If the court finds Defendant acted in **bad faith**, the court **"MUST"** order sanctions:
    - First offense: not more than $250
    - Second offense: not more than $500
    - Third or subsequent offense: not more than $1,000

24. MCL 552.644(8) further provides that upon a finding of bad faith, the court **"MUST"** order the offending party to pay the other party's costs.

## VII. COMPENSATORY PARENTING TIME — MCL 552.645

25. MCL 552.645 provides for compensatory makeup parenting time and additional enforcement mechanisms:

   a. If the parent found in contempt fails to comply with makeup and ongoing parenting time, the court **may** suspend any licenses conditioned under MCL 552.644(2)(g).

   b. On entry of a license suspension order, the court **may** order a makeup parenting time schedule if the parent demonstrates good faith compliance. MCL 552.645(2)(a).

   c. If a motion to modify parenting time is timely filed (within 21 days of notice), a consolidated hearing **must** be held unless good cause is shown to keep hearings separate. If separate, the modification hearing **must** precede the contempt proceeding. MCL 552.645(3).
