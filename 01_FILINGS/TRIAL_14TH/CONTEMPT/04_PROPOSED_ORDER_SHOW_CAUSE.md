## 2. NOTICE TO DEFENDANT

Defendant is hereby notified that:

### A. Allegations

Plaintiff alleges that Defendant has willfully violated this Court's parenting time order by denying Plaintiff all parenting time with the minor child L.D.W. for a period exceeding 571 days, including but not limited to:

- Unilateral withholding of the child for 37 consecutive days (March 26 – May 2, 2024)
- Explicit denial of Easter parenting time (April 13–15, 2025)
- Complete prohibition of all contact since approximately August 8, 2025
- Withholding of medical information
- Coordinated Watson family intimidation campaign (305 documented incidents)

### B. Possible Sanctions

If the Court finds Defendant in contempt, the following sanctions may be imposed pursuant to MCL 552.644(2) and MCL 600.1715:

1. **Makeup parenting time** for all wrongfully denied parenting time — MCL 552.644(2)(c)
2. **Fines** — up to $100 per finding under MCL 552.644(2)(d); up to $250/$500/$1,000 for bad faith under MCL 552.644(6); up to $7,500 under MCL 600.1715
3. **Jail commitment** — up to 45 days for first finding, up to 90 days for subsequent findings — MCL 552.644(2)(e), (4)
4. **Jail with work-release privileges** — MCL 552.644(2)(f)
5. **License suspension** (occupational, driver's, recreational, sporting) — MCL 552.644(2)(g)
6. **Community corrections program** — MCL 552.644(2)(h)
7. **Supervised compliance** with conditions — MCL 552.644(2)(i)
8. **Payment of Plaintiff's costs and expenses** — MCL 552.644(8); MCL 600.1721
9. **Additional terms and conditions** — MCL 552.644(2)(a)
10. **Modification of parenting time** — MCL 552.644(2)(b)

### C. Right to Modify Parenting Time

Pursuant to MCL 552.644(1) and MCR 3.208(B)(3), Defendant is notified that she has the right to request a modification of the parenting time schedule within **21 days** after the date of service of this Order. If a timely modification request is filed, a consolidated hearing will be held unless good cause is shown on the record to keep the hearings separate. MCL 552.645(3).

### D. Right to Counsel

Defendant is advised that she has the right to retain legal counsel for the show cause hearing. If Defendant is indigent and faces incarceration, the Court may appoint counsel. *See Mead v. Batchlor*, 453 Mich 480 (1990); *Turner v. Rogers*, 564 U.S. 431 (2011).

### E. Failure to Appear

If Defendant **fails to appear** at the show cause hearing, the Court **MAY**:

- Issue a **bench warrant** requiring Defendant to be brought before the Court — MCL 552.644(5)
- Order law enforcement to render any vehicle owned by Defendant temporarily inoperable — MCL 552.644(9)
- Order Defendant to pay costs of the hearing, bench warrant, arrest, and later hearings — MCL 552.644(7)
