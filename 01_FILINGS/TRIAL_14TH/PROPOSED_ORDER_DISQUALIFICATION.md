## FINDINGS OF FACT

The Court finds as follows:

### I. Procedural History

1. This domestic relations case was filed on January 15, 2024, and has been assigned to Judge Daphne Means McNeill since inception.

2. Over 18 months, Judge McNeill has presided over approximately 47 court appearances and issued 102 orders.

3. Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Disqualify on _____________, 2026, presenting statistical evidence, specific instances, and systematic patterns allegedly demonstrating bias.

### II. Statistical Evidence

4. The Court has reviewed Plaintiff's statistical analysis of all orders issued in this case:

   **Ex Parte Orders:**
   - Mother's requests granted: 24 of 55 (44%)
   - Father's requests granted: 0 of 18 (0%)
   - Disparity: 44 percentage points

   **Motion Success Rates:**
   - Mother's motions granted: 49 of 57 (86%)
   - Father's motions granted: 5 of 45 (11%)
   - Disparity: 75 percentage points

   **Custody Determinations:**
   - Mother's motions granted: 8 of 8 (100%)
   - Father's motions granted: 0 of 12 (0%)
   - Disparity: 100 percentage points

   **Evidence Admission:**
   - Mother's evidence admitted: 156 of 164 (95%)
   - Father's evidence admitted: 42 of 120 (35%)
   - Disparity: 60 percentage points

5. These statistical disparities are extreme and cannot be explained by random chance, differences in the quality of evidence, or legitimate application of legal standards.

### III. Specific Instances

6. The Court finds credible Plaintiff's documentation of specific instances demonstrating disparate treatment in identical or analogous circumstances, including but not limited to:

   - **March 15, 2024**: Father's emergency ex parte request for 7-day makeup time denied; Mother's request to postpone 3-hour visit granted same day
   
   - **May 8, 2024**: Father's police report (documented domestic violence) excluded as "hearsay"; Mother's unsworn statement admitted same hearing
   
   - **August 2024**: Father's parenting time suspended immediately based on unsubstantiated claim; Mother's suspension denied despite medical evidence of child injuries
   
   - **February 2025**: Father's $8,500 forensic evaluation by Board Certified psychologist excluded; Mother's therapist (4 sessions, no forensic training) testimony admitted
   
   - **February 14, 2026**: Father's custody motion (87 exhibits, 200+ page brief) denied after 45-minute hearing; Mother's order adopted verbatim including typos

### IV. Systematic Patterns

7. The Court finds five systematic patterns of disparate treatment:

   **Pattern 1**: Heightened evidentiary standards applied to Father (hearsay objections sustained 89% vs. 12%; authentication required 87% vs. 23%)
   
   **Pattern 2**: Procedural accommodations granted to Mother (continuances 89% vs. 13%; extensions 92% vs. 17%)
   
   **Pattern 3**: Mother's violations excused (49 documented violations, zero consequences; Father's 1 violation resulted in show cause)
   
   **Pattern 4**: Coached child preference given controlling weight (8-year-old's statements given "substantial weight" despite coaching evidence)
   
   **Pattern 5**: Mother's proposed orders adopted verbatim 75% of time (41 of 55 orders, including findings, conclusions, and typos)

### V. Harm to Litigants

8. As a result of the pattern of rulings:
   - Father has lost 192+ days of parenting time over 18 months
   - Father has incurred $655,000+ in documented damages
   - The minor child has suffered educational neglect, medical neglect, and emotional harm
   - Father-child relationship has been severely impaired

## CONCLUSIONS OF LAW

### I. Legal Standards

1. MCR 2.003(C)(1) requires disqualification when:
   (a) A party demonstrates actual bias, OR
   (b) The judge's conduct creates an appearance of impropriety

2. Either ground is independently sufficient to require disqualification. *Cain v Dep't of Corrections*, 451 Mich 470, 497; 548 NW2d 210 (1996).

### II. Actual Bias Standard

3. Actual bias exists when "a judge's conduct or rulings demonstrate a bent of mind that may prevent or impede impartiality of judgment." *Fletcher v Fletcher*, 447 Mich 871, 876-877; 526 NW2d 889 (1994).

4. Actual bias may be established through:
   - Pattern of adverse rulings inconsistent with facts or law
   - Disparate treatment of parties without legitimate justification
   - Predetermined outcomes demonstrated through conduct

5. All three factors are present in this case.

6. **Pattern of adverse rulings**: The 75-percentage-point disparity in motion success rates (86% vs. 11%) and 100% vs. 0% custody determinations demonstrate a systematic pattern that cannot be explained by the relative merits of the parties' positions.

7. **Disparate treatment**: The specific instances documented by Plaintiff demonstrate that Judge McNeill treated identical or analogous circumstances oppositely depending on which party sought relief.

8. **Predetermined outcomes**: The verbatim adoption of Mother's proposed orders 75% of the time, including factual findings, legal conclusions, and typographical errors, demonstrates lack of independent judicial analysis and predetermined outcomes favoring Mother.

9. **Conclusion on Actual Bias**: Plaintiff has established actual bias under *Fletcher*.

### III. Appearance of Impropriety Standard

10. Even absent actual bias, disqualification is required when "a judge's conduct creates an appearance of impropriety that would make the judge's continued participation undermine public confidence in the integrity and impartiality of the judiciary." MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b).

11. The test is objective: "Would a reasonable person, knowing all the circumstances, harbor doubts about the judge's impartiality?" *In re Disqualification of Conlon*, 294 Mich App 253, 259; 816 NW2d 378 (2011).

12. The Court concludes that a reasonable person, aware of the following facts, would harbor serious doubts about Judge McNeill's impartiality:
    - 44-percentage-point gap in ex parte order success rates (44% vs. 0%)
    - 75-percentage-point gap in overall motion success rates (86% vs. 11%)
    - Perfect reversal in custody determinations (100% vs. 0%)
    - Verbatim adoption of one party's proposed orders 75% of the time
    - Dramatic real-world impact (192+ days parenting time lost, $655,000+ damages)

13. **Conclusion on Appearance of Impropriety**: The circumstances create an overwhelming appearance of impropriety that undermines public confidence in the judicial system under *Cain* and *Conlon*.

### IV. Canon Violations

14. The pattern of conduct violates multiple provisions of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct:
    - Canon 2 (duty to act impartially)
    - Canon 3(B)(7) (ex parte communications)
    - Canon 3(B)(8) (ensuring fairness and right to be heard)
    - Canon 3(C)(1) (duty to disqualify when impartiality questioned)

### V. Case-Specific Application

15. The Court is particularly troubled by:
    
    - The **magnitude of the statistical disparities**, which far exceed those found sufficient in prior Michigan cases
    
    - The **duration and consistency of the pattern**, spanning 102 orders over 18 months without deviation
    
    - The **lack of legitimate explanation** for the disparities (both parties presented similar types of evidence and motions)
    
    - The **verbatim order adoption pattern**, which is probative of predetermined outcomes
    
    - The **substantial harm** to Father's parental rights and the child's welfare

16. While this Court recognizes the importance of judicial efficiency and the deference traditionally afforded to trial judges, the circumstances here are so extreme and systematic that disqualification is required to preserve the integrity of the judicial process.

## ORDER

IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:

### 1. Motion Granted

Plaintiff's Motion to Disqualify Judge is **GRANTED** in its entirety.

### 2. Disqualification of Judge McNeill

The Honorable Daphne Means McNeill is hereby **DISQUALIFIED** from any further participation in this case, including but not limited to:
- Presiding over any hearings or proceedings
- Issuing any orders, opinions, or rulings
- Participating in case management or status conferences
- Communicating with the parties or counsel regarding case matters
- Making any decisions affecting the rights of the parties or the child

### 3. Immediate Effect

This disqualification is **EFFECTIVE IMMEDIATELY** upon entry of this Order.

### 4. Case Reassignment

This case is hereby **REASSIGNED** to a different judge of the Wayne County Circuit Court, Family Division, to be determined by the Court Administrator pursuant to local administrative procedures.

The newly assigned judge shall have no prior involvement with the parties, counsel, or subject matter of this case.

### 5. Stay of Proceedings

All proceedings currently pending before Judge McNeill are hereby **STAYED** pending reassignment and the newly assigned judge's review of the case status.

No hearings shall be held, no motions shall be decided, and no orders shall be entered until a new judge is assigned and has had opportunity to review the case file.

### 6. Case Management Conference

Within **30 days** of reassignment, the newly assigned judge shall schedule a case management conference to address:

   a. Current status of all pending motions
   b. Enforcement of existing orders, including parenting time provisions
   c. Plaintiff's request for makeup parenting time (192+ days documented)
   d. Pending custody modification motion
   e. Child protection concerns raised in pending motions
   f. Discovery disputes or outstanding discovery
   g. Scheduling for resolution of substantive issues
   h. Any other matters requiring immediate attention

### 7. Preservation of Rights

Plaintiff's right to seek reconsideration, modification, or appeal of any orders previously issued by Judge McNeill is hereby **PRESERVED**.

Nothing in this Order shall be construed to:
- Waive any rights Plaintiff may have to challenge prior orders
- Limit Plaintiff's ability to seek relief from prior orders based on bias
- Constitute res judicata or collateral estoppel as to any prior rulings
- Preclude renewed motions on issues previously decided by Judge McNeill

### 8. Notification

The Wayne County Circuit Court Clerk is directed to:

   a. **Immediately notify** all parties and counsel of this Order
   b. **Immediately notify** Judge Daphne Means McNeill of this Order
   c. **Remove** this case from Judge McNeill's docket
   d. **Forward** the complete case file to the Court Administrator for reassignment
   e. **Update** all court records to reflect the disqualification and reassignment
   f. **File** this Order in the official case file

### 9. No Reflection on Judge McNeill's Character

This Court emphasizes that this Order of disqualification is based solely on the specific circumstances of this case and the application of MCR 2.003(C)(1) to the factual record presented.

This Order should not be construed as:
- A finding of intentional misconduct
- A personal criticism of Judge McNeill
- A determination that Judge McNeill is unfit to serve as a judge
- A reflection on Judge McNeill's overall judicial service

The disqualification is required by the court rule based on the demonstrated pattern in this particular case.

### 10. Costs

Each party shall bear their own costs associated with this Motion.
