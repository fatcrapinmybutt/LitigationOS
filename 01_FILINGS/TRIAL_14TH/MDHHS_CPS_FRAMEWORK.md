## KEY STATUTORY PROTECTIONS

### MCL 722.628 — Parental Rights During CPS Investigation
- Right to be informed of specific allegations
- Right to know the investigator's identity
- Right to legal counsel
- Right to review and appeal findings
- Right to remain together with child unless removal necessary for safety
- 24-hour commencement requirement

### MCL 722.623 — Mandatory Reporting
Professionals including social workers, law enforcement, and court officials
MUST report suspected child abuse or neglect. Failure to report is itself a violation.

### MCL 722.622 — Definitions
"Child abuse" requires nonaccidental physical/mental injury, sexual abuse, exploitation,
or maltreatment. "Child neglect" requires failure to provide adequate food, clothing,
shelter, or medical care.

**NOTE:** Exercising parenting time rights, filing motions, or being a pro se litigant
does NOT constitute abuse or neglect. Any suggestion otherwise is a weaponization of
the CPS system that itself constitutes a constitutional violation.
