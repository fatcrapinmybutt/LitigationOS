## 8. CONSTITUTIONAL ARGUMENT FOR FEE WAIVER

The filing fee in this case implicates Plaintiff's fundamental right to parent his child — a right of the highest constitutional magnitude. *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 US 57, 65 (2000). The Supreme Court has held that a State may not condition access to the judicial process on the ability to pay when fundamental rights are at stake:

> *"[A] cost requirement, valid on its face, may offend due process because it operates to foreclose a particular party's opportunity to be heard."* — *Boddie v. Connecticut*, 401 US 371, 374 (1971).

> *"Choices about marriage, family life, and the upbringing of children are among associational rights [the Supreme Court has] ranked as of basic importance in our society, rights sheltered by the Fourteenth Amendment against the State's unwarranted usurpation, disregard, or disrespect."* — *M.L.B. v. S.L.J.*, 519 US 102, 116 (1996).

The filing fee, combined with the previously imposed $250 sanction, effectively bars Plaintiff from seeking judicial relief regarding his parenting time — relief to which he is constitutionally entitled. Waiver is required.
