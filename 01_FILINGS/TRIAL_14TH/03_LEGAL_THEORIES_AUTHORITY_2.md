## Pigors v. Watson et al. — Civil Tort Action

---

## COUNT I: INTENTIONAL INFLICTION OF EMOTIONAL DISTRESS (IIED)

**Elements (Michigan):** (1) Extreme and outrageous conduct; (2) Intent or recklessness; (3) Causation; (4) Severe emotional distress.

**Authority:**
- *Roberts v Auto-Owners Ins Co*, 422 Mich 594 (1985) — IIED elements
- *Terzano v Wayne County*, 216 Mich App 522 (1996) — Extreme and outrageous standard
- MCL 600.2913 — Emotional distress actions

**Application:** 571+ days of complete parental separation, coordinated false allegations, PPO weaponization, deliberate alienation of child. The Watson family's conduct exceeds all bounds of decency.

## COUNT II: CIVIL CONSPIRACY

**Elements (Michigan):** (1) Concerted action; (2) Between two or more persons; (3) To accomplish an unlawful purpose or lawful purpose by unlawful means; (4) Resulting damage.

**Authority:**
- *Advocacy Org for Patients & Providers v Auto Club Ins Ass'n*, 257 Mich App 365 (2003)
- *Fenestra Inc v Gulf American Land Corp*, 377 Mich 565 (1966)
- *Early Detection Ctr v New York Life Ins Co*, 157 Mich App 618 (1986)

**Application:** Watson family coordinated with each other, and potentially with court actors, to: (a) file false CPS reports; (b) obtain PPO through misrepresentation; (c) block parenting time; (d) present perjured testimony. Pattern of coordinated action across multiple family members.

**Evidence Strength:** {cat_counts.get('CONSPIRACY_COORDINATION', 0)} coordination instances documented.

## COUNT III: TORTIOUS INTERFERENCE WITH PARENTAL RIGHTS

**Elements:** (1) Existence of parental relationship (constitutionally protected); (2) Defendant's knowledge; (3) Intentional interference; (4) Causation; (5) Damages.

**Authority:**
- *Bylsma v Bylsma*, 164 Mich App 484 (1987) — Third-party interference with custody
- US Const Amend XIV — Fundamental liberty interest in parent-child relationship
- *Troxel v Granville*, 530 US 57 (2000) — Parental rights fundamental
- MCL 722.27a — Parenting time rights

**Application:** Watson family members (Albert, Lori, Cody) actively interfered with court-ordered parenting time, made false allegations to prevent contact, and coordinated to maintain separation.

**Evidence Strength:** {cat_counts.get('CHILD_WELFARE', 0)} child welfare harms + {cat_counts.get('PPO_WEAPONIZATION', 0)} PPO weaponization instances.

## COUNT IV: PARENTAL ALIENATION TORT

**Authority:**
- *Eyde v Rich Township*, Unpublished (Mich Ct App) — Alienation as tort
- *Beecher v Beecher*, Unpublished (Mich Ct App) — Parental alienation damages
- MCL 722.23(j) — Willingness to facilitate relationship as best interest factor

**Application:** Systematic pattern of alienating behavior over 571+ days including: denying communication, making disparaging statements, obstructing contact, weaponizing legal process.

---

## COUNT V: ABUSE OF PROCESS

**Elements:** (1) Ulterior purpose; (2) Act in use of process not proper in regular prosecution.

**Authority:**
- *Friedman v Dozorc*, 412 Mich 1 (1981)
- *Valenti v Qualitec Inc*, 136 FRD 621 (ED Mich 1991)

**Application:** PPO obtained not for genuine protection but as custody weapon; emergency motions filed for strategic advantage rather than actual emergency.

---

## COUNT VI: DEFAMATION PER SE

**Elements:** (1) False and defamatory statement; (2) Unprivileged communication to third party; (3) Fault; (4) Actionable irrespective of special damages (per se categories).

**Authority:**
- MCL 600.2911 — Defamation statute
- *Mitan v Campbell*, 474 Mich 21 (2005) — Defamation per se categories
- *Smith v Anonymous Joint Enterprise*, 487 Mich 102 (2010)

**Application:** False allegations of child abuse/neglect communicated to CPS, law enforcement, and courts. Under Michigan law, false allegations of criminal conduct are defamation per se.

---

## COUNT VII: TORTIOUS INTERFERENCE WITH ECONOMIC RELATIONS

**Authority:**
- *Health Call of Detroit v Berg*, 228 Mich App 505 (1998)
- *Dalley v Dykema Gossett*, 287 Mich App 296 (2010)

**Application:** Watson actions directly caused loss of employment, housing instability, and economic devastation through fabricated legal proceedings.
