# PROPOSED ORDER NO. 1: RESTORATION OF PARENTING TIME

## ORDER GRANTING PLAINTIFF'S EMERGENCY MOTION TO RESTORE PARENTING TIME

At a session of said Court held in the Courthouse in the County of Muskegon, State of Michigan, on the _____ day of _________________, 2026.

**PRESENT: Honorable ________________________, Circuit Court Judge**

The Court, having considered Plaintiff's Emergency Motion to Restore Parenting Time, the file and record herein, and being otherwise fully advised in the premises:

**THE COURT FINDS:**

1. The ex parte orders entered August 8, 2025 suspended all of Plaintiff-Father's parenting time without the procedural safeguards required by MCR 3.206(B)(5)-(6), MCR 3.207(B)(1), and MCR 2.517(A)(1);

2. No findings were made under MCL 722.23 or MCL 722.27a(3);

3. The 329+ day separation constitutes irreparable harm to the parent-child bond;

4. MCL 722.27a(1) establishes a presumption that parenting time with both parents is in the child's best interests.

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

A. The ex parte orders of August 8, 2025 suspending Plaintiff's parenting time are **VACATED**;

B. Plaintiff's parenting time is **RESTORED** effective immediately, with the following schedule:
   - Supervised parenting time: twice weekly, two hours minimum, at a neutral location, for the first 30 days;
   - Transition to unsupervised parenting time per the pre-August 2025 schedule upon completion of the supervised period, absent specific findings of danger;

C. A full evidentiary hearing on custody and parenting time shall be scheduled within **14 days** of this Order;

D. Make-up parenting time shall be awarded pursuant to MCL 722.27a(9);

E. This Order supersedes and replaces the August 8, 2025 ex parte orders to the extent they are inconsistent.
