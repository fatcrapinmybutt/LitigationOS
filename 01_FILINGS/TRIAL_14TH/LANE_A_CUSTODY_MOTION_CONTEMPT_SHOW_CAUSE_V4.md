## A. Jurisdiction and Authority

1. This Court has authority to enforce its orders through contempt proceedings under MCR 3.606 (contempt outside the immediate presence of the court) and MCL 600.1701(a) (inherent contempt power).

2. MCL 722.27a(9) provides that if a parent is **wrongfully denied parenting time**, the court "shall" do one or more of the following: (a) modify the parenting time order; (b) make-up parenting time; (c) hold the non-compliant party in contempt; (d) award costs and attorney fees; (e) modify custody.

3. The standard for civil contempt requires proof by a **preponderance of the evidence** that (1) a valid court order existed, (2) the contemnor had knowledge of the order, (3) the contemnor had the ability to comply, and (4) the contemnor willfully failed to comply. *In re Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81, 96; 413 NW2d 392 (1987).

## F. Remedies Available Under MCL 722.27a(9)

**Issue:** What mandatory remedies are available to the Court upon a finding that parenting time was wrongfully denied.

**Rule:** MCL 722.27a(9) provides that when parenting time is wrongfully denied, the court **"shall"** do one or more of the following: (a) modify parenting time, (b) order make-up parenting time, (c) hold the offending parent in contempt, (d) award costs, or (e) modify custody. The word "shall" makes these remedies mandatory, not discretionary. *Harvey v Harvey*, 470 Mich 186 (2004).

**Application:**

13. MCL 722.27a(9) provides mandatory remedies when parenting time is wrongfully denied. The court **"shall"** do one or more of:

   d. **Award costs and reasonable attorney fees** (or reasonable costs for a pro se litigant);
   e. **Modify custody** if the pattern demonstrates that the custodial parent is unwilling to facilitate the parent-child relationship (MCL 722.23(j)).

14. The word "shall" in MCL 722.27a(9) makes these remedies **mandatory**, not discretionary, upon a finding that parenting time was wrongfully denied.

### Defense 1: "No Valid Order Was Violated — Ex Parte Orders Superseded Prior Parenting Time"

**Watson/Barnes may argue:** The August 8, 2025 ex parte orders lawfully suspended all parenting time, and therefore no "violation" of a court order occurred. Watson was complying with the most recent court order by denying Father contact.

**This argument fails because:** The contempt encompasses TWO distinct periods: (1) the pre-August 2025 pattern of unilateral withholding beginning approximately March 26, 2024 — when valid parenting time orders were in effect and Watson denied them without court authorization; and (2) the post-August 2025 period, where the ex parte orders themselves are challenged as procedurally void (unsworn motion in violation of MCR 3.207(B)(1), no MCL 722.23 findings, no MCR 2.517(A)(1) factual findings). A void order cannot confer authority to deny parenting time. Moreover, even under the ex parte orders, Watson's complete refusal to offer ANY voluntary contact (phone calls, video calls, letters) goes beyond the orders' requirements and demonstrates willful alienation under MCL 722.23(j). *Harvey v Harvey*, 470 Mich 186, 192 (2004).

**Record evidence refuting defense:** Exhibit D (Documentation of parenting time denial pattern beginning March 26, 2024 — before any ex parte order); the facially deficient August 8, 2025 motion (unsworn); Watson's zero voluntary contact during 329+ days; MCL 722.27a(9) mandating remedies for "wrongfully denied" parenting time.

---

### Defense 2: "Watson Did Not Willfully Violate — She Acted in Good Faith on Safety Concerns"

**Watson/Barnes may argue:** Watson genuinely believed L.D.W. was in danger based on the officer's report and friend's concern. Her conduct was protective, not contemptuous. She lacked the willfulness required for civil contempt. *In re Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81, 96 (1987).

**This argument fails because:** *Dougherty* requires proof that the contemnor "willfully failed to comply." Willfulness is demonstrated by the pattern: (a) withholding began March 2024 — months before the "safety" allegations; (b) the ex parte motion sought total elimination rather than supervised contact — inconsistent with genuine safety concern; (c) Watson offered zero voluntary contact for 329+ days — a parent genuinely concerned about drug use would offer phone calls or video visits, not total severance; (d) Watson's nine years at the Kent County Prosecutor's Office, Family Court Division, means she knew exactly how to weaponize ex parte procedures. The "good faith" defense is contradicted by the pattern of escalation and the 333 documented Watson impeachment items showing contradictions between her assertions and the record.

**Record evidence refuting defense:** The March 2024 withholding predating any safety allegation; the unsworn nature of the ex parte motion; Father's NEGATIVE narcotics screen; the zero-voluntary-contact record; 333 Watson impeachment items.

---

### Defense 3: "Inability to Comply — The Court Order Prevented Contact"

**Watson/Barnes may argue:** Watson could not facilitate contact because the court order prohibited it. She had no ability to comply with parenting time provisions that were superseded by the ex parte suspension.

**This argument fails because:** The ex parte orders suspended Father's court-ordered parenting time; they did not prohibit Mother from voluntarily offering contact. No order prohibited phone calls, video calls, letters, or photographs. A parent committed to the child's best interests under MCL 722.23(j) would have offered some form of contact during a 329+ day separation from a bonded parent. Watson's complete silence demonstrates willful alienation, not inability to comply. MCL 722.27a(9) imposes mandatory remedies when parenting time is "wrongfully denied" — the wrongfulness encompasses both the pre-August 2024 unilateral denial and the post-August 2025 exploitation of procedurally deficient orders.

**Record evidence refuting defense:** No court order prohibited voluntary contact (phone, video, letters); Watson's zero effort to facilitate any contact during 329+ days; comparison with Watson's facilitation of Berry family contact with L.D.W. during the same period (see Discovery Requests, Admission No. 18).

---

### Defense 4: "Contempt is Punitive, Not Remedial — Father Seeks Punishment"

**Watson/Barnes may argue:** Father's motion is really seeking punitive contempt (criminal contempt), which requires proof beyond a reasonable doubt and other criminal procedure protections. Civil contempt should not be used to punish past conduct.

**This argument fails because:** This motion seeks **civil contempt** — the purpose is remedial, not punitive. MCR 3.606 governs contempt "outside the immediate presence of the court." The remedies sought (make-up parenting time, costs, enforcement mechanisms) are all prospective and remedial — they seek to restore the parent-child relationship and prevent future denial, not to punish past conduct. *In re Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich at 96, distinguishes civil from criminal contempt by the purpose of the sanction. MCL 722.27a(9) itself provides the remedial framework: make-up time, modified orders, costs. The standard is preponderance of the evidence, not beyond a reasonable doubt.

**Record evidence refuting defense:** The Prayer for Relief requests make-up parenting time (MCL 722.27a(9)(b)), modified enforcement provisions (MCL 722.27a(9)(a)), and costs (MCL 722.27a(9)(d)) — all remedial; no request for incarceration or fine payable to the court.

---

### Defense 5: "Father's Contempt Motion is Moot Because Ex Parte Orders Remain in Effect"

**Watson/Barnes may argue:** So long as the ex parte suspension remains in effect, there is no enforceable parenting time order to violate, rendering the contempt motion moot.

**This argument fails because:** The pre-August 2025 pattern of withholding (March 2024 onward) occurred when valid parenting time orders were in effect — those violations are not mooted by subsequent ex parte orders. Additionally, if the companion Emergency Motion to Restore Parenting Time is granted and the ex parte orders are vacated, the contempt motion becomes operative for the entire 329+ day period. A court may address contempt retroactively — the violation occurred, and MCL 722.27a(9)'s mandatory remedies (including make-up parenting time) require the court to address the wrongful denial regardless of current order status. The adversary_models database confirms this: the statutory word "shall" in MCL 722.27a(9) makes remedies mandatory upon a finding of wrongful denial.

**Record evidence refuting defense:** The March 2024 withholding pattern predating any ex parte order; MCL 722.27a(9)'s mandatory remedies operating regardless of current order status; the companion motions seeking vacatur of the ex parte orders.
