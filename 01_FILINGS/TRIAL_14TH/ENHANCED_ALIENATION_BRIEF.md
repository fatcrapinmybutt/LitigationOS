## VIII. LEGAL ANALYSIS — PARENTAL ALIENATION UNDER MICHIGAN LAW

### A. Michigan's Recognition of Parental Alienation

Michigan courts have consistently recognized parental alienation as a significant factor in custody determinations. Under MCL 722.23, the court **must** evaluate each parent's willingness to facilitate a close and continuing relationship between the child and the other parent. A pattern of alienation is among the strongest grounds for custody modification. *See Pierron v. Pierron*, 282 Mich App 222 (2009).

### B. The Eight Hallmarks of Alienation Present in This Case

The evidence establishes all recognized indicators of parental alienation:

| # | Hallmark | Evidence in This Case |
|---|----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Denigration campaign** | Mother coaching child: "Daddy doesn't want to see you" |
| 2 | **Weak, frivolous, or absurd rationalizations** | PPO based on five false statements; no police findings supporting allegations |
| 3 | **Lack of ambivalence** | Mother permits zero contact for 192+ consecutive days |
| 4 | **Independent thinker phenomenon** | Child parrots Mother's language at age 3 (developmentally impossible without coaching) |
| 5 | **Reflexive support of alienating parent** | Watson family members uniformly participate in alienation |
| 6 | **Absence of guilt** | Mother's 27 violations with zero remorse or self-correction |
| 7 | **Borrowed scenarios** | Child's statements about Father mirror Mother's sworn allegations verbatim |
| 8 | **Spread to extended family** | Albert, Lori, and Cody Watson all actively participate |

### C. Constitutional Dimensions

The right of a parent to the care, custody, and management of their child is a **fundamental liberty interest** protected by the Fourteenth Amendment. *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 U.S. 57 (2000); *Santosky v. Kramer*, 455 U.S. 745 (1982).

**329+ days of separation without an evidentiary hearing** constitutes a deprivation of this fundamental right without due process of law. The court's failure to address documented alienation, combined with its refusal to consider new evidence ("we're not going to relitigate it") and directive to Father to stop filing motions ("Do not file any more"), effectively extinguishes Father's constitutional rights without adjudication on the merits.
