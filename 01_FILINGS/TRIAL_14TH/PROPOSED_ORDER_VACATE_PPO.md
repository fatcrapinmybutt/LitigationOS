## THE COURT FINDS:

1. **Jurisdiction.** This Court has jurisdiction over the parties and the subject matter of this proceeding pursuant to MCL 600.2950 and MCR 3.707.

2. **Changed Circumstances.** Changed circumstances exist since entry of the Personal Protection Order, including but not limited to:

   (a) The passage of significant time without any incident of domestic violence;

   (b) The absence of criminal charges, police reports, or other evidence of domestic violence during the pendency of the PPO;

   (c) Movant's maintenance of a stable living situation;

   (d) Movant's negative drug screen;

   (e) The dissipation of the conditions alleged to have given rise to the PPO.

3. **PPO No Longer Needed.** Pursuant to MCL 600.2950(12), the Court finds that the Personal Protection Order is no longer needed. The factual predicate for the PPO has been undermined by the changed circumstances described above.

4. **Statutory Standard Satisfied.** The standard for rescission under MCL 600.2950(12) has been satisfied. *Hayford v. Hayford*, 279 Mich App 324 (2008); *Pickering v. Pickering*, 268 Mich App 1 (2005).

5. **Parental Rights.** The continuation of the PPO infringes upon Movant's fundamental right to parent his minor child, Lincoln (DOB 11/9/2022), without sufficient justification. *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 US 57 (2000); *Hunter v. Hunter*, 484 Mich 247 (2009).

---

## IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:

6. The Personal Protection Order entered in Case No. 2023-5907-PP is **TERMINATED AND VACATED**, effective immediately.

7. All provisions of the PPO are dissolved and of no further force or effect.

8. The Michigan State Police and the Law Enforcement Information Network (LEIN) shall be notified of the termination of this PPO and shall remove the PPO from all databases.

9. The Muskegon County Sheriff and all law enforcement agencies that received copies of the PPO shall be notified that the PPO has been vacated.

10. This Order does not affect or modify any separate custody, parenting-time, or child-support orders that may exist between the parties. Custody and parenting-time matters shall be addressed through the appropriate proceedings under the Child Custody Act, MCL 722.21 *et seq.*
