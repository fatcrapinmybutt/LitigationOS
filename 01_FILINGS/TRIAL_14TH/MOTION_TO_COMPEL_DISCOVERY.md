## IV. LEGAL STANDARD

22. **MCR 2.302(B) — Scope of Discovery:** "Parties may obtain discovery regarding any matter, not privileged, which is relevant to the subject matter involved in the pending action, whether it relates to the claim or defense of the party seeking discovery or to the claim or defense of any other party." Relevance is construed broadly; information need not be admissible at trial if it "appears reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence."

23. **MCR 2.310 — Request for Production of Documents:** A party may serve a request to produce documents, electronically stored information, or tangible things within the scope of MCR 2.302(B) that are in the possession, custody, or control of the party upon whom the request is served.

24. **MCR 2.313(A) — Motion to Compel:** A party may move for an order compelling discovery when the opposing party fails to answer interrogatories (MCR 2.313(A)(2)(b)(iii)), fails to respond to document requests (MCR 2.313(A)(2)(b)(iv)), or provides evasive or incomplete responses (MCR 2.313(A)(4)).

25. **MCR 2.313(B) — Sanctions for Failure to Comply:** If a party fails to obey an order to provide or permit discovery, the court may enter sanctions including:

   a. An order that designated facts be taken as established (MCR 2.313(B)(2)(a));

   b. An order refusing to allow the disobedient party to support or oppose designated claims (MCR 2.313(B)(2)(b));

   c. An order striking pleadings or parts thereof (MCR 2.313(B)(2)(c));

   d. An order rendering a default judgment against the disobedient party (MCR 2.313(B)(2)(d));

   e. An order treating the failure as contempt of court (MCR 2.313(B)(2)(e)).

26. **MCR 2.314 — Discovery in Domestic Relations Actions:** Discovery in family law matters is governed by MCR 2.302 through 2.313, with the additional recognition that the best interest of the child is paramount and that full disclosure of relevant information is necessary to enable the court to fulfill its statutory obligations under MCL 722.23.

27. **MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors:** The court must consider 12 enumerated factors in determining the best interest of a child. Adequate discovery is essential for the court — and the parties — to properly evaluate these factors. Obstruction of discovery directly impedes the court's ability to render decisions in L.D.W.'s best interest.

## VI. ARGUMENT

### A. Discovery Is Essential to Assess Best Interest Factors Under MCL 722.23

58. The Michigan Child Custody Act, MCL 722.23, requires the court to consider twelve enumerated best interest factors when making custody and parenting time determinations. These factors encompass the love, affection, and emotional ties between the parties and the child (factor a); the capacity to provide care (factor c); the stability of the home environment (factor d); moral fitness (factor f); mental and physical health (factor g); the child's reasonable preference (factor i); willingness to facilitate the parent-child relationship (factor j); domestic violence (factor k); and any other relevant factor (factor l).

59. Adequate discovery is a prerequisite to the court's statutory obligation to evaluate these factors. Without the discovery sought herein, neither the court nor Plaintiff can meaningfully assess:

   a. Whether Defendant has been withholding medical or educational information about L.D.W.;

   b. Whether the Watson family has engaged in coordinated alienation of L.D.W. from Plaintiff;

   c. Whether the home environment at 2160 Garland Drive is safe and stable for L.D.W.;

   d. Whether Defendant's financial circumstances warrant modification of child support;

   e. Whether the PPO was obtained in bad faith as a custody tactic.

### B. Plaintiff Has Been Denied Access to Information About His Own Child for 226+ Days

60. As of the date of this filing, Plaintiff has been completely separated from L.D.W. for over 226 consecutive days. During this period, Plaintiff has been denied not only physical contact with his child but also access to any information about L.D.W.'s health, education, welfare, and daily life.

61. MCL 722.27a(7) provides that a parent is entitled to access information regarding his or her child's health, education, and welfare. Defendant's refusal to provide any information, either voluntarily or through discovery, constitutes a continuing violation of Plaintiff's parental rights.

62. The urgency of this discovery cannot be overstated. Every day that passes without disclosure is another day that Plaintiff is denied fundamental information about his child.

### C. Berry-McNeill Structural Conflict Requires Full Discovery

63. Plaintiff has filed a Motion for Disqualification of Hon. Jenny L. McNeill based on the structural conflict of interest arising from the relationship between Ronald Berry (Defendant's domestic partner) and Cavan Berry (Judge McNeill's spouse).

64. MCR 2.003(C)(1) provides that a judge is disqualified when the judge "is biased or prejudiced for or against a party or attorney." The Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 2, requires that a judge "should avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety in all activities."

65. Discovery in Category 7 (Berry-McNeill documentation) and Category 8 (phone records) is essential to determine:

   a. The nature and extent of the Berry-McNeill connection;

   b. Whether Ronald Berry served as an intermediary between Defendant and the judge's household;

### D. Watson Family Coordination Requires Discovery to Prove Civil Conspiracy

67. The pattern of conduct in this case — including coordinated police reports, CPS referrals, PPO petitions, and false allegations — is consistent with a civil conspiracy among Watson family members to deprive Plaintiff of his parental rights.

68. Michigan recognizes civil conspiracy as an actionable tort. *See Advocacy Org for Patients & Providers v Auto Club Ins Ass'n*, 257 Mich App 365, 384 (2003). To establish civil conspiracy, Plaintiff must demonstrate: (1) a concerted action; (2) by a combination of two or more persons; (3) to accomplish an unlawful purpose; (4) or to accomplish a lawful purpose by unlawful means.

69. Discovery in Categories 1 (communications), 3 (police reports), and 6 (PPO records) is essential to establish the concerted action element and to identify the scope of the conspiratorial agreement.
