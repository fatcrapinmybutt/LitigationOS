## II. LEGAL STANDARD

### A. MCL 552.517 -- Modification of Support

MCL 552.517(1)(a) provides that a support order may be modified if "the court determines that a change of circumstances has occurred that causes the current support amount to be unreasonable and unjust." The Friend of the Court is required to review support orders periodically, but either party may also petition for modification.

### B. Change of Circumstances

A change of circumstances sufficient to warrant modification exists when the current support amount deviates by more than 10% from the amount that would result from application of the Michigan Child Support Formula to the parties' current circumstances. MCL 552.517(1)(a); *Stallworth v. Stallworth*, 275 Mich App 282 (2007).

### C. Michigan Child Support Formula (MCSF)

The Michigan Child Support Formula (2021 edition, effective January 1, 2021, and as subsequently updated) provides the framework for calculating child support obligations. The formula considers: (1) each parent's income; (2) the number of overnights with each parent; (3) childcare costs; (4) health insurance costs; and (5) other relevant factors.

## IV. APPLICATION OF THE MICHIGAN CHILD SUPPORT FORMULA

### A. Income Determination

The MCSF requires determination of each parent's "net income" as defined by the formula. This includes:

1. **Gross income**: All income from all sources, including wages, salaries, commissions, bonuses, overtime, tips, self-employment income, interest, dividends, rental income, retirement income, and any other income
2. **Deductions**: Taxes, mandatory retirement contributions, union dues, and other allowable deductions
3. **Net income**: Gross income minus allowable deductions

Plaintiff requests that the Court order both parties to submit updated Uniform Child Support Orders (UCSO) and verified income information.

### B. Parenting Time Credit

The MCSF provides a credit for parenting time based on the number of annual overnights each parent has with the children. The formula recognizes that parents who have the children in their care for more overnights incur greater direct costs.

Plaintiff requests that the support calculation be based on the parenting time schedule that should be in effect -- i.e., the schedule that would exist but for the improper ex parte orders -- rather than the current zero-overnight arrangement that was wrongfully imposed.

In the alternative, if the Court bases the calculation on actual overnights, Plaintiff requests a retroactive adjustment once parenting time is restored to credit Plaintiff for the period during which he was wrongfully denied overnights.

### C. Imputed Income

If Defendant is voluntarily unemployed or underemployed, the Court should impute income to Defendant at a level consistent with her education, skills, and employment history. MCL 552.605(2); MCSF 2.01(G). The MCSF provides that "if the court finds that a parent is voluntarily unemployed or underemployed, the court may impute income to that parent."

Plaintiff requests that the Court investigate whether Defendant is employed at a level consistent with her capacity and, if not, impute income accordingly.

### D. Childcare and Healthcare Costs

The MCSF requires allocation of childcare and healthcare costs between the parents. Plaintiff requests:

1. Verification of actual childcare costs incurred by Defendant
2. Verification of healthcare coverage and costs for the children
3. Proper allocation of these costs per the MCSF formula
4. Credit to Plaintiff for any healthcare coverage he provides or could provide

## VI. MICHIGAN CHILD SUPPORT FORMULA -- DETAILED APPLICATION

### A. Base Support Calculation

Under the MCSF, the base support obligation is calculated as follows:

1. **Determine each parent's net income** per MCSF 2.01
2. **Calculate the total support need** based on the number and ages of the children using the MCSF income shares table
3. **Allocate the obligation** between parents proportional to their respective net incomes
4. **Apply the parenting time offset** based on the number of annual overnights per MCSF 3.02
5. **Calculate the final support amount** after all adjustments

### B. Parenting Time Offset -- MCSF 3.02

The parenting time offset is critical in this case. Under MCSF 3.02:

- The offset recognizes that parents incur direct costs when children are in their care
- The offset increases as the number of overnights increases
- At zero overnights (the current wrongful arrangement), Plaintiff receives no offset -- an unjust result given that the zero-overnight arrangement was imposed against Plaintiff's will

Plaintiff argues that the support calculation should either:
1. **Use the parenting time schedule that should be in effect** -- i.e., the schedule prior to the improper ex parte orders -- to avoid penalizing Plaintiff for a situation he did not create; or
2. **Credit Plaintiff retroactively** once parenting time is restored, to recoup the excess support paid during the wrongful denial period

### C. Childcare Cost Allocation -- MCSF 3.05

The MCSF requires that net childcare costs be allocated between parents proportional to their net incomes. Plaintiff requests:

1. Verification of Defendant's actual childcare expenses
2. Documentation that childcare is necessary for employment or education
3. Proper allocation per the formula

### D. Healthcare Cost Allocation -- MCSF 3.03-3.04

Healthcare costs, including insurance premiums and uninsured medical expenses, must be allocated per the MCSF. Plaintiff requests:

1. Identification of which parent provides healthcare coverage
2. Allocation of the premium cost attributable to the children
3. A mechanism for sharing uninsured medical expenses
4. Credit to Plaintiff if he provides or can provide more cost-effective coverage

### E. Tax Considerations

The support calculation should reflect current tax law:
1. Federal and state tax withholding based on current filing status
2. Child tax credit allocation
3. Any dependency exemption agreement between the parties
