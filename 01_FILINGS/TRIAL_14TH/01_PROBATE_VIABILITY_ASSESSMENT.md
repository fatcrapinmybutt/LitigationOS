## III. GUARDIANSHIP / CONSERVATORSHIP ANALYSIS

### A. Guardianship of Minor Child

**Question:** Could probate court guardianship proceedings affect Andrew's custody rights?

| Factor | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| **Current custody forum** | Muskegon County Circuit Court — Family Division (Case 24-01507-DC) |
| **Guardianship trigger** | Would require petition showing both parents unable/unfit (MCL 700.5204) |
| **Risk assessment** | Watson could theoretically petition for guardian appointment if she argues Andrew is "absent" due to homelessness |
| **Defense** | Andrew is an available parent — guardianship is improper when a fit parent exists (*In re Hatcher*, 443 Mich 426 (1993)) |

**RISK LEVEL: LOW** — Guardianship requires showing BOTH parents unable. Watson as custodial parent would not benefit from guardianship petition. Family court is the proper forum.

### B. Conservatorship

**Not applicable.** Conservatorship protects assets of incapacitated individuals (MCL 700.5401). Andrew is not incapacitated, and his mobile home/property claims are handled through circuit court.

### C. Emergency Guardianship Risk

**Question:** Could anyone seek emergency guardianship over Andrew's child based on housing instability?

| Scenario | Likelihood | Defense |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Watson petitions for emergency guardian | Very Low | She already has custody; no basis |
| Third party (grandparent) petitions | Low | MCL 700.5204 requires showing parent unavailable |
| CPS-initiated guardianship | Low | CPS reports unsubstantiated |

## V. PROBATE-ADJACENT ISSUES

### A. PPO (Personal Protection Order)

PPOs in Michigan are filed in circuit court (MCL 600.2950), NOT probate court. However:
- Family Division (circuit court) handles domestic PPOs
- Watson's PPO against Andrew is already in circuit court
- PPO modifications/challenges remain in circuit court

### B. Child's Property/Assets

If the minor child had property interests (unlikely here), probate court could have jurisdiction over a conservatorship for the child's assets. This is not relevant to current claims.

### C. Wrongful Death / Survival Actions

Not applicable — no death involved.

## VI. RECOMMENDATION

### ❌ DO NOT FILE IN PROBATE COURT

**Reasoning:**

1. **No probate jurisdiction** over any housing claims
2. **No guardianship basis** — Andrew is an available, fit parent
3. **No conservatorship need** — Andrew is not incapacitated
4. **Family court is the proper forum** for custody matters (already pending)
5. **Federal court is the proper forum** for Fair Housing Act claims
6. **Circuit court is the proper forum** for state housing claims

### Defensive Measures

**Monitor for:**
- Any guardianship petition filed against Andrew's interests
- Any third-party petition regarding Andrew's child
- Any attempt to use housing instability as basis for guardianship

**If guardianship petition filed:**
1. Object immediately under MCL 700.5204 (fit parent available)
2. Cite *In re Hatcher*, 443 Mich 426 (1993) — parental rights presumption
3. File response showing housing instability was caused by Defendants' illegal conduct (not parental unfitness)
4. Cross-reference Fair Housing Act claims — Defendants' conduct created the instability being weaponized
