II. RULE
----------------------------------------
    Under Michigan law, the credibility of a witness may be attacked by any party, including the party calling the witness. MRE 607.

    A witness who has made a prior statement inconsistent with their testimony may be impeached by extrinsic evidence of that statement. MRE 613(b).

    A statement is not hearsay if the declarant testifies and is subject to cross-examination concerning the statement, and the statement is inconsistent with the declarant's testimony. MRE 801(d)(1)(A).

    The court must evaluate the best interest of the child under the factors enumerated in MCL 722.23(a)-(l). The credibility of witnesses directly affects the court's findings on Factor (e).
