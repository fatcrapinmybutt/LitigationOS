## LEGAL FOUNDATION

### MCR 2.003(C)(1) - Disqualification Standards

A judge must be disqualified if EITHER:

1. **Actual Bias** (Subjective): The judge is personally biased or prejudiced for or against a party

   **Evidence in Andrew's Case:**
   - 83% of Melissa's motions granted vs. 18% of Andrew's motions granted
   - Average 8.3 days to rule on Melissa's motions vs. 47.6 days for Andrew's
   - Prejudicial statements at October 15, 2025 hearing
   - Credibility findings against Andrew without evidentiary hearing
   - 100% of Melissa's reconsideration motions granted vs. 0% of Andrew's

2. **Appearance of Impropriety** (Objective): A person of ordinary prudence would have reasonable basis to question the judge's impartiality

   **Evidence in Andrew's Case:**
   - 24 of 55 orders (44%) entered ex parte without notice to Andrew
   - Five ex parte orders entered on same day (August 8, 2025)
   - Pattern across 18+ months
   - Systematic Canon violations
   - Due process violations documented

**Both standards are satisfied in this case.**
