1. Navigate to **https://mifile.courts.michigan.gov**

4. Log in to your MiFILE account

1. After logging in, click "File into Existing Case"
2. Select **Court**: "14th Circuit Court - Muskegon County"
3. Enter **Case Number**: 2024-001507-DC
4. Click "Search" to locate the case
5. Verify the case caption: Pigors v. Watson

### C. Filing a Motion

For each motion in the stack:

1. Click "File into Existing Case" and locate Case No. 2024-001507-DC
2. Select **Filing Type**: "Motion"
3. Select **Document Type** appropriate to the filing:
   - Emergency Motion --> "Emergency Motion"
   - Motion to Set Aside --> "Motion"
   - Motion to Disqualify --> "Motion to Disqualify Judge"

4. Upload the motion document (PDF format required)
5. Upload any supporting exhibits
6. Add a brief description in the "Filing Comments" field
7. Review the filing for completeness
8. Submit and pay the filing fee (or request fee waiver)
9. **SAVE THE CONFIRMATION NUMBER** for each filing

### D. Document Format Requirements

- **Format**: PDF only (convert .md files to PDF before filing)
- **Page size**: 8.5" x 11" (Letter)
- **Margins**: Minimum 1" on all sides
- **Font**: 12-point, standard readable font (Times New Roman, Arial, or similar)
- **Line spacing**: Double-spaced for body text
- **Page numbers**: Bottom center
- **Caption**: First page of each document must include full case caption

### E. Converting Markdown to PDF

To convert the .md files to properly formatted PDF:

1. **Option A -- Microsoft Word**: Open .md in a text editor, copy content to Word, format per court requirements, Save As PDF
2. **Option B -- Online converter**: Use a markdown-to-PDF converter (e.g., md2pdf, pandoc)
3. **Option C -- Command line**: `pandoc motion_file.md -o motion_file.pdf --pdf-engine=wkhtmltopdf`
4. **CRITICAL**: After conversion, review each PDF to ensure:
   - Caption is properly formatted
   - Page numbers are present
   - All text is readable
   - No formatting artifacts

## III. FILING FEES

### A. Standard Motion Fee

- **Motion filing fee**: $175.00 per motion
- **Total for all six motions**: $1,050.00
- **Payment methods accepted by MiFILE**: Credit card, debit card, electronic check

### B. Fee Waiver (MC 20)

If you cannot afford the filing fees, you may request a fee waiver:

1. Download **Form MC 20 -- Fee Waiver Request** from the Michigan Courts website: https://www.courts.michigan.gov/siteassets/forms/scao-approved/mc20.pdf
2. Complete the form, providing:
   - Current income information
   - Monthly expenses
   - Assets and debts
   - Reason for requesting waiver
3. File the MC 20 **BEFORE** or **WITH** your first motion
4. If the waiver is granted, all filing fees for the case are waived
5. If the waiver is denied, you will be required to pay the fees within a specified time

### C. Fee Waiver Tips

- Be thorough and accurate in reporting income and expenses
- Include documentation of income (pay stubs, tax returns)
- If currently unemployed or underemployed due to incarceration history, state this clearly
- The fee waiver applies to all filings in the case, not just the first one
- File the fee waiver first to avoid paying fees that may later be waived

## IV. SERVICE OF PROCESS -- MCR 2.107

### A. Who Must Be Served

All motions must be served on:
1. Defendant Emily A. Watson (or her attorney of record)
2. Friend of the Court (for custody and support motions)
3. Any Guardian Ad Litem appointed in the case
4. Any other party of record

### B. Methods of Service

MCR 2.107 authorizes the following methods of service:

1. **MiFILE electronic service**: If all parties are registered MiFILE users, electronic filing provides automatic service. This is the preferred method.
2. **Email service**: If the parties have stipulated to email service, or if the Court has ordered email service, send a copy to the email address on file.
3. **First-class mail**: Mail a copy to the party's last known address. **Important**: If serving by mail, you must serve at least **7 days before the hearing date** (MCR 2.107(C)(3) adds 3 days to the notice period for mail service, in addition to the standard notice period).
4. **Personal service**: Deliver a copy directly to the party or their attorney.

### C. Certificate of Service

Each filing MUST include a Certificate of Service stating:
- The date of service
- The method of service
- The name and address of each person served
- A signature under oath or declaration

### D. Service Timeline

| Method | Additional Time Required |
|--------|------------------------|
| MiFILE (electronic) | Same day (no additional time) |
| Email (if stipulated) | +1 day |
| First-class mail | +3 days added to notice period |
| Personal service | Same day |

## V. HEARING SCHEDULING -- MCR 2.119(C)

### A. Motion Day

MCR 2.119(C) provides that each court shall designate specific motion days. For the 14th Circuit Court, Muskegon County:

- **Motion days**: [Check with clerk -- typically Tuesday or Wednesday mornings]
- **Motion call time**: [Typically 8:30 AM or 9:00 AM]
- **Location**: Hall of Justice, 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, MI 49442

### B. Notice Requirements

- **Standard motions**: 9 days' notice before the hearing (MCR 2.119(C)(1))
- **Add 3 days** if serving by mail (total: 12 days before hearing)
- **Emergency motions**: May be heard on shortened notice per MCR 2.119(B)(7)
- **Motion to Disqualify**: Must be heard within 14 days per MCR 2.003(D)(2)

### C. Scheduling Procedure

1. **Standard motions**: File the motion via MiFILE. The clerk will assign a hearing date on the next available motion day, or you may be required to contact the clerk to schedule.
2. **Emergency motions**: File via MiFILE with "Emergency" designation. Contact the clerk's office immediately by telephone to request expedited scheduling.
3. **Disqualification motion**: This must be decided by the chief judge or another judge if Judge McNeill does not grant it. The MCR 2.003 timeline controls.

### D. Hearing Preparation

For each hearing, prepare:
1. Three copies of the motion and all exhibits (for the Court, yourself, and opposing party)
2. A proposed order for the Court's signature
3. Any additional evidence you intend to present
4. A brief outline of your oral argument (2-3 minutes per motion)
5. Copies of key cases you intend to cite

## IX. IMPORTANT DEADLINES AND REMINDERS

1. **Emergency motions**: No specific deadline, but file as soon as possible. Every day of delay adds to the harm.
2. **Motion to disqualify**: MCR 2.003(D)(2) -- must be decided within 14 days of filing unless extended by the chief judge.
3. **Standard motions**: Must be noticed for hearing at least 9 days in advance (12 days if serving by mail).
4. **Responses**: Opposing party has 7 days to file a response (MCR 2.119(C)(2)).
5. **Replies**: Moving party may file a reply within 4 days of the response (MCR 2.119(C)(3)).
6. **Support modification**: File a current Uniform Child Support Order (UCSO) with the motion.

## X. TROUBLESHOOTING

### MiFILE Issues
- **Cannot find case**: Verify case number exactly: 2024-001507-DC. Try searching by party name.
- **Upload fails**: Ensure PDF is under 25MB. Split large documents if necessary.
- **Payment fails**: Try a different payment method. Contact MiFILE support: support@mifile.courts.michigan.gov or (517) 373-0130.
- **Filing rejected**: Check rejection reason. Common issues: incorrect document type, missing caption, wrong case number. Correct and refile promptly.

### Service Issues
- **Cannot locate Defendant**: File a motion for alternative service (MCR 2.105).
- **Defendant refuses service**: Document the refusal and file an affidavit of attempted service.
- **Electronic service bounces**: Switch to first-class mail and add 3 days to the notice period.

### Hearing Issues
- **Hearing not scheduled**: Call the clerk's office at (231) 724-6241 to inquire.
- **Need to adjourn**: File a motion to adjourn at least 3 business days before the hearing.
- **Remote hearing**: If the hearing is by Zoom, test your connection and equipment beforehand. Dress professionally. Treat it as an in-person hearing.

## XI. FINAL NOTES

1. **Keep copies of everything.** Maintain a complete file of all documents filed, served, and received.
2. **Document all communications.** Keep a log of all calls to the clerk, service attempts, and communications with opposing counsel.
3. **Be professional.** All filings should be professionally formatted, clearly written, and respectful in tone.
4. **Follow up.** Do not assume that filing a motion will automatically result in a hearing. Follow up with the clerk's office to confirm hearing dates.
5. **Prepare for hearings.** Each motion hearing is an opportunity to present your case. Be organized, concise, and focused on the facts and law.
6. **Stay current.** Court rules and local practices may change. Check the Michigan Courts website and Muskegon County local rules for updates.
