The Court having considered Plaintiff's Emergency Motion to Restore Parenting Time, and the Court having reviewed the file and being otherwise fully advised in the premises,

THE COURT FINDS AS FOLLOWS:

A. The ex parte order of August 8, 2025, suspended all of Plaintiff's parenting time without compliance with MCR 3.207(B).

B. No finding was made that the child(ren)'s "physical, mental, or emotional health would be seriously endangered" by contact with Plaintiff as required by MCL 722.27a(3).

C. No best-interest analysis under MCL 722.23(a)-(l) was conducted prior to the suspension.

D. As of the date of this Order, Plaintiff has been separated from his child(ren) for approximately 567 days, constituting ongoing, irreparable harm to the parent-child relationship.

E. It is the public policy of this state to assure that each minor child has close contact with both parents. MCL 722.27a(1).

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED THAT:**

1. Plaintiff's parenting time is IMMEDIATELY RESTORED consistent with the parenting time schedule established in the order most recently in effect prior to August 8, 2025.

2. The ex parte order of August 8, 2025, and all subsequent orders continuing the suspension of Plaintiff's parenting time, are hereby VACATED.

3. Defendant shall not interfere with, obstruct, or impede Plaintiff's court-ordered parenting time.

4. A full best-interest hearing under MCL 722.23(a)-(l) shall be scheduled within twenty-one (21) days of the date of this Order, at which both parties shall be present and given full opportunity to be heard.

5. The Friend of the Court shall monitor compliance with this Order and shall report any violations to the Court within seven (7) days of occurrence.

6. Neither party shall remove the minor child(ren) from the State of Michigan without prior written consent of the other party or order of this Court.

IT IS FURTHER ORDERED that any violation of this Order may result in a finding of contempt pursuant to MCR 3.606 and MCL 600.1701.

IT IS FURTHER ORDERED that this Order shall take effect immediately upon entry.

This Order does not resolve all pending claims and does not close the case.
