Judge McNeill's ex parte conduct violates Canon 2 by:

- Engaging in conduct that undermines public confidence in judiciary
- Failing to maintain appearance of impartiality

### Canon 3: Performing Judicial Duties Impartially

Judge McNeill violated Canon 3 by:

- **Canon 3(B)(7)**: Engaging in ex parte communications about pending matters
- **Canon 3(B)(8)**: Failing to accord parties right to be heard
- **Canon 3(C)**: Demonstrating bias and prejudgment in rulings

## DUE PROCESS VIOLATIONS

### Constitutional Violations

The ex parte violations constitute violations of:

1. **Fourteenth Amendment Due Process**: Denial of notice and opportunity to be heard
2. **Michigan Constitution Art. 1, §17**: Right to due process under state law
3. **Fundamental Fairness**: Violation of basic principles of judicial fairness

### Specific Due Process Failures

- **Notice**: Failed to provide adequate notice of proceedings
- **Hearing**: Decided matters without affording oral argument
- **Opportunity to Respond**: Issued orders without allowing parties to present evidence
- **Impartial Tribunal**: Demonstrated bias through ex parte conduct

## LEGAL AUTHORITY

### Michigan Court Rules

**MCR 2.119(E)**: Requires notice of oral argument
- *Violated*: Multiple orders issued without notice or argument

**MCR 2.119(F)**: Governs hearings on motions
- *Violated*: Hearings conducted without proper notice

**MCR 3.207**: Friend of Court investigations and reports
- *Violated*: Improper consideration of ex parte FOC communications

### Case Law

- *In re MKK*, 286 Mich App 664 (2009): Ex parte communications are prohibited
- *Fletcher v Fletcher*, 447 Mich 871 (1994): Notice and hearing required
- *Wickens v Oakwood Healthcare System*, 465 Mich 53 (2001): Due process requires notice
