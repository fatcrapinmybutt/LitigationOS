# I. OVERVIEW OF DAMAGES FRAMEWORK

This damages schedule calculates the full scope of harm inflicted by Defendants' coordinated scheme of ledger fraud, unlawful eviction, property destruction, blocked sales, and the resulting cascade into homelessness and custody destruction. Damages are organized by:

1. **Actual/Direct Damages** -- Quantifiable out-of-pocket losses
2. **Consequential Damages** -- Downstream harms caused by Defendants' conduct
3. **Statutory Damages** -- Multipliers and penalties under Michigan and federal law
4. **Punitive/Exemplary Damages** -- Deterrence for willful misconduct

Per MCL 750.159j (RICO), actual damages are trebled. Per MCL 600.2919a (Conversion), actual property damages are trebled. These multipliers stack where distinct statutory bases apply.

# V. PUNITIVE / EXEMPLARY DAMAGES

## A. Legal Standard

**State Farm v. Campbell, 538 U.S. 408 (2003):**
The Supreme Court established that punitive damages must bear a reasonable relationship to compensatory damages. While single-digit multipliers are generally appropriate, higher ratios may be warranted where:
- The conduct was particularly reprehensible
- The compensatory damages are modest relative to the harm
- The conduct was repeated and systematic rather than isolated

## B. Reprehensibility Analysis (Per State Farm Factors)

| Factor | Assessment | Score |
|--------|------------|-------|
| Physical vs. economic harm | Both -- homelessness causes physical harm; destruction of property | HIGH |
| Indifference/reckless disregard for health/safety | Sewage exposure; homelessness with child | HIGH |
| Target's financial vulnerability | Pro se plaintiff, rendered homeless | MAXIMUM |
| Repeated/isolated conduct | Systematic, multi-year pattern (ledger fraud, 3 blocked sales, eviction) | MAXIMUM |
| Intentional malice vs. accident | Deliberate scheme (text messages, ledger fraud, res judicata) | MAXIMUM |

**Reprehensibility Score: MAXIMUM** -- All five State Farm factors weigh heavily in favor of substantial punitive damages.
