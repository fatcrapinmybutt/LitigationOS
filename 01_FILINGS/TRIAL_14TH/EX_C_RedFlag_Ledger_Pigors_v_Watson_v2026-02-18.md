## Claim Router (Michigan-first gates; NO hallucinated torts)

- **Civil Conspiracy (derivative):** Must anchor to an underlying actionable tort; conspiracy alone usually fails without the predicate. **Gate:** identify predicate tort + overt acts + agreement inference + damages.
- **Malicious Prosecution:** High bar. **Gate:** prior proceeding must end in your favor; you must show lack of probable cause + malice + damages. If the PPO is still active/extended, this gate may be **blocked** until termination.
- **Abuse of Process:** Focus is misuse of legal process for an improper purpose **after** process issues. **Gate:** identify specific process (PPO show-cause, custody motion, etc.) + improper act in using it + ulterior purpose + damages.
- **Perjury:** Typically treated as **criminal** conduct / impeachment evidence, not a stand-alone civil tort. **Gate:** use as proof of fraud/misrepresentation; consider referral to prosecutorial authority rather than civil count.
- **Fraud on the Court / Relief from Judgment:** Often pursued through court rules (e.g., relief from judgment) or an independent action. **Gate:** clear proof of intentional deception that corrupted the judicial process; tie to specific orders.


## Higher-Court / Oversight Options (structure, not legal advice)

- **Judicial Tenure Commission (JTC):** Targets judicial misconduct (bias, ex parte handling, unequal treatment, improper restrictions). Remedy can include discipline; removal is possible in extreme cases, but requires strong, well-documented record.
- **Superintending Control / Extraordinary Relief:** Typically pursued to correct a lower court’s action where there’s no adequate legal remedy and urgent harm. Often starts in the **Court of Appeals** with possible escalation to the **Michigan Supreme Court**. (Exact vehicle and standards must be locked to current rules before filing.)
- **Standard Appellate Routes (COA):** Appeals/leave applications from custody/PT orders and related rulings, depending on finality and timing.
- **Criminal Complaint / Referral:** If you believe crimes occurred (false report, perjury, obstruction), the normal path is referral to appropriate prosecuting authority / state authorities. The persuasive artifact is **a clean record packet**: who/what/when + exhibits + statute candidates (to be verified) + witness list.
