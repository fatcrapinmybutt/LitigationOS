## ANTICIPATED DEFENSE RESPONSES AND REBUTTALS

### Count VII (Malicious Prosecution)
- **Defense**: "Litigation privilege protects filing of PPO and CPS reports"
- **Rebuttal**: Litigation privilege does not protect knowingly false statements. *Friedman v. Dozorc*, 412 Mich at 47; MCL 750.423 (perjury)

### Count IX (Fraud on the Court)
- **Defense**: "Litigation privilege" / "Quasi-judicial immunity"
- **Rebuttal**: tort_claims table chess_rebuttal: "privilege_no_protect_knowing_false." Fraud on the court is specifically excepted from litigation privilege. MCR 2.612(C)(1)(c)

### Count XIII (Loss of Parental Companionship)
- **Defense**: "Not a recognized independent cause of action in Michigan"
- **Rebuttal**: tort_claims table chess_rebuttal: "Berger_v_Weber_principle_applies." *Berger v. Weber*, 411 Mich 1 (1981) established that the parent-child relationship independently supports companionship damages. Combined with *Troxel v. Granville* constitutional framework, the claim is viable.
