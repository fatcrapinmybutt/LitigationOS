## IV. COUNT I — INTENTIONAL INFLICTION OF EMOTIONAL DISTRESS
### (Against All Defendants)

### Issue

43. Whether the coordinated and sustained campaign by all Defendants to sever the parent-child relationship between Plaintiff and Lincoln, resulting in 329+ consecutive days of total separation, constitutes extreme and outrageous conduct that intentionally or recklessly caused severe emotional distress to Plaintiff.

### Rule

44. Under Michigan law, a claim for intentional infliction of emotional distress ("IIED") requires proof of four elements: (1) extreme and outrageous conduct; (2) intent or recklessness; (3) causation; and (4) severe emotional distress. *Roberts v Auto-Owners Ins Co*, 422 Mich 594, 602; 374 NW2d 905 (1985). Conduct is "extreme and outrageous" when it is "so outrageous in character, and so extreme in degree, as to go beyond all possible bounds of decency, and to be regarded as atrocious, and utterly intolerable in a civilized community." *Id.* at 603.

45. The Michigan Supreme Court has recognized that conduct involving the deliberate exploitation of a position of power or knowledge to inflict suffering may satisfy the outrageousness standard. *Graham v Ford*, 237 Mich App 670, 674; 604 NW2d 713 (1999). A course of conduct that is not individually outrageous may become so when considered in its cumulative effect. *Doe v Mills*, 212 Mich App 73, 91; 536 NW2d 824 (1995).

### Application

46. Defendants' collective conduct is extreme and outrageous because: (a) it involves the deliberate, coordinated destruction of a fundamental parent-child relationship; (b) it has resulted in 329+ consecutive days of complete separation between a father and his three-year-old son — approximately 30% of the child's life; (c) it exploits Emily Watson's nine-year employment at the Kent County Prosecutor's Office, Family Court Division, to gain tactical legal advantages over a self-represented father; (d) it includes the weaponization of a PPO obtained on false claims; (e) it involves the submission of ex parte evidence reviewed in chambers without Plaintiff's knowledge; and (f) it constitutes a sustained pattern of alienation tactics including false allegations of abuse, blocking communication, interfering with visitation, and fabricating evidence.

47. Defendants acted with intent or reckless disregard. The sustained nature of the campaign — spanning from March 2024 through the present — and the escalating severity of the tactics (from withholding parenting time, to PPO weaponization, to ex parte emergency motions, to 329+ days of total separation) demonstrate purposeful conduct, not mere negligence or isolated bad judgment.

48. Plaintiff has suffered severe emotional distress as a direct and proximate result of Defendants' conduct, including: profound grief from the loss of daily contact with his son; psychological trauma from prolonged separation; the anguish of knowing his three-year-old son is being raised without his father's presence; the humiliation and stigma of incarceration on PPO violations driven by fabricated complaints; and the financial devastation of defending against a sustained legal assault.

### Defense Anticipation

49. Defendants may argue their conduct constitutes mere "insults" or "indignities" insufficient to support IIED. This defense fails because a 329+ day pattern of deliberate parent-child separation, accomplished through coordinated legal manipulation and institutional advantage, transcends any definition of mere insult. The sustained, systematic nature of the campaign — involving four family members acting in concert, weaponized court processes, and exploitation of prosecutorial office connections — places this conduct squarely within the "extreme and outrageous" category. *See Roberts*, 422 Mich at 603 (conduct must be evaluated in its totality, not in isolated incidents).

### Conclusion

50. Defendants' coordinated campaign of parental alienation constitutes extreme and outrageous conduct that intentionally caused severe emotional distress to Plaintiff. Plaintiff is entitled to compensatory damages, including damages for mental anguish, emotional suffering, and loss of parental companionship.

## V. COUNT II — TORTIOUS INTERFERENCE WITH PARENTAL RELATIONSHIP
### (Against All Defendants)

### Issue

51. Whether each Defendant, through intentional acts, tortiously interfered with the parent-child relationship between Plaintiff and Lincoln, causing damage to that relationship.

### Rule

52. Michigan recognizes a cause of action for tortious interference with a parental relationship. The Restatement (Second) of Torts § 700 provides: "One who, with knowledge that the parent does not consent, abducts or otherwise compels or induces a minor child to leave a parent legally entitled to its custody or not to return to the parent after a visit or other authorized stay, is subject to liability to the parent." Michigan courts have adopted this principle. *See Plumley v Klein*, 199 Mich App 447, 450; 502 NW2d 714 (1993). The interference need not be physical abduction; any intentional conduct designed to disrupt the parent-child relationship is actionable. *See* Restatement (Second) of Torts § 700, comment b.

### Application

53. Each Defendant has engaged in specific acts of interference with Plaintiff's parental relationship:

54. **Emily Watson** interfered by: (a) filing the ex parte motion on August 8, 2025 to suspend all parenting time without legitimate basis; (b) withholding Lincoln for 329+ consecutive days; (c) failing to appear for the scheduled August 8, 2025 exchange without notice; (d) filing repeated PPO complaints to generate criminal contempt proceedings against Plaintiff; (e) submitting ex parte evidence (Ron Berry voicemail, USB flash drive) to influence the court outside Plaintiff's presence; (f) using her position at the Kent County Prosecutor's Office to gain tactical advantage.

55. **Lori Watson** interfered by: (a) sending the March 26, 2024 text message initiating the custody conflict; (b) despite being compensated by Plaintiff as Lincoln's caregiver, aligning with Emily against Plaintiff; (c) participating in the removal of Plaintiff's personal property on November 14-16, 2023; (d) blocking Plaintiff's access to Lincoln at the Garland Drive residence; (e) providing false or misleading information in support of Emily's legal filings.

56. **Albert Watson** interfered by: (a) providing the family residence as a base from which Plaintiff was excluded and Lincoln was harbored; (b) supporting and ratifying Emily's withholding of Lincoln; (c) physically and practically blocking Plaintiff's access to his child; (d) providing financial and logistical support for the alienation campaign.

57. **Cody Watson** interfered by: (a) providing undisclosed financial support to Emily, enabling her to sustain the protracted litigation campaign; (b) participating in the coordinated family effort to alienate Lincoln from Plaintiff; (c) funding and supporting the legal actions used to sever the parent-child bond.

### Defense Anticipation

58. Defendants may argue the "family privilege" or that interference was justified by legitimate concern for the child's welfare. This defense fails because: (a) the August 23, 2024 Judgment itself recognized that "both parents have a strong bond" with Lincoln — negating any claim that Plaintiff posed a danger; (b) the PPO was obtained through "false claims and manipulative tactics"; (c) the ex parte suspension occurred without best interest factor analysis; (d) the 329+ day total separation far exceeds any reasonable protective measure; and (e) the involvement of the entire Watson family unit — including financial support from Cody and the triggering text from Lori — demonstrates a coordinated campaign, not protective parenting.

### Conclusion

59. Each Defendant intentionally interfered with Plaintiff's parental relationship with Lincoln, causing profound and ongoing damage. Plaintiff is entitled to compensatory damages.

## VI. COUNT III — TORTIOUS INTERFERENCE WITH CUSTODY AND PARENTING TIME ORDERS
### (Against Defendant Emily Watson)

### Issue

60. Whether Defendant Emily Watson tortiously interfered with the custody and parenting time orders entered in Case No. 2024-001507-DC by systematically denying, obstructing, and frustrating Plaintiff's court-ordered parenting time.

### Rule

61. MCL 722.27a establishes the right of a noncustodial parent to parenting time and provides that parenting time shall be granted in the best interests of the child. MCL 722.27a(3) provides that the court shall order compliance with parenting time orders and may impose remedies including make-up parenting time, modification of the custody order, civil contempt, and costs and attorney fees. Under Michigan law, a custodial parent who willfully and repeatedly violates parenting time orders may be liable in tort for the resulting damages. The Restatement (Second) of Torts § 700 and Michigan public policy expressed in MCL 722.27a support a civil remedy for interference with lawful parenting time.

### Application

62. The August 23, 2024 Judgment granted Plaintiff specific parenting time with Lincoln. Defendant Emily Watson violated these orders by: (a) failing to appear for scheduled parenting time exchanges; (b) failing to provide notice when parenting time would not occur, in violation of the order's notice requirements; (c) withholding Lincoln from a child-centered Halloween 2024 event; (d) filing the ex parte motion on August 8, 2025 to suspend all parenting time; (e) maintaining the suspension for 329+ consecutive days; (f) refusing to facilitate any form of parent-child contact — including telephone, video, or supervised contact — during the suspension period.

63. The parenting time order expressly provides: "Any other reasonable condition determined to be appropriate in the particular case. MCL 722.27a(9)." Despite these mandatory provisions, the Court failed to enforce any of the MCL 722.27a(9) remedies, and Emily Watson took advantage of this failure to maintain the total separation.

64. The order also expressly recognized: "The minor child of the parties has the inherent right to the natural affections and love of both parents and neither parent shall do anything to estrange the child from the other parent." Emily Watson's 329+ day withholding of Lincoln is a direct and willful violation of this order provision.

### Defense Anticipation

65. Emily Watson may argue that the ex parte order of August 8, 2025 authorized the suspension of parenting time and therefore her conduct was lawful. This defense fails because: (a) the ex parte order was obtained without notice to Plaintiff, without a hearing, without best interest factor analysis, and in violation of due process; (b) the ex parte order did not relieve Emily of her obligation to facilitate the parent-child relationship; (c) the 329+ day duration of total separation far exceeds any emergency justification; and (d) the order was obtained through Emily's submission of false or misleading information and ex parte evidence reviewed in chambers.

### Conclusion

66. Defendant Emily Watson willfully and repeatedly interfered with court-ordered parenting time, causing 329+ days of total parent-child separation. Plaintiff is entitled to compensatory and exemplary damages.

## VII. COUNT IV — ABUSE OF PROCESS
### (Against Defendant Emily Watson)

### Issue

67. Whether Defendant Emily Watson committed abuse of process by using legal proceedings — including the PPO, ex parte motions, and show cause petitions — for an ulterior purpose beyond their legitimate function, namely, to alienate Plaintiff from Lincoln and to punish Plaintiff for asserting his parental rights.

### Rule

68. Under Michigan law, abuse of process requires: (1) an ulterior purpose, and (2) an act in the use of process that is improper in the regular prosecution of the proceeding. *Friedman v Dozorc*, 412 Mich 1, 30-31; 312 NW2d 585 (1981). The "ulterior purpose" element is satisfied when the proceedings are used to accomplish a result beyond their legitimate scope. *Bonner v Chicago Title Ins Co*, 194 Mich App 462, 472; 487 NW2d 807 (1992). The "improper act" element requires more than filing with a bad motive — it requires a perversion of the process to accomplish something the process was not designed to do. *Friedman*, 412 Mich at 31.

### Application

69. Defendant Emily Watson's ulterior purpose is demonstrated by the pattern of escalation: (a) obtaining a PPO in 2023 based on "numerous false claims and manipulative tactics"; (b) using the PPO not for protection but to restrict Plaintiff's parenting access and to generate criminal contempt proceedings; (c) filing the ex parte emergency motion on August 8, 2025 to suspend all parenting time — not because of a genuine emergency, but to achieve total separation; (d) submitting ex parte evidence (Ron Berry voicemail, USB flash drive) in chambers to influence the court outside the adversarial process; (e) opposing every motion Plaintiff filed to restore contact with his son.

70. The improper acts include: (a) presenting false or exaggerated allegations in the PPO petition; (b) filing serial show cause motions that resulted in Plaintiff's incarceration for 45 days; (c) exploiting the ex parte process to obtain a parenting time suspension without notice or hearing; (d) submitting evidence to the court ex parte and off-the-record; (e) using her specialized knowledge as a Kent County Prosecutor's Office caseworker to navigate and manipulate the legal system against a self-represented litigant.

71. The legitimate purpose of a PPO is protection from domestic violence, not the destruction of a parent-child relationship. The legitimate purpose of an ex parte motion is to address genuine emergencies, not to engineer long-term custody arrangements. Emily Watson perverted both processes to accomplish parental alienation.

### Defense Anticipation

72. Emily Watson may argue she was exercising her legitimate right to seek legal remedies and court protection. This defense fails because: (a) the PPO was based on "false claims and manipulative tactics" as documented in the record; (b) the August 8, 2025 ex parte motion resulted in 329+ days of total separation — a result far beyond any legitimate emergency protective purpose; (c) the serial show cause motions resulting in Plaintiff's incarceration constitute a pattern of weaponization, not legitimate protection; and (d) the submission of evidence ex parte and in chambers demonstrates a perversion of process that cannot be defended as legitimate advocacy.

### Conclusion

73. Defendant Emily Watson abused the legal process by using PPOs, ex parte motions, and show cause petitions as instruments of parental alienation rather than for their legitimate protective purposes. Plaintiff is entitled to compensatory and exemplary damages.

## VIII. COUNT V — CIVIL CONSPIRACY
### (Against All Defendants)

### Issue

74. Whether Defendants Emily Watson, Albert Watson, Lori Watson, and Cody Watson entered into a civil conspiracy to accomplish the tortious alienation of Plaintiff from Lincoln, with each Defendant performing overt acts in furtherance of the common plan.

### Rule

75. Under Michigan law, a civil conspiracy is "a combination of two or more persons, by some concerted action, to accomplish a criminal or unlawful purpose, or to accomplish a lawful purpose by criminal or unlawful means." *Admiral Ins Co v Columbia Cas Ins Co*, 194 Mich App 300, 313; 486 NW2d 351 (1992). A civil conspiracy claim requires: (1) a concerted action; (2) by two or more persons; (3) to accomplish an unlawful objective or a lawful objective by unlawful means; and (4) resulting damage to the plaintiff. *Early Detection Ctr, PC v New York Life Ins Co*, 157 Mich App 618, 632; 403 NW2d 830 (1986). Each conspirator is liable for the tortious acts of co-conspirators committed in furtherance of the conspiracy. *Advocacy Org for Patients & Providers v Auto Club Ins Ass'n*, 176 Mich App 142, 156; 438 NW2d 875 (1989).

### Application

76. The Watson family acted as a coordinated unit with a common objective: the alienation of Plaintiff from Lincoln and the destruction of the parent-child relationship. The concerted action is demonstrated by:

77. (a) **Lori Watson** initiated the March 26, 2024 text message that triggered the custody dispute, despite having been compensated by Plaintiff as Lincoln's caregiver — demonstrating that she chose family loyalty over his relationship with Plaintiff and Lincoln's father.

78. (b) **Emily Watson** served as the primary executor of the alienation campaign, filing the PPO, the custody action, the ex parte emergency motion, and serial show cause petitions — all designed to progressively sever Plaintiff's parental access.

79. (c) **Cody Watson** provided undisclosed financial support to Emily, enabling her to sustain the protracted litigation. Without this financial backing, Emily could not have maintained the years-long legal campaign against a self-represented litigant.

80. (d) **Albert Watson** provided the family residence at 2160 Garland Drive as the base of operations, where Lincoln was harbored away from Plaintiff. He supported and ratified Emily's conduct through his continued provision of housing, logistical support, and physical presence blocking Plaintiff's access.

81. (e) **Lori and Emily Watson** together removed Plaintiff's personal property on November 14-16, 2023, demonstrating coordinated action against Plaintiff's interests.

82. The unlawful objective — parental alienation accomplished through false PPO allegations, abuse of process, custodial interference, and fraud upon the court — is amply demonstrated in the preceding counts.

### Defense Anticipation

83. Defendants may invoke the "intrafamily" defense, arguing that family members cannot conspire with each other. ## X. COUNT VII — CUSTODIAL INTERFERENCE
### (Against Defendant Emily Watson)

### Issue

98. Whether Defendant Emily Watson committed custodial interference in violation of MCL 750.350a (criminal custodial interference) and MCL 600.2919a (civil remedy for criminal acts), by willfully withholding Lincoln from Plaintiff in violation of court orders for 329+ consecutive days.

### Rule

99. MCL 750.350a provides that a person shall not take or retain a child from a person who has lawful custody or parenting time rights with the intent to detain or conceal the child from that person. MCL 600.2919a provides a civil cause of action for damages arising from criminal acts, including custodial interference: "A person who suffers an injury to his or her person or property as a result of a violation of [criminal statutes] may bring an action to recover actual damages sustained, together with costs." This civil remedy is independent of criminal prosecution. MCL 722.27a(3) mandates that "parenting time shall be granted in accordance with the best interests of the child" and provides remedies for violations.

### Application

100. Defendant Emily Watson retained Lincoln and withheld him from Plaintiff beginning August 8, 2025, with the intent to detain and conceal the child from Plaintiff. The withholding has continued for 329+ consecutive days. Specifically:

101. (a) On August 8, 2025, Emily failed to appear for the scheduled parenting time exchange, despite having filed the ex parte motion to suspend earlier that same day — deliberately engineering a situation where Plaintiff would arrive for an exchange that Emily had already arranged to cancel.

102. (b) Emily made no effort to provide any form of parent-child contact during the 329+ day suspension — no telephone calls, video calls, letters, or supervised visits.

103. (c) Emily obtained the ex parte suspension through false or misleading representations to the court, rendering the suspension order voidable and providing no lawful basis for the continued withholding.

104. (d) The parenting time order expressly required that "neither parent shall do anything to estrange the child from the other parent." Emily's 329+ day total withholding constitutes an extreme and willful violation of this order provision. The Court's failure to apply any of the five (5) mandatory remedies under MCL 722.27a(9) — including makeup time, contempt, modified schedule, costs, and additional conditions — enabled the continued withholding. *Walters v. Nadell*, 481 Mich 377, 383 (2008) (the word "shall" in MCL 722.27a(9) is mandatory, not discretionary).

### Defense Anticipation

105. Emily Watson may argue that the ex parte order authorized her conduct. This defense fails because: (a) a court order obtained through fraud is voidable and does not confer lawful authority; (b) even under the ex parte order, Emily had an obligation to facilitate some form of parent-child contact; (c) the 329+ day duration of total separation far exceeds any reasonable interpretation of the ex parte order's scope; and (d) the ex parte order was issued without due process protections — no notice, no hearing, no best interest analysis — and is subject to challenge under MCR 3.207(B) and U.S. Const. amend. XIV.

### Conclusion

106. Defendant Emily Watson committed custodial interference by retaining Lincoln and withholding him from Plaintiff for 329+ consecutive days. Plaintiff is entitled to actual damages and costs under MCL 600.2919a.

## XI. COUNT VIII — DEFAMATION PER SE
### (Against Defendant Emily Watson)

### Issue

107. Whether Defendant Emily Watson's false statements — made to the court, to law enforcement, and to third parties — imputing criminal behavior, mental instability, and unfitness as a parent to Plaintiff, constitute defamation per se under Michigan law.

### Rule

108. Under Michigan law, defamation per se exists when the statement "imputes the commission of a criminal offense; imputes the existence of a loathsome disease; charges a person with being unchaste; or tends to injure the plaintiff in his or her business, trade, or profession." *Mitan v Campbell*, 474 Mich 21, 27; 706 NW2d 420 (2005). Defamation per se does not require proof of special damages — damages are presumed. *Id.* A statement is defamatory if it "tends to expose a person to public contempt, ridicule, aversion, or disgrace, or to induce an evil opinion of him or her in the minds of right-thinking persons." *Thomas M Cooley Law School v Doe 1*, 300 Mich App 245, 263; 833 NW2d 331 (2013).

### Application

109. Defendant Emily Watson made the following false statements imputing criminal conduct and unfitness to Plaintiff:

110. (a) **False allegations in the PPO petition (2023):** Emily alleged conduct warranting a personal protection order, imputing stalking, harassment, and threatening behavior to Plaintiff. The record establishes that "the PPO issued against Andrew Pigors is based on numerous false claims and manipulative tactics." These statements impute criminal conduct (MCL 750.411h, MCL 750.411i — stalking).

111. (b) **False allegations in the ex parte motion (August 8, 2025):** Emily represented to the Court that an emergency existed requiring suspension of Plaintiff's parenting time, imputing danger and unfitness to Plaintiff. These allegations were made without disclosing exculpatory evidence (photographs of Lincoln healthy and happy with Plaintiff on July 29, 2025) and were used to obtain a total suspension of parenting time.

112. (c) **False allegations in show cause proceedings:** Emily filed or supported multiple show cause petitions alleging PPO violations, resulting in Plaintiff's incarceration for 45 days. The serial filing of PPO violation complaints imputed repeated criminal conduct to Plaintiff.

113. (d) **Statements imputing mental instability:** Through the proceedings, Emily caused the court to order multiple mental health assessments at HealthWest, imputing mental instability and unfitness to Plaintiff in a manner that was disseminated through court filings and proceedings accessible to third parties.

114. Each of these statements imputes criminal conduct (stalking, harassment, custodial unfitness) and is therefore defamatory per se under *Mitan*, 474 Mich at 27. Damages are presumed.

### Defense Anticipation

115. Emily Watson may invoke the absolute privilege for statements made in judicial proceedings. This defense fails because: (a) the judicial proceedings privilege does not protect knowingly false statements; (b) the privilege applies only to statements relevant and pertinent to the proceedings — allegations made in bad faith to achieve an ulterior purpose (parental alienation) are not protected; (c) statements made outside the judicial proceedings — including to law enforcement, to third parties, and through the ex parte channel — are not privileged; and (d) the privilege is subject to the "abuse of process" exception when the proceedings themselves are used as a vehicle for defamation.

### Conclusion

116. Defendant Emily Watson defamed Plaintiff per se by imputing criminal conduct, mental instability, and parental unfitness through false statements in court filings and proceedings. Plaintiff is entitled to presumed damages, actual damages, and exemplary damages.

## XII. COUNT IX — LOSS OF PARENTAL COMPANIONSHIP
### (Against All Defendants)

### Issue

117. Whether Plaintiff has suffered a compensable loss of parental companionship with Lincoln as a result of Defendants' tortious conduct.

### Rule

118. The Michigan Supreme Court recognized the tort of loss of companionship in *Berger v Weber*, 411 Mich 1, 16-17; 303 NW2d 424 (1981). While *Berger* addressed a child's loss of parental companionship, the underlying principle — that the parent-child relationship has intrinsic value deserving of legal protection — applies equally to a parent's loss of companionship with a child. MCL 722.23(j) expressly recognizes the importance of a parent's "willingness and ability to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship" — and conversely, the harm caused when that relationship is destroyed. The Restatement (Second) of Torts § 700 recognizes the parent's interest in maintaining the parent-child relationship as a legally protected interest.

### Application

119. Plaintiff has suffered profound and quantifiable loss of parental companionship:

120. (a) **Duration:** 329+ consecutive days of total parent-child separation, from July 29, 2025 to the present.

121. (b) **Developmental loss:** Lincoln was approximately three years old at the onset of the separation. The ages of three and four are critical developmental periods during which parent-child bonding, attachment, and identity formation occur. Plaintiff has lost the ability to participate in these irreplaceable developmental milestones.

122. (c) **Relationship deterioration:** Three-year-old children do not have the cognitive capacity to understand why a parent has disappeared. The 329+ day absence will inevitably cause Lincoln to perceive Plaintiff as absent, disinterested, or dangerous — precisely the alienation outcome sought by Defendants.

123. (d) **Quantification:** At the time of separation, Plaintiff was entitled to alternating weekends, holidays, and summer parenting time. Over 329+ days, Plaintiff has been deprived of approximately 24 weekends (48 days), holiday parenting time (Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Year's, Easter, Father's Day), and summer parenting time — a total of approximately 80-100 days of court-ordered contact.

### Defense Anticipation

124. Defendants may argue that Michigan has not recognized a parent's claim for loss of companionship with a child. While *Berger* addressed the inverse claim, the legal principle of protecting the parent-child relationship is firmly established in Michigan law through MCL 722.23, MCL 722.27a, and constitutional due process jurisprudence. *See Troxel v Granville*, 530 US 57, 65; 120 S Ct 2054 (2000) (recognizing the fundamental right of parents to the care, custody, and control of their children as "perhaps the oldest of the fundamental liberty interests recognized by this Court").

### Conclusion

125. Plaintiff has suffered a devastating loss of parental companionship — 329+ days of complete separation from his three-year-old son — caused by Defendants' coordinated tortious conduct. Plaintiff is entitled to substantial compensatory damages.

## XIII. COUNT X — NEGLIGENT INFLICTION OF EMOTIONAL DISTRESS
### (Against All Defendants)

### Issue

126. Whether Defendants' conduct, even if not found to be intentional, constitutes negligent infliction of emotional distress by breaching their duty to not unreasonably interfere with Plaintiff's parental relationship, proximately causing serious emotional harm.

### Rule

127. Michigan recognizes a claim for negligent infliction of emotional distress ("NIED") where the plaintiff demonstrates: (1) the defendant owed a duty to the plaintiff; (2) the defendant breached that duty; (3) the breach proximately caused the plaintiff's injuries; and (4) the plaintiff suffered serious emotional distress. *Daley v LaCroix*, 384 Mich 4, 12; 179 NW2d 390 (1970). The emotional distress must be accompanied by a definite and objective physical injury or a definite and objective physical manifestation of the emotional distress. *Id.*

### Application

128. Each Defendant owed a duty to Plaintiff not to unreasonably interfere with his fundamental parental relationship with Lincoln. This duty arises from: (a) the common law duty not to tortiously interfere with protected relationships; (b) the court orders establishing Plaintiff's parenting time rights; (c) the constitutional protection of the parent-child relationship under the Fourteenth Amendment; and (d) Michigan's statutory scheme (MCL 722.23, MCL 722.27a) establishing the importance of both parental relationships.

129. Each Defendant breached this duty through the conduct described in the preceding counts, including: the coordinated alienation campaign; the PPO weaponization; the ex parte suspension; the 329+ day withholding; and the submission of false evidence.

130. Plaintiff has suffered definite and objective physical manifestations of emotional distress, including: sleep disturbance and insomnia; loss of appetite and weight loss; chronic anxiety and hypervigilance; depression requiring treatment; and physical symptoms of grief and loss. These manifestations are the direct and proximate result of 329+ days of complete separation from his three-year-old son.

### Defense Anticipation

131. Defendants may argue that NIED requires a "physical impact" or that the emotional distress is not objectively manifested. Under *Daley*, 384 Mich at 12-13, the physical impact requirement was relaxed, and Michigan courts recognize that severe emotional distress causing physical symptoms satisfies the objective manifestation requirement. The severity of the harm — 329+ days of father-child separation — distinguishes this case from cases involving transient emotional upset.

### Conclusion

132. Even to the extent Defendants' conduct is found to fall short of intentional infliction, it constitutes at minimum negligent infliction of emotional distress, causing Plaintiff serious and objectively manifested harm. Plaintiff is entitled to compensatory damages.
