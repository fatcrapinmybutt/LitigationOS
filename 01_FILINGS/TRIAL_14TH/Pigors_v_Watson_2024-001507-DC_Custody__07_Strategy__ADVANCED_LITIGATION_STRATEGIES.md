## PART I: ADVANCED PROCEDURAL TACTICS

### TACTIC 1: IMMEDIATE WRIT OF MANDAMUS (EXTRAORDINARY REMEDY)

**Forum:** Michigan Court of Appeals (MCR 7.203(C))  
**Timing:** File concurrently with trial court motions  
**Standard:** Extraordinary remedy when trial court acts without legal authority

#### Legal Basis

Mandamus is available when: (1) petitioner has a clear legal right; (2) respondent has a clear legal duty; (3) respondent wrongfully refuses or neglects to perform duty; (4) no other adequate remedy exists.

**Application to Your Case:**

The trial court has a clear legal duty under MCL 722.27a to presume parenting time is in the child's best interest. Judge McNeill has wrongfully refused to perform this duty by suspending parenting time without clear and convincing evidence of endangerment. The ex parte suspension violates MCR 3.207(B), which contains no exception for family law matters.

#### Unique Advantage

Unlike an appeal (which reviews for abuse of discretion), mandamus challenges whether the trial court had legal authority to act at all. This is a higher standard that shifts the burden to the trial court to justify its authority.

#### Specific Allegations

The trial court lacked legal authority to:
- Issue ex parte suspension without affidavit (MCR 3.207(B) violation)
- Suspend parenting time without clear and convincing endangerment findings (MCL 722.27a(3) violation)
- Rely on unauthenticated evidence (MRE 901 violation)
- Impose $250 filing fee without vexatious litigant finding (MCR 2.002 violation)

#### Relief Requested

- Writ of mandamus directing trial court to restore parenting time
- Writ of mandamus directing trial court to issue adequate findings
- Writ of mandamus directing trial court to comply with MCL 722.27a
- Writ of mandamus directing trial court to comply with MCR 3.207(B)

#### Strategic Advantage

Mandamus creates immediate appellate pressure. The Court of Appeals can grant relief immediately without waiting for trial court decision. This is faster than emergency motion practice.

### TACTIC 2: RULE 2.313 MOTION FOR IMMEDIATE STAY (AUTOMATIC APPEAL STAY)

**Forum:** Trial Court (MCR 2.313)  
**Timing:** File with emergency motion  
**Standard:** Stay pending appeal; automatic if conditions met

#### Legal Basis

MCR 2.313 provides that when a party appeals, the trial court's judgment is automatically stayed if the appellant files a bond or other security. However, MCR 2.313 also allows the trial court to impose conditions on the stay.

#### Advanced Application

File motion requesting trial court impose zero conditions on the automatic stay. Argue that:
- The suspension order is likely to be reversed on appeal
- Continued suspension causes irreparable harm
- No bond should be required (Father has no ability to pay)
- Stay should be automatic and unconditional

#### Strategic Advantage

This creates an immediate mechanism to stop the suspension while appeal is pending. The trial court cannot refuse to stay the judgment; it can only impose conditions. By requesting zero conditions, you force the trial court to either grant the motion or explain why conditions are necessary.

### TACTIC 3: RULE 2.612 MOTION TO VACATE FOR FRAUD ON THE COURT

**Forum:** Trial Court (MCR 2.612)  
**Timing:** File within 1 year of discovery of fraud  
**Standard:** Fraud on the court (not fraud by a party)

#### Legal Basis

MCR 2.612 allows vacation of judgment for fraud on the court. This is distinct from fraud by a party. Fraud on the court includes:
- Presenting false evidence to the court
- Concealing evidence from the court
- Misrepresenting facts to the court
- Deceiving the court about jurisdiction or procedure

#### Application to Your Case

**Fraud on the Court by Judge McNeill:**

The trial court committed fraud on the court by:

- Representing to Father that a "second" mental health evaluation existed when Father never completed one

- Concealing the HealthWest evaluation from the record
- Misrepresenting the basis for suspension in the order

**Fraud on the Court by Emily Watson:**

Emily Watson committed fraud on the court by:
- Presenting false allegations of arsenic poisoning (no medical evidence)
- Presenting false allegations of physical assault (no police report)
- Presenting false allegations of surveillance (mischaracterization of ordinary devices)
- Presenting false allegations of drug use/hallucinations (no medical evidence)
- Presenting false allegations of threat to child (contradicted by court's own custody order)

#### Relief Requested

- Vacate all orders based on fraudulent evidence
- Restore parenting time to July 17, 2024 order
- Disqualify Judge McNeill
- Sanction Emily Watson for fraud on the court
- Award attorney fees and costs

#### Strategic Advantage

Fraud on the court is a ground for vacation that is not subject to the normal appeal standard. It can be raised at any time and does not require showing abuse of discretion. This is a powerful mechanism to overturn the suspension.

### TACTIC 4: RULE 2.003 MOTION FOR DISQUALIFICATION UNDER "APPEARANCE OF BIAS" STANDARD

**Forum:** Trial Court (MCR 2.003(C))  
**Timing:** File immediately  
**Standard:** Appearance of bias; loss of impartiality

#### Advanced Application: Caperton Standard

The U.S. Supreme Court in Caperton v. AT Massey Coal Co., 556 US 868 (2009), established that due process requires disqualification when there is "a serious, objective probability that a judge is biased."

This is a lower standard than the traditional "actual bias" standard. It requires only that a reasonable observer would conclude the judge is biased.

#### Application to Your Case

A reasonable observer would conclude Judge McNeill is biased based on:

**Pattern 1: Systematic Exclusion of Father's Evidence**
- Excluded HealthWest evaluation (favorable to Father)
- Excluded police reports (favorable to Father)

- Admitted only Mother's evidence

**Pattern 2: Systematic Inclusion of Unauthenticated Evidence**
- Admitted "second" evaluation (unauthenticated)
- Admitted Mother's unsworn allegations
- Admitted curated audio recordings
- No authentication; no foundation

**Pattern 3: Stigmatizing Language**
- Called Father "crazy" on record
- Called Father "liar" on record
- Made assumptions about mental health without diagnostic basis
- No similar language directed at Mother

**Pattern 4: Procedural Barriers to Father's Participation**
- Stated "Do not file any more"
- Imposed $250 filing fee without vexatious litigant finding
- Limited Father's oral argument time
- Muted Father during remote hearings

**Pattern 5: Unequal Treatment of Parties**
- Sanctioned Father for disputed communication
- Did not sanction Mother for documented withholding
- Imposed parenting time restrictions on Father only
- Imposed no restrictions on Mother's gatekeeping

#### Relief Requested

- Disqualify Judge McNeill
- Reassign all cases to different judge
- Vacate all orders issued by Judge McNeill (or preserve for review)

#### Strategic Advantage

The Caperton standard is objective and does not require proving actual bias. It focuses on what a reasonable observer would conclude. This is a powerful tool for disqualification.

### CLAIM 2: VIOLATION OF UNIFORM CHILD CUSTODY JURISDICTION AND ENFORCEMENT ACT (UCCJEA)

**Forum:** Trial Court or Court of Appeals  
**Basis:** Trial court lacked jurisdiction to modify custody

#### Legal Basis

MCL 722.1301 et seq. (UCCJEA) governs jurisdiction in custody cases. A court has jurisdiction to make an initial custody determination only if:
- Michigan is the home state of the child
- Michigan has significant connections to the child and parties
- No other state has jurisdiction

#### Application to Your Case

The trial court may have lacked jurisdiction to modify the July 17, 2024 custody order because:
- The modification was based on allegations that arose after the initial order
- The modification was based on unauthenticated evidence
- The modification violated the presumption in MCL 722.27a

#### Relief Requested

- Award attorney fees and costs

#### Strategic Advantage

A jurisdictional challenge is not subject to appeal standards. If the trial court lacked jurisdiction, the orders are void regardless of the merits.
