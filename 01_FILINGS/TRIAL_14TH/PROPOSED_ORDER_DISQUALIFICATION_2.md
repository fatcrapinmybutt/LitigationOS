The Court having considered Plaintiff's Motion to Disqualify the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill pursuant to MCR 2.003(D), and the Chief Judge having reviewed the motion, the supporting affidavit, the court file, and being otherwise fully advised in the premises,

THE COURT FINDS AS FOLLOWS:

A. Plaintiff timely filed a Motion to Disqualify Judge McNeill pursuant to MCR 2.003(D), which requires the Chief Judge — not the challenged judge — to decide the motion.

B. The record demonstrates grounds for disqualification under MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b) in that Judge McNeill has demonstrated personal bias or prejudice for or against a party, as evidenced by:

   i. Entry of 24 of 55 orders (43.6%) on an ex parte basis, all without notice to Plaintiff;

   ii. A documented pattern of same-day rulings on Defendant's filings without providing Plaintiff an opportunity to respond;

   iii. Refusal to consider Plaintiff's exculpatory photographic evidence at the August 28, 2025 hearing;

   iv. The entry of five (5) separate ex parte orders on a single day (August 8, 2025);

   v. Multiple documented instances where the Court's stated reasoning is inconsistent with the record evidence.

C. The record further demonstrates grounds for disqualification under MCR 2.003(C)(1)(a) in that Judge McNeill has acquired personal knowledge of disputed evidentiary facts through ex parte communications and off-record submissions.

D. A reasonable, objective person would have serious doubt about the judge's impartiality in this matter.

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED THAT:**

1. Plaintiff's Motion to Disqualify is GRANTED.

2. The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill is DISQUALIFIED from all further proceedings in Case No. 2024-001507-DC (Custody) and Case No. 2023-5907-PP (PPO), and from all other matters involving the parties and their minor child(ren).

3. The State Court Administrator is DIRECTED, pursuant to MCR 2.003(E), to assign a replacement judge to preside over Case No. 2024-001507-DC and Case No. 2023-5907-PP within fourteen (14) days of the date of this Order.

4. Upon assignment of a replacement judge, the replacement judge shall:

   a. Review the complete case file, including all ex parte orders, hearing transcripts, and motions;

   b. Schedule a status conference within twenty-one (21) days of assignment;

   c. Schedule a de novo best-interest hearing under MCL 722.23(a)-(l) at the earliest practicable date;

   d. Address Plaintiff's pending motions, including the Emergency Motion to Restore Parenting Time and the Motion for Reconsideration.

5. All orders entered by Judge McNeill that are currently the subject of pending motions or appeal shall remain in effect until the replacement judge has had the opportunity to review and rule on the pending motions, EXCEPT that Plaintiff's parenting time shall be restored on an interim basis pending the replacement judge's review.

6. The Clerk of the Court shall transmit the complete case file to the replacement judge within seven (7) days of assignment.

IT IS FURTHER ORDERED that this Order shall take effect immediately upon entry.

This Order does not resolve all pending claims and does not close the case.
