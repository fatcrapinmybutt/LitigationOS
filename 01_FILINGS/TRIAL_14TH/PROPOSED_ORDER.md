## I. FINDINGS OF FACT

1. A valid parenting time order was entered by this Court on March 05, 2026,
   establishing Plaintiff's parenting time with the minor child(ren).

2. Said order has not been modified, stayed, or vacated and remains in full
   force and effect.

3. Defendant had actual knowledge of the parenting time order.

4. Defendant willfully denied Plaintiff all court-ordered parenting time for
   a period of **571+ consecutive days**, from [start date] through
   [end date / present].

5. The denial was total. No parenting time of any kind was permitted during
   this period.

6. Plaintiff made continuous, documented efforts to exercise his
   court-ordered parenting time throughout the denial period.

7. Defendant offered no lawful justification for the denial.

8. No court order existed suspending, restricting, or terminating
   Plaintiff's parenting time during the denial period.

9. Defendant's conduct was willful, deliberate, and without legal excuse.

10. Defendant possesses specialized knowledge of family court procedures by
    virtue of her approximately nine (9) years of employment in the Kent
    County Prosecutor's Office, Family Court Division.

11. The prolonged separation has caused harm to the minor child(ren) and to
    the Father-child relationship.

---

## II. CONCLUSIONS OF LAW

12. The elements of civil contempt are established pursuant to *In re
    Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81 (1987):
    - A court order existed;
    - Defendant knew of the order; and
    - Defendant willfully violated the order.

13. Defendant's parenting time denial was wrongful within the meaning of
    MCL 722.27a(7).

14. MCL 722.27a(7)–(9) mandates that the Court impose remedies upon a
    finding of wrongful denial.

15. MCL 722.27a(8) requires that make-up parenting time "shall be at least
    the same type and duration" as the parenting time denied.

---
