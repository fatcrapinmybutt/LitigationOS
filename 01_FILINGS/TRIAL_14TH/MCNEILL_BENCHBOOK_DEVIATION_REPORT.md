### Key Finding

Of 1,127 documented violations, **620 (55.0%)**
are rated CRITICAL or HIGH severity — indicating conduct that directly harmed the Plaintiff's
fundamental parental rights and denied due process protections guaranteed by both the Michigan
and United States Constitutions.

The violations are not isolated incidents but reflect a **systematic pattern** of:
- Ex parte proceedings without proper notice or emergency justification
- Denial of the right to be heard on critical custody matters
- Failure to apply mandatory best-interest-factor analysis (MCL 722.23)
- Refusal to consider exculpatory evidence presented by the Plaintiff
- Disparate treatment favoring the Defendant in all procedural and substantive rulings
- Self-ruling on disqualification motions in violation of MCR 2.003(D)

**This pattern has resulted in 567+ days of parent-child separation** without any best-interest
hearing, any finding of unfitness, or any evidence-based determination that separation serves
the child's welfare.

- Conduct clearly prejudicial to the administration of justice (MCR 9.205)
- Conduct in violation of the Code of Judicial Conduct (MCR 9.205)
