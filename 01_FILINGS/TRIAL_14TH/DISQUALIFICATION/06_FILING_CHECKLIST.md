STEP 5: BOND CHALLENGE STRATEGY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    Judge McNeill previously imposed a $250 bond as a condition for
    disqualification. The Motion and Brief include a constitutional
    challenge to this bond under Boddie v Connecticut. Strategy:

☐ OPTION A — File WITHOUT paying the bond
    File the motion and state that the bond is challenged as
    unconstitutional and unauthorized by MCR 2.003. The Motion
    includes the legal argument. If the court insists on the bond,
    you have preserved the issue for appeal.

☐ OPTION B — Pay the bond AND challenge it simultaneously
    Pay the $250 bond under protest. File the motion with the bond
    payment, but include the Boddie challenge. The Proposed Order
    requests the bond be vacated and refunded. This ensures the
    motion is heard even if the bond challenge fails.

    RECOMMENDED: Option B is safer. Pay under protest, then seek
    refund through the Proposed Order. This prevents the court from
    refusing to consider the motion on procedural grounds.

☐ If you cannot afford $250:
    - File a Fee Waiver (MC 20) simultaneously
    - Reference Boddie v Connecticut, 401 U.S. 371 (1971)
    - Reference M.L.B. v S.L.J., 519 U.S. 102 (1996)
    - Attach proof of indigency (income/bank statements)

STEP 9: WHAT TO EXPECT AFTER FILING
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    TIMELINE:
    - Judge McNeill may grant or deny within days
    - If denied, you may request Chief Judge review under MCR
      2.003(D)(3)(a)
    - Chief Judge must rule on the motion
    - If Chief Judge denies, you may appeal to Court of Appeals

    POSSIBLE OUTCOMES:
    1. Judge McNeill voluntarily recuses — best outcome
    2. Judge McNeill denies — proceed to Chief Judge review
    3. Chief Judge grants — case reassigned to new judge
    4. Chief Judge denies — appeal to Michigan Court of Appeals
       under MCR 7.203 within 21 days

    IMPORTANT: Even if denied at all levels, the filing creates a
    record for appeal and preserves the issue.
