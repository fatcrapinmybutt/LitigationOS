II. BELIEF IN BIAS AND PREJUDICE

    6. I believe that Judge Jenny L. McNeill is biased and prejudiced
against me and in favor of Defendant Emily A. Watson in this matter.

    7. I believe that I cannot receive a fair and impartial hearing
before Judge McNeill based on the facts set forth below.

    8. My belief is based on the specific facts, incidents, and
patterns described in this Affidavit, all of which I have personally
witnessed, experienced, or discovered through my review of the case
record.


III. CHRONOLOGICAL INCIDENTS OF BIAS

    9. The following specific incidents demonstrate the bias I have
experienced. Each incident is based on my personal knowledge and/or
the court record:

    a. EVALUATION INTERFERENCE: After the Court ordered a HealthWest
evaluation, I participated fully and cooperated with the evaluator.
The initial evaluation returned "ALL ZEROS" — meaning no basis for
concern regarding me as a parent. I then learned that Judge McNeill
sent a letter to the HealthWest evaluator after receiving the initial
favorable evaluation. Following this judicial communication, the
evaluator changed the finding from "ALL ZEROS" to a "Rule Out"
designation. I believe this constitutes judicial interference with an
independent evaluation process. A neutral judge would accept the
independent findings, not contact the evaluator to seek a different
outcome.

    b. EX PARTE SUSPENSION OF PARENTING TIME (AUGUST 8, 2025): On or
about August 8, 2025, Judge McNeill entered an ex parte order
suspending all of my parenting time without prior notice to me and
without a hearing. I was not given an opportunity to respond before
the order was entered. The order was based on a recording that I
believe was obtained in violation of MCL 750.539c, Michigan's
eavesdropping statute.

    c. 571+ DAYS WITHOUT PARENTING TIME: As of the date of this
Affidavit, I have been denied meaningful parenting time with my child
for 571+ consecutive days. Despite my repeated motions and requests,
no evidentiary hearing on the merits has been conducted. I have filed
multiple motions to restore parenting time, all of which have been
denied or left unaddressed.

    d. PATTERN OF EX PARTE ORDERS: Throughout this case, I have
documented twenty (20) instances of ex parte communications or ex
parte orders entered without proper notice to me. Every one of these
ex parte orders favored Defendant Watson. Not a single ex parte order
was entered in my favor. I have compiled these 20 ex parte documents
and they are attached as Exhibits 1 through 20 to the Exhibit Index.

    e. EXCULPATORY EVIDENCE IGNORED: I tested negative for all
substances on a court-ordered drug screen. Multiple police
investigations yielded zero findings against me. No prosecutor has
found sufficient basis to file charges based on any allegations made
by Defendant. Despite this exculpatory evidence, Judge McNeill has not
scheduled a hearing to reconsider the basis for suspending my
parenting time.

    f. INCARCERATION: I was incarcerated for 59+ days on civil
contempt. No counsel was appointed despite the deprivation of my
liberty. The conditions of purge were not clearly defined or
achievable. I believe the incarceration was punitive rather than
coercive in nature.

    g. RETALIATION FOR PROTECTED ACTIVITY: After I filed a complaint
with the Judicial Tenure Commission regarding Judge McNeill's conduct,
the pattern of adverse rulings against me intensified. After I filed
a bar complaint regarding opposing counsel, additional sanctions were
imposed. After I filed appellate filings, further restrictions were
placed on me. I believe this escalating pattern of adverse action
constitutes retaliation for the exercise of my First Amendment right
to petition the government for redress of grievances.

    h. $250 BOND REQUIREMENT: Judge McNeill imposed a $250 bond as a
condition for considering a disqualification motion. MCR 2.003 does
not authorize such a bond. As a pro se litigant who was incarcerated
for 59+ days and has suffered employment disruption, this bond
effectively denies me access to an impartial tribunal.


IV. EX PARTE COMMUNICATIONS DISCOVERED
