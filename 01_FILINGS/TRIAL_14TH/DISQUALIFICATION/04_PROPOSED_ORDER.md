    This matter having come before the Court on Plaintiff's Motion to
Disqualify Judge pursuant to MCR 2.003(C)(1), filed ______________,
seeking disqualification of the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill from
further proceedings in Case No. 2024-001507-DC;

    The Court having reviewed:

    - Motion to Disqualify Judge McNeill pursuant to MCR 2.003(C)(1)
    - Verified Affidavit of Andrew J. Pigors in support
    - Brief in Support of Motion to Disqualify
    - Exhibit Index and supporting exhibits (Exhibits 1-20)
    - Response filed by Defendant (if any)
    - The complete case file, docket entries, and orders
    - Oral argument (if presented)

    And the Court being fully advised in the premises;

    IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:


1. DISQUALIFICATION

    The Motion to Disqualify is GRANTED. The Honorable Jenny L.
McNeill is hereby DISQUALIFIED from any further participation in Case
No. 2024-001507-DC, Pigors v Watson, including but not limited to:

    a. Presiding over any hearings or proceedings;
    b. Issuing any orders, opinions, or rulings;
    c. Participating in case management or status conferences;
    d. Communicating with the parties regarding case matters;
    e. Making any decisions affecting the rights of the parties or the
       minor child.


2. REASSIGNMENT

    This case is hereby REASSIGNED to a different judge of the 14th
Circuit Court, or if necessary, to a visiting judge pursuant to MCR
8.111, to be determined by the Chief Judge or Court Administrator
pursuant to local administrative procedures.

    The newly assigned judge shall have no prior involvement with the
parties or subject matter of this case.


3. BOND VACATED

    The $250 bond imposed as a condition for consideration of the
disqualification motion is hereby VACATED as:

    a. Unauthorized by MCR 2.003, which contains no provision for a
       bond requirement; and

    b. Unconstitutional as applied to this indigent pro se litigant
       under Boddie v Connecticut, 401 U.S. 371 (1971) and M.L.B. v
       S.L.J., 519 U.S. 102 (1996).

    If Plaintiff has posted the $250 bond, it shall be refunded in
full within fourteen (14) days of entry of this Order.


4. STAY OF PROCEEDINGS

    All proceedings currently pending before Judge McNeill are hereby
STAYED pending reassignment.

    No hearings shall be held, no motions shall be decided, and no
orders shall be entered by Judge McNeill from the date of this Order.


5. CASE MANAGEMENT CONFERENCE

    Within thirty (30) days of reassignment, the newly assigned judge
shall schedule a case management conference to address:

    a. Current status of all pending motions;
    b. Enforcement of existing parenting time provisions;
    c. Plaintiff's request for restoration of parenting time;
    d. Review of ex parte orders entered during the case;
    e. Any other matters requiring immediate attention.


6. PRESERVATION OF RIGHTS

    Plaintiff's right to seek reconsideration, modification, or appeal
of any orders previously issued by Judge McNeill is hereby PRESERVED.
Nothing in this Order shall be construed to waive any such rights or
to constitute res judicata or collateral estoppel as to any prior
rulings.


7. NOTIFICATION

    The Muskegon County Circuit Court Clerk is directed to:

    a. Immediately notify all parties of this Order;
    b. Immediately notify Judge McNeill of this Order;
    c. Remove this case from Judge McNeill's docket;
    d. Forward the complete case file to the Chief Judge or Court
       Administrator for reassignment;
    e. Update all court records to reflect the disqualification and
       reassignment.


IT IS SO ORDERED.
