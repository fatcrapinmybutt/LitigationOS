## II. LEGAL STANDARD

### A. Criminal Statutes

1. **MCL 750.457a — Witness Tampering/Intimidation**: A person who threatens, intimidates, or attempts to intimidate a witness in a legal proceeding is guilty of a felony punishable by imprisonment for not more than 10 years or a fine of not more than $20,000, or both.

2. **MCL 750.411h — Stalking**: Willful course of conduct involving repeated or continuing harassment that would cause a reasonable person to feel terrorized, frightened, intimidated, threatened, harassed, or molested and that actually causes the victim to feel terrorized, frightened, intimidated, threatened, harassed, or molested.

3. **MCL 750.411i — Aggravated Stalking**: Stalking committed in violation of a restraining order, involving a minor, or accompanied by credible threats.

4. **MCL 600.2950 — Personal Protection Orders**: The court may enter a PPO to restrain a person from threatening, assaulting, or engaging in conduct that would make a reasonable person afraid.

### B. Evidentiary Standards

5. **Sullivan v. Gray, 117 Mich App 476 (1982)**: Michigan is a one-party-consent state. Audio recordings made by a party to the conversation are admissible without the other party's consent. This is the controlling authority for the kitchen recording and other audio/video evidence.

6. **MRE 801(d)(2) — Admissions by Party-Opponent**: Statements by a party, or by a party's agent or co-conspirator, offered against that party are not hearsay and are admissible. Albert Watson's statements as Defendant's agent/family member acting in concert are admissible under this rule.

7. **MRE 404(b) — Other Acts Evidence**: Evidence of other crimes, wrongs, or acts is admissible to prove motive, opportunity, intent, preparation, scheme, plan, or system.

### C. Best Interest Factors (MCL 722.23)

8. **Factor (j)** — The willingness and ability of each party to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent.

9. **Factor (k)** — Domestic violence, regardless of whether directed against the child.

10. **Factor (l)** — Any other factor considered by the court to be relevant, including threats, intimidation, and witness tampering that undermine the integrity of the proceedings.
