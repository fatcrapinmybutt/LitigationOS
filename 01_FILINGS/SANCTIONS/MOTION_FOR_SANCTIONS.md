## II. LEGAL STANDARD

### A. MCR 1.109(E) — Sanctions for Improper Filings

MCR 1.109(E)(5)-(6) authorizes the court to impose sanctions when a party files documents for an improper purpose, including harassment, unnecessary delay, or needless increase in the cost of litigation. The court may order the offending party to pay reasonable expenses, including attorney fees, caused by the violation.

### B. MCR 2.114(D)-(E) — Signature Certification and Sanctions

Every document signed by a party certifies that: (1) it is not filed for an improper purpose; (2) the claims are warranted by existing law or a nonfrivolous argument; (3) the factual contentions have evidentiary support; and (4) denials are warranted on the evidence. MCR 2.114(E) mandates sanctions when a filing violates these certifications.

### C. MCR 2.313 — Discovery Sanctions

MCR 2.313 authorizes sanctions for discovery abuse, including failure to comply with discovery orders, providing evasive or incomplete answers, and concealment of evidence. Available sanctions include:
- An order treating designated facts as established;
- Prohibiting the disobedient party from supporting or opposing designated claims;
- Striking pleadings;
- Rendering default judgment;
- Treating the failure as contempt.

### D. MCL 600.2591 — Frivolous Civil Actions

MCL 600.2591 provides that upon motion and a finding that a civil action or defense was frivolous, the court shall award costs and fees to the prevailing party. A claim is "frivolous" when:
- The party's primary purpose was to harass, embarrass, or injure the other party;
- The party had no reasonable basis to believe the facts underlying the claim were true;
- The party's position was devoid of arguable legal merit.

### E. Inherent Court Authority

Michigan courts possess inherent authority to sanction litigation misconduct, protect the integrity of judicial proceedings, and prevent abuse of the court process. *Maldonado v. Ford Motor Co.*, 476 Mich. 372 (2006).

### F. 28 USC § 1927 — Vexatious Litigation (Federal Claims)

To the extent federal claims under 42 USC § 1983 are implicated in this matter, 28 USC § 1927 authorizes sanctions against any attorney or party who multiplies proceedings unreasonably and vexatiously.
