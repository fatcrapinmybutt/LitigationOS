The Application for Writ of Mandamus having been filed by Petitioner Andrew J. Pigors, and the Court having reviewed the Application, Brief in Support, Appendix, and the responses of Respondent and Real Party in Interest (if any), and being otherwise fully advised in the premises;

The Court finds that:

1. This Court has jurisdiction pursuant to Const 1963, Art 6, § 4 and MCR 7.306.

2. Petitioner has demonstrated a clear legal right to the performance of duties by Respondent, including the enforcement of parenting time under MCL 552.644 and the adjudication of a disqualification motion under MCR 2.003.

3. Respondent has a clear legal duty to enforce parenting time orders, to process disqualification motions without imposing unconstitutional financial barriers, and to conduct proceedings in compliance with due process.

4. The duties at issue are ministerial in nature, requiring performance mandated by statute and court rule.

5. No adequate legal remedy exists, as the Petitioner has exhausted lower court remedies and the pending Court of Appeals proceeding (Case No. 366810) cannot provide the immediate relief necessary to protect the rights of Petitioner and the minor child, L.D.W.

## IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:

### I. WRIT OF MANDAMUS — RECUSAL

**A.** The Hon. Jenny L. McNeill is hereby **directed to immediately recuse** herself from all proceedings in Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP in the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, Muskegon County.

**B.** The Chief Judge of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, or, if the Chief Judge determines reassignment within the circuit is inappropriate, the State Court Administrator, shall **reassign** all proceedings in Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP to a different judge within **14 days** of the date of this Order.

### II. WRIT OF MANDAMUS — PARENTING TIME ENFORCEMENT

**C.** The successor judge, upon reassignment, shall **immediately schedule an emergency hearing** on Petitioner's pending motion(s) to enforce parenting time, to be held no later than **21 days** from the date of reassignment.

**D.** The successor judge shall enforce parenting time pursuant to MCL 552.644, including consideration of:
   - (i) Make-up parenting time for denied parenting time;
   - (ii) A modified parenting time schedule to restore the parent-child relationship;
   - (iii) Appropriate sanctions for documented parenting time violations;
   - (iv) A reunification plan, which may include reunification therapy, if supported by the evidence.

### III. VACATUR OF SPECIFIC ORDERS

**E.** The ex parte order entered on or about August 8, 2025, suspending Petitioner's parenting time, is hereby **vacated** as having been entered without notice, evidentiary hearing, or the affidavits required under MCR 3.206(A).

**F.** The order imposing a $250 bond as a condition precedent to the disqualification motion is hereby **vacated** as an unconstitutional barrier to court access under *Boddie v. Connecticut*, 401 U.S. 371 (1971).

### IV. STAY OF LOWER COURT PROCEEDINGS

**G.** All proceedings in Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP before the 14th Judicial Circuit Court are hereby **stayed** pending reassignment to a successor judge, except for:
   - (i) Emergency matters involving the immediate health or safety of the minor child;
   - (ii) Administrative matters necessary to effectuate reassignment.

**H.** The stay shall automatically dissolve upon reassignment to the successor judge.

### V. DUE PROCESS COMPLIANCE

**I.** The successor judge shall **review** all contempt findings and orders of incarceration entered against Petitioner to determine compliance with the procedural requirements of *In re Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81 (1987), and shall vacate any findings that do not comply with those requirements.

### VI. BEST INTEREST FACTOR ANALYSIS

**J.** The successor judge shall conduct a **de novo best interest factor analysis** under MCL 722.23 before entering or modifying any custody order, with written findings on each of the twelve statutory factors.

### VII. COMPLIANCE REPORTING

**K.** The Chief Judge of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court shall file a **compliance report** with this Court within **30 days** of this Order, confirming:
   - (i) Recusal of Judge McNeill;
   - (ii) Reassignment to a successor judge;
   - (iii) Scheduling of the emergency parenting time hearing.

### VIII. COSTS

**L.** No costs are assessed, a public question being involved.
