## ORDER GRANTING APPLICATION FOR EMERGENCY RELIEF

**PRESENT:** [All Justices present]

---

The Court having considered the Application for Emergency Relief filed by Plaintiff-Appellant Andrew J. Pigors pursuant to MCR 7.311(D), together with the Brief in Support, the Affidavit of Andrew J. Pigors, and all other pleadings and papers filed herein, and having determined that:

### FINDINGS

1. Plaintiff-Appellant has demonstrated that relief is not available by other adequate means, as required by MCR 7.311(D)(1), because:

    a. The 14th Circuit Court has issued twenty-four (24) ex parte orders substantively modifying custody, parenting time, and child support without notice to Plaintiff-Appellant and without evidentiary hearings;

    b. The Court of Appeals proceeding (No. 366810) cannot provide timely relief to address the ongoing irreparable harm;

    c. The trial court has demonstrated through its pattern of conduct that it will not voluntarily restore Plaintiff-Appellant's parenting time.

2. Plaintiff-Appellant has demonstrated that the need for relief is immediate, as required by MCR 7.311(D)(2), because:

    a. Plaintiff-Appellant has been denied all parenting time with his minor child, L.D.W., for more than 200 consecutive days;

    b. The developmental harm to L.D.W. from prolonged parental deprivation compounds daily and cannot be remedied by retroactive schedule adjustments;

    c. Plaintiff-Appellant faces ongoing risk of contempt incarceration under orders entered without constitutionally adequate process.

3. The trial court's issuance of twenty-four (24) ex parte orders — representing 44% of all orders entered (24 of 55) — substantively modifying custody, parenting time, and child support without notice and hearing, raises serious concerns regarding compliance with the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution, MCR 2.119(B), and MCL 722.27(1)(c).

4. The fundamental liberty interest of parents in the care, custody, and control of their children, as recognized in *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 U.S. 57 (2000), and *Stanley v. Illinois*, 405 U.S. 645 (1972), is at stake and requires the immediate exercise of this Court's superintending control authority under Const 1963, art 6, § 4.

---

### IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED:

**I. RESTORATION OF PARENTING TIME**

5. Effective immediately upon entry of this Order, Plaintiff-Appellant Andrew J. Pigors shall be afforded minimum parenting time with his minor child, L.D.W., on the following interim schedule pending further order of this Court or resolution of the underlying proceedings:

    a. Every other weekend from Friday at 6:00 p.m. through Sunday at 6:00 p.m.;

    b. One midweek visit each week on Wednesday from 4:00 p.m. through 8:00 p.m.;

    c. [ANDREW_REQUIRED: Review and adjust proposed parenting time schedule as appropriate — this is a proposed minimum schedule for the Court's consideration];

    d. Parenting time exchanges shall occur at [ANDREW_REQUIRED: proposed exchange location — e.g., a police station, public library, or other neutral location].

6. Defendant Emily A. Watson shall make L.D.W. available for all parenting time ordered herein and shall not interfere with, obstruct, or impede Plaintiff-Appellant's exercise of parenting time.

7. Failure to comply with this Order shall be subject to the contempt powers of this Court.

**II. STAY OF TRIAL COURT ORDERS**

8. All orders entered by Judge McNeill of the 14th Circuit Court in Case No. 2024-001507-DC and related Case No. 2023-5907-PP that affect custody, parenting time, and child support are hereby STAYED pending this Court's further order or the resolution of the pending appeal in the Court of Appeals (No. 366810), whichever occurs first.

9. The stay entered in Paragraph 8 shall not affect orders pertaining to the physical safety of L.D.W. that are based on specific, documented findings of imminent danger entered after notice and hearing consistent with due process requirements.

**III. ASSIGNMENT OF SUBSTITUTE JUDGE**

10. The State Court Administrator is hereby DIRECTED to assign a substitute judge for all further proceedings in 14th Circuit Court Case No. 2024-001507-DC and related Case No. 2023-5907-PP. The substitute judge shall be from outside the 14th Judicial Circuit.

11. Judge McNeill is hereby RECUSED from any further involvement in the above-captioned cases and in any related proceedings involving the parties.

12. The substitute judge shall, within thirty (30) days of assignment, conduct a status conference with all parties to review the current status of the case and establish a schedule for further proceedings, including but not limited to a best interest evaluation under MCL 722.23.

**IV. COMPLIANCE DIRECTIVE**

13. Defendant Emily A. Watson is hereby DIRECTED to comply with all parenting time provisions of this Order and to facilitate, rather than impede, the restoration of the parent-child relationship between Plaintiff-Appellant and L.D.W.

14. Any interference with the parenting time schedule ordered herein shall constitute contempt of this Court and may result in sanctions including modification of the custody arrangement.

**V. CONTEMPT ENFORCEMENT HALT**

15. All contempt proceedings and contempt enforcement against Plaintiff-Appellant Andrew J. Pigors in Case No. 2024-001507-DC and Case No. 2023-5907-PP are hereby STAYED pending further order of this Court.

16. Any existing warrants for Plaintiff-Appellant's arrest based on contempt findings in the above-captioned cases shall be QUASHED during the pendency of this stay.

17. The trial court and all law enforcement agencies within the State of Michigan are directed to take notice of this stay and to refrain from executing any contempt-related arrest or incarceration orders in the above-captioned cases.

**VI. RESPONSE AND FURTHER PROCEEDINGS**

18. Defendant Emily A. Watson shall file any response to the Application for Emergency Relief within fourteen (14) days of service of this Order.

19. The Clerk of this Court shall schedule this matter for further consideration within thirty (30) days of the filing of Defendant's response, or within forty-five (45) days of the entry of this Order if no response is filed.

20. The parties shall participate in good faith in any mediation, evaluation, or other process ordered by the substitute judge.

**VII. SERVICE**

21. Plaintiff-Appellant shall serve a copy of this Order upon:

    a. Defendant Emily A. Watson;
    b. Counsel for Defendant, if any;
    c. Clerk, 14th Circuit Court, Muskegon County;
    d. Clerk, Michigan Court of Appeals;
    e. Friend of the Court, Muskegon County;
    f. State Court Administrator.

22. Service shall be completed within seven (7) days of the entry of this Order.

**VIII. RETENTION OF JURISDICTION**

23. This Court retains jurisdiction over this matter for the purpose of enforcing the terms of this Order, modifying the parenting time schedule as appropriate, and addressing any further issues arising from the underlying proceedings.

24. This Order shall remain in full force and effect until further order of this Court, resolution of the pending appeal in the Court of Appeals (No. 366810), or resolution of the underlying proceedings, whichever occurs first.

---

**IT IS SO ORDERED.**
