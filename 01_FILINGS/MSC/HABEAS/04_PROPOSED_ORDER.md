## ORDER

This matter having come before the Court on Petitioner Andrew J. Pigors's Petition for Writ of Habeas Corpus filed pursuant to MCR 3.303, MCL 600.4301 et seq., and Const 1963, art 1, § 10, and the Court being fully advised in the premises;

The Court having reviewed the Petition, Brief in Support, Affidavit of Andrew J. Pigors, and all attachments and exhibits filed therewith;

The Court finding that:

1. L.D.W. (Lincoln David Watson), a minor child, has been completely denied all contact with his father, Andrew J. Pigors, for **200+ consecutive days**;

2. No valid court order exists authorizing the complete termination of all contact between L.D.W. and his father;

3. No termination of parental rights has been adjudicated under MCL 712A.19b;

4. The practical effect of the current situation is a de facto termination of the parent-child relationship without the procedural safeguards required by the Michigan and United States Constitutions;

5. The 14th Judicial Circuit Court has failed to enforce mandatory statutory protections under MCL 552.644;

6. Respondent Emily A. Watson has engaged in a pattern of interference with Petitioner's parental rights, with 305 documented incidents;

7. L.D.W.'s constitutional right to maintain a relationship with both parents is being violated;

8. Irreparable harm accrues daily to both L.D.W. and Petitioner;

9. No adequate alternative remedy exists;

---

## IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:

### 1. Issuance of Writ

The Writ of Habeas Corpus is hereby **ISSUED** and directed to Respondent Emily A. Watson.

### 2. Production of the Child

Respondent Emily A. Watson **SHALL produce L.D.W.** (Lincoln David Watson) at the following location within **forty-eight (48) hours** of service of this Order:

**Location:** _________________________________ [To be designated by the Court]

**Date:** _____________

**Time:** _____________

Failure to produce L.D.W. as ordered shall constitute **contempt of this Court** and may result in sanctions including but not limited to arrest, fine, and modification of custody.

### 3. Interim Parenting Time

Pending further proceedings in this matter or in the underlying case (No. 2024-001507-DC), the following **interim parenting time schedule** is hereby ordered:

a. Petitioner shall have parenting time with L.D.W. on **alternating weekends** from Friday at 5:00 p.m. to Sunday at 5:00 p.m., commencing the first weekend following L.D.W.'s production under this Order;

b. Petitioner shall have **midweek parenting time** every Wednesday from 4:00 p.m. to 7:00 p.m.;

c. Petitioner shall have **daily telephone or video contact** with L.D.W. at a regular time to be agreed upon by the parties or, absent agreement, at 7:00 p.m. each evening for not less than fifteen (15) minutes;

d. Neither party shall interfere with the other's parenting time. Any interference shall be reported to this Court immediately.

### 4. Reunification Therapy

a. Within **fourteen (14) days** of this Order, the parties shall enroll L.D.W. in a **reunification therapy program** with a licensed therapist mutually agreed upon by the parties or, absent agreement, appointed by the Court;

b. Both parents shall cooperate fully with the reunification therapist and follow all therapeutic recommendations;

c. The cost of reunification therapy shall be borne by **Respondent Emily A. Watson**, whose conduct necessitated the therapeutic intervention;

d. The reunification therapist shall provide written progress reports to this Court at thirty (30)-day intervals.

### 5. Judicial Recusal and Case Reassignment

a. The Honorable **Jenny L. McNeill** is hereby **RECUSED** from all further proceedings involving Petitioner Andrew J. Pigors and Respondent Emily A. Watson in Case No. 2024-001507-DC, Case No. 2023-5907-PP, and any related matters;

b. The Chief Judge of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, or the State Court Administrative Office, shall **reassign** all pending matters to a different judge within **fourteen (14) days** of this Order;

c. The basis for recusal is Judge McNeill's sustained pattern of failure to enforce mandatory statutory protections under MCL 552.644 and failure to protect the fundamental parental rights of Petitioner and the constitutional rights of L.D.W.

### 6. Show Cause — Contempt

Respondent Emily A. Watson is hereby **ORDERED TO SHOW CAUSE** within **twenty-one (21) days** of service of this Order why she should not be held in contempt for:

a. Willful denial of Petitioner's parental access for 200+ days;

b. 305 documented incidents of interference with the parent-child relationship;

c. Violation of the fundamental rights of both Petitioner and L.D.W.

### 7. Protective Provisions

a. Neither party shall make disparaging remarks about the other parent in the presence of L.D.W.;

b. Neither party shall discuss the details of this litigation with L.D.W.;

c. Neither party shall interfere with L.D.W.'s relationship with the other parent in any manner;

d. Respondent shall immediately restore Petitioner's access to all communication channels with L.D.W. (telephone, electronic, written);

e. Respondent shall provide Petitioner with current information regarding L.D.W.'s school enrollment, medical providers, extracurricular activities, and general welfare within **seven (7) days** of this Order.

### 8. Enforcement

a. Any violation of this Order shall be punishable as **contempt of the Michigan Supreme Court**;

b. The Muskegon County Sheriff's Office is authorized to enforce this Order, including the production of L.D.W. if Respondent fails to comply with Section 2 above;

c. This Order shall remain in effect until further order of this Court or the reassigned lower court judge.

### 9. Declaratory Relief

This Court **DECLARES** that the complete denial of all contact between a parent and child for 200+ consecutive days, absent a valid court order issued with proper procedural safeguards, violates:

a. The Michigan Constitution, Const 1963, art 1, § 17 (due process);
b. The Michigan Constitution, Const 1963, art 1, § 10 (habeas corpus);
c. The United States Constitution, amend XIV, § 1 (due process and equal protection);
d. The fundamental liberty interests recognized in *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 U.S. 57 (2000), *Stanley v. Illinois*, 405 U.S. 645 (1972), and *Santosky v. Kramer*, 455 U.S. 745 (1982).
