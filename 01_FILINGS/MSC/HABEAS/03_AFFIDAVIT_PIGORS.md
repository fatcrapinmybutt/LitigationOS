## AFFIDAVIT

I, Andrew J. Pigors, being first duly sworn, depose and state as follows:

### Personal Information and Relationship

1. I am over the age of eighteen (18) years and am competent to testify to the matters stated herein. I have personal knowledge of the facts set forth in this affidavit.

2. I reside at 1977 Whitehall Rd, Laketon Township, Muskegon County, Michigan 49445.

3. I am the biological father of L.D.W. (L.D.W. (minor child, born November 9, 2022)), a minor child. I have been L.D.W.'s father since the day he was born, and I love him more than anything in this world.

4. Emily A. Watson ("Respondent") is L.D.W.'s biological mother. Respondent currently has physical custody of L.D.W.

5. The custody dispute between myself and Respondent is pending in the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, Muskegon County, under Case No. 2024-001507-DC, with the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill presiding.

### The Separation

6. As of the date of this affidavit, I have been completely denied all contact with my son L.D.W. for **more than two hundred (200+) consecutive days** and counting.

7. My last contact with L.D.W. was on or about **[DATE TO BE INSERTED]**. Since that date, I have had:

   - **Zero (0)** in-person visits
   - **Zero (0)** telephone calls
   - **Zero (0)** video calls (FaceTime, Zoom, or otherwise)
   - **Zero (0)** text messages or emails
   - **Zero (0)** letters or cards sent or received
   - **Zero (0)** contact of any kind, directly or through third parties

8. I did not voluntarily relinquish contact with L.D.W. I have fought every single day of more than 200 days to see my son. The separation was imposed upon me by Respondent's willful refusal to allow contact and the lower court's failure to enforce my rights.

### My Efforts to Maintain Contact

9. Throughout the 200+ day period, I have made continuous, documented efforts to exercise my parental rights, including but not limited to:

   a. Filing multiple motions with the 14th Circuit Court requesting enforcement of parenting time under MCL 552.644;

   b. Appearing at every scheduled court hearing regarding custody and parenting time;

   c. Attempting to communicate with Respondent to arrange parenting time, which attempts were refused or ignored;

   d. Contacting L.D.W.'s school, medical providers, and other institutions to remain informed and involved in his life, which efforts were obstructed by Respondent;

   e. Requesting the appointment of a guardian ad litem to investigate the situation;

   f. Filing an appeal with the Michigan Court of Appeals (No. 366810);

   g. Filing an Application for Writ of Mandamus with the Michigan Supreme Court;

   h. Filing an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis with the Michigan Supreme Court.

10. I have never stopped trying to be L.D.W.'s father. I have never abandoned my child. Every day that I am denied contact, I feel as though a piece of my heart is missing.

### Respondent's Interference

11. Respondent Emily A. Watson has engaged in a systematic campaign to sever my relationship with L.D.W. I have documented **three hundred and five (305) separate incidents** of interference with my parental rights, including but not limited to:

    a. **Denying court-ordered parenting time** — Respondent has repeatedly refused to make L.D.W. available for scheduled parenting time, offering pretextual excuses or simply failing to respond;

    b. **Blocking communication** — Respondent has blocked my phone number, returned my letters, and prevented L.D.W. from communicating with me by any means;

    c. **Withholding information** — Respondent has refused to provide information about L.D.W.'s health, education, school activities, medical appointments, and general welfare;

    d. **Making false allegations** — Respondent has made allegations against me that are false and designed to justify the continued denial of my parental access;

    e. **Alienating behaviors** — Based on the totality of Respondent's conduct, the 305 documented interference incidents by the Watson family demonstrate extreme, systematic parental alienation.

12. I believe that Respondent's goal is not to protect L.D.W., but to **permanently erase me from L.D.W.'s life** — to accomplish through persistent interference what she could not accomplish through legal process, because no court has ever found me to be an unfit parent.

### The Lower Court's Failure to Act

13. Judge Jenny L. McNeill has presided over the custody proceedings since their inception.

14. Despite my motions for enforcement and the overwhelming evidence of Respondent's interference, Judge McNeill has:

    a. **Failed to enforce parenting time** as mandated by MCL 552.644;

    b. **Failed to order makeup parenting time**, despite the statute's mandatory "shall" language;

    c. **Failed to hold Respondent in contempt** for her willful refusal to comply with court orders;

    d. **Failed to modify the custody arrangement** to protect L.D.W.'s relationship with me;

    e. **Failed to appoint a guardian ad litem** to investigate L.D.W.'s welfare;

    f. **Failed to take any meaningful enforcement action** despite 200+ days of total denial and 305 documented interference incidents.

15. I have lost confidence in Judge McNeill's willingness or ability to protect my parental rights or L.D.W.'s best interests. The pattern of inaction is not inadvertent — it is systematic.

### No Valid Order Authorizes This Separation

16. **No court has terminated my parental rights** under MCL 712A.19b or any other provision of Michigan law.

17. **No court has entered a formal order** suspending all parenting time following a proper evidentiary hearing with procedural safeguards.

18. **No court has found me to be an unfit parent** or determined that contact with me poses a danger to L.D.W.

19. To the best of my knowledge, there is **no lawful basis** for the complete denial of all contact between L.D.W. and me. The separation is the product of Respondent's unilateral decision, enabled by the lower court's inaction.

### Impact on L.D.W.

20. I am gravely concerned about the impact of this prolonged separation on L.D.W.'s emotional, psychological, and developmental well-being.

21. L.D.W. is a minor child who, prior to this separation, had an active and loving relationship with me. He knew me as his father. He relied on me.

22. Research in child development and psychology consistently establishes that prolonged separation from a parent causes:

    a. **Attachment disruption** — damaging the child's ability to form and maintain healthy relationships;

    b. **Anxiety and depression** — resulting from the sudden, unexplained loss of a parent;

    c. **Identity confusion** — particularly as the child loses access to one half of their family, heritage, and history;

    d. **Behavioral problems** — including acting out, regression, and academic difficulties;

    e. **Long-term psychological harm** — including increased risk of mental health issues in adolescence and adulthood.

23. I believe that every additional day of separation deepens these harms and makes eventual reunification more difficult. The psychological damage is **cumulative and accelerating**.

24. I am not asking this Court to take L.D.W. away from his mother. I am asking this Court to give L.D.W. his father back. A child should not have to lose one parent in order to have the other.

### No Adequate Alternative Remedy

25. I have pursued every available legal remedy:

    a. **Lower court motions** — filed and effectively ignored;

    b. **Court of Appeals appeal (No. 366810)** — pending, but the appellate timeline is too slow to address the ongoing harm;

    c. **MSC Mandamus petition** — filed, pending;

    d. **MSC IFP application** — filed, pending.

26. None of these remedies provides the **immediate relief** that L.D.W. and I need. The Court of Appeals could take months or longer. Mandamus compels the judge but does not directly compel Respondent to produce L.D.W. Contempt requires the lower court's cooperation, which has not been forthcoming.

27. Habeas corpus is the only remedy that can produce **immediate, direct relief**: an order commanding Respondent to produce L.D.W. and restore the parent-child relationship.

### What I Am Asking For

28. I am asking this Honorable Court to:

    a. Issue the Writ of Habeas Corpus directing Respondent to produce L.D.W.;
    b. Order immediate interim parenting time so L.D.W. and I can begin to rebuild our relationship;
    c. Order reunification therapy to address the harm caused by 200+ days of separation and alienation;
    d. Order the recusal of Judge McNeill and reassignment to a new judge;
    e. Protect L.D.W.'s right to know and love both of his parents.

29. I make this affidavit in support of my Petition for Writ of Habeas Corpus, and I attest that every statement herein is true and correct to the best of my knowledge, information, and belief.
