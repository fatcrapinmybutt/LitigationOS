## EMERGENCY DESIGNATION

### Request Expedited Consideration

This petition should be designated as an **EMERGENCY** filing. To request expedited consideration:

1. **Cover letter** — State clearly that this is an emergency. Explain:
   - L.D.W. has been denied all contact with his father for 200+ days
   - Irreparable harm accrues daily
   - No adequate alternative remedy exists
   - A child's developmental needs cannot wait for ordinary appellate timelines

2. **"Emergency" label** — Mark the filing envelope and cover page:
   ```
   *** EMERGENCY — EXPEDITED CONSIDERATION REQUESTED ***
   ```

   - **Phone:** (517) 373-0120

   - Ask about the Court's procedures for expedited review

## STRATEGIC NOTES

### Relationship to Mandamus Petition
- The **mandamus** petition asks the MSC to order Judge McNeill to act
- The **habeas** petition asks the MSC to order Watson to produce L.D.W.
- These are **complementary, not duplicative** — they target different parties and different failures
- The cover letter should explain why both filings are necessary

### Key Arguments to Emphasize
1. **200+ days** — the sheer duration is the most powerful fact. Lead with it.
2. **No valid order** — there is no termination order, no order suspending contact. The separation has no legal basis.
3. **The child** — this is about L.D.W.'s rights as much as Andrew's. The child is the person being restrained.
4. **Exhaustion** — every other remedy has been tried and failed. Habeas is the last resort.
5. **Emergency** — every day matters. A child's time cannot be refunded.

### Common Respondent Arguments (anticipate and preempt)
- "Habeas doesn't apply to custody" → Brief addresses this in Argument II with case law
- "There's an adequate remedy at law" → Brief addresses this; every remedy has failed
- "Father is unfit/dangerous" → No finding of unfitness exists; burden is on Respondent to prove
- "Best interest of the child" → 200+ days without a parent is NEVER in a child's best interest
