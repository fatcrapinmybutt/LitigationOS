### III. STRATEGIC CONSIDERATIONS

#### A. Filing Sequence — Emergency Application vs. Declaratory Judgment

**RECOMMENDED APPROACH:** File both packages simultaneously or within the same day.

- The Emergency Application (Package 1) seeks immediate, case-specific relief
- The Declaratory Judgment (Package 2) seeks broader legal declarations with statewide significance
- Filing both together demonstrates the breadth of the constitutional violations and the need for both immediate and structural relief
- Reference the Declaratory Judgment Complaint in the Emergency Application, and vice versa

**ALTERNATIVE APPROACH:** File the Emergency Application first, then file the Declaratory Judgment within 7-14 days.

- This approach prioritizes getting the emergency request before the Court
- The Declaratory Judgment can be filed as a companion action with reference to the pending Emergency Application
- [ANDREW_REQUIRED: Decide which approach to take and note your decision]

#### B. Exhibit Strategy for the Ex Parte Pattern

The strongest possible presentation of the ex parte pattern includes:

1. **Summary Exhibit:** A single-page table listing all 24 ex parte orders by date, subject matter, and impact — this is the most important exhibit for the Declaratory Judgment

2. **Individual Orders:** Copies of as many of the 24 ex parte orders as you can obtain — attach as a separate exhibit binder

3. **Docket Sheet:** The official court docket showing the total number of orders entered (to verify the 44% calculation)

4. **Timeline Graphic:** [OPTIONAL] A visual timeline showing the progressive restriction of parenting time through ex parte orders
