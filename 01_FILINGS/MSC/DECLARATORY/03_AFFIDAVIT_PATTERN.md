### COMPARISON TO NORMAL JUDICIAL PRACTICE

15. I understand, based on publicly available judicial statistics and legal scholarship, that the normal rate of ex parte orders in comparable custody proceedings is less than 2% of all orders entered. The trial court's rate of 44% (24 of 55 orders) is approximately nine times this average.

16. I further understand that ex parte orders in custody proceedings are typically reserved for:

    a. Genuine emergencies involving imminent risk of physical harm to a child;
    b. Situations where delay for notice would result in the child being removed from the jurisdiction;
    c. Temporary protective orders subject to prompt post-deprivation hearing.

17. The ex parte orders in this case do not fit these categories. They were not limited to emergencies, they addressed long-term custody and support arrangements, and they were not subject to prompt post-deprivation hearings at which I could challenge them.
