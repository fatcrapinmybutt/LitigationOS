## APPLICATION FOR WAIVER OF FEES AND COSTS
### (In Forma Pauperis — MCR 7.302(D)(2); MCR 2.002)

---

TO THE HONORABLE CHIEF JUSTICE AND JUSTICES OF THE MICHIGAN SUPREME COURT:

NOW COMES Petitioner, **ANDREW J. PIGORS**, appearing Pro Se, and respectfully applies to this Honorable Court for leave to proceed *in forma pauperis* pursuant to MCR 7.302(D)(2) and MCR 2.002, and in support thereof states as follows:

### I. JURISDICTIONAL STATEMENT

1. This Court has jurisdiction pursuant to Const. 1963, Art. 6, § 4, which vests the Michigan Supreme Court with general superintending control over all courts.

2. Petitioner seeks to file an Application for Leave to Appeal and/or Petition for Extraordinary Writs (mandamus, superintending control, and habeas corpus) arising from proceedings in the 14th Judicial Circuit Court — Family Division, Muskegon County (Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP), currently on appeal before the Court of Appeals (Case No. 366810).

3. The filing fee for this Court is **$375.00**, which Petitioner is unable to pay without being deprived of the necessities of life for himself and his dependents.

### II. STATUTORY AND RULE AUTHORITY

4. **MCR 7.302(D)(2)** provides that a party who is unable to pay the filing fee may apply for leave to proceed in forma pauperis.

5. **MCR 2.002** establishes the standards and procedures for waiver of fees and costs in Michigan courts, providing that fees shall be waived where a party demonstrates inability to pay.

6. **SCAO Form MC 20** (Affidavit and Order, Suspension of Fees/Costs) is the approved form for fee waiver applications and is substantially incorporated in the attached Affidavit of Inability to Pay (Exhibit A).

7. The United States Supreme Court has held that the right of access to courts is a fundamental right, and an indigent party cannot be denied access to the judicial system solely due to inability to pay filing fees. *Boddie v. Connecticut*, 401 U.S. 371, 374 (1971).

### III. STATEMENT OF CLAIMS

8. Petitioner seeks extraordinary relief from this Court based on the following claims:

   **a. Judicial Misconduct and Denial of Due Process (42 USC § 1983)**
   - The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill has engaged in a pattern and practice of judicial conduct that violates Petitioner's rights under the First, Fourth, Fifth, and Fourteenth Amendments to the United States Constitution, and Article 1, §§ 2, 3, 13, 17, and 20 of the Michigan Constitution of 1963.

   **b. Wrongful Contempt and Incarceration**
   - Petitioner has been subjected to multiple contempt findings and jailings totaling 59+ days of incarceration, imposed without adequate procedural safeguards, resulting in the loss of three (3) jobs, including USPS employment, and two (2) or more homes.

   **c. Denial of Parental Rights**
   - Petitioner has been denied all parenting time with his minor son, L.D.W., for 332+ cumulative days (207+ days since the ex parte suspension order of August 8, 2025), without proper evidentiary hearing and in violation of MCL 722.27a and the Parenting Time Guideline.

   **d. Improper Child Support Order**
   - A child support obligation of $1,056.60 per month was imposed without a proper evidentiary hearing and without consideration of Petitioner's actual ability to pay, in violation of the Michigan Child Support Formula and due process.

   **e. Ex Parte Proceedings**
   - The trial court conducted an excessive number of ex parte proceedings (documented ex parte rate of 44% — 24 of 55 orders entered without notice), denying Petitioner notice and the opportunity to be heard.

   **f. Violation of Right of Access to Courts**
   - A $250.00 bond was imposed as a precondition to filing any motion, without indigency inquiry, effectively barring Petitioner from the court.

### IV. FINANCIAL INABILITY TO PAY

9. As set forth in detail in the attached **Affidavit of Inability to Pay Costs** (Exhibit A), Petitioner is financially unable to pay the $375.00 filing fee required by this Court.

10. Petitioner's financial hardship is directly caused by the very judicial conduct he seeks to challenge, including wrongful incarceration causing job loss, litigation-induced poverty, and court-ordered financial obligations imposed without due process.

11. Petitioner's total documented financial damages from these proceedings exceed **$82,250**.

### V. MERITORIOUS CLAIMS

12. The underlying claims are not frivolous. They are supported by:
   - A comprehensive evidence archive of **224,000+ evidence rows** compiled over 18+ months of litigation;
   - A documented **91% parental alienation score** based on independent behavioral indicators;
   - An independently verified **44% ex parte rate** (24 of 55 orders) in the trial court proceedings;
   - Contemporaneous audio recordings, court transcripts, and documentary evidence of judicial misconduct.

13. The claims raise substantial questions of constitutional law regarding judicial accountability, parental rights, and due process that merit this Court's review.

WHEREFORE, Petitioner respectfully requests that this Honorable Court:

   a. **GRANT** this Application to proceed *in forma pauperis*;
