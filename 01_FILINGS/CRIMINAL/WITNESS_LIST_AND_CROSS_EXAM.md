## PART II: CROSS-EXAMINATION PLAN

### A. Cross-Examination of Prosecution Witnesses

> **NOTE:** The prosecution's witness list has not yet been provided. The following anticipates likely prosecution witnesses based on the nature of the charges. This plan will be updated when the actual witness list is received.

---

### Anticipated Prosecution Witness 1: Arresting Officer / Responding Officer

**Objectives:**
- Establish that the officer was aware of the threat against Andrew
- Elicit the body camera statement about five inmates planning to kill Andrew
- Show the officer's own assessment confirms Andrew was the target, not the aggressor
- Establish timeline inconsistencies in the prosecution's theory

**Cross-Examination Questions:**

1. Officer, you were present at the Muskegon County Jail on [VERIFY — date], correct?
2. You were wearing a body-worn camera during the incident, correct?
3. Your body camera was activated and recording during the incident, correct?
4. You are aware that your body camera captured audio and video of your interactions, correct?
5. On that body camera footage, you made a statement about inmates planning to harm Mr. Pigors, correct?
6. Specifically, you stated that **five inmates had planned to kill Mr. Pigors**, correct?
7. You made that statement based on information you received during your investigation, correct?
8. Who provided you with that information?
9. Did you consider that information credible at the time?
10. Did you take any steps to protect Mr. Pigors from that threat?
11. Were you aware of any threats against Mr. Pigors **before** the incident occurred?
12. Was the jail administration aware of threats against Mr. Pigors?
13. Were any protective measures taken — such as separating Mr. Pigors from the threatening inmates?
14. If a person learns that five people are planning to kill them, would you consider that a reasonable basis to fear for their life?
15. In your training and experience, would a reasonable person in Mr. Pigors's situation believe their life was in imminent danger?

**Impeachment Points:**
- If the officer denies or minimizes the statement → Play body camera footage
- If the officer claims the threat was not credible → Challenge: then why did you memorialize it on camera?
- If the officer claims Andrew was the aggressor → Confront with five-on-one disparity

---

### Anticipated Prosecution Witness 2: Alleged Victim(s) / Complaining Inmate(s)

**Objectives:**
- Establish that the witness was part of or aware of the plan to attack Andrew
- Expose motive to fabricate — the "victims" were the would-be killers
- Show prior threats or hostile conduct toward Andrew

**Cross-Examination Questions:**

1. You were an inmate at the Muskegon County Jail on [VERIFY — date], correct?
2. Were you housed in the same unit as Mr. Pigors?
3. How long had you been housed with Mr. Pigors before the incident?
4. Did you have any conflict with Mr. Pigors before the incident?
5. Did you discuss Mr. Pigors with other inmates before the incident?
6. Were you aware of any plan by inmates to harm Mr. Pigors?
7. Did you participate in any plan to attack Mr. Pigors?
8. Did any other inmate tell you they planned to harm Mr. Pigors?
9. How many inmates were involved in the altercation?
10. Who made the first physical contact?
11. Were you acting alone, or were other inmates involved?
12. A law enforcement officer has stated on body camera that five inmates planned to kill Mr. Pigors. Do you dispute that?
13. If five people attacked one person, who is the victim?

**Impeachment Points:**
- Prior criminal record (admissible under MRE 609 for felony convictions)
- Motive to testify favorably for prosecution (plea deals, sentence reductions)
- Inconsistent statements to police vs. testimony

---

### Anticipated Prosecution Witness 3: Investigating Detective

**Objectives:**
- Expose gaps in the investigation
- Establish that exculpatory evidence was collected but not disclosed
- Show the investigation focused only on charging Andrew, not on the inmates who threatened him

**Cross-Examination Questions:**

1. You investigated the incident at the Muskegon County Jail on [VERIFY — date], correct?
2. You collected body camera footage from officers present, correct?
3. Did you review the body camera footage?
4. On that footage, did you hear an officer state that five inmates planned to kill Mr. Pigors?
5. Did you investigate whether five inmates had, in fact, planned to kill Mr. Pigors?
6. Did you interview the other inmates involved?
7. Did any of the five inmates who planned to kill Mr. Pigors face charges?
8. Why was Mr. Pigors charged, but the inmates who planned to murder him were not?
9. Did you provide the body camera footage to the Defendant or his attorney?
10. Are you aware that the Defendant filed a FOIA request for this footage?
11. Are you aware that the Defendant filed a formal Brady demand for exculpatory evidence?
12. Has the body camera footage been preserved?
13. If not, why not?
