## V. SCREENING UNDER 28 U.S.C. § 1915(e)(2)

21. Plaintiff acknowledges that under 28 U.S.C. § 1915(e)(2), the Court shall dismiss the case if it determines the action is: (a) frivolous or malicious; (b) fails to state a claim; or (c) seeks monetary relief against a defendant who is immune. Plaintiff's Complaint is none of these:

    a. **Not frivolous:** The Complaint alleges specific constitutional violations supported by documented evidence, including 24 ex parte orders, 59 days of wrongful incarceration, and more than 200 consecutive days of parent-child separation.

    b. **States plausible claims:** Seven counts under established federal law (§ 1983, § 1985(3), First and Fourteenth Amendments) supported by specific factual allegations satisfying *Twombly*/*Iqbal*.

    c. **Immunity properly addressed:** Judicial immunity is pierced by non-judicial acts, absence of jurisdiction, and conspiracy. Official-capacity claims seek only prospective relief per *Pulliam v. Allen*. Municipal defendant has no immunity per *Owen v. City of Independence*.
