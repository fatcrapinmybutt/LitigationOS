# FILING READINESS SCORE -- Federal Section 1983 (42 USC 1983)

---

## OVERALL SCORE: 55/100 -- PROCEED WITH CAUTION

| Element | Score | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Factual basis | 85/100 | Strong evidence of due process violations |
| Constitutional theory | 80/100 | Clearly established parental rights |
| Pleading standard (Iqbal/Twombly) | 60/100 | Must be very specific -- no conclusory allegations |
| Immunity hurdles | 30/100 | Judicial immunity is nearly insurmountable |
| Timing | 40/100 | Should wait for state proceedings to conclude |
| Strategic value | 75/100 | Significant pressure/leverage even if dismissed |

## RECOMMENDED SEQUENCE
1. Complete COA appeal first
2. Complete JTC complaint
3. Complete state tort actions (Watson, Shady Oaks)
4. THEN evaluate federal filing based on outcomes

## RISKS
- **Sanctions risk:** If filing clearly barred by immunity, court may sanction
- **Judicial hostility:** Federal judges protective of judicial immunity
- **Cost:** $405 filing fee (or IFP application required)
- **Time:** Federal litigation can take 2+ years

## BENEFITS EVEN IF DISMISSED
- Creates federal record of constitutional violations
- Generates discovery that may help state cases
- Pressure on institutional defendants
- Establishes pattern for any future federal claims
