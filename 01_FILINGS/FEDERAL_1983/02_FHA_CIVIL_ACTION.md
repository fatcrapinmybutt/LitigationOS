## FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

### A. Plaintiff's Home Ownership and Tenancy

1. Plaintiff is the lawful titleholder of a manufactured home at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community. [CL-0001]
2. Plaintiff maintained a lot lease with Shady Oaks for the placement of his manufactured home.
3. Plaintiff exercised his housing rights lawfully at all relevant times.

### B. Defendants' Corporate Structure and Control

4. Homes of America, LLC manages manufactured housing communities including Shady Oaks and acted through on-site personnel. [CL-0004]
5. Alden Global Capital and Partridge Securities exercised ownership and/or control over HOA and park entities in an enterprise structure designed to shield liability while directing operations. [CL-0005]
6. At all relevant times, Defendants acted in concert as a unified enterprise.

### C. Pattern of Discriminatory and Retaliatory Conduct

7. **Ledger Fraud**: Defendants systematically manipulated Plaintiff's lot rent ledger, fabricating arrears and layering unauthorized fees. [CL-0009, CL-0021]
8. **Payment Rejection**: Defendants refused to accept or properly credit valid payments, including MDHHS and TrueNorth housing assistance, while accepting identical payment forms from other tenants. [CL-0009]
9. **Coerced Lease**: On March 26, 2024, Plaintiff was coerced under threat of eviction into signing an off-site lease at different terms and higher rent. [CL-0010]
10. **Sewage Conditions**: From late 2024 through mid-2025, raw sewage was present near Plaintiff's lot; EGLE issued violations. Defendants continued billing utilities without remediation credits. [CL-0008]
11. **Blocked Sales**: Defendants interfered with Plaintiff's attempts to sell his mobile home on at least three (3) separate occasions by:
    - Telling prospective buyers Plaintiff "no longer owns his home"
    - Telling prospective buyers Plaintiff had "abandoned" the home
    - Representing a "lawyer is working on taking/selling" the home
    - Refusing buyer applications without lawful basis
12. **Slander of Title**: Community manager made false statements to third parties regarding Plaintiff's ownership and occupancy status.
13. **Constructive Eviction**: Through cumulative predatory conduct, Defendants constructively evicted Plaintiff.
14. **Property Destruction**: On July 17, 2025, with sheriff presence, locks were drilled, Plaintiff's home cleared, property and appliances removed or destroyed. A "FREE" sign was placed within 24 hours. [CL-0012]
15. **Custody Weaponization**: As a direct result, Plaintiff became homeless; "unstable housing" was weaponized in custody proceedings to suspend his parenting time. [CL-0014]

## COUNT I: DISCRIMINATORY TERMS, CONDITIONS, AND PRIVILEGES
### (42 USC § 3604(b))

16. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.
17. Defendants violated 42 USC § 3604(b) by discriminating against Plaintiff in the terms, conditions, and privileges of his tenancy, including:
    - Imposing fabricated charges and unauthorized fees not imposed on similarly situated tenants
    - Refusing to accept valid government housing assistance payments
    - Coercing Plaintiff into signing a new lease under duress at different terms
    - Failing to remediate sewage conditions while continuing to bill utilities
    - Selectively and harshly enforcing community rules against Plaintiff
18. These discriminatory terms and conditions were imposed as part of a pattern of conduct targeting Plaintiff specifically.
19. As a direct and proximate result, Plaintiff suffered actual damages including loss of his home, personal property, housing stability, and emotional distress.

---

## COUNT II: RETALIATION
### (42 USC § 3617)

20. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.
21. Defendants retaliated against Plaintiff for exercising his lawful housing rights, including:
    - Escalating eviction proceedings after Plaintiff reported sewage violations to EGLE
    - Escalating eviction proceedings after Plaintiff challenged fabricated ledger charges
    - Blocking Plaintiff's sale of his mobile home three (3) times after Plaintiff asserted his rights
    - Filing a fraudulent res judicata motion through attorney Jeremy Brown
    - Destroying Plaintiff's property and home during the July 17, 2025 set-out
22. Each retaliatory act was motivated by Plaintiff's exercise of protected housing rights.
23. As a direct and proximate result, Plaintiff suffered actual damages.

---

## COUNT III: INTERFERENCE WITH HOUSING RIGHTS
### (42 USC § 3617)

24. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.
25. Defendants coerced, intimidated, threatened, and interfered with Plaintiff in the exercise and enjoyment of his housing rights, including:
    - Interfering with Plaintiff's right to sell his manufactured home by making false statements to prospective purchasers
    - Interfering with Plaintiff's right to peaceful occupancy through fabricated charges and hostile conditions
    - Interfering with Plaintiff's right to government housing assistance by rejecting valid payments
    - Continuing to interfere with Plaintiff's housing rights by weaponizing the resulting homelessness in custody proceedings
26. Defendants' interference was intentional, willful, and malicious.
27. As a direct and proximate result, Plaintiff suffered actual damages.
