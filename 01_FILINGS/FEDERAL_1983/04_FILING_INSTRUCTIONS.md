## Three Filing Tracks: HUD Administrative | Federal Court (WDMI) | State MDCR

---

## TRACK 1: HUD ADMINISTRATIVE COMPLAINT (42 USC § 3610)

### Filing Procedure

**Step 1: Prepare Complaint**
- [ ] Complete HUD Form 903 (Housing Discrimination Complaint) or file online
- [ ] Include: full name, address, phone, email
- [ ] Identify all respondents with addresses
- [ ] Describe each discriminatory act with dates
- [ ] Note property address: Lot 17, 1977 Whitehall Rd, Shady Oaks MHC, Muskegon County, MI
- [ ] State whether filed with other agencies

**Step 2: File with HUD Region V**

| Method | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Online** | https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/fair_housing_equal_opp/online-complaint |
| **Phone** | (800) 669-9777 (Fair Housing Hotline) |
| **Mail** | HUD FHEO Region V, 77 W. Jackson Blvd, Suite 2101, Chicago, IL 60604 |
| **Email** | Contact Region V office for email submission |
| **In Person** | Grand Rapids HUD Field Office (nearest to Muskegon) |

**Step 3: Post-Filing**
1. HUD acknowledges receipt and assigns investigator
2. HUD notifies respondents within 10 days
3. Investigation (target: 100 days)
4. Conciliation attempted during investigation
5. Determination: cause or no cause
6. If cause → administrative hearing (ALJ) or DOJ referral
7. **At any point**: Complainant may elect to have case heard in federal court (42 USC § 3612(a))

### Deadline
- **1 year from last discriminatory act** (42 USC § 3610(a)(1)(A)(i))
- Last act: July 17, 2025 → Deadline: **July 17, 2026**

### Advantages
- No filing fee
- HUD investigates (no discovery burden on complainant)
- Conciliation may produce faster resolution
- Preserves right to go to federal court

### Disadvantages
- Slower process
- Cannot control investigation scope
- ALJ hearing more limited than jury trial
- No punitive damages in administrative track (civil penalties only)

## TRACK 2: FEDERAL COURT — WDMI (42 USC § 3613)

### Filing Procedure

**Step 1: Prepare Federal Complaint**
- [ ] Draft complaint per Federal Rules of Civil Procedure (see 02_FHA_CIVIL_ACTION.md)
- [ ] Civil cover sheet (JS-44)
- [ ] Summons for each defendant
- [ ] IFP application if needed (28 USC § 1915)

**Step 2: File via CM/ECF or In Person**

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Court** | U.S. District Court, Western District of Michigan — Southern Division |
| **Address** | 399 Federal Building, 110 Michigan St. NW, Grand Rapids, MI 49503 |
| **CM/ECF** | https://ecf.miwd.uscourts.gov |
| **Filing Fee** | $405 (or IFP motion) |
| **Clerk** | (616) 456-2381 |

**Step 3: Service (FRCP 4)**
- Personal service on individuals (FRCP 4(e))
- Service on corporations: officer, managing agent, or registered agent (FRCP 4(h))
- Out-of-state service: permitted for federal claims
- Service must be completed within 90 days of filing (FRCP 4(m))

**Step 4: Post-Filing**
- Defendants have 21 days to answer (FRCP 12(a))
- Initial disclosures (FRCP 26(a)) within 14 days of Rule 26(f) conference
- Scheduling conference (FRCP 16(b))
- Discovery period
- Dispositive motions
- Trial

### Deadline
- **2 years from last discriminatory act** (42 USC § 3613(a)(1)(A))
- Last act: July 17, 2025 → Deadline: **July 17, 2027**
- Continuing violation doctrine may extend deadline if ongoing retaliation

### Advantages
- Jury trial available
- Punitive damages available (42 USC § 3613(c)(1))
- Full discovery rights
- Attorney fees recoverable (42 USC § 3613(c)(2))
- Longer statute of limitations (2 years vs. 1 year HUD)

### Disadvantages
- Filing fee ($405 or IFP)
- Full litigation burden on plaintiff (pro se)
- Longer timeline to resolution
- Must prove case by preponderance of evidence

## TRACK 3: STATE — MICHIGAN DEPARTMENT OF CIVIL RIGHTS (MDCR)

### Elliott-Larsen Civil Rights Act (MCL 37.2101 et seq.)

Michigan's Elliott-Larsen Act provides parallel state protections:
- **MCL 37.2502** — Discrimination in real estate transactions
- **MCL 37.2502a** — Discrimination in terms and conditions of real property
- **MCL 37.2507** — Retaliation prohibited

### Filing Procedure

**Step 1: File with MDCR**

| Method | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Online** | https://www.michigan.gov/mdcr/file-a-complaint |
| **Phone** | (800) 482-3604 |
| **Mail** | MDCR, Capitol Tower Building, 110 W. Michigan Ave, Suite 800, Lansing, MI 48933 |
| **In Person** | Grand Rapids MDCR Office, 350 Ottawa Ave NW, Unit 1, Grand Rapids, MI 49503 |

**Step 2: MDCR Process**
1. Intake review
2. Investigation
3. Conciliation / fact-finding conference
4. Determination
5. Public hearing (if cause found) or right-to-sue letter

### Deadline
- **180 days from discriminatory act** for MDCR complaint
- Last act: July 17, 2025 → Deadline: **January 13, 2026**
- **⚠️ WARNING: This deadline is approaching fastest — file immediately if pursuing MDCR track**

### Advantages
- No filing fee
- State investigation resources
- Work-share agreement with HUD (dual-filing possible)
- Additional state law protections

### Disadvantages
- Shortest deadline (180 days)
- State remedies may be more limited
- May need to exhaust before state court action

## RECOMMENDED FILING STRATEGY

### Dual-File: HUD + Federal Court Preparation

1. **Immediately**: File HUD complaint (free, preserves rights, triggers investigation)
2. **Simultaneously**: Prepare federal complaint for WDMI filing
3. **Within 180 days of July 17, 2025**: Consider MDCR filing for additional state claims
4. **If HUD investigation insufficient**: File federal action under 42 USC § 3613
5. **At any point during HUD investigation**: Elect to remove to federal court

### Timeline Summary

| Track | Deadline | Days Remaining (from July 17, 2025) |
|-------|----------|--------------------------------------|
| MDCR (Elliott-Larsen) | ~Jan 13, 2026 | ~180 days |
| HUD Administrative | ~Jul 17, 2026 | ~365 days |
| Federal Court (WDMI) | ~Jul 17, 2027 | ~730 days |

**⚠️ NOTE**: Calculate exact deadlines from the actual last discriminatory act. If Defendants' retaliation/interference continued after July 17, 2025, the deadlines extend from the last act.
