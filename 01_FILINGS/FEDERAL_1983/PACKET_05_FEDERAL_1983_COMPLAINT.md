VERIFIED COMPLAINT FOR DEPRIVATION OF CIVIL RIGHTS
UNDER 42 U.S.C. Section 1983

Plaintiff Andrew J. Pigors, appearing pro se, brings this action
pursuant to 42 U.S.C. Section 1983 and 28 U.S.C. Sections 1331 and 1343
against the above-named Defendants for deprivation of his constitutional
rights secured by the First and Fourteenth Amendments to the United
States Constitution, and in support thereof alleges as follows:

                I. JURISDICTION AND VENUE

1. This Court has subject matter jurisdiction pursuant to 28 U.S.C.
Section 1331 (federal question), 28 U.S.C. Section 1343(a)(3) and (4)
(civil rights actions), and 42 U.S.C. Section 1983.

2. Venue is proper in the Western District of Michigan pursuant to 28
U.S.C. Section 1391(b) because the events giving rise to this action
occurred in Muskegon County, Michigan, which is within this District.

3. This Court has supplemental jurisdiction over related state law
claims pursuant to 28 U.S.C. Section 1367.

                    II. PARTIES

4. Plaintiff Andrew J. Pigors is a natural person and citizen of the
State of Michigan, residing in Muskegon County.

5. Defendant Hon. Jenny L. McNeill is sued in her official capacity
as a judge of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court. Plaintiff seeks only
declaratory and prospective injunctive relief against Judge McNeill,
consistent with Pulliam v. Allen, 466 U.S. 522 (1984), and Ex parte
Young, 209 U.S. 123 (1908). No monetary damages are sought against
Judge McNeill.

6. Defendant Emily Watson is a natural person and citizen of the State
of Michigan. Watson is sued in her individual capacity for her role in
conspiring to deprive Plaintiff of his constitutional rights under
color of state law through abuse of the PPO process.

7. Defendant Attorney Rusco is an attorney licensed in Michigan, acting
under color of state law as an officer of the court. Rusco is sued for
conspiring with Watson and engaging in conduct that deprived Plaintiff
of his constitutional rights.

8. Defendant Muskegon County is a Michigan municipal corporation
responsible for the administration of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court.

            III. STATEMENT OF FACTS

9. Plaintiff is the biological father of L.D.W., a minor child born
November 9, 2022. Plaintiff has been actively involved in L.D.W.'s
life from birth.

10. Beginning on or about 2024-05-02, Defendants engaged in a course of
conduct that deprived Plaintiff of all contact with his son for
a period exceeding 329 consecutive days, without:
    a. An evidentiary hearing;
    b. A finding of unfitness;
    c. A finding of endangerment by clear and convincing evidence;
    d. Application of the best interest factors;
    e. Notice and opportunity to be heard on the merits.

11. The deprivation was accomplished through the following mechanism:
    a. Defendant Watson filed a PPO petition containing allegations
       that were never tested through adversarial proceedings;
    b. Defendant McNeill issued the PPO ex parte;
    c. Defendant McNeill denied all of Plaintiff's motions to restore
       parenting time without hearing;
    d. Defendant Rusco facilitated the process by filing frivolous
       responsive pleadings designed to delay proceedings;
    e. The PPO was used as a de facto custody order, circumventing
       statutory protections.

            IV. CAUSES OF ACTION

COUNT I: VIOLATION OF SUBSTANTIVE DUE PROCESS
(14th Amendment; 42 U.S.C. Section 1983)
Against All Defendants

12. The fundamental right of a parent to the care, custody, and
companionship of their child is a liberty interest protected by the
Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment. Troxel v. Granville,
530 U.S. 57 (2000).

13. Defendants deprived Plaintiff of this fundamental right without
constitutionally adequate justification, without an evidentiary hearing,
and without findings supported by clear and convincing evidence.

14. This deprivation "shocks the conscience" and constitutes a
substantive due process violation. County of Sacramento v. Lewis, 523
U.S. 833, 846 (1998).

COUNT II: VIOLATION OF PROCEDURAL DUE PROCESS
(14th Amendment; 42 U.S.C. Section 1983)
Against Defendant McNeill (official capacity)

15. Before a parent may be deprived of their fundamental right to the
parent-child relationship, procedural due process requires, at minimum:
notice, an opportunity to be heard, and a determination by a neutral
arbiter based on evidence. Mathews v. Eldridge, 424 U.S. 319 (1976).

16. Defendant McNeill failed to provide Plaintiff with these minimum
procedural protections before suspending all parenting time.

COUNT III: VIOLATION OF FIRST AMENDMENT RIGHT OF ASSOCIATION
(1st and 14th Amendments; 42 U.S.C. Section 1983)
Against All Defendants

17. The First Amendment protects intimate association, including the
parent-child relationship. Roberts v. United States Jaycees, 468 U.S.
609, 619-20 (1984).

18. Defendants' actions have completely destroyed Plaintiff's ability
to associate with his son, in violation of the First Amendment.

COUNT IV: CONSPIRACY TO DEPRIVE CIVIL RIGHTS
(42 U.S.C. Section 1983; 42 U.S.C. Section 1985(3))
Against Defendants Watson, Rusco

19. Defendants Watson and Rusco conspired and acted in concert to
deprive Plaintiff of his constitutional rights by:
    a. Filing a PPO petition based on unverified allegations to prevent
       paternal contact;
    b. Using court procedures as a tool of parental alienation;
    c. Filing frivolous motions to delay restoration of parenting time;
    d. Communicating ex parte with the court.

COUNT V: MUNICIPAL LIABILITY
(42 U.S.C. Section 1983; Monell v. Dep't of Social Services)
Against Defendant Muskegon County

20. Muskegon County, through its policies, practices, or customs in
the administration of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, has maintained
a system that permits the deprivation of parental rights without
adequate due process protections. Monell v. Dep't of Social Services,
436 U.S. 658 (1978).

                    V. DAMAGES

21. As a direct and proximate result of Defendants' actions, Plaintiff
has suffered:
    a. Loss of companionship with his son for 329+ days;
    b. Emotional distress, anxiety, and depression;
    c. Economic damages including lost employment opportunities,
       litigation costs, and travel expenses, estimated at $94,250;
    d. Damage to reputation;
    e. Irreparable harm to the parent-child bond.

                VI. RELIEF REQUESTED

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court:

1. DECLARE that Defendants violated Plaintiff's rights under the
   First and Fourteenth Amendments to the United States Constitution;

2. ENJOIN Defendant McNeill from presiding over Plaintiff's cases and
   ORDER reassignment to a neutral judge;

3. ENJOIN enforcement of the PPO to the extent it prevents parental
   contact, or ORDER modification to permit supervised parenting time;

4. ORDER an evidentiary hearing in the state court applying proper
   constitutional standards;

5. AWARD compensatory damages against Defendants Watson, Rusco, and
   Muskegon County in an amount to be proven at trial;

6. AWARD punitive damages against Defendants Watson and Rusco;

7. AWARD reasonable costs and attorney fees (or pro se litigation
   costs) pursuant to 42 U.S.C. Section 1988;

8. GRANT such other and further relief as this Court deems just.

                VII. JURY DEMAND

Plaintiff hereby demands trial by jury on all claims and issues so
triable.
