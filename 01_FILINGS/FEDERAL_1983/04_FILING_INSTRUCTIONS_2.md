## REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
- [ ] Civil complaint (follow FRCP Rule 8)
- [ ] Civil cover sheet (JS 44)
- [ ] IFP application if seeking fee waiver
- [ ] Summons for each defendant

## PLEADING STANDARDS
- Must meet *Ashcroft v Iqbal*, 556 US 662 (2009) plausibility standard
- Must meet *Bell Atlantic v Twombly*, 550 US 544 (2007)
- Short and plain statement showing entitlement to relief (FRCP 8(a)(2))
- Each defendant must have specific allegations
- Do NOT merely recite elements -- provide factual support

## SERVICE OF PROCESS (FRCP 4)
- US Marshals may serve process for IFP plaintiffs (FRCP 4(c)(3))
- Individual defendants: Personal service or waiver of service
- Government/entity defendants: FRCP 4(j) (state or local government)
- 90 days to complete service (FRCP 4(m))

## STRATEGIC TIMING
- **BEST:** File AFTER COA appeal resolves (avoids Younger abstention)
- **ALTERNATIVE:** File simultaneously with JTC complaint for maximum pressure
- **AVOID:** Filing while motions are pending in state court

## ESTIMATED TIMELINE
| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Filing to screening (IFP) | 2-4 weeks |
| Screening to service | 1-2 weeks |
| Service to answer | 21 days (FRCP 12(a)) |
| Answer to discovery | Per scheduling order |
| Discovery period | 6-12 months typical |
| Dispositive motions | 3-6 months |
| Trial (if reached) | 12-24 months from filing |
