### Electronic Filing Requirements
- PDF format (text-searchable preferred)
- Maximum file size: check current Sixth Circuit limit

- Appendices may be filed in volumes

## IV. DEADLINES

### Critical Deadlines

| Action | Deadline | Rule | Calculated From |
|--------|----------|------|----------------|
| **Notice of Appeal** | **30 days** | FRAP 4(a)(1)(A) | Entry of judgment |
| Extension of time to file NOA | +30 days (excusable neglect) | FRAP 4(a)(5) | Original deadline |
| Transcript request | 14 days | FRAP 10(b)(1) | Filing of NOA |
| Designation of record | 14 days | FRAP 10(b)(3) | Filing of NOA |
| **Appellant's brief** | **40 days** | FRAP 31(a)(1) | Record filed/date set by court |
| **Appellee's brief** | **30 days** | FRAP 31(a)(1) | Appellant's brief served |
| **Reply brief** | **21 days** | FRAP 31(a)(1) | Appellee's brief served |
| Petition for rehearing | 14 days | FRAP 40(a)(1) | Entry of judgment |
| Petition for rehearing en banc | 14 days | FRAP 35(c) | Entry of judgment |
| Petition for certiorari (SCOTUS) | 90 days | Sup. Ct. R. 13 | Sixth Circuit judgment |

### Extension Requests
- Motion for extension must be filed BEFORE deadline expires
- Must show good cause (FRAP 26(b))
- First extension: routinely granted (usually 14–30 days)
- Second+ extensions: harder to obtain; must show extraordinary circumstances

## VI. SIXTH CIRCUIT LOCAL RULES — KEY PROVISIONS

### 6 Cir. R. 28 — Briefs
- Must contain statement regarding oral argument
- Cover must include case number, case caption, brief title
- Certificate of compliance required

### 6 Cir. R. 30 — Appendix
- Joint appendix preferred
- Must be consecutively paginated
- Relevant portions of record only

### 6 Cir. R. 34 — Oral Argument
- Court screens cases for oral argument suitability
- Parties may request oral argument in their briefs
- Typically 15 minutes per side

### 6 Cir. R. 35 — En Banc Consideration
- Petition for en banc: 14 days after panel decision
- Must certify compliance with FRAP 35(b)(1)
- Granted only for questions of exceptional importance or to maintain uniformity

## VII. PRO SE CONSIDERATIONS

### Sixth Circuit Pro Se Resources
- Pro se litigants held to same deadlines but briefs construed liberally
- *Haines v. Kerner*, 404 U.S. 519 (1972): pro se pleadings held to less stringent standard
- Clerk's office will answer procedural questions (not legal advice)
- Paper filing permitted without CM/ECF registration

### Common Pro Se Mistakes to Avoid
1. Missing the 30-day Notice of Appeal deadline (jurisdictional — cannot be waived)
2. Failing to designate the record
3. Brief exceeding word/page limits
4. Failing to include required certificates
5. Raising issues not preserved in district court
6. Failing to cite to the appendix/record

### Preservation of Issues
- Only issues raised in district court can be raised on appeal
- Objections must be timely made at trial
- Post-trial motions (FRCP 50, 59) preserve issues for appeal
- Plain error review available for unpreserved issues (very deferential standard)

## VIII. CHECKLIST — STEPS TO APPEAL

### Phase 1: Post-Judgment (Days 0–30)
- [ ] Review judgment for errors
- [ ] Determine issues to appeal
- [ ] Evaluate post-trial motions (FRCP 50(b), 59)
- [ ] File post-trial motions (tolls appeal deadline)
- [ ] File Notice of Appeal with WDMI clerk
- [ ] Pay $505 filing fee or file IFP motion
- [ ] Order transcript (if needed)
- [ ] Designate record on appeal

### Phase 2: Briefing (Days 30–130)
- [ ] Obtain record from district court
- [ ] Research issues and authority
- [ ] Draft appellant's opening brief (FRAP 28)
- [ ] Prepare appendix (FRAP 30)
- [ ] File brief + appendix in Sixth Circuit
- [ ] Serve on opposing counsel
- [ ] Review appellee's response brief
- [ ] Draft and file reply brief (optional)

### Phase 3: Decision (Days 130–365)
- [ ] Attend oral argument (if granted)
- [ ] Await panel decision
- [ ] If unfavorable: evaluate petition for rehearing/en banc
- [ ] If unfavorable: evaluate certiorari petition to SCOTUS
