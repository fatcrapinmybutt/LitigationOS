## CIVIL COMPLAINT FOR DAMAGES AND INJUNCTIVE RELIEF
### Pursuant to 42 USC 1983, Fair Housing Act (42 USC 3601 et seq.), and Supplemental State Claims

---

## I. INTRODUCTION

1. This is a civil rights action brought pursuant to 42 USC 1983 and the Fair Housing Act, 42 USC 3601 et seq., arising from Defendants' systematic pattern of discriminatory housing practices, retaliatory conduct, and deprivation of constitutional rights against Plaintiff, a resident of Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community in Muskegon County, Michigan.

---

## II. JURISDICTION AND VENUE

2. This Court has original jurisdiction under 28 USC 1331 (federal question) and 28 USC 1343(a)(3)-(4) (civil rights jurisdiction).

3. Supplemental jurisdiction over state-law claims exists under 28 USC 1367.

4. Venue is proper in the Western District of Michigan under 28 USC 1391(b)(2), as a substantial part of the events giving rise to the claims occurred in Muskegon County, Michigan.

---

## III. PARTIES

5. **Plaintiff Andrew J. Pigors** is a natural person residing at 1423 W. Norton Ave, Norton Shores, MI 49441. Plaintiff is a custodial parent and protected tenant under Michigan and federal law.

6. **Defendant Shady Oaks MHC, LLC** is a Michigan limited liability company that owns and operates Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community in Muskegon County, Michigan.

7. **Defendant Homes of America, Inc. ("HOA")** is a corporation that manages Shady Oaks MHC and other mobile home communities.

8. **Defendant Alden Global Capital, LLC** is a limited liability company with principal offices in New York that controls and directs the operations of HOA and Shady Oaks MHC.

9. **Defendant Partridge Securities, LLC** is a limited liability company affiliated with Alden Global Capital that participates in the ownership/control structure.

10. **Defendants John Does 1-10** are individuals whose identities are presently unknown but who participated in the acts described herein.

---

## IV. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

### A. Background

11. Plaintiff has been a resident of Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community since approximately 2019.

12. Defendants own, manage, and/or control Shady Oaks MHC and are responsible for housing conditions, lease terms, and tenant relations.

### B. Discriminatory Housing Practices

13. Defendants engaged in a pattern of discriminatory conduct against Plaintiff, including but not limited to:
    a. Imposing discriminatory terms and conditions of tenancy in violation of 42 USC 3604(b);
    b. Retaliating against Plaintiff for exercising protected rights in violation of 42 USC 3617;
    c. Interfering with Plaintiff's housing rights in violation of 42 USC 3617.

### C. Deprivation of Constitutional Rights Under Color of State Law

14. Defendants, acting under color of state law and/or in concert with state actors, deprived Plaintiff of rights secured by the Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution, including:
    a. Due process rights (procedural and substantive);
    b. Equal protection of the laws.

15. The state action element is satisfied because Defendants utilized state court proceedings, law enforcement, and/or government agencies to carry out the deprivation of rights alleged herein.

---

## V. CLAIMS FOR RELIEF

### COUNT I: 42 USC 1983 -- Deprivation of Civil Rights

16. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

17. Defendants, acting under color of state law, deprived Plaintiff of rights, privileges, and immunities secured by the Constitution and laws of the United States, specifically the Fourteenth Amendment.

18. Plaintiff is entitled to compensatory damages, punitive damages, and equitable relief pursuant to 42 USC 1983 and 42 USC 1988.

### COUNT II: Fair Housing Act -- Discriminatory Terms and Conditions (42 USC 3604(b))

19. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

20. Defendants discriminated against Plaintiff in the terms, conditions, or privileges of rental of a dwelling, or in the provision of services or facilities in connection therewith, in violation of 42 USC 3604(b).

### COUNT III: Fair Housing Act -- Retaliation (42 USC 3617)

21. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

22. Defendants retaliated against, threatened, coerced, or intimidated Plaintiff for exercising rights protected under the Fair Housing Act, in violation of 42 USC 3617.

### COUNT IV: Fair Housing Act -- Interference (42 USC 3617)

23. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

24. Defendants interfered with Plaintiff in the exercise or enjoyment of rights granted by the Fair Housing Act, in violation of 42 USC 3617.

### COUNT V: Supplemental State Claims

25. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs.

26. Under 28 USC 1367, Plaintiff asserts supplemental state-law claims including:
    a. Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.);
    b. Michigan Truth in Renting Act (MCL 554.631 et seq.);
    c. Breach of contract / lease violations;
    d. Tortious interference with housing rights;
    e. Intentional infliction of emotional distress;
    f. Negligence.
