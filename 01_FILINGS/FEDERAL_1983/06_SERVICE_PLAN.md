# I. SERVICE VIA U.S. MARSHALS (IFP PRIVILEGE)

## A. Legal Authority

Under 28 USC § 1915(d):

> "The officers of the court shall issue and serve all process, and perform all duties in [IFP] cases."

Under FRCP 4(c)(3):

> "At the plaintiff's request, the court may order that service be made by a United States marshal or deputy marshal or by a person specially appointed by the court."

**Key Benefit**: If IFP is granted, the U.S. Marshals Service will serve all Defendants at government expense. Plaintiff does not need to arrange or pay for service.

## B. Process for Obtaining Marshal Service

1. File the Complaint and IFP Application.
2. Upon grant of IFP, request that the Court order the U.S. Marshals to effect service.
3. Provide the Clerk's office with:
   - Completed USM-285 forms (one per Defendant)
   - Sufficient copies of the Summons and Complaint for each Defendant
   - Known addresses for each Defendant

# V. POST-SERVICE ACTIONS

1. **File Proof of Service** (FRCP 4(l)): The U.S. Marshals will file proof of service. Verify that proofs are filed for all Defendants.

2. **Monitor Response Deadlines**:
   - Individual Defendants: 21 days to answer or file FRCP 12 motion.
   - Government entities: 60 days per FRCP 12(a)(2).

3. **Default Judgment** (FRCP 55): If any Defendant fails to respond, move for entry of default and default judgment.

4. **Waiver of Service** (FRCP 4(d)): Consider sending waiver of service requests first — Defendants who waive get 60 days to respond (instead of 21) and Plaintiff saves service costs. However, under IFP, costs are not an issue.
