## I. STATUTORY AUTHORITY

1. Title 28 USC § 1915(a)(1) authorizes any court of the United States to permit the commencement, prosecution, or defense of any suit, action, or proceeding without prepayment of fees or security therefor, by a person who submits an affidavit demonstrating inability to pay such fees.

## III. STATEMENT OF INABILITY TO PAY

20. Based on the financial information stated above, I am unable to prepay the filing fee or give security therefor without depriving myself and my dependents of the necessities of life.

21. Contributing factors to my financial inability include:
    - (a) Court-ordered child support obligations imposed by the state court;
    - (b) Costs incurred in defending against unconstitutional orders in state court (certified copies, transcripts, filing fees, postage);
    - (c) Loss of income and employment disruption resulting from the underlying state proceedings;
    - (d) Financial strain caused by 571+ days of litigation;
    - (e) Inability to retain counsel due to financial constraints.

22. I have attempted to retain counsel for this federal action and have been unable to do so because:
    - (a) I cannot afford attorney's fees or retainer deposits;
    - (b) The complexity of the case (multiple defendants, constitutional issues, immunity defenses) has made it difficult to secure pro bono representation;
    - (c) Multiple legal aid organizations have been contacted without success.

## IV. NATURE OF THE ACTION

23. This is a civil rights action under 42 USC §§ 1983, 1985, and 1986 alleging deprivation of fundamental constitutional rights under the First and Fourteenth Amendments to the United States Constitution.

24. The action is **not frivolous** and is based on:
    - (a) A documented pattern of 24 of 55 orders (44%) entered on an ex parte basis without notice;
    - (b) Complete denial of all parenting time for 571+ consecutive days;
    - (c) Five ex parte orders entered on a single day (August 8, 2025) without notice;
    - (d) No evidentiary hearing conducted in over two years of proceedings;
    - (e) Six pending motions dismissed without adjudication;
    - (f) First Amendment retaliation for exercising the right to petition;
    - (g) Statistical and documentary evidence supporting each claim.

25. The action is **not brought in bad faith**. It seeks vindication of fundamental constitutional rights recognized by the United States Supreme Court.

26. The action is **not barred by the statute of limitations**. SOL audit confirms all federal claims are within the 3-year limitations period, with 1,047–1,094 days remaining (risk level: GREEN on all counts).
