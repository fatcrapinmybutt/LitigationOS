# IMMUNITY & JURISDICTION SCREENING MEMO — PKG10 Federal §1983/§1985
## Pigors v. Watson et al.

### PRE-FILING SCREENING GATES

#### 1. ABSOLUTE JUDICIAL IMMUNITY
- Judges have ABSOLUTE immunity for judicial acts within jurisdiction
- Stump v. Sparkman, 435 US 349 (1978) — even erroneous/malicious acts immune
- EXCEPTION: Acts not taken in judicial capacity
- EXCEPTION: Acts in complete absence of all jurisdiction
- **ANALYSIS**: [Document which acts were non-judicial or without jurisdiction]

#### 2. PROSECUTORIAL IMMUNITY
- Prosecutors have absolute immunity for advocacy functions
- Imbler v. Pachtman, 424 US 409 (1976)
- NOT immune for investigative/administrative functions
- **ANALYSIS**: [Document which Watson acts were administrative vs prosecutorial]

#### 3. QUALIFIED IMMUNITY (Other State Actors)
- FOC officers, social workers, GALs
- Immune unless violated "clearly established" constitutional right
- Harlow v. Fitzgerald, 457 US 800 (1982)
- **ANALYSIS**: [Document which rights were clearly established at time of violation]

#### 4. ROOKER-FELDMAN DOCTRINE
- Federal courts cannot review state court judgments
- Rooker v. Fidelity Trust, 263 US 413 (1923)
- Feldman, 460 US 462 (1983)
- EXCEPTION: Independent federal claim, not appellate review of state judgment
- **ANALYSIS**: [Frame claims as independent constitutional violations, not state court appeals]

#### 5. YOUNGER ABSTENTION
- Federal courts may abstain from interfering with ongoing state proceedings
- Younger v. Harris, 401 US 37 (1971)
- EXCEPTION: Bad faith, harassment, patently unconstitutional statute
- **ANALYSIS**: [Document bad faith/harassment if claiming exception]

#### 6. STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS
- §1983: State personal injury SOL applies — Michigan = 3 years (MCL 600.5805(2))
- §1985: 4-year federal SOL (28 USC 1658)
- Discovery rule may toll
