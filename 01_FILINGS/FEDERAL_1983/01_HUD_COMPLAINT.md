## III. DISCRIMINATORY HOUSING PRACTICES

### A. Discriminatory Terms and Conditions (42 USC § 3604(b))

Respondents imposed discriminatory and predatory terms, conditions, and privileges on Complainant's tenancy that were not imposed on similarly situated tenants:

1. **Ledger Fraud / Fabricated Charges** — Respondents systematically manipulated Complainant's lot rent ledger, fabricating arrears and layering unauthorized fees to create a pretext for eviction. [CL-20260123-0009; CL-20260123-0021]
2. **Payment Rejection** — Respondents refused to accept or properly credit valid payments, including MDHHS and TrueNorth housing assistance payments, while accepting identical payment forms from other tenants. [CL-20260123-0009]
3. **Coerced Lease Modification** — On or about March 26, 2024, Complainant was coerced under threat of eviction into signing an off-site lease at different terms and higher rent, contrary to Michigan law. [CL-20260123-0010]
4. **Utility Billing Without Remediation** — Despite raw sewage conditions near Complainant's lot (EGLE violations issued), Respondents continued billing utilities without remediation credits. [CL-20260123-0008]
5. **Disparate Application of Community Rules** — Community rules regarding lot maintenance, guest policies, and payment terms were selectively and more harshly enforced against Complainant compared to other residents.

### B. Retaliation for Exercising Housing Rights (42 USC § 3617)

Respondents engaged in a pattern of retaliation against Complainant for exercising lawful housing rights, including:

1. **Retaliation for Complaints** — After Complainant reported sewage violations to EGLE and challenged fabricated ledger charges, Respondents escalated eviction proceedings.
2. **Blocked Sales (3 Instances)** — Respondents interfered with Complainant's attempts to sell his mobile home on at least three (3) separate occasions:
   - Told prospective buyers that Complainant "no longer owns his home"
   - Told prospective buyers that Complainant had "abandoned" the home
   - Represented that a "lawyer is working on taking/selling" the home
   - Refused to approve buyer applications without lawful basis
3. **Slander of Title** — Community manager made false statements to third parties that Complainant had abandoned his home and no longer owned it, directly interfering with sale prospects.
4. **Constructive Eviction** — Through the cumulative effect of fabricated charges, sewage conditions, payment rejection, and harassment, Respondents constructively evicted Complainant.
5. **Post-Eviction Retaliation** — After the eviction:
   - Locks drilled on July 17, 2025 [CL-20260123-0012]
   - Personal property removed and destroyed
   - "FREE" sign placed on Complainant's belongings within 24 hours
   - Neighbor (Brandel) facilitated destruction of property and appliances

### C. Interference with Housing Rights (42 USC § 3617)

1. **Interference with Right to Sell** — Respondents systematically prevented Complainant from exercising his right as titleholder to sell his manufactured home by making false statements to prospective purchasers.
2. **Interference with Right to Occupy** — Through fabricated charges, coerced lease modifications, and hostile conditions, Respondents interfered with Complainant's right to peaceful enjoyment of his home.
3. **Weaponization of Housing Loss** — The resulting homelessness was used in custody proceedings (Pigors v. Watson, No. 24-01507-DC) to suspend Complainant's parenting time, constituting ongoing interference with housing rights.
