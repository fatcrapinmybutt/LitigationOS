# SIXTH CIRCUIT COURT OF APPEALS — APPEAL FRAMEWORK
## Federal Rules of Appellate Procedure (FRAP) 28, 30, 32
## For Use After WDMI Judgment

---

## I. OVERVIEW

The United States Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit hears appeals from the Western District of Michigan. This framework prepares for appeal after a WDMI judgment in the Fair Housing Act / § 1983 cases.

## II. WHEN TO FILE AN APPEAL

### Triggering Events
| Event | Appeal Available? | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Final judgment (all claims resolved) | YES — as of right | FRAP 4(a)(1): 30 days |
| Partial summary judgment (some claims) | NO (usually) | Must wait for final judgment or obtain FRCP 54(b) certification |
| Denial of preliminary injunction | YES — interlocutory | 28 USC § 1292(a)(1) |
| Denial of IFP status | YES — appeal to circuit | Mandamus may also be available |
| Remand to state court | Generally NO | May petition for mandamus |

### Notice of Appeal — FRAP 3, 4

**Deadline:** **30 days** after entry of judgment (FRAP 4(a)(1)(A))
- If government is a party: 60 days (FRAP 4(a)(1)(B))
- Extension: up to 30 additional days for excusable neglect (FRAP 4(a)(5))

**Filing:** Notice of Appeal filed with the **District Court** clerk (WDMI), not the Sixth Circuit
- Fee: $505 (or IFP motion)
- Form: Sixth Circuit Form 1 (Notice of Appeal)

## III. BRIEF FORMAT REQUIREMENTS — FRAP 28

### Appellant's Opening Brief

| Section | Requirement | FRAP Rule |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| **Corporate Disclosure** | Statement per FRAP 26.1 | 28(a)(1) |
| **Table of Contents** | With page references | 28(a)(2) |
| **Table of Authorities** | Cases, statutes, other materials with page references | 28(a)(3) |
| **Jurisdictional Statement** | Basis for district court jurisdiction + appellate jurisdiction, filing date of notice of appeal | 28(a)(4) |
| **Statement of Issues** | Issues presented for review | 28(a)(5) |
| **Statement of the Case** | Nature of case, course of proceedings, disposition below | 28(a)(6) |
| **Statement of Facts** | Relevant facts with citations to appendix | 28(a)(7) |
| **Summary of Argument** | Concise summary | 28(a)(8) |
| **Argument** | Contentions with reasons and citations; standard of review for each issue | 28(a)(9) |
| **Conclusion** | Short conclusion stating precise relief sought | 28(a)(10) |
| **Certificate of Compliance** | Word count compliance | 32(g) |

### Appellee's Response Brief
- Same format as appellant's brief (FRAP 28(b))
- Due: 30 days after appellant's brief served (FRAP 31(a)(1))

### Appellant's Reply Brief
- Optional; limited to issues raised in appellee's brief (FRAP 28(c))
- Due: 21 days after appellee's brief served (FRAP 31(a)(1))

## IV. BRIEF LENGTH AND TYPE SPECIFICATIONS — FRAP 32

### Length Limits

| Brief | Word Limit | Page Limit (if no word count) |
|-------|-----------|------------------------------|
| Opening / Response | 13,000 words | 30 pages |
| Reply | 6,500 words | 15 pages |
| Amicus (by consent) | 6,500 words | 15 pages |

### Formatting Requirements (FRAP 32(a))

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|--------------|
| Paper size | 8.5 × 11 inches |
| Margins | At least 1 inch on all sides |
| Font | 14-point proportional (e.g., Century Schoolbook, Times New Roman) or 12-point monospaced |
| Line spacing | Double-spaced (except quotations, headings, footnotes) |
| Binding | Left side only; secure but allowing flat opening |
| Cover color | Blue (appellant), Red (appellee), Gray (reply), Green (amicus) |
| Cover content | Court name, case number, case caption, brief title, attorney name/address |

### Electronic Filing (CM/ECF)
- Sixth Circuit uses CM/ECF for electronic filing
- PDF format required
- Must comply with Sixth Circuit electronic filing policies
- Pro se litigants: verify whether CM/ECF registration is required or paper filing permitted

## V. APPENDIX REQUIREMENTS — FRAP 30

### Required Contents (FRAP 30(a))

The appendix must contain:
1. Relevant docket entries from district court
2. Relevant portions of pleadings, charge, findings, or opinion
3. The judgment, order, or decision being appealed
4. Any other parts of the record referenced in the briefs

### Sixth Circuit Specific Requirements
- Appendix filed with appellant's brief
- Designation of record: appellant designates within 14 days of filing notice of appeal (FRAP 10)
- Cost: appellant initially bears appendix costs; court may reallocate

### Appendix Format
- Consecutively paginated
- Table of contents with page references
- Copies of documents as they appeared in district court record
- Tab or divider for each separate document

### Recommended Appendix Contents for This Case

| Tab | Document | Purpose |
|-----|----------|---------|
| A | District court judgment/order being appealed | Required |
| B | Docket sheet | Required |
| C | Complaint (Fair Housing Act) | Background |
| D | Key motions and responses | As referenced in brief |
| E | Summary judgment order (if applicable) | If appealing SJ |
| F | Key evidence exhibits | As referenced in brief |
| G | Transcript excerpts (if trial occurred) | As needed |

## VI. ORAL ARGUMENT — FRAP 34

### Request for Oral Argument

Sixth Circuit grants oral argument in many cases. To request:

**Statement in Brief:** Include a statement on the inside cover or at the end of the brief:
> "Appellant respectfully requests oral argument. This case presents [complex/novel] issues regarding the Fair Housing Act's application to [describe]. Oral argument will assist the Court in understanding the factual record and legal issues."

### Oral Argument Procedure
- Sixth Circuit typically allows 15 minutes per side
- Panel of 3 judges
- May be held in Cincinnati (main courthouse) or other locations
- Sixth Circuit may decide case without oral argument (FRAP 34(a)(2))

### Oral Argument Template

**Opening (2 minutes):**
> "May it please the Court. My name is Andrew Pigors, and I am the appellant appearing pro se. This case involves a pattern of discriminatory and retaliatory housing practices under the Fair Housing Act, 42 USC §§ 3604(b) and 3617. The district court erred in [describe error]. I respectfully request [X] minutes and reserve [Y] minutes for rebuttal."

**Body (10 minutes):**
- Issue 1: Standard of review and error below
- Issue 2: Evidence supporting Fair Housing Act claims
- Issue 3: Damages / relief

**Conclusion (1 minute):**
> "For these reasons, the judgment of the district court should be [reversed/vacated] and the case remanded for [trial/further proceedings]. Thank you."

**Rebuttal (2 minutes):**
- Address strongest points raised by appellee
- Reassert key argument

## VII. STANDARDS OF REVIEW

| Issue | Standard | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Findings of fact (bench trial) | Clearly erroneous | Reversed only if definite conviction of mistake |
| Summary judgment | De novo | Sixth Circuit reviews independently |
| Legal conclusions | De novo | Pure questions of law reviewed fresh |
| Evidentiary rulings | Abuse of discretion | Reversed only if unreasonable |
| Jury verdict | Substantial evidence | Reversed only if no reasonable jury could find |
| Damages | Abuse of discretion | Affirmed unless clearly excessive/inadequate |
