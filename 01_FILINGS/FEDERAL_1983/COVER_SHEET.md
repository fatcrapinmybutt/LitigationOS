## CRITICAL DEADLINES AND TIMING

### Statute of Limitations
- **§ 1983 claims**: Michigan's 3-year personal injury statute (MCL 600.5805(10))
- **Accrual**: Claims accrue when Plaintiff knew or should have known of the injury
- **Continuing violations**: The ongoing separation (Jul 29, 2025–present) is a continuing violation; the statute has NOT begun to run on the ongoing deprivation
- **Incarceration tolling**: Michigan law may toll the statute during periods of incarceration

### Filing Timeline (Recommended)
1. **Week 1**: Convert complaint to PDF, complete JS 44, prepare summonses
2. **Week 1**: File complaint + IFP application (or pay $405 fee)

4. **60 days from service**: Defendants must file Answer or Motion to Dismiss (FRCP 12)
5. **90 days from service**: Government defendants get 60 days (FRCP 12(a)(1)(A))

## EXPECTED DEFENSE MOTIONS AND RESPONSES

### 1. Judicial Immunity (McNeill)
- **Response**: Immunity is pierced for acts in clear absence of jurisdiction and non-judicial acts. *Mireles v. Waco*, 502 U.S. 9 (1991). 68 ex parte orders without notice = clear absence of jurisdiction. Birthday-message punishment = non-judicial retaliation.

### 2. Rooker-Feldman Doctrine
- **Response**: Complaint challenges process (absence of notice/hearing), not the merits of custody. This is not an appeal of a state court judgment.

### 3. Younger Abstention
- **Response**: Exceptions apply — bad faith (44% ex parte rate), inadequate state forum ($250 bond blocks all motions), and no opportunity to raise federal claims.

### 4. Qualified Immunity (Rusco)
- **Response**: The constitutional right to notice before deprivation of a fundamental liberty interest is clearly established. Any reasonable court employee would know that screening a litigant's filings to prevent judicial review violates the Constitution.

### 5. Monell — No Policy (Muskegon County)
- **Response**: 68 ex parte orders in a single case = pattern/custom. Failure to investigate or correct = deliberate indifference. *Owen v. City of Independence*, 445 U.S. 622 (1980).
