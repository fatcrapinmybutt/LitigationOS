## X. JUDICIAL IMMUNITY ANALYSIS

### A. The General Rule and Its Exceptions

96. Judges are generally immune from § 1983 damages for acts performed in their judicial capacity. *Mireles v. Waco*, 502 U.S. 9 (1991). However, two well-established exceptions apply, and both are met here:

97. **Exception 1 — Non-Judicial Acts:** Judicial immunity does not apply to acts that are "not taken in the judge's judicial capacity." *Id.* at 11-12; *Forrester v. White*, 484 U.S. 219 (1988). The following actions by McNeill are non-judicial:
    - Ex parte communications with Watson and/or Berry outside the formal record;
    - Self-ruling on her own disqualification motion — a function assigned by MCR 2.003(D) to the Chief Judge;
    - Administrative actions in imposing the $250 bond without statutory authority or findings;
    - Cross-case coordination between the custody and PPO proceedings to achieve a unified result against Plaintiff.

98. **Exception 2 — Complete Absence of All Jurisdiction:** Judicial immunity does not apply to acts "taken in the clear absence of all jurisdiction." *Stump v. Sparkman*, 435 U.S. 349, 356-57 (1978). The jurisdiction to act ex parte under MCR 3.207(C)(2) is *conditional* — it requires specific sworn facts showing imminent danger. When McNeill entered five ex parte orders on August 8, 2025, without compliance with any of the mandatory prerequisites that confer jurisdiction to act ex parte, she acted in the **complete absence of the jurisdiction that due process confers**. The absence of the required affidavit under MCR 3.207(B) is a jurisdictional defect, not merely a procedural error.

### B. The Dennis v. Sparks Conspiracy Exception

99. Even if immunity would otherwise shield McNeill's actions, the conspiracy with Watson and Berry pierces that immunity. Under *Dennis v. Sparks*, 449 U.S. 24 (1980), when a judge acts as a co-conspirator rather than as a judicial officer, the judge may be liable. The conspiracy transforms what would otherwise be judicial action into private conspiratorial action not entitled to immunity protection. The private co-conspirators — Watson and Berry — have **no judicial immunity whatsoever**. *Id.* at 28-29.

### C. Official-Capacity Claims — Immune from Immunity Defense

100. Regardless of individual immunity, Plaintiff seeks prospective injunctive and declaratory relief against McNeill in her official capacity. *Pulliam v. Allen*, 466 U.S. 522 (1984) (injunctive relief available against judges); *Ex parte Young*, 209 U.S. 123 (1908) (official-capacity suits for prospective relief not barred by sovereign immunity). Judicial immunity is **no defense** to claims for declaratory and injunctive relief.

### D. Volume of Violations Negates "Judicial Discretion" Defense

101. Any defense that McNeill's actions constitute protected exercises of judicial discretion must be evaluated against the scale of documented violations. **1,127 discrete violations** across 19 conduct categories, **150 ex parte violations**, **167 disqualification violations**, **2,722 contempt sanction atoms**, and **76,329 total adverse findings** are categorically inconsistent with the exercise of lawful judicial discretion.
