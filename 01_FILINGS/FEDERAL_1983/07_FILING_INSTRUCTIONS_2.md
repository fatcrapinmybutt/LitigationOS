## C. Filing the Initial Complaint

### Step-by-Step Procedure

1. **Prepare Documents** (all in PDF format):
   - Complaint (01_COMPLAINT_1983.md → PDF)
   - IFP Application (03_IFP_APPLICATION.md → PDF)
   - Civil Cover Sheet (JS-44 form)
   - Summons forms (AO 440 — one per Defendant)
   - USM-285 forms (one per Defendant)

2. **Complete Civil Cover Sheet (JS-44)**:
   - Available at: https://www.uscourts.gov/forms/civil-forms
   - **Basis of Jurisdiction**: Box 3 (Federal Question)
   - **Nature of Suit**: 440 (Other Civil Rights) or 441 (Voting) — use **440**
   - **Cause of Action**: 42 USC § 1983
   - Check "Jury Demand: Yes"

3. **File with Clerk**:
   - Submit all documents to the Clerk's office.
   - If filing by mail, include a self-addressed stamped envelope for the file-stamped copy.
   - If filing IFP, submit the IFP Application simultaneously with the Complaint.

4. **Case Assignment**:
   - The Clerk will assign a case number and judge.
   - Receive file-stamped copies of all documents.

5. **IFP Screening**:
   - The assigned judge (or magistrate) will review the IFP Application.
   - Under 28 USC § 1915(e)(2), the Court will also screen the Complaint for frivolousness.
   - If IFP is granted, the Court will order the U.S. Marshals to serve Defendants.

### Statute of Limitations Note
Michigan's statute of limitations for § 1983 claims is **3 years** (borrowing from MCL 600.5805(10)). The limitations period runs from the date of each constitutional violation. For continuing violations, the period runs from the most recent violation.

**Most recent violations**: Ongoing (parenting time still denied as of filing). The continuing violation doctrine may apply to extend the limitations period for the entire pattern of conduct.
