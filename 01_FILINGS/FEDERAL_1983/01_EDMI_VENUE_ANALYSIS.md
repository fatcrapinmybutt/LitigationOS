# EDMI VENUE ANALYSIS
## Eastern District of Michigan — Venue Transfer Considerations
## Pigors v. Shady Oaks MHC, LLC et al.

---

## I. OVERVIEW

The **Eastern District of Michigan (EDMI)** covers the eastern portion of Michigan, with the primary courthouse in Detroit. The **Western District of Michigan (WDMI)** covers the western portion, including Muskegon County. This analysis evaluates when filing in EDMI might be strategically preferable to WDMI.

## III. WHEN EDMI IS PREFERABLE TO WDMI

### A. Corporate Defendant Connections to EDMI

If Defendants have greater connections to EDMI jurisdiction:

| Defendant | WDMI Connection | EDMI Connection | Favors |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|--------|
| Shady Oaks MHC | Property in Muskegon (WDMI) | None known | WDMI |
| Homes of America | Manages Muskegon property | May have offices/operations in EDMI | Depends |
| Alden Global Capital | No MI presence (NYC) | No MI presence (NYC) | Neutral |
| Partridge Securities | No MI presence | No MI presence | Neutral |

**Analysis:** Unless HOA or corporate defendants have significant EDMI presence, WDMI is the natural venue.

### B. Judicial Assignment Considerations

| Factor | WDMI | EDMI |
|--------|------|------|
| Judges on bench | ~6 active Article III judges | ~15 active Article III judges |
| Caseload | Moderate | Heavy (Detroit) |
| Time to trial | ~18–24 months | ~24–36 months |
| Fair housing experience | Moderate | More extensive (urban center) |
| Pro se experience | Standard | High volume |

**EDMI Advantage:** Larger bench with more judges experienced in housing discrimination and civil rights litigation.
**EDMI Disadvantage:** Heavier caseload, potentially longer time to resolution.

### C. Legal Climate

| Factor | WDMI | EDMI |
|--------|------|------|
| Civil rights case volume | Lower | Higher |
| Fair Housing Act precedent | Standard Sixth Circuit | Same Sixth Circuit |
| Jury pool demographics | West Michigan (more rural/suburban) | Detroit metro (more urban/diverse) |
| Pro se receptivity | Standard | Experienced with pro se cases |

**EDMI Advantage:** EDMI jury pools may be more receptive to housing discrimination claims given Detroit's housing discrimination history.

### D. When EDMI May Be Preferable

1. **If corporate defendants reside/operate in EDMI**: Venue is proper where any defendant resides (28 USC § 1391(b)(1))
2. **If Alden/Partridge/HOA have EDMI offices**: May establish personal jurisdiction + venue
3. **If venue transfer is strategically advantageous**: More favorable judicial assignment or jury pool
4. **If Andrew relocates to EDMI counties**: May create additional venue basis
5. **If pattern/practice extends to EDMI properties**: If HOA/Alden operate communities in EDMI, broader pattern evidence may favor EDMI

## IV. VENUE TRANSFER ARGUMENTS

### Transferring FROM WDMI TO EDMI — 28 USC § 1404(a)

**Standard:** "For the convenience of parties and witnesses, in the interest of justice, a district court may transfer any civil action to any other district or division where it might have been brought."

#### Arguments FOR Transfer to EDMI
1. Corporate defendants may have stronger contacts with EDMI
2. HOA/Alden's operations span multiple Michigan communities (if any are in EDMI)
3. Key witnesses (corporate officers) may be located nearer to EDMI
4. Broader pattern/practice evidence may be concentrated in EDMI

#### Arguments AGAINST Transfer (Stay in WDMI)
1. Events occurred in Muskegon County (WDMI)
2. Property located in Muskegon County (WDMI)
3. Andrew resides in Muskegon area (WDMI)
4. Local witnesses (Shafer, community residents) in WDMI
5. EGLE records, sheriff reports, LARA records accessible from WDMI
6. Transferring would be inconvenient for plaintiff (pro se, limited resources)

### Transferring FROM EDMI TO WDMI — (If Filed in EDMI)
Defendants would likely move to transfer to WDMI (or dismiss for improper venue). Must show venue is proper in EDMI under 28 USC § 1391(b).

## VI. RECOMMENDATION

### File in WDMI (Grand Rapids)

**WDMI is the strongest venue** for this case because:

1. ✅ Property located in Muskegon County (WDMI)
2. ✅ All discriminatory events occurred in WDMI
3. ✅ Andrew resides in WDMI
4. ✅ Key witnesses are local to WDMI
5. ✅ Convenience for pro se plaintiff
6. ✅ Venue is clearly proper under 28 USC § 1391(b)(2)
7. ✅ Defendants cannot credibly challenge venue in WDMI

**EDMI should only be considered if:**
- Discovery reveals HOA/Alden operate discriminatory practices at EDMI-located communities (pattern/practice expansion)
- Andrew relocates to EDMI
- Strategic reassessment after initial proceedings in WDMI

### Alternative: Parallel Filing in EDMI

If broader pattern/practice claims develop (e.g., HOA discriminates across multiple Michigan communities), a separate EDMI action targeting EDMI-located communities could be filed. This would NOT be a transfer — it would be a separate case based on different property/events in the EDMI jurisdiction.
