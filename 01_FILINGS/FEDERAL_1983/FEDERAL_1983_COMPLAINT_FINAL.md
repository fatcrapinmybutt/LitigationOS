## IX. JUDICIAL IMMUNITY ANALYSIS

### A. The General Rule and Its Exceptions

66. Judges are generally immune from § 1983 damages for acts performed in their judicial capacity. *Mireles v. Waco*, 502 U.S. 9 (1991). However, two well-established exceptions apply:

67. **Exception 1 — Non-Judicial Acts:** Judicial immunity does not apply to acts that are "not taken in the judge's judicial capacity." *Id.* at 11-12; *Forrester v. White*, 484 U.S. 219 (1988). Ex parte communications with a party or their attorney are not judicial acts — they are administrative or personal acts that violate MCR 2.003 and Canon 3A(4) of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct. McNeill's self-ruling on her own disqualification motion — a function assigned by MCR 2.003(D) to the Chief Judge — is likewise a non-judicial administrative act.

68. **Exception 2 — Complete Absence of All Jurisdiction:** Judicial immunity does not apply to acts "taken in the clear absence of all jurisdiction." *Stump v. Sparkman*, 435 U.S. 349, 356-57 (1978). The jurisdiction to act ex parte under MCR 3.207(C)(2) is *conditional* — it requires specific sworn facts showing imminent danger. When McNeill entered five ex parte orders on August 8, 2025, without compliance with any of the mandatory prerequisites that confer jurisdiction to act ex parte, she acted in the **complete absence of the jurisdiction that due process confers**. *See Boddie v. Connecticut*, 401 U.S. 371 (1971).

### B. The Dennis v. Sparks Conspiracy Exception

69. Even if immunity would otherwise shield McNeill's actions, the conspiracy with Watson and Berry pierces that immunity. Under *Dennis v. Sparks*, 449 U.S. 24 (1980), when a judge acts as a co-conspirator rather than as a judicial officer, the judge may be liable. The conspiracy transforms what would otherwise be judicial action into private conspiratorial action not entitled to immunity protection. The private co-conspirators — Watson and Berry — have **no judicial immunity whatsoever**. *Id.* at 28-29.

### C. Official-Capacity Claims

70. Regardless of individual immunity, Plaintiff seeks prospective injunctive and declaratory relief against McNeill in her official capacity. *Pulliam v. Allen*, 466 U.S. 522 (1984) (injunctive relief available against judges); *Ex parte Young*, 209 U.S. 123 (1908) (official-capacity suits for prospective relief not barred by sovereign immunity).

### D. Application to Each Defendant

| Defendant | Immunity Claim | Response |
|---|---|---|
| McNeill (individual) | Judicial immunity | Pierced: (1) non-judicial acts (ex parte communications, self-ruling on disqualification); (2) complete absence of jurisdiction (no MCR 3.207(C)(2) compliance); (3) conspiracy (*Dennis v. Sparks*) |
| McNeill (official) | Sovereign immunity | Not applicable to prospective injunctive/declaratory relief (*Ex parte Young*; *Pulliam*) |
