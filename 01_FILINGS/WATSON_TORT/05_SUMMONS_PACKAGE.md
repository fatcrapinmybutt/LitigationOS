## I. GENERAL SERVICE REQUIREMENTS

### A. Michigan Court Rules

- **MCR 2.102**: Summons must be issued by the court clerk upon filing of the complaint.
- **MCR 2.103**: Service must be made within 91 days of filing (or within the time specified by the court).
- **MCR 2.105(A)**: Personal service on individuals — serve a copy of the summons and complaint upon the defendant personally.
- **MCR 2.105(A)(2)**: If personal service cannot be made, service may be made by leaving copies at the defendant's usual place of abode with a member of the household of suitable age and discretion.
- **MCR 2.104**: The person who serves process must be at least 18 years old and not a party to the action.

### B. Filing Fee

- **Circuit Court Filing Fee**: $175.00 (civil filing fee for Muskegon County Circuit Court)
- **Alternative**: If Plaintiff cannot afford the filing fee, file a **Motion and Affidavit for Fee Waiver** (SCAO Form MC 20) pursuant to MCR 2.002.
- **Summons Fee**: $0 (included in filing fee)

### C. Process Server

- A professional process server or any adult (18+) who is not a party to the action may serve process.
- The Muskegon County Sheriff's Office may also serve process for a fee.

## VII. PROOF OF SERVICE FORMS

A Proof of Service (SCAO Form MC 104) must be completed for **each** defendant after service is accomplished. The Proof of Service must include:

1. **Identity of person served**: Full legal name of the defendant
2. **Date of service**: Exact date service was accomplished
3. **Time of service**: Approximate time of day
4. **Place of service**: Street address where service was accomplished
5. **Manner of service**: Personal service, substitute service, or other authorized method
6. **Description of person served**: Physical description of the defendant served (or substitute recipient)
7. **Server's information**: Name, address, and signature of the process server
8. **Server's declaration**: Signed under oath or penalty of perjury

## VIII. ALTERNATIVE SERVICE OPTIONS

### A. If Defendant Cannot Be Located

If personal and substitute service cannot be accomplished for any Defendant, Plaintiff may file a **Motion for Alternative Service** under MCR 2.106, requesting the court to authorize service by:

1. **Service by posting** at the last known address and at the courthouse;
2. **Service by publication** in a newspaper of general circulation in Muskegon County;
3. **Service by certified mail** to the last known address;
4. **Service by email** if the Defendant's email address is known;
5. **Any other method** reasonably calculated to provide actual notice.

### B. Motion for Alternative Service Requirements

A motion for alternative service must include:
- An affidavit of due diligence describing all attempts to serve the defendant;
- The defendant's last known address;
- Any other information about the defendant's whereabouts;
- The proposed alternative method of service.

### C. If Defendants Share an Address

If Lane Watson and Lori Watson (or other Defendants) reside at the same address, each must still be served individually. A single service on one household member does not constitute service on all members.
