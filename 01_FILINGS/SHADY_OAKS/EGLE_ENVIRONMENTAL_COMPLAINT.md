## IV. FACTUAL BACKGROUND AND VIOLATION HISTORY

### A. Initial Discovery and Reporting (Late 2024 – March 2025)

Beginning in late 2024, raw sewage began surfacing on the ground near occupied manufactured homes at Shady Oaks MHC, including near Complainant's home at Lot 17. The sewage emanated from a failing interceptor tank / community sewer infrastructure.

Complainant first reported the condition to Park Manager Kim Davis in approximately March 2024. No remediation was performed. Conditions worsened through late 2024 and into 2025.

On **March 18, 2025**, Complainant filed a formal environmental complaint with EGLE.

### B. EGLE Inspection (March 20, 2025)

EGLE conducted an on-site inspection on March 20, 2025. The inspection confirmed:

- **Raw sewage discharging onto the ground surface** near occupied homes;
- **Visible sewage pooling** in common areas of the manufactured housing community;
- **Proximity to minor children** — Complainant and other residents had young children living in affected areas;
- **Infrastructure failure** — interceptor tank and/or sewer conveyance system in a state of disrepair.

### C. Violation Notice VN-017235 (April 15, 2025)

On April 15, 2025, EGLE issued **Violation Notice VN-017235** to the facility operator, confirming:

| Element | Rating |
|---------|--------|
| Facility Site Review | **NOT IN COMPLIANCE** |
| Operation & Maintenance | **NOT IN COMPLIANCE** |
| Notification to EGLE within 24 hours | **VIOLATION — Failed to notify** |
| Written reports submission | **VIOLATION — Failed to submit** |

### D. Inadequate Remediation (April–June 2025)

Following VN-017235, Respondents performed minimal remediation consisting of approximately one hour of "TV and jetting" (camera inspection and pipe clearing) with **no excavation and no pipe replacement**. This cosmetic repair did not address the root infrastructure failure.

### E. Recurrence (Late June 2025)

Raw sewage resumed leaking onto the ground surface in late June 2025, confirming that the TV/jetting-only approach was insufficient. The condition persisted through Complainant's forced removal on July 17, 2025.

### F. Retaliatory Eviction Following EGLE Complaint

Within approximately four months of filing the EGLE complaint, Respondents:
1. Escalated eviction proceedings against Complainant;
2. Destroyed Complainant's mobile home and personal property (July 17, 2025);
3. Billed Complainant $7,633.13 five days after the destruction (July 22, 2025).

The temporal proximity between the EGLE complaint and the retaliatory actions is evidence that the environmental reporting was a motivating factor in the adverse action. *See* MCL 600.5720 (retaliatory eviction).

## V. CURRENT STATUS OF VIOLATIONS

As of the date of this supplemental complaint, Complainant believes the following violations remain unremediated:

1. **Sewer infrastructure failure** — Root cause (failing interceptor tank and/or conveyance piping) has not been repaired or replaced;
2. **Ongoing or recurring sewage discharge** — TV/jetting was a temporary measure that did not prevent recurrence;
3. **Failure to comply with VN-017235 corrective action requirements**;
4. **No evidence of EGLE-approved remediation plan** submitted by Respondents;
5. **Community remains occupied** — Other manufactured homes and residents remain at the site, potentially exposed to ongoing sewage contamination.
