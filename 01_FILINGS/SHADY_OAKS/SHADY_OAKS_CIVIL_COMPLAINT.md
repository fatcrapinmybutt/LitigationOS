   The **$2,241.82 discrepancy** between Ledger Versions A and B cannot be explained by normal rent accrual. The software version changed between printings (enabling retroactive editing), and the page count expanded (proving transactions were added or removed). *(Exhibits PIGORS-SO-L007 through L012.)*

18. Defendants omitted at least **$6,085.99** in documented payments from Plaintiff's ledger, including:

## IV. CLAIMS FOR RELIEF

### COUNT I — WRONGFUL EVICTION
#### MCL 600.5714; MCL 600.2918

45. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs by reference.

46. **MCL 600.5714** governs summary proceedings to recover possession of premises and requires strict compliance with statutory procedures, including proper notice and opportunity to cure, before a landlord may obtain possession.

47. **MCL 600.2918** provides that any person who is put out of or deprived of any lands by force, intimidation, fraud, or stealth may recover possession and treble damages.

48. Defendants wrongfully evicted Plaintiff through the following acts:

   a. **Eviction without proper notice.** Defendants failed to provide Plaintiff with legally sufficient notice under MCL 600.5714 before commencing eviction proceedings. Plaintiff's rent was current — Ledger Version A (August 22, 2024) showed a **credit balance of negative $61.64**. The eviction was based on fabricated arrears created by retroactively manipulating the ledger.

   b. **Eviction by a dissolved entity.** All three eviction dispositions (File Nos. 2405891BLT and 250616264T, totaling $9,551.13) were obtained by Homes of America LLC, a dissolved entity lacking legal capacity to sue under MCL 450.4802, rendering the judgments **void ab initio**.

   d. **Eviction based on fabricated arrears.** The eviction proceedings relied upon manipulated ledger records showing fabricated balances, when Plaintiff's actual account showed a credit balance once $6,085.99 in omitted payments are credited.

49. The eviction was accomplished through a combination of **fraud** (fabricated ledger records and dissolved-entity filings), **force** (physical removal of property and drilling of locks), and **stealth** (prior attempted break-in on July 3, 2025).

50. As a direct and proximate result, Plaintiff suffered: loss of his titled mobile home; loss of all personal property; loss of his residence and housing stability; increased housing costs; and severe emotional distress including 200+ consecutive days of separation from his minor child L.D.W., as Defendants' creation of housing instability was weaponized against Plaintiff in custody proceedings (Pigors v. Watson, Case No. 2024-001507-DC).

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against all Defendants, jointly and severally, for:

### COUNT II — MOBILE HOME COMMISSION ACT VIOLATIONS
#### MCL 125.2301 et seq.

51. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs by reference.

52. The **Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act, MCL 125.2301 et seq.**, governs the operation of manufactured housing communities and protects the rights of mobile home owners and tenants.

53. Defendants violated the following provisions of the Mobile Home Commission Act:

   a. **MCL 125.2307 — License Requirements.** Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC, the entity that operated the community and collected rent, was dissolved. A dissolved entity cannot hold or maintain a valid MHC license. All actions taken by the dissolved entity are ultra vires and potentially void.

   b. **MCL 125.2312a — Prohibited Community Rules.** Defendants imposed unauthorized utility submetering and other prohibited conditions not authorized by the Act.

   c. **MCL 125.2315 — Abandonment Procedures.** Defendants failed to follow mandatory abandonment procedures before claiming Plaintiff's home was "abandoned." No proper notice was given, no statutory waiting period was observed, and no required procedures were followed.

   d. **MCL 125.2319 — Interference with Home Sales.** Defendants blocked at least three documented home sales by making false statements to prospective buyers, refusing to approve qualified buyers, and claiming Plaintiff did not own his titled home.

   e. **MCL 125.2321 — Lot Rent Increase Requirements.** Defendants increased Plaintiff's rent from $325 to $720 per month (121% in 14 months) without providing the required 60-day written statutory notices.

   f. **MCL 125.2325 — Maintenance of Common Areas and Utility Systems.** Defendants failed to maintain the sewage system, leading to raw sewage discharge confirmed by EGLE inspection and Violation Notice VN-017235.

   g. **MCL 125.2328(1)(b) — Utility Rate Regulation.** Defendants imposed unauthorized water/sewer submeter charges of approximately $87/month for meters installed without Plaintiff's notice or consent, with demonstrably fabricated readings.

   h. **MCL 125.2334 — Community Standards.** Defendants failed to maintain the community in compliance with minimum health and safety standards.

   i. **MCL 125.2335 — Eviction Notice Requirements.** Defendants failed to provide proper written notice before commencing eviction proceedings in the manner and timeframes prescribed by statute.

54. The coerced lease with Cricklewood MHP LLC was signed off-site under express threat of eviction, raising Plaintiff's rent 121% in 14 months. No proper 60-day statutory notice under MCL 554.632 was provided for any increase.

55. As a direct result, Plaintiff suffered: overpayment of rent in excess of $4,000; loss of housing; loss of ability to sell his titled home; and deprivation of statutory protections.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against all Defendants, jointly and severally, for:
   a. All statutory remedies available under the Mobile Home Commission Act;
   b. Actual damages for rent overcharges and lost sale opportunities;
   c. Declaratory relief that the coerced Cricklewood lease is void;
   d. Such further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.

### COUNT VIII — FRAUD ($1,300.26 CHECK ENDORSEMENT)
#### Common Law Fraud; MCL 600.2911

84. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs by reference.

85. Under Michigan law, a claim of fraud requires: (1) a material representation; (2) the representation was false; (3) the defendant knew it was false or made it recklessly; (4) the defendant made it with the intention that the plaintiff act upon it; (5) the plaintiff acted in reliance on it; and (6) the plaintiff suffered damage. *Hi-Way Motor Co v International Harvester Co*, 398 Mich 330, 336 (1976).

86. **The $1,300.26 Check.** On or about August 14, 2024, Plaintiff issued a personal check in the amount of **$1,300.26** payable to Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC for rent and related charges. The check was endorsed, deposited, and cleared Plaintiff's bank account.

87. In Ledger Version A (August 22, 2024), the $1,300.26 payment was **fully credited** to Plaintiff's account. In Ledger Version B (January 8, 2025), this same payment was **completely omitted** — erased from Plaintiff's account as if it never existed.

88. **Elements of fraud as applied:**

   a. **Material representation:** Defendants presented Ledger Version B to the 60th District Court as an accurate record of Plaintiff's account, representing that Plaintiff owed $2,180.18;

   b. **Falsity:** The ledger was false — it omitted the $1,300.26 payment that had been credited just months earlier in Version A;

   c. **Knowledge:** Defendants knew the payment had been received and credited, as reflected in their own earlier ledger;

   d. **Intent:** The revised ledger was created and presented three days before filing the second eviction — the timing proves intent to induce reliance by the Court;

   e. **Reliance:** The 60th District Court relied on the fraudulent ledger in entering judgment against Plaintiff;

   f. **Damage:** Plaintiff was evicted, lost his home and property, and suffered the consequences set forth herein.

89. The endorsement and appropriation of Plaintiff's $1,300.26 while simultaneously removing the credit from his account constitutes both **fraud** and **conversion** of Plaintiff's funds — Defendants took the money and then pretended it was never paid.

90. The broader ledger manipulation scheme involved omission of **$6,085.99** in total documented payments, constituting a systematic pattern of fraud upon Plaintiff and upon the Court.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against all Defendants, jointly and severally, for:
   a. Compensatory damages of not less than **$1,300.26** (the check amount);
   b. Additional actual damages for all harm caused by the fraudulent ledger scheme;

   d. Such further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.
