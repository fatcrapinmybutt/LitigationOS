## III. NATURE OF VIOLATIONS

### A. MCL 125.2307 — License Requirements (CRITICAL)

Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC and Homes of America LLC, the entities that operated the community, collected rent, and initiated eviction proceedings, are believed to be administratively dissolved. A dissolved entity cannot hold or maintain a valid MHC license. All actions taken by dissolved entities — including rent collection, lease enforcement, eviction proceedings, and lien assertions — are ultra vires and potentially void. *See* MCL 450.4802.

**LARA Action Requested:** Investigate the licensure status of all entities operating Shady Oaks MHC. If the licensed operator is dissolved, revoke or suspend the community license until a legally existing operator is designated.

### B. MCL 125.2315 — Abandonment Procedures (CRITICAL)

On July 17, 2025, Defendants' agents (Nicole Browley, Henry Brandel, and others) forcibly entered Complainant's titled mobile home (1970 Marlette, VIN 1646732), drilled locks, removed all personal property, and posted a "FREE" sign inviting public scavenging. No mandatory abandonment procedures were followed:

- No written notice of intent to declare abandonment was provided;
- No statutory waiting period was observed;
- No inventory was made of removed property;
- No court officer supervised the removal;
- Complainant's title was valid and current with the Michigan Secretary of State.

Park manager Casey VanDam subsequently told a prospective buyer on February 18, 2026 that "Andrew Pigors does not own a home at Shady Oaks MHC" and that the home was "abandoned" — statements that are materially false.

**LARA Action Requested:** Investigate violations of MCL 125.2315 abandonment procedures and order restoration of Complainant's property rights.

### C. MCL 125.2319 — Interference with Home Sales (CRITICAL)

Defendants blocked at least three documented home sales by:
- Refusing to approve qualified buyers without legitimate basis;
- Making false statements to prospective buyers that Complainant "does not own" the home;
- Claiming the home was "abandoned" and that Defendants intended to sell it themselves;
- Agent Nicole Browley harassed prospective buyers.

**LARA Action Requested:** Investigate interference with home sales in violation of MCL 125.2319.

### D. MCL 125.2321 — Lot Rent Increase Requirements

Defendants increased Complainant's lot rent from $325/month to $720/month (121% increase in 14 months) without providing the required 60-day written statutory notices. The rent escalation occurred through a coerced lease modification with Cricklewood MHP LLC — a different entity — signed off-site under express threat of eviction.

**Timeline:**
- November 2023: $325/month (original lease with Joe Matyk)
- March 26, 2024: Coerced off-site lease with Cricklewood → $395/month
- Subsequent increase: $695/month (no notice)
- February 2025: $720/month (no notice)

**LARA Action Requested:** Investigate failure to provide statutory rent increase notices and order restitution of overcharges.

### E. MCL 125.2325 — Maintenance of Common Areas and Utility Systems

Raw sewage overflowed from an interceptor tank onto the ground surface near occupied homes beginning in late 2024. EGLE inspected on March 20, 2025 and issued Violation Notice VN-017235 on April 15, 2025, confirming:
- Raw sewage discharging onto the ground surface;
- NOT IN COMPLIANCE ratings for Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance;
- Failure to notify EGLE within 24 hours;
- Failure to submit required written reports.

Defendants performed only minimal remediation (one hour of "TV and jetting") with no excavation or pipe replacement. Sewage resumed leaking in late June 2025.

**LARA Action Requested:** Investigate maintenance failures, coordinate with EGLE, and order full remediation.

### F. MCL 125.2328(1)(b) — Utility Rate Regulation

Beginning May 2025, Defendants imposed unauthorized water/sewer submeter charges of approximately $87/month. The meters were self-installed by park management without:
- Tenant notice or consent;
- Municipal oversight or calibration;
- Third-party verification.

Analysis of Zego billing records reveals identical water and sewer gallons every month (e.g., 4,245 = 4,245; 3,233 = 3,233) — a physical impossibility, proving fabricated readings.

**LARA Action Requested:** Investigate unauthorized utility submetering and order cessation and restitution.

### G. MCL 125.2331 — Retaliatory Eviction

Complainant was evicted in retaliation for exercising rights under the Mobile Home Commission Act, including:
- Reporting sewage violations to EGLE and county health authorities;
- Challenging fabricated ledger charges;
- Filing a small claims action for ledger fraud;
- Attempting to sell his mobile home.

Each protected activity was followed by escalating adverse action by Defendants within 90 days.

**LARA Action Requested:** Investigate retaliatory conduct and impose penalties.
