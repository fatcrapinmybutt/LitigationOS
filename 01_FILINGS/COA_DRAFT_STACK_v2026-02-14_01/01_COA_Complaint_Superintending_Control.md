## I. INTRODUCTION
1. Plaintiff seeks **extraordinary relief** compelling the trial court to perform **clear legal duties** imposed by Michigan Court Rules governing ex parte custody/parenting-time orders and the timely provision of a meaningful evidentiary hearing.
2. ## V. ARGUMENT
### A. MCR 3.207 imposes mandatory hearing/timing and process safeguards for ex parte custody/parenting-time orders.
14. Plaintiff alleges the trial court issued/maintained an order altering parenting time without ensuring the mandatory, timely evidentiary process required by MCR 3.207 for ex parte custody/parenting-time orders.
15. Plaintiff alleges the trial court’s “continue until assessment” condition operates as a procedural **barrier** that defeats the purpose of the rule’s expedited hearing protections when parenting time is suspended.

### B. Conditioning the right to be heard on completion of an assessment, while maintaining an ex parte suspension, violates basic due-process requirements in custody/parenting-time proceedings.
16. Plaintiff alleges the trial court’s approach creates a **one-way ratchet**: deprivation first, adjudication later (or never), contrary to the due-process baseline of notice + meaningful opportunity to be heard at a meaningful time.

### C. Plaintiff lacks an adequate alternative remedy because the harm is ongoing deprivation of parenting time and the lower court process has not provided a timely evidentiary hearing.
17. Plaintiff alleges conventional motion practice has not produced a timely evidentiary hearing, and the deprivation continues during repeated continuances.

## VI. RELIEF REQUESTED
Plaintiff requests that this Court:
18. Issue an order of superintending control directing the trial court to:
