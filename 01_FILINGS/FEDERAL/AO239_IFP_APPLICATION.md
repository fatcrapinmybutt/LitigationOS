## MOTION FOR LEAVE TO PROCEED IN FORMA PAUPERIS

Plaintiff Andrew James Pigors respectfully moves this Court, pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1915(a)(1), for leave to commence this civil action without prepayment of the filing fee of $405.00, or any portion thereof.

In support of this Motion, Plaintiff states:

1. Plaintiff is a natural person appearing pro se in this civil rights action under 42 U.S.C. § 1983 and § 1985(3).

2. Plaintiff is unable to pay the costs of these proceedings or to give security therefor, and believes he is entitled to relief.

3. In support of this motion, Plaintiff submits the attached Affidavit in Support of Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis, made under penalty of perjury.

4. The United States Supreme Court has held that the statute governing IFP proceedings "is designed to ensure that indigent litigants have meaningful access to the federal courts." *Neitzke v. Williams*, 490 U.S. 319, 324 (1989). The IFP privilege should be granted when the applicant demonstrates financial inability to pay. *Adkins v. E.I. DuPont de Nemours & Co.*, 335 U.S. 331, 339 (1948) ("We cannot agree with the court below that one must be absolutely destitute to enjoy the benefit of the statute.").

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court grant leave to proceed in forma pauperis without prepayment of the $405.00 filing fee.
