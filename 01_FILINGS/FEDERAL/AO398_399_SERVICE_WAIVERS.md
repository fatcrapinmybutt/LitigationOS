Dear [Defendant / Counsel for Defendant]:

I am writing to notify you that I have filed a civil rights complaint against you in the United States District Court for the Western District of Michigan (Grand Rapids Division) under 42 U.S.C. § 1983 and § 1985(3). A copy of the complaint is enclosed with this letter.

**Under Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 4(d), I am requesting that you waive formal service of process.** Enclosed you will find:

1. **AO 398** — Notice of a Lawsuit and Request to Waive Service of a Summons
2. **AO 399** — Waiver of the Service of Summons (for you to sign and return)
3. A copy of the Complaint with all exhibits
4. A prepaid return envelope

**Why you should waive service:**

Under FRCP 4(d), a defendant located within the United States who is properly notified of a lawsuit has a **duty to avoid unnecessary expenses of serving the summons**. If you agree to waive service:

- You will have **60 days** from the date this request was sent to file your answer or responsive motion (instead of the standard 21 days after formal service).
- You will **not** waive any objection to venue or personal jurisdiction.
- You will save the costs of formal service, which would otherwise be taxable against you.

**If you do NOT return the waiver within 30 days:**

- I will arrange for formal service of process upon you.
- Under FRCP 4(d)(2), **you will be required to pay the expenses incurred in making service**, including reasonable attorney's fees for any motion to collect those costs — unless good cause is shown for the failure to waive.

**To waive service**, please:

1. Complete and sign the enclosed **AO 399 Waiver of Service of Summons** form
2. Return it to me at the address above within **30 days** of the date this notice was sent
3. Keep a copy of the waiver and the complaint for your records

This request does not mean that you agree with or admit to any of the allegations in the complaint. It simply avoids the expense and formality of personal service.

Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter.

Respectfully,

___________________________________________
Andrew James Pigors, Pro Se Plaintiff

### NOTICE TO: EMILY A. WATSON

A lawsuit has been filed against you, a copy of which is attached to this notice. It has been filed in the **United States District Court for the Western District of Michigan** and has been assigned Civil Action Number ________________.

This is not a formal summons or notification from the court, but rather my request that you sign and return the enclosed waiver of service in order to save the cost of serving you with a judicial summons and an extra copy of the complaint. To avoid these expenses, please sign and return the enclosed **waiver form**. This is authorized by **Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 4(d)**.

**Consequences of compliance and noncompliance:**

If you return the signed waiver, I will file it with the court. The action will then proceed as if you had been served on the date the waiver is filed, but **you will not be required to answer the complaint for 60 days from the date indicated below** (instead of the normal 21 days after service).

If you do not return the signed waiver within the time indicated below, **I will arrange to have the summons and complaint served on you.** And I will ask the court to require you (or the entity you represent) to **pay the expenses of making service**, as provided in Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 4(d).

**Important information:**

- A waiver of service **does not waive** any objection you may have to the jurisdiction of the court or to the venue of the action.
- You retain all defenses and objections available under the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure.

**Please keep a copy of this notice and the enclosed waiver for your records.**

> **PRACTITIONER NOTE:** While FRCP 4(d) applies to all defendants, a sitting judge sued in individual capacity may decline to waive and require formal service. This is not uncommon. The waiver request is still proper and preserves the right to seek service costs if the waiver is unreasonably refused.

> **SERVICE NOTE — GOVERNMENTAL ENTITIES:** FRCP 4(d) provides that the duty to avoid unnecessary expenses of service applies to individuals, corporations, and associations. Whether a county government has such a duty is debatable. However, sending the waiver request is harmless and may save costs. If the County declines, formal service under FRCP 4(j)(2) must be accomplished as described in the Summons document.
