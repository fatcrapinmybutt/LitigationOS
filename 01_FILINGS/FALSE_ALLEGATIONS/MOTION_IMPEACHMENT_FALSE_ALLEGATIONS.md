## III. LEGAL STANDARD

### A. Michigan Rules of Evidence

**MRE 608(b) — Specific Instances of Conduct (Character for Truthfulness)**

Specific instances of conduct of a witness, for the purpose of attacking the witness's character for truthfulness, may be inquired into on cross-examination of the witness. A pattern of making false allegations is directly probative of a witness's character for untruthfulness.

**MRE 613 — Prior Inconsistent Statements**

In examining a witness concerning a prior statement made by the witness, the statement need not be shown nor its contents disclosed to the witness at that time, but on request the same shall be shown or disclosed to opposing counsel. Extrinsic evidence of a prior inconsistent statement by a witness is admissible if the witness is afforded an opportunity to explain or deny the same.

**MRE 801(d)(2) — Admissions by Party-Opponent**

A statement is not hearsay if it is offered against a party and is the party's own statement. Defendant's prior false allegations, made in sworn filings and to law enforcement, constitute admissions by party-opponent.

### B. Michigan Compiled Laws

**MCL 750.423 — Perjury**

Any person authorized by a statute of this state to take an oath, or any person of whom an oath is required by law, who willfully swears falsely in regard to any matter or thing respecting which such oath is authorized or required, is guilty of perjury.

**MCL 722.23(j) — Best Interest Factor**

The willingness and ability of each of the parties to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent. Making false allegations to interfere with parenting time and to alienate the minor child L.D.W. from Plaintiff directly implicates this factor.

**MCL 722.23(l) — Domestic Violence**

False allegations of domestic violence, when used to obtain protective orders and restrict parenting time, constitute an abuse of process that this Court should consider in evaluating best interest factors.

### C. Michigan Case Law

**Sullivan v Gray, 117 Mich App 476 (1982)** — Recording admissibility in Michigan; relevant to documented contradictions captured in recordings.

**Foskett v Foskett, 247 Mich App 1 (2001)** — Court must consider false allegations as relevant to custody determinations under MCL 722.23.

**MCR 1.109(E) — Sanctions**

The court may impose sanctions on a party who signs a document in violation of this rule, including filing documents containing false statements of material fact.
