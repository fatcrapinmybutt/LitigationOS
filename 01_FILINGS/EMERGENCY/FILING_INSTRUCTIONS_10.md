# HUD COMPLAINT FILING INSTRUCTIONS
# Step-by-Step Guide — Pigors v. Shady Oaks et al.

**Prepared:** March 04, 2026

---

## FILING METHODS (Choose One)

### ⭐ OPTION 1: Online Filing (RECOMMENDED — Fastest)

**URL:** https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/fair_housing_equal_opp/online-complaint

**Steps:**
1. Navigate to the HUD online complaint portal
2. Select "File a Complaint" 
3. Complete all fields using information from HUD_COMPLAINT_FORM.md
4. Upload supporting documents (PDF format)
5. Review and submit
6. **SAVE YOUR CONFIRMATION NUMBER** — This is your case reference
7. Print/screenshot the confirmation page

**Processing Time:** HUD must begin investigation within 100 days of filing (42 U.S.C. § 3610(a)(1)(B)(iv))

---

Office of Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity — Region V

**Steps:**

4. Include copies of key exhibits (Category A, B, E, F priority)
5. Send via **Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested**
6. Keep copies of EVERYTHING you send
7. Note the certified mail tracking number

---

### OPTION 3: Telephone Filing

**HUD Housing Discrimination Hotline:** **(800) 669-9777**
**TTY:** (800) 927-9275
**Hours:** Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM EST

**Steps:**
1. Call the hotline
2. State you wish to file a housing discrimination complaint
3. The intake specialist will ask questions from HUD Form 903
4. Have HUD_COMPLAINT_FORM.md and SUPPORTING_STATEMENT.md in front of you
5. Provide all respondent names and addresses
6. Describe each discriminatory act with dates
7. Request a written confirmation of your complaint
8. Note the complaint number and the specialist's name

## CONCURRENT FILINGS (RECOMMENDED)

### Michigan Department of Civil Rights (MDCR)
- **URL:** https://www.michigan.gov/mdcr
- **Phone:** (800) 482-3604
- HUD has a work-sharing agreement with MDCR
- Filing with HUD may automatically dual-file with MDCR
- Confirm dual-filing status when you file

### LARA — Mobile Home Commission
- File separate complaint for violations of MCL 125.2301 et seq.
- **URL:** https://www.michigan.gov/lara
- Focus on: lease violations, unlawful fees, failure to maintain

## WHAT TO EXPECT AFTER FILING

| Step | Timeline | What Happens |
|------|----------|--------------|
| 1. Acknowledgment | 10–20 days | HUD sends written acknowledgment |
| 2. Investigation | 100 days target | HUD investigates (interviews, document requests) |
| 3. Conciliation | During investigation | HUD attempts voluntary resolution |
| 4. Determination | After investigation | HUD issues "Cause" or "No Cause" finding |
| 5a. If Cause | After determination | HUD files charge; ALJ hearing scheduled |
| 5b. Election | Before ALJ hearing | You may elect federal court (42 U.S.C. § 3612(a)) |
| 6. Resolution | Varies | Conciliation agreement, ALJ order, or federal court judgment |
