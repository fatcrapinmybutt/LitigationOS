## SECTION 5 — DESCRIPTION OF DISCRIMINATORY ACTS

### Summary of Complaint

From approximately late 2023 through July 17, 2025 (and continuing), Respondents engaged in a systematic pattern of discriminatory, retaliatory, and unlawful housing practices against Complainant Andrew J. Pigors at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community, Muskegon, Michigan.

### COUNT I — Discriminatory Terms, Conditions, and Privileges (42 U.S.C. § 3604(b))

Respondents subjected Complainant to discriminatory terms and conditions of housing by:

1. **Fabricating charges on Complainant's lot rent ledger** — Unauthorized fees were layered onto Complainant's account, including a $120/month water/sewer charge imposed without proper metering (February–June 2025), inflated administrative fees of $738.24 (May 15, 2025), and other unexplained charges.

2. **Refusing to accept valid government housing assistance** — Respondents refused to properly accept or credit payments from MDHHS (Michigan Department of Health and Human Services) and TrueNorth Community Services, government-funded housing assistance programs. This refusal had a disparate impact on low-income tenants.

3. **Coercing a new lease under duress** — On March 26, 2024, Complainant was coerced under threat of immediate eviction into signing a new lease at an off-site location (Cricklewood) at higher rent ($695/month, up from original rate), contrary to Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act requirements.

4. **Billing for utilities during uninhabitable conditions** — Respondents continued billing full utilities including water/sewer charges while raw sewage was present near Complainant's lot (a condition confirmed by EGLE violations from late 2024 through mid-2025).

5. **Selective enforcement** — Community rules were enforced more harshly against Complainant than similarly situated tenants.

### COUNT II — Retaliation (42 U.S.C. § 3617)

Respondents retaliated against Complainant for exercising protected housing rights, including reporting health and safety violations to EGLE and asserting his right to sell his manufactured home:

1. **Escalated eviction proceedings after EGLE complaint** — After Complainant reported raw sewage near his lot to EGLE (Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy), Respondents accelerated eviction efforts. On January 21, 2025, Complainant was served with a Notice to Quit for nonpayment, based on a fabricated balance inflated by unlawful fees.

2. **Blocked three separate home sales** — Respondents blocked Complainant's attempts to sell his manufactured home on at least three (3) occasions by making false statements to prospective buyers.

3. **False statements to prospective buyers** — The community manager told prospective buyers that Complainant "no longer owns his home" and had "abandoned" it — statements known to be false as Complainant held valid title throughout.

4. **Filed fraudulent res judicata motion** — On July 15, 2025, attorney Jeremy Brown filed a fraudulent res judicata motion designed to extinguish Complainant's rights.

5. **Destruction of home and personal property** — On July 17, 2025, with sheriff presence, Respondents drilled Complainant's locks, cleared his home, and removed or destroyed his personal property and appliances. Within 24 hours, a "FREE" sign was placed on Complainant's home. No proper inventory was conducted. Non-officers (including neighbor Henry Brandel) participated in removal/distribution of Complainant's property.

### COUNT III — Interference with Housing Rights (42 U.S.C. § 3617)

Respondents interfered with Complainant's exercise of fair housing rights by:

1. **Preventing sale of manufactured home** — Respondents prevented Complainant from exercising his right to sell his manufactured home under MCL 125.2319, which guarantees manufactured home owners the right to sell their homes.

2. **False statements to third parties** — Respondents made false statements regarding Complainant's ownership and occupancy status to prospective buyers, effectively preventing any sale.

3. **Using eviction to weaponize custody proceedings** — The resulting homelessness was used in family court proceedings to obtain an ex parte order on August 8, 2025, suspending Complainant's parenting time. Complainant was separated from his minor child for approximately two (2) months solely because Respondents' conduct rendered him homeless.

4. **Attempted break-in** — On July 3, 2025, two individuals attempted to break into Complainant's home, captured on security cameras. Those same individuals appeared during the July 17, 2025 set-out, suggesting coordination with Respondents.

5. **Operation through dissolved entity** — Respondents filed eviction actions using "Shady Oaks MHP LLC," an entity dissolved on approximately February 15, 2023, per LARA records. This entity appeared in court on July 10, 2025, despite being legally defunct — raising questions about the legitimacy of the entire eviction proceeding.

### COUNT IV — Constructive Eviction and Uninhabitable Conditions

Respondents constructively evicted Complainant by:

1. **Allowing raw sewage near Complainant's lot** — From late 2024 through mid-2025, raw sewage was present near Lot 17. EGLE issued violations. Respondents failed to remediate.

2. **Continuing to bill full rent and utilities** — Despite uninhabitable conditions, Respondents continued billing full lot rent and utility charges.

3. **Failing to maintain premises** — Respondents breached their obligation to maintain the premises in reasonable repair under MCL 554.139 and the Mobile Home Commission Act.
