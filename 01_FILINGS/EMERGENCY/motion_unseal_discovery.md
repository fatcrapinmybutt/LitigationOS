# PLAINTIFF'S MOTION TO COMPEL PRODUCTION AND UNSEAL MENTAL HEALTH RECORDS

---

**NOW COMES** Plaintiff, Andrew Pigors, pro se, and respectfully moves this Honorable Court for an Order compelling production of and/or unsealing mental health evaluation records in this matter, and in support states as follows:

## I. INTRODUCTION

1. This Motion concerns the HealthWest mental health evaluation(s) relied upon by this Court in proceedings affecting Plaintiff's parenting time. Plaintiff was never served with, notified of, or given an opportunity to respond to at least one evaluation report used by the Court.

2. Plaintiff seeks production of all HealthWest evaluation reports, communications between HealthWest and the Court, and any related records, so that Plaintiff may exercise his fundamental right to confront evidence used against him.

## II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

3. On August 15, 2025, Plaintiff completed a mental health evaluation at HealthWest of Muskegon, as instructed by the Court. Per the Court's instruction, Plaintiff submitted the evaluation results privately to the Court via email. No written court order authorized this evaluation — it was conducted "off the books."

4. The Court subsequently relied upon at least one additional HealthWest evaluation report in proceedings. Plaintiff was never served with this report and only discovered its existence through a FOIA response received on November 21, 2025.

5. Discovery documents (Requests to Admit #1-4, served September 17, 2025) confirm that Plaintiff had no pre-hearing notice and only received evidence after hearings concluded.

6. On August 8, 2025, the Court entered an ex parte order (MCR 3.207(B)) suspending all of Plaintiff's parenting time (from 50% to 0%) based in part on evidence Plaintiff was never served.

## III. LEGAL AUTHORITY

### A. MCR 2.302(B)(1) — Scope of Discovery

Parties may obtain discovery regarding any matter, not privileged, which is relevant to the subject matter involved in the pending action. The withheld HealthWest evaluation report(s) are directly relevant to the Court's decisions regarding parenting time and are central to the fairness of these proceedings.

### B. MCR 2.313 — Failure to Provide Discovery / Motion to Compel

Where a party fails to answer interrogatories or produce documents, the discovering party may move for an order compelling discovery. To the extent Defendant or the Court possesses HealthWest evaluation records not produced to Plaintiff, this Court should compel their production.

### C. MRE 106 — Remainder of or Related Writings or Recorded Statements

When a writing or recorded statement or part thereof is introduced in evidence, an adverse party may require the introduction at that time of any other part or any other writing or recorded statement which ought in fairness to be considered contemporaneously. If the Court relied on HealthWest evaluations, fairness requires Plaintiff have access to all such evaluations.

### D. MCL 330.1748 — Confidentiality / Disclosure Framework

While MCL 330.1748 protects the confidentiality of mental health records, this protection belongs to the patient (Plaintiff). Plaintiff hereby consents to the production and unsealing of his own mental health evaluation records for purposes of this litigation. The patient's right to confidentiality cannot be used against him to deny access to evidence used in proceedings affecting his fundamental rights.

### E. Constitutional Due Process

The 14th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution and Michigan Constitution Art. I, § 17 guarantee Plaintiff the right to notice and an opportunity to be heard. Reliance on undisclosed evidence violates these fundamental protections.

## IV. ARGUMENT

7. The Court relied on HealthWest evaluation report(s) in making critical decisions affecting Plaintiff's parenting time — a fundamental liberty interest. See *Troxel v Granville*, 530 US 57; 120 S Ct 2054 (2000); *Santosky v Kramer*, 455 US 745; 102 S Ct 1388 (1982). Plaintiff was denied access to this evidence, denied notice of its submission, and denied an opportunity to respond.

8. This denial of access violates both the Michigan Court Rules governing discovery and Plaintiff's constitutional right to due process. No protective order, confidentiality rule, or procedural mechanism justifies withholding from a party the very evidence used against him.

9. Plaintiff consents to a protective order if the Court deems one necessary, but requests that all HealthWest evaluation reports and related communications be produced to Plaintiff forthwith.

## V. RELIEF REQUESTED

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Honorable Court:

1. **COMPEL** the production of all HealthWest mental health evaluation reports related to Plaintiff in this matter, including but not limited to any reports submitted to the Court by HealthWest or any other party.

2. **UNSEAL** any sealed records related to the HealthWest evaluation(s).

3. **ORDER** production of all communications between HealthWest and the Court (or any party) regarding Plaintiff's evaluation.

4. **ORDER** that no further reliance be placed on undisclosed evaluation reports until Plaintiff has had a meaningful opportunity to review and respond.

5. **GRANT** any other relief this Court deems just and equitable.
