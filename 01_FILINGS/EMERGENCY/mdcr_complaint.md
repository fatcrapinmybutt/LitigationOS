## III. STATEMENT OF FACTS

1. On or about April 1, 2024, Custody/Support Case No. 2024-001507-DC was filed in the 14th Circuit Court, Muskegon County, before Judge Jenny L. McNeill.

2. On August 8, 2025, Defendant Emily Watson filed an Emergency Ex Parte Motion to suspend Complainant's parenting time. The court entered an Ex Parte Order (MCR 3.207(B)) that same day, reducing Complainant's parenting time from 50% to 0% — without prior notice to Complainant.

3. On August 15, 2025, Complainant completed a mental health evaluation at HealthWest of Muskegon, as instructed by the court. Per the court's instruction, Complainant submitted the evaluation privately to the court via email — conducted "off the books" with no written court order authorizing the evaluation.

4. The court subsequently relied upon a second, undisclosed HealthWest evaluation report in subsequent proceedings. Complainant was never notified of this second report, never consented to its disclosure, and was never given the opportunity to confront or respond to this evidence.

5. MCL 330.1748 requires that mental health records remain confidential absent patient consent. HealthWest disclosed Complainant's protected mental health evaluation to the court without Complainant's consent or notice, in direct violation of Michigan's Mental Health Code (MCL 330.1001 et seq.).

6. Discovery documents (Requests to Admit, served September 17, 2025) confirm the court used evidence Complainant was never served. Specifically, Requests to Admit #1-4 establish that Complainant had no pre-hearing notice and only received evidence after hearings had concluded.

7. Watson's attorney (Mandi Martini) and court personnel (Pamela Rusco, clerks) facilitated ex parte submissions of evidence, including a USB drive containing audio recordings, which were delivered to the judge's chambers without Complainant's knowledge or presence.

8. The opposing party (Emily Watson and Lori Watson) orchestrated false police reports and misused Personal Protection Order (MCL 600.2950; MCR 3.705)s to strip Complainant of parenting time. Audio recordings exist wherein Emily and Lori Watson admit they fabricated police complaints, including staging a bogus suicide "welfare check" to obtain a PPO.

9. Court filings confirm repeated unproven allegations (drug use, threats) were relied upon without evidentiary support. Complainant's motions and continuance requests were consistently ignored or denied.

10. Complainant was jailed twice on meritless show-cause orders: first on approximately February 10, 2025, and again from November 23, 2025, through January 6, 2026 — without any new incident justifying incarceration.

11. On October 31, 2025, Complainant submitted a FOIA request to HealthWest. The FOIA response was received via secure email on November 21, 2025, and revealed the existence of a previously undisclosed evaluation report.

12. These actions resulted in: loss of all parenting time, job loss due to incarceration, eviction notices, and severe emotional distress.

## IV. LEGAL THEORIES

1. **MCL 330.1748 Violation:** HealthWest disclosed confidential mental health records without patient consent, violating Michigan's Mental Health Code (MCL 330.1001 et seq.).

2. **Michigan Civil Rights Act Violation:** Complainant's civil rights were violated through discriminatory treatment in family court proceedings, including gender-based bias in parental rights determination.

3. **Due Process Violation (14th Amendment):** Complainant was deprived of fundamental liberty interests (parental rights) without notice or an opportunity to be heard, through ex parte proceedings and secret evidence. See *Troxel v Granville*, 530 US 57; 120 S Ct 2054 (2000); *Santosky v Kramer*, 455 US 745; 102 S Ct 1388 (1982).

4. **FOIA Violation:** Mental health records exempt under FOIA were improperly handled and disclosed.

5. **Abuse of Process:** Legal procedures (PPO, ex parte motions) were used for ulterior purposes — specifically, parental alienation and deprivation of parenting time.
