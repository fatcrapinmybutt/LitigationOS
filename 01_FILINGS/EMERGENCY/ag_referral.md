## II. LEGAL BASIS

### Federal Civil Rights — 42 U.S.C. § 1983
The above-named state actors, acting under color of state law (42 USC 1983), deprived Complainant of rights secured by the United States Constitution:

- **14th Amendment, Due Process Clause (U.S. Const. amend. XIV, Sec. 1; Const. 1963, art. 1, Sec. 17):** Complainant was deprived of his fundamental liberty interest in parental rights without notice or an opportunity to be heard. See *Troxel v Granville*, 530 US 57; 120 S Ct 2054 (2000); *Santosky v Kramer*, 455 US 745; 102 S Ct 1388 (1982). The court relied on secret evidence (undisclosed HealthWest evaluation, ex parte USB audio submissions) and entered orders reducing parenting time from 50% to 0% based on ex parte proceedings.

- **14th Amendment, Equal Protection Clause (U.S. Const. amend. XIV, Sec. 1; Const. 1963, art. 1, Sec. 2):** Complainant was subjected to discriminatory treatment in family court proceedings. Complainant's motions and requests for continuance were consistently denied, while opposing party's ex parte motions were granted without scrutiny.

### State Constitutional Violations
- **Michigan Constitution, Art. I, § 17** — Due process
- **Michigan Constitution, Art. I, § 2** — Equal protection

### Michigan Civil Rights Act (MCL 37.2101 et seq.)
Discrimination in the administration of justice, specifically in family court proceedings.

## III. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

### A. Deprivation of Due Process Under Color of Law (42 USC 1983)

1. In Case No. 2024-001507-DC, Judge McNeill and court personnel systematically deprived Complainant of procedural due process by:

   a. Entering an ex parte order (MCR 3.207(B)) on August 8, 2025, suspending all parenting time (50% to 0%) without prior notice to Complainant, in violation of MCR 3.206(C).

   b. Receiving and reviewing evidence (USB audio recordings) ex parte — delivered by opposing counsel (Mandi Martini) to the judge's chambers without Complainant's knowledge.

   c. Relying on an undisclosed second HealthWest mental health evaluation that was never served on Complainant and to which he had no opportunity to respond.

   d. Ignoring Complainant's motions, continuance requests, and objections while granting opposing party's motions without evidentiary hearings.

2. Complainant was jailed twice on show-cause orders without new incidents justifying incarceration:
   - February 10, 2025 — jailed for contempt at PPO hearing
   - November 23, 2025 – January 6, 2026 — jailed on alleged PPO violation (MCL 600.2950; MCR 3.705) (no new act)

### B. Conspiracy to Deprive Civil Rights (42 U.S.C. §§ 1985/1986)

3. The Watson family (Emily Watson, Lori Watson), court personnel (Pamela Rusco), and opposing counsel (Mandi Martini) acted in concert to deprive Complainant of his civil rights by:

   a. Filing seven false police reports in 2023 containing fabricated allegations (poisoning, assault, drug use, threats)
   b. Staging incidents (including a bogus suicide "welfare check") to obtain a Personal Protection Order (MCL 600.2950; MCR 3.705)
   c. Submitting evidence ex parte to the court without Complainant's knowledge
   d. Facilitating unauthorized disclosure of Complainant's confidential mental health records

### C. Unauthorized Disclosure of Protected Records

4. HealthWest of Muskegon, a state-funded community mental health agency, disclosed Complainant's confidential mental health evaluation to the court without consent, violating MCL 330.1748 and HIPAA (42 USC 1320d; 45 CFR Parts 160, 164) (45 C.F.R. § 164.502).
