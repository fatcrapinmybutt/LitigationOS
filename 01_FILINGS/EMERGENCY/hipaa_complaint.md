## III. DESCRIPTION OF VIOLATION

### What Happened

On August 15, 2025, Complainant completed a court-ordered mental health evaluation at HealthWest of Muskegon in connection with Muskegon County Family Court Case No. 2024-001507-DC (Judge Jenny L. McNeill, 14th Circuit Court).

Per the court's instruction, Complainant submitted the evaluation results privately to the court via email. No written court order authorized this evaluation — it was conducted "off the books."

Between approximately August 2025 and November 2025, HealthWest disclosed a **second evaluation report** to the court **without Complainant's written authorization or consent**. Complainant was never notified of this disclosure. Complainant discovered the existence of this unauthorized disclosure only through a FOIA response received on November 21, 2025.

### What Protected Health Information (PHI) Was Disclosed

- Mental health evaluation report(s) — including diagnoses, clinical observations, treatment recommendations, and other sensitive mental health information
- The PHI was disclosed to the 14th Circuit Court without Complainant's written authorization

### When Did the Violation Occur

- **Evaluation completed:** August 15, 2025
- **Unauthorized disclosure:** Between August 2025 and November 2025 (exact date unknown)
- **Discovery of violation:** November 21, 2025 (via FOIA response)

## IV. HIPAA (42 USC 1320d; 45 CFR Parts 160, 164) PROVISIONS VIOLATED

1. **45 C.F.R. § 164.502(a)** — Uses and disclosures of protected health information: general rules. HealthWest disclosed PHI without a valid authorization from the patient.

2. **45 C.F.R. § 164.508** — Uses and disclosures for which an authorization is required. Mental health evaluation records require written patient authorization before disclosure. No such authorization was obtained.

3. **45 C.F.R. § 164.528** — Accounting of disclosures of protected health information. Complainant was never provided notice or an accounting of the disclosures made to the court.

4. **45 C.F.R. § 164.524** — Access of individuals to protected health information. Complainant was denied knowledge of the second evaluation report until FOIA disclosure.

5. **42 U.S.C. § 1320d-6** — Wrongful disclosure of individually identifiable health information.

## VII. HARM SUFFERED

As a result of HealthWest's unauthorized disclosure:

1. The 14th Circuit Court relied on the undisclosed evaluation in ex parte proceedings, resulting in the suspension of all parenting time (50% to 0%).
2. Complainant was deprived of his right to confront and respond to evidence used against him.
3. Complainant suffered loss of parenting time, incarceration (Nov 23, 2025 – Jan 6, 2026), job loss, and severe emotional distress.
4. Complainant's private mental health information was used as evidence without his knowledge or consent.
