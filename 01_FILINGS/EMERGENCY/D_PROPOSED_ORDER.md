## FINDINGS OF FACT

1. Plaintiff Andrew J. Pigors is the biological father of the minor child, A.P. (DOB: November 9, 2022).

2. Plaintiff's parenting time with the minor child has been suspended since approximately August 8, 2025, a period of approximately 571+ consecutive days as of the date of this Order.

3. The suspension of parenting time was entered on an ex parte basis without prior notice to Plaintiff and without an evidentiary hearing at which Plaintiff could present evidence or cross-examine witnesses.

4. Plaintiff has voluntarily submitted to drug screening, with results NEGATIVE for all controlled substances.

5. Plaintiff was evaluated by HealthWest, with the initial evaluation returning a finding of "ALL ZEROS" -- no basis for concern regarding Plaintiff's parenting capacity.

6. multiple separate law enforcement investigations have been conducted based on allegations made in this matter, and every investigation resulted in zero (0) findings against Plaintiff.

7. No criminal charges have been filed against Plaintiff based on any allegation in this case.

8. No child protective services findings have been substantiated against Plaintiff.

9. The continuing separation of the minor child from Plaintiff causes ongoing, irreparable harm to the parent-child relationship and to the minor child's emotional and developmental well-being.

10. There is no clear and convincing evidence on the record that parenting time with Plaintiff would endanger the minor child's physical, mental, or emotional health.

---

## CONCLUSIONS OF LAW

1. MCL 722.27a(3) provides that a child has a right to parenting time with a parent unless it is shown on the record by clear and convincing evidence that parenting time would endanger the child's physical, mental, or emotional health. No such showing has been made.

2. MCL 722.27a(1) creates a presumption that parenting time is in the best interest of the child. This presumption has not been rebutted.

3. The Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution protects the fundamental right of parents to the care, custody, and control of their children. Troxel v. Granville, 530 U.S. 57 (2000); Santosky v. Kramer, 455 U.S. 745 (1982).

4. MCR 2.119(B)(7) authorizes emergency relief upon a showing of irreparable harm, reasonable notice to the adverse party, and an affidavit establishing the necessity for emergency relief. These requirements are satisfied.

5. The best interest factors under MCL 722.23 support restoration of Plaintiff's parenting time.

## ORDER

IT IS HEREBY ORDERED as follows:

### 1. IMMEDIATE SUPERVISED PARENTING TIME

Plaintiff shall have supervised parenting time with the minor child, A.P., commencing within **seventy-two (72) hours** of the date of this Order, on the following schedule:

   a. **Three (3) visits per week**, each lasting a minimum of **four (4) hours**;

   b. **Days and times**: To be agreed upon by the parties within 48 hours of this Order, or failing agreement, on Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday from 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM;

   c. **Location**: A neutral, court-approved supervised visitation facility, or such other location as the Court may designate;

   d. **Supervisor**: A court-approved professional supervisor who is not a family member of either party. The cost of supervision shall be [allocated by the Court / split equally / borne by ___________].

### 2. DAILY TELEPHONE/VIDEO CONTACT

Plaintiff shall have daily telephone or video contact with the minor child for a minimum of **thirty (30) minutes per day**, at a time to be agreed upon by the parties or, failing agreement, at **7:00 PM daily**. Defendant shall ensure that the minor child is available for such contact and shall facilitate the call in a positive and supportive manner.

### 3. TRANSITION TO UNSUPERVISED PARENTING TIME

Beginning thirty (30) days after the commencement of supervised parenting time, and provided no substantiated safety concerns arise:

   a. Parenting time shall transition to **unsupervised daytime visits**, initially two (2) visits per week of six (6) hours each;

   b. By sixty (60) days, parenting time shall include **one (1) overnight visit per week**;

   c. By ninety (90) days, parenting time shall transition to a **standard parenting time schedule** as follows:
      - Alternating weekends, Friday at 6:00 PM to Sunday at 6:00 PM
      - One weeknight overnight per week (Wednesday 5:00 PM to Thursday 8:00 AM)
      - Equal division of holidays and school breaks per Michigan Friend of the Court guidelines
      - Two (2) weeks of summer parenting time
      - Right of first refusal for childcare exceeding four (4) hours

### 4. COOPERATION REQUIRED

Defendant shall fully cooperate with the parenting time schedule and telephone/video contact set forth in this Order. Defendant shall not make negative or disparaging comments about Plaintiff to or in the presence of the minor child. Defendant shall encourage the minor child's relationship with Plaintiff.

### 5. REUNIFICATION THERAPY

Within fourteen (14) days of this Order, the parties shall begin participating in reunification therapy with a qualified mental health professional approved by the Court. Both parties shall cooperate fully with the reunification process. The cost of reunification therapy shall be [allocated by the Court].

### 6. EXPEDITED EVIDENTIARY HEARING

An expedited evidentiary hearing on all pending motions shall be scheduled within **fourteen (14) days** of the date of this Order. Both parties shall have the opportunity to present evidence, testify, and cross-examine witnesses. The hearing shall address:

   a. The permanent parenting time schedule;
   b. All pending motions;
   c. Any necessary modifications to this Order.

### 7. NO RETALIATION

Neither party shall take any retaliatory action against the other party, any witness, or any third party based on the filing of the Emergency Motion or the entry of this Order.

### 8. NO INTERFERENCE

Defendant shall not interfere with Plaintiff's parenting time or telephone/video contact in any manner. Any interference with court-ordered parenting time may result in contempt proceedings and/or modification of custody.

### 9. TEMPORARY NATURE OF THIS ORDER

This Order is temporary and shall remain in effect pending the expedited evidentiary hearing referenced in Paragraph 6, at which time the Court will consider all pending motions and enter appropriate further orders.

### 10. VIOLATIONS

Violation of any provision of this Order may result in a finding of contempt of court, sanctions, modification of custody, and/or such other relief as the Court deems appropriate.
