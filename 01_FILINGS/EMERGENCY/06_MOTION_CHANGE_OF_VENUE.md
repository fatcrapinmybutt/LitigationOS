## I. AUTHORITY

1. **MCR 2.222** — Transfer of actions for convenience of parties and witnesses, or in the interest of justice.

2. **MCR 2.003(D)** — When a judge is disqualified, the case must be assigned to another judge. If no judge in the circuit is available, the State Court Administrator shall assign a visiting judge.

3. **MCL 600.1651** — Change of venue may be granted when an impartial trial cannot be had in the county where the action is pending.

4. **MCR 8.111(C)** — Assignment of actions within a court; provisions for reassignment when necessary to promote the administration of justice.

---

## II. GROUNDS FOR CHANGE OF VENUE

### A. Inability to Obtain Impartial Proceedings in Current Assignment

5. As set forth in Plaintiff's contemporaneously filed Motion to Disqualify Judge (Document 04), the presiding judge has demonstrated a pervasive pattern of bias against Plaintiff, including:
   - **52 ex parte order (MCR 3.207(B))s** with 100%/0% grant rate disparity
   - **540 benchbook violations** documented
   - **1,005 judicial bias harm entries** in the record
   - Improper ex parte communication (MCR 3.207(B))s with evaluators
   - Retaliatory sanctions for exercise of legal rights

6. The contamination of the record is so extensive that no fair proceedings can continue under the current assignment, regardless of whether the presiding judge is formally disqualified.

### B. Structural Contamination of the Record

7. The record in this matter has been fundamentally compromised by:
   - 52 ex parte order (MCR 3.207(B))s entered without due process
   - Judicial communications with HealthWest evaluator that biased expert evidence
   - A pattern of rulings reflecting predetermined outcomes rather than impartial analysis
   - Cumulative prejudice that cannot be cured by reassignment alone

8. Under *Liteky v. United States*, 510 U.S. 540 (1994), when a judge's knowledge and impressions formed during the course of proceedings demonstrate deep-seated favoritism or antagonism, reassignment is required.

### C. Interest of Justice Requires Transfer

9. The interest of justice requires transfer because:
   - The child has been deprived of a relationship with his father for 571+ days
   - Every additional day of delay under the current proceedings causes irreparable harm
   - A fresh judicial perspective is needed to evaluate the record impartially
   - The integrity of the judicial system requires that the appearance of impropriety be addressed

### D. Local Considerations

10. Given that:
    - The 14th Circuit is a small circuit with limited judicial resources
    - The presiding judge's bias has been widely documented
    - Local attorneys and court staff may be influenced by the existing dynamics
    
    Transfer to a visiting judge from outside the circuit, or transfer to an adjacent circuit, may be necessary to ensure impartiality.
