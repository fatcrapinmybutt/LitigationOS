# PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR SANCTIONS UNDER MCR 2.114

---

**NOW COMES** Plaintiff, Andrew Pigors, pro se, and respectfully moves this Honorable Court for an Order imposing sanctions against Defendant Emily Watson and her counsel, Mandi Martini, Esq., for violations of MCR 2.114, and in support states as follows:

## I. LEGAL STANDARD — MCR 2.114

MCR 2.114(D) provides:

> "The signature of an attorney or party.. constitutes a certification by the signer that:
> (1) he or she has read the document;
> (2) to the best of his or her knowledge, information, and belief formed after reasonable inquiry, the document is well grounded in fact and is warranted by existing law.."

MCR 2.114(E) provides:

> "If a document is signed in violation of this rule, the court, on the motion of a party or on its own initiative, shall impose upon the person who signed it, a represented party, or both, an appropriate sanction, which may include an order to pay to the other party or parties the amount of the reasonable expenses incurred because of the filing of the document, including reasonable attorney fees."

## II. FACTUAL BASIS FOR SANCTIONS

### A. False Factual Allegations in Court Filings

1. Defendant Emily Watson, through her counsel Mandi Martini, filed and signed court documents containing **false factual allegations** that had no evidentiary support, including:

   a. **Emergency Ex Parte Motion (August 8, 2025)** — Contained false or unsubstantiated allegations of danger to the child, resulting in the ex parte suspension of Plaintiff's parenting time from 50% to 0%. These allegations were not supported by evidence and were contradicted by the factual record.

   b. **PPO Petition and related filings** — Based on seven (7) false police reports filed in 2023 by Emily Watson and Lori Watson. Audio recordings exist in which Emily and Lori Watson **admit to fabricating** the incidents underlying these reports, including staging a bogus suicide "welfare check" and fabricating allegations of poisoning, assault, drug use, and threats.

   c. **Show-cause motions** — Filed motions resulting in Plaintiff's incarceration on two separate occasions (February 10, 2025; November 23, 2025 – January 6, 2026) without any new incident justifying the motions.

### B. Unverified Assertions Without Evidentiary Support

2. Defendant's filings repeatedly alleged drug use (methamphetamine), threats, and domestic violence — **none of which were supported by evidence**. No drug test results, police reports with charges, medical records, or independent witness statements were produced to support these allegations.

### C. Ex Parte Submission of Evidence

4. Defendant's counsel submitted a USB drive containing audio recordings directly to the judge's chambers without serving copies on Plaintiff or providing notice — a further violation of procedural rules and an abuse of the litigation process.
