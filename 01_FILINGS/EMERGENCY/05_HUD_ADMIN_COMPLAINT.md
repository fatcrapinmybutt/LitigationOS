## I. OVERVIEW: ADMINISTRATIVE vs. JUDICIAL TRACK

The Fair Housing Act provides two enforcement tracks:

| Feature | Administrative (HUD) | Judicial (Federal Court) |
|---------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Filing** | HUD Form 903 / online | Federal complaint (FRCP) |
| **Filing Fee** | None | $405 (or IFP) |
| **Deadline** | 1 year from last act | 2 years from last act |
| **Investigation** | HUD investigates | Plaintiff bears discovery burden |
| **Hearing** | ALJ administrative hearing | Jury trial available |
| **Damages** | Actual damages + civil penalties | Actual + punitive damages |
| **Civil Penalties** | Up to $21,039 (1st) / $105,194 (pattern) | N/A (punitive instead) |
| **Attorney Fees** | Yes (if prevailing) | Yes (42 USC § 3613(c)(2)) |
| **Election** | Can elect federal court at any time | N/A |

**Recommended Strategy:** File administratively first (free, HUD investigates), preserve right to elect federal court under 42 USC § 3612(a).

## III. COMPLAINT NARRATIVE

### Summary of Discriminatory Housing Practices

From approximately late 2023 through July 2025, Respondents engaged in a systematic pattern of discriminatory and retaliatory housing practices targeting Complainant Andrew J. Pigors, including:

**1. Discriminatory Terms and Conditions (42 USC § 3604(b))**
- Fabricated charges on Complainant's lot rent ledger
- Refused to accept valid government housing assistance (MDHHS, TrueNorth)
- Coerced Complainant into signing a new lease under duress at higher rent (March 26, 2024)
- Billed utilities during unabated sewage conditions near Complainant's lot
- Selectively enforced community rules more harshly against Complainant

**2. Retaliation (42 USC § 3617)**
- Escalated eviction proceedings after Complainant reported sewage violations to EGLE
- Blocked Complainant's sale of his mobile home three (3) times
- Community manager told prospective buyers Complainant "no longer owns his home" and had "abandoned" it
- Filed fraudulent res judicata motion through attorney Jeremy Brown
- Destroyed Complainant's home and personal property during July 17, 2025 set-out

**3. Interference with Housing Rights (42 USC § 3617)**
- Prevented exercise of right to sell manufactured home (MCL 125.2319)
- False statements to third parties regarding ownership and occupancy
- Resulting homelessness weaponized in custody proceedings to suspend parenting time

## VI. ELECTION TO FEDERAL COURT

Under 42 USC § 3612(a), Complainant may elect to have the claim heard in federal court at any time before the ALJ commences a hearing. This preserves:
- Right to jury trial
- Right to punitive damages (not available in administrative track)
- Right to full federal court discovery
