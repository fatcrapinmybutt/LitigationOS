### B. Continuing Violation Doctrine

The FHA's continuing violation doctrine may extend the filing window. Key arguments:

1. **Ongoing displacement** — Complainant remains homeless as a direct result of Respondents' conduct. This is a continuing harm.
2. **August 8, 2025 ex parte order** — The weaponization of homelessness in custody proceedings is a continuing violation flowing from the discriminatory eviction.
3. **Property not returned** — Respondents continue to retain/withhold Complainant's personal property.

If the August 8, 2025 ex parte order is treated as the last discriminatory act:
- FHA deadline extends to **August 8, 2026** (156 days remaining)

## IV. TOLLING ARGUMENTS (IF NEEDED)

If the primary deadline is approached or missed, the following tolling arguments may apply:

1. **Continuing Violation Doctrine** — Each day Complainant remains displaced is a new violation
2. **Equitable Tolling** — Complainant's homelessness and pro se status may justify equitable tolling
3. **Discovery Rule** — Some discriminatory acts (e.g., ledger manipulation) were not discoverable until later
4. **Fraudulent Concealment** — Respondents' use of a dissolved entity concealed the true identity of the discriminating party

---

## V. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATION

**FILE THE HUD COMPLAINT IMMEDIATELY.**

The 1-year FHA deadline provides adequate time, but there is no strategic advantage to delay. Filing sooner:
- Triggers HUD's 100-day investigation clock
- Preserves evidence while fresh
- Demonstrates diligence
- May prompt early conciliation
- Preserves MDCR cross-filing through HUD work-sharing agreement
- Does NOT waive the right to elect federal court later

**Bottom Line:** File now, supplement later. HUD allows supplemental filings after initial complaint.
