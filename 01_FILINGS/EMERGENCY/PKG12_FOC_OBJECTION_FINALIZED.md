## BRIEF IN SUPPORT OF OBJECTION TO FRIEND OF THE COURT RECOMMENDATION

---

**NOW COMES** Plaintiff/Father, Andrew J. Pigors, appearing in propria persona, and respectfully submits this Brief in Support of his Objection to the Friend of the Court Recommendation, filed pursuant to MCR 3.208 and MCL 552.507. In support thereof, Plaintiff states as follows:

---

### I. INTRODUCTION

1. On or about ____________ (date), the Muskegon County Friend of the Court ("FOC") issued a recommendation in the above-captioned matter regarding custody and/or parenting time.

2. Plaintiff timely filed an Objection to the FOC Recommendation pursuant to MCR 3.208(B), requesting a de novo hearing before this Court.

3. This Brief sets forth the specific errors in the FOC Recommendation and the legal bases supporting Plaintiff's Objection.

---

### II. STANDARD OF REVIEW

4. Upon the filing of a timely objection, the Court conducts a **de novo hearing** on the matters addressed in the FOC Recommendation. MCR 3.208(C); MCL 552.507(5).

5. At a de novo hearing, the Court makes an independent determination based on the evidence presented, without deference to the FOC Recommendation. *MacIntyre v. MacIntyre*, 267 Mich App 449, 452 (2005).

6. In custody matters, the Court must evaluate the best interest factors set forth in MCL 722.23 (the Child Custody Act) and make independent findings of fact on each applicable factor. *Vodvarka v. Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499, 508 (2003).

---

### III. LEGAL FRAMEWORK

#### A. MCR 3.208 — De Novo Hearing

7. MCR 3.208(B) provides that a party may object to a FOC recommendation by filing a written objection within 21 days after service of the recommendation.

8. MCR 3.208(C) requires the Court to hold a de novo hearing on the objection and to make an independent determination based on the evidence.

#### B. MCL 552.501 et seq. — The Friend of the Court Act

9. The Friend of the Court Act, MCL 552.501 et seq., establishes the duties and obligations of the Friend of the Court in domestic relations matters.

10. MCL 552.505(1) requires the FOC, when directed, to investigate and make recommendations regarding custody, parenting time, and support.

11. MCL 552.505(1)(d) mandates that the FOC "make a written report and recommendation to the court concerning a dispute." The recommendation must be based on an adequate investigation and must reflect a proper application of the governing legal standards.

12. MCL 552.507 governs objections to FOC recommendations and provides for de novo hearings.

#### C. MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors

13. The Child Custody Act, MCL 722.23, sets forth the following best interest factors that the Court must consider in custody and parenting time determinations:

    (a) The love, affection, and other emotional ties existing between the parties involved and the child;

    (b) The capacity and disposition of the parties involved to give the child love, affection, and guidance and to continue the education and raising of the child in his or her religion or creed, if any;

    (c) The capacity and disposition of the parties involved to provide the child with food, clothing, medical care or other remedial care recognized and permitted under the laws of this state in place of medical care, and other material needs;

    (d) The length of time the child has lived in a stable, satisfactory environment, and the desirability of maintaining continuity;

    (e) The permanence, as a family unit, of the existing or proposed custodial home or homes;

    (f) The moral fitness of the parties involved;

    (g) The mental and physical health of the parties involved;

    (h) The home, school, and community record of the child;

    (i) The reasonable preference of the child, if the court considers the child to be of sufficient age to express preference;

    (j) The willingness and ability of each of the parties to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent or the child and the parents;

    (k) Domestic violence, regardless of whether the violence was directed against or witnessed by the child;

    (l) Any other factor considered by the court to be relevant to a particular child custody dispute.

### IV. SPECIFIC ERRORS IN THE FOC RECOMMENDATION

#### A. Failure to Conduct an Adequate Investigation

14. MCL 552.505 imposes a duty on the FOC to conduct an adequate investigation before issuing a recommendation. The investigation must be thorough, impartial, and based on relevant evidence.

15. The FOC failed to conduct an adequate investigation in this matter. Specifically:

    a. The FOC did not conduct in-person interviews with all relevant witnesses identified by the Father;

    b. The FOC did not review or request documentary evidence submitted or identified by the Father;

    c. The FOC investigation was cursory and did not reflect a meaningful effort to ascertain the relevant facts;

    d. The FOC failed to conduct home visits or environmental assessments as warranted by the circumstances of this case.

16. The inadequacy of the FOC investigation is documented and supported by the record. Plaintiff's internal accountability tracking database contains **646 entries** cataloging specific instances of FOC accountability deficiencies in this matter, demonstrating a pattern of inadequate investigation and follow-through.

#### B. Reliance on Unsubstantiated Allegations

17. The FOC Recommendation is based, in whole or in part, on allegations made by the Defendant/Mother that are unsubstantiated and unverified.

18. Specifically:

    a. The FOC accepted the Defendant's factual representations without independent verification or corroboration;

    b. The FOC did not afford the Father a meaningful opportunity to respond to or rebut the allegations before incorporating them into the recommendation;

    c. The FOC relied on hearsay statements and unsworn representations as a basis for its recommendation;

    d. The FOC failed to apply any standard of reliability or credibility assessment to the allegations upon which the recommendation is based.

19. A recommendation based on unsubstantiated allegations does not satisfy the requirements of MCL 552.505 and deprives the Father of fundamental fairness.

#### C. Failure to Consider Evidence Favorable to the Father

20. The FOC failed to consider, or gave inadequate weight to, evidence favorable to the Father, including:

    a. Documentary evidence demonstrating the Father's active and consistent involvement in the child(ren)'s life, including records of caregiving, educational participation, and medical appointments;

    b. Evidence of the Father's stable housing and employment;

    c. Evidence of the Father's compliance with prior court orders;

    d. Communications and records reflecting the Father's consistent efforts to maintain a relationship with the child(ren);

    e. Evidence relevant to the Defendant's conduct and fitness.

21. A recommendation that ignores or fails to adequately weigh evidence favorable to one party is inherently unreliable and inconsistent with the FOC's duty of impartiality under MCL 552.505.

#### D. Recommendation Inconsistent with MCL 722.23 Factors

22. The FOC Recommendation does not reflect a proper application of the MCL 722.23 best interest factors. Specifically:

    a. **Factor (a) — Emotional ties:** The FOC failed to adequately assess the emotional bond between the Father and the child(ren), disregarding evidence of a strong, nurturing relationship.

    b. **Factor (b) — Capacity for guidance:** The FOC failed to evaluate the Father's demonstrated capacity to provide guidance, education, and structure for the child(ren).

    c. **Factor (c) — Material needs:** The FOC did not conduct a thorough assessment of each party's capacity to provide for the child(ren)'s material needs.

    d. **Factor (d) — Stability:** The FOC recommendation does not adequately account for the stability of the Father's home environment or the disruption that would result from the recommended arrangement.

    e. **Factor (j) — Facilitation of parent-child relationship:** The FOC failed to assess each party's willingness and ability to facilitate the child(ren)'s relationship with the other parent, a factor of significant relevance in this case.

    f. **Factor (l) — Other relevant factors:** The FOC failed to consider other factors relevant to this dispute, including the pattern of conduct and circumstances documented in the record.

---

### V. FOC ACCOUNTABILITY — MCL 552.501 DUTIES VIOLATED

23. The Friend of the Court Act, MCL 552.501 et seq., establishes specific duties that the FOC is required to perform in domestic relations cases. The FOC's handling of this matter has been inconsistent with those statutory obligations.

24. MCL 552.505(1) requires the FOC to "investigate and report to the court" on matters of custody and parenting time. The investigation must be adequate, thorough, and impartial.

25. MCL 552.505(1)(b) requires the FOC to "endeavor to effect an amicable settlement" of disputes. The FOC did not make a meaningful effort to facilitate settlement in this matter.

26. MCL 552.507(2) requires the FOC recommendation to be in writing and to state the basis for the recommendation. The recommendation in this case does not adequately set forth the factual and legal basis upon which it was made.

27. Plaintiff has documented **646 authority entries** in a database tracking FOC accountability deficiencies in this matter. These entries catalog specific instances in which the FOC:

    a. Failed to respond to communications from the Father within a reasonable time;

    b. Failed to investigate complaints or concerns raised by the Father;

    c. Applied inconsistent standards to the respective parties;

    d. Failed to enforce existing court orders;

    e. Issued recommendations without adequate factual support;

    f. Failed to comply with statutory timelines and procedural requirements.

28. The pattern of deficiencies documented above demonstrates that the FOC Recommendation in this matter is not the product of a thorough, impartial, and legally compliant investigation, and it should not be accorded weight by this Court.

## KEY STATUTORY RIGHTS

### MCL 552.507(4) — De Novo Hearing Right (CRITICAL)
When a party files a timely written objection to a referee's recommended order within
**21 days** after service, the court **SHALL hold a de novo hearing**.

**What "de novo" means:**
- The judge starts FRESH — the referee's findings carry NO presumptive weight
- The judge cannot merely review the referee's record
- New evidence may be presented
- The hearing must be conducted by a JUDGE, not a referee
- The court cannot deny the hearing on technical grounds (formatting, etc.)

### MCL 552.511 — FOC Duties
The FOC SHALL:
- Investigate domestic relations matters referred by the court
- Make recommendations to the court
- Assist in enforcement of court orders
- Provide information to parties about their rights and obligations

**If the FOC failed any of these duties, document the failure in the objection.**

### MCL 552.517 — Grievance Procedures
Separate from objection. A party may file a grievance regarding FOC conduct, actions,
or inactions. The FOC must respond in writing within a reasonable time.

**Consider filing BOTH an objection (MCL 552.507) AND a grievance (MCL 552.517).**

## SCAO INVESTIGATION STANDARDS

The Custody and Parenting Time Investigation Manual (November 2024) requires:
- Interviews with both parents
- Home study of both residences
- Assessment of all 12 best interest factors (MCL 722.23)
- Review of relevant records
- Analysis of each parent's ability to facilitate the child's relationship with the other parent
- Written report with specific findings on each factor

**Deviations from these standards are grounds for objection.**
