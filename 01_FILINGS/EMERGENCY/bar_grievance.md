## III. MICHIGAN RULES OF PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT (MRPC) VIOLATIONS

### Violation 1: Ex Parte Communication (MCR 3.207(B))s — MRPC 3.5(b)

**Rule:** A lawyer shall not communicate ex parte with a judge during a proceeding unless authorized to do so by law or court order.

**Facts:** Attorney Martini communicated with Judge McNeill outside of Complainant's presence by:
- Delivering a USB drive containing audio recordings directly to the judge's chambers without providing copies to Complainant or notice of the submission
- Submitting evidence and argument to the court through ex parte channels, including via Friend of the Court (MCL 552.501 et seq.) (Pamela Rusco) and court clerks
- Filing an emergency ex parte motion (August 8, 2025) that resulted in same-day suspension of Complainant's parenting time without notice

Discovery documents (Requests to Admit #1-4) confirm Complainant was never served with the evidence used against him prior to hearings.

### Violation 2: Candor Toward the Tribunal — MRPC 3.3(a)

**Rule:** A lawyer shall not knowingly make a false statement of material fact or law to a tribunal or fail to disclose a material fact when disclosure is necessary to avoid assisting a criminal or fraudulent act.

**Facts:** Attorney Martini presented and/or facilitated the presentation of:
- False allegations of drug use, threats, and domestic violence that had no evidentiary support
- An undisclosed HealthWest mental health evaluation without informing the court that Complainant had not been served with or notified of the evaluation
- Watson's testimony and affidavits containing fabricated claims (staging incidents, bogus welfare check, false police reports)

### Violation 3: Fairness to Opposing Party — MRPC 3.4

**Rule:** A lawyer shall not unlawfully obstruct another party's access to evidence, falsify evidence, or knowingly disobey an obligation under the rules of a tribunal.

**Facts:** Attorney Martini:
- Facilitated the submission of evidence (USB audio, HealthWest evaluation) without service on Complainant
- Failed to provide discovery responses to Complainant's properly served requests (Interrogatories, RFP, RTA served September 17, 2025)
- Assisted in the obstruction of Complainant's access to evidence used against him in court proceedings

### Violation 4: Assisting Misconduct — MRPC 8.4(a), (c), (d)

**Rule:** It is professional misconduct for a lawyer to:
- (a) Violate or attempt to violate the Rules of Professional Conduct, or assist another to do so
- (c) Engage in conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or misrepresentation
- (d) Engage in conduct that is prejudicial to the administration of justice

**Facts:** Attorney Martini aided and assisted in:
- Filing false police reports (by preparing or advising on filings based on fabricated allegations)
- Misuse of PPO proceedings based on fabricated evidence
- Ex parte removal of Complainant's parenting time based on secret evidence
- Obstruction of Complainant's due process rights

### Violation 5: Meritorious Claims — MRPC 3.1

**Rule:** A lawyer shall not bring or defend a proceeding unless there is a basis in law and fact for doing so.

**Facts:** Attorney Martini pursued meritless motions and allegations without factual basis, including:
- Emergency ex parte motion based on unsubstantiated claims
- Show-cause motions resulting in Complainant's incarceration without new incidents
- PPO proceedings based on fabricated police reports
