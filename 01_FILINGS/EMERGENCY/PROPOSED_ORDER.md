## ORDER ON MOTION TO PRESERVE EVIDENCE

---

At a session of said Court held in the Muskegon County Courthouse, City of Muskegon, County of Muskegon, State of Michigan, on the ______ day of ______________, 20____.

**PRESENT: HONORABLE __________________________, Circuit Court Judge**

---

This matter having come before the Court on Plaintiff/Father's Motion to Preserve Evidence, and the Court having reviewed the Motion and supporting materials, and the Court being otherwise fully advised in the premises;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

### 1. GENERAL PRESERVATION OBLIGATION

All parties to this action shall immediately preserve and maintain, and shall refrain from destroying, altering, concealing, or otherwise disposing of, all documents, electronically stored information, and tangible items that are or may be relevant to the claims and defenses in this matter.

### 2. SPECIFIC CATEGORIES

The preservation obligation applies to, but is not limited to, the following categories of evidence:

**a. Electronic Communications.** All text messages (SMS, MMS, iMessage, RCS), application-based messages (including Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp, Signal, Snapchat, Instagram Direct, and similar platforms), email communications (sent, received, drafted, and deleted), and social media content (posts, comments, direct messages, stories, photographs, videos, and account activity) on all platforms.

**b. Phone Records.** All telephone records, call logs, voicemail recordings, voicemail transcripts, and carrier-maintained records.

**c. Court Recordings.** All audio and/or video recordings of court proceedings, hearings, and conferences related to this matter.

**d. Friend of the Court Records.** All records pertaining to this case maintained by or submitted to the Muskegon County Friend of the Court, including investigation notes, reports, memoranda, correspondence, and recommendation materials.

**e. Medical and Psychological Records.** All medical, psychological, psychiatric, counseling, and therapeutic records pertaining to the parties and the minor child(ren).

**f. Financial Records.** All bank account statements, transaction records, employment records, pay stubs, tax returns, credit card statements, loan documents, and records of cash transactions or asset transfers.

**g. Photographs, Videos, and Recordings.** All photographs, videos, and audio recordings depicting or relating to the minor child(ren), the parties, or the parties' residences.

### 3. ELECTRONIC DEVICE PRESERVATION

No party shall destroy, factory reset, sell, trade in, or otherwise dispose of any electronic device (including smartphones, tablets, computers, and external storage devices) that may contain evidence relevant to this matter without prior leave of Court.

### 4. ACCOUNT PRESERVATION

No party shall delete, deactivate, or alter any social media accounts, email accounts, or messaging application accounts that may contain relevant evidence. All parties shall suspend automatic deletion settings on messaging applications, email accounts, and cloud storage services.

### 5. THIRD-PARTY CUSTODIANS

Each party shall take reasonable steps to preserve evidence held by third parties on their behalf or at their direction, including cloud service providers, wireless carriers, and social media platforms.

### 6. DURATION

This Order shall remain in effect until the final resolution of this matter, including all appeals, or until further order of this Court.

### 7. SANCTIONS

Failure to comply with this Order may result in the imposition of sanctions as permitted under MCR 2.313(B), including but not limited to:

a. Adverse inference instructions;
b. Exclusion of evidence;
c. Monetary sanctions, including reasonable attorney fees and costs;
d. Default judgment or dismissal;
e. Contempt of court.

### 8. SERVICE

A copy of this Order shall be served upon all parties and their counsel within seven (7) days of entry.
