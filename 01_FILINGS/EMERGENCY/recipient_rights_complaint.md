## III. RECIPIENT RIGHTS VIOLATION

### A. Summary

HealthWest violated Complainant's rights as a recipient of mental health services by disclosing confidential mental health evaluation records to the 14th Circuit Court of Muskegon County without Complainant's consent or authorization, in violation of the Michigan Mental Health Code (MCL 330.1001 et seq.).

### B. Specific Violation — MCL 330.1748 (Confidentiality of Records)

MCL 330.1748 provides that information in the record of a recipient shall be kept confidential and shall not be open to public inspection. The statute permits disclosure only with the consent of the recipient or under specific statutory exceptions (court order, imminent danger, etc.).

HealthWest disclosed Complainant's mental health evaluation — including diagnoses, clinical observations, and treatment recommendations — to the court **without**:
- Complainant's written consent or authorization
- A written court order compelling disclosure
- Any statutory exception justifying disclosure
- Notice to Complainant that a disclosure was being made

### C. MCL 330.1748a — Limitations on Disclosure

MCL 330.1748a further limits the circumstances under which mental health records may be disclosed. HealthWest failed to comply with these limitations.
