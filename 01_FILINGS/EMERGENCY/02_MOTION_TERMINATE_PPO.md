## II. STATUTORY AUTHORITY FOR TERMINATION

5. **MCL 600.2950(12)** provides that the respondent may file a motion to modify or terminate a PPO. The court shall schedule a hearing on such motion.

6. **MCL 600.2950(13)** provides that a PPO may be terminated if:
   - The petitioner has not shown reasonable cause that the PPO should continue;
   - Changed circumstances warrant modification or termination;
   - The PPO was improperly issued.

7. **MCR 3.310(B)(5)** provides that the court may modify or rescind a PPO upon motion by either party.

8. The PPO fails to comply with statutory requirements under MCL 600.2950:
   - The petition did not allege facts sufficient to demonstrate an "immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage" under MCL 600.2950(10).
   - The Respondent was not served in compliance with MCR 3.310(C)(1).
   - Service was effectuated by Emily's parents (Albert and Lori Watson), creating a conflict of interest and service defect.

9. Under *Hayford v. Hayford*, 279 Mich App 324, procedural defects in PPO issuance require reversal.

    - All drug screenings: **negative**
    - All welfare checks: **cleared**
    - All abuse/violence allegations: **unfounded**
    - Multiple police investigations: **zero findings**

11. Under *Pickering v. Pickering*, 268 Mich App 1, a PPO cannot be sustained on unsubstantiated allegations. Where the factual basis for the PPO has been disproven by subsequent investigation, termination is warranted.

12. The evidence demonstrates that the PPO was obtained and maintained not for legitimate safety purposes, but as a **litigation tactic** to gain custody advantage:

15. See *Troxel v Granville*, 530 US 57; 120 S Ct 2054 (2000); *Santosky v Kramer*, 455 US 745; 102 S Ct 1388 (1982). Under *Fuentes v. Shevin*, 407 U.S. 67 (1972), a deprivation of a protected liberty interest via ex parte process violates due process unless narrowly tailored emergency circumstances exist, which have not been demonstrated.
