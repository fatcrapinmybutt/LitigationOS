## III. SUMMARY OF VIOLATIONS

This complaint details systematic and ongoing violations of the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act (MHCA) and applicable administrative rules by Shady Oaks Park MHP, LLC and its management agent Homes of America, LLC. The violations constitute a pattern of predatory, retaliatory, and unlawful conduct directed at Complainant and potentially other residents.

The violations include, but are not limited to:

1. **Fraudulent and Manipulated Ledger Charges** (MCL 125.2328, MCL 125.2328a)
2. **Illegal Rent Increases Without Proper Notice** (MCL 125.2328c, R 125.1921)
3. **Blocked Mobile Home Sales / Interference with Homeowner Rights** (MCL 125.2335)
4. **Failure to Maintain Common Areas and Infrastructure** (MCL 125.2333)
5. **Sewage / Environmental Violations (EGLE Involvement)** (MCL 125.2333, MCL 324.3101 *et seq.*)

## IV. DETAILED STATEMENT OF VIOLATIONS

### VIOLATION 1: FRAUDULENT AND MANIPULATED LEDGER CHARGES
**Statutes Violated:** MCL 125.2328, MCL 125.2328a

Shady Oaks and Homes of America have maintained fraudulent and inaccurate ledger records for Complainant's account. Specifically:

- **Security Deposit Converted to a "Charge":** Complainant's security deposit was listed as a *charge* rather than a credit or deposit on the account ledger, creating a false appearance of arrears. This fraudulent ledger entry was used as the basis for the first eviction filing in approximately May 2024.

- **Failure to Update Ledger After Court-Ordered Payment:** After Complainant made a payment of approximately $1,300.26 by check (which was cashed by Shady Oaks and/or an affiliated LLC), the ledger was **never updated** to reflect this payment. The uncorrected ledger was used during Complainant's custody proceedings on July 17, 2024, to create a false impression of financial irresponsibility.

- **Stacked Unauthorized Fees:** Over $500 in late fees and other charges have been added to Complainant's account month after month without authorization, proper notice, or lease basis. The community has layered fees continuously, inflating the account balance.

- **Refusal to Provide Accurate Account Statement:** Despite repeated requests, management has refused to provide a corrected, accurate ledger reflecting the court's findings in the summary disposition, including proper credits for payments made.

- **Refusal to Accept or Properly Credit Valid Payments:** Defendants refused to accept or properly credit valid payments including MDHHS and TrueNorth housing assistance payments, misapplied payments, and layered unauthorized fees to prevent Complainant from knowing any true "amount due."

**Evidence:** Exhibit C (Ledgers — Full, Unredacted); Exhibit C-1 (Ledger/Balance Screenshots); cashed check records; email correspondence re: "SHADY OAKS CASSANDRA UNPROFESSIONAL FALSE LEDGER."

### VIOLATION 2: ILLEGAL RENT INCREASES WITHOUT PROPER NOTICE
**Statutes Violated:** MCL 125.2328c, R 125.1921

Complainant's lot rent has been increased multiple times in rapid succession without lawful notice:

- **Original Rent:** $395/month (upon move-in, approximately November 2023)
- **First Increase:** $695/month (effective approximately March 2024, upon new management takeover by Homes of America)
- **Second Increase:** $720/month (subsequent increase)
- **Additional Charges:** Water and sewer bills added on top of rent, of suspicious amounts and without clear basis

Under MCL 125.2328c and applicable MHCA rules, a community owner must provide written notice of rent increases at least 30 days prior to the effective date. The increases here were imposed without proper written notice, without justification, and in amounts constituting de facto constructive eviction—a rent increase from $395 to $720 represents an 82% increase.

**Evidence:** Lease agreements; payment records; ledger records showing rent amounts.

---

### VIOLATION 3: BLOCKED MOBILE HOME SALES / INTERFERENCE WITH HOMEOWNER RIGHTS
**Statute Violated:** MCL 125.2335

Upon information and belief, Defendants have attempted to transfer, sell, or vacate Complainant's property to avoid liability. MCL 125.2335 protects a homeowner's right to sell their manufactured home in place. Defendants have interfered with this right by:

- Creating conditions that prevent sale (fraudulent ledger showing false arrears)
- Attempting to coerce abandonment of the home through constructive eviction
- Facilitating unauthorized removal of property and placing "FREE" signs on Complainant's belongings after the July 17, 2025 set-out, effectively distributing Complainant's personal property to third parties

**Evidence:** Security camera footage; witness statements (Mitchell Shafer affidavit); Exhibit D-1, D-2 (security camera photos).

---

### VIOLATION 4: FAILURE TO MAINTAIN COMMON AREAS AND INFRASTRUCTURE
**Statute Violated:** MCL 125.2333

The community has failed to maintain the premises in reasonable repair and fit condition, including:

- **Active Sewage Leak:** From late 2024 through mid-2025, raw sewage was present near Complainant's lot. The Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy (EGLE) issued violation notices. Despite this, Defendants continued billing utilities without remediation credits and failed to timely respond (approximately 31–34 days to respond to EGLE).

- **No On-Site Management:** The community has had virtually no on-site management presence. The manager, Cassandra, is rarely present, leaving residents without a point of contact for maintenance requests and emergency issues.

- **General Deterioration:** The community infrastructure, including roads, drainage, and common areas, has been allowed to deteriorate.

**Evidence:** Exhibit B-1 (EGLE Violation Letters/Notices); Exhibit B-2 (Sewage Complaint Email); EGLE investigation records.

---

### VIOLATION 5: SEWAGE AND ENVIRONMENTAL VIOLATIONS
**Statutes Violated:** MCL 125.2333, MCL 324.3101 *et seq.* (NREPA Part 31)

As noted above, EGLE has issued violations against the community for sewage conditions near Complainant's lot. The community owner is obligated under both the MHCA and NREPA to maintain sanitary conditions. The failure to remediate sewage conditions while continuing to charge rent and utility fees constitutes a violation of the duty to maintain habitable conditions.

The sewage leak has been active since at least late February 2024/early 2025 and the community has failed to abate the hazard despite EGLE enforcement action.

**Evidence:** EGLE violation letters; photographs; resident complaints.

---

### VIOLATION 6: ILLEGAL EVICTION PROCEDURES AND SELF-HELP EVICTION
**Statutes Violated:** MCL 125.2316, MCL 125.2316(1), MCL 600.2918

Defendants have engaged in a pattern of illegal eviction attempts and ultimately conducted a self-help eviction:

- **First Eviction Attempt (May 2024):** Filed based on fraudulent ledger showing security deposit as a charge, creating false appearance of arrears.

- **Coerced Lease Signing (March 26, 2024):** Complainant was coerced under threat of eviction into signing an off-site lease at different terms and higher rent, contrary to Michigan law and without meaningful consent.

- **Break-In Attempt (July 3, 2025):** Two individuals attempted to break into Complainant's home and were captured on security cameras. These same individuals appeared during the later set-out.

- **Unlawful Set-Out (July 17, 2025):** With sheriff presence, locks were drilled, Complainant's home was cleared, and personal property and appliances were removed or destroyed. Within 24 hours, a "FREE" sign was placed on Complainant's belongings and neighbors took items. Defendant Henry Brandel removed appliances and supplied a drill used to defeat locks. No proper inventory or receipts were provided.

- **Result:** Complainant became homeless, was forced to sleep in his car, and "unstable housing" was subsequently used in custody proceedings to suspend his parenting time with his son.

**Evidence:** Exhibit D-1 (Security Camera Photos); Exhibit D-2 (Side-by-Side Identification); Exhibit A (Affidavit of Mitchell Shafer); court records from Case No. 2025-25061626-LT.

---

### VIOLATION 7: WATER UTILITY SHUTOFF AND UTILITY ABUSE
**Statute Violated:** MCL 125.2328(1)(b)

Defendants have threatened and/or executed water shutoff to Complainant's lot as a coercive measure, and have added suspicious water and sewer charges to the account without clear basis or proper metering. Under the MHCA, a community owner may not terminate utility services as a means of eviction or coercion.

**Evidence:** Ledger records showing water/sewer charges; Complainant testimony; correspondence.

---

### VIOLATION 8: RETALIATORY ACTIONS
**Statute Violated:** MCL 125.2337

Defendants have engaged in a pattern of retaliatory conduct against Complainant for asserting his legal rights, including:

- Filing serial eviction actions after Complainant raised complaints about ledger inaccuracies
- Increasing rent and fees after Complainant challenged charges
- Harassment by management and affiliated persons
- Conducting the July 17, 2025 set-out in a manner designed to humiliate and destroy Complainant's property
- PPO papers served upon Complainant by ambush in a garage at the property during an eviction period in November 2023 by individuals associated with the community

MCL 125.2337 prohibits a community owner from taking retaliatory action against a tenant for exercising rights under the MHCA, filing complaints, or participating in tenant organizations.

**Evidence:** Timeline of eviction filings correlated with complaint activity; rent increase timeline; witness statements.

---

### VIOLATION 9: FAILURE TO PROVIDE REQUIRED DISCLOSURES
**Statutes Violated:** MCL 125.2322, MCL 125.2322(c)

Defendants have failed to provide Complainant with required disclosures, including:

- Accurate community rules and regulations
- Proper notice of fee changes
- Required information about the community's licensing status
- Required information about the owner and management company
- Proper disclosure of the basis for charges on the account

**Evidence:** Correspondence; absence of required disclosures in file.

---

### VIOLATION 10: COERCED LEASE EXECUTION
**Statutes Violated:** MCL 125.2328(2), MCL 125.2328(3)

On or about March 26, 2024, Complainant was coerced under threat of eviction into signing an off-site lease at different terms and higher rent, contrary to Michigan law and without meaningful consent. The lease terms were not properly disclosed and the execution was obtained through duress.

Under the MHCA, lease terms that violate the Act are void and unenforceable.

**Evidence:** Exhibit G (Lease PDF); Exhibit H (Coerced Lease Event Summary); Complainant testimony.

## V. PATTERN OF CONDUCT

The violations described above are not isolated incidents but constitute a systematic pattern of predatory conduct by Shady Oaks Park MHP, LLC and its management company Homes of America, LLC. The database of evidence compiled in this matter documents:

- **1,805 mentions** of Shady Oaks in connection with housing harm
- **4,580 documented harms** across categories including housing harm, financial harm, emotional/psychological harm
- **1,019 mentions** of Homes of America in connection with documented harms

This pattern suggests that the violations may affect other residents of the community and warrant a comprehensive investigation by the Mobile Home Commission.
