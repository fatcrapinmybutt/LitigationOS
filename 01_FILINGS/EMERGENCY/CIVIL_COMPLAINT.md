## III. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

5. At all times relevant to this Complaint, Plaintiff has resided at 1977 Whitehall Rd, Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park, Laketon Township, Muskegon County, Michigan 49445 (hereinafter "the Premises").

6. Plaintiff entered into a lease agreement (written or oral) with Defendant for the rental of the Premises, including the lot, common areas, and park infrastructure upon which Plaintiff's dwelling depends for essential services.

7. In exchange for Plaintiff's payment of rent and compliance with lease terms, Defendant was obligated to maintain the Premises and common areas in a condition reasonably fit for their intended use and in compliance with applicable health and safety codes.

8. Despite Plaintiff's consistent payment of rent and compliance with lease terms, Defendant has failed and refused to maintain the Premises and park infrastructure in a habitable condition.

### A. Specific Habitability Violations

9. The following conditions have existed at the Premises and/or within Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park, and Defendant has had actual and/or constructive notice of each:

   **(a) Structural and Physical Deficiencies**
   - Deterioration of lot-level infrastructure, including but not limited to: failing utility connections, damaged or inadequate water supply lines, and compromised sewer/septic connections serving Plaintiff's lot.
   - Failure to maintain roads, drainage systems, and common areas in safe and passable condition.
   - Structural defects in park-owned infrastructure affecting the habitability of Plaintiff's dwelling.

   **(b) Utility and Essential Services Failures**
   - Interruptions, inadequacies, and/or failures in water service delivery to the Premises.
   - Failures in sewage and waste disposal systems serving the Premises.
   - Electrical infrastructure deficiencies creating unsafe conditions.

   **(c) Health and Safety Hazards**
   - Failure to address and remediate conditions posing health risks to Plaintiff and other residents.
   - Failure to comply with local housing codes, health ordinances, and applicable building standards.
   - Failure to maintain the Premises free from conditions that endanger the health, safety, and welfare of Plaintiff.

   **(d) Failure to Make Repairs**
   - Despite repeated written and verbal requests from Plaintiff to Defendant to repair the conditions described herein, Defendant has failed, refused, and/or neglected to make necessary repairs in a timely or adequate manner.
   - Defendant's pattern of non-responsiveness to repair requests is documented across **15 direct housing violations** identified in Plaintiff's records.
   - Plaintiff's records further reflect **3,397 entries** categorized as HOUSING_HARM, documenting the cumulative pattern and practice of Defendant's habitability failures.

10. Each of the foregoing conditions existed for a substantial period of time, and Defendant had actual or constructive notice of each condition through Plaintiff's direct communications, code enforcement activity, and/or the obvious and visible nature of the conditions.

11. Defendant failed to cure the foregoing conditions within a reasonable time after notice.

### B. Consequential Harm

12. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant's failure to maintain the Premises in a habitable condition, Plaintiff has suffered:

   - Diminished use and enjoyment of the Premises;
   - Health impacts from exposure to unsafe and unhealthy conditions;
   - Out-of-pocket expenses for repairs and mitigation that were Defendant's responsibility;
   - Emotional distress and anxiety arising from living in substandard conditions;
   - Housing instability that was subsequently used against Plaintiff in related custody proceedings, causing additional harm to Plaintiff's parental rights and family integrity.

## IV. CLAIMS FOR RELIEF

### COUNT I — BREACH OF IMPLIED WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY (MCL 554.139)

13. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates by reference paragraphs 1 through 12 above.

14. Under MCL 554.139(1), in every lease or license of residential premises, the lessor or licensor covenants:

   > (a) That the premises and all common areas are fit for the use intended by the parties.
   >
   > (b) That the premises are kept in reasonable repair during the term of the lease or license, and comply with the applicable health and safety laws of the state and of the local unit of government where the premises are located, except when the disrepair or violation of an applicable health or safety law has been caused by the tenants' own deliberate or negligent act or omission.

15. Defendant breached the covenant of habitability established by MCL 554.139 by failing to maintain the Premises and common areas in a condition fit for their intended use and by failing to keep the Premises in reasonable repair.

16. Defendant further breached MCL 554.139 by failing to maintain compliance with applicable health and safety laws of the State of Michigan and local ordinances applicable to Laketon Township and Muskegon County.

17. The conditions described in paragraph 9 above constitute material breaches of the implied warranty of habitability.

18. None of the conditions described herein were caused by Plaintiff's deliberate or negligent act or omission.

19. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant's breach, Plaintiff has suffered damages as described in paragraph 12 above.

### COUNT II — VIOLATION OF MCL 600.5720 (TENANT REMEDIES)

20. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates by reference paragraphs 1 through 19 above.

21. Under MCL 600.5720, when a landlord fails to maintain the premises in reasonable repair and in a condition fit for use as required by MCL 554.139, the tenant is entitled to remedies including but not limited to:

   - Rent abatement or reduction proportional to the diminished value of the premises;
   - Recovery of damages sustained as a result of the landlord's breach;
   - Such other relief as equity and justice require.

22. Plaintiff provided notice to Defendant of the conditions requiring repair, and Defendant failed to make repairs within a reasonable time.

23. Plaintiff is entitled to rent abatement, actual damages, and such other remedies as provided under MCL 600.5720.

### COUNT III — VIOLATION OF TRUTH IN RENTING ACT (MCL 554.601a–.641)

24. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates by reference paragraphs 1 through 23 above.

25. Upon information and belief, Defendant's lease and/or park rules contain provisions that violate the Truth in Renting Act, MCL 554.601a et seq., including but not limited to provisions that:

   - Purport to waive or limit Defendant's obligations under MCL 554.139;
   - Impose obligations on the tenant that are the legal responsibility of the landlord;
   - Contain terms that are unconscionable or contrary to public policy.

26. To the extent such provisions exist, they are void and unenforceable under MCL 554.633, and Plaintiff is entitled to actual damages and reasonable attorney's fees (or equivalent costs for a pro se litigant) under MCL 554.636.

## V. DAMAGES

29. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant's conduct as described herein, Plaintiff has suffered and continues to suffer the following damages:

   **(a) Rent Abatement.** Plaintiff is entitled to abatement of rent for the period during which the Premises were not maintained in a habitable condition, representing the difference between the rent paid and the fair rental value of the Premises in their defective condition.

   **(b) Actual Damages.** Plaintiff has incurred out-of-pocket costs for:
   - Repairs and maintenance that were Defendant's obligation;
   - Temporary alternative housing necessitated by uninhabitable conditions;
   - Replacement of personal property damaged by Defendant's failures;
   - Medical expenses related to health conditions caused or aggravated by the substandard conditions.

   **(c) Statutory Damages.** As provided under MCL 554.636 and other applicable statutes.

   **(d) Consequential Damages.** Including but not limited to the loss of Plaintiff's housing stability, which was subsequently used as a factor against Plaintiff in custody proceedings, causing harm to Plaintiff's parental rights and relationship with his children.

   **(e) Emotional Distress.** Plaintiff has suffered significant emotional distress, anxiety, and diminished quality of life as a direct result of living in substandard and unsafe conditions.

   **(f) Costs and Fees.** Plaintiff is entitled to recovery of filing fees, service costs, and other litigation expenses.

30. Plaintiff's total damages are in an amount to be proven at trial, but are believed to exceed $3,000.00.
