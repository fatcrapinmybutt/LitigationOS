## III. CRIMINAL ALLEGATIONS

### A. Filing False Police Reports — MCL 750.411a

**MCL 750.411a** provides that a person who knowingly makes a false report of the commission of a crime to a peace officer is guilty of a misdemeanor (up to 93 days imprisonment and/or $500 fine), or a felony (up to 4 years) if the false report involves a felony.

Emily Watson and Lori Watson filed **at least seven (7) false police reports in 2023**, containing fabricated allegations against Complainant, including but not limited to:
- Allegations of poisoning
- Allegations of assault
- Allegations of drug use (methamphetamine)
- Allegations of threats
- A staged suicide "welfare check" — a fabricated emergency call to law enforcement

### B. Perjury — MCL 750.423

**MCL 750.423** provides that any person who makes a false statement under oath in any judicial proceeding is guilty of perjury, a felony punishable by up to 15 years imprisonment.

Emily Watson made sworn statements in court filings and proceedings in Case No. 2024-001507-DC containing material misrepresentations of fact, including:
- Sworn affidavits/petitions for Personal Protection Order (MCL 600.2950; MCR 3.705) based on fabricated incidents
- Sworn testimony in hearings regarding alleged drug use, threats, and domestic violence — none of which were supported by evidence
- Emergency Ex Parte Motion (August 8, 2025) containing false allegations used to suspend Complainant's parenting time

### C. Official Misconduct — MCL 750.505

**MCL 750.505** (Common law offenses) — If Emily Watson and/or Lori Watson are county employees, their use of county resources and relationships to file false reports and manipulate court proceedings may constitute official misconduct.
