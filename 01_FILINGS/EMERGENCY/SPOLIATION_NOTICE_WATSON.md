---

## II. LEGAL OBLIGATION

Once litigation is pending or reasonably anticipated, all parties have a duty to preserve evidence that may be relevant to the claims or defenses at issue. *Brenner v. Kolk*, 226 Mich App 149 (1997). This duty extends to electronically stored information and applies to evidence within your possession, custody, or control, or that of your agents, representatives, or service providers.

Failure to preserve relevant evidence may constitute **spoliation**, which may result in the following court-imposed sanctions:

- **Adverse inference instructions** — the Court may instruct the finder of fact to presume that destroyed evidence was unfavorable to the spoliating party;
- **Exclusion of evidence** — the Court may exclude testimony or evidence related to the spoliated materials;
- **Monetary sanctions** — the Court may impose costs and fees incurred as a result of spoliation;
- **Default judgment or dismissal** — in cases of egregious or willful spoliation, the Court may enter dispositive sanctions;
- **Contempt of court** — violation of a preservation order may result in contempt proceedings.

See *Bloemendaal v. Town & Country Sports Ctr., Inc.*, 255 Mich App 207 (2002); MCR 2.313(B).

You are hereby directed to preserve, and to refrain from destroying, altering, concealing, or disposing of, the following categories of documents and information:

### A. Electronic Communications

1. All **text messages** (SMS, MMS, iMessage, RCS) sent or received on any device, for all telephone numbers used by you, for the period from January 1, 2023 to the present.

2. All **messages transmitted through applications**, including but not limited to Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp, Signal, Snapchat, Instagram Direct, Telegram, and any other messaging platform.

3. All **email communications** — sent, received, drafted, and deleted — from all email accounts used by you.

4. All **social media content**, including posts, comments, direct messages, stories, photographs, videos, check-ins, and account activity on all platforms including but not limited to Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, Twitter/X, and any other social media platform.

### B. Phone Records

5. All **telephone records**, including call logs, voicemail recordings, voicemail transcripts, and carrier-maintained records.

### C. Court and Legal Records

6. All **recordings of court proceedings**, hearings, and conferences, whether audio or video, in your possession or obtainable from your counsel.

7. All **correspondence with the Friend of the Court**, including letters, emails, complaint forms, and any documents submitted to or received from the FOC.

### D. Medical and Psychological Records

8. All **medical, psychological, psychiatric, counseling, and therapeutic records** pertaining to you and/or the minor child(ren), including treatment records, prescriptions, evaluations, and provider correspondence.

### E. Financial Records

9. All **bank statements, transaction records, deposit records, pay stubs, employment records, W-2/1099 forms, tax returns, credit card statements, loan documents**, and records of cash transactions, gifts, or asset transfers.

### F. Photographs, Videos, and Recordings

10. All **photographs, videos, and audio recordings** depicting or relating to the minor child(ren), the parties, or the parties' residences, regardless of the device or platform on which they are stored.

### G. Device and Account Preservation

11. You must **not destroy, factory reset, sell, trade in, or otherwise dispose of** any electronic device (including smartphones, tablets, computers, and external storage devices) that may contain relevant evidence.

12. You must **not delete, deactivate, or alter any social media accounts, email accounts, or messaging application accounts** that may contain relevant evidence.

13. You must **suspend any automatic deletion settings** on messaging applications, email accounts, and cloud storage services.

## IV. SCOPE OF PRESERVATION DUTY

This preservation obligation extends to all documents and ESI:

- In your **possession, custody, or control**;
- Maintained by **third parties** on your behalf or at your direction (including cloud service providers, wireless carriers, and social media platforms);
- That you have the **legal right or practical ability** to obtain or preserve.
