## III. SPECIFIC MISCONDUCT ALLEGATIONS

### Allegation 1: Ex Parte Proceedings and Secret Evidence

Judge McNeill entered an emergency ex parte order (MCR 3.207(B)) on August 8, 2025, suspending Complainant's parenting time from 50% to 0% based on the opposing party's motion — without providing prior notice to Complainant, in violation of MCR 3.206(C).

The court received and reviewed evidence ex parte, including:
- A USB drive containing audio recordings, delivered by opposing counsel (Mandi Martini) directly to the judge's chambers without Complainant's knowledge or presence.
- A second HealthWest mental health evaluation report that was never disclosed to Complainant.

Discovery documents (Requests to Admit #1-4, served September 17, 2025) confirm Complainant had no pre-hearing notice and only received evidence after hearings concluded.

**Violated:** Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 3(A)(4) — "A judge shall not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communication (MCR 3.207(B))s.."

### Allegation 2: Bias and Prejudice

Judge McNeill demonstrated a pattern of bias against Complainant:
- Consistently denied Complainant's motions and continuance requests while granting opposing party's motions without scrutiny.
- Relied on unproven allegations (drug use, threats) without requiring evidentiary support.
- Ordered Complainant jailed twice on show-cause orders without new incidents:
  - February 10, 2025 — contempt at PPO hearing
  - November 23, 2025 – January 6, 2026 — alleged PPO violation (MCL 600.2950; MCR 3.705) with no new act
- Permitted opposing party and counsel to submit evidence outside of Complainant's presence.

**Violated:** Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 3(A)(1) — Duty to be "faithful to the law and maintain professional competence in it"; Canon 2 — "A judge should avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety."

### Allegation 3: Failure to Enforce Confidentiality

Judge McNeill failed to enforce the confidentiality of Complainant's mental health evaluation, despite MCL 330.1748 requiring such records remain confidential absent patient consent. The court:
- Instructed Complainant to email the HealthWest evaluation "off the record" (August 2025, Exhibit K).
- Subsequently relied on an undisclosed second evaluation from HealthWest in court proceedings without informing Complainant.
- Failed to protect the integrity of confidential mental health records used in the proceeding.

**Violated:** Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 3(A)(1) — duty to be faithful to the law; Canon 3(B)(2) — "A judge shall be patient, dignified, and courteous to.. parties."

### Allegation 4: Denial of Right to Confront Evidence

Complainant was denied his fundamental right to confront and respond to evidence used against him:
- The ex parte order (MCR 3.207(B)) was based on evidence Complainant never saw.
- The HealthWest evaluation was used in proceedings without Complainant's knowledge.
- USB audio evidence was reviewed by the judge outside any hearing.

**Violated:** Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 3(A)(4) — prohibition on ex parte communication (MCR 3.207(B))s; Canon 1 — "An independent and honorable judiciary is indispensable to justice."
