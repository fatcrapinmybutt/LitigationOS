## III. NATURE OF COMPLAINT

Andrew J. Pigors files this administrative complaint against MDHHS Children's Protective Services for:

1. **Failure to properly investigate** false and malicious CPS reports filed by Emily Watson

### C. MDHHS CPS Response Failures

1. **Failed to identify pattern**: Despite multiple unsubstantiated reports from the same reporter (Watson) in an active custody dispute, CPS failed to flag the pattern as potential weaponization
2. **Failed to investigate reporter**: MDHHS policy requires assessment of reporter motivation in custody contexts; CPS did not adequately investigate Watson's motives

## V. LEGAL BASIS

### Michigan Child Protection Law (MCL 722.621 et seq.)

- **MCL 722.628** — MDHHS duty to investigate reports of child abuse/neglect
- **MCL 722.628d** — CPS must assess reporter credibility and motivation
- **MCL 722.633(5)** — Knowingly making a false CPS report is a misdemeanor (93 days / $100 fine)
- **MCL 722.633(6)** — Making a false CPS report is grounds for civil action by the person falsely reported

### MDHHS Policy (PSM 711-7 and related)

- CPS investigators must assess whether reports are being used as weapons in custody disputes
- Pattern of unsubstantiated reports from the same reporter triggers enhanced scrutiny
- CPS must coordinate with family court in custody-related investigations

### Constitutional Protections

- **14th Amendment Due Process** — Right to parent without interference based on false reports
- **1st Amendment** — Retaliation for exercising legal rights (filing custody motions, challenging PPOs)
