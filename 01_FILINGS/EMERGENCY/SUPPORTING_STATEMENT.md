## I. INTRODUCTION

This Supporting Statement provides a detailed chronological account of the discriminatory and retaliatory housing practices committed by Respondents Shady Oaks MHC, LLC, Homes of America, LLC, Alden Global Capital, LLC, Partridge Securities, LLC, and their agents and employees against Complainant Andrew J. Pigors at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community, 1977 Whitehall Rd, Muskegon, MI 49445.

---

## II. BACKGROUND

### A. The Parties

**Complainant** Andrew J. Pigors is a Michigan resident who was the lawful titleholder of a manufactured home at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community. He is a custodial parent of a minor child ("Lincoln"). At all relevant times, Complainant maintained his home at Lot 17 and paid lot rent as required.

**Respondent Shady Oaks MHC, LLC** is a Michigan limited liability company that owns and operates the Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community at 1977 Whitehall Rd, Muskegon, MI. Note: A related entity, **Shady Oaks MHP LLC**, was dissolved on approximately February 15, 2023, per LARA records, yet continued to appear in eviction proceedings.

**Respondent Homes of America, LLC ("HOA")** manages and operates manufactured housing communities including Shady Oaks through on-site personnel. HOA acted as the management agent during all relevant events.

**Respondent Alden Global Capital, LLC** exercises ownership and control over HOA and the park entities through an enterprise structure. Alden is a New York-based investment firm that controls manufactured housing communities nationwide.

**Respondent Partridge Securities, LLC** is a financial entity affiliated with the park ownership structure. Complainant's rent check for $1,300.26, made payable to "Shady Oaks MHP," was cashed by Partridge Securities — demonstrating the interrelated corporate structure.

## III. CHRONOLOGY OF DISCRIMINATORY ACTS

### A. Lease and Occupancy History (2022–2023)

1. **February 2, 2022** — Mobile Home Title issued to Andrew J. Pigors by Shady Oaks MHP LLC (via Joe Matyk), establishing Complainant as the lawful titleholder of the manufactured home at Lot 17.

2. **November 15, 2023** — Lot Rental Agreement in effect between Complainant and Shady Oaks at original lot rent of approximately $395/month.

3. **December 2023** — Respondents unilaterally raised Complainant's lot rent to $695/month — a 76% increase — without following proper procedures under the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.).

### B. Ledger Manipulation and Payment Interference (Late 2023–2025)

4. **Late 2023 through 2025** — Respondents began layering unauthorized charges onto Complainant's lot rent ledger, including:
   - Unexplained administrative fees
   - A $120/month "utility" charge for water/sewer imposed without proper metering (February–June 2025)
   - Inflated administrative and trash fees totaling $738.24 (as of May 15, 2025)
   - Charges assessed during active EGLE sewage violation when the premises were uninhabitable

5. **2024–2025** — Respondents refused to accept or properly credit valid payments from MDHHS and TrueNorth Community Services — government-funded housing assistance programs designed to prevent homelessness. This refusal of legitimate government housing assistance constitutes source-of-income discrimination under Michigan's Elliott-Larsen Civil Rights Act.

6. **May 27, 2025** — A billing ledger submitted by Shady Oaks shows unlawful fees charged after the April 16, 2025 eviction order, demonstrating Respondents' continued bad-faith billing practices even after obtaining a court order.

### C. Coerced Lease (March 2024)

7. **March 26, 2024** — Complainant was coerced under threat of immediate eviction into signing a new lease at an off-site location (Cricklewood, not at the park office) at higher rent ($695/month). The lease:
   - Did not properly identify the contracting entity (used only "Shady Oaks" without specifying the LLC)
   - Was signed under duress at a location away from the park
   - Violated MCL 125.2302(3) regarding lease requirements for manufactured home communities

### D. Sewage and Habitability Crisis (Late 2024–2025)

8. **Late 2024 through mid-2025** — Raw sewage appeared near Complainant's Lot 17. Complainant reported the condition to EGLE (Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy), which issued violations against the park.

9. Despite the acknowledged sewage condition, Respondents:
   - Continued billing full lot rent and utilities
   - Added a $120/month "utility" charge during the active sewage crisis
   - Failed to remediate the condition in a timely manner
   - Did not reduce rent or provide alternative accommodations

### E. Retaliation After EGLE Complaint (January–July 2025)

10. **January 21, 2025** — Following Complainant's EGLE complaint, Respondents served Complainant with a Notice to Quit for nonpayment. The alleged nonpayment was based on a fabricated balance inflated by the unlawful fees described above.

11. **March–July 2025** — Respondents blocked Complainant's three (3) separate attempts to sell his manufactured home by:
    - Making false statements to prospective buyers
    - Community manager telling buyers Complainant "no longer owns his home"
    - Community manager telling buyers Complainant had "abandoned" his home
    - Community manager telling buyers a "lawyer is taking it"
    - These statements were false: Complainant held valid title throughout

12. **July 3, 2025** — Two unidentified individuals attempted to break into Complainant's home. The attempted break-in was captured on Complainant's security cameras. These same individuals subsequently appeared during the July 17, 2025 set-out, suggesting coordination with Respondents.

### F. The July 17, 2025 Lockout and Property Destruction

13. **July 10, 2025** — "Shady Oaks MHP LLC" — an entity dissolved since February 15, 2023 — appeared in court proceedings related to the eviction.

14. **July 15, 2025** — Attorney Jeremy Brown filed a fraudulent res judicata motion designed to extinguish Complainant's remaining rights.

15. **July 17, 2025** — With sheriff presence, Respondents executed a set-out that exceeded the scope of any lawful writ:
    - Locks on Complainant's home were drilled
    - Complainant's personal property, including appliances and household goods, was removed
    - Property was destroyed or distributed to non-parties, including neighbor Henry Brandel
    - No proper inventory or receipt was prepared
    - Within 24 hours, a "FREE" sign was placed on Complainant's home
    - The writ's execution did not authorize non-officers to destroy or distribute personal property

### G. Aftermath and Continuing Harm (August 2025–Present)

16. **August 8, 2025** — An ex parte order was entered in family court suspending Complainant's parenting time, using "unstable housing" (i.e., the homelessness caused by Respondents' conduct) as justification.

17. **August–October 2025** — Complainant was separated from his minor child for approximately two (2) months as a direct result of the homelessness caused by Respondents' discriminatory conduct.

18. **Continuing** — Complainant remains displaced. His manufactured home and personal property have not been returned or compensated.

## IV. PATTERN AND PRACTICE

The conduct described above is not isolated. Information and belief suggest that Respondents, particularly Homes of America, LLC and Alden Global Capital, LLC, have engaged in similar practices at other manufactured home communities they own and operate. HUD investigation may reveal:

- Similar ledger manipulation at other HOA-managed communities
- Pattern of refusing government housing assistance
- Pattern of blocking manufactured home sales to force abandonment
- Systematic use of fabricated charges to generate eviction grounds
