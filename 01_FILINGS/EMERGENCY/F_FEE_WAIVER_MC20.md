### PART 3: STATEMENT OF FINANCIAL HARDSHIP

I, Andrew J. Pigors, state under oath that:

1. I am appearing pro se in this matter because I am unable to afford an attorney. My financial resources have been exhausted by the proceedings in this case.

2. I have lost three (3) jobs as a direct result of these proceedings, including:
   - Job losses caused by 59+ days of incarceration for civil contempt
   - Job losses caused by required court appearances that conflicted with work schedules
   - Job losses caused by the stigma and disruption of ongoing litigation

3. I have lost two or more residences as a direct result of these proceedings and the cascading financial consequences of incarceration and job loss.

4. My estimated economic damages from these proceedings range from $259,000 to $500,000+.

5. The Court previously imposed a $250 filing fee requirement as a condition for filing parenting time modification motions. This fee, combined with my financial circumstances, creates a barrier to my access to the courts that is particularly harmful given the emergency nature of my motion.

6. I am filing an Emergency Motion to Restore Parenting Time -- a motion that is constitutionally compelled by the 571+ day deprivation of my fundamental parental rights. The inability to pay filing fees should not be a barrier to the exercise of fundamental constitutional rights. See Boddie v. Connecticut, 401 U.S. 371 (1971) (holding that due process prohibits a state from denying access to its courts solely because of inability to pay filing fees in cases involving fundamental rights).

7. Requiring payment of fees in this case would effectively deny me access to the court at a time when emergency relief is needed to prevent irreparable harm to my relationship with my three-year-old daughter.
