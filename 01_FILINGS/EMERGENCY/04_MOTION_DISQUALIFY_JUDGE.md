## I. STATUTORY AND RULE AUTHORITY

1. **MCR 2.003(C)** provides that disqualification of a judge is warranted when:
   - **(C)(1)(a)** — The judge is biased or prejudiced for or against a party or attorney;
   - **(C)(1)(b)** — The judge has personal knowledge of disputed evidentiary facts;
   - **(C)(1)(c)** — The judge was consulted or retained as a lawyer in the matter;
   - **(C)(1)(d)** — The judge has been a material witness concerning the matter;
   - **(C)(1)(e)** — The judge or judge's family has a financial or personal interest in the matter.

2. **MCR 2.003(D)** provides the procedure for disqualification motions, including the requirement that the motion be assigned to the chief judge or another judge if the challenged judge denies the motion.

3. This motion is timely under MCR 2.003(D)(1), filed as soon as the grounds for disqualification became apparent through the cumulative pattern of bias.
