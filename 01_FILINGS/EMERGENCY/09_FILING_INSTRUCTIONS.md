## V. SERVICE REQUIREMENTS

### A. Service Methods

Under MCR 2.107, service must be made by one of the following methods:

1. **Personal service** — Delivery to the party in person
2. **Mail** — First-class mail to the party's last known address
3. **Email** — If the party has consented to electronic service
4. **MiFILE e-service** — If the other party is registered on MiFILE, the system provides automatic e-service

## VI. EMERGENCY HEARING REQUEST

### A. How to Request Emergency Hearing

1. **At filing:** Select "Emergency" or "Ex Parte" filing type in MiFILE
2. **Separate request:** File a "Request for Emergency Hearing" or contact the court clerk
3. **Contact information:**
   - 14th Circuit Court Clerk: (231) 724-6241
   - Address: 990 Terrace Street, Muskegon, MI 49442
   - Hours: 8:00 AM — 5:00 PM, Monday — Friday

### B. Emergency Hearing Basis

Under MCR 2.119(F)(1), a motion may be heard on shorter notice than the normal 7-day requirement if:
- The motion shows that there is good cause for early hearing
- The moving party has made a genuine effort to notify the opposing party

State in your emergency request:
> "Plaintiff requests emergency hearing pursuant to MCR 2.119(F)(1) because every additional day of parenting time suspension causes irreparable harm to the parent-child bond, and the child has been deprived of contact with his father for 571+ consecutive days."

## X. IMPORTANT NOTES

1. **Pro Se Filing:** As a pro se litigant, you are held to the same procedural requirements as an attorney. Follow all rules carefully.

2. **Retain Everything:** Keep copies of all documents, filing confirmations, and service proofs.

3. **Timeliness:** File promptly. Delays can be used against you as evidence of lack of urgency.

4. **Demeanor:** Maintain a respectful, professional tone in all filings and court appearances. Focus on facts and law, not emotions.

5. **DISCLAIMER:** This filing guide is generated for organizational purposes. It does not constitute legal advice. Consider consulting with a licensed Michigan attorney before filing.
