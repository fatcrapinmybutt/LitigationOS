
Executive Summary
I present this complaint package regarding unauthorized disclosures and abuse of process in Muskegon County Family Court Case No. 24-01507-DC (Judge McNeill). Our evidence shows HealthWest of Muskegon improperly released private mental-health evaluations to the court without my consent or notice, in violation of Michigan’s confidentiality laws
. In fact, discovery documents admit that the court used a HealthWest evaluation conducted “off the books” with no written order
. State law (MCL 330.1748) requires such records remain confidential, and Michigan’s FOIA rules exempt mental-health records absent patient consent
.

Simultaneously, the opposing party (Emily/Lori Watson) orchestrated false reports and misused PPOs to strip me of parenting time. Audio recordings show them admitting they fabricated police complaints (even staging a bogus suicide “welfare check”) to get a PPO. Court filings confirm repeated unproven allegations (drug use, threats) were relied on without evidence. Court personnel (Pamela Rusco, clerks) and Watson’s attorney (Mandi Martini) appear to have facilitated ex parte submissions of evidence (e.g. USB audio) to the judge, denying me due process. My motions and continuance requests were consistently ignored or denied, and I was jailed twice on meritless show-cause orders (Feb 2025; Nov 23, 2025–Jan 6, 2026).

Complaint Summary: We will file complaints with multiple agencies and courts: Michigan Department of Civil Rights (state discrimination/confidentiality violation), Michigan Attorney General (civil rights enforcement), Michigan Judicial Tenure Commission (judicial misconduct), and the Court of Appeals/Michigan Supreme Court (appellate relief). We also target federal remedies: a Department of Justice Civil Rights Division complaint (42 U.S.C. §1983 violation of due process), an HHS OCR HIPAA complaint (unauthorized disclosure of protected health info), and related actions (MDHHS Recipient Rights, county prosecutor ethics, state bar grievance, sheriff’s internal affairs). We seek immediate relief (stay or vacate ex parte order, restore parenting time) as well as investigation of misconduct.

Consolidated Complaint Narrative
In one unified narrative (for reference), the core allegations are:

Confidentiality Breach: I was ordered to get a HealthWest evaluation, which I completed on Aug 15, 2025. I sent the report privately to the judge. Later, the court relied on an undisclosed “second” HealthWest report in hearings. I only discovered its existence by FOIA
. Michigan law (MCL 330.1748) prohibits disclosing mental-health records without consent
. This unauthorized disclosure deprived me of the right to confront the evidence against me.

Ex Parte Misuse: Watson obtained an ex parte parenting-time suspension by secretly submitting evidence (recorded phone calls) to the court. Discovery shows that Watson gave a USB to chambers before I saw it, and the judge reviewed it outside my presence
. I was denied service of the ex parte motion until after the fact (Requests to Admit #1 in discovery). This violates the court rule requiring notice to the other party (MCR 3.206(C)). The ex parte order drastically cut my time with my child (50% to 0%) based on these secret proceedings.

False Allegations: Audio recordings (to be provided) contain Emily and Lori Watson admitting they staged incidents (arson poisoning, fake suicide call) to get the PPO. Watson’s police reports list unfounded allegations (claimed meth use, threats) that have no evidence. Requests to Admit #1-4 state Plaintiff had no notice and only got evidence after hearings
. Thus the PPO was obtained through fraudulent means.

Harms: These actions resulted in immediate harm: loss of visitation, job loss due to jail, eviction notices from residence, and severe emotional distress. They also caused ongoing harm by labeling me unfit without basis. These harms, along with the breach of privacy and procedure, violate civil-rights protections.

Requested Relief: Investigate and remedy the above. Specifically:
(a) Determine if HealthWest illegally disclosed protected records (referring to MCL 330.1748/330.1748a and HIPAA).
(b) Determine if court officials conspired in this disclosure and ex parte handling (potential 42 USC §1983 violation).
(c) Address misuse of PPOs and false reporting (potential prosecutorial oversight).
(d) Order restoration of my parenting time pending full review, and any other equitable relief.

Confidentiality Breach: I was ordered to get a HealthWest evaluation, which I completed on Aug 15, 2025. I sent the report privately to the judge. Later, the court relied on an undisclosed “second” HealthWest report in hearings. I only discovered its existence by FOIA
. Michigan law (MCL 330.1748) prohibits disclosing mental-health records without consent
. This unauthorized disclosure deprived me of the right to confront the evidence against me.

Ex Parte Misuse: Watson obtained an ex parte parenting-time suspension by secretly submitting evidence (recorded phone calls) to the court. Discovery shows that Watson gave a USB to chambers before I saw it, and the judge reviewed it outside my presence
. I was denied service of the ex parte motion until after the fact (Requests to Admit #1 in discovery). This violates the court rule requiring notice to the other party (MCR 3.206(C)). The ex parte order drastically cut my time with my child (50% to 0%) based on these secret proceedings.

False Allegations: Audio recordings (to be provided) contain Emily and Lori Watson admitting they staged incidents (arson poisoning, fake suicide call) to get the PPO. Watson’s police reports list unfounded allegations (claimed meth use, threats) that have no evidence. Requests to Admit #1-4 state Plaintiff had no notice and only got evidence after hearings
. Thus the PPO was obtained through fraudulent means.

Harms: These actions resulted in immediate harm: loss of visitation, job loss due to jail, eviction notices from residence, and severe emotional distress. They also caused ongoing harm by labeling me unfit without basis. These harms, along with the breach of privacy and procedure, violate civil-rights protections.

Requested Relief: Investigate and remedy the above. Specifically:
(a) Determine if HealthWest illegally disclosed protected records (referring to MCL 330.1748/330.1748a and HIPAA).
(b) Determine if court officials conspired in this disclosure and ex parte handling (potential 42 USC §1983 violation).
(c) Address misuse of PPOs and false reporting (potential prosecutorial oversight).
(d) Order restoration of my parenting time pending full review, and any other equitable relief.
