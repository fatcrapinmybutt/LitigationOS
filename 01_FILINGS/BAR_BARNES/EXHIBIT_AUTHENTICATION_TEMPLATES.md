# 1. MRE 901(a) — GENERAL AUTHENTICATION REQUIREMENT

## Rule Text
> *"The requirement of authentication or identification as a condition precedent to admissibility is satisfied by evidence sufficient to support a finding that the matter in question is what its proponent claims."* — MRE 901(a)

## Foundation Questions Template

When offering any exhibit, be prepared to establish the following:

```
Q: I am showing you what has been marked as Exhibit [EXHIBIT #].
   Do you recognize this document/photograph/recording?
A: [Yes.]

Q: What is it?
A: [Description of the exhibit.]

Q: How do you know what this is? / How are you familiar with it?
A: [Basis of knowledge — e.g., "I received this text message," 
   "I was present when this was created," "I took this photograph."]

Q: Is this a fair and accurate [copy/representation/depiction] of 
   [what it purports to be]?
A: [Yes, it is.]

Q: Your Honor, I move to admit Exhibit [EXHIBIT #] into evidence.
```

# 6. MRE 902(1) — DOMESTIC PUBLIC DOCUMENTS UNDER SEAL

## Use For
- Court orders bearing the official seal of the 14th Circuit Court
- Certified documents from Michigan state agencies

## Requirements
No extrinsic evidence of authenticity needed if the document:
- Bears the **seal** of the United States, a state, district, territory, commonwealth, or political subdivision thereof; **AND**
- Bears the **signature** of a judge, clerk, or other authorized officer

## Checklist

- [ ] Document bears the official seal of the issuing court or agency
- [ ] Document bears the signature of the judge, clerk, or authorized officer
- [ ] Document is an original or a copy certified by an authorized person
- [ ] If a copy, the certifying person attests it is a "true and correct copy"

## Offer of Evidence Statement

> "Your Honor, I offer Exhibit [EXHIBIT #] as a self-authenticating document under MRE 902(1). It is a [description — e.g., 'court order entered by the Honorable Jenny McNeill'] bearing the official seal of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, Muskegon County, and the signature of the presiding judge. No extrinsic evidence of authenticity is required."

# 8. MRE 902(11) — CERTIFIED DOMESTIC RECORDS OF REGULARLY CONDUCTED ACTIVITY

## Use For
- HealthWest clinical records (Exhibits D-1 through D-4, D-6)
- Services of Hope therapy records (Exhibit D-6)
- HealthWest phone logs documenting Rusco contact (Exhibit E-5)
- Any medical, business, or organizational records

## Requirements
The proponent must obtain a **written declaration** from the custodian of records that:
1. The record was made at or near the time of the occurrence
2. The record was made by (or from information transmitted by) a person with knowledge
3. The record was kept in the course of a regularly conducted activity
4. It was the regular practice of the business to make such records

The declaration must be provided to all parties **at least 14 days before trial** (or with sufficient time for a challenge).

# 11. OBJECTION TEMPLATE — MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE

## For Exhibit H-3 (USB Recording — Illegally Obtained)

---

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE EXHIBIT [H-3]
### UNDER MRE 901(a), MCL 750.539c, AND MRE 403

**NOW COMES** Plaintiff, Andrew James Pigors, in pro per, and respectfully moves this Honorable Court to exclude from evidence the USB recording submitted by Defendant on or about August 5, 2025, and in support states:

**I. THE RECORDING LACKS PROPER AUTHENTICATION UNDER MRE 901(a)**

1. MRE 901(a) requires "evidence sufficient to support a finding that the matter in question is what its proponent claims."

2. The USB recording was submitted to the Court Clerk without:
   a. Testimony from any authenticating witness;
   b. Foundation regarding who made the recording;
   c. Foundation regarding when the recording was made;
   d. Foundation regarding the method of recording;
   e. Foundation regarding whether the recording has been edited or altered;
   f. Foundation regarding the chain of custody.

3. Emily Watson's father, Albert Watson, testified to the *phone call* but **not to the recording itself**. *Gould v. Gould*, 360 Mich 331 (1960).

**II. THE RECORDING IS THE FRUIT OF A FELONY — MCL 750.539c**

4. The recording was made by Lori Watson without Andrew Pigors's knowledge or consent.

5. Michigan is a two-party consent state. MCL 750.539c provides: "Any person who is present or who is not present during a private conversation and who willfully uses any device to eavesdrop upon the conversation without the consent of all parties thereto... is guilty of a felony."

6. Evidence obtained through criminal conduct must be excluded. *People v. Burge*, 722 NW2d 470 (Mich Ct App 2006); *People v. Bednarz*, 360 Mich 552 (1960).

**III. THE RECORDING'S PROBATIVE VALUE IS SUBSTANTIALLY OUTWEIGHED BY UNFAIR PREJUDICE — MRE 403**

7. Even if otherwise admissible, the recording should be excluded under MRE 403 because its probative value is substantially outweighed by the danger of unfair prejudice, given that:
   a. It was obtained through criminal means;
   b. Plaintiff had no notice or opportunity to prepare a response;
   c. The Court and its staff reviewed the recording ex parte before Plaintiff was served.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court exclude the USB recording from evidence and strike any findings or orders based upon it.
