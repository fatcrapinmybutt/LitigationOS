## III. SUMMARY OF COMPLAINT

This grievance alleges multiple violations of the Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct (MRPC) by Attorney Barnes in her representation of Emily Watson in the above-referenced custody case.

Attorney Barnes is associated with 0 documented professional conduct violations.

## IV. SPECIFIC VIOLATIONS

### VIOLATION 1: MRPC 3.1 -- Meritorious Claims and Contentions
Attorney Barnes filed motions and made claims without adequate factual or legal basis, including assertions known to be false or misleading.

### VIOLATION 2: MRPC 3.3 -- Candor Toward the Tribunal
Attorney Barnes made representations to the court that were misleading or failed to disclose material facts. This includes:
- Presenting allegations without evidentiary support
- Failing to correct known misrepresentations in the record
- Obtaining orders through ex parte communications without required notice

### VIOLATION 3: MRPC 3.4 -- Fairness to Opposing Party
Attorney Barnes engaged in unfair litigation tactics against a pro se opposing party:
- Exploiting Plaintiff's pro se status
- Failing to provide required notice of motions and hearings
- Using procedural technicalities to deprive Plaintiff of rights

### VIOLATION 4: MRPC 4.1 -- Truthfulness in Statements to Others
Attorney Barnes made false statements of material fact to third parties in connection with this representation.

### VIOLATION 5: MRPC 8.4 -- Misconduct
Attorney Barnes engaged in conduct prejudicial to the administration of justice:
- Participating in obtaining ex parte orders without justification
- Facilitating PPO weaponization for custody advantage
- Pattern of procedural sharp practice against pro se litigant
