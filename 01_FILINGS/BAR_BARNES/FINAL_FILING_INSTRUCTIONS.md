## General Filing Steps — 14th Circuit

### Step 1: PRINT ALL DOCUMENTS
- Print the **original** for the court file
- Print **2 copies** (one for Judge McNeill's chambers; one for your records)
- Print **1 copy** for service on opposing counsel (Jennifer Barnes)
- **Total per filing: 4 copies** (original + 3)
- Use **8.5" × 11" white paper**, single-sided, 12-point font minimum (MCR 2.113(C)(1))
- **1-inch margins** on all sides
- **Number all pages** sequentially

### Step 2: SIGN ALL DOCUMENTS
- **Verifications:** Sign under penalty of perjury (MCR 2.114) — does NOT require notary
- **Affidavits:** MUST be signed before a notary public
  - Bring valid government ID to the notary
  - Many UPS Stores, banks, and libraries offer notary services ($5-$10 per signature)
  - Sign in the notary's presence — do NOT pre-sign
- **Certificates of Service:** Sign and date after you actually serve the documents

### Step 3: COMPILE EXHIBITS
- Print all exhibits in the order listed in your Exhibit Index
- Insert a **tab separator** (numbered tab dividers) between each exhibit
- Mark each exhibit with its exhibit letter/number in the upper-right corner
- Ensure all exhibits are legible and complete (no cut-off pages)
- Place the **Exhibit Index** as the first page of the exhibit section
- Bates stamps (PIGORS-0001 through PIGORS-0412) should be visible if pre-stamped

### Step 4: COMPLETE PROOF OF SERVICE
- For each filing, complete the Certificate of Service section
- Method of service options:
  - **Personal service** (hand-delivery) — most effective
  - **First-class mail** — add 3 days for mailing time (MCR 2.107(C)(3))
  - **E-service** (email, if opposing counsel has consented) — MCR 2.107(C)(4)
- Record the date, method, and address of service on each Certificate

### Step 5: FILE AT THE CLERK'S WINDOW OR VIA MiFILE
**Option A — In Person (RECOMMENDED for emergency motions):**
1. Arrive before 4:00 PM to allow processing time
2. Go to the Clerk's Office, 990 Terrace St, 1st floor

**Option B — E-Filing via MiFILE:**

2. Create an account (or log in if you already have one)
3. Select "14th Circuit Court — Muskegon County"
4. Upload documents as PDF (scanned with signatures, or digitally signed)
5. Pay filing fee online (credit/debit card) or attach MC 20 fee waiver
6. System generates a confirmation receipt — SAVE THIS

### Step 6: REQUEST CONFORMED COPIES
- Ask the clerk to file-stamp your copies "RECEIVED" or "FILED" with the date
- These conformed copies are your proof of filing
- **For e-filings:** Download and save the filing confirmation from MiFILE
- Keep one conformed copy in your records; use the other for service

### Step 7: SERVE OPPOSING COUNSEL

## Federal Court Filing Requirements

### Required Documents:
1. **Civil Complaint** (42 USC § 1983) — typed, double-spaced
2. **Civil Cover Sheet** (Form JS-44) — REQUIRED; available at court website
3. **Summons** for EACH defendant — court issues upon filing
4. **IFP Application** (if seeking fee waiver) — with affidavit of income/assets
5. **Certificate of Service** — after service is complete

### FRCP (Federal Rules of Civil Procedure) Requirements:
- Complaint must contain: (1) jurisdiction statement (28 USC § 1331/1343), (2) short and plain statement of the claim (FRCP 8(a)), (3) demand for relief
- Paper: 8.5" × 11", numbered lines (local rule), double-spaced
- Margins: at least 1 inch on all sides
- Font: 14-point (W.D. Mich. Local Rule 10.1)
- All pages numbered

### Service of Process (FRCP 4):
- Summons must be served within **90 days** of filing (FRCP 4(m))
- For government defendants (judges, county officials acting under color of law):
  - Serve the individual AND the governmental entity
  - Serve the Michigan Attorney General for state defendants (FRCP 4(j))
  - Serve the county per FRCP 4(j) for county defendants
- Service methods: personal service, waiver of service (FRCP 4(d)), or by U.S. Marshal (available for IFP plaintiffs)

- [ ] State courts at ALL levels have failed to provide relief
- [ ] Clear constitutional violations documented (due process, equal protection)
- [ ] Pattern of state court failure established through prior filings
- [ ] Exhaustion of state remedies demonstrated (or futility shown)

**⚠️ IMPORTANT — Younger Abstention:**
Federal courts may abstain from hearing cases where state proceedings are ongoing (*Younger v. Harris*, 401 U.S. 37 (1971)). Best to file after state proceedings conclude or when you can show state remedies are futile. Document every denial and failure at the state level — this becomes your exhaustion record.

**⚠️ IMPORTANT — Rooker-Feldman Doctrine:**
Federal district courts cannot review state court judgments (*Rooker v. Fidelity Trust Co.*, 263 U.S. 413 (1923); *D.C. Court of Appeals v. Feldman*, 460 U.S. 462 (1983)). Frame your complaint as challenging the **unconstitutional conduct** of state actors, NOT asking the federal court to reverse the state court's custody decision.
