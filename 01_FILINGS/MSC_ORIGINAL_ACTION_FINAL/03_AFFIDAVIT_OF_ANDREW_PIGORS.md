### III. SEPARATION FROM MY SON

9. The following is the accurate timeline of custody and separation in this case:

    a. On or about December 3, 2023, a Personal Protection Order was initiated against me.

    b. From approximately March 26 through May 5, 2024 (40 days), Watson withheld L.D.W. from me — the first period of complete withholding.

    c. On May 5, 2024, I obtained an ex parte temporary custody order establishing **50/50 equal parenting time**. From May 5, 2024 through July 17, 2025 — a period of approximately **438 days** — I maintained successful 50/50 parenting time with L.D.W. under this temporary order. The arrangement worked. L.D.W. thrived. Shared custody was both workable and in the child's best interest.

    d. On July 17, 2025, Watson's objection to the temporary order was granted. The ex parte order was overturned, and Watson was awarded 100% custody — without an evidentiary hearing and without findings on the MCL 722.23 best interest factors.

    e. Since July 29, 2025, Watson has again withheld L.D.W. from me entirely, under a false and exaggerated ex parte suspension of parenting time. As of the date of this Affidavit, this current separation has lasted approximately **229 consecutive days** and is ongoing. This is not a reduction in parenting time — it is a complete and total severance of the parent-child relationship.

    f. During this current withholding period, Watson removed L.D.W. from the country entirely from October 20 through November 15, 2025 — with the trial court's permission — while simultaneously filing a show cause motion against me for an alleged verbal exchange at a parenting time exchange, for which no evidence was presented.

    g. In total, I have been denied contact with my son for **269 days** across two separate withholding periods (40 days + 229 days), despite 438 days of demonstrated successful shared custody between those periods.

10. The denial of parenting time is not the result of a court order finding me unfit. It is the result of Emily A. Watson's unilateral refusal to comply with parenting time provisions, combined with Judge McNeill's refusal to enforce those provisions. The fact that 50/50 parenting time functioned successfully for over fourteen months under the temporary order demonstrates that shared custody was both workable and in the child's best interest — making the trial court's subsequent revocation of this arrangement, without evidentiary hearing, all the more arbitrary.

11. Despite documented violations of parenting time by Watson, Judge McNeill has taken zero enforcement actions under the mandatory provisions of MCL 552.644. Not a single compensatory parenting time session has been ordered. Not a single fine has been imposed. Not a single contempt finding has been made against Watson for interfering with parenting time.

Notably, the 438 days of successful 50/50 custody between the withholding periods demonstrate that L.D.W. had a functioning relationship with both parents, which the trial court's actions destroyed.

    - Anxiety and depression;
    - Attachment disorders;
    - Identity confusion;
    - Difficulty forming healthy relationships;
    - Academic and behavioral problems.

24. Every day that passes without intervention deepens this harm. L.D.W.'s childhood developmental stages are not recoverable. ### X. WHY SUPERINTENDING CONTROL IS NECESSARY

27. I am not asking this Court to decide custody. I am not asking this Court to act as a trial court. I am asking this Court to exercise its constitutional authority under Const 1963, art 6, § 4, to ensure that the lower court functions as a court — with impartiality, due process, and compliance with mandatory statutes.

28. The lower court's handling of my case has been characterized by:
    - A biased judge who cannot be disqualified because she imposed a financial barrier to disqualification;
    - Mandatory statutes (MCL 552.644) that are being ignored;
    - Due process that is being denied;
    - Contempt power that is being abused;
    - A child who is being separated from a fit parent for 229 consecutive days — after 438 days of successful shared custody — without any legal basis.

29. This is precisely the kind of systemic judicial failure that the Constitution's superintending control provision was designed to address.

30. I respectfully request that this Court assume superintending control over Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP, remove Judge McNeill, reassign the case, order emergency parenting time, vacate unconstitutional orders, and establish oversight to ensure lawful proceedings going forward.
