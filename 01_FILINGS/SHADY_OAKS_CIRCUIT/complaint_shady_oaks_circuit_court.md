## IV. CLAIMS FOR RELIEF

*(Violation of MCL 125.2301 et seq. — Mobile Home Commission Act;*
*MCL 554.631 et seq. — Truth in Renting Act;*
*MCL 125.2312a — Prohibited Community Rules;*
*MCL 125.2321 — Lot Rent Increase Requirements)*

47. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs by reference.

49. **MCL 125.2321** requires that lot rent increases comply with notice and procedural requirements, including written notice of at least 60 days before the effective date of any increase. Defendants increased Plaintiff's rent from $325 to $720 per month (**121% in 14 months**) without providing the required statutory notices.

50. The **Michigan Truth in Renting Act, MCL 554.631 et seq.**, prohibits void and unenforceable lease provisions (**MCL 554.633**) and provides tenants a cause of action for violations (**MCL 554.636**). The coerced lease with Cricklewood MHP LLC — signed off-site under threat of eviction — contains provisions that violate Plaintiff's rights under this Act.

51. Defendants committed the following specific acts of rent overcharge and lease coercion:

   a. Coerced Plaintiff into signing an off-site lease with a different entity (Cricklewood MHP LLC) under express threat of eviction;
   b. Raised lot rent 121% in 14 months without proper statutory notice;
   c. Fabricated rental arrears through three irreconcilable ledger versions, creating a $2,241.82 discrepancy;
   d. Omitted $6,085.99 in documented payments (MDHHS, TrueNorth, personal checks) from Plaintiff's ledger;
   e. Charged unauthorized fees: NSF fee from another tenant ($35), pre-judgment attorney fees ($178), inflated late fees ($53 in overcharges), and retroactive administrative charges;

   g. Continued billing rent ($7,633.13) after physically destroying Plaintiff's property and removing him from the lot on July 17, 2025;
   h. Refused $1,962.45 in MDHHS government housing assistance, then sued Plaintiff for nonpayment of the amounts they refused.

52. The foregoing acts constitute violations of the Mobile Home Commission Act, the Truth in Renting Act, and common law fraud, entitling Plaintiff to actual damages, statutory damages, and treble damages where available.

53. Under **MCL 554.636**, Plaintiff may recover **actual damages or $250 per violation, whichever is greater**, plus reasonable attorney's fees and costs.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendants, jointly and severally, for all actual damages sustained, statutory damages under MCL 554.636, treble damages where available under statute, and such further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.

### COUNT III: BREACH OF WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY — SEWAGE CONTAMINATION
*(Violation of MCL 554.139 — Implied Warranty of Habitability;*
*MCL 125.2325 — Maintenance of Common Areas and Utility Systems;*
*MCL 125.2334 — Community Standards;*
*MCL 324.3101 et seq. — NREPA Part 31)*

59. Plaintiff incorporates all preceding paragraphs by reference.

60. **MCL 554.139** establishes an implied warranty of habitability in every residential lease, requiring that: (a) premises and common areas are **fit for use intended**; (b) landlord keeps premises in **reasonable repair** during the lease term; and (c) landlord complies with applicable **health and safety laws**. This warranty cannot be waived.

61. **MCL 125.2325** (Mobile Home Commission Act) specifically requires manufactured housing community operators to maintain common areas and utility systems in a safe, sanitary condition.

62. Defendants breached the implied warranty of habitability and statutory maintenance requirements through the following specific acts and omissions:

   a. **Raw sewage discharged from an interceptor tank** onto the ground surface near Lot 17 and adjacent lots beginning in late 2024, creating a severe public health hazard;
   b. Sewage conditions were reported to park manager Kim Davis as early as **March 2024** — over one year of inaction before formal enforcement;
   c. EGLE field inspection on **March 20, 2025** confirmed raw sewage discharging onto the ground, with **NOT IN COMPLIANCE** ratings;
   d. EGLE issued **Violation Notice VN-017235** on April 15, 2025, citing violations of Part 31 NREPA (MCL 324.3101 et seq.) for: illegal sewage discharge; failure to notify within 24 hours; failure to submit required written reports;
   e. Defendants **completely ignored all EGLE contact** for 17+ days following the inspection;
   f. Management's response was wholly inadequate — plumbing crew on-site less than one hour, performing only "TV and jetting" with no excavation or pipe replacement;
   g. By late June 2025, **sewage was again leaking** during heavy rains, demonstrating the "repair" was ineffective;
   h. Defendants failed to maintain the facility in compliance with **4 of 7 Rule 2204 compliance standards**, including nuisance conditions (odors, vectors, pathogens), runoff/ponding, and erosion;
   i. An **unsecured septic/holding tank lid** was found during EGLE inspection, presenting an additional hazard.

63. The foregoing habitability violations are documented by EGLE's Violation Notice VN-017235, field inspection reports, and photographic evidence, constituting direct proof of breach of the implied warranty of habitability.

64. As a direct and proximate result of Defendants' breaches, Plaintiff suffered: diminished use and enjoyment of the premises; health impacts from exposure to raw sewage and unsanitary conditions; emotional distress; out-of-pocket expenses; and housing instability that was subsequently weaponized against Plaintiff in related custody proceedings (Pigors v. Watson, Case No. 2024-001507-DC). Plaintiff became homeless, sleeping in his car, and Defendants' creation of "unstable housing" was used to suspend his parenting time with L.D.W.

**WHEREFORE**, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendants, jointly and severally, for all actual damages, rent abatement for the period of uninhabitable conditions pursuant to **MCL 600.5720**, compensatory damages for health impacts and emotional distress, and such further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.
