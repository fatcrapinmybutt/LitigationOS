## III. LEGAL STANDARD — MCR 3.310

MCR 3.310 governs the issuance of temporary restraining orders and preliminary injunctions. A TRO may be issued without notice to the adverse party if:

> *"[I]t clearly appears from specific facts shown by affidavit or by the verified complaint that immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage will result to the applicant from the delay required to effect notice."* MCR 3.310(B)(1).

A preliminary injunction requires consideration of four factors:

1. **Irreparable harm** to the movant absent injunctive relief;
2. **Likelihood of success** on the merits;
3. **Balance of equities** favoring the movant; and
4. **Public interest** served by the injunction.

*Thermatool Corp v. Borzym*, 227 Mich App 366, 376 (1998); *Michigan Coalition of State Employee Unions v. Michigan Civil Service Comm*, 465 Mich 212, 219 (2001).

## IV. THE FOUR MCR 3.310 FACTORS ARE SATISFIED

### A. IRREPARABLE HARM — FACTOR 1 (Overwhelmingly Established)

9. **The harm is irreparable because Plaintiff's home will be permanently lost if sold.**

10. Defendants have stated their **intent to sell** Plaintiff's home. If they succeed in transferring the home to a third party, the injury cannot be remedied by monetary damages alone because:

   a. The mobile home is **unique personal property** — a 1970 Marlette of specific configuration, containing Plaintiff's remaining possessions;

   b. A sale to a bona fide purchaser could create competing title claims requiring additional years of litigation;

   c. A transfer would **extinguish Plaintiff's ability to recover the physical home** and force him to accept monetary substitution for an irreplaceable asset;

   d. Plaintiff has **already been rendered homeless** by Defendants' conduct. Loss of the home permanently eliminates any possibility of restoration to the status quo ante;

   e. Defendants have demonstrated a **pattern of immediate, unilateral action** — they drilled locks, destroyed property, and posted a "FREE" sign within 24 hours on July 17-18, 2025. There is no reason to believe they will not act with equal speed to complete a sale.

11. **Money damages cannot make Plaintiff whole.** Even if Plaintiff ultimately prevails at trial and recovers the full damages claimed ($2.4M–$17.2M), no monetary award can restore the physical home itself or undo the cascading harms of its loss — homelessness, custody destruction, and community displacement.

### B. LIKELIHOOD OF SUCCESS ON THE MERITS — FACTOR 2 (Strong)

12. Plaintiff is substantially likely to prevail on the merits because:

   a. **Plaintiff holds valid Michigan title** (VIN 1646732, 1970 Marlette, registered with SOS). Under Michigan law, a mobile home is personal property belonging to the titled owner. MCL 125.2330i; MCL 125.1305.

   b. **Defendants have NO title.** No court has awarded title to any Defendant. No abandonment proceeding under MCL 125.2315 has been conducted or completed.

   c. **Defendants' own statements prove their intent to convert.** The February 18, 2026 messages demonstrate knowledge that Plaintiff claims the home, rejection of that claim, and intent to sell it.

   d. **Three irreconcilable ledger versions** (Exhibits B-1 through B-3) demonstrate deliberate fraud — the $2,241.82 discrepancy between Version A (-$61.64 credit) and Version B ($2,180.18 debt) cannot be explained by legitimate charges.

   e. **The eviction was obtained by a dissolved entity.** Homes of America LLC was dissolved ~2022. All court actions in its name are ultra vires and void under MCL 450.4802.

   f. **EGLE confirmed environmental violations.** Violation Notice VN-017235 (April 15, 2025) documented illegal sewage discharge, supporting the retaliatory eviction and constructive eviction claims.

### C. BALANCE OF EQUITIES — FACTOR 3 (Overwhelmingly Favors Plaintiff)

13. The balance of equities strongly favors Plaintiff:

| Plaintiff's Interests | Defendants' Interests |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| Loss of titled property — permanent | Cannot sell property they don't own — no loss |
| Already homeless for 200+ consecutive days | Park continues to operate with 60+ other lots |
| Separated from minor child | No impact on operations |
| No alternative remedy | Can pursue monetary claims if they believe they are owed rent |
| Constitutional property right at stake | No constitutional right to sell another's property |

14. **Defendants will suffer NO cognizable harm** from being restrained from selling property that does not belong to them. A party cannot claim harm from being prevented from doing what they have no legal right to do.

15. **Plaintiff will suffer catastrophic, permanent harm** without relief. The home is the last remaining asset from his pre-eviction life. Its sale would complete the destruction that began on July 17, 2025.

### D. PUBLIC INTEREST — FACTOR 4 (Strongly Favors Injunction)

16. The public interest is served by:

   a. **Protecting property rights of titled owners.** Michigan's title registration system exists precisely to prevent this type of conversion. If a mobile home park can override a valid title by simply declaring the home "abandoned," the entire title system is undermined.

   b. **Deterring corporate predation on vulnerable populations.** MHAction reports document a nationwide pattern of Alden Global Capital/Homes of America targeting manufactured housing residents. An injunction signals that Michigan courts will protect residents' rights.

   c. **Preventing fraud on the court.** Defendants obtained eviction judgments through a dissolved entity using fabricated ledgers. The public interest requires that courts not be instruments of fraud.

   d. **Protecting minor children.** L.D.W. (age 3) has been separated from his father for 200+ consecutive days partly due to Defendants' conduct. The public interest in preserving parent-child relationships is paramount. *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 U.S. 57 (2000).
