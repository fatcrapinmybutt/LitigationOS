## II. DISQUALIFICATION STANDARD

Michigan Court Rule 2.003 provides for disqualification of a judge when:

> **(C)(1)** The judge is biased or prejudiced for or against a party or attorney.
>
> **(C)(1)(b)** The judge has personal knowledge of disputed evidentiary facts concerning the proceeding.

### Applicable Authority

1. **MCR 2.003(C)(1)** — Bias or prejudice for or against a party
2. **MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b)** — Personal knowledge of disputed facts
3. **Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 1** — Integrity and independence of the judiciary
4. **Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 2** — Avoiding impropriety and the appearance of impropriety
5. **Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 3** — Performing duties impartially and diligently
6. **Crampton v Dep't of State, 395 Mich 347 (1975)** — Due process requires an impartial decision-maker; bias demonstrated through pattern of conduct
7. **Cain v Dep't of Corrections, 451 Mich 470 (1996)** — Judicial bias may be established by a pattern of rulings and conduct demonstrating predetermined outcomes
8. **In re Contempt of Henry, 282 Mich App 656 (2009)** — Appearance of impartiality is as important as actual impartiality
9. **Armstrong v Ypsilanti Charter Twp, 248 Mich App 573 (2001)** — Structural conflicts requiring disqualification

- McNeill cannot impartially decide her own disqualification motion

- No judge within the 14th Circuit can be free of this firm's influence

This structural conflict necessitates **Michigan Supreme Court original jurisdiction** under Const 1963, Art 6, §4 to ensure an impartial reassignment outside the 14th Circuit.
