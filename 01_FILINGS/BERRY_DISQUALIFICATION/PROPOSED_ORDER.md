---

This matter having come before the Court on Plaintiff's Motion for Disqualification of the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill Pursuant to MCR 2.003, accompanied by the Affidavit of Bias of Andrew James Pigors, Brief in Support, and Exhibit Index; and the Court having reviewed the Motion, Affidavit, Brief, Exhibits, and any response filed by Defendant; and the Court being otherwise fully advised in the premises;

**THE COURT FINDS:**

1. Plaintiff's Motion for Disqualification was timely filed within 14 days of discovery of the grounds for disqualification, as required by MCR 2.003(D)(1).

2. The Motion is accompanied by an Affidavit setting forth facts and reasons for the claim that Judge McNeill should be disqualified, as required by MCR 2.003(D).

3. Judge McNeill is married to attorney Cavan John Berry, who practices family law in Muskegon County. Cavan John Berry is, on information and belief, a family member of Ronald Berry, who is in a domestic relationship with the Defendant, Emily A. Watson.

4. These familial connections create circumstances in which Judge McNeill's impartiality might reasonably be questioned, within the meaning of MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b) and Canon 3(C)(1) of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct.

5. Judge McNeill did not disclose the Berry familial connection at any point during 290+ days of proceedings, as required by her duty of sua sponte disqualification under MCR 2.003(B).

6. The failure to disclose, combined with the totality of circumstances, undermines public confidence in the integrity and impartiality of the proceedings, contrary to Canons 1 and 2(A) of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct.

7. Disqualification is warranted under MCR 2.003(C)(1)(a), (b), and Canon 3(C)(1).

---

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

1. **DISQUALIFICATION GRANTED.** Plaintiff's Motion for Disqualification is **GRANTED**. The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill is hereby disqualified from all further proceedings in Case No. 2024-001507-DC and any consolidated matters, including Case No. 2023-5907-PP.

2. **REASSIGNMENT.** This case is hereby reassigned to the Honorable ________________________________ [or: referred to the State Court Administrative Office for assignment of a visiting judge, pursuant to MCR 2.003(D)(4), if no conflict-free judge is available within the 14th Judicial Circuit].

3. **REVIEW OF PRIOR ORDERS.** The successor judge shall have discretion to review all prior orders entered by Judge McNeill in this matter for potential modification or vacatur, upon motion by any party, to the extent such orders may have been affected by the conflict of interest identified herein.

4. **PRESERVATION OF RECORDS.** All court records, communications, case assignment records, and administrative files related to Case No. 2024-001507-DC and consolidated Case No. 2023-5907-PP shall be preserved in their entirety.

5. **STATUS CONFERENCE.** A status conference before the successor judge shall be scheduled within **21 days** of entry of this Order, to address the status of all pending motions, parenting time, custody, and support matters.

6. **STAY OF PROCEEDINGS.** All deadlines and pending motions are **STAYED** for a period of **21 days** from the date of this Order, or until the initial status conference before the successor judge, whichever occurs first, to allow the successor judge to review the file.
