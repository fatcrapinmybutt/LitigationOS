### VII. SPECIFIC INCIDENTS DEMONSTRATING BIAS IN LIGHT OF THE CONFLICT

20. While Plaintiff acknowledges that adverse rulings alone do not establish judicial bias, the following incidents — viewed through the lens of the now-discovered familial conflict of interest — demonstrate a pattern consistent with bias rather than neutral adjudication:

#### A. Ex Parte Emergency Order Suspending All Parenting Time (August 2025)

21. On or about August 8, 2025, Judge McNeill signed an ex parte "emergency" order suspending **all** of my parenting time with L.D.W. without advance notice to me, without an opportunity to respond before the order issued, and without specific factual findings of immediate and irreparable harm as required by MCR 3.207(B).

22. This order remained in effect for approximately **four months** (August–November 2025), during which I had zero contact with my child. I missed L.D.W.'s birthday, Labor Day 2025, and all holiday parenting time.

#### B. Incarceration Without Proper Process (59 Days)

23. Judge McNeill found me in contempt and ordered me incarcerated for a total of **59 days** without the procedural protections required by *In re Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81 (1987), and without the right to counsel required by *Mead v Batchlor*, 435 Mich 480 (1990).

24. As a direct result of my incarceration, I lost three (3) jobs, compounding my financial hardship and undermining my ability to meet the child support obligation that the Court set without an evidentiary hearing on my actual income.

#### C. Differential Treatment of Motions

25. Defendant's ex parte motions have been **consistently granted**, often without notice to me and without requiring the legal prerequisites (sworn factual basis, findings of immediate harm) applicable to emergency ex parte relief.

26. My motions — including motions for enforcement of parenting time, make-up parenting time, and modification or rescission of ex parte orders — have been **denied, delayed, or left without ruling**.

#### D. Child Support Set Without Evidentiary Hearing

27. The Court set child support at **$1,056.60 per month** without conducting an evidentiary hearing on my actual income, expenses, or ability to pay. This amount was then used as the basis for contempt proceedings resulting in incarceration — creating a punitive cycle.

#### E. Off-Record Evidence Handling

28. A USB recording and mental-health evaluation were handled off-record and ex parte, depriving me of the opportunity to challenge the evidence, its authenticity, or its admissibility. The Court informed an assessor of "ALL the information that was presented to the Court," but I was not provided with the same information packet or an opportunity to supplement it.
