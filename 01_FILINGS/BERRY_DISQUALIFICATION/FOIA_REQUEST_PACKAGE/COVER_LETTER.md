**The defendant**, Emily A. Watson (2160 Garland Drive, Norton Shores, MI 49441), is in a domestic relationship with **Ronald Berry**, a resident of the Muskegon County area.

### III. THE DISQUALIFICATION ISSUE

I have reason to believe that **Ronald Berry and Cavan John Berry are related** — potentially as siblings, father and son, or in another close familial relationship. If this relationship exists, Judge McNeill would have a familial connection, through her husband, to a person who is in a domestic relationship with the opposing party in my case. This raises serious questions about judicial impartiality.

Under **Michigan Court Rule 2.003(C)(1)**, a judge is disqualified when "the judge's impartiality might reasonably be questioned," including situations where the judge or the judge's spouse has a familial relationship with a party or a person having a significant interest in the proceeding.

The specific concern is:

```
Judge McNeill → married to → Cavan John Berry → [familial relationship?] → Ronald Berry → domestic partner of → Emily A. Watson (Defendant)
```

If Cavan Berry and Ronald Berry are related, then the presiding judge's spouse is a family member of the defendant's domestic partner — a relationship that a reasonable, objective person would find creates at minimum an **appearance of partiality** warranting disqualification.

---

### IV. WHY FOIA RECORDS ARE NECESSARY

The familial relationship between Cavan John Berry and Ronald Berry is not a matter of public judicial disclosure. Judge McNeill has not recused herself or disclosed any potential conflict. Without formal records establishing the Berry family structure, I cannot:

1. **File a properly supported Motion for Disqualification** under MCR 2.003, which requires specific factual allegations;
2. **Demonstrate the familial chain** connecting the presiding judge to the opposing party through family relationships;
3. **Satisfy my burden of proof** that disqualification is warranted based on objective facts rather than speculation.

Public records — birth certificates, marriage records, property deeds, voter registrations, and municipal records — are the proper and least intrusive means of establishing these family connections.

---

### V. PUBLIC INTEREST JUSTIFICATION

This inquiry serves a compelling public interest:

1. **Judicial Integrity** — The public's confidence in the judiciary depends on judges being, and appearing to be, free from conflicts of interest. *In re Murchison*, 349 U.S. 133, 136 (1955) ("justice must satisfy the appearance of justice").

2. **Due Process** — A litigant's right to a fair and impartial tribunal is a fundamental component of due process under both the **United States Constitution (14th Amendment)** and the **Michigan Constitution (Art. 1, § 17)**.

3. **Transparency** — Undisclosed judicial conflicts of interest undermine public trust. Identifying and resolving such conflicts through proper legal channels strengthens the judicial system.

4. **Self-Represented Litigant Access** — As a pro se plaintiff, I lack the investigative resources available to represented parties. FOIA provides a critical tool for accessing public records necessary to protect my rights.
