Evidence obtained through a violation of MCL 750.539 is **inadmissible** under **MCL 750.539g** (fruits of the poisonous tree).

   *(Source: Exhibit R — Transcript excerpt; Exhibit AG — USB Recording Chain of Custody; Exhibit AH — MCL 750.539 Analysis.)*

The same-day timeline — evidence submitted, ex parte staff review, emergency order issued — demonstrates a **pre-determined outcome**: the evidence was reviewed and the order issued before Plaintiff had any opportunity to respond, challenge authentication, or raise the felony-wiretapping objection. *See* **Mapp v. Ohio**, 367 U.S. 643 (1961) (exclusionary rule); **Caperton v. A.T. Massey Coal Co.**, 556 U.S. 868 (2009) (due process requires recusal when probability of bias is too high).

## IV. LEGAL STANDARD

20. Under MCR 2.003(C)(1), disqualification is warranted when a judge is "personally biased or prejudiced for or against a party or attorney." The test is **objective**: whether a reasonable person, fully informed of the circumstances, would perceive bias. **Crampton v. Dep't of State**, 395 Mich 347, 351 (1975).

21. Disqualification "should resolve any doubts in favor of disqualification." **In re Contempt of Henry**, 282 Mich App 656, 679 (2009). The purpose of the rule is to preserve public confidence in the judiciary and protect the litigant's right to a fair tribunal. *Id.*

22. "Repeated rulings against a litigant, even if erroneous, are generally not alone sufficient to establish disqualification." **People v. Stevens**, 498 Mich 162, 168 (2015). However, when adverse rulings are accompanied by **procedural irregularities** — such as ex parte contacts, deprivation of hearing rights, and conditions on court access — the cumulative picture may warrant disqualification. *Id.* at 169.

23. Under MCR 2.003(D), when the subject judge does not grant disqualification, "the motion shall be heard by the chief judge." The Chief Judge's review is de novo on the papers. MCR 2.003(D)(3)(a). However, when the Chief Judge is also disqualified, **MCR 2.003(D)(4)** provides that the matter shall be referred to the **SCAO for assignment of a visiting judge**.
