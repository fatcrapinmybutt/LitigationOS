## III. LEGAL STANDARD FOR CIVIL CONTEMPT

24. **MCL 600.1701(a)** grants courts the power to punish contempt, including disobedience of any lawful court order.

25. **MCR 3.606** governs contempt in domestic relations cases. Under MCR 3.606(A), a party may move for an order to show cause why the other party should not be held in contempt for failure to comply with a court order.

26. To establish civil contempt, Plaintiff must demonstrate:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. A valid court order existed;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. The respondent had knowledge of the order;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. The respondent violated the order; and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. The violation was willful.

*In re Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81, 100-101 (1987).

27. The purpose of civil contempt is **coercive, not punitive** — to compel compliance with the court's orders. *Id.* at 93. The contemnor "carries the keys of the prison in his own pocket" — compliance with the order results in the lifting of sanctions.

28. Once the moving party establishes a prima facie case of noncompliance, **the burden shifts to the respondent** to demonstrate an inability to comply or other defense. *Dougherty*, 429 Mich at 101.

### Parenting Time Enforcement — MCL 722.27a(3)

29. MCL 722.27a(3) provides that if the court determines that a parent has denied parenting time without good cause, the court **"shall do 1 or more of the following"**:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) Modify the parenting time order to give additional parenting time to make up for the parenting time denied;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) Order the parent who violated the order to pay the costs and attorney fees of the other parent;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) Impose a fine;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(d) Order community service;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(e) Order the parent to attend a parenting program.

30. The statute's use of "shall" is mandatory — the court is **required** to impose at least one remedy when a violation is established.
