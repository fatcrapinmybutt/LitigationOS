### II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

#### A. Plaintiff's Ownership of the Mobile Home

7. Plaintiff is the titled owner of a **1970 Marlette mobile home, VIN 1646732**, located at Lot 17, 1977 Whitehall Road, Laketon Township, Muskegon County, Michigan 49445.

8. The mobile home is titled in Plaintiff's name through the **Michigan Secretary of State**, as required by the Mobile Home Commission Act.

9. Under Michigan law, a mobile home that retains its title with the Secretary of State is classified as **personal property** — not real property. MCL 125.2330i provides: *"A manufactured home for which a certificate of title has been issued . . . is personal property."*

10. Plaintiff's ownership of the mobile home is wholly independent of any tenancy dispute regarding the underlying lot. The land is owned by Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC; the home is owned by Andrew James Pigors. These are separate legal interests.

#### B. Defendants' Pattern of Unlawful Property Seizure

11. On or about **July 17, 2025**, without any court order, writ of restitution, or lawful process, Defendants' agents — including **Nicole Browley**, who claimed to represent the dissolved entity "Homes of America" — drilled the locks on Plaintiff's mobile home, forcibly removed Plaintiff's personal belongings, and placed a sign marked "FREE" on the residence, inviting the public to take Plaintiff's property.

12. This conduct constitutes **forcible entry and detainer** under MCL 600.2918, which provides for **treble damages** when a person forcibly enters or detains real or personal property.

13. This conduct further constitutes **conversion** of personal property, **trespass**, and **violation of due process** under both the Michigan and United States Constitutions.

14. On **May 20, 2025**, Defendants shut off water service to Plaintiff's lot while Plaintiff's minor child, L.D.W. (approximately three years old), was present in the home. This constitutes a constructive eviction and violation of MCL 554.139 (implied warranty of habitability).

15. Raw sewage has overflowed from Defendants' interceptor tank since late 2024. The Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy (**EGLE**) inspected the facility on **March 20, 2025**, rated it **"NOT IN COMPLIANCE,"** and issued **Violation Notice VN-017235** on **April 15, 2025**. Defendants continued to bill Plaintiff for water and sewer service during this period.

#### C. Entity Fraud and Standing Deficiencies

16. Defendants have operated under no fewer than **four separate entity names** in connection with Plaintiff's tenancy:

| Entity | Status | Role |
|--------|--------|------|
| **Homes of America, LLC** | DISSOLVED (Michigan) | Original lease counterparty (Nov. 2023); filed eviction proceedings after dissolution |

Under **MCL 450.4802**, a dissolved LLC may only conduct activities "appropriate to wind up and liquidate its business and affairs." A dissolved LLC **cannot initiate new legal proceedings**, including eviction actions, beyond winding up activities.

18. Despite its dissolved status, Homes of America, LLC continued to operate the park, collect rent, issue lease modifications, and initiate eviction proceedings — all in excess of its statutory authority.

19. **Nicole Browley** appeared at the **July 14, 2025** eviction proceeding claiming to represent "Homes of America" — a dissolved entity with no legal authority to prosecute the action.

#### D. Coerced Lease and Unlawful Rent Increases

20. Plaintiff's original lease (November 2023) established lot rent at **$325 per month** with "Homes of America" as the named landlord.

21. On **March 26, 2024**, park manager **Kim Davis** coerced Plaintiff into signing a new lease with **Cricklewood MHP LLC** — a different entity — at an increased rate of **$395 per month**, with a secondary increase to **$695 per month**.

22. By **February 2025**, the rent had been unilaterally increased to **$720 per month** — a **121% increase** in approximately 14 months — without the **60 days' advance written notice** required by **MCL 554.632** (Truth in Renting Act).

23. Defendants maintain **three irreconcilable ledger versions** for Plaintiff's account. One version, printed **August 22, 2024** at 11:01 AM, reflects a **credit balance of negative $61.64** — meaning Plaintiff had *overpaid*. A second version, printed **January 8, 2025** at 1:50 PM, shows a purported balance due of **$2,180.18**. These contradictory records demonstrate that Defendants' claimed arrearages are unreliable and potentially fraudulent.

### III. LEGAL STANDARD FOR TEMPORARY RESTRAINING ORDER

24. Under **MCR 3.310(B)**, a court may issue a temporary restraining order without notice to the adverse party if:

> (1) It clearly appears from specific facts shown by affidavit or verified complaint that **immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage** will result from the delay required to effect notice, or that notice itself will precipitate adverse action before a restraining order can be issued.

25. The four factors governing issuance of a TRO and preliminary injunction in Michigan, as established in **Thermatool Corp v Borzym, 227 Mich App 366 (1998)** and its progeny, are:

> **(1)** Irreparable harm to the movant absent injunctive relief;
> **(2)** Likelihood of success on the merits;
> **(3)** The balance of hardships favors the movant; and
> **(4)** The public interest would not be adversely affected.

---

### IV. APPLICATION OF THE FOUR TRO FACTORS

#### A. Factor One: Irreparable Harm

26. **Plaintiff has already suffered irreparable harm.** On July 17, 2025, Defendants forcibly entered Plaintiff's home, removed his belongings, and posted a "FREE" sign inviting the public to take his property. This was done without any court order.

27. Plaintiff's mobile home is his **only residence** and the primary residence of his minor child, L.D.W. Loss of this home would render Plaintiff and his child homeless. Courts have consistently recognized that the **loss of one's home constitutes irreparable harm** that cannot be adequately compensated by monetary damages alone. *See* **Thermatool Corp**, 227 Mich App at 376.

28. Plaintiff's mobile home — a **1970 Marlette, VIN 1646732** — is a unique, titled asset. It cannot be replaced with a fungible substitute. Its destruction or removal would permanently deprive Plaintiff of titled personal property in violation of constitutional due process.

29. Defendants' demonstrated willingness to act without legal authority — drilling locks, removing belongings, shutting off water with a child present, posting "FREE" signs — establishes that the threat is **not speculative but imminent and ongoing**.

30. Money damages, while available under MCL 600.2918, cannot undo the homelessness, displacement of a minor child, or destruction of irreplaceable personal property that will result if Defendants are not restrained.

#### B. Factor Two: Likelihood of Success on the Merits

31. **Plaintiff is likely to succeed on the merits** for multiple independent reasons:

32. **Michigan law is unambiguous that a mobile home titled with the Secretary of State is the personal property of the titleholder.** MCL 125.2330i. Defendants have no ownership interest in Plaintiff's mobile home. The lot lease grants occupancy of the land only — it does not convey any interest in the home itself.

33. **Defendants' forcible entry and seizure violates MCL 600.2918**, which imposes **treble damages** for forcible entry and detainer. Defendants drilled locks, removed belongings, and posted a "FREE" sign without any writ of restitution or court order. This is textbook forcible entry.

34. **Defendants failed to follow mandatory eviction procedures.** MCL 600.5714 requires summary proceedings to be initiated by proper complaint, with proper service, before a court with jurisdiction. MCL 125.2312f establishes specific procedures for eviction from manufactured housing communities, including written notice with specific statutory grounds. Defendants bypassed these requirements.

35. **The entity that initiated eviction proceedings (Homes of America, LLC) is dissolved.** Under MCL 450.4802, a dissolved LLC may only take actions to wind up its affairs. Initiating new eviction proceedings exceeds this statutory authority. Any eviction judgment obtained by a dissolved entity is **void or voidable**.

36. **Defendants have committed multiple violations of the Michigan Consumer Protection Act (MCL 445.903)**, including operating under multiple undisclosed entity names, presenting irreconcilable ledger versions, coercing lease modifications, and retaliating against habitability complaints.

37. **The implied warranty of habitability under MCL 554.139 cannot be waived.** *Trentadue v Gorton*, 479 Mich 378 (2007). Defendants' sewage violations (EGLE Violation Notice VN-017235) and water shutoff constitute material breaches that undermine any claim of nonpayment.

#### C. Factor Three: Balance of Equities

38. **The balance of equities overwhelmingly favors Plaintiff.** Plaintiff stands to lose his home — his only residence and the home of his minor child. Defendants stand to lose nothing by being restrained from seizing property they do not own.

39. Plaintiff's mobile home is titled in his name. Defendants' ownership interest extends only to the land. An order preventing Defendants from seizing Plaintiff's mobile home costs Defendants nothing — they never had a right to take it.

40. Defendants are a **New Jersey LLC** that purchased the park for **$1.4 million**. They are a substantial commercial entity. Plaintiff is a pro se litigant with a minor child. The equities strongly favor protecting the individual from the corporation.

41. If a TRO is denied and Defendants seize or destroy the mobile home, **the harm is irreversible.** If a TRO is granted and later determined to have been improvidently issued, Defendants suffer **no cognizable harm** because they have no ownership interest in the mobile home.

#### D. Factor Four: Public Interest

42. **The public interest strongly favors injunctive relief.** Michigan's manufactured housing regulatory scheme — including the Mobile Home Commission Act, the Truth in Renting Act, and the summary proceedings statutes — exists specifically to **protect mobile home residents** from the precise conduct Defendants have engaged in.

43. The public has a strong interest in ensuring that:

   - Property owners are not dispossessed of titled assets without due process;
   - Landlords cannot bypass statutory eviction procedures through self-help;
   - Dissolved entities cannot continue to initiate legal proceedings;
   - Minor children are not rendered homeless by extrajudicial landlord action;
   - The manufactured housing regulatory framework is respected and enforced.

44. Allowing Defendants to seize Plaintiff's titled personal property outside the judicial process would send a dangerous signal to every manufactured housing community operator in Michigan that self-help remedies are permissible — directly undermining the Legislature's comprehensive regulatory scheme.
