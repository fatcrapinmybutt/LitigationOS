### II. THE 50/50 SHARED CUSTODY PERIOD — 438 DAYS OF SUCCESS

4. On or about May 5, 2024, this Court entered a temporary order establishing a 50/50 shared physical custody arrangement for L.D.W. between myself and Defendant Emily A. Watson.

5. That 50/50 arrangement remained in effect for approximately **438 consecutive days**, through July 17, 2025. During those 438 days, I cared for L.D.W. during every one of my scheduled parenting time periods.

6. During my parenting time, I performed all daily parenting duties. I fed L.D.W. her meals, bathed her, dressed her, changed her diapers and later assisted with potty training. I read to her at bedtime. I attended to her when she woke at night. I took her to medical appointments. I provided age-appropriate activities and developmental stimulation consistent with her growth from approximately 17 months to 2.5 years of age.

7. During the entire 438-day period, no court, law enforcement agency, or child protective services agency entered any finding of abuse, neglect, or harm to L.D.W. attributable to me. The arrangement worked. L.D.W. was thriving.

8. I facilitated all of Defendant's scheduled parenting time during this period. I did not withhold L.D.W. from Defendant. I did not interfere with Defendant's parenting time. I communicated with Defendant through the AppClose co-parenting application as required.

### III. THE EX PARTE ORDERS — DESTRUCTION OF SHARED CUSTODY

9. On July 17, 2025, without any prior notice to me and without any evidentiary hearing, this Court entered an ex parte order that overturned the 438-day shared custody arrangement and awarded Defendant sole physical custody of L.D.W.

10. I was not served with the underlying motion or supporting exhibits that Defendant filed to obtain this ex parte order. I received only the order itself. This violated MCR 2.107 regarding service of pleadings.

11. On August 8, 2025 — just three weeks later — this Court entered a second ex parte order, on the same day it was requested, suspending **all** of my parenting time with L.D.W. From that day forward, I have had zero contact with my daughter.

12. I was not provided notice or opportunity to be heard before either of these ex parte orders were entered. No evidentiary hearing was held. No analysis of the twelve best interest factors under MCL 722.23 was conducted. The 438-day arrangement was destroyed in a single day without any procedural safeguards.

### V. FALSE ALLEGATIONS — SEVEN CATEGORIES, ZERO FINDINGS

19. Since the commencement of these proceedings, Defendant and members of the Watson family have lodged seven separate categories of allegations against me. I address each:

20. **Allegation 1: Methamphetamine use.** This allegation originated from statements made by Emily Watson herself to the North Shores Police Department. The officer's report reflects that Emily was the source of the allegation — it was not an independent finding. I have never used methamphetamine. No drug test has ever returned a positive result for methamphetamine or any illegal substance. *(See Exhibit B-1)*

22. **Allegation 3: Child abuse / danger to L.D.W.** One CPS referral was made. It was investigated and resulted in **zero findings** of abuse or neglect. Not "inconclusive" — zero substantiation. *(See Exhibit B-3)*

23. **Allegation 4: Arsenic / poisoning of child.** This allegation alleged I poisoned L.D.W. with arsenic. In fact, I had self-administered Ozempic, a prescribed medication, by injection. 24. **Allegation 5: Sexual assault.** This allegation was made without any police report being filed, without any forensic examination, and without any criminal charges being brought. It exists only as an uncorroborated accusation. *(See Exhibit B-5)*
