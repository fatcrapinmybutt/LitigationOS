## THE COURT FINDS:

### Finding 1 — Proper Cause and Change of Circumstances

1. Plaintiff has established proper cause and a change of circumstances sufficient to revisit the existing custody order under the standard set forth in *Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499 (2003), based on the following:

   (a) The favorable HealthWest psychological evaluation completed September 5, 2025, which constitutes a new fact that did not exist at the time of the prior custody order;

   (b) Plaintiff's completion of all court-ordered conditions;

   (c) The passage of more than 300 consecutive days of complete separation between Plaintiff and L.D.W. during the child's critical developmental years;

   (d) The systematic pattern of debunked allegations against Plaintiff;

   (e) The recorded statement by Albert Watson evidencing a coordinated effort to interfere with Plaintiff's parental relationship.

### Finding 2 — Best Interest Analysis (MCL 722.23)

2. The Court has evaluated the twelve best interest factors under MCL 722.23(a) through (l) and finds as follows:

   (a) **Factor (a) — Love, affection, emotional ties:** Plaintiff maintained a bonded relationship with L.D.W. during 438 days of shared custody. The 300+ day separation has damaged but not severed this bond. **This factor favors Plaintiff.**

   (b) **Factor (b) — Capacity for love, affection, guidance:** Plaintiff demonstrated consistent capacity during the 438-day period. Defendant's 300+ day denial of all contact reflects negatively on Defendant's disposition. **This factor favors Plaintiff.**

   (c) **Factor (c) — Capacity to provide material needs:** Plaintiff has maintained employment and continued financial support ($792 child support, $855 insurance premiums) despite being denied all contact. **This factor favors Plaintiff.**

   (d) **Factor (d) — Stability of custodial environment:** Plaintiff maintained a stable home during the 438-day period. Current stability is adequate at 1977 Whitehall Road, Lot 17, North Muskegon, MI 49445. **This factor is neutral.**

   (e) **Factor (e) — Permanence of family unit:** The current sole-custody arrangement lacks the dual-family stability of the prior 50/50 arrangement. **This factor favors Plaintiff.**

   (f) **Factor (f) — Moral fitness:** The record reflects seven categories of unsubstantiated allegations by Defendant, evidence of Watson family violence, and the recorded threat by Albert Watson. **This factor favors Plaintiff.**

   (g) **Factor (g) — Mental and physical health:** The court-ordered HealthWest evaluation found Plaintiff safe, stable, drug-free, and presenting zero risk factors. **This factor decisively favors Plaintiff.**

   (h) **Factor (h) — Home, school, community record:** L.D.W. is three years old. Insufficient current information is available due to Defendant's information blockade. **This factor is neutral.**

   (i) **Factor (i) — Reasonable preference of child:** L.D.W. is approximately three years old and is not of sufficient age to express a reasonable preference. **This factor is inapplicable.**

   (j) **Factor (j) — Willingness to facilitate relationship:** Defendant has denied all contact between L.D.W. and Plaintiff for 300+ consecutive days. Plaintiff has continued to seek contact through proper legal channels and has maintained financial support. **This factor strongly favors Plaintiff.**

   (k) **Factor (k) — Domestic violence:** No adjudicated finding of domestic violence by Plaintiff exists. Evidence of Watson household violence is in the record. **This factor favors Plaintiff.**

   (l) **Factor (l) — Other relevant factors:** The pattern of ex parte orders without evidentiary support, the disregarded HealthWest evaluation, the 59-day incarceration for birthday messages, and the documented alienation campaign are all relevant. **This factor strongly favors Plaintiff.**

### Finding 3 — Established Custodial Environment

3. L.D.W. had an established custodial environment with both parents during the 438-day shared custody period. The Court recognizes that the ex parte orders disrupted this dual environment and that Plaintiff has met the applicable burden of proof for modification.

### Finding 4 — Child's Best Interest Requires Modification

4. The totality of the best interest analysis — with ten of twelve factors favoring Plaintiff, zero factors favoring Defendant, and two factors being neutral or inapplicable — compels modification of the current custody arrangement. Continued complete denial of all contact between L.D.W. and Plaintiff is contrary to the child's best interests.

---

#### Option 3 — Graduated Reunification Schedule

- [ ] 7(e). The following graduated reunification schedule is hereby **ORDERED**:

   **Phase 1 (Weeks 1–2):** Supervised parenting time, three (3) visits per week, minimum two (2) hours each, at a mutually agreed location or as determined by the Court. Supervision may be provided by a mutually agreed third party or professional supervisor.

   **Phase 2 (Weeks 3–4):** Supervised parenting time, three (3) visits per week, minimum four (4) hours each, including one meal together.

   **Phase 3 (Weeks 5–6):** Unsupervised parenting time, including one (1) overnight per week (Saturday to Sunday) and one (1) daytime visit of six (6) hours.

   **Phase 4 (Weeks 7–8):** Expanded unsupervised parenting time, including two (2) overnights per week and one (1) daytime visit.

   **Phase 5 (Week 9 onward):** Full restoration of 50/50 shared physical custody on a week-on/week-off schedule.

- [ ] 7(f). Advancement from each phase to the next shall be presumed automatic unless Defendant files a specific, evidence-supported objection demonstrating actual harm to L.D.W., supported by affidavit, within 48 hours before the next phase begins.

### Part B — Makeup Parenting Time

8. Pursuant to MCL 722.27a(7), Plaintiff is entitled to **makeup parenting time** to compensate for the 300+ days of wrongful denial. Makeup time shall be implemented as follows:

   (a) An additional full weekend per month (Friday 6:00 p.m. to Monday 8:00 a.m.) until \_\_\_\_ makeup days have been credited;

   (b) First priority for all holiday and school-break parenting time for the next \_\_\_\_ months;

   (c) An extended two-week summer parenting time block in addition to regularly scheduled time.

---

### Part C — Reunification Therapy

9. The parties and L.D.W. shall participate in **reunification therapy** with a licensed family therapist of Plaintiff's choosing (subject to Court approval), commencing within fourteen (14) days of entry of this Order.

10. The cost of reunification therapy shall be borne by Defendant, given that the need for therapy was created by Defendant's 300+ day denial of all contact between L.D.W. and Plaintiff.

11. The therapist shall provide quarterly progress reports to the Court and to both parties.

---

### Part D — Anti-Alienation Provisions

12. Neither party shall make disparaging or negative remarks about the other party, or the other party's family, in the presence or hearing of L.D.W.

13. Neither party shall interfere with the other party's scheduled parenting time. Willful interference with parenting time shall constitute contempt of this Order.

14. Both parties shall actively encourage L.D.W.'s relationship with the other parent. Each parent shall facilitate telephone or video contact between L.D.W. and the other parent at least three (3) times per week during periods when L.D.W. is in that parent's care.

15. Neither party shall permit any third party — including family members, romantic partners, or friends — to make disparaging remarks about the other parent in L.D.W.'s presence.

---

### Part E — Communication and Information Sharing

16. Both parties shall use the **AppClose co-parenting application** (or successor application agreed upon by the parties) for all scheduling and non-emergency communications regarding L.D.W.

17. Defendant shall provide Plaintiff with all of L.D.W.'s current:
   - School/daycare enrollment information, name, and contact information
   - Medical provider names and contact information
   - Copies of all school/daycare progress reports and records
   - Copies of all medical records and developmental assessments

   This information shall be provided within seven (7) days of entry of this Order and updated on an ongoing basis.

18. Both parties shall have equal access to all of L.D.W.'s school, medical, and extracurricular records.

---

### Part F — Enforcement Provisions

19. Willful violation of any provision of this Order shall be punishable as contempt of court.

20. In the event of a dispute regarding parenting time, the Friend of the Court shall attempt mediation before either party seeks judicial intervention.

21. This Order shall be filed with the Friend of the Court, Muskegon County, Michigan.

---

### Part G — General Provisions

22. This Order is entered in the best interests of the minor child, L.D.W., pursuant to MCL 722.23 and MCL 722.27.

23. This Order supersedes all prior orders inconsistent herewith, including the ex parte orders of July 17, 2025, and August 8, 2025, to the extent they conflict with the terms herein.

24. This Court retains jurisdiction over all matters relating to the custody and parenting time of L.D.W.

25. This Order is effective immediately upon entry and shall remain in effect until further order of this Court.
