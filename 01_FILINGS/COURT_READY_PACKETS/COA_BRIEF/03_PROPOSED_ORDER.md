This matter having come before the Court on appeal from orders of the 14th Circuit Court, Muskegon County (Case No. 2024-001507-DC), Hon. Jenny L. McNeill presiding, and the Court having reviewed the record, the briefs of the parties, and the applicable law;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

---

## 1. REVERSAL OF PARENTING TIME SUSPENSION

The trial court's ex parte orders of August 8, 2025, and all subsequent orders continuing the suspension of Plaintiff-Appellant's parenting time with the minor child L.D.W., are **REVERSED**. These orders were entered without the specific factual findings of imminent harm required by MCR 3.207(B)(1) and without compliance with the procedural requirements of *Shade v Wright*, 291 Mich App 17 (2010), and *Vodvarka v Grashorn*, 259 Mich App 559 (2003).

## 2. REVERSAL OF CUSTODY MODIFICATION

The trial court's modification of custody, to the extent it was entered without conducting the mandatory best interest analysis under MCL 722.23(a)–(l), is **REVERSED**. The trial court failed to make specific findings on all twelve best interest factors as required by *Fletcher v Fletcher*, 447 Mich 871, 879 (1994), and *Harvey v Harvey*, 470 Mich 186, 192 (2004).

## 3. REMAND FOR DE NOVO BEST INTEREST HEARING

This case is **REMANDED** to the 14th Circuit Court for a de novo best interest hearing conducted in full compliance with MCL 722.23 and all applicable court rules, including:

(a) Proper notice and service upon all parties under MCR 2.107;

(b) An evidentiary hearing at which both parties have a full and fair opportunity to present evidence and cross-examine witnesses;

(c) Specific, written findings on all twelve best interest factors under MCL 722.23(a)–(l);

(d) Consideration of all competent evidence, including the HealthWest evaluation;

(e) Compliance with MCR 3.210(C) hearing requirements.

## 4. DISQUALIFICATION OF JUDGE McNEILL

All proceedings on remand shall be conducted **before a different judge** — a judge with no personal, familial, or professional connection to any party or to Judge Jenny L. McNeill. The undisclosed familial relationship between Judge McNeill (married to Cavan John Berry, who is related to Ronald Berry, the domestic partner of Defendant Emily A. Watson) and the pattern of adverse conduct toward Plaintiff-Appellant create an objectively intolerable risk of bias under MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b) and *Caperton v A.T. Massey Coal Co.*, 556 US 868 (2009).

Judge Jenny L. McNeill is **DISQUALIFIED** from further proceedings in Case No. 2024-001507-DC and related Case No. 2023-5907-PP.

## 5. MAKEUP PARENTING TIME

The trial court on remand shall, as part of the de novo hearing, determine and order makeup parenting time pursuant to MCL 722.27a(7) to compensate Plaintiff-Appellant for the parenting time lost during the period of unlawful suspension (August 8, 2025 to the date parenting time is restored).

## 6. VACATUR OF FILING BOND

The trial court's Order of May 16, 2025 imposing a $250.00 filing bond requirement on Plaintiff-Appellant (restricting Plaintiff-Appellant's access to the court) is **VACATED**.

## 7. COSTS

Costs are awarded to Plaintiff-Appellant pursuant to MCR 7.219.
