## ORDER ON RESPONDENT'S MOTION TO TERMINATE PERSONAL PROTECTION ORDER

At a session of said Court, held in the Courthouse, County of Muskegon, State of Michigan, on _________________, 20___.

**PRESENT: HON. JENNY L. McNEILL**
Circuit Court Judge, Family Division

---

This matter having come before the Court on Respondent Andrew James Pigors' Motion to Terminate Personal Protection Order, and the Court having reviewed the motion, supporting affidavit, exhibits, and the record of proceedings, and the Court having conducted a hearing on the motion with the parties present, and being otherwise fully advised in the premises;

**THE COURT FINDS:**

A. Respondent filed a Motion to Terminate Personal Protection Order pursuant to MCR 3.707(A) and MCL 600.2950(5);

B. Petitioner was provided with proper notice and an opportunity to be heard;

C. The Personal Protection Order was issued on December 3, 2023, and extended on December 4, 2024;

D. Respondent has demonstrated changed circumstances warranting termination of the PPO, including but not limited to: zero contact with Petitioner for more than 230 consecutive days; the absence of any new allegations of threatening behavior; a favorable professional evaluation (HealthWest H0002, September 5, 2025); and the absence of any objective evidence supporting a continuing reasonable apprehension of violence under MCL 600.2950(1);

E. Seven (7) independent law enforcement investigations found no violation, no threat, and no harm attributable to Respondent;

F. The PPO, as applied, has operated as a barrier to Respondent's fundamental right to the care, custody, and companionship of his minor child, L.D.W., rather than serving its intended protective purpose;

G. Continuation of the PPO is no longer necessary to protect Petitioner from the conduct that gave rise to the order, pursuant to MCR 3.717;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

1. **PPO TERMINATED.** The Personal Protection Order issued against Respondent Andrew James Pigors in Case No. 2023-5907-PP is hereby **TERMINATED AND VACATED IN ITS ENTIRETY**, effective immediately, pursuant to MCL 600.2950(5) and MCR 3.717.

2. **LEIN REMOVAL.** The Muskegon County Clerk shall remove the PPO and all associated entries from the Law Enforcement Information Network (LEIN) and any other state or federal databases within twenty-one (21) days of entry of this Order.

3. **FIREARMS PROHIBITION LIFTED.** The federal firearms prohibition under 18 U.S.C. § 922(g)(8), which was dependent upon the existence of this PPO, is no longer applicable to Respondent upon termination of this Order. The Clerk shall provide Respondent with a certified copy of this Order for purposes of demonstrating the PPO's termination to federal authorities.

4. **NO SURROGATE CUSTODY PROCEEDINGS.** The Court directs that PPO show-cause proceedings shall **NOT** be used as a surrogate mechanism to effect custody or parenting-time changes. All parenting-time and custody matters shall be addressed through Child Custody Act mechanisms in Case No. 2024-001507-DC.

5. **COMMUNICATION RESTORED.** Respondent and Petitioner shall conduct any necessary communications regarding the minor child L.D.W. through the Friend of the Court or a court-approved communication platform, until further order of this Court in Case No. 2024-001507-DC.

6. **EFFECTIVE IMMEDIATELY.** This Order is effective immediately upon entry.
