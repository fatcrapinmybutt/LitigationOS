### PPO ISSUANCE AND FALSE ALLEGATIONS

4. On or about December 3, 2023, Petitioner Emily A. Watson filed a petition for a Personal Protection Order against me. The PPO was issued the same day, ex parte, without notice to me and without any evidentiary hearing. I had no opportunity to be heard before the order took effect.

5. The PPO petition contained the following allegations, each of which I state under oath to be false:

   a. **ARSENIC POISONING:** Petitioner alleged I poisoned her with arsenic. In fact, Petitioner injected herself with a substance from the household refrigerator that she stated was intended to help her lose weight. She developed a skin reaction and blamed me. No medical evidence has ever confirmed arsenic poisoning. Hospital records from November 17, 2023 diagnosed her symptoms as **hypertension**, not toxic ingestion. **No poison of any kind was detected.**

   b. **PHYSICAL VIOLENCE:** No injuries were documented. The police report from the alleged altercation confirms no injuries and no charges were filed against me.

   c. **SURVEILLANCE:** The alleged "surveillance device" was a baby camera — a standard child-monitoring device used for the care of our minor child, L.D.W. I did not surveil Petitioner.

   d. **METHAMPHETAMINE USE:** Police Report NS2505044 (Officer Ella Randall) documents that the statement regarding "meth use" originated from **Petitioner herself**, not from any law enforcement finding attributed to me.

6. Prior to the PPO filing, Petitioner and I maintained **438 consecutive days of successful 50/50 custody** of L.D.W. — more than 14 months — with zero police reports, zero emergency room visits, zero documented incidents, and zero prior requests for protection.

### INCARCERATION FOR BIRTHDAY MESSAGES

However, the conduct underlying those violations consisted of sending birthday messages and other communications to my child, L.D.W., through AppClose — a court-approved co-parenting communication application. I sent those messages out of love for my child, not to threaten, harass, or intimidate Petitioner.

8. I was sentenced to a total of **59 days in jail** for these communications: 14 days at the November 15, 2025 hearing, and 45 days at the November 26, 2025 hearing.
