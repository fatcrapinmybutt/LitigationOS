[PROPOSED] ORDER GRANTING DISQUALIFICATION AND REASSIGNMENT

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    At a session of said Court, held in the County of Muskegon,
State of Michigan, on _________________, 20___.

    PRESENT: Honorable ________________________________


    THE COURT, having considered Plaintiff's Verified Motion for
Disqualification of Judge pursuant to MCR 2.003(C)(1), the Affidavit
in Support, the Exhibit Index, and all accompanying filings, and being
otherwise fully advised in the premises;

    THE COURT FINDS:

    1. Plaintiff has established legally sufficient grounds for
disqualification of the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill under MCR
2.003(C)(1)(a) and (b).

    2. The familial relationship between Judge McNeill (through her
marriage to Cavan John Berry) and Defendant's domestic partner (Ronald
Berry) creates an appearance of impropriety under Canon 2 of the
Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct.

    3. The prior professional relationship among Judge McNeill, Chief
Judge Kenneth Hoopes, and Judge Maria Ladas-Hoopes as named partners at
Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill, PLLC, creates a structural conflict requiring
assignment of a judge from outside Muskegon County.

    4. The documented pattern of disparate treatment, including a 1.9:1
grant ratio favoring Defendant, ex parte orders at a rate of 44% (all
favoring Defendant), muting of Plaintiff during hearings, on-the-record
statements of hostility, and imposition of 59 days of incarceration
without adequate procedural safeguards, establishes that Plaintiff's
right to a fair and impartial tribunal has been compromised.


    IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:

    1. The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill is DISQUALIFIED from all further
proceedings in Case No. 2024-001507-DC and consolidated Case No.
2023-5907-PP.

    2. These cases are REASSIGNED to a judge from outside Muskegon
County, to be designated by the State Court Administrative Office
(SCAO), who has no prior professional or personal relationship with any
judge of the 14th Circuit Court or the 60th District Court who was
formerly associated with the law firm of Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill, PLLC.

    3. All pending orders in these cases shall remain in effect until
reviewed by the newly assigned judge, unless modified by separate
order.

    4. The Clerk of the Court shall transmit the complete case files to
the newly assigned judge within seven (7) days of this Order.

    5. This Order is effective immediately upon entry.
