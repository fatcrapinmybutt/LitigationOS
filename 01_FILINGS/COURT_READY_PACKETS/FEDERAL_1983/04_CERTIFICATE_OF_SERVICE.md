## NOTES ON SERVICE

1. **IFP Status**: If Plaintiff is granted *in forma pauperis* status, the Court shall direct the U.S. Marshal to serve all Defendants per 28 U.S.C. § 1915(d), and Plaintiff shall not be required to prepay costs of service.

2. **Waiver of Service**: Private Defendants are encouraged to waive formal service pursuant to FRCP 4(d)(1). A waiver of service extends the time to answer to **60 days** from the date the request was sent (FRCP 4(d)(3)). Failure to waive without good cause will result in an order requiring the Defendant to pay the costs of formal service (FRCP 4(d)(2)).

3. **Judicial Defendants**: Service on judicial officers in their individual capacity requires personal service per FRCP 4(e)(2)(A). Service in their official capacity requires service on the governmental entity per FRCP 4(j)(2), which is effectuated by delivering a copy of the summons and complaint to the chief executive officer of the governmental entity or by serving in the manner prescribed by state law.
