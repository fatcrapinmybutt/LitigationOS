IV. LEGAL STANDARD

    21. MCR 2.302(B) — Scope of Discovery: "Parties may obtain
discovery regarding any matter, not privileged, which is relevant to
the subject matter involved in the pending action, whether it relates
to the claim or defense of the party seeking discovery or to the
claim or defense of any other party." Relevance is construed broadly;
information need not be admissible at trial if it "appears reasonably
calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence."

    22. MCR 2.310 — Request for Production of Documents: A party may
serve a request to produce documents, electronically stored
information, or tangible things within the scope of MCR 2.302(B) that
are in the possession, custody, or control of the party upon whom the
request is served.

    23. MCR 2.313(A) — Motion to Compel: A party may move for an
order compelling discovery when the opposing party fails to answer
interrogatories (MCR 2.313(A)(2)(b)(iii)), fails to respond to
document requests (MCR 2.313(A)(2)(b)(iv)), or provides evasive or
incomplete responses (MCR 2.313(A)(4)).

    24. MCR 2.313(B) — Sanctions for Failure to Comply: If a party
fails to obey an order to provide or permit discovery, the court may
enter sanctions including:

        a. An order that designated facts be taken as established
(MCR 2.313(B)(2)(a));

        b. An order refusing to allow the disobedient party to support
or oppose designated claims (MCR 2.313(B)(2)(b));

        c. An order striking pleadings or parts thereof
(MCR 2.313(B)(2)(c));

        d. An order rendering a default judgment against the
disobedient party (MCR 2.313(B)(2)(d));

        e. An order treating the failure as contempt of court
(MCR 2.313(B)(2)(e)).

    25. MCR 2.314 — Discovery in Domestic Relations Actions: Discovery
in family law matters is governed by MCR 2.302 through 2.313, with
the additional recognition that the best interest of the child is
paramount and that full disclosure of relevant information is
necessary to enable the court to fulfill its statutory obligations
under MCL 722.23.

    26. MCR 3.922 — Items Discoverable as of Right: In domestic
relations proceedings, all written or recorded statements and notes
of statements made by the parties are discoverable as of right.

    27. MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors: The court must consider
twelve (12) enumerated factors in determining the best interest of a
child. Adequate discovery is essential for the court — and the
parties — to properly evaluate these factors. Obstruction of discovery
directly impedes the court's ability to render decisions in L.D.W.'s
best interest.

   Under MCL 722.23

prerequisite to the court's statutory obligation to evaluate these
factors.

    59. Without the discovery sought herein, neither the court nor
Plaintiff can meaningfully assess:

        a. Whether Defendant has been withholding medical or
educational information about L.D.W.;

        b. Whether the Watson family has engaged in coordinated

        c. Whether the home environment at 2160 Garland Drive is safe
and stable for L.D.W.;

        d. Whether Defendant's financial circumstances warrant
modification of child support;

        e. Whether the PPO was obtained in bad faith as a custody
tactic.


B. Plaintiff Has Been Denied Access to Information About His Own
   Child for 226+ Days

    60. As of the date of this filing, Plaintiff has been completely
separated from L.D.W. for over 226 consecutive days. During this
period, Plaintiff has been denied not only physical contact with his
child but also access to any information about L.D.W.'s health,
education, welfare, and daily life.

    61. MCL 722.27a(7) provides that a parent is entitled to access
information regarding his or her child's health, education, and
welfare. Defendant's refusal to provide any information, either
voluntarily or through discovery, constitutes a continuing violation
of Plaintiff's parental rights.

    62. The urgency of this discovery cannot be overstated. Every day
that passes without disclosure is another day that Plaintiff is denied
fundamental information about his child.


C. Berry-McNeill Structural Conflict Requires Full Discovery

    63. Plaintiff has filed a Motion for Disqualification of Hon.
Jenny L. McNeill based on the structural conflict of interest arising
from the relationship between Ronald Berry (Defendant's domestic
partner) and Cavan Berry (Judge McNeill's spouse).

    64. MCR 2.003(C)(1) provides that a judge is disqualified when the
judge "is biased or prejudiced for or against a party or attorney."
Canon 2 of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct requires that a
judge "should avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety in
all activities."

    65. Discovery in Category 7 (Berry-McNeill documentation) and
Category 8 (phone records) is essential to determine:

        a. The nature and extent of the Berry-McNeill connection;

        b. Whether Ronald Berry served as an intermediary between
Defendant and the judge's household;

        c. Whether the submission of Berry's voicemail as ex parte
evidence was facilitated by this relationship;

        d. Whether the judge's handling of this case has been
influenced, directly or indirectly, by the Berry connection.

    66. Without this discovery, Plaintiff cannot adequately support his
disqualification motion, and the integrity of these proceedings
remains in doubt.


D. Watson Family Coordination Requires Discovery to Prove
   Civil Conspiracy

    67. The pattern of conduct in this case — including coordinated
police reports, CPS referrals, PPO petitions, and false allegations —
is consistent with a civil conspiracy among Watson family members to
deprive Plaintiff of his parental rights.

    68. Michigan recognizes civil conspiracy as an actionable tort.
See Advocacy Org for Patients & Providers v Auto Club Ins Ass'n,
257 Mich App 365, 384 (2003). To establish civil conspiracy, Plaintiff
must demonstrate: (1) a concerted action; (2) by a combination of two
or more persons; (3) to accomplish an unlawful purpose; (4) or to
accomplish a lawful purpose by unlawful means.

    69. Discovery in Categories 1 (communications), 3 (police
reports), and 6 (PPO records) is essential to establish the concerted
action element and to identify the scope of the conspiratorial
agreement.

L.D.W.'s welfare;

        d. Failure to comply with discovery obligations.
