## III. COMMON ALLEGATIONS OF FACT

### A. Plaintiff's Tenancy and Mobile Home Ownership

10. In approximately 2021, Plaintiff became a resident of Shady Oaks Mobile Home Community at Lot 17, 1977 Whitehall Road, Laketon Township, Muskegon County. Plaintiff submitted a rental application to "Homes of America," identified as the Owner/Manager, and was approved. Plaintiff's original monthly lot rent was $325 per month under a November 2023 lease.

11. Plaintiff is the titled owner of a 1970 Marlette mobile home, VIN 1646732, located on Lot 17. Under Michigan law, a mobile home is **personal property** belonging to the titled owner, distinct from the real property on which it sits. MCL 125.2330i; MCL 125.1305.

12. Plaintiff resided at Shady Oaks with his minor child, **L.D.W.**, who was approximately three years old at the time. Lot 17 was L.D.W.'s primary residence during Plaintiff's custodial periods.

### B. Coerced Rent Increase and Lease Manipulation

13. On or about March 26, 2024, Defendants coerced Plaintiff into signing a new lease agreement with a different entity — Cricklewood MHP LLC — under express threat of immediate eviction. The lease was signed off-site, not at the community office. No explanation was given for the entity change.

14. Through this coerced modification and subsequent unnoticed increases, Plaintiff's monthly lot rent escalated as follows:
   - Original rent (November 2023 lease): **$325/month**
   - First increase: **$395/month**
   - Second increase (Cricklewood coerced lease): **$695/month** — a **114% increase** from the original $325
   - Third increase (by February 2025): **$720/month**

   This represents a total increase of **121% in approximately 14 months** ($325 → $720). No evidence of proper 60-day statutory notice under MCL 554.632 or MCL 125.2321 has been produced by Defendants for any of these increases.

15. The timing of the rent escalation correlates directly with Plaintiff's complaints about habitability conditions, establishing a retaliatory motive.

### C. Ledger Manipulation and Payment Fraud

16. Defendants systematically manipulated Plaintiff's payment ledger to fabricate rental arrears and justify eviction proceedings. Forensic analysis reveals **three irreconcilable versions** of the same RentManager ledger (Account #33700, SO LOT 17):

   a. **Ledger Version A** (printed August 22, 2024, at 11:01 AM, software v12.240702): Shows a **credit balance of negative $61.64** — meaning Plaintiff owed nothing and was in fact overpaid.

   b. **Ledger Version B** (printed January 8, 2025, at 1:50 PM, software v12.241104): Shows a **balance due of $2,180.18** — printed three days before Defendants filed the second eviction action.

   c. **Ledger Version C** (submitted to Court): Shows yet different amounts from both Versions A and B.

17. The **$2,241.82 discrepancy** between Ledger Versions A and B cannot be explained by normal rent accrual. The software version changed between printings (enabling retroactive editing), and the page count expanded (proving transactions were added or removed). Version A was emailed by Park Manager Kim Davis at 11:32 AM on the same day it was printed, creating a 39-minute contemporaneous evidence window proving its authenticity.

18. Defendants omitted at least **$6,085.99** in documented payments from Plaintiff's ledger:

   a. **MDHHS Housing Assistance #1: $1,962.45** (April 2024) — Defendants' bank rejected this government assistance payment. Defendants admitted in their Answer (filed January 9, 2025, Affirmative Defense #14) that they have a policy of refusing government housing assistance;

   b. **MDHHS Housing Assistance #2: $768.48** (April 12, 2024) — omitted entirely from all ledger versions;

   c. **TrueNorth CERA Grant: $2,054.80** (check #428979, August 14, 2024) — appears in Ledger Version A but vanishes from Version B;

   d. **Personal Check: $1,300.26** (August 14, 2024) — fully credited in Version A, completely omitted from Version B. This check was endorsed and cashed not by the payee "Shady Oaks MHP" but by **Partridge Securities on behalf of Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC** — a separate entity not named on the check — constituting improper endorsement and evidence of interentity financial commingling.

19. When these documented payments totaling $6,085.99 are credited against Defendants' counter-complaint claim of $3,805.44, **the actual account balance is negative $2,280.55** — meaning Defendants owe Plaintiff, not the reverse.

20. Defendants further inflated Plaintiff's account through unauthorized charges including: an NSF fee of $35.00 originating from another tenant's account (Jamie Semelbauer); legal fees of $178.00 (Defendants' own attorney costs charged to Plaintiff's ledger before any judgment); and late fee overcharges exceeding contractual amounts by $53.00.
