## II. BACKGROUND AND TENANCY

## III. COERCED LEASE AND RENT ESCALATION

7. On or about **March 26, 2024**, I was told that I had to sign a new lease immediately or I would be evicted. I was directed to an off-site location — not the community office — to sign. When I arrived, the lease was with a different entity: **Cricklewood MHP LLC**. No one explained why the entity name had changed.

8. Under the express threat of eviction, I signed the Cricklewood lease. The rent immediately increased to **$695 per month** — a **114% increase** over my original $325 rent. I was not given the required 60-day advance written notice of this increase as required by MCL 125.2321 and MCL 554.632.

9. By approximately February 2025, my rent was increased again to **$720 per month** — again without proper statutory notice. In total, my rent increased **121% in approximately 14 months**, from $325 to $720.

10. I believe the timing of these increases was retaliatory. Each increase came after I raised concerns about conditions at the park, particularly the sewage problems.

## IV. LEDGER MANIPULATION AND PAYMENT FRAUD

11. I have personal knowledge that Defendants manipulated my payment ledger to fabricate rental arrears. I received **three different versions** of the same RentManager ledger for my account (Account #33700, SO LOT 17), and no two of them show the same balance:

   a. **Ledger Version A**, which I received on **August 22, 2024**, printed at 11:01 AM (software version 12.240702): This version showed a **credit balance of negative $61.64**. I owed nothing. The park manager, Kim Davis, emailed this version to me at 11:32 AM the same day. I also accessed the Zego portal at 11:40 AM, completing a 39-minute contemporaneous evidence window proving this ledger is authentic.

   b. **Ledger Version B**, printed on **January 8, 2025**, at 1:50 PM (software version 12.241104): This version showed a **balance due of $2,180.18**. This was printed just three days before Defendants filed the second eviction action against me.

   c. **Ledger Version C**, submitted to the 60th District Court during eviction proceedings: This version showed yet different amounts from both A and B.

12. The **$2,241.82 discrepancy** between Version A (credit) and Version B (amount due) cannot be explained by normal rent accrual. I noticed that the software version changed between the printings, and the page count expanded — meaning transactions were added or removed between August and January.

13. I have documentation that Defendants omitted at least **$6,085.99** in payments that I or government agencies made on my behalf:

   a. **MDHHS Housing Assistance #1: $1,962.45** (approximately April 2024). I applied for government housing assistance through the Michigan Department of Health and Human Services. The payment was sent, but Defendants' bank rejected it. In their own Answer to my lawsuit (filed January 9, 2025), Defendants stated in Affirmative Defense #14: *"Defendant is not required to accept third party rental assistance."* This is an admission that they have a policy of refusing government assistance — then they sued me for nonpayment of the very amounts they refused.

   b. **MDHHS Housing Assistance #2: $768.48** (approximately April 12, 2024). This payment was omitted entirely from all ledger versions. MDHHS records confirm it was issued.

   c. **TrueNorth CERA Grant: $2,054.80** (check #428979, August 14, 2024). This payment appears in Ledger Version A but completely vanishes from Version B.

   d. **Personal Check: $1,300.26** (August 14, 2024). I wrote this check payable to "Shady Oaks MHP." It was fully credited in Ledger Version A but completely omitted from Version B.

14. When all documented payments ($6,085.99) are credited against the $3,805.44 that Defendants claimed I owed, **the actual balance is negative $2,280.55** — meaning Defendants owe me money, not the other way around.

## VI. SEWAGE CONTAMINATION AND HABITABILITY

17. Beginning in approximately late 2024, I observed raw sewage overflowing from an interceptor tank onto the ground surface near my home and adjacent lots. The sewage created pooling, staining, and a strong odor. This was near where my three-year-old son played outdoors.

18. I first reported the sewage issue to park manager Kim Davis as early as **March 2024**. No meaningful action was taken.

19. On **March 7, 2025**, I sent a formal written complaint to Defendants at shadyoaks@ourhomesofamerica.com, demanding remediation within 48 hours. I received no response.

20. On **March 17, 2025**, I sent a second, urgent written complaint citing MCL 554.139 (implied warranty of habitability) and stating I would file complaints with Muskegon County Health Department, City of Muskegon Water & Sewer, EGLE, and Housing Law Enforcement if the issue was not resolved. I received no response.

21. I am aware that on **March 20, 2025**, EGLE Environmental Quality Analysts inspected the property and found raw sewage discharging from an interceptor tank onto the ground surface. They rated the facility **"NOT IN COMPLIANCE"** for both Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance.

22. I am aware that on **April 15, 2025**, EGLE issued **Violation Notice VN-017235** for violations of Part 31 NREPA (MCL 324.3101 et seq.) and the Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.), including illegal sewage discharge and failure to report.

23. In late June 2025, sewage was again leaking during heavy rains, killing vegetation near occupied homes. Defendants' minimal repair (less than one hour of "TV and jetting") did not fix the underlying infrastructure failure.

24. Despite all of this, Defendants continued to charge me **full rent and full utility charges** throughout the period of uninhabitable conditions. They never offered rent abatement or relocation assistance.

## VII. UTILITY METER FRAUD

25. Beginning approximately May 2025, I was billed approximately $87 per month for water and sewer submeter charges. I never consented to the installation of a submeter. No one notified me that a meter was being installed.

26. I reviewed my billing records on the Zego portal and discovered that **the water gallons consumed and sewer gallons discharged are reported as identical every month**: April (4,245 = 4,245); May (3,233 = 3,233); June (3,100 = 3,100). This is physically impossible. I had an outdoor spigot connected to the water line that I used for a sprinkler and garden hose. This water drained into the ground, not the sewer system. Therefore, water consumption must always be higher than sewer discharge. Identical numbers prove the readings were fabricated.
