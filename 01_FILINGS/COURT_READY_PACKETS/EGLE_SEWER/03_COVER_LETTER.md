Dear EGLE Water Resources Division:

I respectfully submit the enclosed complaint and supporting evidence regarding continuing sewage discharge violations at Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park, Muskegon County, Michigan. This complaint supplements and documents events subsequent to EGLE Violation Notice VN-017235, issued April 15, 2025.

## Background

I am a former resident of Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park, and the titled owner of the manufactured home located on that lot. I resided at the Park from approximately February 2021 until July 17, 2025, when I was forcibly removed by the Park operators. During my residence, I was directly impacted by the sewage conditions at the facility.

## Summary of Continuing Violations

On March 20, 2025, EGLE analysts Connor O'Brien and Amanda St. Amour inspected the Park and confirmed raw sewage discharging from an interceptor tank onto the ground surface near occupied residential lots. The facility was rated "NOT IN COMPLIANCE" in both Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance categories. EGLE issued Violation Notice VN-017235 on April 15, 2025.

Despite this enforcement action:

1. **As of April 24, 2025**, the Park operators had not contacted EGLE — 17 days after inspection and 9 days after the Violation Notice;

2. **In late June 2025**, sewage leaked again during heavy rains, killing vegetation near occupied homes, demonstrating that remediation was inadequate;

3. **On July 17, 2025**, rather than remediating the sewage conditions, the Park operators forcibly removed me from the property — drilling locks, destroying personal property, and posting a "FREE" sign on my titled manufactured home while my three-year-old child was present;

4. **The operating entities** (Homes of America LLC and Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC) are both **dissolved corporate entities**, raising questions about legal accountability for environmental compliance.

## Why Escalated Enforcement Is Needed

The Park operators demonstrated through their actions that they view resident complaints and government enforcement as nuisances to be ignored rather than obligations to be met. Their response to my sewage complaints was not remediation — it was retaliation. Their response to EGLE's Violation Notice was silence for at least 17 days.

I request that EGLE:
- Conduct a follow-up inspection to verify remediation status;
- Assess administrative penalties for continuing noncompliance;
- Require groundwater testing near the discharge area;
- Require a comprehensive assessment of the Park's entire sewage infrastructure;
- Coordinate with LARA regarding the facility's operating license;
- Refer to the Attorney General's office if compliance is not achieved.

Thank you for your continued attention to this matter. The sewage conditions at Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park posed — and may continue to pose — a serious environmental and public health risk to residents of Laketon Township.
