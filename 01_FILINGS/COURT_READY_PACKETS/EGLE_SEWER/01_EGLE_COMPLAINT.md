## V. STATEMENT OF VIOLATIONS

### A. Illegal Sewage Discharge — Part 31 Violations (MCL 324.3101 et seq.)

1. **MCL 324.3109 — Prohibition on discharge of sewage.** Beginning in approximately late 2024, raw sewage began overflowing from an interceptor tank located on the Park property near Lot 17 and adjacent occupied lots. The sewage discharged directly onto the ground surface — not into an approved treatment system — in violation of MCL 324.3109, which prohibits the discharge of any waste or waste effluent into the waters of the state (including groundwater) without a permit.

2. **MCL 324.3112 — Duty to notify.** Respondents failed to notify EGLE or local health authorities within 24 hours of becoming aware of the sewage discharge, as required under EGLE reporting obligations. The discharge persisted for months before the Muskegon County Department of Public Health initiated a referral to EGLE on March 18, 2025 — and that referral came from the county health department, not from the facility operators.

3. **MCL 324.3115 — Duty to submit written reports.** Respondents failed to submit required written reports to EGLE regarding the sewage discharge, as documented in EGLE's inspection findings and Violation Notice VN-017235.

4. **Groundwater contamination risk.** The discharge of raw sewage onto the ground surface adjacent to occupied residential lots creates an imminent risk of groundwater contamination. The Park is located in Laketon Township, Muskegon County, where groundwater is a primary drinking water source for many residents. Prolonged surface discharge of untreated sewage allows pathogens, nitrates, and other contaminants to infiltrate the soil and migrate to the water table.

### B. Sewage System Failures — Part 41 Violations (MCL 324.4101 et seq.)

5. **MCL 324.4104 — Sewerage system operation and maintenance.** The Park's sewage collection and treatment infrastructure has failed to function as designed. The interceptor tank overflow that discharged raw sewage onto the ground surface is direct evidence of inadequate operation and maintenance of the facility's sewerage system.

6. **Inadequate capacity or structural failure.** The repeated overflow events — initially discovered in late 2024 and recurring in late June 2025 during heavy rains — indicate either structural failure of the interceptor tank/collection system or inadequate capacity for the number of connected lots. The recurrence after Respondents' initial remediation efforts demonstrates that superficial repairs did not address the root cause.

### C. Mobile Home Commission Act Violations (MCL 125.2301 et seq.)

7. **MCL 125.2325 — Habitability and maintenance.** Respondents failed to maintain the sewage infrastructure in a condition that provides safe and habitable conditions for Park residents, including families with young children.

8. **MCL 125.2305 — Licensing requirements.** Respondents operated the Park through dissolved corporate entities (Homes of America LLC and Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC), raising questions about whether the facility's operating license is held by an entity with legal capacity.

## VII. PUBLIC HEALTH AND ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT

### A. Groundwater Contamination Risk

9. Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park is located in Laketon Township, Muskegon County, an area where groundwater serves residential water supply needs. The discharge of raw sewage onto the ground surface over a period of months creates a significant risk that pathogens, nitrates, phosphorus, and other contaminants have infiltrated the subsurface and may affect groundwater quality.

10. EGLE's own inspection confirmed **raw sewage discharging onto the ground surface**, with residual pooling and staining visible. The contamination zone extended to areas near occupied residential lots, meaning the risk extends not only to the aquifer but to direct human contact.

### B. Health Hazard to Residents

11. The Park houses multiple families, including families with young children. Complainant resided at Lot 17 with his minor child, L.D.W. (age three at the time). Young children are particularly vulnerable to the health hazards posed by exposure to raw sewage, including:
    - Gastrointestinal illness from fecal pathogens (E. coli, Salmonella, Cryptosporidium);
    - Skin infections from direct or indirect contact with sewage-contaminated surfaces;
    - Respiratory illness from aerosolized sewage particles.

12. Neighboring residents confirmed to EGLE inspectors that they experienced **persistent odors and plumbing backups**, indicating the sewage problem affected multiple lots — not just Lot 17.

### C. Inadequate Remediation

13. The recurrence of sewage leaks in **late June 2025** — approximately three months after the EGLE inspection and two months after the Violation Notice — demonstrates that Respondents' remediation efforts were superficial and inadequate. Vegetation near occupied homes was killed by sewage exposure during this event, confirming continuing contamination.

14. As of the date of this complaint, Complainant has no information that full and permanent remediation has been completed, and sewage infrastructure at the Park may continue to pose an environmental and public health risk to current residents.

## VIII. RETALIATORY CONTEXT

15. Complainant notes that the Respondents' response to his sewage complaints and to EGLE enforcement was not remediation — it was retaliation against the complainant:

   - After Complainant's March 2025 complaints triggered EGLE involvement, Respondents **accelerated eviction proceedings** rather than addressing the environmental conditions;
   - On **July 17, 2025**, Respondents **forcibly removed Complainant** from the property, drilling locks and destroying personal property, rather than fixing the sewage system;
   - Respondents continue to **operate the Park** despite two of the primary operating entities being dissolved.

16. This retaliatory pattern is relevant to EGLE's enforcement because it demonstrates Respondents' willful disregard for environmental compliance and their preference for punishing complainants rather than remediating violations.

## IX. DISSOLVED ENTITY OPERATION — COMPLIANCE IMPLICATIONS

17. Both the property owner (Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC — dissolved ~2022 in New Jersey) and the operator (Homes of America LLC — dissolved in Michigan) are dissolved corporate entities. This raises critical questions:

   a. **Who is the legally responsible party** for environmental compliance at this facility?
   b. **Is the facility's LARA license** (License #1201891) held by an entity with legal capacity?
   c. **Who has authority** to authorize and fund remediation of the sewage infrastructure?
   d. **Is Alden Global Capital LLC** — the ultimate parent entity — liable for environmental compliance as the controlling entity?

18. EGLE enforcement actions should be directed at all entities in the corporate chain, including Alden Global Capital LLC, to ensure that a party with legal capacity and financial resources is held accountable for remediation.

## X. RELIEF REQUESTED

19. Complainant respectfully requests that EGLE:

   a. **Escalate enforcement** of Violation Notice VN-017235 to ensure full and permanent remediation of the sewage discharge;

   b. **Conduct a follow-up inspection** to determine whether the violations identified in the March 20, 2025 inspection have been fully remediated, particularly in light of the June 2025 recurrence;

   c. **Assess administrative penalties** against Respondents for:
      - Illegal sewage discharge in violation of Part 31 (MCL 324.3101 et seq.);
      - Failure to notify EGLE within 24 hours of the discharge;
      - Failure to submit required written reports;
      - Failure to achieve timely compliance after issuance of VN-017235;

   d. **Require groundwater testing** in the vicinity of the interceptor tank overflow area to determine whether contamination has reached the water table;

   e. **Require a comprehensive sewage system assessment** of the entire Park — not just the area near Lot 17 — to determine whether the infrastructure is adequate for the number of connected lots and meets current regulatory standards;

   f. **Refer this matter** to the Michigan Attorney General's office if Respondents continue to fail to achieve compliance, pursuant to MCL 324.3115;

   g. **Coordinate with LARA** Mobile Home Commission regarding the facility's operating license, given that the operating entities are dissolved;

   h. **Coordinate with the Muskegon County Health Department** regarding the ongoing public health risk to Park residents;

   i. **Require Respondents to identify** the party with legal authority and financial capacity to fund full remediation, given that multiple operating entities are dissolved;

   j. **Issue a compliance schedule** with clear deadlines and penalties for non-compliance;

   k. **Grant such other relief** as EGLE deems appropriate to protect public health and the environment.
