## EGLE Environmental Complaint — Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park

**Complainant:** Andrew James Pigors
**Facility:** Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park, 1977 Whitehall Road, North Muskegon, MI 49445
**EGLE Violation:** VN-017235 (April 15, 2025)
**Date Prepared:** [DATE]

---

## I. OVERVIEW

This document summarizes the evidence supporting the EGLE complaint regarding continuing sewage discharge violations at Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park. The evidence is organized by category: (A) EGLE's own enforcement records; (B) Complainant's direct documentation; (C) chronological sewage event records; (D) pattern of management neglect; and (E) health impact on residents.

---

## II. EVIDENCE CATEGORY A — EGLE ENFORCEMENT RECORDS

### A-1. Muskegon County Public Health Referral (March 18, 2025)

The Muskegon County Department of Public Health referred the sewage conditions at Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park to EGLE on March 18, 2025. This referral was prompted by reports of raw sewage overflow at the facility — reports that originated from Complainant's formal complaints to the Park operators (March 7 and March 17, 2025) and subsequent outreach to county health authorities.

**Significance:** The health department determined the conditions serious enough to warrant state-level environmental enforcement, rather than handling the matter locally.

### A-2. EGLE Inspection Report (March 20, 2025)

EGLE Environmental Quality Analysts **Connor O'Brien** and **Amanda St. Amour** conducted an on-site inspection on March 20, 2025. Their findings:

| Finding | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Sewage discharge** | Raw sewage discharging from interceptor tank onto ground surface |
| **Pooling** | Residual pooled sewage observed near occupied residential lots |
| **Staining** | Ground staining consistent with prolonged sewage discharge |
| **Odor** | Sewage odor present at the time of inspection |
| **Tank condition** | Unsecured septic/holding tank lid observed |
| **Resident reports** | Neighboring residents confirmed persistent odors and plumbing backups |
| **Compliance rating** | **"NOT IN COMPLIANCE"** — Facility Site Review |
| **Compliance rating** | **"NOT IN COMPLIANCE"** — Operation & Maintenance |

**Significance:** Two independent EGLE analysts confirmed the conditions. The "NOT IN COMPLIANCE" rating in both categories demonstrates systemic failure, not an isolated incident.

### A-3. Violation Notice VN-017235 (April 15, 2025)

EGLE issued Violation Notice VN-017235 on April 15, 2025, documenting the following violations:

| Statute | Violation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Part 31, NREPA** (MCL 324.3101 et seq.) | Illegal discharge of sewage onto ground surface — violation of water resources protection requirements |
| **Mobile Home Commission Act** (MCL 125.2301 et seq.) | Failure to maintain sewage infrastructure in compliance with mobile home park operating requirements |
| **Notification requirements** | Failure to notify EGLE or health authorities within 24 hours of the discharge |
| **Reporting requirements** | Failure to submit required written reports regarding the discharge |

### A-4. Respondent Non-Communication (Through April 24, 2025)

As of April 24, 2025 — seventeen (17) days after the EGLE inspection and nine (9) days after issuance of Violation Notice VN-017235 — the Park operators had **not contacted EGLE** to discuss remediation plans or submit a corrective action response.

**Significance:** This prolonged silence demonstrates willful disregard for EGLE enforcement authority and a lack of urgency in addressing a confirmed public health and environmental hazard.

## III. EVIDENCE CATEGORY B — COMPLAINANT DIRECT DOCUMENTATION

### B-1. March 7, 2025 Formal Written Complaint

On March 7, 2025, Complainant sent a formal written complaint to the Park operators at the email address shadyoaks@ourhomesofamerica.com. The complaint documented:
- Raw sewage overflow from the interceptor tank near Lot 17 and adjacent lots;
- Unsanitary conditions affecting the health and safety of residents, including Complainant's minor child, L.D.W.;
- Demand for remediation within 48 hours.

**No response was received from Park operators.**

### B-2. March 17, 2025 Urgent Follow-Up

On March 17, 2025, Complainant sent an urgent follow-up communication that:
- Cited MCL 554.139 (Michigan covenant of habitability for residential tenancies);
- Documented the continuing failure to remediate;
- Stated Complainant's intent to file complaints with:
  - Muskegon County Health Department
  - City of Muskegon Water & Sewer Division
  - Michigan EGLE
  - Housing Law Enforcement

**No response was received from Park operators.**

### B-3. Sewage Overflow Photographs (Late 2024 – June 2025)

Complainant photographed the sewage conditions at multiple points:
- Late 2024: Initial overflow from interceptor tank showing raw sewage on ground surface;
- Early 2025: Continued pooling and staining near occupied lots;
- Late June 2025: Post-VN-017235 recurrence — sewage leaking during heavy rains, killing vegetation near occupied homes.

**Significance:** The photographic evidence spans approximately six months, demonstrating that this was not a one-time event but a persistent, inadequately addressed infrastructure failure.

### B-4. Dead Vegetation Documentation (Late June 2025)

In late June 2025, approximately two months after EGLE issued Violation Notice VN-017235, sewage leaked again during heavy rains. The discharge killed vegetation near occupied homes. Complainant documented the dead vegetation as evidence that:
- The sewage discharge continued after EGLE enforcement;
- Respondents' remediation efforts were superficial and inadequate;
- The contamination zone was expanding to areas of visible environmental damage.

## V. EVIDENCE CATEGORY D — PATTERN OF MANAGEMENT NEGLECT

### D-1. Failure to Respond to Resident Complaints

Park operators failed to respond to both of Complainant's formal written complaints (March 7 and March 17, 2025). This silence — in the face of documented sewage overflow near occupied homes — demonstrates a pattern of deliberate indifference to environmental and habitability conditions.

### D-2. Failure to Contact EGLE After Violation Notice

Seventeen days after the EGLE inspection and nine days after issuance of VN-017235, Park operators had not contacted EGLE. This failure to engage with the regulatory authority demonstrates willful noncompliance with environmental enforcement.

### D-3. Inadequate Remediation

The recurrence of sewage leaks in late June 2025 demonstrates that any remediation efforts undertaken by Respondents were superficial. The fact that sewage continued to leak during heavy rain events suggests:
- The interceptor tank was not properly repaired or replaced;
- The root cause (structural failure or inadequate capacity) was not addressed;
- The remediation was cosmetic rather than substantive.

### D-4. Dissolved Entity Operation

Both the property owner (Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC) and the operator (Homes of America LLC) are dissolved corporate entities. Operation of a mobile home park through dissolved entities raises serious questions about:
- Who bears legal responsibility for environmental compliance;
- Whether the facility's operating license is valid;
- Whether there is a solvent, legally existing party that can fund and execute full remediation.

### D-5. Retaliatory Response to Environmental Complaints

Rather than addressing the sewage conditions, Respondents responded to Complainant's complaints by:
- Accelerating eviction proceedings based on a fabricated ledger with a $2,241.82 discrepancy;
- Ultimately forcibly removing Complainant and destroying his property on July 17, 2025;
- Operating as if environmental compliance is optional and complaints are grounds for retaliation.

### D-6. Utility Meter Fraud as Cover

In May 2025, Respondents installed unauthorized water/sewer submeters and began billing residents. Billing records from the Zego portal showed **physically impossible identical readings** (water consumed = sewer discharged) for three consecutive months:

| Month | Water (gal) | Sewer (gal) | Identical? |
|-------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| April 2025 | 4,245 | 4,245 | Yes — impossible |
| May 2025 | 3,233 | 3,233 | Yes — impossible |
| June 2025 | 3,100 | 3,100 | Yes — impossible |

This suggests that even as Respondents were billing residents for sewer service, they were not actually measuring or maintaining the sewage system properly.

## VI. EVIDENCE CATEGORY E — HEALTH IMPACT ON RESIDENTS

### E-1. Affected Population

Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park houses multiple families. The sewage discharge near occupied lots created a health hazard for all residents in the affected area, including:
- **Families with young children** — Complainant resided with L.D.W. (age three), who was particularly vulnerable to sewage-borne pathogens;
- **Neighboring residents** — EGLE inspectors documented that neighbors confirmed persistent odors and plumbing backups, indicating the problem extended beyond Lot 17.

### E-2. Exposure Pathways

The ground surface discharge of raw sewage near occupied residential lots created multiple exposure pathways:
- **Direct contact** — Children playing in yards adjacent to the discharge area;
- **Inhalation** — Aerosolized sewage particles from the discharge, confirmed by the odors documented during the EGLE inspection;
- **Contaminated surfaces** — Sewage staining on ground surfaces frequented by residents;
- **Potential groundwater contamination** — Infiltration of untreated sewage through soil into the water table.

### E-3. Duration of Exposure

The sewage discharge persisted for approximately six months (late 2024 through late June 2025), with inadequate remediation after the EGLE violation notice. This prolonged exposure increased the cumulative health risk to residents.

### E-4. Environmental Damage

The late June 2025 recurrence killed vegetation near occupied homes. This visible environmental damage confirms that the sewage discharge was of sufficient volume and toxicity to cause ecological harm, further supporting the public health risk assessment.

---

## VII. CONCLUSION

The evidence summarized in this document demonstrates:

1. **Confirmed violation**: EGLE's own inspection and Violation Notice VN-017235 confirm illegal sewage discharge at the facility;
2. **Continuing violation**: Post-VN-017235 recurrence in late June 2025 proves the violation was not remediated;
3. **Willful noncompliance**: Respondents failed to respond to resident complaints, failed to contact EGLE after enforcement, and retaliated against the complainant;
4. **Public health hazard**: Raw sewage discharge near occupied homes with families and young children creates ongoing health risks;
5. **Dissolved entity operation**: The entities responsible for the facility are dissolved, raising questions about accountability and remediation capacity;
6. **Need for escalated enforcement**: The pattern of noncompliance warrants escalated EGLE enforcement action, including administrative penalties, mandatory groundwater testing, and referral to the Attorney General if necessary.
