**PRESENT: HON. JENNY L. McNEILL, Circuit Court Judge**

---

This matter having come before the Court on Plaintiff's Verified Emergency Motion to Restore Parenting Time, and the Court having considered the Motion, the Affidavit of Andrew James Pigors, the file and record in this matter, and the arguments of the parties,

**THE COURT FINDS:**

1. Plaintiff has established proper cause and/or a change of circumstances sufficient to revisit the existing parenting time order pursuant to MCL 722.27(1)(c) and *Vodvarka v. Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499 (2003).

2. Plaintiff has completed all court-ordered requirements, including a favorable mental health evaluation from HealthWest dated September 5, 2025.

3. The statutory presumption under MCL 722.27a(1) — that parenting time is in the best interest of the child — has not been rebutted by clear and convincing evidence.

4. The best interest factors under MCL 722.23 support restoration of Plaintiff's parenting time with the minor child, L.D.W.

5. Continued denial of all parenting time causes irreparable harm to the parent-child bond and to L.D.W.'s emotional and psychological development.

---

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

## I. IMMEDIATE PARENTING TIME (Phase 1 — Weeks 1 through 4)

6. Plaintiff's Emergency Motion to Restore Parenting Time is **GRANTED**.

7. Commencing within seventy-two (72) hours of the entry of this Order, Plaintiff shall have supervised parenting time with L.D.W. as follows:

   (a) **Three (3) visits per week**, each a minimum of **four (4) hours** in duration;

   (b) Visits shall occur on **Tuesday**, **Thursday**, and **Saturday** at times agreed upon by the parties or, absent agreement, at 10:00 AM;

   (c) Supervision shall be provided by a **court-approved professional supervisor**, not a family member of either party;

   (d) Plaintiff shall bear the cost of supervision unless the Court orders otherwise.

8. Plaintiff shall have **daily telephone or video contact** with L.D.W. of a minimum of **thirty (30) minutes** per day at 7:00 PM or such other time as the parties may agree.

## II. TRANSITION TO UNSUPERVISED PARENTING TIME (Phase 2 — Weeks 5 through 8)

9. Beginning in Week 5 after entry of this Order, Plaintiff's parenting time shall transition to unsupervised parenting time as follows:

   (a) **Two (2) daytime visits per week** (unsupervised), each a minimum of **six (6) hours**;

   (b) **One (1) overnight per week**, from 5:00 PM to 10:00 AM the following morning;

   (c) Continued **daily telephone or video contact** of a minimum of thirty (30) minutes.

10. Advancement from Phase 1 to Phase 2 shall be presumed unless Defendant files a specific, supported objection demonstrating actual harm to L.D.W. within five (5) days of the scheduled advancement. General or speculative objections shall not be sufficient to delay advancement.

## III. STANDARD PARENTING TIME SCHEDULE (Phase 3 — Week 9 Onward)

11. Beginning in Week 9 after entry of this Order, Plaintiff shall have a standard parenting time schedule as follows:

### Regular Parenting Time

   (a) **Alternating weekends** from Friday at 6:00 PM through Sunday at 6:00 PM;

   (b) **One midweek overnight** each week, from Wednesday at 5:00 PM through Thursday at 8:00 AM (or drop-off at school/daycare);

   (c) **Daily telephone or video contact** of a minimum of fifteen (15) minutes on days when L.D.W. is not with Plaintiff.

### Holiday and Vacation Sharing

   (d) **Holidays** shall be shared on an alternating basis per the Muskegon County Friend of the Court guidelines, including but not limited to:
   - Thanksgiving (alternating years; in even-numbered years with Plaintiff)
   - Christmas Eve / Christmas Day (alternating; Christmas Eve with Plaintiff in odd-numbered years; Christmas Day with Plaintiff in even-numbered years)
   - Easter / Spring Break (alternating years)
   - Fourth of July (alternating years)
   - Labor Day / Memorial Day (alternating years)
   - Father's Day (always with Plaintiff)
   - Mother's Day (always with Defendant)
   - L.D.W.'s birthday (alternating years; with Plaintiff in even-numbered years)

   (e) **Summer parenting time:** Plaintiff shall have two (2) non-consecutive weeks of extended summer parenting time, with at least thirty (30) days' written notice to Defendant of the selected weeks.

   (f) **Spring and winter break:** Shall be divided equally, with Plaintiff having the first half in odd-numbered years and the second half in even-numbered years.

### Transportation and Exchange Provisions

   (g) All exchanges shall occur at a **neutral public location**, such as the North Muskegon Police Department or Muskegon District Library, unless the parties agree in writing to an alternative location;

   (h) The **receiving parent** shall be responsible for transporting L.D.W. to his or her home at the beginning of the parenting time period; the **delivering parent** shall be responsible for returning L.D.W. at the end of the parenting time period;

   (i) Neither party shall permit Albert Watson, Lori Watson, or Cody Watson to be present at exchange locations, pending further order of this Court.

## IV. MAKEUP PARENTING TIME

12. In recognition of the more than 230 consecutive days during which Plaintiff has been denied all contact with L.D.W., Plaintiff is awarded **makeup parenting time** as follows:

   (a) An additional weekend per month (the first weekend of each month) for twelve (12) consecutive months following entry of this Order;

   (b) An additional one (1) week of summer parenting time in the first summer following entry of this Order;

   (c) Such additional makeup time as may be agreed upon by the parties or ordered by the Court.

## V. COMMUNICATION PROVISIONS

13. Both parties shall use the **AppClose co-parenting application** for all scheduling and co-parenting communications. Both parties shall maintain active accounts and respond to messages within twenty-four (24) hours.

14. Neither party shall monitor, record, or intercept the other parent's telephone or video communications with L.D.W., except as permitted by law.

15. In the event of a scheduling conflict, both parties shall make good-faith efforts to reschedule within the same week. Parenting time shall not be forfeited due to scheduling conflicts unless the receiving parent affirmatively declines rescheduling.

## VI. ANTI-ALIENATION AND COOPERATION PROVISIONS

16. Neither party, nor any member of either party's household or extended family, shall make disparaging, demeaning, or derogatory remarks about the other parent in the presence of L.D.W. or within L.D.W.'s hearing.

17. Neither party shall discuss the details of this litigation, court orders, or legal proceedings with or in the presence of L.D.W.

18. Both parties shall affirmatively encourage and support L.D.W.'s relationship with the other parent.

19. Defendant shall provide Plaintiff with L.D.W.'s current address, school or daycare information, and medical provider information within seven (7) days of entry of this Order.

## VII. REUNIFICATION THERAPY

20. Both parties and L.D.W. shall participate in **reunification therapy** with a licensed mental health professional experienced in family reunification, to be selected by the parties or, absent agreement, appointed by the Court. The cost of therapy shall be shared equally unless the Court orders otherwise.

## VIII. RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

21. If either parent is unable to personally care for L.D.W. during his or her scheduled parenting time for a period exceeding four (4) hours, that parent shall first offer the other parent the opportunity to care for L.D.W. before arranging alternative childcare.

## IX. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

22. Any violation of this Order by either party may result in:

   (a) A finding of contempt of court pursuant to MCL 600.1701;

   (b) Makeup parenting time for the aggrieved parent;

   (c) Modification of the custody and parenting time arrangement;

   (d) Assessment of attorney fees, costs, and expenses;

   (e) Such other sanctions as the Court deems appropriate.

23. The Court specifically admonishes both parties that willful interference with parenting time is a violation of Michigan law and may be punished as contempt of court.

## X. EVIDENTIARY HEARING

24. An evidentiary hearing on Plaintiff's Motion, including full consideration of the best interest factors under MCL 722.23, shall be scheduled within **fourteen (14) days** of entry of this Order.

25. At the hearing, both parties shall have the opportunity to present evidence, call witnesses, and cross-examine the other party's witnesses.

## XI. GENERAL PROVISIONS

26. This Order is entered in the best interests of the minor child, L.D.W., pursuant to MCL 722.23 and MCL 722.27a.

27. This Order shall remain in effect until further order of this Court.

28. This Order is effective immediately upon entry.
