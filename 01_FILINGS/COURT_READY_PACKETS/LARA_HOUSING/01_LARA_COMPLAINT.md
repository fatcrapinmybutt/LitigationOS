## I. INTRODUCTION

1. Andrew James Pigors ("Complainant") respectfully submits this formal complaint to the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs ("LARA"), Mobile Home Commission, pursuant to the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act, MCL 125.2301 *et seq.*, and the Michigan Truth in Renting Act, MCL 554.631 *et seq.*, requesting investigation and enforcement action against the operators and owners of Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park ("the Park").

2. Complainant is the titled owner of a manufactured home situated at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park, 1977 Whitehall Road, North Muskegon, Michigan 49445, where he resided from approximately February 2021 until his unlawful forced removal on July 17, 2025.

3. This complaint documents a systematic pattern of violations of the Mobile Home Commission Act, the Truth in Renting Act, and related Michigan consumer protection statutes by the Park's corporate operators, including but not limited to: retaliatory eviction, habitability failures, utility fraud, ledger manipulation, illegal lockout, operation under dissolved corporate entities, and environmental violations confirmed by the Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy ("EGLE").

4. Complainant previously filed a verified complaint in Muskegon County Circuit Court, Case No. 2025-002760-CZ, raising nine counts arising from the same pattern of violations. That case was dismissed by Hon. Kenneth S. Hoopes. LARA intervention is now essential because the administrative enforcement mechanisms of the Mobile Home Commission are specifically designed to address the type of systematic park-level violations documented herein, and the Complainant has exhausted local judicial remedies.

## III. STATEMENT OF FACTS

### A. Tenancy and Original Lease Terms

12. Complainant moved into Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park on or about February 1, 2021, pursuant to a lease agreement with Homes of America LLC. Initial lot rent was **$335 per month**.

13. The original lease was a standard mobile home park lot rental agreement governed by MCL 125.2301 *et seq.* Complainant is the titled owner of the manufactured home situated on the lot.

14. By November 2023, Complainant's lot rent had increased to approximately **$325 per month** under Homes of America LLC management.

### B. Coerced Lease and Illegal Rent Increase (March 2024)

15. On or about **March 26, 2024**, Complainant was summoned to an off-site location and presented with a new lease under the name of **Cricklewood MHP LLC** — an entity that had never been disclosed to Complainant as having any interest in the Park.

16. The new lease demanded lot rent of **$695 per month**, representing a **114% increase** over the prior rate of $325 per month.

17. Complainant was told that if he did not sign the Cricklewood lease, he would face eviction. Under duress and threat of homelessness, Complainant signed.

18. No proper **60-day advance written notice** of the rent increase was provided as required by MCL 125.2321. The entity substitution from Homes of America LLC to Cricklewood MHP LLC was not disclosed in advance, nor was the corporate relationship between these entities explained.

19. By **February 2025**, lot rent had further increased to **$720 per month** — a cumulative **121% increase** from the original rate in approximately 14 months.

### C. Sewage Crisis and Habitability Violations (Late 2024 – Present)

20. Beginning in **late 2024**, raw sewage began overflowing from an interceptor tank located near Lot 17 and adjacent lots within the Park. Sewage discharged onto the ground surface, creating unsanitary and hazardous conditions.

21. On **March 7, 2025**, Complainant sent a formal written complaint to the Park operators at shadyoaks@ourhomesofamerica.com documenting the sewage conditions and requesting remediation.

22. On **March 17, 2025**, Complainant sent an urgent follow-up communication citing MCL 554.139 (covenant of habitability) and stating his intent to file complaints with the Muskegon County Health Department, City of Muskegon Water & Sewer Division, and EGLE if conditions were not immediately addressed.

23. On **March 18, 2025**, EGLE received a referral from the Muskegon County Department of Public Health regarding the sewage discharge at the Park.

24. On **March 20, 2025**, EGLE Environmental Quality Analysts **Connor O'Brien** and **Amanda St. Amour** conducted an on-site inspection of the Park. Their findings confirmed **raw sewage discharging onto the ground surface**. The facility was rated **"NOT IN COMPLIANCE"** for both Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance categories.

25. On **April 15, 2025**, EGLE issued **Violation Notice VN-017235**, documenting violations of Part 31 of the Natural Resources and Environmental Protection Act (NREPA) and the Mobile Home Commission Act.

26. As of **April 24, 2025** — more than 17 days after the EGLE inspection — the Park operators had not contacted EGLE to discuss remediation.

27. In **late June 2025**, sewage leaked again during heavy rains, killing vegetation near Lot 17. No remediation had been completed.

### D. Fabricated Water Meters and Utility Fraud (May 2025)

28. In **May 2025**, the Park operators installed unauthorized water and sewer submeters at Lot 17 without prior written notice or consent, in violation of MCL 125.2328(1)(b).

29. Subsequent billing records from the Zego billing portal revealed **physically impossible readings**: the number of water gallons consumed was reported as identical to the sewer gallons discharged each month:

| Month | Water Gallons | Sewer Gallons | Difference |
|-------|--------------|---------------|------------|
| April 2025 | 4,245 | 4,245 | 0 |
| May 2025 | 3,233 | 3,233 | 0 |
| June 2025 | 3,100 | 3,100 | 0 |

30. This 1:1 ratio is a **physical impossibility**. Complainant maintained an outdoor irrigation spigot; water used for outdoor purposes never enters the sewer system. Identical readings across multiple months indicate fabricated meter data, not actual measurement.

### E. Ledger Manipulation and Check Fraud

31. Three irreconcilable versions of Complainant's payment ledger exist, each generated from the Park's RentManager accounting software:

## IV. VIOLATIONS ALLEGED

### COUNT I — RETALIATORY EVICTION (MCL 600.5720)

51. MCL 600.5720 prohibits a landlord from retaliating against a tenant who complains about violations of health, safety, or housing codes or who exercises any legal right.

52. Each of the three eviction actions was initiated in close temporal proximity to Complainant's protected activities (sewage complaints, requests for code enforcement, assertion of habitability rights).

53. The timeline documented in Section III.G above establishes a clear retaliatory pattern in violation of MCL 600.5720.

### COUNT II — HABITABILITY VIOLATIONS (MCL 554.139; MCL 125.2325)

54. MCL 554.139 imposes a covenant of fitness for habitation on every residential lease. MCL 125.2325 requires mobile home park operators to maintain common areas and utilities in safe and habitable condition.

55. The confirmed raw sewage overflow, inadequate waste treatment, and environmental contamination constitute material breaches of the covenant of habitability and the Mobile Home Commission Act's maintenance requirements.

56. The EGLE "NOT IN COMPLIANCE" rating and Violation Notice VN-017235 constitute official governmental confirmation of these habitability failures.

### COUNT III — UTILITY FRAUD / FABRICATED WATER METERS (MCL 125.2328)

57. MCL 125.2328(1)(b) prohibits mobile home park operators from installing utility submeters without proper notice and regulatory compliance.

58. The unauthorized installation of water and sewer submeters in May 2025 and the subsequent billing based on physically impossible identical water/sewer readings constitute utility fraud.

59. The 1:1 water-to-sewer ratio documented across three consecutive months is evidence of fabricated meter data, not legitimate utility measurement.

### COUNT IV — CHECK FRAUD AND LEDGER MANIPULATION (MCL 440.3403; MCL 600.2919a)

60. The existence of three irreconcilable ledger versions, the $2,241.82 discrepancy, and the retroactive alteration of Complainant's account history constitute financial fraud.

61. The omission of $6,085.99 in documented payments from the ledgers was designed to manufacture false balances as a pretext for eviction.

62. The improper endorsement of Complainant's $1,300.26 check by Partridge Securities — an entity with no disclosed relationship to the Park — constitutes check endorsement fraud under MCL 440.3403.

### COUNT V — DISSOLVED ENTITY OPERATING ILLEGALLY (MCL 450.4802; MCL 125.2305)

63. MCL 125.2305 requires mobile home park operators to be properly licensed and authorized to conduct business in Michigan.

64. Both Homes of America LLC and Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC were dissolved at the time they continued to operate the Park, collect rent, execute leases, and file eviction actions.

65. All eviction judgments totaling $9,551.13, obtained by entities lacking legal capacity, are void ab initio.

66. The entity substitution from dissolved entities to Cricklewood MHP LLC was conducted without proper disclosure, in a manner designed to obscure the dissolved status of the original operators.

### COUNT VI — UNLAWFUL LOCKOUT AND ILLEGAL ENTRY (MCL 600.2918)

67. MCL 600.2918 prohibits a landlord from using self-help to remove a tenant or the tenant's property without lawful court process.

68. The July 17, 2025 forced removal — including drilling of locks, destruction of property, removal of appliances, and posting of a "FREE" sign on titled property — constitutes an unlawful lockout and illegal entry.

69. No lawful writ of restitution authorized the destruction of Complainant's titled personal property. The presence of a minor child (L.D.W., age 3) during the removal compounds the severity of this violation.

### COUNT VII — TRUTH IN RENTING ACT VIOLATIONS (MCL 554.631 *et seq.*)

70. MCL 554.633 prohibits lease provisions that waive tenant rights under the Mobile Home Commission Act or other protective statutes.

71. The Cricklewood lease, executed under duress on March 26, 2024, contained terms that violated the Truth in Renting Act, including the unconscionable $750 "buyout" offer conditioned on complete waiver of all legal rights and claims.

72. MCL 554.636 provides penalties of $250 per violation for illegal lease terms. The coerced rent increases without proper statutory notice constitute additional Truth in Renting Act violations.

### COUNT VIII — MOBILE HOME COMMISSION ACT VIOLATIONS (MCL 125.2301 *et seq.*)

73. MCL 125.2325 requires park operators to maintain common areas, roads, utilities, and waste disposal systems in safe and sanitary condition. The documented sewage failures, environmental contamination, and EGLE violation establish noncompliance.

74. MCL 125.2328 regulates utility charges and submeter installations. The unauthorized meter installation and fabricated billing data violate these provisions.

75. MCL 125.2321 requires 60 days' advance written notice for rent increases. The successive increases from $325 to $695 to $720 were imposed without compliant notice.

76. MCL 125.2331 prohibits retaliatory action against tenants who exercise rights under the Act. The retaliatory eviction pattern documented herein violates this provision.

77. MCL 125.2305 requires operators to be properly licensed. Operation under dissolved entities violates licensing requirements.

### COUNT IX — EGLE VIOLATION VN-017235 (Reference for LARA Awareness)

78. EGLE Violation Notice VN-017235, issued April 15, 2025, is referenced for LARA's awareness and coordinated enforcement. The violations documented by EGLE include:
    - Raw sewage discharge in violation of Part 31 NREPA (MCL 324.3101 *et seq.*)
    - Facility "NOT IN COMPLIANCE" for Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance
    - Mobile Home Commission Act violations within EGLE's concurrent jurisdiction

79. Complainant requests that LARA coordinate enforcement with EGLE to ensure comprehensive remediation of both regulatory and environmental violations at the Park.
