Dear Mobile Home Commission:

I am writing to formally request investigation and enforcement action regarding systematic violations of the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 *et seq.*), the Michigan Truth in Renting Act (MCL 554.631 *et seq.*), and related consumer protection statutes at Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park in North Muskegon, Michigan.

## Summary of Complaint

I am the titled owner of a manufactured home that was situated at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park, where I resided from February 2021 until my unlawful forced removal on July 17, 2025. During that time, the Park's corporate operators engaged in a pattern of violations including:

1. **Retaliatory eviction** in response to my complaints about sewage conditions and billing fraud
2. **Habitability failures** including raw sewage overflow confirmed by EGLE inspection and Violation Notice VN-017235
3. **Fabricated utility meter readings** showing physically impossible identical water and sewer consumption every month
4. **Ledger manipulation** involving three irreconcilable financial records and a $2,241.82 fabricated discrepancy
5. **$6,085.99 in omitted payments** including government housing assistance, a CERA grant, and a personal check improperly endorsed by an unrelated entity
6. **Operation under dissolved corporate entities** that continued collecting rent and filing eviction actions after dissolution
7. **Illegal lockout and property destruction** including drilling locks, destroying personal property, and posting a "FREE" sign on my titled manufactured home — while my three-year-old son was present
8. **Truth in Renting Act violations** including a coerced 114% rent increase and an unconscionable $750 "buyout" offer conditioned on waiver of all legal rights
9. **Continuing interference with property rights** including false statements to prospective buyers blocking sale of my home

## Why LARA Intervention Is Necessary

I previously filed a verified nine-count complaint in the 14th Circuit Court, Muskegon County (Case No. 2025-002760-CZ) raising these violations. That case was dismissed by Hon. Kenneth S. Hoopes.

The Mobile Home Commission's enforcement mechanisms — including investigation, administrative penalties, license revocation, and referral to the Attorney General — are specifically designed for the type of systematic park-level violations documented in this complaint. The evidence, including an official EGLE Violation Notice and three irreconcilable financial ledgers, warrants immediate administrative investigation.

I also note that the corporate entities operating this Park include at least two dissolved LLCs, and a related Alden Global Capital subsidiary (North Morris Estates in New Jersey) has received a criminal conviction for operating an unlicensed mobile home park. This suggests a systemic pattern of regulatory noncompliance that extends beyond a single facility.
