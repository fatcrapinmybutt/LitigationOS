# SUPPORTING FACTUAL NARRATIVE
## HUD Fair Housing Complaint — Andrew James Pigors v. Homes of America LLC et al.

**Complainant:** Andrew James Pigors
**Property:** Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park, 1977 Whitehall Road, North Muskegon, MI 49445
**Protected Class:** Familial Status (single father with minor child, L.D.W.)
**Complaint Basis:** 42 U.S.C. §§ 3604(b), 3617

---

## I. INTRODUCTION

This narrative provides detailed factual support for each claim of housing discrimination and retaliation in the accompanying HUD Complaint. Every fact cited herein is based on documentary evidence, government records, or direct personal knowledge. No fact has been fabricated or extrapolated.

---

## II. COMPLAINANT'S BACKGROUND AND PROTECTED STATUS

Andrew James Pigors is a single father who resided at Lot 17, Shady Oaks Mobile Home Park with his minor child, **L.D.W.**, beginning approximately February 2021. Mr. Pigors is the titled owner of a 1970 Marlette manufactured home (VIN 1646732), as confirmed by the Michigan Secretary of State. L.D.W. was approximately three years old during the primary events described herein and resided at Lot 17 during Mr. Pigors's custodial periods.

Mr. Pigors's status as a parent with a minor child constitutes familial status, a protected class under the Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. § 3604(b).

---

## III. TIMELINE OF DISCRIMINATION AND RETALIATION

### Phase 1: Stable Tenancy (February 2021 – Late 2023)

Mr. Pigors moved into Lot 17 in approximately February 2021 after submitting a rental application to Homes of America LLC, the identified Owner/Manager of the Park. He was approved and began paying lot rent, which stood at approximately $325/month by November 2023. During this period, tenancy was uneventful. The relationship between Mr. Pigors and Park management was not adversarial.

### Phase 2: Protected Activity and Retaliation Begins (Late 2023 – March 2024)

In late 2023 through early 2024, Mr. Pigors began reporting habitability concerns to Park management, including sewage overflow conditions near his lot. These complaints constitute protected activity under both the Fair Housing Act (42 U.S.C. § 3617) and the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2331).

**Instead of addressing the habitability conditions, Respondents retaliated.**

On March 26, 2024, Mr. Pigors was summoned to an off-site location — not the community office — and presented with a new lease under the name of **Cricklewood MHP LLC**, an entity that had never been disclosed to Mr. Pigors as having any interest in the Park. The new lease demanded lot rent of **$695/month** — a **114% increase** from the $325/month he had been paying. Mr. Pigors was told explicitly that if he did not sign, he would face eviction.

As a single father with a young child, Mr. Pigors was especially vulnerable to this threat. Eviction would render his family homeless and disrupt L.D.W.'s housing stability. Under duress, Mr. Pigors signed.

**No proper 60-day advance written notice** of the rent increase was provided as required by MCL 125.2321. The entity substitution was not explained.

### Phase 3: Ledger Manipulation and Eviction Campaign (Summer 2024 – Early 2025)

On **June 26, 2024**, Respondents obtained the first eviction judgment (File No. 2405891BLT, $1,928.17) against Mr. Pigors — filed by or on behalf of a dissolved entity lacking legal capacity to sue.

On **August 22, 2024**, Park bookkeeper Kim Davis generated and emailed Mr. Pigors a copy of his payment ledger (Ledger Version A) from the RentManager accounting system. This ledger showed a **credit balance of -$61.64** — Mr. Pigors owed nothing and was in fact overpaid.

Months later, on **January 8, 2025**, a second version of the same ledger (Ledger Version B) was generated, this time showing a **balance due of $2,180.18**. The discrepancy between Versions A and B is **$2,241.82**. The RentManager software version changed between printings (v12.240702 → v12.241104), and the page count expanded, proving transactions were retroactively added or removed.

**Three days later**, on January 11, 2025, Respondents filed the second eviction action based on the fabricated ledger.

This sequence — fabricate arrears, then immediately file eviction — is direct evidence of retaliatory intent.

### Phase 4: Escalation After Government Intervention (March – July 2025)

On **March 7, 2025**, Mr. Pigors sent a formal written complaint to Respondents at shadyoaks@ourhomesofamerica.com documenting the continuing sewage overflow and demanding remediation within 48 hours.

On **March 17, 2025**, Mr. Pigors sent an urgent follow-up citing MCL 554.139 and threatening complaints to Muskegon County Health, City of Muskegon Water & Sewer, EGLE, and Housing Law Enforcement.

On **March 18, 2025**, EGLE received a referral from Muskegon County Department of Public Health regarding the sewage discharge.

On **March 20, 2025**, EGLE Environmental Quality Analysts Connor O'Brien and Amanda St. Amour inspected the Park. They confirmed **raw sewage discharging onto the ground surface** near occupied homes and rated the facility **"NOT IN COMPLIANCE"** for both Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance.

On **April 15, 2025**, EGLE issued **Violation Notice VN-017235**, citing violations of Part 31 of the Natural Resources and Environmental Protection Act (MCL 324.3101 et seq.) and the Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.).

**Respondents' response to government enforcement was not remediation — it was escalation against the complainant.**

By **February 2025**, lot rent had increased again to **$720/month** — a cumulative **121% increase** in approximately 14 months.

On **April 14, 2025**, Respondents obtained a third eviction judgment ($3,638.65), again through a dissolved entity.

In **May 2025**, Respondents installed unauthorized water/sewer submeters without notice, and billing records from the Zego portal revealed fabricated readings (water consumed = sewer discharged, which is a physical impossibility).

### Phase 5: Forced Removal and Property Destruction (July 2025)

On **July 3, 2025**, two unidentified individuals attempted to break into Mr. Pigors's manufactured home.

On **July 17, 2025**, Respondents executed a forced removal:
- Locks on the home and outbuildings were **drilled open**
- All personal property was removed and/or destroyed
- Contractor Henry Brandel took Mr. Pigors's stove, washer, and dryer
- A **"FREE" sign** was posted on Mr. Pigors's titled manufactured home
- No court officer supervised the removal
- No inventory or receipts were provided
- **L.D.W., Mr. Pigors's three-year-old child, was present during the forced removal**

Mr. Pigors's lot rent was paid through April 2025. No lawful writ authorized the destruction of his titled personal property.

On **July 22, 2025** — five days after destroying Mr. Pigors's home — Respondents billed him **$7,633.13** for the now-empty lot.

### Phase 6: Continuing Interference (July 2025 – Present)

Mr. Pigors had **three documented willing buyers** for his manufactured home. Respondents blocked each sale by making false statements to prospective purchasers:
- "Plaintiff does not own the home"
- "Home is abandoned"
- "We are handling it through our legal team"

On **February 18, 2026**, Park Manager Cassandra "Casey" VanDam repeated these false statements to yet another prospective buyer, demonstrating that the discriminatory and retaliatory conduct is **continuing and ongoing**.

Estimated lost equity from blocked sales: **$60,000 to $105,000**.

## IV. EVIDENCE OF DISPARATE TREATMENT

### A. Rent Increase Disparity

The 114% rent increase imposed on Mr. Pigors (from $325 to $695/month) is facially extraordinary. A rent increase of this magnitude, imposed on a tenant who had just reported habitability violations and who had a young child in residence, warrants investigation into whether similarly situated tenants without children or without a history of complaints received comparable treatment.

### B. Refusal of Government Housing Assistance

Respondents admitted in their own court filing (Affirmative Defense #14, filed January 9, 2025) that they maintain a **policy of refusing government housing assistance payments**. Their bank rejected a $1,962.45 MDHHS housing assistance payment intended for Mr. Pigors's rent.

This policy has a discriminatory impact on families with children, who are disproportionately represented among recipients of government housing assistance. The rejection of housing assistance payments is a facially discriminatory practice that warrants HUD investigation.

### C. Pattern of Targeting Complainant After Protected Activity

Every significant adverse housing action taken against Mr. Pigors followed a protected activity:

1. Complaint about sewage → coerced lease with 114% rent increase
2. Ledger showed credit balance → fabricated new ledger showing arrears
3. Challenged billing → second eviction filed within 3 days
4. Formal sewage complaints → accelerated eviction proceedings
5. EGLE confirmed violations → forced removal and property destruction
6. Filed circuit court complaint → continuing interference with property sale

This temporal pattern satisfies the burden-shifting framework for establishing retaliatory intent.

### D. Forced Removal with Child Present

The forced removal on July 17, 2025 was conducted with knowledge that L.D.W. was present. The deliberate destruction of a family's home in the presence of a three-year-old child reflects either callous disregard for the well-being of a family with children or intentional targeting of that family because of its composition.

## V. CORPORATE STRUCTURE SUPPORTING DISCRIMINATION

The multi-entity corporate structure — involving at least six entities across four states, including two dissolved LLCs — served as a mechanism to:

1. **Shield discriminatory actors from accountability**: By shuffling tenancy between Homes of America LLC, Cricklewood MHP LLC, and Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC, Respondents obscured which entity was responsible for fair housing compliance.

2. **File void eviction actions**: Three separate eviction judgments totaling $9,551.13 were obtained by dissolved entities that lacked legal capacity to sue under MCL 450.4802.

3. **Divert payments**: A $1,300.26 rent check written to "Shady Oaks MHP" was endorsed and deposited by Partridge Securities/Partridge Equity Group — a separate entity with no disclosed relationship to the payee — then concealed from the ledger.

4. **Evade regulatory enforcement**: Operation through dissolved entities allowed Respondents to avoid licensing requirements and regulatory oversight.

## VII. IMPACT ON COMPLAINANT AND CHILD

The discriminatory conduct has caused severe and continuing harm:

- **Housing displacement**: Mr. Pigors and L.D.W. were forcibly removed from their home;
- **Financial devastation**: Loss of home equity ($60,000–$105,000), void judgments ($9,551.13), omitted payments ($6,085.99), unauthorized charges, and post-destruction billing ($7,633.13);
- **Emotional trauma**: L.D.W. witnessed the forced removal and destruction of the family home at age three;
- **Custodial disruption**: Loss of stable housing interfered with Mr. Pigors's custodial arrangements for L.D.W.;
- **Continuing victimization**: Respondents continue to block property sale and make false statements about Mr. Pigors's ownership rights, preventing recovery.

---

## VIII. CONCLUSION

The facts documented in this narrative establish that Respondents engaged in a systematic pattern of housing discrimination and retaliation against a single father and his minor child, in violation of the Fair Housing Act. The evidence demonstrates discriminatory terms and conditions of tenancy, retaliation for exercising legal rights, refusal of government housing assistance, and deliberate destruction of a family's home in the presence of a three-year-old child.

Complainant respectfully requests that HUD investigate this complaint, make a determination of reasonable cause, and pursue all appropriate remedies.
