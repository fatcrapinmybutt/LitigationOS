## V. BASIS OF COMPLAINT — DISCRIMINATORY ACTS

### CLAIM 1: Familial Status Discrimination (42 U.S.C. § 3604(b))

1. Complainant is a single father who resided at Lot 17 with his minor child, **L.D.W.** (age three at the time of the primary events). Complainant's family composition constitutes a protected class under the Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. § 3604(b), which prohibits discrimination based on familial status in the terms, conditions, or privileges of sale or rental of a dwelling.

2. Respondents subjected Complainant to discriminatory terms and conditions of tenancy, including:

   a. **Coerced 114% rent increase**: On March 26, 2024, Respondents coerced Complainant into signing a new lease with a different entity (Cricklewood MHP LLC) under express threat of eviction. Lot rent was increased from $325/month to $695/month — a 114% increase — without the 60-day notice required by Michigan law (MCL 125.2321). By February 2025, rent had further increased to $720/month, representing a cumulative 121% increase in approximately 14 months.

   b. **Disparate treatment targeting family**: Upon information and belief, Complainant — a single father with a young child exercising custody rights — was subjected to more aggressive rent increases, more frequent eviction filings, and more hostile management actions than similarly situated tenants without children. The presence of L.D.W. made Complainant more vulnerable to Respondents' coercive tactics because eviction would directly impact a minor child's housing stability.

   c. **Property destruction with child present**: On July 17, 2025, Respondents' agents forcibly removed Complainant's property from Lot 17 — drilling locks, destroying personal property, and posting a "FREE" sign on Complainant's titled manufactured home — while Complainant's three-year-old child, L.D.W., was present. This forced removal of a family with a young child, conducted without court supervision, constitutes discrimination against a household with children.

   d. **Refusal of government housing assistance**: Respondents admitted in court filings (Affirmative Defense #14 in their Answer filed January 9, 2025) that they maintain a policy of refusing government housing assistance payments. Respondents' bank rejected a Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (MDHHS) housing assistance payment of $1,962.45 intended for Complainant's lot rent. This policy disproportionately impacts families with children, who are the primary recipients of government housing assistance programs.

### CLAIM 2: Retaliation (42 U.S.C. § 3617)

3. Section 3617 of the Fair Housing Act makes it unlawful to "coerce, intimidate, threaten, or interfere with any person in the exercise or enjoyment of" fair housing rights. Respondents engaged in a systematic pattern of retaliation against Complainant for exercising his legal rights:

   a. **Retaliation for habitability complaints**: When Complainant reported raw sewage overflowing near his family's home (late 2023 through March 2025), Respondents responded with a coerced lease at a 114% rent increase rather than addressing the habitability conditions.

   b. **Retaliation for government agency involvement**: After Complainant's complaints triggered an EGLE inspection on March 20, 2025, which confirmed raw sewage discharge and resulted in Violation Notice VN-017235 on April 15, 2025, Respondents accelerated eviction proceedings rather than remediating the conditions.

   c. **Retaliation for exercising legal rights**: Complainant filed a verified nine-count complaint in Muskegon County Circuit Court (Case No. 2025-002760-CZ). In retaliation for this protected legal activity, Respondents:
      - Fabricated ledger entries to create false arrears (three irreconcilable ledger versions with a $2,241.82 discrepancy);
      - Filed three separate eviction actions on behalf of dissolved entities (totaling $9,551.13 in void judgments);
      - Forcibly removed Complainant and destroyed his property on July 17, 2025;
      - Continued to block Complainant's sale of his titled manufactured home by making false statements to prospective buyers.

   d. **Retaliatory timeline**:

   | Complainant's Protected Activity | Respondents' Retaliatory Response | Time Gap |
   |--------------------------------|----------------------------------|----------|
   | Late 2023: Reported sewage issues | Mar 2024: Coerced lease, 114% rent increase | ~3 months |
   | Aug 2024: Ledger showed credit balance (-$61.64) | Jan 2025: Fabricated ledger showing $2,180.18 due | ~5 months |
   | Jan 2025: Challenged billing discrepancies | Jan 11, 2025: Second eviction filed | 3 days |
   | Mar 7–17, 2025: Formal sewage complaints to Park | Jul 17, 2025: Forced removal and property destruction | ~4 months |
   | Mar 2025: EGLE inspection confirmed violations | Accelerated eviction; no remediation | Immediate |
   | Circuit Court complaint filed (2025-002760-CZ) | Continuing interference with property rights | Ongoing |

### CLAIM 3: Discriminatory Terms and Conditions (42 U.S.C. § 3604(b))

4. Respondents imposed discriminatory terms, conditions, and privileges of rental on Complainant:

   a. **Ledger manipulation**: Three irreconcilable versions of Complainant's payment ledger were generated from the same RentManager software:
      - Version A (August 22, 2024): Credit balance of -$61.64 (Complainant owed nothing);
      - Version B (January 8, 2025): Balance due of $2,180.18 (printed 3 days before second eviction filing);
      - Version C (submitted to court): Yet different amounts.
      The $2,241.82 discrepancy, combined with the omission of $6,085.99 in documented payments, demonstrates intentional manipulation of Complainant's tenancy records.

   b. **Omission of documented payments**: Respondents omitted at least $6,085.99 in documented payments:
      - MDHHS Housing Assistance #1: $1,962.45 (bank rejected);
      - MDHHS Housing Assistance #2: $768.48 (omitted from all ledgers);
      - TrueNorth CERA Grant: $2,054.80 (present in Version A, vanished from Version B);
      - Personal check: $1,300.26 (improperly endorsed by Partridge Securities, then concealed from ledger).

   c. **Utility fraud**: Unauthorized water/sewer submeters were installed without notice (May 2025). Billing records showed physically impossible identical readings (water consumed = sewer discharged) across three consecutive months, indicating fabricated meter data.

   d. **Post-eviction billing fraud**: On July 22, 2025, Respondents billed Complainant $7,633.13 for a lot from which his home had been forcibly removed five days earlier.

### CLAIM 4: Failure to Maintain Habitable Conditions (42 U.S.C. § 3604(b))

5. Respondents' failure to maintain the property disproportionately affected Complainant and his family:

   a. Beginning in late 2024, raw sewage overflowed from an interceptor tank near Lot 17, creating a severe public health hazard for Complainant and his minor child.

   b. On March 20, 2025, EGLE inspectors confirmed raw sewage discharging onto the ground surface. The facility was rated "NOT IN COMPLIANCE" for both Facility Site Review and Operation & Maintenance.

   c. On April 15, 2025, EGLE issued Violation Notice VN-017235 citing violations of Part 31 of the Natural Resources and Environmental Protection Act (MCL 324.3101 et seq.).

   d. As of April 24, 2025, Respondents had not contacted EGLE to discuss remediation — 17 days after the inspection and 9 days after the Violation Notice.

   e. In late June 2025, sewage leaked again during heavy rains, killing vegetation near occupied homes, demonstrating continuing failure to remediate.

   f. These uninhabitable conditions endangered the health and safety of Complainant's child, L.D.W., and were used as a pretext for adverse actions against Complainant when he complained about them.
