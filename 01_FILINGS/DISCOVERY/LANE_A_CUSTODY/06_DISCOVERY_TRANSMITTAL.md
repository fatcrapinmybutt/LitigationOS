## IMPORTANT NOTICES

### 1. Deemed Admissions (MCR 2.312(B)(1))
Each Request for Admission is **deemed admitted** if Defendant fails to serve a written answer or objection within twenty-eight (28) days. Deemed admissions are conclusively established for purposes of this action and may be used at trial. MCR 2.312(D).

### 2. Continuing Obligation (MCR 2.302(E))
These discovery requests are continuing in nature. If Defendant obtains additional information after the initial response, Defendant must promptly supplement responses.

### 3. ESI Preservation
Defendant is on notice of her obligation to preserve all electronically stored information (ESI) responsive to these requests. See enclosed Litigation Hold and Preservation Demand. Spoliation of ESI may result in sanctions under MCR 2.313(B) and adverse inference instructions per *Brenner v. Kolk*, 226 Mich App 149 (1997).

### 4. Discovery Not Filed with Court
Per MCR 2.302(F), discovery requests and responses are served on the parties but are NOT filed with the court unless attached to a motion or used at trial.

### 5. Motion to Compel
If responses are not timely served, or if responses are evasive or incomplete, Plaintiff will file a Motion to Compel pursuant to MCR 2.313(A) and will seek costs, fees, and sanctions.
