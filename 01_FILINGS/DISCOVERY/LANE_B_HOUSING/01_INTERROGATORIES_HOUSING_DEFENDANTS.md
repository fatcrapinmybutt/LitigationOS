## INTERROGATORIES — ALL DEFENDANTS

### CORPORATE STRUCTURE AND AUTHORITY

**INTERROGATORY NO. 1:** State the full legal name, state of incorporation or organization, registered agent, principal place of business, and current status (active, dissolved, revoked) of each Corporate Defendant. Identify the date of formation and, if dissolved, the date and manner of dissolution.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 2:** Describe in detail the corporate relationship between each Corporate Defendant, including: (a) ownership percentages; (b) shared officers, directors, or managers; (c) shared employees; (d) shared office space; (e) shared bank accounts; (f) shared insurance policies; and (g) any management agreements between entities.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 3:** Identify every entity that has owned, managed, or operated the Subject Property from January 1, 2018 through the present. For each entity, state: (a) the dates of ownership or management; (b) the nature of the entity's role; and (c) the individual(s) who directed operations.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 4:** For Shady Oaks Park MHP, LLC: (a) state the date of dissolution; (b) identify who authorized the dissolution; (c) describe what winding-up activities occurred; (d) explain how the Subject Property continued to be operated after dissolution; and (e) identify who authorized the filing of any lawsuit or legal action in the name of Shady Oaks Park MHP, LLC after its dissolution.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 5:** For Alden Global Capital, LLC: (a) describe the manufactured housing portfolio, including the total number of communities owned or managed; (b) identify the total number of residents affected; (c) identify any subsidiary or affiliated entity that has been the subject of a criminal conviction, civil enforcement action, or regulatory finding (including North Morris Estates LLC); and (d) describe the relationship between Alden Global Capital and each entity listed in response.

### LEDGER AND FINANCIAL RECORDS

**INTERROGATORY NO. 6:** Identify every ledger, account statement, or financial record maintained for Plaintiff's account (Lot 17) at the Subject Property. For each version: (a) identify the preparer; (b) the date prepared; (c) the software or system used; (d) the beginning balance; (e) the ending balance as of the most recent entry; and (f) explain any discrepancies between versions.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 7:** Explain in detail why at least three irreconcilable versions of Plaintiff's account ledger exist, showing materially different balances. For each discrepancy, state: (a) the amounts that differ; (b) the reason for the difference; and (c) which version you contend is accurate and why.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 8:** Identify every payment made by Plaintiff from January 1, 2020 through the present. For each payment: (a) the date received; (b) the amount; (c) the method of payment; (d) who received the payment; (e) which account it was credited to; and (f) if not credited, explain why.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 9:** Identify the total of $6,085.99 in payments that Plaintiff contends were omitted from or rejected by the ledger. For each disputed payment: (a) state whether the payment was received; (b) if received, explain why it does not appear on the ledger; (c) if rejected, state who authorized the rejection and why; and (d) explain the basis for any claim that Plaintiff owes a balance.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 10:** With respect to the $1,300.26 check Plaintiff wrote payable to "Shady Oaks MHP": (a) identify the entity that cashed or deposited the check; (b) identify the bank and account into which the check was deposited; (c) explain why Partridge Securities cashed a check made payable to a different entity; and (d) identify any authorization for Partridge Securities to receive funds on behalf of Shady Oaks Park MHP, LLC.

### LEASE AND TENANCY

**INTERROGATORY NO. 11:** Produce and describe every lease, rental agreement, or occupancy agreement between Plaintiff and any Defendant. For each agreement: (a) the date signed; (b) the parties; (c) the monthly rent; (d) any terms that changed from a prior agreement; and (e) the circumstances under which Plaintiff signed.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 12:** With respect to the lease modification that changed Plaintiff's rent from $325 to $695 per month: (a) state the date; (b) identify who presented the new lease to Plaintiff; (c) describe the circumstances, including any pressure, threats, or ultimatums; (d) state whether Plaintiff was given an opportunity to negotiate; (e) identify what consideration was provided for the rent increase; and (f) identify any community improvement or service that justified doubling the rent.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 13:** Identify every attempt by Plaintiff to sell his manufactured home at the Subject Property. For each attempt: (a) the date(s); (b) the prospective buyer(s); (c) the offered price; (d) whether any Defendant refused to approve the sale or transfer; (e) the stated reason for any refusal; and (f) identify the person who refused.

### MAINTENANCE, HABITABILITY, AND SAFETY

**INTERROGATORY NO. 14:** Identify every maintenance request, work order, or complaint submitted by Plaintiff during the period in question. For each: (a) the date submitted; (b) the nature of the request; (c) the date it was addressed, if ever; (d) who performed the work; and (e) the cost.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 15:** Describe every known sewage system issue, overflow, backup, or failure at the Subject Property during the period in question, including: (a) dates; (b) lots affected; (c) cause; (d) remediation performed; (e) EGLE reports or citations received; and (f) identify EGLE Violation Notice VN-017235 and the current status of remediation.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 16:** Describe every known health, safety, or habitability issue at the Subject Property during the period in question, including but not limited to: (a) water quality issues; (b) road conditions; (c) drainage problems; (d) pest infestations; (e) structural deficiencies in common areas; and (f) any government inspection findings.

### TAX ASSESSMENT AND PROPERTY VALUES

**INTERROGATORY NO. 17:** State the current assessed value, taxable value, and true cash value of the Subject Property. Identify any tax assessment appeals filed during the period in question, including: (a) the date; (b) the assessing entity; (c) the original assessment; (d) the requested value; (e) the outcome; and (f) identify any evidence of a 74% undervaluation of the property ($1.036M discrepancy).
