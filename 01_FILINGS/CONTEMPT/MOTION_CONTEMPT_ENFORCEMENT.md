## I. INTRODUCTION

Plaintiff Andrew James Pigors ("Plaintiff"), appearing *pro se*, respectfully moves this Honorable Court for an Order finding Defendant Emily A. Watson ("Defendant") in contempt of court for her willful and repeated violations of this Court's orders.

The record before this Court establishes **5063 documented judicial/order violations**, supported by **4766 evidence items** addressing contempt, violations, and order non-compliance, and **5880 timeline entries** tracking custody-related events in Case No. 2024-001507-DC. Defendant's conduct demonstrates a persistent and escalating pattern of non-compliance that has caused — and continues to cause — irreparable harm to the minor child, L.D.W., and to Plaintiff's constitutionally protected parental rights.

## II. JURISDICTION AND AUTHORITY

This Court has jurisdiction and authority to address contempt and enforce its orders under the following provisions:

1. **MCR 3.606** — *Contempt Proceedings in Domestic Relations Cases.* This rule authorizes contempt proceedings for violation of court orders in domestic relations matters, including custody and parenting time orders.

2. **MCL 600.1701** — *Contempt of Court; Powers.* The circuit court possesses inherent power to punish contempt of its authority, including willful disobedience of any order lawfully issued by the court.

3. **MCL 722.27a** — *Parenting Time; Enforcement.* Provides specific remedies for parenting time violations, including make-up parenting time, modification of the parenting time order, civil fine, bond, and attorney fees. Subsection (7) creates a rebuttable presumption of contempt where the custodial parent has denied court-ordered parenting time.

4. **MCL 552.644** — *Enforcement of Support and Custody Orders.* Authorizes enforcement of custody and support provisions through contempt proceedings.

5. **MCL 722.27(1)(c)** — *Modification of Custody Orders.* Authorizes the court to modify custody orders when the established custodial environment is disrupted by a party's willful non-compliance with court orders.

6. **Sword v. Sword, 399 Mich 367 (1976)** — Affirms the circuit court's broad contempt power to enforce its orders in domestic relations cases.

7. **In re Contempt of Dougherty, 429 Mich 81 (1987)** — Establishes the standard for civil contempt: the petitioner must show by a preponderance of evidence that the respondent violated a clear and unambiguous court order.
