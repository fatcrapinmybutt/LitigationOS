## SECTION 6: MCR 2.003 ANALYSIS

### Applicable Subsections

#### MCR 2.003(C)(1)(a) — Personal Bias or Prejudice

**Applicability:** If Cavan Berry is confirmed as McNeill's husband, then McNeill has a direct familial connection to a relative of the opposing party's domestic partner. This creates:
- Personal bias through family loyalty
- Knowledge of case facts through pillow-talk channels
- Motivation to protect Berry family interests

#### MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b) — Personal Knowledge of Disputed Facts

**Applicability:** The documented ex parte pattern (Berry voicemail pre-listened in chambers, temporal coordination of filings) suggests McNeill may have personal knowledge of disputed evidentiary facts obtained outside the proceedings.

#### MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b)(i) — Interest That Could Be Substantially Affected

**Applicability:** If McNeill's husband (Cavan Berry) is related to Ronald Berry (Emily Watson's partner), McNeill's family interests are directly affected by the case outcome. Favorable rulings for Watson benefit Ronald Berry, which benefits the Berry family, which benefits McNeill's household.

#### MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b)(ii) — Judge Previously Involved as Attorney

**Applicability:** While McNeill may not have represented any party, her **prior partnership** at Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill means she practiced law alongside the current **Chief Judge** (Hoopes). This professional entanglement means:
- Hoopes cannot independently review McNeill's conduct
- Hoopes cannot impartially reassign cases after McNeill's disqualification
- The appearance of self-dealing pervades the entire circuit

#### Canon 2 — Appearance of Impropriety

**Applicability:** Even absent confirmed bias, the sheer density of connections between the presiding judge, the chief judge, a district court judge, a magistrate, the FOC, and the opposing party's domestic partner creates an appearance of impropriety that a reasonable person would find disqualifying.

### Procedural Requirements Under MCR 2.003

1. **Timing:** Motion must be filed "before the hearing of the case begins" or upon discovery of grounds — MCR 2.003(D)(1)
2. **Affidavit:** Motion must be supported by affidavit of facts — MCR 2.003(D)(3)(a)
3. **Specificity:** Must state facts and reasons — MCR 2.003(D)(3)(a)
4. **Referral:** If not granted, referred to chief judge for decision — MCR 2.003(D)(3)(b)
   - **PROBLEM:** Chief Judge is Kenneth Hoopes (McNeill's former partner) → request reassignment outside 14th Circuit
5. **SCO Assignment:** If entire circuit is disqualified, SCAO assigns from another circuit — MCR 2.003(D)(4)
