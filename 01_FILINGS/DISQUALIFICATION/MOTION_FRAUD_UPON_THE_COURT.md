I. PRELIMINARY STATEMENT

    1. This Motion is brought under MCR 2.612(C)(1)(c), which
provides that on motion and just terms, the court may relieve a party
from a final judgment, order, or proceeding for "fraud (whether
heretofore denominated intrinsic or extrinsic), misrepresentation, or
other misconduct of an adverse party."

    2. Under MCR 2.612(C)(1)(c), there is no time bar for fraud upon
the court itself; a motion may be brought within a reasonable time.
MCR 2.612(C)(2). Where the fraud permeates the entire proceeding, the
reasonable-time standard applies rather than the one-year limit
applicable to subdivisions (a), (b), and (c). See Heugel v Heugel,
237 Mich App 471, 478; 603 NW2d 121 (1999).

    3. The fraud documented herein is not a single misstatement — it
is a systematic pattern of perjury, fabricated evidence, manufactured
police reports, and ex parte manipulation that has infected every
significant order entered in this proceeding. Relief is warranted
because the entire framework of judicial determinations rests on a
foundation of demonstrable falsehoods.


II. LEGAL STANDARD

    4. MCR 2.612(C)(1)(c) authorizes relief when an adverse party has
engaged in fraud, misrepresentation, or other misconduct that
prevented the moving party from fully and fairly presenting his case.
The moving party must demonstrate:

        (a) The opposing party engaged in fraud or misconduct;
        (b) The fraud or misconduct was material — i.e., it
            influenced the court's decision;
        (c) The moving party was prevented from fully and fairly
            presenting his case as a result; and
        (d) Relief is warranted in the interest of justice.

    See Heugel, 237 Mich App at 478-479; Sprague v Buhagiar, 213
Mich App 310, 313; 539 NW2d 587 (1995).

    5. "Fraud upon the court" is a subcategory that involves conduct
so egregious that it corrupts the judicial process itself, and is not
subject to the one-year time limit. Hazel Park v Highland Park, 480
Mich 105, 118; 745 NW2d 108 (2008). It encompasses situations where
a party has "sentiently set in motion some unconscionable scheme
calculated to interfere with the judicial system's ability
impartially to adjudicate a matter." Id. at 123.

    6. Perjury in judicial proceedings constitutes fraud upon the
court when it is material to the outcome. MCL 750.423 (perjury);
MCL 750.424 (subornation of perjury). See also Demarest v Rivard,
183 Mich App 319, 327; 454 NW2d 233 (1990) (fraud on the court
standard requires showing that the very temple of justice has been
defiled).

    7. A court has an independent duty to investigate and remedy fraud
upon the court once it is brought to the court's attention. In re
Hatcher, 443 Mich 426, 440; 505 NW2d 834 (1993) (duty of candor to
the tribunal).

IX. LEGAL ANALYSIS

A. Each False Allegation Meets the Elements of Perjury (MCL 750.423)

    54. MCL 750.423 provides that any person who, being lawfully
required to depose the truth in any proceeding in a court of justice,
willfully swears or affirms falsely in a matter material to the issue
or point in question, is guilty of perjury.

    55. Defendant's false allegations regarding arsenic, assault,
sexual assault, cocaine, methamphetamine, stalking, and mental
instability were:

        (a) Made under oath or in verified filings;
        (b) Material to the issues of custody, protection, and
            parenting time;
        (c) Demonstrably false, as shown by hospital records (arsenic),
            police findings (assault, stalking), absence of any report
            or investigation (sexual assault), absence of testing
            (cocaine), Plaintiff's negative drug tests
            (methamphetamine), and favorable HealthWest evaluation
            (mental instability); and
        (d) Willfully made — the coordinated timing, consistent
            pattern, and manufactured evidence chain demonstrate
            deliberate falsehood, not innocent mistake.

B. The Fraud Corrupted the Judicial Process

    56. This is not a case where a party embellished one aspect of
testimony. Defendant constructed an entire false narrative — a
fictional portrait of Plaintiff as a violent, drugged, sexually
abusive, stalking, mentally unstable individual — and presented it
to the Court through perjured testimony, manufactured police reports,
and ex parte submissions.

    57. The Court relied on this false narrative to enter protective
orders, restrict custody, suspend parenting time, impose drug testing,
order mental health evaluations, and ultimately incarcerate Plaintiff
for contempt of orders that were themselves fruits of fraud.

    58. "Fraud upon the court" exists when the misconduct "harms the
integrity of the judicial process, regardless of whether it can
technically be called 'extrinsic.'" Hazel Park, 480 Mich at 118.
The pattern here — perjury, manufactured evidence, ex parte
manipulation, and suppression of Plaintiff's ability to expose the
fraud — meets this standard.

C. Sanctions Are Warranted Under MCR 2.114

    59. MCR 2.114(D) provides that if a document is signed in
violation of this rule, the court shall impose upon the person who
signed it an appropriate sanction.

    60. MCR 2.114(E) provides that in addition to sanctions under
(D), a party who signs a document in violation of this rule shall be
subject to the payment of the reasonable expenses incurred by the
other party, including reasonable attorney fees.

    61. Defendant's verified filings containing the false allegations
documented above violated MCR 2.114(B), which requires that the
signer certify that the allegations have "evidentiary support" and
that denials are "warranted on the evidence."
