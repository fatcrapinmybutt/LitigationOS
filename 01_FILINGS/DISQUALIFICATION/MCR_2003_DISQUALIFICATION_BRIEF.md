## III. LEGAL STANDARD

21. MCR 2.003(C)(1) provides that a judge is disqualified when:

> **(a)** The judge is biased or prejudiced for or against a party or attorney;
> 
> **(b)** The judge, based on objective and reasonable perceptions, has either (i) a serious risk of actual bias impacting the due process rights of a party as enunciated in Caperton v Massey, 556 US 868 (2009), or (ii) has been a partner of a party, attorney for a party, or a member of a law firm representing a party within the preceding two years, or was associated with an attorney during the time the attorney represented a party in the matter.

22. MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b) further provides disqualification when the judge has:

> A personal bias or prejudice concerning a party or a party's lawyer, or personal knowledge of disputed evidentiary facts concerning the proceeding.

23. **Canon 2** of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct requires that a judge avoid the **appearance of impropriety** in all activities.

24. **Canon 3(C)** requires that a judge disqualify herself in any proceeding in which the judge's impartiality **might reasonably be questioned**, including but not limited to instances in which:
    - The judge has a personal bias or prejudice concerning a party;
    - The judge or the judge's spouse has a financial or other interest that could be affected by the outcome;
    - The judge or the judge's spouse is a party, officer, director, or trustee of a party.

25. The standard is objective: *"Would a reasonable person, knowing all the circumstances, conclude that the judge's impartiality might reasonably be questioned?"* See *Cain v Michigan Dep't of Corrections*, 451 Mich 470, 497 (1996).

## IV. ARGUMENT

### A. McNeill's Prior Partnership with Chief Judge Hoopes Creates a Structural Conflict

26. Judge McNeill and Chief Judge Kenneth Hoopes were **named partners** at the same law firm — Ladas, Hoopes & McNeill. This is not a casual professional acquaintance; named partners share:
    - Financial interests (profit-sharing, capital accounts)
    - Fiduciary duties to each other
    - Joint liability for firm obligations
    - Deep professional bonds forged over years of joint practice

27. Under MCR 2.003(D)(3)(b), when a judge does not grant a motion for disqualification, the motion is referred to the **chief judge** for decision. Here, the chief judge is McNeill's former law partner — Kenneth Hoopes. This structural defect means:
    - Hoopes cannot impartially decide a disqualification motion against his former partner
    - The entire internal review mechanism of the 14th Circuit is compromised
    - There is no independent judicial officer within the 14th Circuit to review McNeill's conduct

28. This situation is precisely what *Caperton v. A.T. Massey Coal Co.*, 556 US 868 (2009) warns against: a structural conflict so severe that the probability of actual bias rises to an unconstitutional level, violating the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment.

### B. The Berry Family Connection to the Judicial System

29. If Cavan Berry is confirmed as Judge McNeill's husband, then:
    - McNeill's **spouse** is a relative of **Ronald Berry**
    - Ronald Berry is the **domestic partner** of Defendant Emily A. Watson
    - McNeill's household has a direct familial connection to the opposing party's household
    - This triggers mandatory disqualification under Canon 3(C)(1)(d) — the judge's spouse has an interest that could be affected by the outcome

30. Even if the Cavan Berry–McNeill marriage connection requires further verification, the **shared Berry surname** between a judicial officer (Cavan Berry, Magistrate) and a case participant (Ronald Berry, Defendant's partner) demanded — at minimum — **disclosure and inquiry** by Judge McNeill. No such disclosure occurred.

### C. FOC Rusco's Co-Location with Cavan Berry Taints Recommendations

31. The Friend of the Court is designed to provide **independent** recommendations to the court. When the FOC case manager (Rusco) shares a building — and potentially daily professional contact — with a magistrate (Cavan Berry) who is connected to the opposing party's domestic partner, that independence is compromised.

32. Ms. Rusco's documented pattern of ex parte contacts (including the October 29, 2025 HealthWest call) raises additional concerns about the integrity of FOC recommendations in this case.

33. Plaintiff is entitled to FOC recommendations free from any taint of Berry family influence, actual or perceived.

### D. The Appearance of Impropriety Is Overwhelming

34. The reasonable-person standard of *Cain*, 451 Mich at 497, asks whether a disinterested observer, knowing all the facts, would harbor a significant doubt about the judge's impartiality. Consider what such an observer would know:

    - The presiding judge was a named partner with the chief judge
    - The chief judge's wife is also a judge, from the same firm
    - A magistrate sharing the surname of the opposing party's domestic partner works in the same building as the FOC case manager
    - The database of record documents a temporal coordination pattern between Watson filings, McNeill rulings, and Berry actions
    - None of these relationships were disclosed

35. No reasonable person could conclude that the appearance of impartiality has been maintained.
