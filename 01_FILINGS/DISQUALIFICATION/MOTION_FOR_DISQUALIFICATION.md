## IV. LEGAL STANDARD

7. Under **MCR 2.003(C)(1)**, a judge is disqualified when:
   - **(a)** The judge is biased or prejudiced for or against a party or attorney; or
   - **(b)** The judge, based on objective and reasonable perceptions, has either (i) a serious risk of actual bias impacting the due process rights of a party, or (ii) has failed to adhere to the appearance of impropriety standard as set forth in Canon 2 of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct.

8. The Michigan Supreme Court has held that a judge's conduct giving rise to an appearance of impropriety is sufficient grounds for disqualification, even absent actual bias. *In re Contempt of Henry*, 282 Mich App 656, 766 NW2d 44 (2009).

9. The test is "whether the conduct would create in reasonable minds a perception that the judge's ability to carry out judicial responsibilities with integrity, impartiality and competence is impaired." *Crampton v Dep't of State*, 395 Mich 347, 351 (1975).

10. In contempt proceedings resulting in incarceration, Michigan law requires heightened procedural protections. *In re Contempt of Dougherty*, 429 Mich 81, 95 (1987) (holding that a contemnor facing imprisonment must be afforded the right to counsel, notice of specific charges, and the opportunity to present a defense). Judge McNeill's imposition of 59 days of incarceration without these safeguards independently warrants disqualification.
