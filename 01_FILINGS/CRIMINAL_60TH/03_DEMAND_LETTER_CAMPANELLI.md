## LEGAL BASIS

Your failure to pursue readily available exculpatory evidence, communicate with your client, and prepare for an imminent trial constitutes **ineffective assistance of counsel** in violation of:

- **Sixth Amendment** to the United States Constitution (right to effective assistance of counsel);
- **Strickland v. Washington, 466 U.S. 668 (1984)** (two-prong test for ineffective assistance);
- **Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct 1.1** (competent representation);
- **MRPC 1.3** (diligent representation);
- **MRPC 1.4** (communication with client);
- **MRPC 1.16** (duty to withdraw when representation will result in violation of rules).

Under *Strickland*, your failure to investigate and pursue the body camera footage — evidence that directly supports the sole viable defense — falls below an objective standard of reasonableness and creates a reasonable probability that the outcome of trial will be affected.
