# MOTION TO SUBSTITUTE COUNSEL AND FOR CONTINUANCE OF TRIAL

---

**NOW COMES** Defendant, Andrew J. Pigors, and respectfully moves this Honorable Court for an Order substituting appointed counsel and continuing the jury trial currently scheduled for April 7, 2026, pursuant to **MCR 6.005**, **MCR 6.610**, and the **Sixth and Fourteenth Amendments** to the United States Constitution. In support thereof, Defendant states as follows:

&nbsp;

## I. COMPLETE BREAKDOWN IN ATTORNEY-CLIENT RELATIONSHIP

1. Current appointed counsel, **Amy P. Campanelli** of the Muskegon County Public Defender's Office, was appointed to represent Defendant in this matter.

2. Despite the scheduling of a **jury trial for April 7, 2026**, Ms. Campanelli has failed to:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. Communicate with Defendant about trial preparation, defense strategy, or case status;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Respond to Defendant's repeated attempts to contact her;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Pursue critical **exculpatory evidence** — specifically, body camera footage from the Muskegon County Jail showing a corrections officer's statement that five (5) inmates were planning to kill Defendant on December 7, 2025;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. File any discovery motions or evidence preservation demands;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;e. Meet with Defendant to discuss the case at any time since the January 21, 2026 pre-trial hearing;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f. Provide Defendant with copies of any discovery materials received from the prosecution.

3. On **March 19, 2026**, Defendant sent Ms. Campanelli a written demand for immediate action, requiring a response within 48 hours. As of the date of this filing, Defendant has received no response.

4. The attorney-client relationship has completely broken down. Defendant has no confidence that Ms. Campanelli will adequately represent his interests at trial.

&nbsp;

## II. INEFFECTIVE ASSISTANCE OF COUNSEL

5. Body camera footage exists from the Muskegon County Jail, recorded on **December 7, 2025**, in which a corrections officer states words to the effect that **"5 guys were getting ready to kill him"** — referring to Defendant.

6. This footage is **directly exculpatory**: it establishes that Defendant faced an imminent threat of deadly violence from multiple inmates, supporting Defendant's claim of **self-defense** under **MCL 780.972** (Michigan Self-Defense Act).

7. Under **Brady v. Maryland, 373 U.S. 83 (1963)**, the prosecution has a constitutional obligation to disclose this footage. Under **MCR 6.201**, defense counsel has a professional obligation to pursue it.

8. Ms. Campanelli's failure to file a motion to compel production of this exculpatory evidence falls below the objective standard of reasonable professional assistance required by **Strickland v. Washington, 466 U.S. 668 (1984)**.

9. There is a reasonable probability that the outcome of trial would be different if this body camera footage were obtained and presented to the jury — satisfying the prejudice prong of *Strickland*.

10. Additionally, the primary attacker in the December 7 incident has **two (2) felony methamphetamine charges** that are material for impeachment purposes. Counsel has not investigated or pursued this information.

&nbsp;

## III. CONTEXTUAL PATTERN OF INADEQUATE REPRESENTATION

11. Defendant is currently a party to family court proceedings in the **14th Circuit Court** (Case No. 2024-001507-DC) before Judge Jenny L. McNeill, in which Defendant's parental rights have been suspended for more than **223 consecutive days** without a meaningful hearing.

12. The December 7, 2025 incident — for which Defendant is now charged — occurred **while Defendant was incarcerated** on a 45-day sentence imposed by Judge McNeill in the family court matter.

13. Defendant was arrested on the current charge on **December 22, 2025** — a full **15 days after the incident** — raising questions about the timing and motivation of the charge.

14. Defendant reasonably fears that the failure of appointed counsel to adequately represent him in this criminal matter is part of a broader systemic pattern of inadequate legal representation that has characterized his experience across multiple courts.

&nbsp;

## IV. CONSTITUTIONAL RIGHT TO EFFECTIVE COUNSEL

15. The **Sixth Amendment** to the United States Constitution, made applicable to the states through the **Fourteenth Amendment**, guarantees every criminal defendant the right to the effective assistance of counsel. *See Gideon v. Wainwright*, 372 U.S. 335 (1963).

16. Where, as here, appointed counsel has demonstrably failed to investigate exculpatory evidence, communicate with the defendant, and prepare for an imminent trial, the constitutional guarantee of effective assistance is not being met.

17. Under **MCR 6.005(E)**, the court must ensure that a defendant's right to counsel is protected. Where a legitimate basis for substitution exists, the court should appoint new counsel to ensure the defendant receives constitutionally adequate representation.

&nbsp;

## V. NEED FOR CONTINUANCE

18. If the Court grants substitution of counsel, a **continuance of the April 7, 2026 trial** will be necessary to allow new counsel adequate time to:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. Review the case file and all discovery materials;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. File and litigate the emergency motion to compel production of body camera footage;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Subpoena the corrections officer who made the recorded statement;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. Investigate the criminal backgrounds of the inmates involved in the December 7 incident;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;e. Meet with Defendant and prepare a defense strategy.

19. The interest in a fair trial — supported by effective counsel and complete evidence — outweighs any interest in proceeding on the current trial date.

&nbsp;

## VI. RELIEF REQUESTED

**WHEREFORE**, Defendant respectfully requests that this Honorable Court enter an Order:

1. **Substituting counsel** — appointing a new public defender or allowing Defendant to retain private counsel, in place of Amy P. Campanelli;

2. **Continuing the trial** currently scheduled for April 7, 2026, to a date that allows new counsel adequate preparation time (no less than 60 days);

3. **Ordering production** of all body camera footage from the Muskegon County Jail from December 7, 2025, and ordering preservation of all related records;

4. **Scheduling a hearing** on this motion at the earliest available date;

5. For such other and further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.
