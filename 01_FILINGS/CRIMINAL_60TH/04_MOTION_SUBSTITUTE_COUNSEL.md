## II. LEGAL ARGUMENT

### A. Defendant Has a Sixth Amendment Right to Effective Counsel

10. The Sixth Amendment guarantees every criminal defendant the right to the assistance of counsel. The right to counsel necessarily includes the right to *effective* assistance of counsel. *Strickland v. Washington*, 466 US 668, 686 (1984).

11. Counsel is ineffective when performance falls below an objective standard of reasonableness AND there is a reasonable probability that, but for counsel's unprofessional errors, the result of the proceeding would have been different. *Strickland*, 466 US at 687-88.

12. Complete failure to communicate with a client in the weeks before trial, failure to pursue documented exculpatory evidence, and failure to respond to the client's written demands all fall below any objective standard of reasonable attorney performance.

### B. The Attorney-Client Relationship Has Broken Down Completely

13. Michigan courts recognize that when there is an irreconcilable conflict between attorney and client — particularly one that renders effective communication impossible — the court must consider substituting counsel. *People v. Mack*, 190 Mich App 7, 14 (1991).

14. Here, the attorney-client relationship has broken down entirely. Defendant cannot trust his appointed counsel to pursue critical exculpatory evidence or to communicate about trial preparation. This is not a minor disagreement — it is a complete failure of the attorney-client relationship on the eve of trial.

### C. Continuance Is Necessary to Protect Defendant's Rights

15. If this Court substitutes counsel, a continuance of the April 7, 2026, trial date is necessary to allow new counsel to:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. Review the case file and all discovery;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. File emergency motions to compel production of body camera footage;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Subpoena the corrections officer witness; and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. Prepare an adequate defense.

16. Without a continuance, any newly appointed counsel would be forced to try this case without adequate preparation time, perpetuating the same constitutional problem.

17. The prosecution cannot claim prejudice from a continuance where the delay is caused by the constitutional failure of the defendant's own appointed counsel.
