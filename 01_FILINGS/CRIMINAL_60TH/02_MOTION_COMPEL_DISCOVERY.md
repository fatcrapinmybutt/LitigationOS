## III. LEGAL ARGUMENT

### A. The Prosecution Has a Constitutional Duty to Disclose This Evidence Under Brady

12. The Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment requires the prosecution to disclose all material evidence favorable to the accused. *Brady v. Maryland*, 373 US 83, 87 (1963).

13. Evidence is material if there is a **reasonable probability** that, had the evidence been disclosed, the result of the proceeding would have been different. *United States v. Bagley*, 473 US 667, 682 (1985).

14. The body camera footage showing a corrections officer identifying five (5) inmates as planning to kill Defendant is unquestionably material. It goes directly to the central defense in this case — self-defense.

15. Brady obligations extend to **all** exculpatory evidence within the prosecution's possession or that which can be obtained through reasonable diligence, including records in the possession of law enforcement agencies. *Kyles v. Whitley*, 514 US 419, 437 (1995).

16. The Muskegon County Jail and Muskegon County Sheriff's Office are arms of the same government as the prosecution. Their records are within the prosecution's constructive possession.

### B. Michigan Court Rule 6.201 Requires Disclosure

17. **MCR 6.201(B)(1)** requires the prosecution to disclose, upon request, "any written or recorded statements, including electronically recorded statements, by a defendant."

18. **MCR 6.201(B)(3)** requires disclosure of "all exculpatory information or evidence known to the prosecutor or that which could be discovered through the exercise of due diligence."

19. Body camera footage constitutes a "recorded statement" and is subject to mandatory disclosure under MCR 6.201.

### C. Defendant Has the Right to Present a Complete Defense

20. The Sixth Amendment guarantees Defendant the right to present a complete defense, including the right to compel the production of evidence necessary to that defense. *Washington v. Texas*, 388 US 14, 19 (1967); *Crane v. Kentucky*, 476 US 683, 690 (1986).

21. Suppression of this body camera footage — whether intentional or through negligence — violates Defendant's right to a fair trial.

### D. Immediate Production Is Necessary Given Trial Date

22. Trial is scheduled for April 7, 2026. Defendant needs this footage at minimum **7 days before trial** to allow adequate preparation.

23. Courts have the authority and obligation to order production of Brady material on an expedited basis when trial is imminent. *See* MCR 6.004(C).
