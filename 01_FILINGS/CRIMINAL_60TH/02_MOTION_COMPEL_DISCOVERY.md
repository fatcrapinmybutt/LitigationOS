# DEFENDANT'S PRO SE SUPPLEMENTAL MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY OF EXCULPATORY BODY CAMERA FOOTAGE

---

**NOW COMES** Defendant, Andrew J. Pigors, appearing *pro se* in supplement to appointed counsel, and pursuant to **MCR 6.201**, **MCR 6.610(E)**, **Brady v. Maryland, 373 U.S. 83 (1963)**, and the **Fifth, Sixth, and Fourteenth Amendments** to the United States Constitution, and respectfully moves this Honorable Court to compel immediate production of exculpatory body camera footage and related records. In support thereof, Defendant states as follows:

&nbsp;

## I. INTRODUCTION

1. Defendant Andrew J. Pigors is charged with Assault & Battery (Misdemeanor) arising from an incident at the Muskegon County Jail on **December 7, 2025**.

2. Defendant was incarcerated at the Muskegon County Jail at the time of the incident, serving a 45-day sentence imposed by Judge Jenny L. McNeill in **Case No. 2024-001507-DC** (14th Circuit Court, Family Division).

3. **Critical exculpatory evidence exists**: A corrections officer, captured on body camera, stated words to the effect that **"5 guys were getting ready to kill him"** — referring to Defendant — on or about December 7, 2025, prior to the incident for which Defendant is now charged.

4. This body camera footage constitutes **Brady material** that is essential to Defendant's self-defense claim. Trial is scheduled for **April 7, 2026** — less than three (3) weeks from this filing.

&nbsp;

## II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

5. On or about December 7, 2025, while Defendant was incarcerated at the Muskegon County Jail, Defendant was confronted by multiple inmates. Defendant acted in self-defense to protect his life and physical safety.

6. A corrections officer witnessed or became aware of the threat against Defendant and made a recorded statement — captured on body-worn camera — indicating that **five (5) inmates were preparing to assault or kill Defendant**.

7. Despite this recorded evidence of a deadly threat necessitating self-defense, Defendant was charged with Assault & Battery on **December 22, 2025** — a full **15 days after the incident**. The delay in charging further supports the inference that the initial assessment by jail staff recognized Defendant's actions as defensive.

8. Defendant was released on bond on **December 25, 2025**. A pre-trial hearing was held on **January 21, 2026**.

9. The primary attacker involved in the December 7 incident has **two (2) felony methamphetamine charges**, which are relevant to impeachment and to establishing the threatening nature of the individuals confronting Defendant.

10. Appointed counsel, Amy P. Campanelli, has not filed any discovery motions related to this critical exculpatory evidence despite repeated requests from Defendant.

&nbsp;

## III. LEGAL AUTHORITY

### A. Brady v. Maryland — Obligation to Disclose Exculpatory Evidence

11. Under **Brady v. Maryland, 373 U.S. 83 (1963)**, the prosecution is constitutionally required to disclose all evidence favorable to the defense, including evidence that is material to guilt or punishment.

12. Body camera footage showing a corrections officer's statement that five inmates were planning to kill Defendant is **directly exculpatory** — it establishes the existence of an imminent deadly threat that justified Defendant's use of force in self-defense.

13. Suppression of this footage would violate Defendant's right to due process under the **Fourteenth Amendment**. *See also Giglio v. United States*, 405 U.S. 150 (1972); *Kyles v. Whitley*, 514 U.S. 419 (1995).

### B. MCR 6.201 — Discovery in Criminal Cases

14. Under **MCR 6.201(A)**, upon request, the prosecutor must provide the defendant with all information and evidence known to the prosecutor that tends to negate the guilt of the defendant.

15. Under **MCR 6.201(B)**, the court may, upon motion, order the prosecutor to provide additional discovery upon a showing of good cause.

16. Body camera footage establishing a deadly threat against Defendant constitutes the strongest possible showing of good cause and materiality.

### C. MCR 6.610(E) — Misdemeanor Discovery

17. Under **MCR 6.610(E)**, the discovery provisions of MCR 6.201 apply to misdemeanor proceedings in district court. Defendant is entitled to all exculpatory evidence regardless of the classification of the charge.

### D. Self-Defense Under Michigan Law

18. Under **MCL 780.972** (Michigan Self-Defense Act), an individual may use force or deadly force against another individual if the individual **honestly and reasonably believes** that the use of force is necessary to defend himself or another individual from the imminent use of unlawful force.

19. A corrections officer's contemporaneous recorded statement that five inmates were planning to kill Defendant is **powerful corroborating evidence** that Defendant's belief in the imminence and severity of the threat was both honest and reasonable.

&nbsp;

## IV. GROUNDS FOR EMERGENCY RELIEF

20. Trial is scheduled for **April 7, 2026** — less than three (3) weeks from this filing. Without the body camera footage, Defendant cannot present a complete defense.

21. Appointed counsel has failed to pursue this critical evidence despite Defendant's repeated requests, necessitating this pro se supplemental motion.

22. There is a substantial risk that body camera footage may be **overwritten, deleted, or destroyed** pursuant to routine retention policies if a preservation order is not entered immediately. Standard jail footage retention policies typically range from **30 to 90 days**.

23. The footage has been in the prosecution's constructive control since December 7, 2025 — more than **three (3) months** — and has not been disclosed to the defense.

&nbsp;

## V. RELIEF REQUESTED

**WHEREFORE**, Defendant respectfully requests that this Honorable Court enter an Order:

1. **Compelling the People** to produce, within seven (7) days, all body camera footage from the Muskegon County Jail recorded on December 7, 2025, between the hours of 12:00 AM and 11:59 PM, relating to any incident involving Defendant Andrew J. Pigors;

2. **Compelling the People** to produce all incident reports, use-of-force reports, and internal memoranda related to any incident involving Defendant on December 7, 2025;

3. **Ordering the Muskegon County Jail** to immediately preserve all body camera footage, surveillance footage, incident reports, and related records from December 7, 2025;

4. **Ordering disclosure** of the identity of the corrections officer who made the recorded statement regarding the threat against Defendant;

5. **Continuing the trial** if the requested footage is not produced at least seven (7) calendar days before the April 7, 2026 trial date, to ensure Defendant has adequate time to review and incorporate this evidence into his defense;

6. For such other and further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.
