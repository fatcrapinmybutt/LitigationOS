## DEFENSE THEORY: SELF-DEFENSE (MCL 780.972)

**One-Sentence Summary:** Andrew Pigors was forced to defend himself against an imminent, deadly attack by five inmates in the Muskegon County Jail on December 7, 2025, as confirmed by a corrections officer's body camera statement that "5 guys were getting ready to kill him."

---

## KEY LEGAL STANDARD

### Michigan Self-Defense Act — MCL 780.972

An individual who has not committed a crime at the time he uses force may use force against another individual if the individual **honestly and reasonably believes** that the use of force is necessary to defend himself from the **imminent use of unlawful force** by another individual.

**Elements the defense must establish:**

| # | Element | Evidence |
|---|---------|----------|
| 1 | Defendant had not committed a crime at the time force was used | Defendant was an inmate lawfully housed; no prior infraction |
| 2 | Defendant honestly believed force was necessary | 5 inmates were planning to kill him — corrections officer confirmed |
| 3 | Defendant's belief was reasonable | Officer's body camera statement confirms deadly threat was real |
| 4 | The threat was imminent | Attack was actively being planned/executed |
| 5 | Force used was proportional to the threat | 5-on-1 deadly threat justified defensive force |

## TRIAL OUTLINE

### Opening Statement (5-7 minutes)

**Theme:** "Andrew Pigors was locked in a cage with five men who wanted to kill him. A corrections officer knew it. The body camera captured it. Andrew had no choice but to defend his life."

**Key Points:**

1. On December 7, 2025, Andrew was in the Muskegon County Jail — not by choice, but because a family court judge sentenced him to 45 days for a civil contempt matter.

2. Five inmates targeted Andrew. A corrections officer, captured on body camera, stated that "5 guys were getting ready to kill him."

3. When the attack came, Andrew did the only thing any reasonable person would do — he defended his life.

4. The prosecution charged Andrew with Assault & Battery — but they waited 15 days. Why? Because even the jail staff initially recognized this was self-defense.

5. The body camera footage proves it. The corrections officer's own words prove it. Andrew Pigors is not guilty.

### Prosecution's Case — Anticipated and Rebuttal

### Closing Argument (5-7 minutes)

**Theme:** "The evidence is clear. Andrew Pigors was the victim, not the aggressor. He defended himself because five men wanted to kill him — and a corrections officer said so on camera."

**Key Points:**

1. The body camera footage is the most important evidence in this case. A corrections officer — a neutral party with no reason to lie — stated that 5 inmates were planning to kill Andrew.

2. Under Michigan law (MCL 780.972), a person has the right to use force when they honestly and reasonably believe it is necessary to defend themselves from imminent unlawful force.

3. No reasonable person, facing 5 attackers intent on killing them, would simply accept their fate. Andrew did what any of you would do — he fought for his life.

4. The 15-day delay in charging tells you everything. Even the authorities initially recognized this was self-defense. Something changed — and that something wasn't the facts.

5. The prosecution has not and cannot overcome the self-defense evidence. Find Andrew Pigors not guilty.
