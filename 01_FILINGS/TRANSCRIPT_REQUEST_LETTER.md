## THE PROBLEM
Muskegon County 14th Circuit uses **exclusively 3rd-party court reporters** for transcripts. There is a specific court form to request them. Cost is **$1.00/page**. With 10 hearings, estimated transcripts could be 500-2,000+ pages = **$500-$2,000+** — unaffordable.

## THREE ALTERNATIVE PATHS

---

### PATH A: MC 20 FEE WAIVER FOR TRANSCRIPT COSTS (Best option — try first)

**Authority:** MCR 2.002; MCR 7.210(B)(2)

Under MCR 2.002, if the court grants a fee waiver (MC 20), transcript costs can be ordered paid at public expense. The 2025 amendment to MCR 2.002 expanded coverage for transcript costs.

**Steps:**
1. File MC 20 Fee Waiver at 14th Circuit (with disqualification motion — same trip)
2. On the MC 20, specifically request waiver of **transcript preparation costs**
3. Cite MCR 7.210(B)(2): "If the appellant is unable to pay for the transcript..."
4. Cite the two incarcerations (SC#5 + SC#6/7) causing job/housing loss as proof of indigency
5. If granted, the court orders the 3rd-party reporter to prepare transcripts at public expense
6. If denied at trial court level → **appeal to COA** (see Path C below)

**Key language for MC 20:**
> "I am unable to pay transcript costs of approximately $1.00/page for 10 hearing
> transcripts needed for my appeal (COA 366810). I was incarcerated twice in
> contempt proceedings arising from this case (14 days Nov 2024, 45 days Nov 2025),
> causing me to lose both my employment and housing on both occasions. I request
> the court order transcripts prepared at public expense per MCR 7.210(B)(2)."

### PATH B: SETTLED STATEMENT OF FACTS (Bypass transcripts entirely)

**Authority:** MCR 7.210(B)(2); MCR 7.212(C)(7)

If transcripts cannot be obtained (cost or otherwise), Michigan allows an appellant to file a **"Settled Statement of Facts"** — a written summary of what happened at each hearing, certified by the trial court.

**Steps:**
1. Prepare a written "Proposed Statement of Proceedings" for each hearing
   - Summarize testimony, rulings, objections, and evidence from your notes/memory
   - Use evidence already in litigation_context.db to support the factual narrative
2. File a **Motion to Settle Record** in the trial court (14th Circuit)
   - Cite MCR 7.210(B)(2)
   - State that transcripts are unavailable due to cost
   - Attach Proposed Statement of Facts for each hearing
3. Serve the Proposed Statement on Emily Watson / her counsel
4. Opposing party has **14 days** to object or propose amendments
5. Trial court holds a hearing (if disputed) and settles the statement
6. The **settled statement replaces the transcript** in the appellate record

**Advantages:**
- **FREE** — no transcript costs
- You control the narrative (within bounds of accuracy)
- DB has 308K evidence quotes + 221 docket events — strong factual foundation
- Already have detailed summaries of each hearing from your notes

**Risks:**
- McNeill (or visiting judge) settles the statement — may alter your version
- Opposing party can challenge your characterization
- COA may give less weight than certified transcripts

**Template for Proposed Statement:**
```
PROPOSED STATEMENT OF PROCEEDINGS
Hearing Date: 2025-08-06
Case No. 2024-001507-DC
Before: Hon. Jenny L. McNeill

1. [Summary of testimony and evidence presented]
2. [Rulings made from the bench]
3. [Objections raised and how ruled upon]
4. [Orders entered as a result of the hearing]

Respectfully submitted by Andrew J. Pigors, Appellant
```

### PATH C: COA MOTION FOR TRANSCRIPTS AT PUBLIC EXPENSE (Nuclear option)

**Authority:** MCR 7.210(B); *Griffin v. Illinois*, 351 U.S. 12 (1956); *Mayer v. City of Chicago*, 404 U.S. 189 (1971)

If the trial court denies the MC 20 for transcript costs, file a **motion directly with the Court of Appeals** requesting transcripts be provided at public expense.

**Constitutional argument:**
- *Griffin v. Illinois* (1956): The U.S. Supreme Court held that states cannot deny appellate review to indigent defendants solely because they cannot afford a transcript
- *Mayer v. City of Chicago* (1971): Extended Griffin — a state must provide an indigent appellant with a record of sufficient completeness for effective appellate review
- 14th Amendment Due Process + Equal Protection: Conditioning access to appellate review on ability to pay for transcripts creates an unconstitutional wealth-based barrier

**Steps:**
1. File MC 20 at trial court (Path A) — get denied (if they deny)
2. File **Motion for Transcripts at Public Expense** in COA 366810
3. Attach:
   - Denied MC 20 order from trial court
   - Affidavit of indigency (same as MC 20 financial info)
   - List of hearings and estimated transcript costs
   - Citation to Griffin/Mayer/14th Amendment
4. COA can **order** the trial court to provide transcripts at public expense

**Key language:**
> "Appellant moves this Court for an order directing that hearing transcripts be
> prepared at public expense. The trial court denied Appellant's MC 20 fee waiver
> for transcript costs. Appellant was incarcerated twice on contempt sentences
> arising from this case, losing employment and housing both times. Under Griffin
> v. Illinois, 351 U.S. 12 (1956) and Mayer v. City of Chicago, 404 U.S. 189
> (1971), denying an indigent appellant access to transcripts necessary for
> meaningful appellate review violates the Equal Protection and Due Process
> Clauses of the Fourteenth Amendment."
