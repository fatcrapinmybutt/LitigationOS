#### 2. [ ] Brief/Memorandum in Support

**Required Analysis:**

- [ ] **Jurisdiction** — Federal question, 28 U.S.C. § 1331; civil rights, 28 U.S.C. § 1343
- [ ] **Judicial Immunity Analysis:**
  - General rule: judges immune from § 1983 damages (Mireles v. Waco, 502 U.S. 9 (1992))
  - Exception 1: acts not taken in judicial capacity (Forrester v. White, 484 U.S. 219 (1988))
  - Exception 2: acts taken in complete absence of jurisdiction (Stump v. Sparkman, 435 U.S. 349 (1978))
  - **Exception 3 (primary theory): conspiracy with private parties strips immunity** (Dennis v. Sparks, 449 U.S. 24 (1980); Tower v. Glover, 467 U.S. 914 (1984))
- [ ] **Conspiracy Theory (Dennis v. Sparks):**
  - Private party (Watson/Berry) corrupted judicial proceedings
  - Judge (McNeill) acted in concert with private parties
  - Resulted in deprivation of federal rights
  - Both state and private actors liable under § 1983
- [ ] **Monell Municipal Liability Theory:**
  - County of Muskegon as municipality
  - Policy or custom of inadequate judicial oversight
  - Failure to train/supervise regarding conflict-of-interest protocols
  - Deliberate indifference to constitutional violations
  - *Bd. of County Comm'rs v. Brown*, 520 U.S. 397 (1997)
- [ ] **Damages Analysis:**
  - Compensatory: loss of parental relationship (225+ days separation), emotional harm
  - Punitive: willful, wanton disregard for constitutional rights
  - Cite *Carey v. Piphus*, 435 U.S. 247 (1978) — damages framework for § 1983
