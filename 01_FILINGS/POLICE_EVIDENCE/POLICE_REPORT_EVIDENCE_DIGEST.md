## VI. EVIDENTIARY VALUE

### A. MRE 803(8) — Public Records Exception

Police reports qualify as public records under MRE 803(8), admissible as an exception to the hearsay rule. Reports prepared by law enforcement officers in the regular course of duty carry presumptive reliability.

**Application:** Each police report documenting unfounded allegations or exculpatory findings is admissible to demonstrate the pattern of false reporting by Defendant Emily A. Watson.

### B. MRE 801(d)(2) — Admissions by Party-Opponent

Statements made by Defendant Emily A. Watson to police officers, as documented in these reports, constitute admissions by a party-opponent and are not hearsay under MRE 801(d)(2). This includes:
- Statements made during 911 calls
- Written statements provided to officers
- Verbal allegations recorded in officer narratives
- Contradictions between different reports

### C. MRE 404(b) — Pattern Evidence

The cumulative pattern of police reports supports admission under MRE 404(b) to show scheme, plan, or pattern of conduct — specifically the pattern of weaponizing law enforcement contact against Plaintiff in connection with custody proceedings.

### D. Authentication Requirements — MRE 901

Each police report must be authenticated per MRE 901:
- Official records via MRE 901(b)(7) — certified copies from the agency
- Self-authentication under MRE 902(4) — certified copies of public records
- FOIA requests should be filed for any reports not already in possession
