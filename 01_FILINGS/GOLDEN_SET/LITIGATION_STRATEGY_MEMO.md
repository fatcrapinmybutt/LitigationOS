# 2. STRENGTHS & WEAKNESSES ANALYSIS

## MOTION FOR RECONSIDERATION (MCR 2.119(F))

### Strengths
- **HealthWest evidence is devastating.** Every clinical domain scored at zero. This is not a marginal case — it's categorical exoneration.
- **Court ordered the very evaluation** that now contradicts its own orders. Hard for court to disregard its own expert.
- **Pre-planned ex parte filing** (Albert Watson's police report admission) is a smoking gun.
- **200+ days without hearing** creates compelling urgency.
- **Well-established legal standard** — palpable error is clear.

### Weaknesses
- **21-day filing deadline** may have passed. If so, the MCR 2.612(C)(1) filing is the fallback.
- **Same judge** who entered the orders is being asked to reverse them — institutional resistance.
- **Second HealthWest evaluation** had elevated scores (Psychosis = 2) — opposing counsel will focus on this.
- **Court may characterize Andrew's statements** at HealthWest (FBI, "30 people chasing," etc.) as concerning regardless of clinical scores.
- **Judge McNeill may have already formed an opinion** after 2+ years of proceedings.

### Rebuttal Preparation
- Second evaluation scores rose ONLY after court's biasing letter — argue confirmation bias.
- "Rule Out" is explicitly NOT a confirmed diagnosis — clinician herself couldn't confirm.
- The 48-minute screening was "brief screening to non-inpatient program" — not comprehensive psychiatric evaluation.

## MOTION TO SET ASIDE EX PARTE ORDERS (MCR 2.612(C)(1))

### Strengths
- **Six independent grounds** — any one is sufficient. Redundancy is strategic advantage.
- **Void order ground (d) is dispositive** — void orders MUST be vacated, no discretion.
- **Fraud ground (c)** has direct evidence: Albert Watson's police report admission.
- **No time limit** on void order challenge — MCR 2.612(C)(1)(d) has no deadline.
- **Constitutional violations** are well-documented and multi-layered.
- **Statistical evidence** (44% ex parte rate, all against Father) is powerful pattern evidence.

### Weaknesses
- **Scope is aggressive** — challenging 52+ orders may seem overreaching.
- **Court may distinguish** between different ex parte orders, granting relief on some but not others.
- **"Voidness" argument** is strong but courts sometimes find procedural defects were "harmless error."
- **Complete docket may reveal** orders that were entered with some form of notice, weakening the blanket challenge.

### Rebuttal Preparation
- Even partial relief is a win — if court vacates August 8, 2025 orders, primary objective achieved.
- Harmless error doctrine doesn't apply to constitutional violations — cite *Santosky*, *Troxel*.
- Request docket review as part of the motion to nail down exact orders.

## MOTION TO APPOINT GAL (MCL 722.24)

### Strengths
- **Very hard to oppose.** Mother opposing a GAL looks like she's hiding something.
- **MCL 722.24(1) uses "shall"** — mandatory when child's interests aren't adequately protected.
- **Removes the "he said/she said" dynamic** — neutral investigator is game-changer.
- **GAL can investigate alienation** independently — 519 findings across 225 files.
- **Low-risk filing** — even if denied, it demonstrates Father's concern for child's welfare.
- **Sets up future appeals** — if court denies GAL despite documented need, it's appellate error.

### Weaknesses
- **Cost** — GAL fees may be substantial. If assessed against Father, financial burden.
- **GAL could find unfavorably** — investigation is independent; results aren't guaranteed.
- **Delay** — GAL investigation takes 30–60 days, during which separation continues.
- **Judge may appoint a friendly GAL** — risk of capture in a small jurisdiction.

### Rebuttal Preparation
- Accept cost risk — the investigation will favor Father if evidence is as documented.
- Push for GAL from outside Muskegon County to avoid local relationships.
- Argue delay is acceptable ONLY if interim supervised visitation is granted simultaneously.

## MOTION FOR SUPERVISED EXCHANGE (MCL 722.27a)

### Strengths
- **Nearly impossible to deny** if clinical evidence shows no danger. What is the basis for denying even supervised contact?
- **Demonstrates reasonableness** — strengthens all other filings and future appellate record.
- **Graduated schedule** shows Father is not demanding everything at once.
- **MCL 722.27a(8)** — "court shall not deny parenting time for the purpose of punishing a parent" — forces court to articulate a non-punitive reason.
- **Child development argument** is strong — more than 200 consecutive days during ages 2–3 is demonstrably harmful.

### Weaknesses
- **Court may impose very restrictive supervised conditions** that are difficult/expensive to comply with.
- **Some courts use supervised visitation as a permanent holding pattern** — never advancing phases.
- **If Andrew refuses any proposed conditions**, it undermines the "reasonableness" narrative.

### Rebuttal Preparation
- Accept ANY supervised conditions offered — the goal is contact, not perfect terms.
- Build automatic phase advancement into proposed order so court must act to prevent progression.
- If court denies supervised visitation, immediately appeal — denial of even supervised contact with zero clinical risk is strong COA issue.
