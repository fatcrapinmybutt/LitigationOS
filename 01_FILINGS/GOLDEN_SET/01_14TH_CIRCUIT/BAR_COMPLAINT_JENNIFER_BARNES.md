## I. INTRODUCTION

1. I, Andrew J. Pigors, file this formal complaint against attorney Jennifer Barnes pursuant to MCR 9.104 and MCR 9.113, requesting investigation of multiple violations of the Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct (MRPC) arising from Ms. Barnes's representation of Emily A. Watson in Pigors v Watson, Case No. 2024-001507-DC, 14th Judicial Circuit Court, Family Division, Muskegon County, Michigan.

2. I am a *pro se* litigant — the Plaintiff/Father in the above-referenced custody case involving our minor child, L.D.W. (DOB: November 9, 2022).

3. Ms. Barnes's conduct as described below demonstrates a pattern of professional misconduct that has directly contributed to the deprivation of my fundamental parental rights for **more than 200 consecutive days** without an evidentiary hearing.

## II. FACTUAL NARRATIVE

### A. Background

4. The underlying custody case (2024-001507-DC) was filed in 2024. Emily Watson and I initially shared approximately equal parenting time (50/50) with our child.

5. Ms. Barnes represents Emily Watson as her custody attorney. Emily Watson's boyfriend, Ron Berry, is NOT a licensed attorney. He is suspected of providing unauthorized off-record legal assistance in this matter, which would constitute the unauthorized practice of law under MCL 600.916.

6. Beginning in 2024, Ms. Barnes filed or facilitated a series of motions, ex parte filings, and show cause actions that have systematically eliminated my parenting time, from 50/50 custody to 0% — a total deprivation accomplished without any evidentiary hearing on the merits.

### B. False and Misleading Ex Parte Motion (August 8, 2025)

7. On August 8, 2025, Ms. Barnes filed or assisted in filing an "Emergency Ex Parte Motion to Suspend Parenting Time" on behalf of Emily Watson.

8. This motion contained the following statements which were false or materially misleading:

   a. **The allegation that my behavior was "a result of meth use,"** attributed to Norton Shores Police Officer Ella Randall. This allegation was:
      - An unsubstantiated opinion by a police officer, presented as fact in a sworn court filing
      - Never supported by any drug test, toxicology report, or clinical finding
      - **Directly contradicted by the HealthWest clinical evaluation** (September 5, 2025), which found: Substance Use Score = **0**, "No problems reported," and "Andrew reports he quit drinking and smoking weed before his son was born."

   b. **Representations that I posed a danger to the child.** HealthWest's clinical evaluation found: Danger to Self = **0**, Danger to Others = **0**, Suicide Risk = **"None"**, Level of Urgency = **"Routine."**

   c. **Representations supporting emergency action.** The clinical record shows SMI/SED = **"Neither SMI nor SED"** and LOCUS Score = **12 (Level One — lowest severity tier)**.

9. Ms. Barnes knew or should have known, through the exercise of reasonable diligence, that these statements were false or misleading prior to presenting them to the court. An attorney has an obligation under MRPC 3.3 to verify the accuracy of representations made to a tribunal.

### C. Strategic Filing of Show Cause Motions as Harassment

10. Ms. Barnes filed or facilitated the filing of **seven (7) separate show cause motions** in the related PPO case (2023-5907-PP).

11. These motions were used not for genuine enforcement of the PPO, but as a litigation weapon to harass me and to create the appearance of ongoing violations:

    a. **Show Cause #7** was filed on **October 27, 2025** — **two days before** a scheduled custody hearing on **October 29, 2025** — as a strategic ambush designed to prejudice the custody proceeding.

    b. The timing pattern of these filings demonstrates their use as tactical tools rather than good-faith enforcement actions.

### D. Pre-Planned Ex Parte Filing

12. On the evening of August 7, 2025, Albert Watson (Emily Watson's father) contacted Norton Shores Police and filed a report regarding a phone call from me (Case No. NS2505044).

13. Albert Watson **told the responding officer that "they want this incident documented so Emily can go tomorrow to get an Ex Parte order for full custody of her son."**

14. The next morning, August 8, 2025, the ex parte motion was filed exactly as Albert Watson had pre-announced to police. This demonstrates:

    a. The ex parte motion was pre-planned, not a genuine emergency
    b. The police report was filed as a pretext for the legal filing
    c. The Watson family (Emily's clients and family) coordinated the strategy in advance

15. As Emily Watson's attorney, Ms. Barnes either participated in this pre-planning or failed to conduct reasonable inquiry into the circumstances of the "emergency," as required by MRPC 3.1.

### E. Withholding Evidence and Obstructing Discovery

16. Throughout this litigation, I have been denied access to critical evidence and information:

    a. The court reviewed USB evidence submitted by Emily Watson's side ex parte, before I received copies
    b. Albert Watson performed "surprise authentication" of evidence at a hearing without prior disclosure
    c. Emily Watson's prior history of alienating another child's father was never disclosed despite its direct relevance to the best interests factors under MCL 722.23

17. Ms. Barnes, as counsel for Emily Watson, has an obligation under MRPC 3.4 to make reasonably diligent efforts to comply with discovery and to not suppress evidence.

### F. Exploitation of Pro Se Status

18. As a *pro se* litigant, I have been systematically disadvantaged in this proceeding. While court staff (Pamela Rusco) told me that explaining how to submit evidence was "legal advice" she could not provide, the same court staff actively coordinated on behalf of the opposing side — calling HealthWest on October 29, 2025, to arrange clinician testimony for a November 26, 2025 hearing.

19. Ms. Barnes has exploited this asymmetry, including filing motions and ex parte actions that a *pro se* litigant could not effectively respond to without notice or time to prepare.

### F. Specific Ex Parte Communications Evidence

19A. The record reveals multiple instances of prohibited or improper ex parte activity in which Ms. Barnes participated or from which she benefited:

a. **USB Evidence Pre-Review (August 5-8, 2025):** Judge McNeill admitted on the record: *"Yes, I had my staff listen to it"* — referring to USB recording evidence submitted by Defendant's side. This admission confirms the court reviewed evidence ex parte before Father was served. Ms. Barnes, as counsel for the submitting party, had a duty under MRPC 3.3 to ensure evidence was properly disclosed to the opposing party before judicial review.

b. **Rusco-HealthWest Ex Parte Contact (October 29, 2025, 1:48PM–2:00PM):** Court staff Pamela Rusco called HealthWest clinician Kassandra M. Gansen to arrange testimony for the November 26, 2025 hearing. This constitutes prohibited ex parte communication between court staff and a prospective witness under MCR 2.003(C)(1)(a). Ms. Barnes, as the attorney who benefited from this coordination, had a duty to object or disclose.

c. **Court Letter to HealthWest (September 2025):** The court provided a letter to HealthWest describing Andrew's alleged behaviors as *"several incidents over the past two years of court proceedings which are suggestive of a mental health disorder."* This letter created confirmation bias: 1st assessment (without letter) scored Psychosis=0; 2nd assessment (after letter) scored Psychosis=2. Ms. Barnes benefited from this court-manufactured clinical finding without disclosing the biasing mechanism.

d. **Judge's "Do not file" Directive:** Judge McNeill stated from the bench: *"Do not file any more, I will not look at it"* — effectively barring Father from filing motions without a vexatious litigant determination under MCL 600.1951. Ms. Barnes failed to object to this violation of Plaintiff's First Amendment right to petition.

21A. The following filings lacked factual basis in violation of MRPC 3.1:

a. **Seven (7) Show Cause Motions in PPO Case (2023-5907-PP):** These motions were used as a litigation weapon rather than genuine enforcement. The pattern of strategic timing (SC#7 filed 10/27/2025, two days before 10/29/2025 custody hearing) demonstrates their tactical purpose.

b. **Emergency Ex Parte Motion (08/08/2025):** Filed based on an officer's unsubstantiated lay opinion of "meth use" without any drug test, toxicology report, or clinical finding. Albert Watson admitted to police the night before that the family planned to use the incident to get an ex parte order — proving the filing was pre-planned, not a genuine emergency. MRPC 3.1 requires a non-frivolous basis in law and fact.

## III. RULES OF PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT VIOLATED

### A. MRPC 3.1 — Meritorious Claims and Contentions

20. MRPC 3.1 provides: "A lawyer shall not bring or defend a proceeding, or assert or controvert an issue therein, unless there is a basis in law and fact for doing so that is not frivolous."

21. Ms. Barnes violated MRPC 3.1 by:
    a. Filing or facilitating an ex parte motion based on an unsubstantiated allegation of "meth use" with no supporting drug test or clinical evidence
    b. Filing seven (7) show cause motions used as tactical harassment rather than genuine enforcement
    c. Filing Show Cause #7 two days before a custody hearing as a strategic ambush
    d. Seeking emergency relief based on claims that were directly contradicted by clinical evidence (HealthWest evaluation)

### B. MRPC 3.3 — Candor Toward the Tribunal

22. MRPC 3.3(a) provides: "A lawyer shall not knowingly (1) make a false statement of material fact or law to a tribunal; (2) fail to disclose a material fact to a tribunal when disclosure is necessary to avoid assisting a criminal or fraudulent act by the client."

23. Ms. Barnes violated MRPC 3.3 by:
    a. Presenting the "meth use" allegation as established fact in sworn filings when it was merely an unsubstantiated police officer opinion, with no drug test ever conducted
    b. Representing that I posed a danger requiring emergency suspension of parenting time, when HealthWest's clinical findings showed Danger to Self = 0, Danger to Others = 0
    c. Failing to disclose Emily Watson's prior history of alienating another child's father — a material fact relevant to the best interests determination
    d. Failing to correct the record after HealthWest's clinical evaluation contradicted the ex parte motion's central allegations

### C. MRPC 3.4 — Fairness to Opposing Party and Counsel

24. MRPC 3.4 provides that a lawyer shall not "unlawfully obstruct another party's access to evidence" or "knowingly disobey an obligation under the rules of a tribunal."

25. Ms. Barnes violated MRPC 3.4 by:
    a. Facilitating the ex parte review of USB evidence by the court before the opposing party (me) had access to it
    b. Permitting "surprise authentication" of evidence at hearing through Albert Watson without prior disclosure
    c. Obstructing my access to evidence and information necessary to defend my parental rights
    d. Exploiting the asymmetry between a represented party and a *pro se* litigant

### D. MRPC 4.1 — Truthfulness in Statements to Others

26. MRPC 4.1 provides: "In the course of representing a client, a lawyer shall not knowingly make a false statement of material fact or law to a third person."

27. Ms. Barnes violated MRPC 4.1 by making or facilitating false statements of material fact in court filings and proceedings, including the unsubstantiated meth allegation and exaggerated danger claims.

### E. MRPC 8.4 — Misconduct

28. MRPC 8.4 provides that it is professional misconduct for a lawyer to:
    - (a) violate or attempt to violate the Rules of Professional Conduct
    - (b) engage in conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or misrepresentation
    - (c) engage in conduct that is prejudicial to the administration of justice

29. Ms. Barnes's cumulative conduct constitutes misconduct under MRPC 8.4:
    a. The pattern of false filings, strategic harassment via show cause motions, evidence withholding, and exploitation of a *pro se* opponent demonstrates conduct prejudicial to the administration of justice
    b. The pre-planned ex parte motion (demonstrated by Albert Watson's police statement) was part of a coordinated effort involving dishonesty and fraud
    c. The result — more than 200 consecutive days of complete parent-child separation without evidentiary hearing — is directly attributable to this pattern of misconduct
