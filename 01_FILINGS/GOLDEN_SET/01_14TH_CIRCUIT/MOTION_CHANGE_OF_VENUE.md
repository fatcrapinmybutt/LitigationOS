### I. INTRODUCTION

1. Plaintiff/Father seeks transfer of both pending cases out of the 14th Circuit Court, Family Division, Muskegon County, to a neutral jurisdiction where Plaintiff can receive a fair and impartial adjudication of his parental rights.

2. This Motion is necessitated by documented judicial bias, the appearance of impropriety, local conditions making a fair trial impossible, and the Defendant's family connections to the Kent County/Muskegon County family court system that create an irreconcilable conflict of interest.

3. Plaintiff has been denied ALL parenting time with his three-year-old son, Lincoln (DOB: November 9, 2022), for **more than 200 consecutive days**. A fair and impartial tribunal is essential to any meaningful review of the circumstances that led to this complete severance of the parent-child relationship.

---

### II. LEGAL STANDARD

4. MCR 2.222(A) provides that a court may order a change of venue when it appears that an impartial trial cannot be had in the county where the action is pending.

5. MCR 2.223 governs the transfer of actions between courts, providing authority for transfer when the interests of justice require it.

6. The Michigan Supreme Court has held that "[a] reasonable person would perceive a significant risk of actual bias" when certain conditions are demonstrated. *In re MCI Telecom Complaint*, 460 Mich 396, 411 (1999).

7. Due process under the 14th Amendment to the United States Constitution requires an impartial tribunal. *Caperton v A.T. Massey Coal Co.*, 556 US 868 (2009). The appearance of bias is constitutionally intolerable when the probability of actual bias is too high.

---

### III. GROUNDS FOR CHANGE OF VENUE

#### A. Documented Judicial Bias Making Fair Trial Impossible

8. The presiding judge, Hon. Jenny L. McNeill, has demonstrated a pattern of bias against Plaintiff/Father that renders a fair trial impossible in this jurisdiction. The following ten (10) documented instances of bias are drawn from the record:

9. **Issue A — Ex Parte Orders Without Legal Basis (MCR 3.207(B))**: The Court issued ex parte orders suspending Father's parental rights without requiring an affidavit, verified complaint, or emergency findings as mandated by MCR 3.207(B). Analysis of 1,435 PDF documents in the record reveals **52 separate ex parte orders** — a rate of approximately **44% of all orders being entered ex parte**.

10. **Issue B — Due Process Violations (14th Amendment)**: Father's parental rights were suspended without proper notice, a meaningful hearing, or the application of the clear and convincing evidence standard required by MCL 722.27a(3).

11. **Issue C — Admission of Unauthenticated Evidence (MRE 901)**: The Court admitted a USB recording without testimony from an authenticating witness, without establishing foundation, and without determining whether the recording was made in violation of Michigan's two-party consent wiretapping statute (MCL 750.539c). Emily's father testified to the phone call but NOT to the recording itself — a critical authentication gap.

12. **Issue D — Pattern of Inadequate Service (MCR 2.305)**: The Court tolerated the **fourth documented instance** of inadequate service. In August 2025, Father was served on a Friday (August 8) for a Monday hearing (August 11) — providing **ZERO business days** to prepare, in violation of MCR 2.119/2.305 (5 business days minimum required).

13. **Issue E — Admission of Felony Wiretapping Evidence (MCL 750.539c)**: The recording used as the basis for suspending Father's parenting rights was made by Lori Watson (Defendant's mother) without Father's knowledge or consent — a felony under MCL 750.539c. The Court admitted evidence obtained through criminal activity. See *People v Burge*, 722 NW2d 470 (Mich App 2006) (evidence from criminal violation constitutes fruit of crime and must be excluded).

14. **Issue F — Secret Evaluation and Brady Violation (MCR 3.210 / Brady v Maryland)**: The Court obtained a SECOND mental health evaluation of Father without his knowledge, authorization, or consent. Father never received this evaluation and was never afforded the opportunity to challenge it. The Court further directed Father to email his first evaluation to the judge's SECRETARY rather than filing through the clerk's office — preventing proper filing, public record creation, and opposing party access.

15. **Issue G — Failure to Apply Correct Legal Standard (MCL 722.27a(3))**: The Court failed to apply the clear and convincing evidence standard required for custody modification, instead relying on disputed and unauthenticated evidence. See *In re Martin*, 450 Mich 204; 538 NW2d 399 (1995).

16. **Issue H — No Written Findings (MCR 3.210(D))**: The Court failed to issue written findings of fact or conclusions of law as required by MCR 3.210(D). See *Woodworth v Woodworth*, 337 Mich 187; 59 NW2d 169 (1953).

17. **Issue I — Demonstrated Prejudgment (MCR 2.003 / Caperton)**: The Court pre-reviewed the USB recording before Father was served, as admitted on the record: **"Yes, I had my staff listen to it"** (August 11, 2025 hearing). The Court used stigmatizing language from the bench, calling Father "crazy" and "liar" without evidentiary support, in violation of Canon 3(A)(3) of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct.

18. **Issue J — Punitive Filing Restrictions (MCR 1.109 / MCR 2.119)**: The Court imposed a **$250 "sanction fee"** for filing motions without any vexatious litigant finding, without a hearing, and without legal basis. The Court further stated on the record: **"Do not file any more, I will not look at it"** — effectively barring Father from accessing the courts in violation of due process and MCR 2.119.
