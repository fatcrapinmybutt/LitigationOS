## VIII. LEGAL ANALYSIS — PARENTAL ALIENATION UNDER MICHIGAN LAW

### A. Michigan's Recognition of Parental Alienation

Michigan courts have consistently recognized parental alienation as a significant factor in custody determinations. Under MCL 722.23(j), the court **must** evaluate each parent's willingness to facilitate a close and continuing relationship between the child and the other parent. A pattern of alienation is among the strongest grounds for custody modification. *See Pierron v. Pierron*, 282 Mich App 222 (2009).

### B. The Eight Hallmarks of Alienation Present in This Case

The evidence establishes all recognized indicators of parental alienation:

| # | Hallmark | Evidence in This Case |
|---|----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Denigration campaign** | Mother coaching child: "Daddy doesn't want to see you" |
| 2 | **Weak, frivolous, or absurd rationalizations** | PPO based on five false statements; no police findings supporting allegations |
| 3 | **Lack of ambivalence** | Mother permits zero contact for 192+ consecutive days |
| 4 | **Independent thinker phenomenon** | Child parrots Mother's language at age 3 (developmentally impossible without coaching) |
| 5 | **Reflexive support of alienating parent** | Watson family members uniformly participate in alienation |
| 6 | **Absence of guilt** | Mother's 27 violations with zero remorse or self-correction |
| 7 | **Borrowed scenarios** | Child's statements about Father mirror Mother's sworn allegations verbatim |
| 8 | **Spread to extended family** | Albert, Lori, and Cody Watson all actively participate |

### C. Constitutional Dimensions

The right of a parent to the care, custody, and management of their child is a **fundamental liberty interest** protected by the Fourteenth Amendment. *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 US 57 (2000); *Santosky v. Kramer*, 455 US 745 (1982).

**More than 200 consecutive days of separation without an evidentiary hearing** constitutes a deprivation of this fundamental right without due process of law. The court's failure to address documented alienation, combined with its refusal to consider new evidence ("we're not going to relitigate it") and directive to Father to stop filing motions ("Do not file any more"), effectively extinguishes Father's constitutional rights without adjudication on the merits.

### D. The HealthWest Evaluation Gap

The HealthWest evaluation — a court-ordered assessment — **documented alienation** and **cleared Father** of the allegations underlying the separation. Despite this, the trial court has:

1. Ignored the evaluation's findings
2. Continued to restrict Father's parenting time
3. Imposed no consequences on Mother for documented violations
4. Refused to hold an evidentiary hearing on the evaluation's conclusions

This selective disregard for favorable evidence while crediting unsubstantiated allegations constitutes an abuse of discretion and a denial of due process.

### E. Harm to the Child

The academic literature, including the MDPI 2022 study on parental alienating behaviors and Bowlby Attachment Theory (2025 review), establishes that:

- **Prolonged separation** from a parent during ages 2–4 causes measurable attachment disruption
- **Alienation coaching** creates loyalty conflicts that damage the child's psychological development
- **Identity formation** is affected when a child is deprived of a parent during this critical window
- **The harm is cumulative and accelerating** — each additional day of separation compounds the damage

L.D.W. has now been separated from Father for the **majority of his conscious memory**. The harm is approaching irreversibility.
