### I. INTRODUCTION

1. Father has been denied ALL parenting time with the minor child, L.D.W. (minor child, born November 9, 2022) (DOB: November 9, 2022), for **more than 200 consecutive days**.

2. During this period, Mother has exercised **100% of custodial time** with the child. Father has exercised **0%** of custodial time.

3. The existing child support order does not reflect this actual custodial arrangement. The Michigan Child Support Formula requires that child support calculations be based on the actual number of overnights each parent exercises. A modification is warranted to reflect reality.

4. Additionally, Mother's wrongful denial of Father's parenting time — in violation of court orders — has caused Father to incur substantial costs. MCL 722.27a(7) authorizes the Court to order a parent who wrongfully denies parenting time to pay the other parent's costs.

### III. LEGAL BASIS FOR CHILD SUPPORT MODIFICATION

#### A. MCL 552.517 — Change in Circumstances

14. MCL 552.517(1) provides that a child support order may be modified upon a showing of a change of circumstances.

15. The complete elimination of Father's parenting time — from 50/50 (182.5 overnights) to ZERO overnights — constitutes a material change of circumstances warranting modification of the support obligation.

16. Under the Michigan Child Support Formula, the number of overnights a child spends with each parent is a central factor in calculating support. When one parent has 100% of overnights and the other has 0%, the formula produces a different result than when parents share time equally or in any other proportion.

#### B. Michigan Child Support Formula — Overnight Calculation

17. The Michigan Child Support Formula uses overnights as a primary input for determining the appropriate level of support. The formula recognizes that a parent exercising more custodial time incurs greater direct costs for the child (housing, food, utilities, transportation, etc.).

18. Mother currently incurs 100% of the direct custodial costs because she has 100% of the overnights. The child support calculation should reflect this reality.

19. Father requests that the Court recalculate child support based on the current actual custodial arrangement: Mother — 365 overnights/year; Father — 0 overnights/year.
