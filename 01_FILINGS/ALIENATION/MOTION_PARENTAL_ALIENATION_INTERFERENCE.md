The evidence presented herein demonstrates Defendant's wholesale failure — and active opposition — to this statutory mandate.

## II. LEGAL STANDARD

### A. Michigan Child Custody Act — Best Interest Factors

The Child Custody Act, MCL 722.23, enumerates the best interest factors that must guide all custody and parenting time determinations. Several factors are directly implicated by alienation and custodial interference:

- **MCL 722.23(a)** — The love, affection, and other emotional ties existing between the parties involved and the child
- **MCL 722.23(b)** — The capacity and disposition of the parties to give the child love, affection, and guidance
- **MCL 722.23(c)** — The capacity and disposition of the parties to provide food, clothing, medical care
- **MCL 722.23(d)** — The length of time the child has lived in a stable, satisfactory environment
- **MCL 722.23(f)** — The moral fitness of the parties involved
- **MCL 722.23(j)** — The willingness and ability of each of the parties to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent
- **MCL 722.23(k)** — Domestic violence, regardless of whether directed against the child
- **MCL 722.23(l)** — Any other factor considered by the court to be relevant

### B. Parenting Time Statute

- **MCL 722.27a(1)** — Parenting time shall be granted in accordance with the best interests of the child
- **MCL 722.27a(3)** — A child has a right to parenting time with each parent unless the court determines parenting time would endanger the child's physical, mental, or emotional health
- **MCL 722.27a(7)** — The court may impose sanctions including makeup parenting time and modifying custody

### C. Criminal Statutes

- **MCL 750.350a** — Parental kidnapping — taking or retaining a child in violation of custody order
- **MCL 750.136b** — Child abuse — may encompass emotional abuse through alienation

### D. Michigan Case Law

- **Bowers v. Bowers, 190 Mich App 85 (1991)** — Recognized parental alienation as a relevant factor in custody determinations; a parent's interference with the other parent's relationship is contrary to the child's best interests
- **Vodvarka v. Grasmeyer, 259 Mich App 499 (2003)** — Established the standard for change of circumstances warranting modification of custody orders; a pattern of alienation constitutes proper cause or change of circumstances
- **Brown v. Loveman, 260 Mich App 576 (2004)** — Court must consider the totality of circumstances including interference with parenting time
- **Shade v. Wright, 291 Mich App 17 (2010)** — A custodial parent's persistent efforts to undermine the child's relationship with the noncustodial parent is relevant to fitness and best interests
- **Pierron v. Pierron, 486 Mich 81 (2010)** — The established custodial environment and best interest factors must be carefully evaluated in modification proceedings

**Conclusion:** Strong claim for enforcement; statutory makeup time and fee-shifting are virtually mandatory upon proof of violation.
