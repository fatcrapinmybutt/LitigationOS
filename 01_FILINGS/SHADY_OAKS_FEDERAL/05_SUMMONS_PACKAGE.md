## INSTRUCTIONS FOR SERVICE

Under Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 4, each Defendant must be served with:
1. A copy of the Summons (specific to that Defendant)
2. A copy of the Complaint
3. Any attachments required by the Court

**Service deadline:** 90 days from the date the Complaint is filed (Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(m)).

**Service methods for LLCs and corporations (Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(h)):**
- Delivering a copy to an officer, managing or general agent, or any agent authorized to receive service
- Following the state law for service in a civil action in the courts of Michigan (MCR 2.105)
- Using waiver of service under Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(d) (recommended for cost savings)

**Service methods for individuals (Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(e)):**
- Personal service (delivering a copy to the individual personally)
- Leaving a copy at the individual's dwelling with a person of suitable age and discretion
- Delivering a copy to an agent authorized by appointment or by law to receive service
- Following Michigan law (MCR 2.105)

---

## WAIVER OF SERVICE RECOMMENDATION

Under Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(d), Plaintiff should first request waiver of service from each Defendant. If a Defendant fails to waive without good cause, the Defendant must pay the costs of formal service. This is strongly recommended for all corporate Defendants — it saves Plaintiff significant costs.

**Waiver request deadline:** Send within 30 days of filing.
**Waiver return deadline:** 60 days from date sent (90 days for out-of-state Defendants).
