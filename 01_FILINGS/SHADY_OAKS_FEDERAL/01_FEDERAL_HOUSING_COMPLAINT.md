## I. INTRODUCTION

1. This is a civil action seeking compensatory damages, treble damages, punitive damages, injunctive relief, and declaratory judgment arising from a coordinated, multi-year scheme by nine Defendants to defraud, harass, retaliate against, and ultimately destroy the home and livelihood of Plaintiff ANDREW J. PIGORS ("Plaintiff" or "Mr. Pigors"), a lawful titleholder of a manufactured home in Muskegon County, Michigan.

2. Through an interlocking network of corporate shells — several of which are dissolved, unfunded, or criminally convicted — Defendants operated a racketeering enterprise whose objective was to extract maximum rent from vulnerable manufactured housing tenants while investing nothing in habitability, and to silence any tenant who dared complain through billing fraud, lease coercion, retaliatory eviction, and ultimately the physical destruction of their home.

3. The enterprise operated under the ultimate direction of Defendant ALDEN GLOBAL CAPITAL, LLC ("Alden"), a New York hedge fund controlling a $275 million manufactured housing portfolio. Alden's operational arm, Defendant HOMES OF AMERICA, LLC ("HOA"), directed the day-to-day fraud through a network of shell entities including SHADY OAKS PARK MHP, LLC — a New Jersey LLC that was **dissolved circa 2022** yet continued to file lawsuits, collect rent, and operate as if it were a lawful entity. This constitutes fraud on the court and ultra vires conduct that voids all proceedings brought in that entity's name.

4. The damages to Plaintiff are catastrophic: destruction of his titled home and all personal property therein; forced homelessness; loss of parenting time with his minor child; economic devastation; and severe emotional distress. The total damages, including RICO treble damages and punitive damages warranted by the pattern of willful misconduct, range from **$2.45 million to $17.24 million**.

5. Plaintiff brings this action under the Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. §§ 3601–3619; the Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act (RICO), 18 U.S.C. §§ 1961–1968; 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (due process); and supplemental state claims under 28 U.S.C. § 1367, including conversion, fraud, Michigan Consumer Protection Act violations, retaliatory eviction, breach of habitability, corporate veil piercing, and civil conspiracy.

Under Michigan law, the corporate veil may be pierced where: (1) the corporate entity is a mere instrumentality of another entity; (2) the corporate form was used to commit a fraud or wrong; and (3) the plaintiff suffered an unjust loss. *Foodland Distribs. v. Initiating new litigation, collecting rent, entering new leases, and executing lockouts are not winding-up activities. All such acts are void and of no legal effect.

126. Alden Global Capital and Homes of America are directly liable as alter egos for all damages caused by their shell entities, with no limitation based on the corporate form.
