## I. INTRODUCTION

1. This is a civil action arising from Defendants' systematic pattern of fraud, housing discrimination, retaliatory conduct, and racketeering activity directed at Plaintiff, a lawful titleholder of a manufactured home at Lot 17, 1977 Whitehall Road, Muskegon County, Michigan 49445 ("the Property"). Defendants engaged in a coordinated scheme of rent overcharging, utility overbilling, lease coercion, habitability violations including raw sewage exposure, retaliatory billing, and ultimately the unlawful destruction of Plaintiff's home and personal property — forcing Plaintiff into homelessness and causing the suspension of his parenting time with his minor child.

2. Plaintiff seeks actual damages, treble damages under the Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act ("RICO"), 18 U.S.C. §§ 1961–1968, punitive damages, injunctive relief, declaratory judgment, and attorney fees and costs under the Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. §§ 3601–3619.

## IV. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

### A. Background — Plaintiff's Residency and Titled Home

12. At all relevant times, Plaintiff was the lawful titleholder of a manufactured home situated at Lot 17, 1977 Whitehall Road, Laketon Township, Muskegon County, Michigan 49445 ("the Home"). Plaintiff held title to the Home itself, which is personal property under Michigan law (MCL 125.2330i), distinct from the underlying land owned by the park. *(Evidence: M1A_00470 — "lawful titleholder of a home wrongfully seized by Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC")*

13. Plaintiff entered into a lot-rental agreement with Shady Oaks for the use of the land upon which his titled Home was situated. Under the terms of that agreement and applicable Michigan law (the Mobile Home Commission Act, MCL 125.2301 et seq.), Plaintiff was entitled to quiet enjoyment of the lot, habitable conditions, and transparent, lawful billing practices.

### B. The Corporate Enterprise — Entity Structure

14. Defendants operate through an interrelated corporate structure designed to obscure responsibility and maximize rent extraction from vulnerable manufactured-housing tenants. Shady Oaks Park MHP is the local park entity. Homes of America, LLC is the corporate operator that exercises financial and operational control. Cricklewood MHP LLC is a related entity involved in lease enforcement and billing administration. *(Evidence: M1CL_06 — Entity Responsibility Mapping, 51 evidence signals; M1A_00470, M1A_00497 — "coercive shell-game scheme involving Homes of America")*

15. Upon information and belief, upstream entities including Partridge Equity Group and Alden Global Capital exercise additional ownership and control over the enterprise. The layered entity structure was used to evade accountability, shift liability, and create confusion among tenants regarding the identity of their landlord.

### C. Systematic Rent Overcharging and Ledger Manipulation

16. Defendants engaged in a pattern of rent overcharging and ledger manipulation directed at Plaintiff and, upon information and belief, other tenants. Park agents refused to accept partial payments, misapplied or failed to apply valid payments, and inflated balances through layered fees (trash fees, administrative fees, late fees) that prevented Plaintiff from ever reaching a fixed "amount due." *(Evidence: M1CL_01 — Rent Overcharge / Lease Coercion, 8,731 evidence signals, proof status SIGNAL_SATISFIED; Acquisition Task M1AQ_01)*

17. Billing statements sent to Plaintiff via U.S. Mail and/or electronic means contained materially false representations of amounts owed, fees assessed, and payments credited. These billing statements constituted the use of the mails and wires in furtherance of a scheme to defraud.

### D. Utility Overbilling

18. Defendants systematically overbilled Plaintiff for utilities, including water and sewer charges that did not correspond to actual usage. Utility billing continued during periods when the property was uninhabitable due to Defendants' failure to maintain essential services. *(Evidence: M1CL_02 — Utility Overbilling, 115 evidence signals, proof status SIGNAL_SATISFIED; M1AQ_02)*

19. Upon information and belief, Defendants assessed utility charges at rates exceeding actual cost, failed to provide itemized utility statements as required, and continued to bill for utilities after shutting off Plaintiff's water service.

### E. Habitability Violations — Sewage Leak and Environmental Hazard

20. Beginning in or about Spring 2025, raw sewage leaked in the vicinity of Plaintiff's lot and persisted through Summer 2025. The sewage constituted a serious health hazard and rendered the premises uninhabitable. *(Evidence: M1CL_03 — Habitability / Sewage Leak, 228 evidence signals, proof status SIGNAL_SATISFIED)*

21. The Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy ("EGLE") issued violations related to the sewage conditions at Shady Oaks Park MHP. Despite notice of the EGLE violations, Defendants failed to remediate the conditions for approximately 34 days. *(Evidence: Exhibit B-1 — EGLE Violation Letters/Notices [Placeholder]; M1A_02647)*

22. Defendants' failure to maintain habitable conditions violated the implied warranty of habitability, the Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.), and constituted a breach of the statutory covenant to maintain the premises in reasonable repair (MCL 554.139).

### F. Lease Coercion

23. Defendants engaged in coercive practices to force Plaintiff to sign lease amendments and new lease agreements containing onerous terms. Defendants used the threat of eviction, refusal to accept rent payments, and manufactured arrearages to pressure Plaintiff into accepting unfavorable terms. *(Evidence: M1CL_01 — Rent Overcharge / Lease Coercion, 8,731 signals; Exhibit H — Coerced Lease Event Summary [Placeholder])*

### G. Retaliatory Conduct

24. After Plaintiff complained about habitability conditions, billing discrepancies, and the sewage leak, Defendants engaged in retaliatory conduct including: increased fees, refusal to accept payments, accelerated eviction proceedings, and other adverse actions designed to punish Plaintiff for exercising his legal rights. *(Evidence: M1CL_05 — Retaliatory Billing / Conduct Pattern, 58 evidence signals, proof status SIGNAL_SATISFIED)*

25. This retaliatory pattern is prohibited by Michigan's Anti-Retaliation statute, MCL 600.5720, which forbids landlords from increasing rent, decreasing services, or commencing eviction proceedings in retaliation for tenant complaints.

### H. Notice and Service Defects

26. Defendants failed to provide proper notices as required by Michigan law, including notice of eviction proceedings, notice of fees, and notice of intent to enter the premises. Service of process was defective in multiple instances. *(Evidence: M1CL_04 — Notice / Service / Process Defects, 818 evidence signals, proof status SIGNAL_SATISFIED)*

### I. Attempted Break-In and Destruction of Home

27. On or about July 3, 2025, two individuals (one Black male, one White male) attempted to break into Plaintiff's home at Lot 17. These individuals were later identified among the crew that returned on July 17, 2025 to destroy Plaintiff's home. *(Evidence: M1A_02647 — Core Facts; Exhibit D-1 — Security Camera Photos [Placeholder])*

28. On or about July 17, 2025, Defendants' agents drilled out the locks on Plaintiff's home, removed and destroyed Plaintiff's personal belongings, posted a "FREE" sign on the home, and seized appliances — including property taken by Henry Brandel. A sheriff's deputy was present but the scope of the destruction exceeded any lawful authorization. *(Evidence: M1A_02647 — "locks were drilled; belongings removed/destroyed; a 'FREE' sign was posted; appliances taken")*

29. At the time of the destruction, Plaintiff had paid rent through April 2025. No lawful writ of restitution authorized the destruction of Plaintiff's titled personal property (the Home and its contents). The destruction was carried out without lawful process against the Home itself, as the eviction proceeding, if any, pertained only to lot possession, not to Plaintiff's titled manufactured home. *(Evidence: M1A_00472 — "Rent paid through April; home destroyed post-writ"; federal/complaint_generator.py — "No lawful possession order over titled home")*

### J. Collateral Damage — Homelessness and Loss of Parental Rights

30. As a direct and proximate result of Defendants' destruction of Plaintiff's home, Plaintiff became homeless and was forced to sleep in his car. *(Evidence: M1A_02647 — "Plaintiff became homeless (sleeping in his car)")*

31. Plaintiff's homelessness was then used as a basis to suspend his parenting time with his minor child in separate custody proceedings. As of September 22, 2025, Plaintiff had not seen his son in over 60 days and suffers severe emotional distress. "Unstable housing" — a condition directly caused by Defendants' unlawful conduct — was weaponized against Plaintiff in family court. *(Evidence: M1A_02647 — "'unstable housing' was used to suspend parenting time"; M1A_00471 — "down to 0 custody, and now because i dont have 'stable housing' again, they have removed my parenting time to 0")*

32. Defendants' unlawful housing practices therefore directly infringed upon Plaintiff's fundamental right to parent his child, as protected by the Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution. *(Evidence: M1A_00473 — "Defendants' unlawful housing practices therefore directly infringed upon Plaintiff's fundamental right to parent, as protected by the Fourteenth Amendment")*

### K. Water Shutoff with Child Present

33. On at least one occasion, Defendants shut off Plaintiff's water service while Plaintiff's minor child was present in the home, creating an immediate health and safety hazard. *(Evidence: federal/complaint_generator.py — "Water shutoff with child present")*

### L. Pattern of Discriminatory and Predatory Practices

34. Upon information and belief, Defendants' pattern of rent overcharging, overbilling, lease coercion, habitability neglect, and retaliatory conduct was not limited to Plaintiff but was directed at similarly situated manufactured-home tenants within the park, disproportionately affecting low-income residents and individuals with children.

35. Defendants' conduct constitutes discriminatory housing practices in that: (a) Defendants imposed different terms and conditions on tenants who complained about habitability conditions; (b) Defendants made housing unavailable by destroying Plaintiff's home and rendering him homeless; and (c) Defendants' practices had a disparate impact on families with children and low-income tenants who lacked resources to resist the coercive scheme.

### COUNT I: VIOLATION OF THE FAIR HOUSING ACT
*42 U.S.C. §§ 3604(a), 3604(b), 3617*
*(Against All Defendants)*

36. Plaintiff realleges and incorporates by reference all prior paragraphs.

37. The Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. § 3604(a), makes it unlawful to refuse to sell or rent, or to refuse to negotiate for the sale or rental of, or otherwise make unavailable or deny, a dwelling to any person because of familial status or other protected characteristics.

38. The Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. § 3604(b), makes it unlawful to discriminate against any person in the terms, conditions, or privileges of sale or rental of a dwelling, or in the provision of services or facilities in connection therewith.

39. The Fair Housing Act, 42 U.S.C. § 3617, makes it unlawful to coerce, intimidate, threaten, or interfere with any person in the exercise of any right granted by §§ 3603–3606.

40. Defendants violated the Fair Housing Act by:
   - (a) Making Plaintiff's dwelling unavailable by destroying his home on or about July 17, 2025 without lawful process, rendering him homeless; *(Evidence: M1A_02647)*
   - (b) Imposing discriminatory terms and conditions on Plaintiff's tenancy, including inflated rents, fabricated fees, refusal to accept valid payments, and coercive lease amendments; *(Evidence: M1CL_01 — 8,731 signals)*
   - (c) Failing to maintain habitable conditions (raw sewage leak) while continuing to charge rent and utilities; *(Evidence: M1CL_03 — 228 signals)*
   - (d) Retaliating against Plaintiff for exercising his rights under the lease and applicable law, including by escalating eviction proceedings and billing in response to habitability complaints; *(Evidence: M1CL_05 — 58 signals)*
   - (e) Coercing, intimidating, and interfering with Plaintiff's exercise of his housing rights in violation of § 3617; *(Evidence: M1CL_01, M1CL_05)*
   - (f) Making housing unavailable to Plaintiff's minor child by destroying the home where the child resided during parenting time, thereby contributing to the loss of Plaintiff's custodial rights. *(Evidence: M1A_00471, M1A_02647)*

41. Defendants' conduct had a discriminatory impact on Plaintiff as a member of a household with a minor child (familial status). The destruction of Plaintiff's home and the resulting homelessness was used to terminate Plaintiff's parenting time, directly harming the child's right to maintain a relationship with his father.

42. The Fair Housing Act provides for fee-shifting. 42 U.S.C. § 3613(c)(2) ("the court, in its discretion, may allow the prevailing party . . . a reasonable attorney's fee and costs").

43. Defendants' violations were intentional, willful, and in reckless disregard of Plaintiff's rights under the Fair Housing Act.

### COUNT II: VIOLATION OF THE RACKETEER INFLUENCED AND CORRUPT ORGANIZATIONS ACT (RICO)

*(Against Shady Oaks Park MHP, Homes of America LLC, Cricklewood MHP LLC, and Kim Davis)*

44. Plaintiff realleges and incorporates by reference all prior paragraphs.

#### The Enterprise

45. Defendants Shady Oaks Park MHP, Homes of America LLC, and Cricklewood MHP LLC constitute an "enterprise" within the meaning of 18 U.S.C. § 1961(4) — an association-in-fact of legal entities and individuals associated together for the common purpose of operating manufactured housing communities and extracting maximum revenue from tenants through fraudulent billing, lease coercion, and habitability neglect. *(Evidence: M1CL_06 — Entity Responsibility Mapping; Entity Map — corporate structure)*

47. The enterprise has an ascertainable structure separate from the pattern of racketeering activity, with defined roles (corporate control, local management, lease administration, and on-site execution) and a common purpose (maximizing revenue through fraudulent practices).

#### Pattern of Racketeering Activity

48. Defendants, through the enterprise, engaged in a "pattern of racketeering activity" within the meaning of 18 U.S.C. § 1961(5), consisting of at least two predicate acts of racketeering activity within the meaning of § 1961(1), including:

   **Predicate Act 1 — Mail Fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1341):**

49. Defendants used or caused to be used the United States mails and private interstate carriers to further a scheme to defraud Plaintiff and other tenants by sending billing statements through the mail that contained materially false representations regarding:
   - (a) Amounts of rent owed, including fabricated arrearages and layered fees (trash, administrative, late fees) that did not reflect actual obligations; *(Evidence: M1CL_01 — 8,731 evidence signals for Rent Overcharge / Lease Coercion)*
   - (b) Utility charges that did not correspond to actual usage or actual cost; *(Evidence: M1CL_02 — 115 evidence signals for Utility Overbilling)*
   - (c) Payment credits not applied or misapplied, creating a perpetually inflated balance; *(Evidence: M1A_02647 — "refused partial payments, misapplied/failed to apply valid payments")*
   - (d) Eviction notices and legal process documents mailed to Plaintiff containing false or misleading statements regarding amounts owed and legal obligations. *(Evidence: M1CL_04 — 818 signals for Notice / Service / Process Defects)*

   **Predicate Act 2 — Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1343):**

50. Defendants used or caused to be used wire communications in interstate commerce, including electronic billing systems, electronic payment processing, telephone communications, and internet-based communications, to further the same scheme to defraud by:
   - (a) Processing electronic rent and utility payments while misapplying those payments to inflate Plaintiff's balance;
   - (b) Sending electronic notices, statements, and communications containing materially false billing information;
   - (c) Using electronic payment systems to collect inflated charges from Plaintiff and other tenants. *(Evidence: M1CL_01, M1CL_02 — systematic overbilling pattern)*

   **Predicate Act 3 — Additional Mail and Wire Fraud:**

51. The fraudulent billing scheme was not a single occurrence but a continuous, systematic pattern repeated over the course of Plaintiff's tenancy. Each fraudulent billing statement, each misapplied payment, and each fabricated fee constitutes a separate use of the mails or wires in furtherance of the scheme. The pattern includes at least the following categories:
   - Monthly rent statements containing inflated amounts (repeated over multiple billing cycles);
   - Utility bills not based on actual usage (repeated monthly);
   - Late-fee assessments calculated on inflated balances (repeated monthly);
   - Administrative-fee charges with no basis in the lease or law (repeated periodically);
   - Eviction-related notices containing false arrearages (at least once). *(Evidence: M1CL_01 — 8,731 signals across multiple evidence sources; M1CL_02 — 115 signals)*

#### Relatedness and Continuity

52. The predicate acts are related because they share: (a) the same purpose — extracting inflated revenue from Plaintiff and other tenants; (b) the same participants — the Defendant entities and Davis; (c) the same victims — Plaintiff and, upon information and belief, other manufactured-home tenants at Shady Oaks; and (d) the same methods — fraudulent billing, ledger manipulation, and lease coercion.

53. The predicate acts demonstrate continuity because: (a) they occurred over a period of years during Plaintiff's tenancy (open-ended continuity); (b) there is a threat of continued activity as Defendants continue to operate the park and apply the same practices to other tenants (closed-ended continuity extending into the future); and (c) the number of predicate acts is substantial (hundreds of fraudulent billing statements).

#### Injury and Causation

54. As a direct and proximate result of the pattern of racketeering activity, Plaintiff suffered injury to his business or property within the meaning of 18 U.S.C. § 1964(c), including:
   - (a) Financial losses from inflated rent and utility charges paid under the fraudulent scheme;
   - (b) Loss of his manufactured home and all personal property therein (destroyed July 17, 2025);
   - (c) Costs of displacement, including temporary housing, storage, and transportation;
   - (d) Loss of parenting time and custodial rights as a direct consequence of Defendants' actions rendering him homeless;
   - (e) Emotional distress and mental anguish. *(Evidence: M1CL_01–M1CL_06, Damages Leverage Grid — "Billing, rent, utility, habitability, displacement leverage")*

55. Under 18 U.S.C. § 1964(c), Plaintiff is entitled to recover **treble damages**, costs, and reasonable attorney's fees.

#### RICO Conspiracy (18 U.S.C. § 1962(d))

56. Defendants also violated 18 U.S.C. § 1962(d) by conspiring to violate § 1962(c). Each Defendant agreed to participate in the enterprise's affairs through the pattern of racketeering activity described above, with knowledge of the enterprise's unlawful objectives.

### COUNT IV: BREACH OF WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY
*(Against Shady Oaks Park MHP and Homes of America LLC)*
*(Supplemental State Claim — 28 U.S.C. § 1367)*

62. Plaintiff realleges and incorporates by reference all prior paragraphs.

63. Under Michigan law, specifically MCL 554.139(1), a landlord who leases or permits the occupation of a residential premises covenants:
   - (a) That the premises and all common areas are fit for the use intended by the parties (MCL 554.139(1)(a)); and
   - (b) That the premises shall be kept in reasonable repair during the term of the lease (MCL 554.139(1)(b)).

64. These covenants are implied in every residential lease in Michigan and cannot be waived. MCL 554.139(3).

65. Defendants Shady Oaks and HOA breached the implied warranty of habitability by:
   - (a) Allowing raw sewage to leak on or near Plaintiff's lot from Spring through Summer 2025, creating a serious health hazard; *(Evidence: M1CL_03 — 228 signals; EGLE violations)*
   - (b) Failing to remediate the sewage condition for approximately 34 days after being notified by EGLE; *(Evidence: M1A_02647)*
   - (c) Shutting off Plaintiff's water service while a minor child was present; *(Evidence: federal/complaint_generator.py)*
   - (d) Failing to maintain the park infrastructure, including sewer lines and common areas, in reasonable repair;
   - (e) Continuing to charge rent and utility fees during the period the premises were uninhabitable.

66. As a direct and proximate result of Defendants' breach, Plaintiff suffered damages including diminished value of use of the premises, health risks from sewage exposure, costs of alternative water and sanitation, and ultimately the complete loss of housing.
