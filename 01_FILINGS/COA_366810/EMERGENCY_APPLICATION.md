## PRELIMINARY STATEMENT

This Emergency Application is filed pursuant to MCR 7.305(H), which authorizes this Court to grant immediate relief where a party demonstrates that "delay may cause substantial harm." Plaintiff Andrew J. Pigors respectfully submits that this case presents the most compelling circumstances for emergency intervention: a father and his young child have been completely separated for over 571 days through constitutionally void ex parte orders, and every additional day of delay causes measurable, compounding, and ultimately irreversible harm to the child during the most critical developmental period of his life.

---

## I. NATURE OF THE EMERGENCY

1. Father has been **completely separated from his minor child, Lane G., for more than 571 consecutive days** — approximately 19 months — as a direct result of ex parte orders entered by the 14th Circuit Court without evidentiary hearing, without findings of fact, and without due process of law.

2. Lane G. is approaching the age of **4 years old**. The separation has spanned approximately **one-third of her entire life** and virtually the entirety of her conscious memory formation.

3. This is not a case where the alleged harm is speculative or theoretical. The harm is occurring **right now, every day**, and it is measurable, documented, and — beyond a certain point — **irreversible**.

4. No court at any level has addressed the merits of the custody or parenting time issues. No hearing has been held. No findings have been made. The constitutional violation is total and ongoing.

---

## II. IRREPARABLE HARM FROM ONGOING SEPARATION

### A. Developmental Harm to Lane G.

5. The scientific and medical literature on childhood development is unequivocal: prolonged separation of a young child from a fit, bonded parent during the ages of 2-4 causes severe and often permanent developmental harm. Specifically:

   a. **Attachment disruption.** Children ages 2-4 are in a critical period of secure attachment formation. Prolonged absence of a primary attachment figure disrupts the child's internal working model of relationships, leading to insecure attachment patterns that persist into adulthood. *See* Bowlby, *Attachment and Loss* (1969); Ainsworth et al., *Patterns of Attachment* (1978).

   b. **Identity formation.** Children in this age range are forming their sense of self and family identity. The complete absence of a parent creates a gap in the child's self-concept that becomes more difficult to repair with each passing month.

   c. **Memory and bonding.** Episodic memory consolidation typically begins around age 3-4. If Father remains absent during this entire window, Lane G. will have **no conscious memory of ever having a relationship with her father**. This constitutes an irreversible loss that no future court order can restore.

   d. **Emotional regulation.** Young children develop emotional regulation skills through interaction with attachment figures. The absence of Father during this critical period impairs Lane G.'s ability to develop healthy emotional coping mechanisms.

6. These harms are not speculative. They are supported by decades of peer-reviewed research and are recognized by every major professional organization in child psychology and child development.

7. **The window is closing.** With each day that passes, the possibility of fully restoring the father-child relationship diminishes. A court order restoring parenting time after 2+ years of separation cannot undo the developmental damage already inflicted.

### B. Constitutional Harm to Father

8. Father's fundamental liberty interest in the care, custody, and control of his child is being violated **every single day** the ex parte orders remain in force. *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 US 57, 65 (2000); *Pierron v. Pierron*, 486 Mich 81, 91 (2010).

9. This deprivation cannot be compensated by damages. There is no monetary remedy for the loss of a parent-child relationship during a child's formative years. The injury is, by definition, irreparable.

10. The passage of 571+ days without hearing means that Father has been deprived of his most fundamental liberty interest for longer than many criminal defendants awaiting trial — yet Father has never been charged with, much less convicted of, any wrongdoing.

## IV. LIKELIHOOD OF SUCCESS ON THE MERITS

15. Father's likelihood of success on the merits is overwhelming:

### A. The Ex Parte Orders Are Constitutionally Void

16. The ex parte orders at issue were entered without:
    - Evidentiary hearing (required by MCL 722.27; *Vodvarka*)
    - Findings of fact (required by MCL 722.23; *Pierron*)
    - Emergency findings (required by MCR 3.207(B))
    - Notice and opportunity to be heard (required by Const 1963, Art 1, §17; US Const, Amend XIV)

17. Orders entered without the constitutional minimum of due process — particularly orders affecting fundamental liberty interests — are not merely voidable but **void**. An order entered without jurisdiction or in violation of due process has no legal force. *Armstrong v. Manzo*, 380 US 545, 550 (1965) ("A fundamental requirement of due process is the opportunity to be heard at a meaningful time and in a meaningful manner.") (internal quotation marks omitted).

### B. The Record Is Uncontested

18. The constitutional violations in this case are not subject to factual dispute. The record affirmatively demonstrates:
    - No hearing was held — this is a matter of record, not opinion;
    - No findings were made — the orders speak for themselves;
    - No emergency was found — the orders contain no such findings;
    - No notice was provided — the procedural history confirms this.

19. Because the violations are apparent from the face of the record, Father's likelihood of success does not depend on credibility determinations or factual findings by this Court.

### C. The Documented Violations Are Overwhelming

This documented record of institutional failure supports an overwhelming likelihood of success on every issue presented.

## V. BALANCE OF EQUITIES

21. The balance of equities overwhelmingly favors granting emergency relief.

### A. Father's Interest

22. Father's interest is the most fundamental one recognized in American law: the right to maintain a relationship with his child. *Troxel*, 530 US at 65. He has been deprived of this right for 571+ days without any finding of unfitness, harm, abuse, or neglect.

### B. The Child's Interest

23. Lane G.'s interest is in having a relationship with both parents. The Michigan Legislature has declared that "it is presumed to be in the best interests of a child to have a strong relationship with both of his or her parents." MCL 722.27a(1). Lane G.'s developmental well-being requires restoration of the father-child relationship before the critical window closes.

### C. Mother's Interest

24. Mother retains all rights and protections afforded by law. Restoring Father's parenting time does not deprive Mother of any legal right. If there are legitimate concerns regarding the child's safety, they can and should be evaluated through the proper process — an evidentiary hearing with findings — which is precisely what Father seeks.

25. No party has a cognizable interest in maintaining a constitutionally void status quo. The equities cannot favor enforcement of orders that were entered in violation of due process.

### D. The Public Interest

26. The public interest favors the enforcement of constitutional rights, compliance with statutory mandates, and the protection of children's developmental needs. There is no public interest in permitting trial courts to sever parent-child relationships without due process.
