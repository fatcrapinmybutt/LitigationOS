## PURPOSE

This document outlines the contingency plan for obtaining the record on appeal in the event that transcripts cannot be obtained from the court reporter. This plan is established pursuant to MCR 7.210(B)(2), which provides for a settled statement procedure as an alternative to transcripts.

---

## LEGAL AUTHORITY: MCR 7.210(B)(2) - SETTLED STATEMENT PROCEDURE

MCR 7.210(B)(2) provides that if a transcript is not available, the appellant may file a proposed settled statement of facts in lieu of the transcript. This rule ensures that an appellant's right to appeal is not defeated by the unavailability of transcripts.

---

## OVERVIEW

If transcripts cannot be obtained from the court reporter, the following steps apply:

1. **MCR 7.210(B)(2) allows filing a proposed settled statement of facts in lieu of transcript.** This is a recognized alternative when transcripts are unavailable for any reason.

2. **The appellant must prepare a proposed statement from best available sources**, including personal notes, recollection, exhibits admitted at the hearing, and any other available documentation of the proceedings.

3. **File proposed statement with the trial court within the time for filing transcripts.** The proposed settled statement must be filed within the same deadline that would apply for transcript filing.

4. **Serve opposing party, who has 14 days to object or propose amendments.** The opposing party is entitled to review the proposed statement and raise any objections or suggest modifications to ensure accuracy.

5. **Trial court settles the statement and certifies it as the record.** After considering any objections or amendments, the trial court issues a final settled statement that becomes the official record on appeal.

---

## REPORTER CERTIFICATE TIMING

Per MCR 7.210(B)(3), the court reporter has **56 days** after the filing of the claim of appeal to file transcripts with the trial court clerk.

If the reporter fails to file transcripts within the 56-day period, the appellant may:

- **Move for an extension of time** to allow the reporter additional time to complete the transcripts, OR
- **File a motion to proceed with a settled statement** under MCR 7.210(B)(2) in lieu of the unavailable transcripts.

---

## STEPS TO FILE PROPOSED SETTLED STATEMENT IF TRANSCRIPTS UNAVAILABLE

### Step 1: Request Transcript Status from Court Reporter

Contact the court reporter to determine the status of transcript preparation. Document all communications regarding transcript availability, including dates, methods of contact, and responses received.

### Step 2: If Unavailable, Prepare Proposed Settled Statement from All Available Sources

Compile a comprehensive proposed statement of facts using:
- Personal notes taken during hearings
- Recollection of proceedings
- Exhibits admitted during hearings
- Court filings and orders
- Any audio or video recordings if available
- Correspondence and documentation related to the proceedings

### Step 3: File Proposed Settled Statement with Trial Court Clerk

File the proposed settled statement with the Clerk of the 14th Circuit Court, Muskegon County, within the applicable deadline for transcript filing.

### Step 4: Serve Copy on All Parties

Serve a copy of the proposed settled statement on the opposing party (Appellee Emily A. Watson and/or her counsel, Jennifer L. Barnes, P55406) via the methods required by the court rules.

### Step 5: Wait 14 Days for Objections

The opposing party has 14 days from service to file objections to the proposed settled statement or to propose amendments.

### Step 6: If Objections Filed, Trial Court Holds Hearing to Settle the Statement

If the opposing party files objections or proposed amendments, the trial court will hold a hearing to resolve any disputes and settle the final statement of facts.

### Step 7: Certified Settled Statement Becomes Part of the Record on Appeal

Once the trial court settles and certifies the statement, it becomes the official record of proceedings for purposes of the appeal and is transmitted to the Court of Appeals as part of the lower court record.
