## II. BASIS FOR FEE WAIVER

### A. Statutory and Rule Authority

1. MCR 2.002 authorizes fee waivers for parties who are unable to pay fees and costs because of indigency.

2. MCR 2.002(B) provides that a party is eligible for a fee waiver if:
   - The party is receiving public assistance (MCR 2.002(B)(1)); or
   - The party's income is below 125% of the federal poverty guidelines (MCR 2.002(B)(2)); or
   - The party is otherwise unable to pay fees and costs (MCR 2.002(B)(3)).

### D. Nature and Importance of the Claims

7. This fee waiver request concerns filings of the highest constitutional importance — the protection of a father's fundamental liberty interest in the parent-child relationship and the protection of a child's developmental well-being during a critical window.

8. Father has been completely separated from his minor child for over 571 days without due process of law. Denying the fee waiver would effectively deny Father access to the only court with the jurisdiction and authority to provide relief in the time required to prevent irreparable harm.
