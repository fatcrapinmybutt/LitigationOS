### NATURE OF THE CASE

This is a domestic relations custody case in which a father has been completely separated from his minor child for over 571 consecutive days through a series of ex parte orders entered without evidentiary hearing, without findings of fact, without notice, and without any opportunity to be heard — in direct violation of the Due Process Clauses of the United States and Michigan Constitutions and in conflict with this Court's published authorities.

The father seeks leave to appeal, superintending control over the 14th Circuit Court, and emergency relief to prevent irreparable harm to both father and child during the child's critical developmental window.
