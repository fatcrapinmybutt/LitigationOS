## 1. MCL 722.27a - PARENTING TIME

### (1) Parenting Time Shall Be Granted in Best Interest of the Child

Parenting time shall be granted in accordance with the best interest of the child. It is presumed to be in the best interest of a child to have a strong relationship with both of his or her parents. Except as otherwise provided in this section, parenting time shall be granted to a parent in a frequency, duration, and type reasonably calculated to promote a strong relationship between the child and the parent granted parenting time.

### (3) Parenting Time Shall Not Be Denied Except on Clear and Convincing Evidence

A child has a right to parenting time with a parent unless it is shown on the record by clear and convincing evidence that it would endanger the child's physical, mental, or emotional health. The court shall not modify a parenting time order except for proper cause shown or because of a change of circumstances.

### (7) Makeup Parenting Time

If a parenting time order is violated by the custodial parent, the court shall make up denied parenting time. Makeup parenting time shall be provided in a manner that is in the best interests of the child.

### (9) Remedies for Denied Parenting Time

If the court determines that parenting time has been wrongfully denied, the court may do one or more of the following:

**(a)** Modify the existing parenting time order to grant additional parenting time to make up for wrongfully denied parenting time.

**(b)** Order the party who wrongfully denied parenting time to pay the other party's expenses, including but not limited to reasonable attorney fees, incurred as a result of the wrongful denial.

**(c)** Modify the existing custody order if the court determines that it is in the best interest of the child to do so.

**(d)** Find the party who wrongfully denied parenting time in contempt of court.

**(e)** Take any other action that the court determines is in the best interest of the child.

## 2. MCR 2.003 - DISQUALIFICATION OF JUDGE

### (C)(1)(a) Bias or Prejudice

Disqualification of a judge is warranted for reasons that include:

**(a)** The judge is biased or prejudiced for or against a party or attorney.

A party may file a motion to disqualify a judge based on a showing of actual bias or prejudice that would prevent the judge from impartially hearing the case. The bias must stem from an extrajudicial source or reveal such a high degree of favoritism or antagonism as to make fair judgment impossible.

### (C)(1)(b) Serious Risk of Actual Bias per Caperton

**(b)** The judge, based on objective and reasonable perceptions, has either (i) a serious risk of actual bias impacting the due process rights of a party as enunciated in Caperton v A.T. Massey Coal Co, 556 US 868; 129 S Ct 2252; 173 L Ed 2d 1208 (2009), or (ii) has failed to adhere to the appearance of impropriety standard set forth in Canon 2 of the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct.

## 3. MCR 3.206(C) / MCR 3.207(B)(1) - VERIFIED STATEMENTS / EX PARTE ORDERS IN DOMESTIC RELATIONS

### MCR 3.206(C) - Verified Statement Requirement

A complaint, petition, or other initiating document in a domestic relations action must be verified by the party or the party's attorney. The verification must state that the contents are true to the best of the signer's knowledge, information, and belief.

### MCR 3.207(B)(1) - Ex Parte Orders

Ex parte interim orders in domestic relations cases require:

A motion for an ex parte order must be supported by a verified complaint, petition, or affidavit containing **specific facts showing that immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage will result** from the delay required to effect notice or from the risk that notice will itself precipitate adverse action before an order can be issued.

The requirement for specific facts demonstrating immediate and irreparable injury is mandatory. General or conclusory allegations are insufficient to support the issuance of an ex parte order.

## 4. MICHIGAN CONSTITUTION 1963, ARTICLE 1, SECTION 17 - DUE PROCESS

> **"No person shall be compelled to be a witness against himself, nor be deprived of life, liberty or property, without due process of law."**

*Const 1963, Art 1, Sec 17*

The due process clause of the Michigan Constitution provides protections equivalent to, and in some cases broader than, the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution. Due process requires, at a minimum, notice and an opportunity to be heard before the government may deprive a person of a protected interest.

## 5. MCL 722.23 - BEST INTEREST OF THE CHILD FACTORS

As used in this act, "best interests of the child" means the sum total of the following factors to be considered, evaluated, and determined by the court:

**(a)** The love, affection, and other emotional ties existing between the parties involved and the child.

**(b)** The capacity and disposition of the parties involved to give the child love, affection, and guidance and to continue the education and raising of the child in his or her religion or creed, if any.

**(c)** The capacity and disposition of the parties involved to provide the child with food, clothing, medical care or other remedial care recognized and permitted under the laws of this state in place of medical care, and other material needs.

**(d)** The length of time the child has lived in a stable, satisfactory environment, and the desirability of maintaining continuity.

**(e)** The permanence, as a family unit, of the existing or proposed custodial home or homes.

**(f)** The moral fitness of the parties involved.

**(g)** The mental and physical health of the parties involved.

**(h)** The home, school, and community record of the child.

**(i)** The reasonable preference of the child, if the court considers the child to be of sufficient age to express preference.

**(j)** The willingness and ability of each of the parties to facilitate and encourage a close and continuing parent-child relationship between the child and the other parent or the child and the parents. A court may not consider negatively for the purposes of this factor any reasonable action taken by a parent to protect a child or that parent from sexual assault or domestic violence by the other parent.

**(k)** Domestic violence, regardless of whether the violence was directed against or witnessed by the child.

**(l)** Any other factor considered by the court to be relevant to a particular child custody dispute.

## 6. MCR 2.517(A)(1) - FINDINGS BY THE COURT

**(1)** In actions tried on the facts without a jury or with an advisory jury, the court shall find the facts specially, state separately its conclusions of law, and direct entry of the appropriate judgment. The findings and conclusions may be stated on the record or may appear in a written opinion.

The court is required to make specific findings of fact and conclusions of law sufficient to allow meaningful appellate review. Conclusory statements or failure to address all relevant factors constitute reversible error.
