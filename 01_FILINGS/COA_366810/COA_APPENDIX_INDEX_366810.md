## EX PARTE ORDER PATTERN — CRITICAL APPELLATE ISSUE

The record reflects that **24 of 55 orders (44%)** in this case were entered ex parte, **all 24 presided by Judge Jenny L. McNeill**. The August 8, 2025 ex parte orders are particularly significant:

- **Five separate ex parte orders** entered the same day suspending Plaintiff's parenting time
- **No prior notice** provided to Plaintiff before the exchange
- Plaintiff arrived expecting regular parenting time on August 8, 2025 and only discovered the suspension after the missed exchange
- Parenting time has been **functionally suspended from August 8, 2025 through present** — amounting to **329+ days of parent-child separation**

This pattern raises fundamental due process concerns under the Fourteenth Amendment and *Troxel v. Granville*, 530 US 57 (2000), as well as violations of MCR 3.207(B) (notice requirements for ex parte orders in domestic relations cases).
