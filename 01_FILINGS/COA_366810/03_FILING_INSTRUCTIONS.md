### Critical Timing Notes:
- The 90-day period runs from the **date of the MSC order**, not the date you receive notice
- If the MSC denies leave to appeal, that IS the final order — the clock starts immediately
- If the MSC grants leave and decides on the merits, the clock starts from that merits decision
- A motion for reconsideration at the MSC **does NOT toll** the 90-day period unless the MSC modifies its original order
- **Calendar the deadline immediately** upon receiving the MSC decision

## 3. FILING FEE

| Option | Amount | Format Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard filing** | **$300** | Booklet format (Rule 33.1) |
| **In Forma Pauperis (IFP)** | **$0** | 8½ × 11 paper (Rule 33.2) |

### Standard Filing ($300):
- Pay electronically through the e-filing system
- Fee is non-refundable regardless of outcome

### In Forma Pauperis (IFP) — No Fee:
- File a **Motion for Leave to Proceed In Forma Pauperis** simultaneously with the petition
- Must include an **affidavit or declaration of indigency** detailing:
  - Income (all sources)
  - Assets
  - Expenses
  - Debts
  - Number of dependents
- Must demonstrate **inability to pay** the $300 fee and costs of printing
- Must certify the petition is **not frivolous**
- **Advantage:** Relaxed format — standard paper size (8½ × 11), no booklet printing required
- **Disadvantage:** Statistically lower grant rate (but substantive quality matters most)

- Questions Presented

### IFP Format (Rule 33.2):
- Standard **8½ × 11 inch** paper
- **12-point font** (any standard font)

- Bound at upper left corner (staple or clip)
- Much simpler and cheaper to produce

## 8. AFTER FILING — WHAT TO EXPECT

1. **Docketing:** The Clerk's Office assigns a docket number (typically within a few days)
2. **Brief in Opposition:** Respondent has **30 days** to file (may request extension)
3. **Reply Brief:** Petitioner may file a reply within **10 days** of the opposition (optional but recommended)
4. **Distribution:** The petition is distributed to the Justices for conference
5. **Conference:** Justices vote on whether to grant cert (Rule of Four — 4 votes needed)
6. **Order:** Cert granted or denied, typically announced on a Monday order list
7. **Timeline:** Typically 3-6 months from filing to conference
