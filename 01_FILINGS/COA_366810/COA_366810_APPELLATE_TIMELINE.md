### Required Sections per MCR 7.212(C):

1. **Table of Contents** — with page references
2. **Index of Authorities** — statutes, rules, cases with page references
3. **Jurisdictional Statement** — basis for COA jurisdiction (MCR 7.203(A); appeal of right from final order)
4. **Statement of Questions Presented** — each issue stated separately; appellant's answer to each
5. **Statement of Facts** — with record citations (Lower Court Register of Actions; Transcript pages)
6. **Argument** — each issue with:
   - Standard of review
   - Legal authority
   - Application to facts
   - Record citations
7. **Relief Requested** — specific relief sought (reversal, remand, modification)
8. **Signature & Certificate of Service**

### Proposed Questions Presented:

I. Did the trial court abuse its discretion by entering an ex parte custody order without particularized evidence of imminent harm to the child, in violation of MCR 3.207(B) and the Due Process Clause?
   - *Appellant answers: Yes.*

II. Did the trial court violate MCR 3.207(B)(6) and the Fourteenth Amendment by failing to hold an evidentiary hearing within 14 days of the ex parte order, resulting in 119+ days of deprivation without hearing?
   - *Appellant answers: Yes.*

III. Did the trial court commit clear error in its established custodial environment determination, thereby applying the wrong burden of proof under MCL 722.27(1)(c)?
   - *Appellant answers: Yes.*

IV. Did the trial court abuse its discretion by failing to properly weigh all best interest factors under MCL 722.23, particularly Factor (j) (willingness to facilitate parent-child relationship)?
   - *Appellant answers: Yes.*

V. Did the trial court abuse its discretion by excluding relevant evidence (audio recording, mental health evaluation), thereby denying Appellant a fair hearing under MRE 401-403?
   - *Appellant answers: Yes.*

VI. Did the trial court's pattern of ex parte conduct and predetermined outcomes deny Appellant due process and require disqualification under MCR 2.003(C)(1)?
   - *Appellant answers: Yes.*
