I, Andrew Pigors, being duly sworn, depose and state as follows:


PERSONAL KNOWLEDGE AND COMPETENCY

1. I am the Plaintiff-Appellant in the above-captioned matter. I am over the age of eighteen (18) and am competent to testify to the matters stated herein. I make this affidavit based on my personal knowledge of the facts stated below.

2. I am the father of Lincoln Pigors (DOB 2022). I submit this affidavit in support of my Emergency Motion for Immediate Consideration and Temporary Relief Pending Appeal filed in this Court.


PARENT-CHILD RELATIONSHIP

3. Prior to the events giving rise to this litigation, I was an active, involved, and loving father to my son Lincoln. I regularly exercised parenting time and participated in all aspects of Lincoln's daily life, including his care, education, and development.

4. Lincoln and I share a deep and meaningful bond. I have been present in his life since birth and have consistently sought to maintain a close and continuing relationship with him.

5. I have never been found to have abused, neglected, or endangered my son. No court has ever made a finding, after a hearing on the merits, that contact between Lincoln and me would endanger Lincoln's physical, mental, or emotional health.


SEPARATION TIMELINE

6. On or about April 11, 2024, the 14th Circuit Court, Muskegon County, entered an ex parte order affecting my parenting time. This order was entered without notice to me and without a hearing.

7. Prior to the entry of this ex parte order, I was not served with a copy of the motion or any verified pleading. I had no advance notice that a motion affecting my parenting time had been filed or that any order had been entered.

8. My last in-person contact with my son Lincoln was on July 29, 2025. As of the date of this affidavit, I have not seen, spoken to, or had any form of contact with my son for approximately 214 days.

9. In total, I have been substantially or completely separated from my son for 569+ days. This is the longest period of separation in Lincoln's entire life.

10. Lincoln is approximately three years old. He is at a critical stage of development where the parent-child bond is being formed and consolidated. Extended separation at this age has been shown to cause lasting psychological harm.


EX PARTE ORDERS

11. During the course of these proceedings, the trial court entered a total of 24 ex parte orders. These ex parte orders were entered at critical junctures in the case and systematically affected my rights without affording me notice or an opportunity to be heard.

12. The initial ex parte order suspending my parenting time was signed by Judge McNeill without prior notice to me. I learned of the order after it was already in effect.

13. I was not afforded an opportunity to present evidence or argument before the ex parte suspension was imposed.

14. When an evidentiary hearing was eventually held, I attempted to present exculpatory photographic evidence from the July 29, 2025 exchange showing Lincoln's condition. The trial court refused to review or consider this evidence.

15. At the same hearing, the trial court considered and relied upon ex parte submissions from Defendant, including unsworn statements and materials that had not been provided to me in advance and to which I had no opportunity to respond.

16. A USB recording and a mental health evaluation were handled off-record and ex parte, outside my knowledge or participation. I was not informed that these materials were being submitted to the court, was not provided copies, and was not given an opportunity to respond.


$250 FILING SANCTION

17. The trial court entered an order imposing a $250 filing bond, stating: "It is further ordered that the Plaintiff must pay a bond of $250 (for each motion) before he may file another motion in this matter. The Court will then review the motion."

18. This order requires me to pay $250 before the court will even read any motion I file. This is not a sanction imposed after adjudication of a frivolous motion — it is a pre-filing financial barrier that applies to every future motion, regardless of its merit.

19. I am proceeding pro se because I cannot afford an attorney. The $250-per-motion filing bond is a significant financial burden that I cannot consistently bear. As a practical matter, this sanction prevents me from seeking any relief in the trial court, including:
      A motion to modify or restore parenting time
      A motion for reconsideration of any order
      A motion for disqualification of the trial judge
      Any other motion seeking relief from the ongoing separation

20. Because of this filing sanction, I have no effective remedy in the trial court. The Michigan Court of Appeals is my only available forum for seeking relief.


DOCUMENTED JUDICIAL VIOLATIONS

21. Through a comprehensive review of the record in this case, I have identified and documented 1,127 judicial violations committed by the trial court. These violations are categorized by severity as follows:
      Critical severity: 377 violations
      High severity: 243 violations
      Medium severity: 484 violations
      Low severity: 23 violations

22. The critical-severity violations include, but are not limited to:
      Ex parte communications and orders in violation of MCR 2.003 and the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 3(A)(4)
      Entry of ex parte custody and parenting-time orders without compliance with MCR 3.207(B)
      Off-record, ex parte handling of evidence (USB recordings and mental health evaluations)
      Refusal to consider exculpatory evidence offered by Appellant
      Systematic denial of Appellant's right to present witnesses and evidence at hearings
      Failure to make required best-interest findings under MCL 722.23(a)-(l)

23. These violations are documented in a comprehensive database that cross-references each violation to the specific court rule, canon, or statute violated, along with the supporting record evidence.


FAILURE TO MAKE BEST-INTEREST FINDINGS

24. The trial court has not entered written findings on each of the twelve best-interest factors set forth in MCL 722.23(a)-(l) in connection with the suspension of my parenting time.

25. Specifically, the trial court has not made findings regarding:
    (a) The love, affection, and emotional ties between me and Lincoln
    (b) My capacity and disposition to give Lincoln love, affection, and guidance
    (c) My capacity to provide Lincoln with food, clothing, and medical care
    (d) The length of time Lincoln lived in a stable environment with both parents
    (e) The permanence of the custodial home
    (f) My moral fitness
    (g) My mental and physical health
    (h) Lincoln's home, school, and community record
    (i) Lincoln's reasonable preference (though at age three, this may not be applicable)
    (j) Each party's willingness to facilitate a close and continuing parent-child relationship — a factor of critical importance given the circumstances
    (k) Any findings regarding domestic violence
    (l) Any other relevant factors

26. The absence of these findings means that the most consequential order in these proceedings — the complete suspension of my parenting time — was entered without the factual and legal analysis required by Michigan law.


FRIEND OF THE COURT

27. The Friend of the Court (FOC) has been involved in these proceedings but has not, to my knowledge, conducted a proper independent investigation as required by MCL 552.505.

28. The FOC's involvement has appeared to rely primarily on Defendant's representations without independent verification of the facts.


HARM TO THE CHILD

29. Lincoln is approximately three years old. At this age, children are actively forming and consolidating attachment bonds. Since the suspension of my parenting time, I have observed through indirect reports that Lincoln has asked for me and expressed confusion about why I am no longer present. I am deeply concerned, based on my knowledge of my son and my experience as his parent, that this prolonged separation is harming him. Specifically, I am concerned about:
      The disruption of the attachment bond we had been building since his birth
      Confusion and distress about the sudden absence of his father
      The increasing difficulty of rebuilding our relationship the longer the separation continues
      The risk that the separation is being used to erode Lincoln's bond with me

30. I am deeply concerned that the prolonged separation is causing Lincoln significant emotional and psychological harm. A child of this age cannot understand why his father has suddenly disappeared from his life.

31. I am further concerned that the extended separation is being used to alienate Lincoln from me, making future reunification progressively more difficult. The longer I am absent from Lincoln's daily life, the more the bond we share will deteriorate.


EFFORTS TO RESOLVE

32. I have made every effort to resolve this matter through the trial court. I have filed motions, attended hearings, and attempted to present evidence supporting restoration of parenting time.

33. My efforts have been thwarted by: (a) the $250 filing sanction that prevents me from filing motions; (b) the trial court's refusal to consider exculpatory evidence; and (c) the pattern of ex parte adjudication that has denied me meaningful participation in these proceedings.

34. I filed a timely Claim of Appeal in this Court (Case No. 366810), which is currently pending. However, the ordinary appellate timeline will result in many additional months of separation before any relief is available.

35. I respectfully submit that the circumstances of this case — 569+ days of separation, 24 ex parte orders, a $250 filing sanction blocking access to the trial court, and 1,127 documented judicial violations — constitute an emergency that warrants this Court's immediate intervention.


RELIEF SOUGHT

36. I respectfully request that this Court:
    a. Grant immediate consideration of my Emergency Motion;
    b. Enter a temporary order restoring reasonable parenting time between me and Lincoln, on such terms and conditions (including supervised visitation) as this Court deems appropriate;
    c. Appoint an independent Guardian ad Litem to investigate and report on Lincoln's best interests;
    d. Order the trial court to permit me to file motions without the $250 filing sanction;
    e. Grant an expedited briefing schedule given the ongoing irreparable harm; and
    f. Grant any other relief this Court deems just and proper.


VERIFICATION

I declare under the penalties of perjury that the statements in this affidavit are true and correct to the best of my knowledge, information, and belief.
