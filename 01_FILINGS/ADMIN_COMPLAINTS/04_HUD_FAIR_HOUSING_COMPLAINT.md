## III. BASIS OF COMPLAINT

### A. Protected Class: Familial Status (42 USC §3604(a))

Complainant is a **father with a minor child** (L.D.W., born November 9, 2022). The Fair Housing Act, 42 USC §3604, prohibits discrimination based on familial status in the sale, rental, and conditions of housing.

### B. Nature of Discriminatory Conduct

The Respondents' conduct constitutes housing discrimination on the basis of familial status and constitutes additional violations of the Fair Housing Act through:

#### 1. Discriminatory Terms and Conditions (42 USC §3604(b))

Respondents imposed discriminatory terms and conditions of tenancy on Complainant:

- **Coerced lease modification:** Rent increased from $325/month to $695/month (114%) under threat of eviction, while similarly situated tenants without custody disputes received standard increases
- **Selective enforcement:** Maintenance complaints by Complainant were ignored while similar complaints by other tenants received responses
- **Retaliatory billing:** After Complainant raised habitability complaints, Respondents generated three irreconcilable ledger versions showing fabricated arrears of $6,085.99

#### 2. Interference with Housing Rights (42 USC §3617)

Respondents interfered with Complainant's exercise of fair housing rights:

- **Blocked home sales:** Three attempts to sell Complainant's manufactured home were obstructed by park management, in violation of both FHA §3617 and Michigan MCL 125.2319
- **Retaliatory eviction:** After Complainant reported sewage violations to EGLE (Violation VN-017235) and challenged billing fraud, Respondents escalated adverse actions
- **Physical intimidation:** Agent Nicole Browley drilled locks on Complainant's home and posted a "FREE" sign — destroying Complainant's property interest and violating 42 USC §3617

#### 3. Discriminatory Impact on Custody Proceedings

The housing conditions created by Respondents' conduct directly impacted Complainant's custody proceedings in Muskegon County Circuit Court (Case No. 2024-001507-DC):

- Manufactured housing instability was weaponized by the opposing party in custody proceedings to argue Complainant was "unfit"
- The deliberate destruction of housing conditions (sewage, lock drilling, blocked sales) created a feedback loop: bad housing → custody denial → inability to improve housing → continued custody denial
- This pattern disproportionately impacts parents (familial status) who are in custody disputes, as housing instability is a factor in MCL 722.23 best interest analysis

### C. Pattern and Practice (42 USC §3614)

The Respondents' conduct is part of a broader pattern affecting Alden Global Capital's manufactured housing portfolio:

- **North Morris Estates LLC** (another Alden-controlled community) has a **criminal conviction** for operating an unlicensed manufactured housing community — demonstrating pattern corporate disregard for housing regulations
- **Alden Global Capital** controls a $275M manufactured housing portfolio across multiple states, creating potential for systemic discrimination
- The corporate structure (dissolved entities, shell companies, opaque ownership) is designed to evade accountability for fair housing violations
