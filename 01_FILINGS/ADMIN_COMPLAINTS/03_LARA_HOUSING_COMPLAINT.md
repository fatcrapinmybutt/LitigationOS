## III. NATURE OF VIOLATIONS

### A. Michigan Mobile Home Commission Act (MCL 125.2301 et seq.)

The following violations of the Mobile Home Commission Act are documented:

#### 1. MCL 125.2307 — License Requirements (CRITICAL)

Shady Oaks Park MHP, LLC, the entity that operated the community and collected rent, was **dissolved** as a New Jersey LLC circa 2022. Michigan law requires that the licensed operator of a manufactured housing community be a legally existing entity. A dissolved entity cannot hold or maintain a valid MHC license.

**Impact:** All actions taken by the dissolved entity — including rent collection, lease enforcement, eviction proceedings, and lien assertions — are ultra vires and potentially void. *See* MCL 450.4802 (effect of dissolution on LLC authority).

#### 2. MCL 125.2319 — Prohibition on Interference with Home Sales (CRITICAL)

The Act prohibits community operators from interfering with a resident's right to sell their manufactured home. Documented violations include:

- **Three (3) blocked home sales:** Complainant attempted to sell his manufactured home on at least three occasions. Each sale was obstructed by park management through:
  - Refusing to approve qualified buyers without legitimate basis
  - Imposing unreasonable conditions not in the lease or park rules
  - Providing false negative information about the community to prospective buyers
  - Agent Nicole Browley personally harassed prospective buyers
- **Witness corroboration:** [ANDREW_REQUIRED: name of witness] can attest to property destruction and lock drilling by park agents

**Statutory remedy:** MCL 125.2319 provides that a community operator "shall not deny a purchaser occupancy based on the condition of the mobile home if the home met the community's standards at the time of sale."

#### 3. MCL 125.2331 — Retaliatory Eviction Prohibited

The Act prohibits retaliatory eviction or adverse actions against residents who exercise their legal rights. Complainant experienced:

- **Retaliatory conduct following maintenance complaints:** After reporting sewage discharge (EGLE Violation VN-017235) and habitability issues, park management escalated adverse actions
- **Retaliatory conduct following rent dispute:** After Complainant challenged fraudulent billing practices ($6,085.99 in disputed charges across 3 irreconcilable ledger versions), park management escalated eviction proceedings
- **Coerced lease modification:** Complainant was forced to sign a new lease under a different entity name (Cricklewood MHP LLC) at more than double the original rent ($325/month → $695/month) under duress

#### 4. MCL 125.2304 — Community Standards and Maintenance

The operator failed to maintain the community in compliance with minimum standards:

- **Sewage system failure:** Raw untreated sewage surfacing near residential lots (documented in EGLE Violation VN-017235)

### B. Billing Fraud and Financial Violations

1. **Three irreconcilable ledger versions:** Park management produced three different ledger documents showing different payment histories and balances for the same account. This constitutes billing fraud.

2. **$6,085.99 in disputed charges:** Complainant's records show payments totaling $6,085.99 that were either omitted from ledgers or rejected/returned without explanation. Complainant's actual balance is **negative $2,280.55** (overpayment).

3. **$1,300.26 payment fraud:** A check payable to "Shady Oaks MHP" was cashed by "Partridge Securities" — an entity not disclosed to Complainant and not a party to any lease agreement.

4. **Coerced rent increase:** Rent was increased from $325/month to $695/month (114% increase) through a coerced lease modification signed under threat of eviction.

### C. Corporate Structure Fraud

The community operates through a deliberately opaque corporate structure designed to evade accountability:

```
Alden Global Capital, LLC (New York — $275M portfolio)
  └─ Homes of America, LLC (Delaware — management)
       ├─ Shady Oaks Park MHP, LLC (NJ — DISSOLVED circa 2022)
       ├─ Cricklewood MHP LLC (used for coerced lease)
       ├─ Partridge Equity Group / Partridge Securities (cashed checks)
       └─ VRM Capital Corp (financial/asset holding)
```

This structure violates the purpose of the Mobile Home Commission Act, which requires a clearly identified, licensed operator responsible for community compliance.
