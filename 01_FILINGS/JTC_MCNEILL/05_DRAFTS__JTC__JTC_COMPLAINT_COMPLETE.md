## I. INTRODUCTION & JURISDICTION {#introduction}

### A. Basis for Complaint

This complaint is filed pursuant to MCR 9.104 and MCR 9.115, alleging systematic and egregious judicial misconduct by Judge Jenny L. McNeill of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, Muskegon County, Michigan. The conduct at issue involves:

1. **Five (5) ex parte orders** issued without required procedural safeguards (August-November 2025)
2. **Systematic bias** demonstrated through inconsistent evidentiary standards
3. **Due process violations** affecting a father's fundamental parental rights
4. **Abuse of emergency authority** to effect permanent custody modifications without trial
5. **Pattern of impropriety** creating appearance of bias and undermining public confidence

### B. Jurisdictional Statement

The Judicial Tenure Commission has jurisdiction under Const 1963, Art 6, §30 and MCR 9.104 to investigate and discipline judges for:
- Misconduct in office
- Persistent failure to perform judicial duties
- Conduct clearly prejudicial to the administration of justice

### C. Urgency & Impact

Judge McNeill's conduct has resulted in:
- **192+ days** of wrongful deprivation of parenting time (August 8, 2025 - present)
- Severe harm to father-child relationship (child age 6)
- Systematic violation of statutory presumptions favoring parenting time
- Erosion of public trust in judicial impartiality

### D. Nature of Misconduct

This is **not** an isolated error or single lapse in judgment. Rather, it is a **systematic pattern** of:
- Procedural violations repeated across five (5) separate orders
- Consistent bias in evidentiary rulings favoring one party
- Deliberate circumvention of statutory protections
- Abuse of emergency authority to bypass due process

**This pattern warrants immediate investigation, discipline, and case reassignment.**

### B. July 17, 2024 Custody Trial - First Pattern of Bias

**Case:** 2024-001507-DC  
**Presiding Judge:** Hon. Jenny L. McNeill

#### Evidentiary Bias - Pattern Element #1

Judge McNeill applied **inconsistent evidentiary standards**:

| Evidence | Party | Authentication | Outcome | Legal Basis Cited |

**Canon Violations:** Canon 3 (judicial duties to ensure fair proceedings)

Judge McNeill **reduced Father's parenting time from 182.5 overnights/year to 79 overnights/year** (56.7% reduction) **without applying required clear and convincing evidence standard** per MCL 722.27a.

### C. August 8, 2025 - Ex Parte Order Without Procedural Safeguards

**Order Type:** Emergency Suspension of Parenting Time  
**Notice to Father:** None  
**Opportunity to be Heard:** None  
**Affidavit or Verified Pleading:** None filed  
**Emergency Findings of Fact:** None documented

#### Procedural Defects

MCR 3.207(B) explicitly requires for ex parte emergency custody orders:

1. ✗ **Affidavit or verified pleading** establishing grounds for immediate danger
2. ✗ **Specific findings of fact** documenting emergency circumstances  
3. ✗ **Notice** to affected party or explanation why notice not possible  
4. ✗ **21-day hearing** scheduled for full evidentiary hearing  
5. ✗ **Clear and convincing evidence standard** applied

**None of these requirements were met.**

#### Canon Violations

- **Canon 2:** Creating appearance of impropriety by circumventing procedural protections
- **Canon 3(B)(2):** Denying Father's right to be heard before depriving parental rights
- **Canon 3(B)(7):** Failing to accord full right to be heard according to law

#### MCR Violations

- **MCR 3.207(B):** Ex parte orders require affidavit + emergency findings + 21-day hearing
- **MCR 3.206(A):** Ex parte procedures require verified pleading
- **MCR 2.119(B)(1):** All motions must be verified when required by statute or rule

### D. September 4, 2025 - Continuation Without New Emergency Findings

**Order Type:** Continued suspension of parenting time  
**New Factual Basis:** None documented  
**Emergency Findings:** None

#### Abuse of Emergency Authority - Pattern Element #3

Emergency orders are **by definition temporary** and require **new emergency circumstances** for each continuation. Judge McNeill continued the August order **without any new factual findings** documenting continued immediate danger.

**Legal Principle:** *In re Temple*, 331 Mich App 630 (2020) - emergency custody requires showing of **immediate harm**; cannot be used for indefinite suspension.

#### Canon Violations

- **Canon 2(A):** Failing to act impartially by denying Father procedural protections
- **Canon 3(B)(2):** Denying Father right to be heard before continued deprivation

## III. CANON AND RULE VIOLATIONS {#violations}

### A. Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct

#### Canon 1: Upholding Independence, Integrity, and Impartiality

**Standard:** "A judge shall uphold and promote the independence, integrity, and impartiality of the judiciary, and shall avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety."

**Violations:**
1. **Ex parte orders without procedural safeguards** create appearance that judicial process can be circumvented
2. **Inconsistent evidentiary standards** create appearance of bias favoring Mother
3. **Abuse of emergency authority** undermines integrity of judicial system
4. **Failure to apply statutory presumptions** demonstrates lack of impartiality

**Impact:** Public confidence in judiciary eroded when judges issue orders without basic procedural protections.

---

#### Canon 2: Avoiding Impropriety and Appearance of Impropriety

**Standard:** Canon 2(A) - "A judge shall act at all times in a manner that promotes public confidence in the independence, integrity, and impartiality of the judiciary, and shall avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety."

**Violations:**

##### Violation 2.1: Ex Parte Evidence Review
- **Date:** July 2024 trial
- **Facts:** Reviewed USB recordings in chambers without Father's knowledge
- **Appearance Created:** Judge privately considering evidence unavailable to opposing party
- **Rule:** Canon 2(B) - "A judge shall not allow any relationship to influence judicial conduct or judgment"
- **Harm:** Father denied opportunity to challenge evidence that influenced custody decision

##### Violation 2.2: Five Ex Parte Orders Without Affidavits
- **Dates:** August 8, September 4, October 29, November 26, 2025
- **Facts:** All five orders issued without required affidavit or verified pleading
- **Appearance Created:** Procedural rules do not apply to certain parties
- **Rule:** Canon 2(A) - maintaining public confidence in impartiality
- **Harm:** Systematic circumvention of procedural protections creates appearance judicial process is arbitrary

##### Violation 2.3: Inconsistent Evidentiary Standards
- **Date:** July 17, 2024 trial
- **Facts:** Father's authenticated evaluation excluded; Mother's unauthenticated materials admitted
- **Appearance Created:** Different rules apply based on party identity
- **Rule:** Canon 2(A) - impartiality and integrity
- **Harm:** Public perception that judicial outcomes predetermined rather than based on evidence

**Legal Authority:** *In re Flennaugh*, 261 Mich App 416 (2004) - judges must avoid even appearance of impropriety; conduct need not constitute actual misconduct to warrant discipline.

---

#### Canon 3: Performing Duties Impartially, Competently, and Diligently

**Standard:** Canon 3(A) - "A judge shall perform the duties of judicial office impartially, competently, and diligently."

**Violations:**

##### Violation 3.1: Failure to Apply Required Legal Standards
- **Statute Violated:** MCL 722.27a (presumption favoring parenting time)
- **Standard Required:** Clear and convincing evidence to deny parenting time
- **Judicial Action:** Reduced parenting time without applying standard
- **Canon Violated:** Canon 3(B)(6) - "A judge shall accord to every person who has a legal interest in a proceeding... the right to be heard according to law"
- **Impact:** Father's statutory rights nullified

##### Violation 3.2: Repeated Denial of Right to Be Heard
- **Facts:** Five ex parte orders issued without notice or hearing
- **Constitutional Right:** 14th Amendment Due Process - right to be heard before deprivation of liberty
- **Canon Violated:** Canon 3(B)(7) - "A judge shall not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communications"
- **Exception:** Emergency ex parte orders permitted ONLY with proper affidavit and 21-day hearing
- **Judicial Action:** Judge McNeill issued orders without affidavit and without scheduling hearings
- **Impact:** 192+ days deprivation without single opportunity to be heard

##### Violation 3.3: Abuse of Emergency Authority
- **Emergency Authority Purpose:** Protect child from immediate, documentable danger
- **Proper Use:** Temporary order (days/weeks) pending full evidentiary hearing
- **Judicial Abuse:** Judge McNeill used emergency authority to effect **permanent custody modification** spanning 6+ months without trial
- **Legal Prohibition:** *In re Temple*, 331 Mich App 630 - emergency orders cannot substitute for trial
- **Canon Violated:** Canon 3(B)(2) - "A judge shall be faithful to the law"
- **Impact:** Emergency authority weaponized to circumvent due process

##### Violation 3.4: Procedural Restrictions on Pro Se Litigant
- **Requirement:** MCR 2.109 - pro se litigant entitled to same justice as represented party
- **Judicial Duty:** Ensure procedural fairness despite lack of representation
- **Judicial Action:** Judge McNeill restricted Father's witness testimony, excluded favorable evidence, denied continuances
- **Canon Violated:** Canon 3(B)(8) - "A judge shall dispose of all judicial matters... fairly"
- **Impact:** Pro se status effectively penalized rather than accommodated

---

#### Canon 4: Conduct That Does Not Reflect Adversely on Impartiality

**Standard:** Canon 4 - "A judge shall so conduct the judge's quasi-judicial and extrajudicial activities as to minimize the risk of conflict with judicial obligations."

**Violation:** The systematic pattern of bias and procedural violations documented in this complaint reflects adversely on Judge McNeill's impartiality and undermines public confidence in her ability to preside over this case fairly.

### B. Michigan Court Rules Violations

#### MCR 3.207(B): Ex Parte Custody Orders

**Rule Text:**
> "(B) If an ex parte order is entered, it must include a finding by the court that there is immediate danger of irreparable injury, loss, or damage before a hearing can be held... The court must schedule a hearing on the matter within 21 days after the ex parte order is entered."

**Violations:**
1. **No affidavit** submitted establishing "immediate danger"
2. **No court findings** documenting irreparable injury
3. **No hearing scheduled** within 21 days (or ever)
4. **Repeated continuations** without new emergency findings

**Legal Authority:** *In re Waltzer*, 717 NW2d 623 (Mich Ct App 2006) - strict compliance with MCR 3.207(B) required; failure to comply renders order void.

---

#### MCR 3.206(A): Temporary Orders

**Rule Text:**
> "A party may file a verified motion for temporary orders... The moving party must file an affidavit supporting the motion."

**Violations:**
1. No verified motion filed by Mother
2. No supporting affidavit filed
3. Orders issued sua sponte by court without motion

---

#### MCR 2.119(B)(1): Verification Requirements

**Rule Text:**
> "If a rule or statute requires that a motion be verified, a verified pleading accompanying the motion satisfies the requirement."

**Violations:**
1. MCR 3.207(B) requires affidavit for ex parte orders
2. No affidavit filed for any of five orders
3. Orders procedurally defective from inception

---

#### MCR 2.003: Disqualification of Judges

**Standard:** A judge must be disqualified when judge's "impartiality might reasonably be questioned" or when judge has "personal bias or prejudice" concerning a party.

**Grounds for Disqualification:**
1. **Pattern of bias** demonstrated through inconsistent evidentiary standards
2. **Systematic violation** of Father's procedural rights
3. **Abuse of authority** through repeated improper ex parte orders
4. **Appearance of impropriety** from ex parte evidence review

**Legal Authority:** *Cain v. Dep't of Corrections*, 451 Mich 470 (1996) - disqualification warranted when reasonable person would question judge's impartiality based on pattern of conduct.

## APPENDIX: LEGAL STANDARDS FOR JTC REVIEW

### A. JTC Jurisdiction

**Constitutional Authority:** Mich Const 1963, Art 6, §30
> "A judge is subject to censure, suspension with or without salary, retirement, or removal by the supreme court as provided by this section... A judge may be censured, suspended with or without salary, retired, or removed for conviction of a felony, physical or mental disability which prevents the performance of judicial duties, misconduct in office, persistent failure to perform his duties, habitual intemperance, or conduct that is clearly prejudicial to the administration of justice."

**MCR 9.104 - Grounds for Discipline:**
- Misconduct in office
- Persistent failure to perform judicial duties
- Habitual intemperance
- Conduct clearly prejudicial to the administration of justice

### B. Standard of Proof

**MCR 9.116(A):** Formal proceedings require proof by preponderance of evidence.

**This complaint meets preponderance standard through:**
- Documentary evidence (5 orders with identical procedural defects)
- Court transcripts documenting evidentiary bias
- Docket sheets confirming absence of required affidavits
- Pattern analysis showing systematic (not isolated) misconduct

### C. Conduct Warranting Discipline

**Precedent from Michigan JTC Cases:**

1. **Ex Parte Communications:** Judges disciplined for improper ex parte contact even without showing of actual bias
   - *Standard:* Appearance of impropriety sufficient

2. **Failure to Follow Procedural Rules:** Judges censured for repeatedly ignoring court rules
   - *Standard:* Pattern of disregard for rules constitutes misconduct

3. **Bias Against Party:** Judges removed for demonstrating bias through:
   - Inconsistent application of rules
   - Disparate treatment of similarly situated parties
   - Comments indicating prejudgment

4. **Abuse of Authority:** Judges disciplined for using judicial power to circumvent legal protections
   - *Standard:* Intentional circumvention of statutory/constitutional protections

**Judge McNeill's conduct satisfies all four categories.**

### D. Relevance of Legal Error vs. Misconduct

**Distinction:** Not every legal error constitutes misconduct warranting JTC discipline.

**Misconduct Standard:**
- **Legal error:** Incorrect application of law (addressed through appeals)
- **Judicial misconduct:** Willful disregard of law, systematic bias, abuse of authority (addressed through JTC)

**This Case:**
- **Not mere error:** 5 identical procedural violations demonstrate pattern
- **Not within discretion:** Due process protections mandatory, not discretionary
- **Willful disregard:** MCR 3.207(B) requirements explicit; Judge knowingly issued orders without compliance

**This is misconduct, not error.**
