## INSTRUCTIONS FOR SERVICE

### For Certified Mail Service:

1. **Package Contents:** Place the Complaint, Verification, and all exhibits in an envelope or package suitable for mailing.

2. **Certified Mail:** Take the package to a U.S. Post Office and request "Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested" service. This provides proof of mailing and delivery.

3. **Tracking Number:** Record the certified mail tracking number on this Certificate of Service.

4. **Return Receipt:** When you receive the green return receipt card showing delivery, attach it to your file copy of this Certificate of Service as proof of delivery.

5. **Cost:** As of 2026, certified mail with return receipt costs approximately $10-15. This cost may be included in any subsequent fee waiver request if applicable.

### For Electronic Filing (if available):

1. **Check Availability:** Contact the Judicial Tenure Commission at (313) 875-5110 or visit their website to determine whether electronic filing is available and accepted.

2. **Electronic Format:** If electronic filing is permitted, convert all documents to PDF format with exhibits bookmarked for easy navigation.

3. **File Size:** Verify any file size limitations. If the complete filing exceeds size limits, you may need to file in multiple parts or use a secure file transfer method.

4. **Confirmation:** Save the electronic filing confirmation or receipt as proof of service. Print a copy and attach to your file copy of this Certificate of Service.

5. **Email Confirmation:** If filing by email (jtc@courts.mi.gov), request a read receipt and save the sent email as proof of transmission.

### For Personal Delivery:

1. **Location:** Judicial Tenure Commission office is located at 3034 W. Grand Blvd., Suite 8-450, Detroit, MI 48202.

2. **Business Hours:** Verify business hours by calling (313) 875-5110 before traveling to deliver documents in person.

3. **Receipt:** Request that JTC staff provide a date-stamped copy of the first page of the Complaint as proof of filing. Have staff sign this Certificate of Service acknowledging receipt, or obtain a separate receipt.

4. **Number of Copies:** Bring at least two copies: one for JTC and one to be date-stamped and returned to you as proof of filing.

## ADDITIONAL FILING INFORMATION

### Filing Fees

Pursuant to MCR 9.115, there is **no filing fee** for complaints to the Judicial Tenure Commission.

### Confidentiality

Pursuant to MCR 9.116(A), JTC proceedings are **confidential** unless and until the Commission files a complaint with the Michigan Supreme Court seeking formal discipline. Maintain confidentiality of the investigation process and do not publicly disclose that you have filed a JTC complaint unless you have received advice from legal counsel that such disclosure is appropriate.

### Timeline for JTC Response

Pursuant to MCR 9.112, the JTC has **168 days** (approximately 5.5 months) from the date of filing to complete its investigation and determine whether to:
- Dismiss the complaint;
- Issue a private admonishment;
- File formal charges with the Michigan Supreme Court.

You will be notified of the JTC's decision.

### Follow-Up

- Keep copies of all filed documents, proof of service, and any correspondence with the JTC.
- You may contact the JTC after 90 days to request a status update on the investigation.
- Respond promptly to any requests from the JTC for additional information or testimony.
