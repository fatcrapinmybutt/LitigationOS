## II. SUMMARY OF VIOLATIONS BY CANON

### CATEGORY 1: Ex Parte Communications — Canon 3(A)(4) MCJC

*A judge shall not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communications.*

¶4. **52 ex parte orders** were entered across proceedings. Twenty-four (24) of fifty-five (55) primary orders (44%) were entered ex parte. **100% favored Defendant Emily A. Watson.** Zero favored Plaintiff-Father.

¶5. **August 5, 2025 — USB Recording Review:** Defendant submitted a USB recording to the Court. Judge McNeill had her staff listen to the recording BEFORE any hearing was held. The Judge admitted on the record: *"Yes, I had my staff listen to it."* The recording was made by Lori Watson without the consent of all parties, in violation of MCL 750.539c (felony eavesdropping). The Court considered illegally obtained evidence ex parte.

¶6. **August 8, 2025 — Five Ex Parte Orders in One Day:** Five separate ex parte orders were entered in a single day. All against Plaintiff. All favoring Defendant. No sworn affidavit. No hearing. No notice. No best-interest analysis.

¶7. **October 29, 2025 — Pamela Rusco Ex Parte Call:** Pamela Rusco (Friend of the Court / Judge's secretary) made a twelve (12)-minute ex parte telephone call to HealthWest regarding Plaintiff's mental health evaluation. This call is documented in a Chart Memo authored by clinician Melissa DeAugustine, LBSW.

¶8. **September 8/9, 2025 — Court-Initiated Second Evaluation:** The Court sent a letter to HealthWest requesting a second evaluation AFTER Plaintiff's first evaluation returned all scores at zero. The Court's letter became the sole basis for elevating Plaintiff's Psychosis score from 0 to 2 in the second evaluation.

¶9. The Court instructed Plaintiff to EMAIL favorable evaluation results to the Judge's secretary rather than file with the Clerk — preventing favorable evidence from becoming part of the public record.

### CATEGORY 2: Denial of Right to Be Heard — Canon 3(B)(2) MCJC

*A judge shall afford to every person who has a legal interest in a proceeding the right to be heard according to law.*

¶10. **Muting:** Judge McNeill muted Plaintiff seven (7) times during a single hearing, preventing him from presenting his case.

¶11. **Filing Ban:** Judge McNeill stated on the record: *"Do not file any more. I will not look at it."* No vexatious litigant finding was ever entered under MCR 2.625 or any other legal authority. This constitutes an extrajudicial ban on Plaintiff's right to petition the court.

¶12. **No Hearing:** On May 16, 2025, the Court entered an order "without a hearing."

¶13. **Bond Barrier:** The Court imposed a $250.00 bond as a prerequisite to court access. No indigency inquiry was conducted as required by *Boddie v Connecticut*, 401 US 371; 91 S Ct 780; 28 L Ed 2d 113 (1971). This effectively barred an indigent pro se litigant from the courthouse.

¶14. **Zero Business Days:** Plaintiff was served on August 8, 2025 (Friday). The first hearing was August 11, 2025 (Monday). Zero business days to prepare a defense to five ex parte orders suspending all parental rights.

¶15. **Attorney Suppression:** Plaintiff's attorney, Mandi Martini, told Plaintiff: *"You are not allowed to talk, the judge is in a bad mood today."*

### CATEGORY 3: Bias and Prejudgment — Canon 2 MCJC

*A judge shall avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety in all activities.*

¶16. **Verbal Abuse on Record:** Judge McNeill called Plaintiff "a liar" and "crazy" on the record.

¶17. **Prior Representation:** Judge McNeill stated on the record: *"I have represented Party A in the past."* A prior attorney-client relationship with a party creates an irreconcilable conflict of interest and mandates disqualification under MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b).

¶18. **Self-Denial of Disqualification:** Plaintiff filed a Motion for Disqualification. Judge McNeill ruled on the motion herself, denying it. MCR 2.003(D)(1) mandates: *"The challenged judge shall not rule on the motion but shall refer it to the chief judge."* The Judge violated this mandatory procedural requirement.

¶19. **Pattern of One-Sided Orders:** 52 ex parte orders, 24 of 55 primary orders (44%) entered ex parte — 100% favoring one party. This statistical pattern alone demonstrates actual bias rather than mere appearance of impropriety.

¶20. **Contradictory Orders:** On February 28, 2025, the Court entered a contempt order imposing 14 days' jail AND dismissal simultaneously — logically irreconcilable remedies revealing arbitrary decision-making.

### CATEGORY 4: Failure to Follow Law — Canon 3(A)(1) MCJC

*A judge shall be faithful to the law and maintain professional competence in it.*

¶21. **MCR 3.207(C)(5) — Parenting Time Suspension Standard:** The Court suspended parenting time for 207+ days without a finding of clear and convincing evidence that the child's physical, mental, or emotional health would be seriously endangered, as required by the court rule.

¶22. **MCL 722.23 — Best Interest Factors:** The Court never entered written best-interest findings at any stage, despite modifying parenting time from full (365 days) to 50/50 (182.5 overnights) to reduced (79 overnights) to terminated (0). *Fletcher v Fletcher*, 447 Mich 871; 526 NW2d 889 (1994); *Ireland v Smith*, 451 Mich 457; 547 NW2d 686 (1996).

¶23. ***Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499; 675 NW2d 847 (2003):** The Court failed to apply the standard requiring a finding that parenting time would endanger the child before restricting it.

¶24. **MCR 2.003(D)(1) — Disqualification Referral:** The Court ruled on its own disqualification motion instead of referring it to the Chief Judge.

¶25. **MCL 600.2950 — PPO Standard:** The Court issued and maintained a PPO without competent evidence meeting the statutory requirements.

¶26. **MCL 750.539c — Felony Eavesdropping:** The Court considered and relied upon a recording made without consent, in violation of the felony eavesdropping statute.

### CATEGORY 5: Evidence Tampering, Brady Violation, and Ghost Evaluation

¶27. **Ghost Evaluation:** The second HealthWest evaluation (September 11, 2025) was initiated by the Court's own letter after the first evaluation cleared Plaintiff with all scores at zero. The second evaluator stated the Psychosis elevation was based on *"review of the letter provided by Andrew's judge."* Both evaluators testified that their analyses were NOT diagnoses supporting suspension of parenting time.

¶28. **Brady Violation:** Plaintiff never received a copy of the second evaluation before it was used against him at a hearing. The Court withheld material exculpatory evidence (the favorable first evaluation) by directing it to the Judge's email rather than the public court file.
