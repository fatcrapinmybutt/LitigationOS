## VIOLATION I: JUDICIAL BIAS AND LACK OF IMPARTIALITY
**Code Violated:** Canon 2, Rule 2.2, 2.3(A), 2.3(B)

**Legal Standard Paragraph (ADD):**

> A judge must uphold and apply the law impartially and without bias. Canon 2, Rule 2.2. A judge shall not manifest bias or prejudice against any party or exhibit behavior that a reasonable person would perceive as biased. Canon 2, Rule 2.3(A). 
>
> The Michigan Supreme Court has emphasized that even the appearance of bias violates judicial ethics. *People v. Johnson*, 492 Mich. 637 (2012) (judge may not express personal views during trial as it creates appearance of bias); *In re Recusal of Brickley*, 514 Mich. 906 (2018) (judge must recuse if reasonable question exists regarding impartiality).

**Supporting Case Law Section (ADD):**

### Judicial Bias Precedents

**Personal Interest and Conflict:** 
A judge who knowingly participates in a proceeding in which they have a personal interest violates the Code of Judicial Conduct. *In re Complaint Against Judge Raffaele*, 506 N.W.2d 97 (Mich. 1993). Similarly, failure to disclose a personal relationship with a party creates an appearance of bias. *In re Complaint Against Judge Kelsey*, 713 N.W.2d 469 (Mich. Ct. App. 2006).

**Prejudicial Remarks:**
A judge who makes prejudicial remarks about parties or attorneys engages in misconduct. *In re Complaint Against Judge Casnocha*, 684 N.W.2d 130 (Mich. 2004) (prejudicial remark about attorney); *In re Complaint Against Judge Krause*, 738 N.W.2d 683 (Mich. Ct. App. 2007) (disparaging remarks during sentencing).

**Pattern of Bias:**
A judge who exhibits a pattern of disregard for rules and procedures demonstrates bias. *In re Complaint Against Judge Groner*, 702 N.W.2d 490 (Mich. Ct. App. 2005); *In re Noecker*, 472 Mich. 1, 691 N.W.2d 440 (2005) (censure for temperament and bias issues).

**Comprehensive Misconduct:**
Multiple violations including bias warrant removal from office. *In re Chrzanowski*, 465 Mich. 468, 636 N.W.2d 758 (2001) (removal for bias, ex parte contacts, and improper conduct); *In re Gorcyca*, 500 Mich. 588, 902 N.W.2d 828 (2017) (suspension for temperament and bias).

### SECTION III: VIOLATION - EX PARTE COMMUNICATIONS

**Header with Code Citation:**
## VIOLATION II: EX PARTE COMMUNICATIONS
**Code Violated:** Canon 2, Rule 2.9(A)

**Legal Standard Paragraph (ADD):**

> A judge shall not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communications concerning a pending or impending matter. Canon 2, Rule 2.9(A). This prohibition is fundamental to maintaining impartiality and public confidence in the judiciary.
>
> Michigan courts have consistently held ex parte communications constitute serious judicial misconduct. *In re Wade*, 425 Mich. 60, 386 N.W.2d 309 (1986) (foundational case on ex parte violations); *In re Disciplinary Proceedings against Riordan*, 603 N.W.2d 874 (Mich. 1999) (ex parte communication during appellate process).

**Supporting Case Law Section (ADD):**

### Ex Parte Communication Precedents

**Political Context:**
Ex parte communications in connection with political campaigns constitute misconduct. *In re Disciplinary Proceedings against Sackstein*, 439 N.W.2d 613 (Mich. 1989).

**Prosecutor Communications:**
A judge's ex parte communication with prosecutors regarding pending cases violates Canon 2.9. *In re Disciplinary Proceedings against Riley*, 682 N.W.2d 591 (Mich. 2004).

**Law Enforcement Communications:**
Ex parte communications with law enforcement officers regarding pending matters constitute misconduct. *In re Disciplinary Proceedings against Kortenkamp*, 629 N.W.2d 10 (Mich. 2001).

**Plea Negotiations:**
While ex parte communications are generally prohibited, courts recognize limited exceptions. However, such communications regarding plea bargains require extreme caution. *People v. Loyd*, 873 N.W.2d 460 (Mich. Ct. App. 2016).

## VIOLATION V: DISCOVERY ABUSE AND FAILURE TO ENFORCE DISCOVERY RULES
**Code Violated:** MCR 2.302; MCR 2.313

**Legal Standard Paragraph (ADD):**

> Trial courts have a duty to enforce discovery rules and ensure compliance. MCR 2.302 governs discovery procedures, and MCR 2.313 provides sanctions for discovery violations. A judge's failure to enforce these rules or participation in discovery abuse constitutes misconduct.
>
> Michigan courts have affirmed that trial courts have broad discretion to impose discovery sanctions. *Vicencio v. Ramirez*, 211 Mich. App. 501, 536 N.W.2d 280 (1995). Sanctions are particularly appropriate for willful discovery violations. *Traxler v. Ford Motor Co.*, 227 Mich. App. 276, 576 N.W.2d 398 (1998).
