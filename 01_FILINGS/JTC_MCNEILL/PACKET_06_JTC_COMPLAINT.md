                    I. INTRODUCTION

1. Pursuant to MCR 9.200 et seq. and the Michigan Code of Judicial
Conduct, the undersigned complainant respectfully requests that the
Judicial Tenure Commission investigate the conduct of the Honorable

Conduct, Canons 1, 2, 3, and applicable Michigan Court Rules.

2. Complainant is a pro se litigant in multiple proceedings before
Judge McNeill: Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC (Custody), 2023-5907-PP
(Personal Protection Order), and 2021-186155-CB.

                    II. SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS

                III. CANON VIOLATIONS

A. CANON 1: Integrity and Independence of the Judiciary

4. Canon 1 provides: "An independent and honorable judiciary is
indispensable to justice in our society. A judge should participate in
establishing, maintaining, and enforcing, and should personally observe,
high standards of conduct so that the integrity and independence of the
judiciary may be preserved."

5. Judge McNeill's conduct has undermined the integrity of the
judiciary by:
    a. Entering orders depriving a parent of all contact with his child
       without evidentiary hearing;
    b. Failing to apply mandatory legal standards (MCL 722.23, MCL
       722.27a) required for custody and parenting time determinations;
    c. Creating an appearance that outcomes are predetermined rather
       than based on law and evidence.

B. CANON 2: Avoiding Impropriety and Its Appearance

6. Canon 2(A) provides: "A judge should respect and comply with the
law and should conduct himself or herself at all times in a manner that
promotes public confidence in the integrity and impartiality of the
judiciary."

7. Canon 2(B) provides: "A judge should not allow family, social, or
other relationships to influence judicial conduct or judgment."

8. Judge McNeill has violated Canon 2 by:
    a. Conducting ex parte proceedings and entering orders without
       notice to all parties;
    b. Applying different procedural standards to the parties;
    c. Creating an appearance of bias through consistent one-sided
       rulings without evidentiary support;
    d. Denying motions without hearing or written findings;
    e. Permitting the use of PPO proceedings as a custody tool,
       circumventing statutory protections.

C. CANON 3: Performing Duties Impartially and Diligently

9. Canon 3(A)(1) provides: "A judge should be faithful to the law and
maintain professional competence in it."

10. Canon 3(A)(4) provides: "A judge should accord to every person who
has a legal interest in a proceeding, or that person's lawyer, the right
to be heard according to law."

11. Canon 3(A)(5) provides: "A judge should dispose promptly of the
business of the court."

12. Canon 3(C)(1) provides: "A judge should disqualify himself or
herself in a proceeding in which the judge's impartiality might
reasonably be questioned."

13. Judge McNeill has violated Canon 3 by:
    a. Failing to accord Complainant the right to be heard on motions
       affecting his fundamental parental rights (Canon 3(A)(4));
    b. Failing to be faithful to mandatory requirements of MCL 722.23,
       MCL 722.27a, MCR 3.207, and MCR 3.707 (Canon 3(A)(1));
    c. Failing to promptly schedule and dispose of Complainant's
       motions (Canon 3(A)(5));
    d. Failing to self-disqualify despite an accumulation of bias
       indicators (Canon 3(C)(1));
    e. Engaging in or permitting ex parte communications regarding
       pending matters (Canon 3(A)(4)).
