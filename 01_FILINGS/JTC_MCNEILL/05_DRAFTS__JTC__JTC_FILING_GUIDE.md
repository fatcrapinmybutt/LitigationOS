## V. TIMELINE AND EXPECTATIONS {#timeline}

### What Happens After Filing?

#### Stage 1: Acknowledgment (7-14 days)
**What to Expect:**
- JTC will send acknowledgment letter confirming receipt
- You'll receive case number for reference
- Letter will explain next steps and investigation timeline

**Action:** Keep acknowledgment letter with your records

---

#### Stage 2: Initial Review (30-90 days)
**What Happens:**
- JTC staff attorney reviews complaint
- Determines if allegations warrant investigation
- May request additional information from you
- May request response from Judge McNeill

**Possible Outcomes:**
1. **Investigation Opened:** Formal investigation begins
2. **Request for More Information:** JTC needs additional evidence
3. **Dismissal:** Complaint does not state grounds for discipline (rare given strength of this complaint)

---

#### Stage 3: Investigation (90-180 days if investigation opened)
**What Happens:**
- JTC investigates allegations
- May interview witnesses (you, court staff, others)
- Reviews court records and transcripts
- Judge McNeill given opportunity to respond
- Investigator prepares report

**Your Role:**
- Respond promptly to any JTC requests
- Provide additional evidence if requested
- Be available for interview if requested
- Do NOT contact Judge McNeill about the complaint

---

#### Stage 4: Decision (After Investigation)
**Possible Outcomes:**

**Option A: Dismissal**
- JTC finds no misconduct warranting discipline
- You'll receive letter explaining decision
- You can appeal to Michigan Supreme Court

**Option B: Informal Resolution**
- JTC works with Judge for corrective action
- May include ethics training, reprimand, conditions
- Your cases may be reassigned

**Option C: Formal Proceedings**
- JTC files formal complaint against Judge McNeill
- Hearing before JTC panel
- More serious discipline possible (suspension, removal)
- Public record

---

### Overall Timeline Estimate

**Best Case:** 4-6 months (informal resolution)  
**Typical Case:** 6-12 months (investigation + decision)  
**Complex Case:** 12-24 months (formal proceedings)

**For This Case:** Given strength of evidence and systematic nature of violations, estimate **6-12 months** to resolution.

## VI. TIPS FOR SUCCESS {#tips}

### A. Do's

✅ **Remain Professional**
- Keep all communications with JTC professional and respectful
- Avoid emotional language or personal attacks on Judge McNeill
- Focus on facts, rules violated, and harm caused

✅ **Respond Promptly**
- JTC may request additional information
- Respond within deadlines provided (usually 14-30 days)
- If you need extension, request it before deadline

✅ **Keep Records**
- Save all JTC correspondence
- Keep copies of everything you send to JTC
- Track dates, deadlines, and communications

✅ **Be Available**
- JTC may want to interview you
- Keep your contact information current
- Check mail/email regularly

✅ **Stay Focused on This Complaint**
- Don't file additional complaints about same judge without consulting JTC
- Don't file frivolous or unrelated complaints
- Quality over quantity

✅ **Continue Case Proceedings**
- Filing JTC complaint doesn't stop your custody case
- Continue with appeals, motions, other remedies
- JTC process is parallel, not substitute for appeals

❌ **Don't Contact Judge McNeill**
- No direct communication about JTC complaint

## VII. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS {#faq}

### Q1: Will filing a JTC complaint help my custody case?
**A:** Not directly or immediately. JTC investigation takes months. However:
- If Judge McNeill is disciplined or disqualified, your case may be reassigned
- JTC findings can support appeals or federal civil rights claims
- Demonstrates serious nature of judicial misconduct
- **Meanwhile, continue pursuing other remedies** (emergency motions, appeals)

---

### Q2: Can Judge McNeill retaliate against me for filing?
**A:** Legally, no. Retaliation for filing JTC complaint would be additional misconduct. However:
- Judge may become aware of complaint during investigation
- JTC may ask judge for response
- **Best practice:** File complaint AND immediately file disqualification motion in your case citing bias

---

### Q3: Do I need a lawyer to file a JTC complaint?
**A:** No, you can file pro se. But:
- Lawyer can ensure complaint is legally sound
- Lawyer may identify additional violations
- Lawyer can assist if case goes to formal proceedings
- **For this complaint:** You have comprehensive documentation; pro se filing is viable

---

### Q4: What if JTC dismisses my complaint?
**A:** You can:
- Appeal to Michigan Supreme Court (30 days)
- File supplemental complaint if new evidence emerges
- Continue pursuing other remedies (appeals, federal court)
- **For this complaint:** Dismissal unlikely given strength of documented violations

---

### Q5: How much does it cost to file?
**A:** Filing the complaint itself is FREE. Costs:
- Notarization: $5-$15
- Copies: $10-$50
- Certified mail: $8-$12
- Obtaining exhibits (transcripts, dockets): $50-$600
- **Total estimated cost:** $75-$700

---

### Q6: Can I file anonymously?
**A:** No. You must identify yourself and sign complaint under penalty of perjury. Your identity will be disclosed to Judge McNeill during investigation.

---

### Q7: What happens to Judge McNeill during investigation?
**A:** During investigation:
- Judge continues to preside over cases (unless voluntarily recuses)
- Judge not publicly identified until formal proceedings
- Investigation confidential

**After findings:**
- If misconduct found: Public discipline, potential removal
- Your cases may be reassigned

---

### Q8: Will this help me get my parenting time back?
**A:** Indirectly, yes, but not immediately:
- JTC may recommend corrective orders
- Judge may recuse from your case
- New judge may review orders differently
- **However:** Don't rely solely on JTC - file emergency motions NOW

---

### Q9: Can I update my complaint after filing?
**A:** Yes, you can file supplemental information:
- New evidence discovered after filing
- Additional violations occurring after filing date
- Clarifications of original complaint
- **File supplement:** Send to JTC with your case number

---

### Q10: What if Judge McNeill retires before investigation complete?
**A:** JTC can still proceed with investigation and issue findings. Discipline may include:
- Public censure (part of permanent record)
- Prohibition on future judicial service
- Referral to Attorney Grievance if judge later practices law
