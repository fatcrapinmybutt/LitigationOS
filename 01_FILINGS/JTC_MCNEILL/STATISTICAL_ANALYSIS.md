**Complaint Against Hon. Jenny L. McNeill, 14th Judicial Circuit**

## I. OVERVIEW

This analysis quantifies the pattern of judicial misconduct by Judge Jenny L. McNeill across all documented violations in the referenced cases. The data is drawn from the court record, including orders, transcripts, docket entries, and contemporaneous documentation.

**Total Documented Violations: 1,127**
**Total Impeachment Items: 15,171**

## II. SEVERITY DISTRIBUTION

### A. Violation Severity Breakdown

| Severity Level | Count | Percentage | Description |
|----------------|-------|------------|-------------|
| CRITICAL | 377 | 33.5% | Constitutional violations, deprivation of fundamental rights |
| HIGH | 243 | 21.6% | Mandatory procedural failures, statutory violations |
| MEDIUM | 484 | 42.9% | Court rule deviations, benchbook departures |
| **TOTAL** | **1,127** | **100.0%** | |

### B. Severity Analysis

More than one-third of all violations (33.5%) are classified as CRITICAL, meaning they involve deprivation of constitutional rights or fundamental procedural protections. Over half (55.1%) are CRITICAL or HIGH severity. This distribution is inconsistent with normal judicial error patterns, which would be expected to show a preponderance of low-severity technical errors with a small tail of serious violations.

The inverted distribution here — where serious violations outnumber minor ones — is a hallmark of systematic misconduct rather than episodic error.

## IV. IMPEACHMENT DATABASE ANALYSIS

### A. Impeachment Item Categories

| Category | Count | % of Total |
|----------|-------|------------|
| Judicial Benchbook Deviation | 545 | 3.6% |
| Judicial PPO Weaponization | 381 | 2.5% |
| Other Impeachment Categories | 14,245 | 93.9% |
| **TOTAL** | **15,171** | **100.0%** |

### B. Benchbook Deviation Analysis

The 545 Judicial Benchbook Deviations represent instances where Judge McNeill's conduct departed from the mandatory procedural guidance established by the Michigan Judicial Institute. Benchbooks represent the minimum expected standard of judicial practice. Deviations at this volume indicate either:

1. Systemic unfamiliarity with mandatory procedures (incompetence), or
2. Deliberate disregard for established standards (misconduct).

In either case, the volume warrants investigation.

### C. PPO Weaponization Analysis

The 381 PPO Weaponization items document instances where the Personal Protection Order mechanism was used in a manner inconsistent with its statutory purpose. The PPO statute (MCL 600.2950) is designed to protect victims of domestic violence and stalking. When PPOs are deployed instead as tools to restrict parental access or gain tactical advantage in custody litigation, the judicial officer who permits or facilitates this misuse engages in misconduct.

## V. COMPARISON TO BASELINE JUDICIAL CONDUCT

### A. Ex Parte Order Rate

| Metric | Judge McNeill | Expected Baseline |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Ex Parte Order Rate | 44% | < 10% |

The expected baseline for ex parte orders in domestic relations cases is below 10%. Ex parte relief is an extraordinary remedy reserved for emergencies where irreparable harm would result from delay. A 44% ex parte rate is 4.4 times the expected baseline and is facially indicative of systemic abuse of ex parte procedures.

### B. Sanctions Rate for Motion Filing

| Metric | Judge McNeill | Expected Baseline |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Sanctions for filing motions | $250+ imposed | Rare; requires MCR 1.109(E) finding |

Sanctions for filing motions are exceedingly rare in Michigan practice and require a specific finding under MCR 1.109(E) that the filing was frivolous, filed for improper purpose, or not well-grounded in fact. No such findings were made by Judge McNeill.

### C. Best-Interest Factor Compliance

| Metric | Judge McNeill | Required by Law |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| MCL 722.23 factors analyzed | 0 of 12 | All 12 mandatory |

In every custody determination, Michigan law requires the court to evaluate all 12 best-interest factors of MCL 722.23. Judge McNeill evaluated zero factors while depriving Complainant of custody. This is not a matter of weight or emphasis; it is a complete failure to apply mandatory law.

## VI. TIMELINE OF ESCALATING MISCONDUCT

### A. Escalation Pattern

The data reveals a pattern of escalating misconduct that correlates with Complainant's exercise of legal rights. The timeline below illustrates the escalation:

**Phase 1: Initial Proceedings**
- Violations present but sporadic
- Primarily procedural in nature (MEDIUM severity)
- Ex parte orders begin appearing

**Phase 2: Post-Motion Escalation**
- Following Complainant's first substantive motions
- Sharp increase in CRITICAL and HIGH severity violations
- Ex parte order rate increases
- Retaliatory sanctions appear

**Phase 3: Sustained Misconduct**
- Violations become routine across all categories
- Sanctions imposed for filing motions ($250)
- Derogatory language appears in orders ("crazy," "liar")
- Custody deprivation without MCL 722.23 findings
- Negative drug screen ignored

**Phase 4: Current — Ongoing Harm**
- Pattern continues unabated
- Minor child continues to be deprived of relationship with fit parent
- Complainant's access to meaningful judicial review effectively foreclosed

### B. Filing-to-Retaliation Correlation

| Complainant Action | Judicial Response | Lag Time |
|-------------------|-------------------|----------|
| Motion to disqualify | Sanctions order | Days |
| Motion for hearing | Ex parte adverse order | Days |
| Motion for reconsideration | Escalated restrictions | Days–Weeks |
| Filing of any motion | Denial without analysis | Routine |

The consistency of this pattern across multiple instances eliminates coincidence as an explanation. The temporal proximity between filing and adverse action, combined with the absence of substantive legal analysis in the responsive orders, supports the conclusion of retaliatory intent.

## VIII. CONCLUSIONS

1. **Volume:** 1,127 violations is not attributable to normal judicial error. By comparison, a judge exercising reasonable diligence might commit a handful of reversible errors across a comparable case. Three orders of magnitude more violations indicates systemic misconduct.

2. **Severity Distribution:** The preponderance of CRITICAL and HIGH violations (55.1%) is inconsistent with error and consistent with deliberate misconduct.

3. **Category Breadth:** Violations spanning seven distinct categories with an additional 15,171 impeachment items demonstrates pervasive rather than isolated misconduct.

4. **Escalation:** The demonstrable escalation over time, correlated with Complainant's exercise of legal rights, supports the conclusion of retaliatory intent.

5. **Deviation from Baseline:** The 44% ex parte rate, zero best-interest factor compliance, and retaliatory sanctions each independently deviate from expected judicial conduct. Together, they establish a pattern that demands investigation.
