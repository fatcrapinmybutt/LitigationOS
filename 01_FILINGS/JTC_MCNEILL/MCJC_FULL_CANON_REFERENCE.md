# MICHIGAN CODE OF JUDICIAL CONDUCT — FULL CANON REFERENCE

## Applicable to JTC Request for Investigation — Judge Jenny L. McNeill

---

## Canon 1: Integrity and Independence of the Judiciary
A judge should participate in establishing, maintaining, and enforcing high standards of conduct
so that the integrity and independence of the judiciary may be preserved.

**McNeill Violations:** Pattern of conduct undermining public confidence in judiciary.

## Canon 2: Avoid Impropriety and Appearance of Impropriety
(A) A judge should respect and comply with the law and act in a manner promoting public confidence.
(B) A judge should not allow family, social, or other relationships to influence judicial conduct.
(C) A judge should not hold membership in discriminatory organizations.

**McNeill Violations:** Appearance of bias; possible relationship influence (defendant's employment
at Kent County Prosecutor's Office, Family Court Division).

## Canon 3: Perform Duties Impartially and Diligently (AMENDED SEPT 1, 2025)
(A)(1) A judge should be faithful to the law and maintain professional competence.
(A)(3) A judge should be patient, dignified, and courteous to all.
(A)(4) A judge should accord every person the right to be heard according to law.
       A judge should not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communications.
**(A)(5) A judge shall perform judicial duties without bias or prejudice.**

### ⚡ 2025 AMENDMENT (Effective September 1, 2025):
Canon 3 was amended to strengthen anti-bias provisions. Judges must not **"intentionally or
recklessly"** manifest bias, prejudice, or harassment based on protected characteristics including
race, sex, gender, religion, national origin, ethnicity, disability, age, sexual orientation,
marital status, or socioeconomic status. This brings Michigan closer to ABA Model Rule 2.3.

**SIGNIFICANCE FOR THIS CASE:** The 2025 amendment was in effect when many of the complained-of
actions occurred. The strengthened language ("intentionally or recklessly") lowers the bar for
establishing judicial misconduct.

## Canon 4: Extrajudicial Activities
A judge may engage in activities to improve the law, legal system, and administration of justice.

## Canon 5: Political Activity Restrictions
A judge should refrain from inappropriate political activity.

## Canon 6: Financial Reporting
A judge shall report compensation for quasi-judicial and extrajudicial activities.

## Canon 7: Political Activity
Judges must minimize political entanglements and remain nonpartisan.

## Canon 8: Collective Activity
Judges may engage in collective activities concerning the law and legal system.
