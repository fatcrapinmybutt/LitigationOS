Dear Members of the Judicial Tenure Commission:

I am writing to file a formal complaint against Judge Jenny L. McNeill of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, Muskegon County, pursuant to MCR 9.104 and MCR 9.115. I am proceeding pro se in this matter.

## NATURE OF COMPLAINT

This complaint alleges systematic judicial misconduct affecting fundamental parental rights in child custody proceedings. The misconduct involves:

1. **Five ex parte orders** (August-November 2025) issued without the procedural safeguards required by MCR 3.207(B);
2. **Inconsistent application of evidentiary standards** during the July 2024 custody trial;
3. **Systematic failure to apply** the statutory presumption favoring parenting time under MCL 722.27a;
4. **Ex parte review of evidence** without notice or opportunity to respond.

## URGENCY OF MATTER

This complaint is submitted with urgency due to ongoing harm. As of the date of this filing, I have been deprived of parenting time with my six-year-old son for **192 consecutive days** (since August 8, 2025) without any evidentiary hearing. The procedurally defective orders suspending parenting time remain in effect.

Each day of continued enforcement causes irreparable harm to:
- The parent-child relationship during a critical developmental period;
- My fundamental constitutional right to maintain a relationship with my child;
- Public confidence in the judicial system's adherence to procedural protections.

## PATTERN OF MISCONDUCT, NOT ISOLATED ERROR

This complaint does not allege isolated legal error subject to appellate review. Rather, it documents a systematic pattern:

- **Five separate ex parte orders** with identical procedural defects (no affidavits, no emergency findings, no hearings scheduled);
- **Repeated failure** to apply mandatory statutory presumptions across multiple orders;
- **Consistent evidentiary bias** favoring one party throughout the trial;
- **Multiple denials** of procedural protections over an 18-month period.

The repetition of identical procedural violations five times over six months demonstrates willful disregard for MCR 3.207(B) requirements rather than inadvertent error.

## SPECIFIC RULE VIOLATIONS

The complaint documents violations of:

**Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct:**
- Canon 1 (upholding integrity of judiciary);
- Canon 2 (avoiding appearance of impropriety);
- Canon 3 (performing duties impartially and being faithful to the law).

**Michigan Court Rules:**
- MCR 3.207(B) (ex parte order requirements) - violated five times;
- MCR 3.206(A) (verified motion requirements);
- MCR 2.119(B)(1) (verification requirements);
- MCR 2.109 (equal treatment of pro se litigants).

**Statutory Law:**
- MCL 722.27a (presumption favoring parenting time) - systematically not applied.

## LEGAL AUTHORITY SUPPORTING COMPLAINT

The complaint cites controlling Michigan case law:

- *In re Waltzer*, 717 NW2d 623 (Mich Ct App 2006) - strict compliance with MCR 3.207(B) required; non-compliance renders order void;
- *In re Temple*, 331 Mich App 630 (2020) - emergency orders cannot substitute for evidentiary hearing;
- *In re Flennaugh*, 261 Mich App 416 (2004) - judges must avoid appearance of impropriety;
- *Cain v Dep't of Corrections*, 451 Mich 470 (1996) - disqualification when reasonable person would question impartiality.

## RELIEF REQUESTED

I respectfully request that the Commission:

1. **Investigate** Judge McNeill's conduct in the referenced cases, including review of all orders, transcripts, and docket entries;

2. **Take appropriate disciplinary action**, including:
   - Public censure for Canon violations;
   - Remedial education on ex parte procedures, statutory requirements, and pro se litigant rights;
   - Suspension from custody cases pending completion of education;
   - Such additional discipline as the investigation may warrant;

3. **Recommend case reassignment** to a different judge to ensure fair proceedings going forward;

4. **Recommend corrective action** to the trial court, including vacatur of the procedurally defective ex parte orders and scheduling of an evidentiary hearing with proper procedural protections.
