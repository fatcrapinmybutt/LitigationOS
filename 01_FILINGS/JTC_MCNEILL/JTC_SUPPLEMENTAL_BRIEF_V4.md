# III. DOCUMENTED VIOLATIONS BY CANON

## A. Canon 1 — Integrity and Independence of the Judiciary

5. Canon 1 requires that a judge "should participate in establishing, maintaining, and enforcing high standards of conduct, and should personally observe those standards so that the integrity and independence of the judiciary may be preserved."

6. **Violation:** Judge McNeill entered five (5) ex parte orders on a single day (August 8, 2025) based on an **unverified, unsworn** motion, without compliance with the procedural safeguards of MCR 3.206(B)(5)-(6) and MCR 3.207(B)(1). This departure from basic procedural requirements undermines the integrity of the judiciary and public confidence in the court system.

## B. Canon 2 — Avoidance of Impropriety and Appearance of Impropriety

7. Canon 2 requires that a judge "should avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety in all activities."

8. **Violations:**

   a. **Appearance of bias:** The systematic pattern of crediting one party's unsworn allegations while refusing to consider the other party's documentary evidence creates an appearance of impropriety to any reasonable observer.

   b. **Disproportionate ex parte action:** Signing five ex parte orders on one day, imposing the most extreme remedy available (total elimination of parenting time), based on unsworn hearsay, without scheduling a timely hearing, creates the appearance of prejudgment.

   c. The litigation record documents 6,512 court speaker impeachment items and 20 court-attributed DUE_PROCESS_VIOLATION evidence entries — an extraordinary volume of documented procedural anomalies attributable to judicial action.

## C. Canon 3(A)(4) — Prohibition on Ex Parte Communications

9. Canon 3(A)(4): "A judge should accord to every person who has a legal interest in a proceeding, or that person's lawyer, full right to be heard according to law . . . A judge should not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communications, or consider other communications concerning a pending or impending proceeding, that occur outside the presence of the parties or their lawyers."

10. **Violations:**

   a. **USB Recording:** Evidence in the record indicates that Judge McNeill received and considered a USB recording submitted ex parte — outside the hearing, outside the record, and without providing the opposing party an opportunity to examine or challenge the content.

   b. **Mental Health Evaluation:** Similarly, mental health evaluation materials were handled off the record and ex parte, depriving Father of the opportunity to examine the source, challenge the methodology, or present rebuttal evidence.

   c. These ex parte communications went directly to the merits of disputed factual issues (Father's fitness, child safety) and cannot be categorized under any exception to the Canon 3(A)(4) prohibition.

## D. Canon 3(A)(5) — Right to Be Heard According to Law

11. Canon 3(A)(5) requires that judges "accord to every person who has a legal interest in a proceeding . . . the right to be heard according to law."

12. **Violations:**

   a. **Refusal to review exculpatory evidence:** Father attempted to present photographs of L.D.W. happy and healthy during exchanges and his NEGATIVE narcotics screen. Judge McNeill refused to review them.

   b. **Characterization of cross-examination as harassment:** The court stated on the record that Father's "continued pattern of questioning is meant to harass the defendant." Cross-examination of adverse witnesses is a fundamental due process right, not harassment.

   c. **329+ days without a merits hearing:** Despite MCR 3.206(B)(6) requiring a hearing "as soon as possible" after ex parte orders, no full evidentiary hearing has been held in over 329 days.

## E. Canon 3(B)(5) — Duty to Decide Without Favoritism

13. Canon 3(B)(5): "A judge should perform judicial duties without bias or prejudice."

14. **Violation:** The documented pattern shows systematic favoritism:

| Indicator | Mother (Watson) | Father (Pigors) |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Unsworn allegations | Accepted | — |
| Ex parte motions | Granted (5 orders same day) | Denied / not heard |

# IV. HARM TO THE ADMINISTRATION OF JUSTICE

15. The conduct documented herein has resulted in:

   a. **329+ days** of complete parent-child separation without a merits hearing — affecting a child under age 4 during the most critical developmental period;

   b. Erosion of public confidence in the impartiality of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court;

   c. Procedural violations that, if normalized, would render the due process safeguards of MCR 3.206, MCR 3.207, and MCR 2.517 meaningless;

   d. A chilling effect on pro se litigants, who witness their legal advocacy characterized as "harassment";

   e. **Facilitation of parental alienation:** Judge McNeill's pattern of granting Defendant Watson's ex parte motions without scrutiny, while refusing to enforce MCL 722.27a(9)'s mandatory remedies for denial of parenting time, has enabled a systematic campaign of parental alienation in violation of MCL 722.23(j). Defendant Watson — employed for nine (9) years as a caseworker in the **Kent County Prosecutor's Office, Family Court Division** — leveraged her institutional familiarity to obtain orders that severed the parent-child bond for **329+ consecutive days**. *Harvey v Harvey*, 470 Mich 186, 192 (2004), recognized Factor (j) as "an important consideration." Judge McNeill's failure to evaluate Factor (j) or enforce mandatory remedies has transformed the court into an instrument of alienation.
