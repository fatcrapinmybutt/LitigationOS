### VIOLATION 2: Failure to Be Impartial (MCJC Canon 3(A)(1))

**Rule:** "A judge should be faithful to the law and maintain professional competence in it. A judge shall be unbiased and impartial."

**Facts:** Judge McNeill demonstrated systematic bias against Plaintiff:
- Granted every substantive motion filed by opposing party
- Denied every substantive motion filed by Plaintiff
- Applied different procedural standards to Plaintiff vs. opposing party
- Made findings unsupported by evidence in the record

### VIOLATION 3: Denial of Due Process (MCJC Canon 3(A)(3))

**Rule:** "A judge shall accord to every person who is legally interested in a proceeding, or the person's lawyer, full right to be heard according to law."

**Facts:** Plaintiff was denied fundamental procedural rights:
- Orders entered without notice
- Motions decided without hearing
- Evidence excluded without legal basis
- Pro se litigant held to higher standard than represented party

### VIOLATION 4: Conduct Prejudicial to Administration of Justice (MCJC Canon 2(A))

**Rule:** "A judge should respect and comply with the law and should act at all times in a manner that promotes public confidence in the integrity and impartiality of the judiciary."

**Facts:** Judge McNeill's conduct undermines public confidence:
- Appearance of favoritism toward represented party
- Failure to ensure pro se litigant understood proceedings
- Rulings that are facially inconsistent with applicable law
- Pattern of conduct suggesting predetermined outcomes

### VIOLATION 5: Failure to Perform Duties Diligently (MCJC Canon 3(A)(5))

**Rule:** "A judge shall dispose of all judicial matters promptly, efficiently, and fairly."

**Facts:**
- Failed to rule on pending motions within reasonable time
- Failed to consider evidence presented by Plaintiff
- Failed to make adequate findings of fact
