# I. INTRODUCTION

1. This Supplemental Brief is filed in support of Complainant's previously filed Formal Complaint against the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill, judge of the 14th Judicial Circuit Court, Muskegon County, Michigan.

2. This Brief provides additional documented evidence of judicial conduct violations, organized by Canon and supported by specific record citations from the underlying proceedings (Case No. 2024-001507-DC and Case No. 2023-5907-PP).

3. Since the original Complaint was filed, comprehensive forensic analysis of the litigation record has identified **540 benchbook violations**, **226 ex parte violation entries**, **258 Canon violations mapped to specific record evidence**, **8 Berry investigation findings** regarding improperly considered ex parte evidence, and **15,171 impeachment items** in the database — of which **6,520 are attributed to COURT speaker conduct**. # III. DOCUMENTED VIOLATIONS BY CANON

## A. Canon 1 — Integrity and Independence of the Judiciary

7. Canon 1 requires that a judge "should participate in establishing, maintaining, and enforcing high standards of conduct, and should personally observe those standards so that the integrity and independence of the judiciary may be preserved."

8. **Violation:** Judge McNeill entered five (5) ex parte orders on a single day (August 8, 2025) based on an **unverified, unsworn** motion, without compliance with the procedural safeguards of MCR 3.206(B)(5)-(6) and MCR 3.207(B)(1). This departure from basic procedural requirements undermines the integrity of the judiciary and public confidence in the court system.

9. The cumulative pattern of 540 benchbook violations across five Canon/rule categories demonstrates a systemic failure to maintain the standards Canon 1 demands:

| Canon/Rule Violated | Violations | Percentage |
|---------------------|-----------|------------|
| Canon 2(B) | 186 | 34.4% |
| MCL 552.511b | 99 | 18.3% |
| MCR 3.207(B) | 96 | 17.8% |

*(See Exhibit A: BENCHBOOK_VIOLATION_REPORT, 05_ANALYSIS/BENCHBOOK_VIOLATION_REPORT.md)*

10. **Parental alienation nexus:** Each of these 540 violations occurred in a case where the net effect was the progressive elimination of a father's contact with his child. The sheer volume demonstrates that this is not inadvertent error but a pattern of conduct that facilitated the ongoing alienation of the parent-child relationship in violation of MCL 722.23(j).

## B. Canon 2 — Avoidance of Impropriety and Appearance of Impropriety

11. Canon 2 requires that a judge "should avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety in all activities."

12. Canon 2(B) specifically provides: "A judge shall not allow family, social, or other relationships to influence judicial conduct or judgment."

13. **186 Canon 2(B) violations** have been documented in the record, making this the single largest category of benchbook violations. *See* Exhibit A. These violations reflect a systematic pattern of deference to Defendant Watson — employed for nine (9) years as a caseworker in the **Kent County Prosecutor's Office, Family Court Division** — whose institutional familiarity with the court system was leveraged to obtain orders that no similarly situated litigant without system connections would receive.

14. **Violations:**

 a. **Appearance of bias:** The systematic pattern of crediting one party's unsworn allegations while refusing to consider the other party's documentary evidence creates an appearance of impropriety to any reasonable observer.

 b. **Disproportionate ex parte action:** Signing five ex parte orders on one day, imposing the most extreme remedy available (total elimination of parenting time), based on unsworn hearsay, without scheduling a timely hearing, creates the appearance of prejudgment.

 c. The litigation record documents **6,520 court speaker impeachment items** and **20 court-attributed DUE_PROCESS_VIOLATION evidence entries** — an extraordinary volume of documented procedural anomalies attributable to judicial action.

 d. **Watson employment connection creates impropriety:** Defendant's prosecutorial office employment created an inherent appearance of impropriety when the presiding judge (i) granted ex parte relief without adequate basis favoring the system insider, (ii) accepted unverified allegations from Watson while requiring strict proof from Pigors, (iii) failed to apply equal procedural standards, and (iv) created a credibility asymmetry — insider vs. outsider. This connection should have triggered recusal under Canon 2 and MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b).

15. **Parental alienation nexus:** The Canon 2(B) violations directly enabled parental alienation by creating a two-tier justice system: Watson's unsworn claims were accepted to suspend parenting time, while Pigors's documented evidence of alienation under MCL 722.23(j) was refused review. This asymmetry is the mechanism by which the court became an instrument of alienation.

A judge should not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communications, or consider other communications concerning a pending or impending proceeding, that occur outside the presence of the parties or their lawyers."

19. **Specific Ex Parte Violations:**

 a. **USB Recording pre-reviewed in chambers:** Evidence in the record indicates that Judge McNeill received and considered a USB flash-drive containing audio recording submitted ex parte — pre-listened in chambers before any adversarial hearing, outside the record, and without providing Father an opportunity to examine or challenge the content. This deprived Father of the right to object (MRE 103(a)), the right to cross-examine (MRE 611; US Const. Amend. XIV), and the right to present rebuttal evidence.

 b. **Mental Health Evaluation handled ex parte:** Mental health evaluation materials were handled off the record and ex parte, with chambers-only instructions to the evaluator after the August 28 hearing, depriving Father of the opportunity to examine the source, challenge the methodology, or present rebuttal evidence.

 c. **Ron Berry voicemail — ex parte evidence taint (Berry Investigation Finding #1):** Ron Berry's voicemail was listed as **Item #6** in Plaintiff's line-by-line objection to ex parte evidence. The voicemail was pre-listened in chambers before Plaintiff had opportunity to object. Berry is Defendant Watson's boyfriend, and his voicemail was submitted **by Watson** as part of the ex parte evidence package. This constitutes an ex parte communication under Canon 3(A)(4) and violates MCR 3.206(B)(5). *(See Exhibit D: BERRY_INVESTIGATION_COMPLETE, 05_ANALYSIS/BERRY_INVESTIGATION_COMPLETE.md — 8 findings documenting Berry's connection to Watson family and role in ex parte proceedings.)*

 d. **Five (5) ex parte orders entered on a single day (August 8, 2025):** The Court entered multiple ex parte orders on a single day without providing notice to Plaintiff — no opportunity to be heard before entry, no emergency justification documented, no findings of irreparable harm, and no MCR 3.207(B) compliance. **Authority:** MCR 3.206(B)(5); MCR 2.119; US Const. Amend. XIV.

 e. These ex parte communications went directly to the merits of disputed factual issues (Father's fitness, child safety) and cannot be categorized under any exception to the Canon 3(A)(4) prohibition.

20. **68 ex parte order evidence items** have been cataloged in the record, with the following legal significance classifications: DUE_PROCESS_VIOLATION (majority), BINDING_DETERMINATION, CONSTITUTIONAL_ISSUE, and others. The pattern shows systematic reliance on ex parte procedures — with **EX_PARTE_ORDER constituting the second-largest evidence category (16.8% of all 405 evidence items)** — bypassing the adversarial process entirely.

21. **Parental alienation nexus:** The ex parte violations are the primary mechanism of alienation. Each ex parte order that suspended or restricted parenting time without adversarial hearing extended the separation. The Berry voicemail — pre-listened in chambers, submitted by Watson's boyfriend — was used to support the suspension that has now exceeded 329 days. The court's willingness to consider ex parte evidence from Watson's social network while refusing to hear Father's evidence of alienation demonstrates how these Canon 3(A)(4) violations directly caused the alienation. **Authority:** MCL 722.23(j); *Harvey v Harvey*, 470 Mich 186, 192 (2004).

## D. Canon 3(A)(5) — Right to Be Heard According to Law

22. Canon 3(A)(5) requires that judges "accord to every person who has a legal interest in a proceeding . . . the right to be heard according to law."

23. **Violations:**

 a. **Refusal to review exculpatory evidence:** Father attempted to present photographs of L.D.W. happy and healthy during exchanges and his NEGATIVE narcotics screen. Judge McNeill refused to review them.

 b. **Characterization of cross-examination as harassment:** The court stated on the record that Father's "continued pattern of questioning is meant to harass the defendant." Cross-examination of adverse witnesses is a fundamental due process right, not harassment. This characterization had a chilling effect on Father's ability to present his case and is itself evidence of bias under Canon 3(B)(5).

 c. **329+ days without a merits hearing:** Despite MCR 3.206(B)(6) requiring a hearing "as soon as possible" after ex parte orders, no full evidentiary hearing has been held in over 329 days. MCR 2.517(A)(1) requires findings of fact and conclusions of law in all contested matters — no findings were made under MCL 722.23 before suspending parenting time.

 d. **$250 motion bond imposed:** The Court ordered that Plaintiff must pay a bond of $250 for each motion before filing, creating a financial barrier to access to justice for a pro se litigant that effectively silenced his ability to seek relief from the very orders causing alienation.

24. **Parental alienation nexus:** The denial of Father's right to be heard is functionally identical to enabling alienation — if a parent cannot present evidence of alienation to the court, the alienation proceeds unchecked. The refusal to review exculpatory evidence, the characterization of cross-examination as harassment, and the motion bond together created a procedural trap that prevented Father from challenging the ex parte orders that severed his relationship with his child.

# III-A. ANTICIPATED DEFENSES AND PRE-REBUTTAL

32. The Commission may receive responsive arguments from Judge McNeill or the court system. The following pre-rebuts the most likely defenses:

 a. **Anticipated Defense: "Adverse rulings do not establish misconduct."** *Pre-Rebuttal:* Complainant does not rely on adverse rulings alone. The claim rests on **540 quantified benchbook violations** — objective deviations from mandatory procedural requirements (MCR 3.207(B), MCR 3.210(C), MCL 552.511b) — plus **226 documented ex parte violations** that constitute independent misconduct regardless of the merits of any individual ruling. A single adverse ruling is within judicial discretion; 540 documented deviations from binding procedural mandates are not.

 b. **Anticipated Defense: "The judge exercised discretion in a difficult custody case."** *Pre-Rebuttal:* Discretion requires analysis. *Fletcher v Fletcher*, 447 Mich 871, 882; 526 NW2d 889 (1994), holds that "custody decisions require findings under all of the best interest factors." Judge McNeill analyzed **zero of twelve** MCL 722.23 factors before suspending all parenting time. Discretion that omits mandatory analysis is not discretion — it is abdication. Moreover, the $250 motion bond, the characterization of cross-examination as "harassment," and the refusal to review exculpatory evidence are not exercises of discretion but acts of bias.

 c. **Anticipated Defense: "Statistical analysis is not evidence of misconduct."** *Pre-Rebuttal:* The 540 benchbook violations and 15,171 impeachment items are derived exclusively from court filings, orders, transcripts, and docket entries — the official record of proceedings. The analysis categorizes and quantifies patterns already present in the record. When the record itself documents 226 ex parte violations, it is the *content* that establishes misconduct, not the analytical methodology.

 d. **Anticipated Defense: "Father was offered supervised parenting time and refused."** *Pre-Rebuttal:* The Court's characterization that Father "categorically rejected" supervised parenting time misrepresents the record. Father's position was that total suspension was unwarranted because, as he testified: "I am not any danger to my son in any way, shape, or form." A parent's objection to the *legal basis* for suspension is not a rejection of contact with his child. The Court's reframing of a legal objection as parental indifference is itself evidence of the bias pattern documented herein.
