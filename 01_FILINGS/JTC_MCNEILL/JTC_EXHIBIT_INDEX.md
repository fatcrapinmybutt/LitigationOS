- **Issued:** 2 days after Father filed motion challenging Judge's ex parte orders

- **Cited in Complaint:** Section IV.E, Section V (Pattern 4)

### D-2: Legal Authority Analysis for Bond Requirement
- **Document:** Memorandum analyzing Michigan law on pre-filing bonds
- **Pages:** 14 pages with statute and case law citations
- **Source:** Legal research memorandum
- **Key Findings:**
  - MCR 2.114(E): Allows sanctions for frivolous filings, but requires specific findings and notice
  - MCL 600.2591: Allows cost bonds for out-of-state parties only (Father is Michigan resident)
  - No Michigan statute or court rule authorizes pre-filing bonds in family law cases
  - Constitutional concern: Violates right of access to courts (Const 1963, art 1, § 17)
- **Case Law:**
  - No Michigan case upholding pre-filing bond in family law context
  - Similar bond requirements struck down in other jurisdictions
- **Cited in Complaint:** Section IV.E, Section VII (Legal Analysis)

- **Document:** Complete list of Father's filings with analysis of frivolity

  - Prior warnings about frivolous filings: 0 (zero)
