## IX. METHODOLOGY AND VERIFICATION

### A. Analysis Methodology

The adverse evidence matrix was generated through systematic, automated analysis of the complete evidentiary record using the following process:

1. **Evidence Collection**: All court filings, docket entries, hearing transcripts, court orders, and correspondence from Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP were collected and digitized.

2. **Pattern Extraction**: The JTC Conduct Pattern Table was constructed by identifying recurring patterns of judicial conduct across both proceedings, categorized into 6 distinct misconduct types.

3. **Adverse Finding Classification**: Each finding was classified by:
   - **Severity**: CRITICAL, HIGH, MEDIUM, LOW
   - **Type**: ex parte, due process, judicial misconduct, housing violation, bias, fraud, financial harm, retaliation
   - **Filing Target**: MSC, COA, Federal §1983, JTC, Circuit Court (Custody, Shady Oaks), Federal Fair Housing
   - **Case Lane**: A (Custody), B (Housing), C (Cross-lane), D (PPO), E (Misconduct), F (Appellate)

4. **Cross-Tabulation**: Findings were cross-tabulated by filing target, severity, and type to enable comprehensive analysis from multiple perspectives.

5. **Priority Scoring**: JTC conduct patterns were assigned priority scores based on volume (row count) and severity, enabling ranking of the most significant misconduct clusters.

### B. Verification

All findings are traceable to specific evidence through the AEM finding ID system (AEM_000001 through AEM_076329). Each finding ID links to:
- The source file from which it was extracted;
- The specific row, entry, or passage within that source file;
- The evidence text snippet documenting the finding;
- The classification metadata (severity, type, filing target, case lane).

The complete adverse evidence matrix (Adverse_Evidence_Matrix.csv) is available for this Court's review as a supplemental exhibit.
