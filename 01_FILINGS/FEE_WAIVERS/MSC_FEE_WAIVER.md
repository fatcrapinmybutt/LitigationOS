### I. LEGAL AUTHORITY

1. **MCR 7.302(D)(2)** provides that "[a] party who is unable because of indigency to pay fees and costs required for an application for leave to appeal may file a motion for waiver of fees and costs with an affidavit establishing the claimed indigency."

2. **MCR 2.002** further authorizes suspension of fees and costs in any Michigan court when a party demonstrates inability to pay.

3. **MCL 600.2529(3)** provides that a court may waive or suspend the payment of fees and costs upon a showing of indigency.

4. The United States Supreme Court has held that due process and equal protection prohibit states from conditioning appellate review of proceedings involving fundamental parental rights upon the litigant's ability to pay. *M.L.B. v. S.L.J.*, 519 U.S. 102 (1996).

5. Article 1, § 13 of the Michigan Constitution guarantees that "[a] suitor in any court of this state has the right to prosecute or defend his suit, either in his own proper person or by an attorney."

### II. PROCEDURAL POSTURE

6. Appellant seeks review before this Court from proceedings in the 14th Circuit Court — Family Division, Muskegon County (Case No. 2024-001507-DC, Hon. Jenny L. McNeill presiding), which is also the subject of Court of Appeals Case No. 366810.

7. The underlying proceedings concern custody of Appellant's minor child, **L.D.W.** (DOB: November 9, 2022), and related matters including personal protection orders and contempt proceedings that resulted in Appellant's incarceration for 59 days.

### IV. CONSTITUTIONAL NECESSITY

18. The filing fee for an application for leave to appeal to this Court is **$375.00**. Appellant cannot pay this amount. He has no income and no assets from which to pay.

19. Denying Appellant access to this Court based on his inability to pay — an inability caused by the very proceedings he seeks to challenge — would violate both the Due Process and Equal Protection clauses of the Fourteenth Amendment. *M.L.B. v. S.L.J.*, 519 U.S. 102 (1996); *Boddie v. Connecticut*, 401 U.S. 371 (1971).

20. The issues Appellant seeks to raise before this Court involve his fundamental parental rights, his liberty (having been jailed for 59 days), and significant questions of Michigan procedural and constitutional law. These are precisely the type of issues that warrant this Court's review regardless of the litigant's financial status.
