## MOTION FOR WAIVER OF FEES AND COSTS

**NOW COMES** Plaintiff-Appellant, **Andrew James Pigors**, appearing in propria persona, and respectfully moves this Honorable Court, pursuant to **MCR 7.202(8)** and **MCR 2.002**, for an order waiving all fees and costs associated with the filing, prosecution, and disposition of this appeal, and in support thereof states as follows:

---

### I. INTRODUCTION

1. Appellant Andrew James Pigors seeks review in this Court of orders entered by the 14th Circuit Court — Family Division, Muskegon County (Hon. Jenny L. McNeill presiding), in Case No. 2024-001507-DC.

2. Appellant is unable to pay the fees and costs required for this appeal due to his indigency, as demonstrated by the sworn affidavit attached hereto.

### II. LEGAL AUTHORITY

3. **MCR 7.202(8)** provides: "A party who is unable because of indigency to pay the fees and costs required for an appeal may file a motion for waiver of fees and costs accompanied by an affidavit establishing the claimed indigency."

4. **MCR 2.002** authorizes any Michigan court to suspend fees and costs for a party who demonstrates inability to pay.

5. The United States Supreme Court has held that a state may not condition a litigant's access to appellate review in cases involving fundamental parental rights on the ability to pay filing fees. *M.L.B. v. S.L.J.*, 519 U.S. 102, 116–17 (1996). The issues in this appeal directly involve Appellant's custody of and relationship with his minor child, L.D.W.

### III. STATEMENT OF FACTS SUPPORTING INDIGENCY

6. Appellant is currently **unemployed** and has **$0.00 monthly income**.

7. Appellant has lost **four (4) jobs** as a direct result of this litigation. Appellant was incarcerated for a total of **fifty-nine (59) days** in connection with proceedings in the lower court, causing him to be terminated from each position, including most recently from **Shape Corp**.

8. Appellant has lost **two (2) homes** (rental housing) because he was unable to pay rent while incarcerated and unemployed.

9. Appellant's current address is 1977 Whitehall Road, Lot 17, North Muskegon, MI 49445. He has minimal personal property and no significant assets.

10. Appellant has outstanding debts including court fines and child support obligations. He has no savings, no investments, and no real property.

11. Appellant has one dependent child, **L.D.W.** (DOB: November 9, 2022), who is currently being withheld from Appellant by the Defendant-Appellee.

12. Appellant's indigency is not voluntary — it is the direct and foreseeable consequence of the lower court proceedings from which he now seeks appellate review. Requiring Appellant to pay filing fees would effectively deny him access to this Court's review of orders affecting his fundamental parental rights.
