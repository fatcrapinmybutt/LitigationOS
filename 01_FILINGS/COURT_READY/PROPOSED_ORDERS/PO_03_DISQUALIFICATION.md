## ORDER GRANTING MOTION FOR DISQUALIFICATION AND REASSIGNMENT OF JUDGE

At a session of said Court, held in the Courthouse, County of Muskegon, State of Michigan, on _________, 2026.

**PRESENT: HON. [CHIEF JUDGE / REVIEWING JUDGE]**
Circuit Court Judge

---

This matter having come before the Court on Plaintiff Andrew James Pigors' Motion for Disqualification of the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill (P58235) pursuant to MCR 2.003(C)(1), and the matter having been referred to the undersigned pursuant to MCR 2.003(D), and the Court having reviewed the motion, supporting brief, exhibits, the record of proceedings, and any response, and being otherwise fully advised in the premises;

**THE COURT FINDS:**

A. Plaintiff has demonstrated that a reasonable person, knowing all the circumstances, would have reason to question the assigned judge's impartiality in this matter, satisfying the standard under MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b);

B. The totality of the record, including the pattern of ex parte proceedings, the manner in which hearings were conducted, and the systematic treatment of the parties, creates an objectively reasonable basis for questioning impartiality;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

1. Plaintiff's Motion for Disqualification of the Honorable Jenny L. McNeill (P58235) is **GRANTED** pursuant to MCR 2.003(C)(1)(a) and (b).

2. The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill is **DISQUALIFIED** from all further proceedings in Case No. 2024-001507-DC and Case No. 2023-5907-PP.

3. Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP are hereby **REASSIGNED** to a different judge within the 14th Judicial Circuit who has no prior connection to, or professional relationship with, the Ladas, Hoopes, or McNeill judicial families, or if no such judge is available within the circuit, to a visiting judge assigned by the State Court Administrative Office.

4. All ex parte orders entered without proper notice and hearing, including but not limited to the July 17, 2025 and August 8, 2025 ex parte orders suspending Plaintiff's parenting time with the minor child L.D.W., are hereby **VACATED** pending rehearing before the newly assigned judge with full notice and opportunity to be heard for all parties.

5. Plaintiff's parenting time with the minor child L.D.W. is **IMMEDIATELY RESTORED** on an interim basis, pending reassignment and rehearing before the newly assigned judge, under the last non-suspensive parenting-time order in effect prior to July 17, 2025.

6. The $250 filing deposit requirement previously imposed upon Plaintiff is hereby **VACATED** as an unconstitutional burden on Plaintiff's right of access to the courts.

7. The newly assigned judge shall conduct a status conference within thirty (30) days of reassignment to establish a case management schedule, including discovery deadlines, hearing dates, and trial date.

**IT IS FURTHER ORDERED** that the Clerk shall serve copies of this Order on all parties and the State Court Administrative Office forthwith.
