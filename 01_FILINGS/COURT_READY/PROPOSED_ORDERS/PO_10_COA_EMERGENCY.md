## ORDER GRANTING EMERGENCY MOTION FOR IMMEDIATE CONSIDERATION AND TEMPORARY RELIEF PENDING APPEAL

Before: _________, P.J., and _________, and _________, JJ.

---

The Court having considered Appellant Andrew James Pigors' Emergency Motion for Immediate Consideration and for Temporary Relief Pending Appeal, and having reviewed the motion, supporting brief, lower court record, and any response, and being otherwise fully informed;

**THE COURT FINDS:**

A. Appellant has demonstrated a strong likelihood of success on the merits of his appeal, given the documented absence of written findings under MCL 722.23 and MCL 722.27a(3), and the pattern of ex parte proceedings without compliance with MCR 3.207(B);

B. Appellant and the minor child L.D.W. will suffer **irreparable harm** absent immediate relief, as the child has been deprived of all contact with Appellant for 248 consecutive days, causing compounding developmental harm to the parent-child bond that cannot be remedied retroactively;

C. The **balance of hardships** favors granting relief, as restoring parenting time under appropriate conditions serves the child's best interests, while the status quo inflicts ongoing irreparable injury;

D. The **public interest** is served by ensuring compliance with court rules, statutory standards, and constitutional due process protections;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED, pursuant to MCR 7.205(F)(2) and MCR 7.209:**

1. **STAY OF EX PARTE ORDERS.** The August 6–8, 2025 ex parte order suspending all of Appellant's parenting time, and any continuations or modifications of that suspension, are hereby **STAYED** pending this Court's decision on the application for leave to appeal and, if granted, on the merits of the appeal.

2. **REINSTATEMENT OF PARENTING TIME.** The last non-suspensive parenting-time order (the 50/50 temporary order in effect from May 5, 2024 through July 17, 2025) is hereby **REINSTATED** as the interim parenting-time order, subject to the following conditions:

   (a) Exchanges of the minor child L.D.W. shall occur at a neutral, public location;

   (b) Communications between the parties regarding parenting-time logistics shall comply with any Personal Protection Order in effect, utilizing the Friend of the Court or a court-approved communication platform;

3. **STAY OF CONTEMPT PROCEEDINGS.** All pending contempt proceedings in Case No. 2023-5907-PP that are premised on parenting-time logistics communications are hereby **STAYED** pending this Court's review of the PPO/custody interaction issues raised in the appeal.

4. **ALTERNATIVELY**, in the event this Court determines that immediate reinstatement of the full prior parenting-time schedule is not appropriate pending full briefing, it is ORDERED that a **graduated interim parenting-time schedule** shall commence as follows:

   (a) Supervised visits shall begin within fourteen (14) days of entry of this Order;

   (b) Unsupervised visits shall commence within thirty (30) days of entry of this Order, absent documented safety concerns supported by specific findings;

   (c) The schedule shall expand progressively toward the prior 50/50 arrangement to prevent further deterioration of the parent-child relationship during appellate proceedings.

5. **RECORD TRANSMISSION.** The trial court shall **promptly transmit** any transcripts and records requested by this Court to expedite review.

6. The trial court shall take **no further action** that would render this appeal moot or further restrict Appellant's parenting time pending resolution of this appeal, absent emergency circumstances documented with specific findings and notice to all parties.

**IT IS FURTHER ORDERED** that Appellee shall file any response to this Order within fourteen (14) days of service.
