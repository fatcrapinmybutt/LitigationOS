## ORDER MODIFYING CUSTODY AND PARENTING TIME

At a session of said Court, held in the Courthouse, County of Muskegon, State of Michigan, on _________, 2026.

**PRESENT: HON. [ASSIGNED JUDGE]**
Circuit Court Judge, Family Division

---

This matter having come before the Court on Plaintiff/Father Andrew James Pigors' Motion to Modify Custody and Parenting Time, Motion for Declaratory Judgment, and Motion for Interim Relief, and the Court having reviewed the motion, brief in support, exhibits, the HealthWest evaluation, and any response filed by Defendant/Mother Emily A. Watson, and the Court having conducted an evidentiary hearing, and being otherwise fully advised in the premises;

**THE COURT FINDS:**

A. The 438-day period from May 5, 2024 through July 17, 2025, during which the minor child L.D.W. resided with both parents under a 50/50 parenting-time arrangement, constituted an **established custodial environment with both parents** under MCL 722.27(1)(c);

B. The ex parte orders of July 17, 2025 and August 8, 2025 **disrupted** — rather than created — the established custodial environment;

C. The applicable burden of proof for restoring the 50/50 arrangement is **preponderance of the evidence**, not clear and convincing evidence;

D. Father has demonstrated by a preponderance of the evidence that modification of the current custody and parenting-time arrangement is in the **best interests** of the minor child L.D.W., after consideration of all twelve (12) factors under MCL 722.23(a)–(l);

E. The 248-day total deprivation of Father's parenting time has caused irreparable harm to the parent-child relationship and the child's developmental well-being;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

1. **CUSTODY MODIFICATION.** The custody arrangement is hereby modified to provide Father and Mother with **joint legal custody and joint physical custody** of the minor child L.D.W..

2. **PARENTING TIME RESTORATION.** The 50/50 parenting-time arrangement that was in effect from May 5, 2024 through July 17, 2025 is hereby **RESTORED**, effective immediately, or such substantially equal parenting-time schedule as this Court determines is in the best interests of L.D.W..

3. **VACATUR OF EX PARTE ORDERS.** The ex parte orders of July 17, 2025 and August 8, 2025, and any subsequent orders continuing or converting the suspension of Father's parenting time, are hereby **VACATED** as having been entered without proper notice and hearing in violation of Father's constitutional due process rights.

4. **MAKE-UP PARENTING TIME.** Father is awarded **make-up parenting time** to compensate for 248 days of total deprivation of the parent-child relationship, pursuant to MCL 722.27a(7), on a schedule to be agreed upon by the parties or established by this Court within thirty (30) days.

5. **GUARDIAN AD LITEM.** A Guardian Ad Litem is hereby **APPOINTED** for the minor child L.D.W. pursuant to MCL 722.24 and MCR 3.211, to independently investigate the child's best interests, including L.D.W.'s reported statements regarding conditions in each parent's home. The GAL shall file a written report within sixty (60) days of appointment.

6. **HEALTHWEST EVALUATION.** The HealthWest evaluation (H0002, approximately September 5, 2025), which was favorable to Father, shall be **given appropriate weight** in determining custody and parenting time.

7. **EXCLUSION OF UNRELIABLE EVALUATION.** The second HealthWest evaluation (September 11, 2025 — Gansen/DeAugustine) is hereby **EXCLUDED** pursuant to MRE 702, MRE 703, and the *Daubert* framework, as unreliable, unauthenticated, procedurally defective, and lacking adequate foundation.

8. **DECLARATORY JUDGMENT.** Pursuant to MCR 2.605, it is **DECLARED** that the 438-day 50/50 parenting arrangement constituted an established custodial environment with both parents under MCL 722.27(1)(c), and that the applicable standard for restoring the 50/50 arrangement is preponderance of the evidence.

9. **INTERIM PARENTING TIME.** Pending full implementation of this Order, Father shall have **immediate interim parenting time**, including videoconferencing contact pursuant to MCR 3.210(A)(4), to begin restoring the parent-child bond and prevent further irreversible developmental harm to L.D.W..

10. **FILING BOND VACATED.** Any order requiring Father to post a cash bond as a precondition to filing motions in this custody proceeding is hereby **VACATED** as violative of Father's right of access to the courts under *Boddie v. Connecticut*, 401 U.S. 371 (1971).

11. **ALIENATION FINDING.** Pursuant to MCL 722.27a(8), the Court makes findings regarding Mother's documented pattern of parental alienation and **ORDERS** appropriate sanctions and remedial measures, including but not limited to therapeutic intervention and compliance monitoring.

12. **SANCTIONS FOR WITHHOLDING.** Defendant/Mother is hereby **SANCTIONED** for documented withholding periods (39 days in March–May 2024 and 26 days in October–November 2024), and Father is awarded make-up parenting time for each withholding episode pursuant to MCL 722.27a(7).

13. Father is awarded his reasonable **costs and fees** incurred in bringing this Motion.

**IT IS FURTHER ORDERED** that the Friend of the Court shall implement and monitor the parenting-time schedule set forth herein, and shall report any violations to this Court within seven (7) days of occurrence.
