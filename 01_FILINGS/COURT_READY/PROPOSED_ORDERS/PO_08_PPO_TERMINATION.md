## ORDER ON RESPONDENT'S MOTION TO TERMINATE PERSONAL PROTECTION ORDER

At a session of said Court, held in the Courthouse, County of Muskegon, State of Michigan, on _________, 2026.

**PRESENT: HON. [ASSIGNED JUDGE]**
Circuit Court Judge, Family Division

---

This matter having come before the Court on Respondent Andrew James Pigors' Motion to Terminate Personal Protection Order and Counter-Complaint, and the Court having reviewed the motion, counter-complaint, supporting brief, exhibits, and the record of proceedings, and the Court having conducted an evidentiary hearing with testimony from the parties and witnesses, and being otherwise fully advised in the premises;

**THE COURT FINDS:**

A. The Personal Protection Order issued in this matter has been **weaponized** as a tool for custody manipulation and interference with the parent-child relationship between Respondent and the minor child L.D.W., rather than serving its legitimate protective purpose;

B. Petitioner Emily A. Watson obtained and/or maintained the PPO through misrepresentation and fraud upon the Court;

C. The PPO, as applied, imposes unconstitutional restrictions on Respondent's fundamental parenting rights, including the right to communicate regarding parenting-time logistics;

D. The PPO has been used as a surrogate mechanism to effect custody and parenting-time changes outside the procedures required by the Child Custody Act, MCL 722.21 *et seq.*;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

1. **PPO TERMINATED.** The Personal Protection Order issued against Respondent Andrew James Pigors in Case No. 2023-5907-PP is hereby **TERMINATED AND VACATED IN ITS ENTIRETY**, effective immediately, pursuant to MCL 600.2950.

2. **VACATUR AS FRAUDULENTLY OBTAINED.** The PPO is additionally vacated on the ground that it was obtained through fraud upon the Court, based on misrepresentations made by Petitioner in the original petition and subsequent filings.

3. **CONTEMPT FINDINGS VACATED.** All contempt findings and sentences entered against Respondent under this PPO that were premised on parenting-time logistics communications, birthday messages, or other constitutionally protected conduct are hereby **VACATED**.

4. **NO SURROGATE CUSTODY PROCEEDINGS.** The Court directs that PPO show-cause proceedings shall **NOT** be used as a surrogate mechanism to effect custody or parenting-time changes. All allegations of interference, alienation, or gatekeeping shall be addressed through Child Custody Act mechanisms, with balanced enforcement against both parents.

5. **COUNTER-COMPLAINT PRESERVED.** Respondent's Counter-Complaint for Abuse of Process (Count I), Malicious Prosecution (Count II), Tortious Interference with Parental Relationship (Count III), Fraud Upon the Court (Count IV), Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress (Count V), Felony Eavesdropping (Count VI), and Unauthorized Disclosure of Protected Medical Information (Count VII) shall proceed to adjudication on the merits.

6. **PERJURY REFERRAL.** The matter of Petitioner's representations in the PPO petition and subsequent filings is hereby **REFERRED** to the Muskegon County Prosecutor's Office for investigation of potential perjury.

7. **EVIDENCE EXCLUSION.** Any evidence derived from Lori Watson's unauthorized recording in violation of MCL 750.539c is hereby **EXCLUDED**.

8. **MEDICAL RECORDS.** Any evidence derived from unauthorized access to Respondent's protected medical information is hereby **EXCLUDED**, and the matter is referred to the appropriate agency for investigation of HIPAA violations.

**IT IS FURTHER ORDERED** that Petitioner and Respondent shall conduct any necessary communications regarding the minor child L.D.W. through the Friend of the Court or a court-approved communication platform, until further order of this Court.
