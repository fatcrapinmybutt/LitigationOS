## ORDER GRANTING EMERGENCY MOTION FOR TEMPORARY RESTRAINING ORDER

At a session of said Court, held in the Courthouse, County of Muskegon, State of Michigan, on _________, 2026.

**PRESENT: HON. [ASSIGNED JUDGE]**
Circuit Court Judge

---

This matter having come before the Court on Plaintiff Andrew James Pigors' Emergency Motion for Temporary Restraining Order, and the Court having reviewed the motion, verified complaint, exhibits, and supporting affidavit, and being otherwise fully advised in the premises; and the Court having found that:

(a) Plaintiff has demonstrated a likelihood of success on the merits of his underlying claims;

(b) Plaintiff will suffer immediate and irreparable injury, loss, or damage if the Temporary Restraining Order is not issued;

(c) The balance of hardships favors the issuance of the Temporary Restraining Order; and

(d) The public interest is served by preserving the status quo pending a hearing on the preliminary injunction;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

1. Defendants Shady Oaks Park MHP LLC, Homes of America LLC, Cricklewood MHP LLC, and Does 1–10, and all persons in active concert or participation with them, are **IMMEDIATELY RESTRAINED AND ENJOINED** from:

   (a) Seizing, moving, entering, damaging, disposing of, demolishing, or in any way interfering with Plaintiff's mobile home (1970 Marlette, VIN 1646732) located at Lot 17, 1977 Whitehall Road, North Muskegon, MI 49445;

   (b) Shutting off, disconnecting, or interfering with utilities (water, sewer, electricity, gas) serving said mobile home;

   (c) Retaliating against Plaintiff in any manner for filing this action or seeking this Order.

2. A hearing on Plaintiff's request for a Preliminary Injunction is **SCHEDULED** for _________, 2026, at _____:_____ a.m./p.m. before this Court, not more than fourteen (14) days from entry of this Order, as required by MCR 3.310(B)(2).

3. The Court finds that **no bond is required**, as Plaintiff has demonstrated that the threatened harm is irreparable and the equities overwhelmingly favor Plaintiff.

4. Plaintiff is awarded his reasonable costs incurred in bringing this Emergency Motion, in an amount to be determined.

5. This Order shall remain in effect until the hearing on the Preliminary Injunction or further order of this Court, whichever occurs first.

**IT IS FURTHER ORDERED** that service of this Order upon Defendants shall be effectuated immediately by any method authorized by MCR 2.105, including personal service and service upon registered agents.
