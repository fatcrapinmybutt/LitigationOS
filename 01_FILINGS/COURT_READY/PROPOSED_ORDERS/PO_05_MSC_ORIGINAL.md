## ORDER GRANTING PETITION FOR SUPERINTENDING CONTROL

At a session of the Michigan Supreme Court, held at Lansing, Michigan, on _________, 2026.

**PRESENT:** Chief Justice _________ and Justices _________

---

On order of the Court, the Petition for Writ of Superintending Control filed by Andrew James Pigors having been considered, and the Court being otherwise fully informed;

**THE COURT FINDS:**

A. Petitioner has demonstrated that the lower court failed to perform a clear legal duty, specifically the requirement to make written findings under MCL 722.23 and MCL 722.27a(3) before restricting parenting time, and to provide notice and hearing before entering ex parte orders modifying custody;

B. Petitioner has no adequate legal remedy in the ordinary course of law, given the documented pattern of ex parte proceedings, denial of the right to be heard, and the 248-day deprivation of all parenting time with the minor child L.D.W.;

C. The lower court's conduct raises serious concerns about compliance with Michigan Court Rules and the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct;

**IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:**

**A. SUPERINTENDING CONTROL — The 14th Judicial Circuit Court is directed to:**

1. **Immediately restore** meaningful parenting time between Petitioner and the minor child L.D.W., beginning within fourteen (14) days of entry of this Order, under the last non-suspensive parenting-time order in effect prior to July 17, 2025, or such other schedule as will promptly restore the parent-child relationship severed for 248 consecutive days;

2. **Conduct a full evidentiary hearing** on custody and parenting time with explicit written findings on all twelve (12) best interest factors under MCL 722.23(a)–(l), to be completed within sixty (60) days of this Court's Order;

3. **Enforce parenting time** as mandated by MCL 552.644, including sanctions against any party for documented violations;

4. **Cease all ex parte communications** and ensure that all evidence is filed on the official record and accessible to all parties;

5. **Ensure Petitioner's filings** are docketed and considered in the ordinary course;

6. **Provide Petitioner** with all documents and evidence previously routed outside the official record;

**B. DISQUALIFICATION — It is FURTHER ORDERED that:**

1. The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill (P58235) is **IMMEDIATELY DISQUALIFIED** from all proceedings involving Petitioner and L.D.W., pursuant to MCR 2.003(C)(1)(b);

2. Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP are **TRANSFERRED** to a different judge in a different division or circuit, to eliminate any appearance of institutional bias;

3. The Honorable Jenny L. McNeill is **PROHIBITED** from taking any further action in either case pending this Court's further review;

**C. VACATUR — It is FURTHER ORDERED that:**

1. All orders entered ex parte without notice to Petitioner and without emergency circumstances required by MCR 3.207(B) are **VACATED**;

2. All orders predicated on the unconstitutional $250 bond requirement are **VACATED**;

3. All contempt orders entered as punitive contempt disguised as civil contempt, without compliance with *Mead v Batchlor*, 435 Mich 480 (1990), and *Turner v Rogers*, 564 US 431 (2011), are **VACATED**;

**D. INDEPENDENT EVALUATION —** An independent custody evaluation shall be conducted by a qualified evaluator selected by this Court or the newly assigned judge, with results provided to all parties and the Court;

**E. CASE MANAGEMENT —** The newly assigned judge shall establish a comprehensive case management schedule within thirty (30) days of reassignment, including regular status conferences, discovery deadlines, and a trial date;

**F. COMPLIANCE REPORTING —** The 14th Circuit Court shall file monthly compliance reports with this Court for a period of twelve (12) months to ensure compliance with this Court's directives;

**G. EMERGENCY PARENTING TIME —** Pending full compliance with this Order, interim parenting time is ordered to commence within fourteen (14) days, to prevent further irreversible developmental harm to L.D.W.;

**H. AUDIT —** A full audit of the trial court's handling of Case Nos. 2024-001507-DC and 2023-5907-PP is ordered, to include review of all off-docket evidence and communications, reconstruction of the complete record, and assessment of compliance with the Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct;

**I. JTC REFERRAL —** The documented misconduct is **REFERRED** to the Michigan Judicial Tenure Commission for investigation pursuant to Const 1963, art 6, § 30;

**J.** Such other and further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.
